2016<br><b>Type:<\/b>  Singleplayer <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">General Impression<\/div>Have you ever wondered what it\u2019s like to be an internet celebrity? Of course you have, only that unlike the lucky few that actually reach that status, you probably lack the charisma, self-confidence, good looks and so on and so forth. Hey, at least look on the bright side of things: you\u2019re not a Steam review writing loser! Youtubers Life is the promising debut of a dev team that nailed the hardships of trying to please an ever critical online crowd. Not to mention severely lacking in common sense since this is the internet, right? We can act like the animals we can\u2019t impersonate in real life, because of society and other reasons.<br><br>The game was nowhere near as buggy as some reviews pointed out. It is true that I focused on the Gaming Channel since the other two channels for Music and Cooking, don\u2019t count among topics I\u2019d feel the need to write about. I listen to almost any genre as long as the current mood fits it and as far as food is concerned, I never understood \u201cthe art of cuisine\u201d. At least in gaming\u2019s favor I can vouch with my nearly 18 years of experience while playing almost daily on PC. I was still dismayed by the limited selection of activities you start with in Youtubers Life. For example, as a novice Gamer on \u201cU-Tube\u201d (that\u2019s how the website is spelled in-game), you can only make and publish gameplay videos. I would have started with reviews directly but the virtual powers that be, decided that you must start your promising career as an infamous gamer, by screaming in your microphone while you\u2019re recording yourself. If some Swedish dude got away with it, surely this must be the formula for universal success!<br><br>I\u2019m almost surprised you don\u2019t kickstart your path to internet stardom, by living in your parents\u2019 basement, but that might have been one stereotype too many. No father figure is present but at least there\u2019s a mother to pester you about \u201cminor\u201d things such as studying or getting a job. It would have felt really meta, if I wasn\u2019t already \u201cenjoying\u201d adult life at its fullest. But as any good story, it is best for the exposition to contain a rags to riches element. Nevermind that most YT celebs in real life, are never average joes recording themselves from their trailer-park homes. We\u2019ll play along the idea that you can start small and once you dream big enough, it will come true. George Carlin summarizes it perfectly: \u201cIt\u2019s called the American Dream, because you have to be asleep to believe it.\u201d<br><br>So we shall dream big and earn in-game a lot of cash. Apparently enough to afford yacht parties after 100 uploaded videos on \u201cU-Tube\u201d. The game does have an air of nostalgia to it. Why? Because I stopped playing The Sims around its second installment, so I never bothered with how they were milking the third and fourth one. But I do miss the house building and design aspects that have made the Sims franchise famous in its own right. Youtubers Life tries to emulate some of those elements, through the implementation of upgrades that you can \u201cbuy online\u201d in-game on some mock-up sites with names similar to their real life counterparts. So the sense of progression is firmly embedded, not just in skills (you can call it experience gain too) but also new objects and hardware that increase your popularity yield and allow players to personalize the living spaces as well, not just the characters themselves.<br><br>How well you manage to compete on U-Tube, is not quite as random as reality can be. As long as you put sufficient effort in the post production part before uploading the videos, you will soon find yourself with a steady supply of virtual cash. As far as I\u2019ve noticed, ultimate success for the gamers\/streamers in Youtubers Life, seems to be the attention you receive at Gaming Conventions. Talent Shows are the pinnacle of success for aspiring musicians and last but not least, Cooking Shows for those that start out by filming themselves as they burn the food while adjusting the focus and zoom level on their streaming cameras. A lot to experience for a game, I agree. The price tag might be ultimately justified, given the attention to detail. I won\u2019t mark it as a weak point, that\u2019s for certain. The hybrid mechanics which feature the economic simulation aspects are balanced enough so you cannot claim the game is punishingly difficult. The start is the only rocky part of your journey to online fame. At least until they add the IRS in a future DLC or something. Better not, U-Play Online. Just focus on making more games such as Youtubers Life.
