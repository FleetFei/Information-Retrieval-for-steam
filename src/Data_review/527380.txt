When you use to hang out at the old corner shop, either before or after school, and starve yourself through the lunch breaks. Just so you could blow your tuckshop money on the old coin op consoles or Pinball machines. The days when 20 cents would give you anywhere up to two hours (YES a whole two hours, of pure unadulterated pixelated fun), before you needed to pop in another coin. <br><br>The pinballs were on the decline and the new shiny consoles were making the grade, Space Invaders, Pacman, Galaga and Defender. Yep, in those days you truly would be the legend in your own lunch time. <br><br>It is the last one, Defender, that Captain Kaon mostly reminds me of. A side scrolling space tunnel shooter (well, that what I use to call them). You could plod through the enemy, tactically eradicating  them slowly, or for fits of giggles, go full warp shooting like a wide man screaming \u2018DIE MUTHU FRACKER!!\u2019 till you either went splat against a wall or and enemy ship managed to pop you off.<br><br>Captain Kaon is no different. You, a rookie fresh out of the academy, is assigned to the Argus, a recommissioned battle cruiser, which is part of a slapped together defence force for earth.  The cool kids, with gnarly ships get to play lazer tag with the enemy alien force, in some far part of the galaxy. While you stay behind and babysit a Miners revolt on Ceres.<br><br>You start off with the WASP, the latest technically advanced gunship, from 30 years ago, and blast any invaders who happen to pop up the their badass ugly heads. <br><br>Your first two missions are training levels to get use to the ship. The frustrating part is you will forget which direction your nose is facing and can end up loosing half you life trying to stop ploughing the walls or getting unstuck from corners.<br><br>Fire control is done via the mouse and your gun rotates 360 (Missiles and bombs too).  On one hand this is great, you can hover in place while you pick the enemy off in any direction. The down side is while you are flying, you have your attention divided. You are either ploughing into walls while you are concentrating on shooting, or wasting ammo shooting like a Storm Trooper while concentrating on your flying (I am a man, I can only concentrate on one thing at a time! Shoot enemy or avoid wall or pee pants, not all at once!). <br><br>The tunnels are in maze form so there is no hitting the throttle and blasting through the levels (sad face), and you need to open doors or hidden areas by either picking up power cells and dropping them off at the required slot, passing through lazer trip wires or grappling levers to pull on or off. The grapple hook is frustrating, there seems to be no \u2018let go\u2019 button. So you need to pull away from some levers, which in turn makes them bounce back to their off position (and plough you face first into a wall). When you do get skilled on your controls though, you can start to hoon around the area in a fashion that would make Ken Brock (or any Drift star) proud.  <br><br>You can refill on ammo and health at any time by returning to the Argus or finding refill stations, if there are any around, but there may be restrictions on hight levels (I suck, so still on level three but having fun dying).<br><br>If your are after latest 3D render and shadow effects then walk away now, you are too cool to hang with us Eggheads. It is all 80\u2019s style pixel art, giving this game a homely feel. <br><br>The music is also 80\u2019s fantabulous. At the right ambient level so as not to be annoying to make you turn it off and fits this game like a glove.<br><br>Story is lite and typical of 80\u2019s games so no in depth love triangle or buddy revenge. There is a little confusion provide by the summery on Steam and in the actual game. On steam it states that you are Captain Kaon, a woman who was facing Court Martial, when suddenly the war started. In the game you are a Captain Kaon a female rookie from the academy. If this affects you mindset during play, well boohoo princess (your character is a girl after all). Me, I\u2019m a D.I.L.L.I.G.A.F. kind of guy, plus, in the 80\u2019s you don\u2019t need no reason to blow away green alien butt! <br><br>There are still more features to be implemented by the
