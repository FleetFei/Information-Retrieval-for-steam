The object of the game is to deliver upon the goals that are set at the beginning of each level. Although it appears to be designed simply the amount of strategy and understanding of each of the building types is significant.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay<\/div><br>Gameplay consists of a playing grid-like playing board. Each level has its own grid. You can place one building in each vector. Each building type has a price in terms of monetary and resources required to build. Each round gives you a deadline to meet the requirements of each level. For example, the tutorial expects you to reach a population of 1000 with 5 happiness, 5 prosperity and 5 culture. Each building type has a different value. Failure occurs when either happiness, prosperity or culture hit rock bottom.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">The Value Proposition<\/div><br>There are more better developed games on Steam that attempt to do the same thing. However, at the price point you're looking at a rock solid title gameplay wise. Therefore, when you factor in the value proposition - cost when compared to entertainment - the game is fairly priced. This is something that I would utilize in order to create a sense of competitiveness among the players. It'd be nice to be able to compare time played and in game days required to achieve the goals of each level. As a result I am disappointed the game is lacking in utilizing Steam to the best of its potential.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Verdict<\/div><br>Although I am disappointed in the absentee developer\/publisher and the lack of Steamification if you are a fan of the genre the you can't ask for much more at this price point.
