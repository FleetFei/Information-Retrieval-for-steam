The aesthetic. <br>The idea of rats waging medieval war on each other via tunnel system is amazing. I love everything about that.<br><br>2. The art. <br>It's not a materpiece, but the spites look good and it looks like a lot of work went into them.<br><br>3. The music. <br>I didn't listen intently, but there are 7 tracks. I don't have strong opinions about music, but they are functional. They seemed to fit the theme quite well and I thought they sounded good.<br><br>4. The price.<br>At this point the game is early access. It certainly isn't complete but the bones are there and assuming the game gets finished, $5 is a steal.<br><br><br><br>What could use some improvement:<br><br>1. The UI.<br>The game has a very Dwarf Fortress feel to it, especially in that commands are given via menu for the most part, but it also shares a similar learning curve. When you hover over an option, a description is shown in the top dialogue box, however that description isn't always clear and you're left not knowing what a lot of buttons do. Building workshops is easy enough, but whether or not they cost anything isn't clear. Resources seem to be spent or created at regular intervals, however there is no timer or window to see where your resources are going or coming from. I was out of food at one point, so I began producing it as fast as possible to no avail. I figured out that I needed additional storage rooms, but nothing indicated that to me.<br><br>2. The combat.<br>Building hordes took some trial and error. There is a helpful menu for creating unit types but I didn't see why not to max the stats of every unit besides a bit of a cost increase. Resources seem unlimited provided you wait for them. <br>There is also mention of &quot;unit type&quot; which isn't explained. I think it's for creating your own templates, but they have pre-assigned images which makes me think their type implies something about them. Weapon and armor choices don't feel meaningful, but I might attribute that to my lack of experience. <br>Moving hordes didn't seem to actually work. The only options that made them move were &quot;attack the enemy king&quot; and &quot;go home&quot;. I have since figured out how to move hordes.<br><br><br><br>Things I liked but could also use improvement:<br><br>1. Trading.<br>I like the idea of trading and it really helped me out a few times, but I think it would be better if I knew who I was trading with and if I couldn't trade whenever I wanted to. As it is now, you can trade any resource for any other resource if you wait for the trader's inventory to change every couple of minutes, essentially giving you 4 kinds of currency. I would like it more if either coins were the only currency and you had to buy or sell from the trader or if there was a bartering system where you made an offer and the trader made a counter offer.<br><br>2. Color palettes.<br>I love that there are pre-made color palettes and they look really good. There are several color palettes that are blank white. I didn't look at it too long, but I think those are for making your own. It might be better if player-made palettes are listed seperately from the rest in the interest of removing those blank ones.<br><br><br><br>Potential bugs:<br><br>1. Digging\/Building.<br>Apparently there are 3-ish undefined &quot;lanes&quot; in which you can build structures. I don't know if that was intended, but I think it would be better if you could build wherever you wanted.<br><br>2. Unit action.<br>It might be a bug or user error, but sometimes seemingly randomly, one of my miners would just walk over to the enemy base and get himself killed. I couldn't figure out if there was a way to tell him to stop. He would even dig through walls to get there if he had to.<br><br>3. Glitched walls.<br>This only ever happened with a single wall, but I could not tell my miners to get rid of it. I just had to build around it.<br><br>4. Horde movement.<br>Sometimes units get temporarily stuck on each other in tight areas and take a while to move.<br><br><br><br>Summary:<br><br>Overall I think this game has amazing potential. I wish it didn't have as steep of a learning curve as it does. I think it would benefit greatly from a soft tutorial. Maybe introduce buildings over time as they become necessary rather than having everything available from the start. The game is listed as strategy, which seemed absent. I just built some hordes and sent them in to claim victory with very little resistance and was never attacked while I built up my own base. I was very much hoping to explore the pre-existing caves to find neutral monsters and loot that would give me an advantage over the enemy. I wish there was a way to achieve smaller victories without completely steamrolling the enemy in a single battle. I look forward to playing more of this game with more updates and hope that the community gets behind this one because it could be a real gem if we give the devs enough support.<br><br>Edit: After playing a few more skirmishes, I realized what happened to me the first time was not common.
