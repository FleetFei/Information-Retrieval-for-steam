The only mechanic that I wish would have been implemented is an &quot;undo&quot; feature, since I constantly made mistakes yet had to go to the menu and restart the entire level, redoing things I had already done in the process. game that stretches your mind and to think outside the box in order to succeed throughout each of the levels that you are put up against. There is some really cool and uniquely styled mechanics that you will find as you play through more of the game.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay<\/div>This is a relaxing puzzle game that has some rather interesting mechanics that are implemented throughout the course of the game. You play as a wizard, of all people, you are put in a tower and you have to escape, along with a friend that will help you understand the mechanics that are being put into place through the first few levels. Obviously, like most puzzle games, the first few levels are simplistic and easy to follow, just so that you can grasp the concept of the mechanics that they are throwing at you. After the first few levels you are then left on your own until a new mechanic is put into place. The game has steady difficulty curve (as long as you are playing the levels in order) and uses quite a lot of mechanics, so getting your head round what each of them do is essential to playing this game.<br><br>But what do you actually have to do in these levels to complete them? Well it is rather simple. There are 5 items that are needed in order to open the escape door to exit the level. Your job is to collect these items and get to the exit. There is no time limit, nor are you being timed to gain 'extra stars,' this just makes the game more relaxing and not rushing you into completing the levels.<br><br>There are a enormous amount of levels that you can play throughout the game. You start off in the castle, and as you complete more levels you will unlock new places with a different setting and a few new mechanics to make the puzzles that bit more difficult. Something that the game does really well is that the game won't force you to stay on a single level and try to figure it out. You can do the levels in any order that you wish to achieve them in and therefore won't be stuck on one specific level and lose interest because of that.<br><br>The game doesn't seem to be repetitive or just remakes of older levels. Each level seems to have had a fresh and new thought process instead of just remakes of previous levels. This really does make the levels that you play that more interesting and gives the game more play ability as you get into more and more levels of the game. Although something that I did find a bit annoying was there is not hotkey for a instant restart of the level, which is a bit of a shame for a puzzle game like this that has a lot of depth and gameplay in it.<br><br>A interesting new concept that I hasn't seen done before was boss battles in puzzle games. Now normally puzzle games don't venture out this far, and even if they do the bosses are only small bosses that aren't really necessary to their game just seems like they took them from nowhere and added them to the game for no reason. The bosses in this game however are put here for a reason. In order to unlock each of the bosses on each of the areas you need to complete a certain amount of levels in order to unlock it. Defeating the boss is a lot harder than the plain simple levels before that boss room. I won't spoil any more of what the boss room contains just know that the boss room is awesome and well worth unlocking.<br><br>The graphics of this game are simplistic which adds more to the innocent looking side of this game, even though this game can get pretty hard in the later stages. Some of the characters that you meet don't have any animations, they are just still and you can talk to them. I would of liked to see maybe them looking at you at a certain distance just to add a bit of life to the characters. As for the player and enemies, their animations are smooth and presented well. Although sometimes I feel like your character tht you play as hasn't got as much of a walking animation as I would've liked to see.
