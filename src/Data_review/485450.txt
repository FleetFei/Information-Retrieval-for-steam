a casual and short 2D stealth game about a ninja who sneaks his way to save the world.<\/b><br><br>I recommend this but only barely, that is, if you can find it on sale (<i>Disclaimer: I got it from a bundle.<\/i>). It's too short for its full price.<br><br><i>Ninja Stealth<\/i> is a rather casual stealth game about a ninja breaking into a high-security facility and stealing the chemical weapon that the categorically evil antagonist has been developing in his evil facility.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Are you even ninja?<\/div><br>Your mission in each room is to find the key and get to the next room without being detected. If detected, you start at the last checkpoint, i.e. at the door or the key you found.<br><br>Your adversaries include moving lasers, cameras, guards, noise detectors and on-and-off switching lasers. Boxes and walls logically block off their line-of-sight. You can also push boxes and hide inside some of them, probably as a tribute to <i>Metal Gear Solid<\/i>.<br><br>Since <i>Ninja Stealth<\/i> is a top-down 2D game, there is no hiding in the ceiling, climbing, jumping or even crouching. The so-called ninja cannot even attack the guards. Detection means instant death. All this makes one wonder whether the protagonist is really a ninja at all despite their looks.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Do you know stealth?<\/div><br>Most adversaries are quite predictable: lasers rotate at constant pace, noise detectors (that only detect you if you move) go on and off at constant intervals etc. The basic mechanic is simple and nice: avoid everything that changes or moves, get the key and leave for the next room. However...<br><br>The moving lasers sometimes have hit box issues, quite literally. The boxes you push may block the laser but sometimes the laser just finds a way through the boxes if there's a tiniest crack and hits you extremely briefly. Of course that counts as a hit.<br><br>Noise detectors work more like motion detectors: when they turn on, you may not move in their area lest be detected. Being a ninja, aren't you supposed to be stealthy (i.e. be <i>silent<\/i> in addition to staying out of sight)?<br><br>The guards behave in a weird fashion. While they follow set routes, when changing direction they just <i>snap<\/i> into the new direction instantaneously. This can be unnerving and surprise you in an unexpected way and it feels unfair.<br><br>Actually the only feature that I would count as stealth is that some guards are sleeping. Bumping a crate or causing other noise like that will wake them up. However, they won't change their routes based on shuffling crates or similar, so that's kind of a bummer.<br><br>There are no stun grenades, poisoned shurikens or similar ninja'esque gadgets to play with. Everything being deterministic, it's not much of a stealth game but more one of smart movement and tactics.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Evil facilities<\/div><br>The gameplay starts extremely easy to the point of being boring, and it slowly works up to moderately challenging in the very last few normal levels. I would have liked the challenge get up sooner. Only the last level and the bonus levels (that you unlock by beating the game) were truly challenging, with multiple fast moving lasers and guards and noise detectors in awkward (i.e. challenging) places.<br><br>A set of Halloween challenge levels, only playable around Halloween, were released this October. These ranged from moderately to very challenging and the level design was nicer than in basic levels.<br><br>They also introduce a bonus enemy type, a ghost that seemingly randomly bounces around the level. The apparently random behaviour was a cause of many unnecessary deaths so I found them mostly annoying or frustrating.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Technical side<\/div><br><i>Ninja Stealth<\/i> is presented as a casual stealth game. Thus I find it a good thing that the developer altogether removed the limited lives that were present in the main game.<br><br>Also, the new keyboard\/gamepad controls are better and more responsive than the mouse controls that were removed. While it has been released from Early Access, there are still content updates planned.<br><br>Menus are very basic: you can only change volume of music and sounds (separately and together), and Unity launcher handles the resolution and controls. Default controls (on keyboard) are WASD for movement and E for action. Action is only used for getting into or out of a box.<br><br>Graphics are clear, and thus good for this game. Music is pulp style with just two or three tracks (check the trailers for sample).<br><br>The DLC for Episode 2 was just released (my review of that is <a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/id\/Twistorian\/recommended\/552950\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\" >here<\/a>), and having played it I find it adds about half an hour of playtime of moderately challenging levels. It does not add any new achievements, so you are &quot;safe&quot; buying just the base game.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Conclusions<\/div><br>It's a really short game. The Episode 1 lasted 25 minutes for me. Add 10-15 minutes for the six bonus levels. Add ten or twenty minutes for the Halloween event. Add half an hour for the DLC if you get it (+idling for cards). It's not terribly lot. <br><br>Nevertheless, I did enjoy it and the bunch of easy achievements that came with it. I'd say get it on sale though - in my opinion, the main game content is currently not enough to warrant the full price.
