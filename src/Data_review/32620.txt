Years later, the two leading warfare game brands, Battlefield and Call of Duty, would use many elements the Delta Force franchise came up with in its first installment.<br><br>Delta Force pioneered in multiple departments. First of all, one must remember that 3D shooters were pretty new. Delta Force not only had good graphics upon release, it offered extremely large areas to fight on when compared to its rivals. The missions in this game are very entertaining. Back in the day, FPS games were pretty generic in terms of design. Delta Force offers missions all around the world, so you get desert, snow and jungle maps, all with various types of buildings and land configurations. You almost immediately get access to those different places right away, so when you get tired of the jungle, you can always switch to some other place with a different scenery. As you complete your missions, you unlock others too. The objectives that you need to accomplish aren't always the same, which is very good.<br><br>As I said, graphics weren't bad at all back in the late 90's. The sky looks pretty good, the ground has some sort of grass thing going on ( which was pretty new back then ) and the characters are pretty detailed. The guns and other weapons could've had better animations, but overall it's more than tolerable. Sound effects aren't bad either, but you kinda wish there would be a little more, considering the fact that there will seldom be a second or two of complete silence even in the middle of a firefight.<br><br>Since the Delta Force is a unit, you have some AI soldiers to help you on your missions. Unfortunately, the AI in this game is quite limited, so they will more than often just go to their scripted waypoint and shoot at the enemies when the time comes. They also offer some radio support, giving you the position of specific enemies. When things get rough, don't expect them to do much. If you are lucky, they'll kill one or two bad guys. They are decoys at best.<br><br>I guess that the weakest thing about the game is its AI. It was already pretty bad when the game came out. The enemies follow a pre-designed path and once they have reached their ultimate destination, they'll just stand at this position. This will sometimes lead you to search all over the place, trying to find the last guy to kill. Luckily, you'll hear him yelling or shooting during your search. If you do the mission a second time, you'll understand that the enemies always do the exact same thing. This hurts replay value a lot. The other bad thing is that, obviously, this game has aged. Unless you are the kind of gamer who isn't repelled by old FPS games, I think Delta Force will mostly appeal to older people in search of cheap nostalgia.<br><br>Overall, I recommend this game for senior gamers, but also for younger gamers in search of a cheap FPS which paved the way for many others. I think that when you buy this game, you also buy a little part of video game history, in a way. It's the best game of the franchise, in my opinion, since it offered multiple groundbreaking elements.
