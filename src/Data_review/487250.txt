class=\"postedDate\">Posted: September 28, 2016\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t<div class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNOTE: This game was given to me by the developer for review purposes. This did not influence my review in any way.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">==========================================================<br>\n---------------------------------------OVERVIEW-------------------------------------<br>\n==========================================================<\/div>Note: Spaera is currently in Early Access. The version reviewed was current as of the date of this review.<br><br>Spaera is a competitive match-three game with a host of anime-themed characters.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">==========================================================<br>\n----------------------------------------QUALITY-------------------------------------<br>\n==========================================================<\/div>Graphics - Graphics are mixed. Anime-style character portraits are generally nicely done, and the magic ability animations are mostly very good. The level backgrounds are also quite good, but they're completely static images. The match-three elements are uninspired, but functional, although in the heat of block-falling action I do sometimes mistake different coloured orbs.<br><br>The interface graphics are pretty abysmal at the moment, but the game has only recently moved engines (it now runs on Unity), so I'm assuming the current options menu buttons and the like are only placeholders.<br><br>Speaking of options, there are none, for graphics anyway. The options menu gives you the ability to adjust three types of sound volume and change your controls.<br><br>Sound - In-game music reminds me of background music for an action scene in an anime, or a JRPG battle. There seem to be at least a few different tracks, but most are a bit frantic.<br><br>Sound effects are functional, if nothing special. I can't help but think some funky cartoon-style voiceovers would fit well when using magic abilities, and maybe at the start and end of each battle.<br><br>Gameplay - Spaera is a slightly confusing head-to-head match-three game. In each game you choose a character from the available pool (six out of eight available to play when you start) and play one or more head-to-head match-three battles against an opponent.<br><br>The battle screen is split into two sides, one for each character. Each side has a portrait, a list of magical abilities, and a Tetris-style game board. There's a small area in the middle showing the next four blocks to appear, as well as a timer. Both game boards start off with an identical pattern of blocks, and both receive the same blocks as the game progresses, but within no time at all they'll look completely different based on the moves made by you and your opponent.<br><br>The match-three part of the game was a little counterintuitive to begin with. With no tutorial, I started off trying to match whole blocks, and just ended up with a lot of full lines across my board. No, in Spaera, each larger block that falls is made up of a pattern of smaller coloured blocks, and your goal is to match three or more of one colour. Blocks may also contain little glowing orbs in place of one or more base colours. I think these are matched to increase your magic level, allowing you to activate one of your four magic abilities, but again, with no tutorial it's not entirely clear whether the different colours do anything special. Some blocks also have little designs on them, which aren't explained either.<br><br>The magic abilities themselves provide the unique aspect to Spaera's gameplay. Each character has four abilities, descriptions of which seem to only be available from the character selection screen. This is big oversight; it would be a very useful addition (in offline modes, at least) to be able to press a button to display the game effects of each character's abilities. There's so much screen real estate available, it could even be a permanent display at the bottom of each character section. Anyway, these have a good variety, ranging from removing blocks from your board or adding a line to your opponent's board, to confusing your opponent's controls or copying whole boards. It's just a shame you'll forget them a lot when you first start out.<br><br>Spaera offers three game modes: Online, Story, and Versus. Online and Versus modes simply pit you against an online or CPU opponent, respectively, in a single battle. There's no story yet in Story Mode, but I can only assume that one is coming, given the name, but in this mode you choose a character -- no background information yet provided -- and enter into a series of battles with each of the other characters in order. I haven't yet managed to complete this mode, but given that a couple of starting characters are not selectable yet, I'm guessing they're unlocked through completion of the story.<br><br>I'm not sure what, if any, progression Spaera will offer. There appear to be only two characters to unlock, but the main menu displays three different progress measures for your profile: Ladder Pts, Online XP, and Offline XP. If these are used to purchase upgrades, or new characters or abilities, or some other sort of progression, then I can see that replayability will be increased significantly. Without this, I suspect the only enduring goal will be bragging rights, and trying to reach the top of the leaderboard.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">==========================================================<br>\n-------------------------------------PROS &amp; CONS------------------------------------<br>\n==========================================================<\/div>Pros:<br>+ It's a fun take on a match-three game<br>+ Seems to be pretty solid net code<br>+ A few different game options<br>+ Nice character graphics<br><br>Cons:<br>- No tutorial to explain the unique aspects of the game<br>- No way to access magic ability descriptions from anywhere other than the character selection screen<br>- No way to change your online name (Spaera uses your Steam name)<br>- No story in Story Mode<br>- It's not clear what the various points (Ladder Pts and XP) actually do<br>- It can hard to find an online opponent<br>- Needs a lot more polish<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">==========================================================<br>\n--------------------------------------CONCLUSION-----------------------------------<br>\n==========================================================<\/div>I often find reviewing an Early Access game tricky, trying to balance what's here at the moment with what is likely to be there in the future. In Spaera's case, there's a reasonably solid match-three game here, with some potential for progression and longevity. Is it a great game now? No. But the core is fun and with a bit more polish and work put in around the edges it could be.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">6\/10<\/div><br><div class=\"bb_code\">For more recommendations by LL's
