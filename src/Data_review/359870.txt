first time ever in my life, I can safely write a review about these games and give my thoughts about them. But since this includes 2 games, I believe I need to review them separately so here goes!<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">FINAL FANTASY X<\/div><br>FINAL FANTASY X was the first game I played and I suppose it should be everyone's first game to play when one buys this.<br><br>The game is a turn-based JRPG where enemies and your characters take turns of action. I feel this system is a very good one and it's ultimately sad that there aren't so many games with this system nowadays. You can have up to 3 characters on the field, but can call out any reserves you have and replace one of the field characters at any time you wish as long as it's your turn. Another thing that's very good about this system is that there is no rush in deciding what to do on your characters' turns. You could just never insert any commands and nothing would happen. Of course there are other elements to the combat, such as Overdrives and Summons but I feel they are best experienced individually.<br><br>Leveling up in this game is different from most other &quot;standard&quot; JRPG games since when you level up you usually gain instantaneous stat gains. In this game, however, you don't just gain levels. You gain <i>Sphere levels<\/i>. You use these sphere levels to move around in this massive menu called the <i>Sphere Grid<\/i>. Moving around in this sphere grid requires sphere levels and to move one step you require one sphere level. The point of moving in this sphere grid is to find nodes with stat increases on them. You then use spheres to unlock these nodes and get the stat increase. There are multiple different spheres which essentially unlock specific nodes. The good thing about this whole sphere grid is that you can level up your characters the way you want to. You can make your healer the best physical damage dealer, and likewise. Or just make a hybrid. Whichever suits you best.<br><br>The story and characters in it are some of the best I have ever had the chance to experience. The characters have actual development and all of the characters feel like individuals. Many get turned off because of the game's main character for some reason.<span class=\"bb_spoiler\"><span>I guess some people just can't take anything lighthearted.<\/span><\/span>The story itself is a very enjoyable part of this game. It has so many plot twists and it surely keeps you on your toes all the time. I for one was waiting in anticipation what would happen next. Then again, I haven't played any other FINAL FANTASY games (other than FINAL FANTASY VIII) so I wouldn't know what else they have to offer.<br><br>Then there's the music, which fits all the moods and environments perfectly. When something dramatic is going on, the music will make sure that you feel it as well. I cannot stop listening to the music after beating the game, it's so good.<br><br>FINAL FANTASY X alone is worth the price.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">FINAL FANTASY X-2<\/div><br>After experiencing the greatness of FINAL FANTASY X I couldn't even imagine how good the sequel would be. I'm afraid I was disappointed horribly.<br><br>I have read what other people had to say about this game. So many people claim the story and characters to be trash and the only redeeming factor for this game to be it's combat system. I guess different strokes for different people, but here's what I think:<br><br>At the end of the day, the story was the only thing in this game that kept me going. It was the only thing that made me force myself to finish and see how the game ends. So what was so bad that made me want to stop half way through?<br><br>The characters in the game were - if not enjoyable - atleast bearable. The real problem hides in the battle system. Now don't get me wrong, I have nothing against ATB (Active time battle system) based battle systems. Quite the opposite actually. I have played FINAL FANTASY VIII in addition to these games in the FINAL FANTASY series. That game had ATB system as well, but that was well made. This game's system has atleast 3 major problems:<br><br>1. When your characters' ATB bar fills up and you decide to attack with a character, the character may MISS. I don't mean that the enemy evades your attacks. I mean that your character does the attack animation, but doesn't actually hit the enemy. After the animation is done your character tries to attack again. <span class=\"bb_spoiler\"><span>It may do it again, which is incredibly infuriating<\/span><\/span><br><br>2. Everything in this game has some form of a wait time, aside from the basic attack (or the equivalent) command. Want to heal your character quick before the enemy gets its turn? Too bad, you have to wait for a while so your character can pick the item from her \u2665\u2665\u2665 first. Want to cast a quick Thundara spell? Too bad, you have to wait for a while so your character can stand still for a while first. This makes especially healing a bloody nightmare. <br><br>3. This problem is related to the first problem. Whenever your character goes out to attack, the game won't stop. It will go on just like it normally would. The problem is, your character is attacking, while your other characters get to fill up their ATB bar and so does the enemy. The frustration grows when your character misses the first 3 times and then the enemy gets to KO your character BECAUSE IT CAN'T BLOODY HIT THE \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665. Meanwhile your other characters are already ready for action.<br><br>Then there is the &quot;Job&quot; system or Dress spheres how they like to call it. Basically all your characters can specialize in different classes for example Black Mage, Warrior, Thief etc. each plays differently, but there are way too many of them. At first I was very into the idea of different playstyles, but then it just became too much effort. I ended up sticking with 1 class for each character, because I just wouldn't want to spend the rest of the game tinkering with the dress spheres.<br><br>I already said before that the story was the only thing that kept me playing didn't I? Well even for that you need to go out of your way to get the story bits in the game, because they're hidden IN SIDE QUESTS!! Can you even believe that? When has a game hidden important story elements behind side quests? Of course you don't need to complete these to beat the game, but you'll be missing out on A LOT of the story. You won't get even 50% of the story completed if you complete none of the side quests.<br><br>This game has also a lot of minigames, most notable of them being spherebreak and blitzball. The other minigames I just couldn't be bothered with. Spherebreak is a bit too complicated to explain, therefore I'll just explain blitzball. It's a mess of stats and an adrenaline meter. You don't even get to control the players yourself. You get to watch as the AI just stupidly throws the ball all over the place. The adrenaline determines wether the players perform 4 times better at the cost of losing morale and gaining fatigue. No fun. Spherebreak I could at least see some people finding it interesting, but I couldn't be bothered to win even 1 game.<br><br>I wouldn't recommend buying this just for FINAL FANTASY X-2. I believe it alone isn't worth the price, but considering you get FINAL FANTASY X which is an extremely exquisite game you might as well try this if you dare.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">After thoughts<\/div><br>Well that was a long review wasn't it? I highly recommend buying this, because it's just something you need to experience before your life on this planet ends.
