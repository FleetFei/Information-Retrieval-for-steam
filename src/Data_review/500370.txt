Sorry for the inconvience!<br><br>This is the DLC that really completes 2205 for me. If you didn't like the game before Orbit, you still won't like it after. If you enjoyed 2205 before Orbit, even if you didn't play a ton, I would reccomend this to you.<br><br>Orbit adds a new &quot;sector&quot; to the game, the orbital station. Accessing the station requires no credits or a certain corperation level. Instead, you need to aquire a new type of scientist from the three base sectors, trained from your population at a new (large) building. Aquiring all three scientist types requires all three types of sectors. This building reuires only operators to build, but be prepared to invest a large amount of credits.<br><br>Once you have access to the station, the real meat of the dlc begins. Orbit adds a technology tree with branches in each industry area. By adding modules to your station and shipping certain items up, you can research these technologies. Each technology adds a large effect to your corperation, including options such as increased yields, decreased requirements, mines producting multiple types of good, and items requiring different componets to produce. At the end of the tech trees are items such as android workers and orbital warehouses. There are 75 technologies to research in total, with 11 for each industry and 20 technologies to upgrade your station.<br><br>Building the station is also part of the challenge. Functioning on a grid, the space station is built out of supports and modules. Modules are mounted on the supports to provide effects (see the screenshots, it makes more sense these). Similar to how service builds operate on the surface, modules on the space station have an effect that decreases over distance. However, the effect of modules is also reduced if the effect passes through another module. Designing your station becomes increasingly difficult as you try to route all the different modules to your research labs.<br><br>I did have a few problems twith the dlc. Orbit does a poor job explaining how all of its systems work, with can be confusing at best and crippling at worst when your station overheats. Also, Orbit does not play well with saved games, the new buildings to create scientists are very large and must be place in the middle of population centers, which could cripple large cities. Finally, the new UI elements can be somewhat confusing until you really sit down and figure out how it works.<br><br>Overall, I give Orbit both thumbs up. I enjoyed Anno 2205 when it first came out, despite the departures from the series history, and Orbit only improves the experience. As to the price, $11.99 seems fair to me for the amount of new content Orbit adds.
