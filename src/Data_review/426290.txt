masterpiece. <br><br>The Away Team is as self described &quot;interactive sci-fi adventure novel&quot;. If you're unsure what that means, it reminds me of a traditional\/older visual novel I've played long time ago, Tsukihime which focused more on text driven narrative than visual and auditory aids. The American equivalent would be Goosebump novels I guess. I wouldn't really call it a text-based adventure <i>game<\/i> as in the same category of say.. Zork. During the course of the story telling, there are actions provided to you that may alter the story... and in some cases harm your crew.<br><br>Some love\/some hate the soundtrack. Honestly, I didn't enjoy the music, not within the game at least, it started off with a good vibe, music well suited to the narrative, but proceeded to provide a jarring hindrance to my immersion. I quickly turned off the music within an hour. Don't get me wrong, I think the soundtrack is fantastic but several times the change in track playing would be suddenly upbeat and lively when the text describing the atmosphere suggests otherwise. Having sampled the music outside of the game, they are excellent tracks: &quot;Astrum Cloud&quot; had hints of FTL while &quot;It's Out There&quot; reminded me of the latest Deus Ex and Tron. &quot;Somewhere Far&quot; being my favorite of the list provided a subtle mysterious yet seductive vibe.<br><br>The mechanism for delivery is excellent. I enjoyed the graphics and the clean nature of the interface. Keeping things simple yet elegant. However, the clarity of the text quickly became apparent--the font had to go.  At its intended resolution was fine but <b>legibility<\/b> at my native resolution of 2560x1440 was somewhat difficult to read with soft edges, some pixel type-face don't scale well. Thankfully a wonderful thread in Discussions shows how to replace the font, with a pretty good alternative selection too!<br><br>Fantastic story-writing. At first, all I could think of was, &quot;Oh great, prepare for wall of text&quot; but it quickly evolved from plain mindless tales of planet hopping to, &quot;Ooooh fu- damn you have my attention I wonder how this will play out&quot;. The story itself isn't long, it took me approximately four hours to get my first ending and of course it's based on the route(s) you take and how quickly you can read. There seems to be four endings according to the Steam Achievements. I've hit two so far but know that I could have gotten another earlier on if I chose different actions. Did I mention there are a slew of crew members each with different traits? These traits may alter how each event plays out on away missions. Time to start with different crew members!<br><br>However, the experience did not go without a few minor hiccups. In one instance selecting an dialogue option broke the game, no further dialogue nor could I progress further; needless to say I reloaded and chose another option. One time, I had a dead crew member still listed after an away mission.. tried using him on my next mission but proved to be dead weight (haha), pun intended, and was removed afterwards. Like I said, minor stuff.<br><br>I speak with a heavy heart, wishing The Away Team was a bit longer of an adventure; it felt like it had just started in a way that I became attached to my character. Sort of like watching a story arc from any of the Star Trek series and if it were to quickly conclude as it started. Ultimately, I still thoroughly enjoyed the story and it's delivery, I hope you give it a chance too!<br><br>Edit: v1.1 fixes the font so what I said about the font has been dealt with. Also wanted to add on the topic of replayability. The game isn't long as I've said before, I've replayed the game over half a dozen times in rapid succession to test various choices and various crew members; some have more dialogue. I ran into some bugs where one of the ending planets showed up as first or second planet when starting a new game. If you wanted all four endings it will take about 7 hours; shorter if you save scum.
