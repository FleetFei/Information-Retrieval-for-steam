class=\"postedDate\">Posted: March 28\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t<div class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShort: Guardians of Orion (previously The Orion Project) is a Top Down\/First Person\/Third Person arena shooter with a lot of customizability. A mishmash of futuristic tech and gibbable dinosaurs to delight your inner twelve year old for awhile. Though not everything promised has been delivered, the game is pretty fun, providing a good way to waste time, with a relatively positive and friendly community, and helpful &amp; responsive customer service.<br><br>Long:<br>Guardians of Orion starts off as a top down game, but by hitting F1 (rebindable) you can switch to First Person or Third Person as well. The FOV is adjustable in the settings, as are a surprising amount of other things. I am sad to see certain settings that were to be implemented still not in-game (such as the crouch\/blink toggle), but the FOV is a nice touch.<br><br>Character customization is really nice. There's a lot of cosmetic options, allowing you to mix and match various Guardians' armor, as well as other purchasables from the shop. Most options are strictly that - cosmetic. The more useful customization options come from leveling up the Guardians. As you rank them up, you unlock their unique grenades and primary weapons to use on other Guardians. The biggest let down of customization is the skill trees right now - I don't think they've been updated in awhile, as some still reference old grenades (i.e. Smoke on Support). Moreover, almost all the skills are the same, with the only variance being based on the Guardian's unique action skill. Though certain characters are spec'd differently than others and legendary items redefine builds, most of my characters end up following very similar paths.<br><br>The population is up, and I regularly see plenty of games I can join. Even if I host one, leaving it Public almost assures I'll get help by wave 2 or 3. Gameplay seems a bit smoother than I recall, though the net code still seems to have some issues, it's much less laggy than when I last played a few months ago. Though I haven't found many chatterboxes, the conversations I've had have generally been positive, with little to no griefing - a pleasant discovery, given the current lack of a kick system. Most Medium and Hard games can be finished in 20-30 minutes by passably geared players, while Redik can take a team of 4, properly geared, an hour. Redik games are intense and fast paced, and generally the reason I'm still around. Coordination and teamwork isn't strictly necessary, but it speeds things up - games will go closer to 2 hours without well played characters, and defeat will loom overhead constantly.<br><br>The customer service has been great in my experiences. I've only had a couple of problems here and there, but they've been able to address them quickly, usually within 2-3 hours if it's during business hours. The developers also maintain an active presence on the Steam forums, assisting people and asking for advice on where to take the game. The amount of player input they take is extraordinary, and their transparency with their playerbase is refreshing to see. When they can't deliver on a deadline, they will almost invariably explain to the playerbase about what happened and why there is the delay. That's my only real issue with the game in its present state: the undelivered promises. But, there has been significant progress since I quit prior to the 2.0 release, so I have hopes that things will get done as the staff have time. It might not be on the exact timeline they established (which was, if I'm being honest, really optimistic), but I have confidence it'll get done. The staff is rather dedicated.<br><br>There's a lot of people here complaining about the wipe: Yeah, I got wiped too. Had 5 or 6 Guardians at 50 and all of 'em geared to the teeth with 600 gear. The staff said they'd wipe the characters before the official release, several times. They have also since then said that there will be no more wipes, so it's not like the threat of it happening again is there. I'm really not sure why people are still whining and writing negative reviews about it.<br><br>Game's a solid 7\/10, great time waster with a lot of potential to be better in the future. If you're looking for something fun, that doesn't necessitate a long time commitment, this is a great game. shooter, with both PvE and PvP, and 3 toggleable view perspectives (FPS, OtS TPS, and top-down TPS). There's many different classes, called Guardians, which each have unique abilities to help you fight. The main focus of the game is the PvE, where the goal is to protect a harvester from incoming waves of dinosaurs and robots. Guardians of Orion is a one-of-a-kind cooperative horde shooter with dinosaurs and robots in space. Noice \ud83d\udc4c<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"><b> Components<\/b> <\/div><br><b>Visuals<\/b> - This game is absolutely stunning, especially on max settings. Just check out the screenshots on the store page, or my <a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=878098974\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\" >screenshot.<\/a> This game is made in Unreal Engine 4, and doesn't fail to deliver some truly incredible visuals. The particle effects are really awesome: the ion blade (lightsabre) leaves a scorch trail through any object it touches, and sparks fly out everywhere. All other visual effects and post processing effects are top notch, and this is the only game I have ever played where I found the motion blur to actually be good (seriously!). The dynamic lighting is amazing, the textures are great, everything is just plain good. And the best part: animated shaders for your character. This is the only game you can customise your character to have flowing lava as the clothing\/weapon texture. Overall, Guardians of Orion is one of the most visually impressive games I have ever played, and I have nothing but praise for its impeccable graphical fidelity. <br><br><b>Audio<\/b> - Guardians of Orion has very good audio. I love the music; it sounds epic and fits the game very well. I wish there was more though so I hope more music tracks will be added to the game. The sound effects are topnotch so the audio feedback is great. All the sounds, from gunfire and explosions to dinosaur's attacks all sound great and give appropriate feedback. Surprsingly, the voice acting is quite good too. Each guardian has their own voice and they all make comments during battle or during certain moments. Overall, Guardians of Orion has awesome music and sound effects.<br><br><b>Gameplay<\/b> - For the gameplay I will only talk about the PvE aspect, as that is the most fleshed out and enjoyable gamemode for me. The gameplay consists of protecting a harvester (while it mines the invaluable ion crystals) from impending waves of dinosaurs and robots. Each wave gets increasing difficult and there are 3 bonus waves offering a loot chest if beaten under a certain time restraint. Killing the enemies is fun, very fun. With multiplayer co-op, this game gets very intense and very hectic: rockets exploding everywhere, ion blades slashing, dinosaurs engulfing your unlucky teammates, it's awesome. There is a wide variety of guardians to choose from, each with their own unique grenade, guns, voice, ability and a skilltree. My personal favourite is the Pyro. He's armed with an auto shotgun and a flamethrower, and if you spec the skilltree for maximum fire and critical damage, and get the right augment bonuses, he is capable of having the highest DPS in the game. All guardians are equally competent in their own right though, with others more geared towards helping the team. While playing the game, you will get plenty of resource and item drops. Drops are very frequent so there's a lot of looting to be had. This game even features crafting, which becomes vital in the lategame. There's a lot of gameplay elements in this game that add up to make a thoroughly enjoyable and replayable experience. Not to mention the myriad of unique maps, the PvP, the spaceship dogfighting, daily quests, story-related missions are more. Overall, Guardians of Orion is an insanely fun game that offers massive amounts of content.<br><br><b>Story<\/b> - The story is outlined in a <a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"https:\/\/www.youtube.com\/watch?v=VFopklaUqvg\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\" >10 minute short movie<\/a> which is played when you launch the game. It's pretty well made and definitely worth a watch. It gives a perfectly logical explanation for why the possible heck you are shooting dinosaurs in space with rocket launchers and laser guns.<br><br><b>Functionality<\/b> - This game performs very well on my system. The most recent update improved both the visual quality and the performance. Unfortunately though, this game has a lot of small bugs\/issues. These range from redundant skills to broken A.I. (dinos gets stuck sometimes). My personal major gripe is that I have a skill which allows my flamethrower to heal myself while using it, but that doesn't even work half the time, forcing me to swap weapons and back to the flamethrower again to get it working. Also, some ion blade trials don't show the correct colour, which makes me very sad :(<br><br><b>Miscellaneous<\/b> - Some weird button mapping. The reload button is also the revive button, which also the initiate-next-wave button. The blink (teleport) button is also the crouch button. \ud83e\udd14<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"><b>Valuation<\/b><\/div><br><b>Longevity\/replayability<\/b> - Guardians of Orion offers a lot of playability with all the different guardians to max out. Even once you reach the max rank, the max item level exceed it by a multiple of a dozen. PvP and spaceship battles are nice and help alleviate repetition from playing PvE constantly. There's easily a few hundred hours worth of fun in this game. <br><br><b>Modablity<\/b> - This game currently is not modable, but hopefully an SDK released for it, allowing custom maps and Workshop support.<br><br><b>Support<\/b> - This game has received a lot of continual support from the developers. The developers are active on the forums and social media and will answer your questions and concerns.<br><br><b>Is it worth the money?<\/b> Absolutely 100%!
