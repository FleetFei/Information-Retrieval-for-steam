class=\"postedDate\">Posted: September 15, 2014\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t<div class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Masterplan is an action\/strategy game focused on pulling off different heists and set in the 70s. If you enjoyed games like Payday 2 or Monaco you\u2019ll most likely enjoy this one, too, even if they are entirely different games. Still, this game can easily hold its own and has more than enough content for an Early Access version.<br><br>The comparison to aforementioned games isn\u2019t a completely honest comparison because Payday 2 is a first-person shooter focused on a whole lot of shooting (if things go wrong, that is) and Monaco is really more about stealth than tactics. But The Masterplan masterfully combines several of the elements used in these games: first of all, you view the action from a top-down perspective and there are several ways to finish a mission; guns blazing, with careful planning or - if things go wrong - a combination of both. You will also holdup civilians and cops among other things related to heists.<br><br>You can walk freely to wherever you want, as long as it\u2019s not towards a place that raises suspicion. You\u2019ll control several goons, which can be controlled individually or as a group. It\u2019s usually a good idea to just walk around and see where everything is located before taking any action. Once you have an idea of what to do you can strategically place your goons and let them do what they\u2019re supposed to do.<br>As an example you could have a goon set up near the entrance of the place that holds people who are entering or about to leave hostage, one that holds the store manager hostage (which you can then control to open things, for example) and another one could knock the store manager out once he\u2019s done with that (or before that, and have a goon open the door him-\/herself). There are many ways to complete the heist, and there are many ways a heist can go wrong. The store manager could call the cops, which will then arrive shortly after, making it nearly impossible to get out without killing anyone. Do something suspicious on camera and again, the police will be alerted. Or civilians could see an unconscious or dead body and, yup, could call the cops.<br>It\u2019s a game that requires a good bit of strategy but thankfully the developers have implemented a feature to slow down time for people that usually don\u2019t play strategy games or have a difficult time improvising when things go wrong. It\u2019s a feature that doesn\u2019t use a time mechanic so you could play the entire heist in slow motion if you wanted to. It makes the game very accessible to all sorts of players.<br>The maps aren\u2019t that big to begin with and that\u2019s actually something that I liked. It\u2019s hard to manage your characters when they\u2019re miles apart from each other. Some of the maps of the later heist missions are just big enough for them to become cluttered. But I think there will actually be bigger maps in the final version. I just hope they don\u2019t become too big.<br>There\u2019s also a hideout map where you can eventually hire new goons and do some other stuff, and this is also where the game tells you about the game\u2019s mechanics, besides the tutorial level (which basically explains the controls).<br><br>This Early Access version has 5 different heists, ranging from very simple to pretty difficult. There are a few features missing right now, like goons having specific skills, a connecting story and, obviously, more heist locations. The developers states that there is about 1,5-2 hours of gameplay in this version and I agree. But you could add an hour or even a few hours more if you want to play things in a different way. So far, this version is already extremely polished and it runs great without any problems. I also didn\u2019t encounter any bugs so far, but maybe I\u2019m just lucky. Who knows ?<br><br>The graphics are 2D and shown from a top-down perspective. The characters lack a bit of personality because of this but the graphics are just beautiful in general. It has a nice 70s look mixed with what looks like some sort of comic book effect(s). It\u2019s absolutely gorgeous to look at, and it\u2019s all made with higher resolutions in mind.<br>The soundtrack consists of some smooth songs that have a jazzy feel that reminded me of old school heist movies. And that sounds exactly what they\u2019re aiming for. So they did an excellent job there. Same goes for the sound effects which have an authentic feeling to them.<br><br>The Masterplan is on its way to become one of my favorite indie games. There\u2019s still some work to be done, and I\u2019m not talking about fixes here, but more about adding (final) content. This current version is already very stable and very playable and if you enjoy strategy games or heist games then this is a game you just must own. You can clearly see this game was made with passion and that\u2019s absolutely something that should be rewarded.
