off, I want to state that Steam should really have a better rating sistem, a mixed option would be nice. I'm sick and tired of having to be forced to choose despite having mixed experiences with the game. I've went with a thumbs up for Wild Terra Online, because I think it deserves a higher rating that it currently has.<br><br>Let me start with a controversial topic a lot of steam reviews mention - the developers. Many people state they had a bad experience and some were banned from the forums, but from what I've talked to them, they seemed professional and friendly. The fact I contacted them on behalf of a review site might have a say in that, but I had no trouble with them whatsoever.<br><br>Now let's get to the game itself. If you've played any other sandbox MMORPG, the base principle is quite similar. You spend most of the time gathering resources and crafting\/constructing various things. There is a large variety of items to craft, some of which you have to unlock first via recipes. You find those while exploring and when you find enough of them, you unlock a new thing - may it be a structure, piece of equipment or simply a tool. Item variety is a very important thing in a sandbox MMORPG, because it's the only thing to keep you going. You won't have a quest, a story or a predetermined cause - you will be doing what you want and for most people that will be building their base\/home and getting better and better gear. <br><br>The skills system is quite nice as well. Quite surprisingly, I've felt discouraged from playing to much, because you get a predetermined amount of bonus exp to help you improve skills each day and when that runs out, leveling them goes down to a crawl. Combine that with the fact that some skills (especially higher levels) require you to spend a lot of resources, you will have to spend more time gathering resources more than you originally intended. That might be fine for someone, who just came home from work and simply wants to relax - grab a pickaxe, run around for a bit and get some metals, however it takes a lot of time to get anywhere. I've been playing for almost 20 hours (granted, some of them were AFK-ing) and I've still only barely managed to get copper gear. It would take a lot more to get to iron or steel. That is, unless you cave in and get micro-transactions.<br><br>Because you see, Wild Terra also has those. They are in no way required and only make progress faster, but it's still not a great thing to see, when you already had to spent money on the game itself. Unless the developers get greedy and add game breaking things to the store, I'm fine with it. Really. If someone wants to progress faster or\/and look awesome, they are free to use the store but it is in no way required to progress. <br><br>The last gameplay related thing I'd like to touch is combat. It's fairly basic, with some active abilities and the same leveling system crafting related skills use. The PVP servers are not that populated and the main game mode is PvE anyway. You will have to use weapons while hunting but you probably won't go further than that. An expansion of the battle system would be nice, however it is in no way required. <br><br>And lastly, graphics and sound. The graphics don't look bad at all. They went for a realistic look and it fits, however the animations are fairly basic. Don't expect much here but you won't get disgusted by it either. As for the sound, I'd like to mention a weird thing. Every time you open the game, the music is turned off. A minor thing, since you can simply change between three options in-game, but it would be nice if the game would remember the setting that was used the last time.<br><br>---------<br>All in all, Wild Terra Online is a decent MMORPG that requires a lot of time to progress. The unique food system is nice, but it doesn't innovate much in other areas. It still shows some signs of early access, such as sometimes missing structure tooltips and other minor things (I have also noticed a situation that seemed like a memory leak in two separate situations), but none of them should cause major problems or discourage you from playing.
