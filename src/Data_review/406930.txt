Instead of enemies dropping weapons however, each character comes equipped with 4 of them, all of which do something different, but they all fit the characters they are attached to. After all, you wouldn\u2019t see the mage of the game holding out a pair of pistols, as that would just completely defeat the point of him being a spell-caster.<br><br>Now, it\u2019s a little difficult to ignore the character design in this game, and you\u2019ll know why now, as here\u2019s the list:<br><br>\u2022\tRotheart \u2013 An evil necromancer with a pumpkin on his head, with access to magical abilities.<br>\u2022\tMrs Groundfire \u2013 An elderly lady equipped with a minigun. Yes! A minigun!!<br>\u2022\tBoris Porkov \u2013 A medieval pig warrior, whom has access to an assortment of melee weapons.<br>\u2022\tSonny \u201cRicochet\u201d Banani \u2013 A monkey who just so happens to be a mobster as well, equipped with a couple of pistols, a shotgun, an SMG, as well as a Molotov to tickle that inner-arsonist<br><br>Each character is equipped with their own 4 different weapons, all of which are specified for one character and one character alone. This makes for a different experience with each and every character, meaning that you will get some variety when you pick a different individual to play for yourself.<br><br>There isn\u2019t too much variety when it comes to game modes, one of which being survival, which is a category that has wave defence and objective inside it. The gist of that being, you either play until you die, or you do the objective the game sets for you. Crypt mode is a little different, as your main task isn\u2019t just kill things and keep yourself alive. Instead, you\u2019re effectively thrown into a maze, which you have to somehow make your way to the end of, while many nasty creatures come to ruin your run. <br><br>In survival mode you can pick and choose one of 5 pre-built stages for you to play in, whereas in crypt mode it\u2019s procedurally generated. I know it says it\u2019s randomly generated on the store page, but it\u2019s not. The game takes tile-sets from a predetermined pool to make your crypt level, as I\u2019ve seen the same area multiple times on different runs without any change, meaning that the areas in the dungeon aren\u2019t random if they\u2019ve already been designed. That being said, it\u2019s nice to have a bit of change here and there when it comes to the stages, so it\u2019s nice to have some procedurally generated dungeons to keep the life of the game going a little more.<br><br>Graphically, the game definitely isn\u2019t anything special, and can probably go as far as ugly. The animations are a bit stiff, and just doesn\u2019t look all that great, regardless of if it\u2019s a walking or running animation, or an attack, they just all look rather clunky and aren\u2019t all that special to witness. Character design is interesting, but the characters themselves, along with whatever enemies you come in contact with don\u2019t look particularly great and that won\u2019t be changing, no matter how many different creatures you come across.<br><br>The 5 stages for the most part are all different from one another, so it\u2019s nice to see the different stages, since one is focused around ice, and another one revolves around fire, so thankfully not every stage looks the same or has the same colour pallet for you to deal with. Where the stages don\u2019t shine so much is in their design, as I\u2019ve found myself clipping into objects numerous times, and even at one point was almost completely stuck in a corner, with little to no way out until spamming the jump button for around 2 minutes solved the problem. Even if you can solve that problem while playing, they shouldn\u2019t be there in the first place. When you\u2019re being stuck on something that you really shouldn\u2019t get stuck on can quickly get frustrating, especially when you\u2019re being followed by a train of mobs that want your guts for garters.<br><br><br>Speaking of mobs, the AI in this game is incredibly simplistic. They do little to nothing more than just walking up to you, a lot of the time in single file, just to bash you on the head. Their AI isn\u2019t complex at all, meaning that you can pretty much just back away from any enemy coming towards you, and kill them without any trouble. Up until the point you get clipped on something that you shouldn\u2019t, which you\u2019ll find will quickly frustrate you when that\u2019s the reason you\u2019ve just died.<br><br>One serious problem the game has is bugs. They\u2019re everywhere, and it really needs to be cleaned up a fair bit to make the game more enjoyable. For instance, when selecting a character in Survival mode, the character model doesn\u2019t change until the moment you hit select and get in-game, so for a first time player, you have little to no idea who you\u2019re picking. Then, once you\u2019ve picked your character, the character model that floats in this tube that you\u2019ll see when picking a character stays on the level, even after you\u2019ve started. <br><br>These bugs don\u2019t really affect gameplay, but this next one does. With Ricochet you have access to an SMG, which obviously will have ammo tied to it. If you use every single bullet in your SMG, but don\u2019t let go of left mouse click, you\u2019ll switch out to your pistol, but it will fire like your SMG until you let go. Oh, and once you have let go, all the sounds that you can hear from then on is multiple layers of the same shooting sound effect being looped over and over again, at a surprisingly high volume. I am going to recommend the game, but that's down to the fact that I'm putting hope that the game will be improved down the line. Plus, I enjoy supporting indie developers.
