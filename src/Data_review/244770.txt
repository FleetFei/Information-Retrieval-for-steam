class=\"postedDate\">Posted: March 17\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t<div class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStar Made<br>Star made is a voxel based space shooter\/ builder. Essentially it could be described as Minecraft in space. Like Minecraft everything is made of blocks (except some objects witch are shaped like wedges), however unlike Minecraft, structures such as ships and planets have their own gravity, meaning a player standing inside a ship they have built will move with the ship. The game can be purchased on steam here <a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"http:\/\/store.steampowered.com\/app\/244770\/\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_0\">http:\/\/store.steampowered.com\/app\/244770\/<\/a> <br><br>The \u201cgoal\u201d of the game is to mine planets and asteroids, scavenge ship wrecks, and trade with other factions to build up the resources (the blocks) needed to build your own Ships, space stations and bases. Ship building is a particular focus of the game, with the ability to add shield blocks, thruster blocks, gun blocks, and more to improve your ships shield, mobility, offensive capabilities respectively, being a key part of game play, with a focus on balancing the different ship systems for the best ship possible, while still having a ship the looks good inside and out. Building is made easier with the use of advanced build settings, access by hoping into the ships control cube. The advanced build settings allow for placing multiple blocks at a time, and the use of symmetry tools which allow for truly immense builds to be made with relative ease. The control cube is also the means by which the ship can be piloted, much like any vehicle in any other game.<br><br>One of the games highlights is the dog fights. Nothing is more thrilling then using the ship you have work so hard on in battle. Firing your custom laser canons at your enemy, watching your shields health slowly tick down with every hit you take, praying to god you have enough shield, before your tracking missiles reload and are ready to fire, wiping out the enemy ship. Then you have the reward of scavenging the blocks from your kill, and adding them to your own ship, allowing you to take down even bigger pray next time.<br><br>One of the games draw backs is the lag online. Online playing with friends, taking down ships with real people and having a crew of even a fleet with several human players running around each ship is great. Your friend might be manning a turret on your ship, or flying alongside you in his own. However the servers are currently not up to the task and lot of lag or not being able to connect is common. And single player can become quite dull, as there is less of a sense of competition against bots.<br><br>Another drawback to the game is how complicated it is, the building system of adding blocks to change your ships stats is surprisingly sophisticated. Weapons fire in different ways depending on how the blocks are placed together and they can be further modified with other support blocks, rail systems are used for docking ships to ships, and logic systems can be built to allow for complicated automatic systems, and much more. This means no two ships will be the same and will all have entirely unique strengths and weaknesses in combat, such as weak stealth ships or slow moving but heavily armed battle ships, but unfortunately it does mean that the learning curve is very steep for someone wanting to master ship building, and fully understanding how to build a good ship will take tens of hours of both game play and watching tutorials, this complexity understandably can turn a lot of people off the game even though you don\u2019t need that much knowledge to make a basic ship that can fly and shoot. The complexity issue is further compounded by the fact that there are frequent updates changing how things work and rendering old knowledge useless. But on the flip side these updates mean there are always new features keeping the game fresh and fixing bugs.<br><br>Ultimately star made is a solid concept of a game that is constantly improving; some people may find the building overly complicated and the game boring as a result. But I personally enjoy the level of uniqueness and customisation this complicated building system allows for. And the satisfaction of walking the corridors of a ship I\u2019ve built, or using it in a high speed dog fight is more than enough to make up for the games current floors in my opinion.
