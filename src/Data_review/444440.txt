developed and published by 3100 games. The dev team\u2019s sole Steam project so far and after briefly reading on the game\u2019s Store page, I noticed that it was developed by 5 eager students. Steam\u2019s the perfect place to jump-start your career and transform a hobby, into something truly creative.<br> <br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay<\/div>And indeed, no one can argue that Scapeland isn\u2019t stretching imagination. In fact, judging the game at first glance you wouldn\u2019t consider that its core gameplay lies within stealth mechanics instead of let\u2019s say, peaceful faming and trading? Because that is precisely what you have to do as soon as you hit the \u201cNew Game\u201d option. Yet after a short while, harvest time is over and you find yourself literally hunted by groups of soldiers along fenced-off areas with tanks sprinkled in-between. Zero explanation for any of this and survival means avoiding the aforementioned soldiers and bypassing them. You\u2019re a peaceful farmer\/trader, remember? Of course the \u201ceevil gov\u2019t\u201d is after your crops or whatever silly motivation you can muster for yourself in order to find some reason for the fictional manhunt you are witnessing. One thing is certain: you have to use your wits and carefully plan your movement as to fully avoid crossing through the cones of vision, featured by your potential captors. <br><br>Luckly, you can distract the soldiers by throwing rocks. Fascinating! Earlier, as in before you were running for your life, you could also toss seeds and animals using the same throwing mechanic. You have to appreciate a game as abstract as Scapeland, no doubt. What I didn\u2019t really like is the predictible AI and equally so, search patterns used by the patrols out there to get you. I\u2019ve had my fair share of stealth games and I declare myself a fan of this subgenre, but sadly, Scapeland lacks providing even a decent modicum of challenge. So a farming simulator it is not and the weird stealth sections besides offering no context, are also fairly superficial. The odds are not in your favor, Scapeland\u2026<br> <br>At least the game uses the Unity engine to its advantage. It also nails a minimalist style of sorts with faceless characters (by the way, you can choose between a male or female protagonist, even if there won\u2019t be any gameplay differences at all) and brightly colored pixelated assets. It\u2019s screaming \u201cindie\u201d all over it and that is not necessarily a bad thing. If only it could stand out somehow. The sounds are most certainly not helping it, since they\u2019re only a handful and repetitive to a fault. Level design also seems to be lacking variety since you will be playing along the same farmlands\/military bases for the majority of time. It gets old pretty fast and no matter how predictable the AI is, you are still prone to mistakes that will sooner have you rage quit than to restart the level yet again.<br> <br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Pros<\/div>+ Bizarre stealth\/farming mechanics<br>+ Pleasant graphics<br>+ Steam Achievements &amp; Trading Cards<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Cons<\/div>- Limited sound selection<br>- No story or coherent context for the in-game actions you must perform<br>- Gets repetitive fast<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Result \/ Final thoughts<\/div>Frankly, if this game wasn\u2019t decently priced (even outside seasonal Sales) and offering both Cards and Achievements, I would have firmly warned potential buyers of the bad investment and advise them to steer clear. But Scapeland manages to offer the said auxiliaries and its vague attempts at mixing stealth with farming represent a novelty, even if poorly implemented and seriously missing even a flimsy storyline.
