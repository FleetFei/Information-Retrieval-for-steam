One day, they run into some sort of interference and have to teleport away. Unfortunately, the interference causes everyone to be sent to random locations in an abandoned, mysterious, and barren section of the multiverse. As Captain Viridian, you must find your missing friends, who lie beyond some challenging obstacles created by the inhabitants who once lived there. <br><br>Just like every other platformer ever released, you can move left and right across the numerous areas this game has. This game has an open world, about 20x20 in area. Instead of scrolling, you move across the grid by getting to the edges of an area in, for the most part, either direction, though some areas will wrap in at least two directions. This is very similar to how I Wanna Be The Guy handled its game world. However, by changing one single mechanic, this game goes from a standard platformer to a very unique experience. You see, Viridian can't jump in a normal way. Instead, he can switch gravity at his will to get past an obstacle. This allows for some very clever puzzles and challenges with platforms that can move and disappear, moving enemies, or even lines that auto-flip you in mid-air.<br><br>The overworld of Dimension VVVVVV is split into six very distinct areas: Your ship is the hub that you return to after each level, and is the smallest part of the map at 3x2; The outside is the largest area of the game, comprising of the dimension's colorful yet empty landscape; The Space Station, where you first take control of Viridian after the opening cutscene, is the largest defined level in the game, so big that you visit the place twice during your playthrough; The Laboratory is located beneath the ship and is where you are introduced to the gravity lines; The Tower is located east of the ship and is the only scrolling level accessible through normal means; and The Warp Zone is accessed from the northeast corner of the map after you go through a maze, and this introduces Warp Tokens that teleport you, as well as the wrapping rooms. There are teleporters all over the map that allow for easy access to areas you've already visited. Aside from that, there's also The Polar Dimension that you warp to after rescuing the third, fourth, and fifth crew members and is not located on the main map.<br><br>There are, as mentioned, five crewmates to rescue:<br>-Doctor Violet(Space Station 1)-She gives you the ship's coordinates at the start of the game so you can teleport there at the end of the first stage.<br>-Doctor Victoria(Laboratory)-She loves to study new things and is very worried about her crewmates.<br>-Professor Vitellary(Space Station 2)-He's the smartest of the crew and can pretty much understand everything that happens.<br>-Officer Vermilion(Tower)-He is very brave and will venture out into the world around him to help you find the other crewmates if you rescue him.<br>-Chief Verdigris(Warp Zone)-He is very committed to repairing the ship so you can escape.<br><br>Hidden among the world are twenty shiny trinkets to collect. Collecting them unlocks the ability to play songs on the ship's jukebox, and find all twenty of them will reward you with something special. They are spread out all over the map:<br>-The Space Station contains six trinkets<br>-The Laboratory contains four trinkets<br>-If you talk to Victoria twice after rescuing her from the Lab, she gives you a trinket<br>-The Tower contains two trinkets<br>-The maze leading to The Warp Zone has a trinket, and The Warp Zone also contains a trinket<br>-The outside, excluding the WZ entrance puzzle, contains four trinkets<br>-You are awarded a trinket for beating the game<br><br>The game is very colorful and charming. The graphics are very well drawn, and the color makes it look even better. Many of the spaces in the grid are named, and many of the names are very amusing and have pop-culture references in them. The story is simple and easy to follow, and the dialogue between the characters can be very entertaining.<br><br>Don't let the game's color fool you! This game is very much a throwback to classic gaming in not just the graphics, but also the difficulty. This game is HARD, and I mean, VERY HARD. Many of the areas require plenty of skill to pass. A few select trinkets will REALLY test your patience if you are new to the game. In particular is the infamous Veni Vidi Vici trinket in SS2 that forces you to flip into a spike filled chute, hit a disappearing platform, and fall back down on to the trinket. That said, the difficulty is very fair and follows a smooth curve if done in a specific way. It makes you want to complete each part to see what new challenges lie ahead, and all it requires is a bunch of skill and patience.<br><br>When all is said and done, VVVVVV is just an incredible game, possibly the best indie game ever made. Everything about it is just so well done. There are optional modes unlocked after certain requirements are met. You can do time trials on the levels, activate a mode that flips everything upside down, or, if you are a true man among men, try to beat the infamous No-Death mode. And there's also a level editor and some pre-installed player levels for you to try out, and you can download more if you find some. Overall, this game gets a well deserved 10\/10 from me.
