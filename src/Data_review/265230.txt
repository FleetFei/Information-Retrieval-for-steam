let me begin by stating that this is the first review I have ever for a game, though I have played a number on steam. If there is anything I could improve upon in my writing or if you did not find this review very helpful, please, don't just click dislike, let me know why. I have only played the demo for the game but have already invested almost 11 hours into it and all I have to say is, OH MY GOD! A: Because I've only &quot;completed&quot; the demo once. B: Because half the time I wanted to throw my computer of the window and listen to the sweet music of it shattering into glorious, gratifying pieces. C: Because I have never found an rpg that gives the player such a sense of accomplishment for any decision that doesn't get you killed and overwhelming despair at failure. Let me expand on this: When you succeed in ANY skill check when you've failed character build after character build you feel like a freaking god among men and that you've reached the epitemy of being. Your life has purpose suddenly, you matter, you cannot be stopped... and then you die... again... and again... AND AGAIN... AND FOR THE LOVE OF, HOW, HOW DID I FAIL? I'VE GOT 7 FREAKING STREETWISE AND IT'S A DAMN STREETWISE CHECK AND NOW YOU'RE SAYING I FAILED AND ALL 6 OF THESE GUYS ARE ABOUT BEND ME OVER AND SCREW ME LIKE A FIVE DOLLAR \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665?!?!?!?! As I said, god, then dead quicker than you can even try to reload that save game. Why do you go from god mode to a rotting corpse so quickly? The combat is BRUTAL. LITERALLY BRUTAL. If you &quot;mess up&quot; (and I use that term loosely) on char. creation, two window licking children with butter knives and wearing nothing but tighty whiteys can kill you. Seriously, in like 3 turns. That being said, you CAN beat said window lickers but it takes a willingness to learn, the ability to adapt, and &quot;good&quot; character development. I used good in quotes because the term is relative. You can create any character you want and develop them any way you see fit. Now before you go jumping down my throat and say &quot;But Dak, you can do that in every Bethesda game ever created!&quot;No, you can't, at least not on the same level that you can in Age of Decadence. No matter what background you choose, you can play that character any way you want. The only thing that remains the same is what guild you start off in (if any) and the personalized opening quest for each background, but that's it. Want to be a a charismatic, intelligent mercenary that's more interested in reading books and charming his way out of trouble than bashing skulls, you can do that. An assassin that gives zero ***** about being sneaky, witty, or deceiving and you just wanna go up to your mark and be all &quot;Hey, how you doing? Don't care, die now!&quot; with a battle axe the size of a grown man? Why not, get up close and personal and shout &quot;**** you Ezio and your parkour BS, I kill 'em like a man!&quot; You can do literally anything you want with any character. That being said, you CANNOT do EVERYTHING with any single character in one playthough. There are too many skill checks, not enough skill points. (this holds true for the demo at least) but that's what makes this game both eternally frustrating and the most satisfying game you will ever play in the near future. You have to dive back in and start anew. Unlike other games however, YOU WILL WANT TO DO THIS, not cringe every time you complete the game going &quot; Well, time to do the same **** again.&quot; You only have to do the same thing if you want to. The non-combat aspect of the game is also one of the most in depth, interesting and satisfying of any game I've ever played. You get drawn in this world and just keep diving deeper and deeper. You don't just click the option that doesn't end the conversation with hopes of unlocking a new quest (though you sometimes do) you're actually interested in what is going on in the game world. After deciding against my better judgment to let some \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665 poor loremaster live under the promise of learning probably nothing from him and knowing the next quest I had to involved fighting those damn window lickers that made me reload a save more times than I count, I went to the inn just so I could talk to the storyteller and learn more about the gameworld that had both crushed my hopes and dreams and yet somehow had me coming back for more. In conclusion, if you have not played Age of Decadence and are not sure if it is worth thirty bucks, download and play the demo. There are probably at least 20 different playthroughs you could do in the demo alone and still not complete every quest available in the demo content. So, make it 21 damnit and make a character designed for the sole purpose of finding out what the hell that glowing tube thing does! The Age of Decadence is a gem of an rpg and I wish more people would play, review and help spread the word about this amazing game! If you want a brutally challenging yet immensely satisfying rpg, don't leave this game page.
