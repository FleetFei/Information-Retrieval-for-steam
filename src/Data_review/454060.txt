At first glance the graphics are very minimalistic and when you first start the game up you might think this a negative. I think it's a rather well thought through design decision. As the game progresses past the setup of your first few buildings the screen quickly gets filled with information, but the simplicity of the graphics design makes it easy to keep track of it all. There's small visual ques on the buildings that show you the rate of production or if there's a hiccup somewhere and production has stopped. Clever design that quickly becomes second nature to read.<br><br>The music adds a soothing backdrop and I quickly find myself in the tycoon game spirit of just wanting to optimize this one thing, just one more supply route to sort and it'll be perfect, but you're never quite done. You keep chasing that next big payoff while trying to manage your resources. Your goal never seems out of grasp and that makes this game rather addicting.<br><br>I will say that at first this game bombards you with information. It's all about the blueprints. You set each building up to produce something and there's generally a blueprint for it. There's default blueprints which you can use to get a basic idea of a setup, but beyond that you'll want to fiddle with them a lot to up production and thus your cash flow. I love the idea of the default blueprints because they let you get on with the game quickly and help you save some 'oh \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665' moments where you've missed a vital part of your production chain. I also love how you can change them and build your own setup using a variety of fun tools for your workers. Who doesn't want to use a jetpack to quicken the process of goods delivery in a supply chain?!<br><br>I see some people are having trouble with the text size, but this is because the game launches at a low resolution. Simply bump it up to where you usually play your games and all the text is easily readable.<br><br>As I'm writing this there are some issues in the game, as is expected with an early access game. Some menus overlap eachother in an annoying way and some internal workings need a little bit of streamlining, but I've not had any crashes or hiccups or gamebreaking bugs. I'll be keen to watch this game develop and evolve because even though some edges are a little bit rough right now you can really tell there's a true gem at the center of this
