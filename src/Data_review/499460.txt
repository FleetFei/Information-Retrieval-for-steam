TL;DR at the bottom)<br><br>Tomato Jones is a challenging 3D puzzle platformer developed by HA Studio Ltd. in which you play a rolling red tomato who bears resemblance to Indiana Jones in both choice of hat and agility. You must roll and jump through castles and tombs collecting gold and artifacts along the way while simutaneously dodging what appears to be Nazi tomatillos and Apache potatoes(?). The game's 20 levels slowly increase in difficulty and occasionally add in new game mechanics like minecarts and swinging blades.<br><br>Upon first starting the game I opened up the &quot;Options&quot; menu and found a decent enough selection of settings from Screen Resolution to graphics options like &quot;Bloom&quot; all while a silly little soundtrack played in the background. I began the first level and was welcomed by unique and decent graphics with an art style that fit the theme of the game. I immediately noticed the solid physics the game presents as Tomato Jones continues to roll even after your fingers leave the keys, his momentum pushing him onward. <br><br>As I progressed through the levels I was often glad to see additional game mechanics added nearly every level. The game would require me to learn how to utilize things like minecarts and buttons to my advantage. I also enjoyed the challenge of collecting each and every coin, sparking the completionist within me. The game's difficulty gradually and constantly increased as the levels took longer to beat and my patience was tested. I appreciated that the developer added in the ability to change camera angles manually, as sometimes you need a different perspective in order to complete a level. <br><br>Unfortunately the game has it's weaknesses, as to be expected with a not-so-serious $2 dollar game. I'm thankful that the developer added in a feature to restart to the last checkpoint as I had to do that quite a few times. For instance, you can get stuck in between two walls and watch as Tomato Jones slowly slides to his death, which takes anywhere from 5 to 20 seconds to happen. You can also get launched off the map if you time your jump off of an elevator incorrectly, which is fine, except for the times when it doesn't kill you. It was also annoying when I'd depress a button with a crate or barrel, just for the barrel or crate to accidentally get moved thus making the button disappear. <br><br>The game, at times, feels unfinished in that I'd like to see quite a few features added. I think that the game could use a skybox or background of sorts, for one. I also would like to see different modes added like a level creator. I suppose it's a bit much to expect from a $2 dollar game, but I'm just throwing ideas out there for the long awaited sequel Tomato Jones 2. Luckily, the developer seems to actually care about his game as he's already fixed some issues and appears to be working hard to fix more while simutaneously adding features to the game. Even AAA developers could learn something from that.<br><br><u><b>TL;DR<\/b><\/u> All in all, Tomato Jones is a fun platforming game where you'll get exactly what it says on the tin. Despite it's flaws, it's a fun, cheap little game that will supply you with a couple hours worth of enjoyment, laughter, and frustration. It's a silly little game with great amounts of potential.
