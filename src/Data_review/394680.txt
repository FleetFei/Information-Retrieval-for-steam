We are together for a review of another HOG again! How marvelously different and interesting, is it? No, of course it isn't. This is our millionth HOG, and after a while they all end up resembling one another. Still, <i>Mountain Crime: Requital<\/i> is one of the better ones out there, with an interesting enough scenario to establish a sense of curiosity.<br><br>We here have Dr. Philip White, who receives an urgent phone call in the middle of the night, which asks him to attend a patient at the White Wolf Mountain Resort, in a really bad storm. As any self-confident wise guy with no common sense spared, he travels to middle of nowhere to attend said patient, only to find a murder-mystery. All communication and transportation is cut off, hotel guests are dropping like flies and there is a sick \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665 on the loose who treats all this chaos as an elaborate game. It seems that everything is connected to a bigger crime buried in the past. &quot;But what crime?&quot; asks Dr. Philip White, and starts his investigation like any Scooby Doo wannabe.<br><br>Cute, huh? Don't be deceived by my mockery. Our protagonist is a naive sod, but the story itself and the connection between hotel guests are a mystery worthy of uncovering. As for the game itself, graphics are pretty good. Background design is interesting and there are many, many lockboxes, automatons and hidden doors around. If I'd visit this hotel in real life - without a murder-mystery present, of course - I know I'd love to spare a week or two there. Character animations look pretty until they start talking. Then with the tongue-tied voice acting and clumsy lip synch, the atmosphere crumbles. It's like a really fine looking lady with no smarts. You'd love to look her all day, as long as she doesn't talk.<br><br>Puzzles, I hear? Oh, they are good. There is a balanced combination of ordinary ones, and 'just annoying enough to stall you off until you were about to give up' ones. HOG scenes are very neat, with open and distinguishable items, with more than only one item of the kind lying around. Each HOG is designed to gather pieces for a machine of sort that you're trying to fix, so you can reach another item of use. This way, items laid around kinda make sense, which is rare in this genre.<br><br>All in all, this is a fine HOG, but I'll attach a warning. This game is included in a Hidden Object Bundle 5 in 1 in Steam, so if you have the bundle already, there is no actual need for you to buy this game. You already have the game within the bundle. If you don't have it and are even mildly curious, go ahead. Gonna end up with 3-4 hours of decent HOG fun. With this said, my work here is done!
