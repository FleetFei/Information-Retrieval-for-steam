Without going into too much detail, each card can be one of three types; Creature, Spell or Triggered. Your creatures battle your opponent's creatures directly, and also serve as your life-blood - ie. you win by eliminating all your oponents creature cards. Spell cards provide a wide variety of effects such as buffs, debuffs or direct damage-dealing, while Triggered cards are similar in effect to spell cards, but will only activate under specific circumstances. The actual battles themselves are completely non-interactive, but don't be fooled; in campaign mode at least, selecting the right cards for each challenge increasingly involves strategy and careful forethought - something which caught me by surprise having played a few CCGs on mobile which are largely devoid of any real gameplay.<br><br>During the 18.5 hours total I've spent playing this game at the time of writing this review, I've completely ignored the cash-shop and PvP elements, instead focusing on campaign mode, daily rewards, and shop items that can be bought with in-game earnings. All up, there's probably a good 10-15 solid hours worth of gameplay to be had from campaign mode before you'll hit a difficulty wall, and will have a hard time progressing further without a more fleshed out collection. From there, you'll find yourself logging in for short sessions, mostly to recieve daily rewards, doing a spot of enchanting\/combining, and playing maybe a round or two here and there; things I've still found to be satisfying in their own way.<br><br>For the type of game this is, I've found the quality of art on the cards to be a little diasppointing - not bad, but not that great either.
