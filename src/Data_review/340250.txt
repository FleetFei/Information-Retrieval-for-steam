to 100% completion a few years ago, put a decent chunk of time in this one to see how it has changed (it has basically refined a few features both technical and game wise).<br><br>To get to the elephant in the room immediately: yes, the graphics are very basic.  I find some of the enemy designs to be charming, but overall the graphics are functional.  Fortunately one gets used to them rather quickly and after the initial &quot;meh&quot; to them one begins to simply focus on the game itself.<br><br>I'd say the greatest strength Crystal Towers 2 has is the rather open level design.  They are Sonic-esque not in the &quot;gotta go fast!&quot; sense but in how you are often presented with divergent paths, that you can fall from one route and end up in another one.  They are not one dimensional in this regard either, you generally get three stages for each area (11 general areas, 33 stages in all plus boss ones) and each area has its own flavor of open. While always a 2d platformer it adds a good deal of variety to the proceedings.<br><br>Unfortunately this ties in to a couple of my issues with the game.  One is that you have to go through all objectives in order, with you receiving access to a new one only after completing the previous one.  Occasionally this results in you having to repeat an accomplishment you had already taken care of.  The second is that... there's probably too much quantity of things in there.  If you have 33 levels with 8 objectives each that's 264 objectives plus the dozen or so bosses and while I would only point to a handful of the objectives as iffy, at that number enough of them are similar enough that some could have been left on the cutting room floor without the game losing much.  Of course you can beat the game without finishing nearly all of them so some of that is on me.<br><br>Still overall this is a very well designed and charming game.  I would recommend it heartily at this price and would urge those on the borderline to try the free demo that EDIT: is now available on Steam and updated to take advantage of all the XL refinements!
