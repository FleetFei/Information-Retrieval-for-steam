class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNote: I don't really play multiplayer unless it's with people I know, so I can't offer any info in terms of playing multiplayer with randoms.<br><br>The best way to explain this game is to say that it takes the most exciting part of other sci-fi games and puts you right in the thick of the action. From space battles that involve entire fleets down to street-to-street urban combat, you're given more freedom and choice than any game I've played with a similar scope. If you've played the Battlefront games this all may sound familiar, and it does definitely feel like an evolution of that concept, but there's more here than Battlefront ever had. Every ship in a fleet engagement is pilotable, and with the relatively simple command interface, it's easy to give orders on the fly.<br><br>The AI bots are surprisingly intelligent, but they do seem to have some trouble with pathfinding in vehicles. In close quarters environments they sometimes can be fatally slow to recognize an enemy running around amongst them, but I've seen that happen enough playing against actual people in other games that I'm not too put off by it. The game allows for all 64 potential players to be bots, which means singleplayer is more viable than most similar games.<br><br>The game used to be a little clunky the last time I really checked it out, but as of the time of writing it runs smoothly and without issue. I haven't experienced any crashes or undue slowness and my aging graphics card has no problem playing with the settings turned all the way up. <br><br>My only real complaint is the relative lack of a sense of progression. The current system allows you to gradually increase the amount of gear you can put into any one loadout, but every weapon and upgrade is available from the start. To be fair, this is more of an issue with my personal preferences than anything that is objectively done wrong; this system would probably be welcomed by people who like the game but can't devote the hours necessary to unlock the best toys.<br><br>There's a good variety to the weapons and upgrades available, and nothing feels gimmicky or out of place. The player and vehicle customization is probably my favorite mechanic; between all of the options on offer pretty much any playstyle will find a nice home here. <br><br>As fun as the vehicles are, I wish more levels included them. At the moment most non-space missions are set in urban environments with infantry being the only option for either side. Those missions still manage to be quite fun on their own, but the vehicles provide a little more flexibility to the way the individual matches play out. <br><br>Space combat is fun and engaging, with essentially three layers of fighting going on at any one time: the capital ships duking it out amongst each other, the strike craft trying to protect their bombers and transports while shooting down those of the enemy, and the boarders who can disable individual weapon systems on the capital ships or even sabotage the core to bring the whole thing down. Infiltrating a battleship, destroying the core, and stealing an enemy fighter to escape seconds before the ship explodes is a moment that most games would spend the entire time building up to: in AFF it can be your day job.<br><br>In short, I'd like a story mode and some unlockables, but the game's in early access right now, plus my opinion might be in the minority, so I can't really complain either way. class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDo you want a FPS game in space with huge capital ships and tiny fighters, 64-player multiplayer support and multiple highly asymmetric roles? A game which takes on mechanical challenges that the big studios have run away from?<br>Then Angels Fall First is the game for you.<br><br>Do you want a super-polished conventional FPS experience? Then you should probably look elsewhere; there are lots of good ones out there, but that's not the strength of AFF.<br><br>Angels Fall First is a combined arms FPS featuring both space combat with battleships, destroyers, dropships, bombers, fighters, space stations and infantry; as well as ground combat with vehicles, infantry and static defenses. It has a heavy multiplayer focus: single-player is just multiplayer against bots. The game supports up to 64 players\/bots, but it doesn't need that many to start a game - really just one, as the bots can fill in all essential roles.<br><br>Pros:<br> - <b><u>Scope:<\/u><\/b> While the ground combat is fairly solid, the aspect that most strikes me about this game is the scope of the space battles. This game includes <i>everything<\/i> I want in a huge fleet battle - like you might see in the movies. You've got Battleships duking it out with giant beam weapons and missiles, escorted by destroyers and corvettes; at the same time, fighters and dropships are launching off those battleships to get space superiority and land infantry on the enemy battleship; finally, you have infantry boarding the enemy ships, defending your own ships and repairing all of the stuff that is on fire because of the giant lasers. To sum it up, it's an epic experience.<br><br> - <b><u>Customization:<\/u><\/b> Apart from the capital ships themselves, everything in the game can be customized as you see fit. There are many different infantry weapons available, from SMGs to MGs and Sniper Rifles, as well as a wide range of support items. You can also choose whether you want more armor or more speed, and in either case the choice makes a big difference to your combat style. Fighters can be tricked out with 5 different main weapons, 5 choices of missiles and 3 choices of defenses, as well as tweaking the armor\/speed balance. Ground vehicles also have various choices for extra equipment.<br><br> - <b><u>Diverse Roles:<\/u><\/b> Very important in a multiplayer game, I feel, as not everyone wants to rely on thier lightning teenage reflexes. Thankfully for the slower-paced gamers among us, it doesn't take much reflexes to pilot a lumbering battleship that needs 30 seconds to turn around anyway. If a friend isn't into piloting a spaceship, there's often fighting to be done either on your ship\/space station or that of the enemy. And if your friend doesn't like fighting at all, you'll be really glad to have him on board when your main gun gets shot to bits in the middle of a fight.<br><br> - <b><u>Feel:<\/u><\/b> This is hard to explain, but I'll try. Things just feel like they're the way they ought to be in this game. Firing your fighter's main gun at a battleship does... nothing, as you'd expect (though with the right missiles you can put a serious dent in its hull). Trying to hit an interceptor with a battleship gun is like swatting insects - hitting is rare, but when you do, the target is gone. In heavy armor you can take a whole clip and keep on coming; in light armor you can run rings around the opponent.<br><br> - <b><u>Dev Team Responsiveness:<\/u><\/b> The dev team is small, but highly active. They've fixed many bugs and responded well to suggestions and feedback from players, which bodes well for the future. The patch schedule is reasonable and there's typically a lot in each patch.<br><br>NEEDS MOAR:<br> - <b><u>Space Maps:<\/u><\/b> There are functionally only three space maps right now: two versions each of two different maps, but one version of one (Meudeverre Incursion) is bugged as of this writing (Patch 5). Admittedly this does get a little same-y after 100 hours or so. The devs have said that there are more maps in the pipeline; however, their focus right now is on bugfixing and performance.<br><br> - <b><u>Players:<\/u><\/b> Unfortunately due to inexplicable circumstances *cough* Fallout 4 *cough* the servers are pretty empty at the moment. That's fine if you enjoy a good comp stomp, but sometimes you want more of a challenge than bots can really provide. Not much anyone can do about this - except, get on the official servers already!<br><br>Could be better:<br> - <b><u>AI:<\/u><\/b> While the AI is in generally pretty competent, it would certainly not get its pilot licence. Bots driving vehicles are very prone to getting stuck, often requiring some human troubleshooting to get out. This is especially noticeable on Meudeverre Territories, which is a cramped map with many, many chunks of ice to crash into.<br><br> - <b><u>UI:<\/u><\/b> Actually, I really don't mind having a massive red marker telling me where my target is. This isn't Where's Wally after all. However, I seem to be decidedly in the minority here. Many other forumers have said the UI needs to improve and the devs have said its on their list.<br><br> - <b><u>Ground Combat:<\/u><\/b> So, I haven't mentioned the ground combat much. That's not to say it's not <i>good<\/i>, but you can probably find a more polished and functionally similar experience elsewhere. I rarely play ground maps anymore, to the point of usually hopping on the official space maps server.<br><br>Cons:<br> - <b><u>Bugs:<\/u><\/b> Well, this is EA for you. However, with Patch 5 I really don't think there's anything game-breaking left. As a mentioned before, Meudeverre Incursion (in particular, the defenders' main spawn point) is badly bugged. There's also some other weirdness with vehicle clipping, etc. Some bugs can be worked around by deleting certain config files and letting the game regenerate them from scratch. The game performs just fine on my $600 desktop, at 1600x900 with moderate settings. As for lag, I haven't seen a hair of it since Patch 5 hit, even playing on EU servers from West NA.<br> <br> - <b><u>Learning Curve:<\/u><\/b> There's no denying it - this game is tough to get into. The tutorial is not the game's strongest point, and the UI and controls certainly take a bit of getting used to (I highly recommend using custom controls anyway). My advice in the beginning is to pick the role that appeals to you most and stick with it.<br> <br> - <b><u>Progression System:<\/u><\/b> Right when you start playing, you usually can't put everything you want in your loadout. There's a limit based on your XP, and at the start you can basically afford one gun plus a few mods and armor. That's it. Later on you can carry plenty. While it's not a huge unbalancing factor, I really don't think it's necessary. You can work around this by starting out with a cap ship, which doesn't use the loadout system at all, although that's not the easiest thing to fly.<br><br>Conclusion:<br>You actually read the whole review? Mad props. I hope this helped you make your decision.
