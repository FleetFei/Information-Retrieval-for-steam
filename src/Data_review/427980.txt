That was Aoi\u2019s point of view until Sora came into his life. <i>Aozora Meikyuu<\/i> is a visual novel that <b>tells the story of Aoi and Sora\u2019s meeting and path to romance<\/b>. This unlikely pair have to figure out how to co-exist as Sora tries to bring out the kinder side of Aoi. A tale of human emotions, sexual desires and personal growth, <i>Aozora Meikyuu<\/i> will take readers on an interesting journey with lovely artwork to bring everything to life.<br><br>Aoi is a normal high school student until his love confession to a classmate goes wrong and he drops out after the rejection. Years later he is now a, hikikomori, shut-in who spends most of his time watching anime and playing video games, especially visual novels. One day on his way home from the store a beautiful young woman with cotton candy-hued hair literally falls from the sky on top of him, naked. The lovely Sora then proceeds to force herself onto Aoi\u2019s life, moving into his home and becoming his housemate. Despite Aoi not wanting to deal with her <b>he can\u2019t help but grow closer and closer to the mysterious girl<\/b>, the game following their relationship until the shocking end.<br><br>I can\u2019t really go much more into detail about the story without spoiling anything, however it did take a bit of an unexpected turn. During most of the game Sora doesn\u2019t talk about herself at all nor does she give much reason for being so attracted to Aoi. He treats her pretty badly for the first half of the game, you can even kick her out of the house and not let her stay but that will end the game prematurely. There is also another girl after his affection, Misaki, however she seems very shoehorned in and it\u2019s also odd that she wants anything to do with the protagonist. <b>Aoi is a shut-in who would rather look at anime girls than real ones<\/b>, he\u2019s rude and rather depressing. He only becomes more likeable after falling in love with Sora. The second half of the game is mainly about their relationship, sex life and him coming to terms with where his life is at the moment. Misaki, despite being another romantic option, only shows up twice and only has two ending; a good and a bad one. She also has no CG pictures which is a shame.<br><br>All of the CG is of Sora, and it\u2019s well done. She is drawn to be very busty and sexy and the artwork ranges from cute to downright sexual. There is no nudity in the game, save for one picture so everything is pretty tame. <b>There are sex scenes in the game<\/b> but they mainly consist of kissing and then fade to black moments. I don\u2019t mind this but for the amount of times sex does happen it makes the lack of CG for the scenes very apparently. The music is nice, there isn\u2019t a huge amount of variety however. There were a few times where the music seemed to be a bit creepy but nothing of the sort was actually happening which created unnecessary suspense.<br><br><i>Aozora Meikyuu<\/i> is a simple game, it won\u2019t take that long to finish one play-through and the other endings are easy to get after that. The story itself has a few dull moments, unfortunately having to do with a lot of the day-to-day interactions between Sora and Aoi. When the plot takes twists and turns, they\u2019re very interesting and well written but overall the game hoovers in neutral territory with not much happening. However, it\u2019s a good read with lots of choices to make and the choices are a timed which is a nice feature for this genre of game. <b>There is plenty of humor in the game<\/b>, especially at the irony of it being a visual novel about a shut-in who plays visual novels. The price is very reasonable for the game\u2019s length and there are also trading cards to collect, if you don\u2019t mind a bit of naughty humor I would recommend this game wholeheartedly.
