The two new beasts were not particularly impressive\u2014Caragaths are reskinned Caragors with the ability to be sneaky (which, admittedly, makes them a lot more fun than Caragors), and Wretched Graug are slightly more interested Graug with a ranged attack of their own. Wretched Graug are still overpowered and, for the most part, unfun, but unlike the original Graug, their attacks reduce their health a little every time you use them, so you'll have to think a little more strategically about when to stop and heal\u2014it's not a huge change, but it's for the better, I feel.<br>Last bit for negatives: this DLC pack only includes access to the Sea of Nurn; there's nothing set near the Black Gate. <br><br>Okay, so good bits, now: <br>It's still Shadow of Mordor, so if you enjoyed the base game, you'll probably enjoy this. It's light on story, which is perfectly fine with me\u2014we don't see much of Talion outside of his conversations with his huntsman friend, which means that most of his dialogue is restricted to snarky banter; that's a huge improvement over Grimdark Manpain Warrior Basically Bat Man But Dead. I understand the need to get across how horrible he feels over the loss of his family\u2014that would have been traumatic for anyone but a complete psychopath\u2014but there's establishing a character, and then there's establishing a cardboard cutout of a character with a voicebox that screams \u201cVENGEANCE\u201d and \u201cLOOK AT MY EMOTIONS\u201d at regular intervals; Talion was firmly in the latter camp. So, uh. Yes; less story is good in this case.<br>The new beasts are definitely improvements over the old ones, in that the Caragaths aren't glorified fast travel for people who don't want to use fast travel and the Wretched Graugs aren't quite as boring as their vanilla counterparts. The thing that really stood out to me, though, was the ability to summon (via bait) and Brand (via Wraith Burn) hordes of Ghuls. There's something immensely satisfying about being followed around by a small horde of tiny monsters. This ability isn't unlocked until late in the campaign, unfortunately. <br>The new Orcs, er. They have personailty. That's all I can really say about them. You might not be. This is a very \u201ctake it or leave it\u201d pack.
