why not check out <i>The Wardrobe<\/i>, the story of a skeleton, friendship and plums? This game offers a unique story told through a point and click adventure game narrative with eye-catching artwork and hip music. If you enjoy classic adventure games from the 90\u2019s and some laughs you should enjoy this game. I will say, however, the humor might not be to everyone\u2019s fancy. While I found nothing in the dialogue offensive, it is clear not all of the jokes will hit their mark and some seem dated. That, however, does not deter from an all-around solid game.<br><br>The story centers around a teenage boy named Skinny, which is probably a nickname but this is never clarified. He\u2019s hanging out with his friend, Ronald, and ends up eating a few plums, which he\u2019s allergic to, and dies. No one knew about this allergy, and this comes as a schock, so much so his friend is traumatized to the point where he doesn\u2019t speak anymore and blames himself. The whole goal of the game is for the now-skeletal Skinny to talk to his buddy Ronald and help him forgive himself, but that\u2019s easier said than done since he waited too long to do it and is now on a time crunch. But Ronald\u2019s family moves and most of the game involves Skinny tracking him down in the most convoluted way possible. At its heart, it\u2019s a very sweet story but most of the things Skinny goes through are wacky and cartoonish. The writing is well done, it\u2019s full of jokes and the quips of a main character with a sharp tongue. It\u2019s full of cultural references, especially in the background but some jokes may not hit their mark for all players.<br><br>Since this is a point and click game, you mainly use your mouse to get through the story. You can click on an item and either look at it, pick it up, use it or talk to it and Skinny has lots of interesting dialogue depending on what you choose to do. People you can usually look at and talk to only, which makes sense. You also have an inventory system to hold items and you can also combine them there as well. Move around was fine, sure Skinny won\u2019t run but he walks swiftly enough so transitioning airings didn\u2019t seem sluggish. You also gain a map at a certain point in the game which allows you to teleport around town. The game also has puzzles to solve and this is where I became frustrated. Some of the solutions struck me as illogical. The game offers no guidance on what to do next, what items to use or combine, nothing. Some things made enough sense to figure it out without any help but honestly, I ended up using a guide to get through the game. Maybe I\u2019m not very good at adventure games but it doesn\u2019t seem intuitive to dip a red chili in green paint to make it a \u201cjalapeno\u201d, nor does it seem useful.<br><br>In the graphics department, the game does very well. Everything is very colorful and vibrant, despite the darker nature of the playing as a poor dead teen everything seems pretty happy. The characters are all unique and memorable, playing off well of the main character. The scenery is highly detailed and full of lots of Easter eggs and jokes. I was taken aback by the sheer number of hidden gems in the backgrounds and I\u2019m certain I missed many of them. The game is fully voice acted and it\u2019s done very well. I had no problem with any of the voice acting, even for the most minor of characters. There is also good music to accompany this package. Is it something I\u2019d buy on a soundtrack? No -but it fit the game well and helped with the overall experience.<br><br>I enjoyed playing <i>The Wardrobe<\/i> but I didn\u2019t enjoy needing to use a guide constantly. The game has Steam trading cards and achievements, but the achievements can also be very confusing. Without using a guide for them, you will probably miss at least half. Some are story related, others aren\u2019t too hard to get by dumb luck or chance but some are just really out there. If you\u2019re not concerned with achievements, however, this won\u2019t be too much of an issue for you. Overall, is this the best adventure game? No. It\u2019s fun and has a good story but I\u2019d probably say grab it on sale if you\u2019re looking to give it a try.<br><br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">My Rating<\/div><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li>Must
