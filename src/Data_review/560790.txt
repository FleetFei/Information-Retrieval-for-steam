Red Spider: Vengeance (link: <a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"http:\/\/store.steampowered.com\/app\/537130)\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_0\">http:\/\/store.steampowered.com\/app\/537130)<\/a>, and the game would be much more enjoyable if you played that first since it builds on its predecessor. It's a visual novel, so the game lives and dies based on its story, so that\u2019s what this review will focus on.<br><br>Red Spider 2 opens up with the protagonist, Lily, killing a corrupt police officer in order to protect an innocent bystander (an event with consequences that served as the driving force for the first game's story) and the game deals with what Lily does as she tries to escape a death squad out to capture and possibly kill her - and while there are a few subplots (which mostly change depending on which of the six routes you choose) that help reinforce the themes of the main story, that\u2019s what Red Spider 2\u2019s plot revolves around.<br><br>Instead of placing more importance on the plot and overall events, the game relies on its characters (who are impressively easy to get emotionally invested to, considering the short length of each route) to move the plot forward, which makes sense since this is Lily\u2019s story and the player wouldn\u2019t care about the story if they didn\u2019t care about the characters first. Most of the cast consists of new characters not found in Red Spider, although there are a couple from the previous games that make appearances as well. The majority of the cast are based on existing archetypes, but are subverted in some way (such as how the strong sense of justice that Sam uses for his work contrasts with inadvertent injustice in his personal life). <br><br>It\u2019s worth noting that, as a prequel, Red Spider 2\u2019s plot does a good job at lending additional nuance to its predecessor and connects with it very well. Often, prequels often don\u2019t build on the story they\u2019re preceding very well and there\u2019s an odd disconnect, or they might even ruin the previous stories retroactively (for example, Valkyrie Profile 2 and arguably Star Ocean 3 both trivialise the events of the games that came before them), but Red Spider 2 thankfully avoids this by showing how characters from the original Red Spider got to where they are (although this is sometimes indirect, as with Shawn and Luk, who are not referenced by their original names). Red Spider 2 does have a \u201ccanon\u201d route by virtue of being a prequel, but some of the ties to the original game are only found on those non-canon routes, so the game doesn\u2019t invalidate or waste your time if you don\u2019t make the canon choice.<br><br>The game also has quite a few features that make it more convenient to replay once you\u2019ve finished a route. On the load screen, there\u2019s a route map that shows you which routes you\u2019ve already completed and, if you click one of the branching points, you can load the game at that point and see the consequences of the choice you didn\u2019t take before, which means you don\u2019t have to replay the game from the beginning to view the consequences of the other choice (a feature that would be nice for WRPGs to take into account with the amount of decisions they have). There is also a trivia mode that explains the inspiration for certain aspects of the narrative and a sound test mode to listen to the music.<br><br>Speaking of the game\u2019s music, most of the soundtrack consists of public domain tracks (which are fine and fit the tone of the story well), but there\u2019s also quite a bit of original music, composed by Yasunori Shiono (the main composer of the Lufia series, and also a former co-worker of Motoi Sakuraba) and Yukio Nakajima (the composer for Energy Breaker and Puzzles &amp; Dragons). These tracks are very good and fit the story as well, but a drawback of them fitting the tone of the story is that they\u2019re a bit difficult to distinguish from each other (the high usage of the piano might have been a contributing factor for this).<br><br>As for complaints or things that the developer could have improved, the game has a significant amount of typos and grammatical errors, and these are all quite noticeable because of the large text size. The text always remains understandable despite these errors (you might expect a free game to have more errors because of the lack of budget, even), and the quality of the translation is good despite the game being free, so this doesn\u2019t have a huge impact on the game.<br><br>To some people, the \u201cromance\u201d aspect of the game might also be a bit off-putting since Steam has a lot of visual novels that focus on that and players (presumably) would have hoped for something different in the Red Spider series, but this aspect doesn\u2019t harm the game (and it could be seen as a good point since it helps humanise members of the underworld and make them easier to relate to), so this isn\u2019t something that the game should be avoided for.<br><br>Red Spider 2: Exiled is free (as in, there\u2019s no microtransactions) and it\u2019s not a waste of your time (unless you dislike reading, but why would you still be reading my review, then?), so I'd recommend at least the first
