 AI has shown the capability to flank however the appearance of rushing the player is apparent.  Aliens do have the ability to melee as well as stasis the player so I won't hold AI rushing against the game.  Aliens can and do flank though they are not the brightest bulbs and will get themselves killed rather easily when isolated.   Boss fights are numerous but straight forward not showing much imagination.<br><br>The game has three difficulty modes each increasing in challenge.  I started with the middle difficulty but initially my life lasted as long as a Gastrotrich, so normal it was as my mood was for limiting restarts as much as possible given the game's propensity to kill you.<br><br>Story elements in the form of recordings are spread around the levels but I only found one third of the content. Whether I missed their obvious placement by chance or because they were hidden is unknown though the story can be ignored from the small portion I heard.  Bonus mini pyramids are also strewn about, three per level but again I never completed a set.  The pyramids add to your score as Alien Rage awards points during game-play and includes leader-boards as well. <br><br>Secondary ammo is also strewn about.  They look like suitcases so they aren't hard to miss although initially I wasn't sure what they were which brings us to the weapons.  An important part of any shooter.   The weapons are imaginative.  You get to carry only two plus a revolver.  There is a high reliance on secondary fire in order to implement much needed crowd control.  Primary ammo is picked up through gun pick ups as a dropped gun retains it's ammo or by walking over the same gun as you possess.   Weapons in the game consist of both alien and human.  Human weapons consist of differing semi automatic rifles, a sniper rifle, mentioned revolver, shotgun and rocket launcher.  Sounds and reads standard however each weapon has an important secondary fire mode as mentioned.  <br><br>The rocket launcher, for example, when equipped will affect your sprinting ability and to use or un-equip the launcher the full animation must be run through making the weapon a bit strategic and difficult to use, a welcome feature considering the weapon's ability to quickly change the battlefield.  Which reminds me of enemy hit-boxes which I found to be well implemented.  I can remember shooting a rocket through enemy legs and cursing myself for lack of careful aiming.   <br><br>The alien arsenal consists of energy weapons and is somewhat more limited.  No rocket launcher for example but the weapons are fun to use.  The alien rifle for instance has no reloads, however the weapon will overheat, again ensuring the player makes strategic use of the weapon.  The alien shotgun is very cool and very powerful, the cool anti gravity  secondary fire helps immensely.  To summarize the S in FPS, the shooting is exceptional, it's explosive and fun.<br><br>Some reviewers have panned the levels as being too generic.  So what.  The game looks and sounds great.  You feel Alien Rage, your feel of the game, both visual and auditory is significant.  Many of the levels have nice set pieces and it's great to see a publisher like City Interactive enable their development team to build a game with strong immersive environmental elements.  Great explosions not only permeate the combat but play a central role.  Graphically the game is exceptional though performance will vary but not enough to detract from the game.   <br><br>Lastly I will address the utter disaster that is <a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=371891267\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\" >the Alien Rage checkpoint system<\/a>.   Wait, it is not a system.  Systems by definition imply some sort of systematic routine.  No, the check-pointing, no, make that the checkpoint restart logic in Alien Rage is not just buggy.   I've come to the conclusion that it is unfinished.  There is no other reason for checkpoint restarts missing assets, many of which will prevent you from continuing the game.  Explosives for blowing apart levels to continue, needed secondary ammunition for combat , platforms not reset so you're left looking like a shopper waiting for a mall elevator.   <br><br>I've found that after each death, instead of using the checkpoint continue, I had to exit to the main menu and continue from there.  It is beyond my wildest imagination how the developers obviously knowing that there checkpoint restart system was broken could not of just rewired their checkpoint code to use the continuation logic from the main menu.  From the continue on the main menu the game loaded correctly every time.  As the game loads very fast,  circumventing the intended method for checkpoint restarts is not a significant issue.<br><br>If you enjoy a good FPS then Alien Rage presents itself as a very challenging
