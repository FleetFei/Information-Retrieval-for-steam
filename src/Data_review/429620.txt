class=\"postedDate\">Posted: January 1\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t<div class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDungeon Rushers is quite nice dungeon crawler, though definitely closer to coffee break one than hardcore ones like Elminage or Wizardry series. DR is somewhat niche, because it has exploration with top down perspective, and combat with side view, while majority of crawlers have both in first person perspective. <br><br>Compared to many crawlers you also don't create your characters in DR, you instead get 10 characters as you progress through story, which in most cases correspond with common rpg classes.  Each one of them has three combat skills (except one who has four) and up to three camp\/exploration skills.  Characters are varied, (especially later ones when you get druid who has two forms or mummy mage, which uses hp instead mana for his skills) and each one, maybe except one which is slightly weaker, are useful. Each character has talent tree divided into three branches, and by investing points in them you can get their skills upgraded or stats increased. You have only enough points to buy maybe 2\/3 of all upgrades at most, so you need to select what you really want, it also makes different builds of the same character possible. I would say though that are choices are equally useful or strong, in fact some things are rather useless.<br><br>Exploration is okay, there is plenty of enemies (there are no random encounters in this game, only set ones), traps, rooms with treasures and shrines which can bless or curse you. Sometimes you need to find switches or keys, the game also supports using passwords to open the doors, but no dungeon in campaign uses this. Fighting is also rather good. You use party of maximum five characters against enemy party of maximum six. Dungeons in game are divided in four large areas: castle with skeletons, mountains with goblins, jungle with amazons and pyramids with mummies. Each arena has about seven different enemies, so enemy pool is not that big - some games like Elminage have more enemy types in one dungeon than DR in entire game. While you have quite plenty of skills, not many of them are that useful, I have mostly used just basic attacks and buffs, offensive attacks are mixed bag - some are good, and some are weak or have occasional use. For example one of the attacks can be upgraded to remove enemy buffs - sounds useful, but there is only one enemy in game which actually gives buffs. Rest of them gain buffs (or rather one buff, critical hit chance increase) by passive skills, and those can't be removed.<br><br>You can give some items to your characters, which can be bought, crafted, or looted from fights and treasures. Item selection starts very weak, but later in game there is quite some items to select from. But the fact that you have only few characters, and many ways to get items make both shop and workshop somewhat not very useful. Workshop has levels for every item category, and crafting items yields you exp used for leveling. You need several items crafted to level up, and since you don't need that many items, you will have to craft random things just to get to higher level.  Shop has eight random items or crafting components, and refreshes after completing a dungeon. You will mostly want to just buy components, since first items are rather expansive, and second it's better to craft them and get exp. This makes gold almost useless, it can be used for gambling, from which you can get special potions. Problem is that many dungeons have challenges which restrict you from using potions, and when there are no challenges you can (or should be able to) destroy everything without them. Also plenty of items give increased magical resistance, but there are not many enemies with magical attacks, and the ones who have them, in most cases have also skills that resurrect or summon new enemies, so you will target them first anyway.<br><br>Speaking of challenges, each normal dungeon has three, after completing dungeon with them you unlock heroic mode of that dungeon. This is something which is not explained very well in game. You can complete those challenges at any time, and heroic mode is not that harder. It has enemies with increased stats, but also better loot, including some unique items that drop only form that mode, and some of them are way stronger that anything that can be crafted by the time you get them. This too makes shop and workshop inferior to looting.<br><br>Game in general has rather rough start, and can be hard for some people in beginning, and later it gets easier. It's mostly because you don't have full party early on, and also because of this game unique approach to healing. There is no classical cleric character in this game, the closest to that is herbalist which you get near the end of second act. The only way early to heal is to use potions, and later vampire character buff. Those potions are somewhat rare, and if you make bad decision you can easily run out of  them, and you will have to grind to get them. There was also certain hard encounter early on, but that was nerfed already.<br><br>The game has also function to create your own dungeons, which is quite nice, but this is only addition to dungeon crawling, not game main focus.<br><br>Currently the game is in somewhat rough state. There are bugs, there are crashes. Some skills are bugged and don't work, shop is bugged too. Inventory only recently got sane item sort by category, and not by tier. The developer is fixing those bugs through, and that's good. The developer is also making mobile version, so let's hope he will fix pc version alongside it. The game is enjoyable and worth getting, though you might want to get it on sale.
