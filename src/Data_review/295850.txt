I doubt; I fear; I think strange things, which I dare not confess to my own soul.\u201d \u2015 Bram Stoker, <i>Dracula<\/i><\/b><br><br>I'd like to start this review with a confession: when this game was recommended to me by a friend, as a most successful visual novel I might enjoy, this wasn't what I was expecting. I got hooked up after the first playthrough and went on full exploration mode here. I really do like visual novels, and I always had a tendency to enjoy Gothic narrative when it's well-done so why not a combination of both? Ladies and gentlemen, I present to you <i>Vlad the Impaler<\/i>.<br><br>Vaguely based on Bram Stoker's timeless Gothic classic <i>Dracula<\/i> and mostly taking its factual background from the Transylvanian-born Voivode Vlad III Dracula of Wallachia - <i>Vlad the Impaler<\/i> is a short visual novel centering around an educated traveler who had the luxury of calling the vile count &quot;an old friend&quot;. Our adventurer receives a letter from the nobleman, urging him to visit the city of Istanbul and discover whatever vile force of darkness may be holding the city in its clutches. He claims that even the Sultan himself wouldn't set foot on the street, knowing full-well the supernatural danger waiting at every corner. With this plea at hand, we travel to Istanbul to either save the city, or die trying as any doe-eyed, good intentioned adventurer would do. Yet, we are to remember that once we gaze onto hell, the hell gazes back.<br><br>This is when things get an interesting turn. For the game mechanics, we are to choose one of the three classes as the game starts: the explorer, the soldier and the mage. All these classes will be relying on 3 of the 6 relevant stats to pass some stat checks in each episode, and eventually face the final boss at the end. You have 15 days - or turns - to raise these stats based on your choice actions and various encounters within the city. Each encounter takes place somewhere in the city, and may result with stat raises or decreases depending on your choices. Choices in these encounters are not various but they are interrelated to several investigation branches with decent narrative.<br><br>There is a 7th semi-relevant stat - which would be the charm. It is mostly an irrelevant stat, yet raising it will present you the only peaceful solution at the end of the game. Aside the stat balance, most of your choices would influence your morality, considering it is a horrendously vile setting with the worst kind of creatures roaming around. Based on your moral standing at the end of chapter 4, your class will automatically chose a sub-class and attain a 4th relevant class skill. Some encounters would be only available if you have visited and solved prior ones, so the game presents some replay value too. If you snatched this game for the purpose of achievement hunting, you'll be replaying it a lot anyhow - since the appearance of legendary weapons are purely luck related, making relevant achievements quite stubborn to fetch.<br><br>Considering this whole game dynamic, <i>Vlad the Impaler<\/i> might be labelled as a visual novel with light RPG elements. Not grand, not spectacular yet somewhat satisfying. Yet, atmospherically it is not lesser than being majestic. Artworks consist of pencil drawn backgrounds, oriental city silhouettes and various mystical depictions of a medieval Istanbul, with the darkest possible elements of horror harbored into one. Music is an eerie beauty, elevating the whole setting at each turn with dreadful suspension. Narrative, aside a couple of overlookable grammar mistakes and some minor disconnections in the event flow, is dark, vile and elegant; brutally effective at describing every unsettling detail in each scene. Sure this effect fades off after your 5th playthrough or so, but it is worth the experience anyhow.<br><br>At the end of the day, <i>Vlad the Impaler<\/i> is a one dollar game for you to take 3-4 hours of enjoyment out of it. If you do not expect a miracle, knowing full-well the core dynamic and genre of the game, it is worth every cent and your time to enjoy a short yet dark adventure.
