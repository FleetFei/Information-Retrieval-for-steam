But, like, *if you try hard enough*, you can win it back? The combat in Cloud Pirates is completely totally utterly lacking in that. There's no slippery slope, there's no winback, it's *utterly linear*.<br><br>Somewhere embedded within the damage calculations, abilities, and the punch of the weapons in most games, is a sort of a music, a chaotic rhythm of big and small gains and losses, which creates a dramatic score to whatever you're doing. Well, this game feels like that all the time to me. Abilities can't save it. Tons of different ship classes don't help. It simply lacks punch and a real sense of danger. There's no urgency.<br><br>So, why do I recommend it?
