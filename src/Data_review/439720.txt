Galaxy: A Review <\/div><b>Game Detail<\/b><br>Developer:Anarkis Gaming<br>Genre:Strategy, 4X-lite, Trading, Space<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">So What Is This Game About?<\/div>Unending Galaxy [UG] is a 1 man game about intergalactic trading, empire building, trading fleet management all in a top down 2D view. It basically plays like the X series except well its 2D, the pace is faster, and it could use some polish in certain part of the gameplay.<br><br>Anyways lets get on with the review! There's not really much to talk about in the intro section.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Graphics<\/div>Lets get this out of the way first, it uses asset packs from the AI war series.  So don't go in expecting fancy graphical settings and so on. But at least its not terrible to look at for one thing. Ok it might be a little be bad if you're not used to it but for a heavy space simulation game like this graphics would be a little be a little be lower on the list.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Sound<\/div>Just your average typical synth space music for the ambient that you might want to mute off and put on something else after 5 continous hours of pay because eventually they grate on your ears like cheese grater at the end of it. Meanwhile the average sound effects are just pew pew pew for guns and lasers and muted boom for explosion.<br><br>Nothing really impressive here but hey it works. Kinda.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Performance<\/div>And here's my favourite part of it. You see you can adjust most things from interpolation, resolution, some settings to set compatibility and increase FPS but if you happen to have an anamolous configuration like me the game will be very finicky. As in it will randomly throw out issues at you at random time. I had sprites corrupted, game crashes, spike lags, and so on. It even hates my mobile Radeon graphic card for some reason so I had to disable that and use my intel integrated graphics.<br><br>But when it does work it works very well at 60 fps and almost no lag. But do note the most important part of your configuration will be your processor. The graphics may not be pretty but a lot of process are running constantly in the background and for the largest map it taxed my i5 processort to 100% usage for all 4 core pretty much all the time. Smaller map while... less fun do offer slightly better performances.<br><br>That said it does depend on your final configuration setup so you might get a lot more mileage with it than me with the game.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay<\/div>The gameplay in UG is pretty simple. At the start of the game you can choose a list of preset maps [that are pretty resource heavy] or from the randomly generated maps that way less fun than the preset maps but will not set your computer on fire from the sheer amount of background processing. Then you choose your starting type ship type and empire affinity. You pretty much immediately start off as an empire in this game and everybody will treat you as such [even though you might only have 1 ship].<br><br>So assuming you pick the default starting merchant class there are a few things you can do in UG. First you can configure your drone ship to start trading supplies for you autonomously while you lay back and watch as the money increases in the background or you can also do a few jobs while the drone does its job that ranges from killing people, ferrying supplies, to destroying bases and these missions are dependant on which faction and base you pick them off from. Drones [and other ships] can also be set to autonomously patrol, hunt for bounties, and a few other things and task that you can set them with.<br><br>You can interact with the faction and improve your standing with them by doing missions but faction relationship can be improve simply bribing a lot of money if you wish although there's a hard cap to how much you can do this.<br><br>Maps are divided into sectors, wormholes provide quick transportation from one point to another while jump drive provide localize instant transportation between close nearby sectors. Late in the game you can make your own wormhole but as of early point you will be most likely be <br><br>The end goal of this game can be freely decided by you but assuming you want to 'win' the game sort of to speak you need to obliberate every factionand come out on top. To do so you need money to build builder ships which will build space stations that allows other factions to trade with you and generate more income. Once you build up a sizeable base you can then start to build military bases and shipyards outside of enemy control point, this will allow you to finally claim a sector for your own and begin your first foothold in conquering the universe. Pumping out fleets of ships while setting their AI, declaring war on other factions and ruthlessly destroying would then be your main objective and at that point you can simply start building your own wormhole that will not allow others to travel into. A true empire so as to speak from a lowly beginning so as to speak. <br><br>That said money isnt too terribly hard to earn and a typical game will just be over in 5-8 hours [I had half the galaxy conquered in 5] which makes it a lot more fast pace that most other space empire simulation game out there. Being a trader only is also viable and fun since you have to acquire bodyguards and make your own trade and space stations to maximise the amount of profit that you will get.<br><br>Randomly to mix things up a few random event might happen to you and it is up to you to adapt to their coming.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Conclusion<\/div>At this present time UG is a completely playable game, you can start from a humble beginning and end as an empire and the speed in which you do so is rather addicting with few downtime in between. That said a lot of things could use a little bit more polish to it which includes the UI, the trade section, more automation stuff, and further faction interaction but that could all come afterward.<br><br>For now its a pretty fun space empire management game [especially the trading screen watching the money go up cha ching cha ching cha ching] and since its feature complete and fully playable at the moment that is all fine for now. Although, people who had never play this kind of game should note that the learning ladder is rather steep but once you are at the top its easy to do a checklist.<br><br>Forgot to mention, the game does have a built in game editor so you can make up your own sector and adjust parameters for the ships.
