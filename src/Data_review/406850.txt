Entertainment, releasing on PC, Android and iOS in July 2016. The game has a humorous take on the real time strategy genre, the same developers who brought us This War of Mine invites you to a magical world of Generia. <br><br>Whilst Crush Your Enemies is part of the RTS genre, it feels a little like a tower defense style game as well, I think this was because of the UI and some of the game mechanics which are on show though. The game is split across numerous levels which have three different objectives. The game has it\u2019s own gating mechanism, meaning in order to progress further in the story you\u2019ll need to complete a specific amount of objectives, which gives you good reason to replay previously completed levels, just to beat the extra objectives on offer. The objectives in each level will vary from straight up wiping your opponent off the map, to defending a building for an amount of time, or even something as simple as making sure you end the level with a certain number of units left. <br><br>The actual gameplay from CYE is square unit based; you have a select amount of units to begin the mission with, and you can turn them into different styles of units, simply by entering buildings, units can be guards, armored warriors, archers, or builders. These units count as your resources for building more units. You\u2019ll need to have a constant churn of units being built in order to combat your opponent\u2019s forces. It takes a little bit of skill to finely tune the amount of units for each task but it is very satisfying once you figure out the perfect balance. The game has a very well done tutorial that doesn\u2019t hand hold you throughout, but gives you plenty of helpful hints along the way when you encounter new mechanics. You learn as you play which is the best way of working these games. <br><br>On top of resource management for your units, you\u2019ll also get a number of consumables which can be used throughout a mission too, such as potions that ramp up units strengths, quick tents that allow you to build more units if you are in a little bit of a bind, and many more. These do become a little bit of a forgotten part of the gameplay for me though, as the UI has them hidden away in the top corner where you don\u2019t really see them unless you are specifically looking. I thought they could have been displayed a little clearer in the regular UI to allow players to take full advantage of them, rather than realizing at the end of the mission they just struggled on, that it could've been much easier with the use of the extra powers. The game\u2019s AI is very intuitive, it has a sufficient challenge level that you never feel like you are being completely out matched, but also you never find yourself just waltzing through missions effortlessly. <br><br>The art style on show here looks beautiful when you look at the graphical title images and character art, but the in game graphics a little bit basic. They do have a pixel art style that is definitely much better than the majority of games that try to pull off this look, but I do think they could have been made much more detailed. Like I said above, the UI could do with a few improvements to make it a little bit more accessible. <br><br>CYE also has fairly unmemorable sound effects and soundtracks. There is also quite a fair bit of text to read in between levels and the lack of voice acting is also quite clearly a missing factor. The comedy aspect of the game is lost ever so slightly because you don\u2019t get to be completely immersed into the world due to having to read the text yourself. It loses a little bit of it\u2019s umpf sadly. <br><br>The game does come with a long enough campaign that you won\u2019t be left short changed, not to mention a fairly active multiplayer scene too. I managed to get a handful of matches in multiplayer within seconds of opening the matchmaking system, and on top of the lengthy campaign, you are definitely going to get a title that\u2019ll hit the double figures mark in time. <br><br>I had no technical issues when playing, granted there isn\u2019t any options menu in order to change any settings, but that\u2019s fine since everything worked as I hoped it would anyway. I will for sure be downloading this game on my ipad for hours worth of content on the 10 hour flight I am going on next week. That being said though, the price of \u00a36.99 on PC is very fair given the amount of content and replayability you are getting from Crush Your Enemies. take you back to the glory days of a genre. I read this far too often. An all too common result is an itch that remains unscratched and a subpar game in and of itself. I believe that some developers think that as long as they use retro-styled graphics it will suffice. When I discovered that the opening words of Crush Your Enemies synopsis I was pessimistic. It reads \u2018Go back to the awesome glory days of Real-Time Strategy games\u2019. Further, it proclaims that it \u2018has everything you love about the genre.\u2019 This is a hard sales pitch. Thankfully, I needn\u2019t have been. Vile Monarch have nailed it in their first game since their free to play insult simulator title, Oh\u2026Sir!<br><br>Crush Your Enemies is a genuine throwback to the real-time strategy genre. It certainly offers everything that RTS fans love \u2013 planning, unit variety, resource management, territory control and most significantly on the fly strategy. These are all important components when considering the whole. All of them have been carefully considered<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay<\/div><br>The gameplay is addictive. The game takes place in rounds and you are given a number of various challenges \u2013 three on each level.  These challenges are fairly generic of the genre but they\u2019ve become that way because they work. You\u2019re going to see a lot of \u2018crush the enemy in X time,\u2019 \u2018survive X time,\u2019 \u2018take control of the enemy\u2019s territory\u2019 type challenges. They give each round a purpose. You have to complete the most basic challenge to advance to the game. What I enjoyed most were the survival rounds because they offered two times for you to beat but also a conquer their territory challenge.<br><br>You begin each round with a small amount of units. Generally these are your foot soldiers or peasants. You need to consider how you will build your army. This is achieved through your housing building which will add units to your army as long as you have at least one person at home. The more people you have at home the faster units will develop. You can have a maximum of fifty units at any one time.<br><br>You have specialist buildings at your disposal that will give you different tools. For example, you can transform your standard units into warriors and archers by simply taking them to those allotted buildings. However, you cannot mix troops (e.g. archers and warriors may not be on the same tile). Be careful, however, as if you move your specialist troops onto a home unit they will transform into standard units. This tripped me up a couple of times.<br><br>Rounds are only a few minutes long at most making the game ideal for those who are time poor. However, I would also say that the shortness of the rounds is an overall advantage of the game itself. There is simply no time for the game to become boring and overly tedious. It allows players who do plan on completing the game to jump from round to round quickly.<br><br>Inventory management starts off small but does become a game changer later on. It\u2019s up to the player to determine how each item is best utilized. That\u2019s a great strength of the game. Almost every important thing in the game comes down to the player\u2019s decision.<br><br>The game controls extremely well. I only have one small criticism. I would like to see a minor improvement on pathing. The player, to a significant degree, controls the paths that their units take. However, it isn\u2019t always accurate. Given that the majority of rounds are a race against the clock a slight deviation can have future consequences. It\u2019s not something that happened frequently enough for it to be a large concern but it did happen.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Presentation<\/div><br>Those looking for a story to tie the game together are in for a treat. The decision to work with a barbarian setting and atmosphere is what makes the game for me. It could have so very easily been just a generic game. However, the comedic touch to the narration is fantastic. This generally takes place between levels but can and does occur during levels. The in-round narrative generally takes place before the rounds begin but there are occasional humor filled pop ups informing you of unlocked events and features.<br><br>Graphically, I really enjoyed the retro look to the game. The colors are bright and sharp. It\u2019s a case of Red Vs Blue. The maps have been well designed and are, by and large, clear. It achieves what it needs to do without being overtly spectacular. The utilization of the color palette is in keeping with the setting and the classic feel. The sound, on the other hand, is definitely a little generic. The music is thumping<br><br>I did run into a weird glitch where the game wouldn\u2019t allow me to pick a unit and move it. I\u2019m not sure of the reason why this glitch occurred only that it did a couple of times. Exiting the game and reloading fixed the error.<br><br>Finally, a word of warning on the humor. It is potty mouthed, sexual and not going to appeal to everybody. You do have the choice of skipping the dialogue. Therefore it may not be a game that you\u2019d allow younger games to play. Then again, there is far worse stuff on television that many children do watch.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Verdict<\/div><br>Crush Your Enemies is super addictive thanks in large part to a wonderful presentation. Those who demand to see innovation in every game that they play may feel disappointed. However, it doesn\u2019t need to reinvent the wheel. This is an easy recommendation for both fans of real-time strategy games and the casuals out there. It\u2019s a game that has something for everybody.
