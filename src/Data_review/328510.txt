Review<\/div>\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\t\t\t\t\t<div class=\"postedDate\">Posted: August 16, 2016\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t<div class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMonsters and Monocles has just been released into Steam Early Access. It is a steampunk twin stick shooter being developed by Retro Dreamer, a California-based game developer. Although this is their first game on Steam they have a history of creating games for mobile devices. Players who fear that this may be a mobile game can put their minds at ease as it has been developed as a PC game. Although it would seem like their ambitious title it shows a lot of promise.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Heavy Emphasis on Co-Op<\/div><br>One of the predominant features of the game is co-op play. Thus far I have spent time with two friends in local co-op mode while online is available as well. Personally, it seems like what makes co-op so much fun is the way in which the ruleset has been implemented. You get an opportunity to revive teammates. However, reviving teammates come at a cost as you have a shared pool of lives. As a result, this made our group seriously strategize on when the best time to revive a teammate was. Typically you would rush in and revive a teammate as soon as possible. However, because of the shared pool of lives we often took our time to do so. We generally cleared entire rooms before reviving a teammate.<br><br>The co-op mode is some of the most fun that I have had in any local co-op game. The game genuinely rewards smart and conscious team play and punishes those who want to go hero. I look forward to spending time in the online multiplayer. I can imagine quite a few people rage quitting and complaining of having poor teammates. The reality here is that the player rage quitting may be the poor teammate. My word of advice is to not be a hero but to work with your teammates even if they are less skilled than you. It's like a team sport. There isn't an I in 'team.'<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">A Note on Single-Player Mode<\/div><br>Despite a significant level of focus on the co-op mode, there is a single-player mode available to play. The single-player game, obviously, feels different to the co-op. There is an increased level of challenge (difficulty) in the single-player mode. Those of you who have played similar games in the twin stick shooter genre you will be prepared for the challenge that this mode brings.<br><br>I have found both modes to be enjoyable for different reasons. I love playing the game with friends who I can develop a level of cohesion with. When we die we've died as a result of our combined actions. When we've succeeded it has been through a joint effort. We've laughed, learned and bonded together. The single-player experience offers a rewarding challenge that feels as though it will only improve with continued development.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Weapons<\/div><br>My favorite part of the game is the variety of weapons that are already available. It's a challenge finding out what the best weapons are for your individual playstyle. This is because there are some that will strengthen your playstyle whereas others weaken it. If you get the right weapon for your style you almost feel as though you're overpowered. However, getting the wrong one can quickly see you become a liability as a teammate or die in single-player mode. Consequently, until you have had a chance to study every weapon type and how it effects your style you'll face a risk-reward system.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Playability<\/div><br>Monsters and Monocles already plays very well. However, it is not nearly as polished as similar games such as Enter the Gungeon. For a game just recently released into early access, this is, in my opinion, acceptable. But there are flaws and weaknesses to the game that the developers are well aware of. Mechanics such as dodge definitely need work. As of writing, I don't believe that you can change the controller options from the settings menu. Every time I choose that option I can hear the sound that I have pressed but I am not taken to the next menu.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Is it Steamified?<\/div><br>Not yet. The game has achievements on the Steam store but they have yet to be added to the early access form of the game. Steam user Batmobilly asked this on the discussion forums and received the following developer response:<br><br>Achievements are planned for, but won't be in until closer to the full release - we know what we want a lot of them to be, but while mechanics are still in flux it doesn't seem right to have them unlock at this point.<br><br>The game has yet to have Steam trading cards included. However, as it is an early access title these are generally added closer to the retail release date as a further motivation for potential buyers.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Verdict<\/div><br>Monsters and Monocles is a tremendously enjoyable experience. Both single-player and co-op modes are developed to a significant degree. The game is very playable. Of course, being an early access title it isn't as polished as some of the instantly recognizable games in the genre but that's to be expected. Consequently, the asking price may be considered a little high when compared to titles such as Enter the Gungeon but I believe that the price will be well worth it as development continues. For now, it's a pixelly rogue-like with a Victorian Steampunk flavour. There are three main zones (with three more mysteriously silhouetted for later updates), each with two levels to fight through, a shop, and a boss fight. Once you've completed all three, you loop through them and they get progressively more difficult - a projectile that does mild damage in your first loop can one-shot you in loop three or four.<br><br>You collect different weapons as you go in each run, but always start with just a basic revolver. In terms of other loot, you have upgradable relics, which you can use up to three of at once; these can enhance your health, speed, the effectiveness of various weapons, etc. There's also power-ups that often do one thing which helps you, and one which... well, doesn't. It's a bit of a tip of the hat to Binding of Isaac in that you don't know what those do until you've picked them up, so for a newbie, you can spend the rest of your run cursing yourself for picking one up. And of course, there's coins too - for spending in shops and upgrading relics. Once your relics are fully upgraded, there isn't much left to spend that on, but it's early access so that could well change.<br><br>The fighting does take a bit of strategy; generally the more powerful your weapon, the quicker it will overheat and need to cooldown before you can use it again. You can carry up to two weapons and switch between them, but even then, when you're faced with a large group of mobs, you need to judge how many you can take down, whether you'll need to kite some away, or retreat for cooldowns, depending on your weapon choice. You can't just go in all guns blazing and button mash your way into finishing the room (trust me, I've tried, it doesn't end well). There thankfully isn't an ammo mechanic, you have infinite shots as long as your weapon hasn't overheated, so this is a nice trade-off for that. You can shoot clumsily, but you need to be able to dodge and kite to make up for it.<br><br>The key thing with this game for me is whether you play it alone or with friends in online co-op. Single-player can quickly feel like a slog, the looping aspect tires quickly, and the cooldown mechanic becomes incredibly frustrating without back-up (especially if like me you're a complete potato). I mostly play this on co-op with two friends while on voice chat, and I cannot stress how much that makes this game for me. To be able to have a varied team for the mobs the game throws at you makes the entire experience immeasurably more fun, seeing someone's health bar dip while they scream at a crowd of mobs so loud it makes their mic crackle? That's what makes this game great. It works well with the cooldown mechanic too, so you can take on the hoards interchangeably; your own guns cooling down while someone else keeps the mobs at bay with their own array of badassery.<br><br>Honestly if you're only planning to play this on single player, I could easily understand why that would tip your review into a negative one, especially at the current price. You'd probably prefer Binding of Isaac, or Enter the Gungeon, which both have a lot more finished content for their price tags.
