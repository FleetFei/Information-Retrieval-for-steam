However, the core mechanics are solid, and this game has some good potential. Hopefully the devs will keep improving on it, though it's not marked as Early Access right now...<br><br>Nature's Wrath is a wave-based tower defense game, and combines good features from several other VR titles into one package. You are defending a tower but have free locomotion around the area, allowing you to take different vantage points or run around to collect item drops. You have several fantasy weapons - a sword, a bow, a fire rod, and a trap-setting device. I love archery in VR but it's nice to have some extra weapons as well.<br><br>Enemies drop mana, as well as different types of arrows, and occasionally traps. The different types of arrows (flaming, exploding, scattershot) are a nice touch and the weapon-switching system is very well designed. One thing I particularly like is that you can use your mana to heal the tower (hold the left trigger) or to fuel the fire rod. The fire rod is completely devastating, but it burns mana like crazy, so you have to balance the use of it carefully.<br><br>The graphics are fairly good, and although everything was probably bought off the Unity Asset Store they made good choices and the end result is pleasing. The environment in particular looks good, though the chairs are as tall as you are... The enemy models don't seem to exactly match the art style of the environment, but no big deal.<br><br>My biggest concern is that the enemies mostly just walk straight towards the tower and I'm worried this will cause the game to get stale in the long run. Other minor complaints are that the sound effects are lackluster and the controls are completely baffling at first and don't always follow conventions. For example, to collect pickups, you have to wave your weapon hand through them (not your off hand). You press buttons in menus by squeezing the grip. Huh? <br><br>If you like archery games and tower defense games I definitely recommend this. If you're a little on the fence, you might wait a few weeks to see if the devs are planning to do any updates.
