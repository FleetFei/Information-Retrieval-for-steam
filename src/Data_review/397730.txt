class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRescue Love Revenge is an episodic runner-platformer action adventure with a spaghetti western theme. If you\u2019re a fan of the types of movies and games where the hero, in this case, a cowboy, must rescue the girl then the basic premise of the game should hold some appeal.<br><br>The game\u2019s first act, the remainder is to be released in an episodic nature when each act is completed, was recently released into Steam Early Access. In fact, the first act may also evolve into something more than the opening two chapters. I am led to believe that there are plans to add further gameplay styles and incorporate other genres into the games, therefore, please realize that this is a very much in progress insight rather than a full-on review.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay<\/div><br>The gameplay is very typical of the runner-platformer style. For those who have not previously played a game in this genre, your basic objective is to get to the end of each level as quickly as possible. Along the way, you must maneuver to avoid obstacles and collect as many coins as possible.<br><br>You will want to be mindful of this last fact. You must collect a thousand coins before you can unlock the second chapter of the act. Getting these coins is not as easy as you might imagine it to be. I almost guarantee that all but the most expert of players will miss a lot of coins on every level. I\u2019m averaging about 45 coins per level.<br><br>Gameplay in each of the levels that I have played thus far has been quite challenging but also enjoyable. The game has been a good test of my skills as a player. I gain a measure of reward whenever I complete each level. The size of each level begins at about half a minute with the longer levels lasting over a minute. Unfortunately, I don\u2019t believe that there are checkpoints in the longer levels as every time I have died in a longer level it has required a complete level restart.<br>The biggest downside to the game is that it tends to feel unfair at times. There are places that you cannot possibly know if you need to jump, double jump or allow yourself to fall to get to the next part of the level. I am not a fan of this as it adds almost needless repetition to the levels. <br><br>This repetition requires you to possess the ability to memorize the layout of each level otherwise you will find yourself dying at the same point repeatedly. This can prove to be aggravating to the point of rage inducing.<br><br>As well as collecting coins to unlock the second chapter those who enjoy unlocking cosmetics and boosters will be delighted to know that there is an in-game store. These purchase options are available through the inventory menu.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Presentation<\/div><br>The developers at Hatchlight and Trepid Studios have a thorough understanding of the right color palette required to capture the atmosphere that they wanted. The game is pleasing on the eyes despite or perhaps because of its simplicity. The first time that I looked at the game feels a bit like what I would imagine Minecraft would look like if it were a platformer. Some of the pieces of the background look like something out of Tetris.<br><br>The soundtrack is suitable although it is not memorable.<br><br>I haven\u2019t run across any bugs in either the gameplay or the presentation to this point in time.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Steam Features<\/div><br>There are two Steam achievements. To unlock these all you need to do is complete all of the levels in each of the two available chapters.<br><br>Rescue Love Revenge also incorporates Steam Leaderboards into the game. This will give competitive players a reason to get the highest score possible.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Verdict<\/div><br>The opening two chapters are a lot of fun and I can recommend them to anyone who enjoys platformer and runner style games. Studios and the publisher, Rich Pine. It is presented in a voxel-based atmosphere with 18-bit styled graphics, but a fully two-dimensional platforming world. <i>Rescue Love Revenge<\/i> takes place in a western setting that conveys new, interesting ideas I have not seen in other platformers I have received the opportunity to play. The main character is a cowboy, a western strangler, but, instead of guns in his arsenal, on the side of his holster, at the start of the game, we are given dual pickaxes. <br><br>     We would all expect that these pickaxes would be capable of slicing enemies in half, right down the middle. Certain enemies are sliced, while others are unable to be dismembered as effectively. The first set of enemies are ninjas that are powerful dismembering the head of the player if we blunder.  However, later on, there are creeper-type enemies in which we dismember their heads by hitting them with a bottle of ale, completely dismembering their head with light-hearted exuding gore undulating with one clear stroke.<br>     <div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay<\/div>     The gameplay mechanics will keep everyone coming back for more once the full game release occurs. According to the developer, they will be adding many new game modes. Right now, the only mode we have by default is called the ' retro' mode. According to Hatchlight, Trepid Studios there will be multiplayer added at a later date, also, a mode with modern-type platformer graphics, controls, and levels. Offering a variety of different graphics from old-school to modern makes this platformer a one-fits-all, jack-of-all-trades. It is exceedingly fast-paced; it has more momentum used than any <i>Sonic<\/i>, <i>Mario<\/i> game I have ever played to date. Everything in the gameplay blends in perfectly, the props used to spice up and alter the momentum on the fly, timing and everything between such as enemies using swords coming to kill you while you are saving a princess ( chapter 1) or your donkey in the desert ( chapter 2).<br><br>     <b>Early Access Analyzation of Gameplay<\/b><br>          Gameplay and controls are the two quintessential elements of a platformer, the presentation which includes the graphics are the smaller things making up, the larger whole; the primary difference between a platformer\u2019s genotype( external characteristics for show such as the presentation, graphics, background setting, platforms, and characters). Then, the resulting byproduct, or the phenotype ( how all of the elements flow together to make one consistent whole; the mechanics, gameplay, controls, platform spacing, timing, memorization of map layouts, trial-and-error mechanics, and any props used).  The ' retro' mode uses tactical objects placed at the precise angle, in the right location and with the right effects ( i.e. cactus of different sizes used as obstacles to spring the cowboy backward.) Platforms non-evenly spread out in later levels that become incriminating, and finally, after the first chapter, TNT that causes knockdown and fallback, but if used right, can propel our cowboy character forward to reach new heights that require careful timing. Rescue Love Revenge causes you to remember map layouts by experimenting with trial and error ( i.e. remembering the gaps on every map, perfect spacing, and hitting all the perfect angles to land on the right platforms). <br><br>        I grew up in the golden era of flash games, the early 2000's. When I was in middle school, during breaks between classes, every student who was a male considered cool at the time, were playing online flash games. This was a long time before Indie ( independent studios) made games for purchase through the digitalization of games in recent years. Through my journey through <i>Rescue Love Revenge<\/i> so far, I became sad knowing that eventually online flash games will become a thing of the past. <br><br>     Flash games were one of the only positives out of my childhood within what I considered my best years before I had to take the journey to becoming a man in adulthood. Besides this fact, <i>Rescue Love Revenge<\/i> in the ' retro' mode is a hallmark of the collection of past myriads of  what was known as  &quot; auto-run.&quot;  <i>Rescue Love Revenge<\/i> is a very welcome addition to the past flash game platformer legacy. <br><br>     <b>Difficulty<\/b><br>     There is no option to change the default difficulty of the game. However, in its own right goes from simple to harder, then to incriminating, separating platforms so far away from each other, like <i>Mario Maker<\/i>, you, have to use trial and error to see the bottom and top platforms. It is not frustrating at all, because the game is so fun with the knockback of the cactus's later on, that the TNT that propels enemies forward, backward is revolutionary.<br><br>    <i> Overall Verdict- 10\/10- Irreproachable - Mechanics already have been perfected in early access, stirring graphics, the utilization of the right props as a game changer, and challenging platforms in the second half of each chapter that requires trial-error, perfect timing, and smooth use of motor controls.<br><br>     I find a game as good as this hard to review in its current state in early access so far. If you are like me, platformers are among your top three genres you play; purchase this game. This game is a must-have in anyone's library next to other platformer greats. Overall, controls are perfect; props perfectly placed, enemies attack as they should.
