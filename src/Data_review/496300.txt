be the one to succeed? <\/div>  <br>Kingdom: New Lands is an expanded version of Kingdom (renamed to Kingdom: Classic) developed by Noio and Licorice. New Lands is free for owners of the original game (so if you owned Kingdom you might want to check your library!). In New Lands you play as a Monarch attempting to build a glorious kingdom from the ground up. However, this time there are five different islands for you to explore and conquer. It's not going to be plain sailing though; the islands are home to mysterious portals, spewing out things that go bump in the night. It'll be up to you to ensure your kingdom is capable of defending itself against them. <br> <br>The main difference between New Lands and the base game is of course the introduction of five different islands which you can travel to (in order, they get progressively more difficult). However, there are some other new additions. I don't want to spoil anything so I'll stick to just what's mentioned on the store page, there are some new mounts for you to acquire as well as some new merchants. Personally, I don\u2019t think the additions by themselves would warrant a new game, however the fact that its given free to those who already owned the base game makes this point moot, it'd definitely be pointless to buy the Classic version once New Lands is available. <br> <br>The most enjoyable part of New Lands, as was the case with Classic, is discovering new things. New Lands throws you in at the deep end of the pool with a weight tied to your foot. You're given no guidance as to what things do what so learning the game is a real case of trial and error, more error than trial. If you're familiar with Kingdom: Classic then there might not be quite as much for you to discover (apart from the new stuff) unless you've forgotten everything but it'll be exciting for new players nonetheless! <br> <br>New Lands features a day and night cycle. For the most part, you're safe during the day to work on expanding\/improving your kingdom while the night is when the things trying to kill you come out. Generally if you're doing work during the day you want to get it started around noon so your workers have plenty of time to get the job done before nightfall. Workers, along with guards and farmers, are hired by buying spare tools, any vagrants you've hired who don't have a tool will go to the nearest available tool they can find and grab it. <br> <br>It's quite difficult to talk about New Lands without spoiling those discoveries I mentioned earlier, but the game's actual mechanics are very simple. You, as King or Queen, walk around the map on horseback gathering gold coins which you then spend on various things such as hiring vagrants, buying tools for said vagrants, building defenses etc. You do start out with a few coins found on the map but you'll need to quickly establish a way of making more money, I'll leave you to figure that out for yourself. You have no actual control over the people who work for your kingdom, you're basically just someone shouting directions at people. For the most part, there's no issue with having no direct influence over your population, but I have had a couple of situations where the lack of any basic commands is very frustrating.  <br> <br>For example, I had reached the second island and had successfully taken out the portal on one side of the island so I only needed to defend the right side from monsters. Some of my archers got killed during the attack on the portal and I had six archers left back at my castle. Five of the archers decided to defend the left side of my castle, where no enemies were coming from, leaving one archer to defend the right side. Suffice to say I lost the game that night. It'd be nice to see a way to avoid situations like this. <br> <br>My main gripe with New Lands is the way the whole five island thing has been implemented. Say you reach island three but due to a mistake you end up losing the game, you'll have to start over from the first island again. Now, you might not think that's so bad, it depends what you're looking for with this game I suppose but I found it to be quite irritating to have to restart every time. It's made worse by the fact a lot of the cool new stuff isn't found on the first or even the second island. I understand why it's implemented this way but I'd love to perhaps see a slightly different game mode where you can save your progress at the beginning of each new island so you don't have to replay it all. <br> <br>The visuals haven't changed from the Classic version but that's certainly not a bad thing. Both Kingdom games have some of the best pixel art I've seen in games, the water effects are particularly impressive. The audio is also great, again unchanged from the previous game. The ambient music is very relaxing and the sound effects are all very good. My only other complaint about the game is that there's no way to re-bind keys. It's not completely necessary, there's nothing wrong with the default bindings per se, it would just be nice to have the option. <br> <br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Verdict <\/div>  <br>New Lands improves upon Kingdom's formula, offering the same great visuals with improved gameplay. It's not without a couple of problems but as a free upgrade for previous owners it's definitely worth playing. As for those who are looking to buy New Lands, it's a worthy expansion to what was a great game. <br> <br><i><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/groups\/lonerangerreviews\"
