Trying to get into the habit and wanted to start with a good one.<br><br>Simple VR Player is, without a doubt, the best VR media player on the market. It beats its competition in nearly every area, and what small problems I have with it are more than offset by the intuitive interface, the graphics options, and the control you have over your UI.<br><br>Compared to Virtual Desktop, it has:<br>+ An intuitive motion interface. You won't really appreciate what a difference this makes until you use it, but once you do, you'll never want to go back to VD. It's easy to move, resize, and angle the screen however you like, and you can do the same with the menu, which is a completely separate floating element. Buttons do what you expect them to do, and the only way I can think to improve it is if they either made the laser pointer brighter or gave an option for touch controls. Honestly, ust the fact that you don't have to Alt-Tab to a separate window just to change settings is a godsend.<br>+ Detailed SSAA and MSAA options. Yeah, both. SSAA up to 2.0 and MSAA up to 8x. Plus, the &quot;Super Enhanced&quot; mode adds a touch of contrast and sharpness to the image, which helps combat HMD fuzziness. With my 1070, I have all three options maxed and never drop below 90 FPS. Looks gorgeous.<br>+ Screen curvature control. VD only allows you to choose between curved and not-curved.<br>+ 3D distance \/ tilt \/ height controls. For your Internet research.<br>+ Price. Even at full price, Simple is barely more than half the price of VD. With the discount, it's a no-brainer.<br>= Environments. There aren't as many (or as high quality) as in VD yet, but they're (a) all you really need to get going and (b) free. Plus, there's a pure black one! Maybe I'm the only one who really wanted that, but I'm glad it's there.<br>= Easy access to 2D \/ 3D \/ SBS \/ OU. VD has hotkeys, and Simple has buttons. Six o' one.<br>= Can play anything. If the filters you pick can play it, so can the player.<br>- Limited filter selection. VD simply displays whatever program you use to play media, so it can use just about any filter that you can get working on your system. Simple takes more of the responsibility on itself, so it has to have support for the filters. Right now, it offers the Windows built-in, DirectShow (LAV), VLC, and PopMedia. The default is godawful because it always is, and VLC is as middle-of-the-road as usual, but the LAV Filters look fantastic. Still wish I could use mpv, like I do in VD and everywhere else I watch anything.<br>- Only does playback. VD allows you to use your desktop, play most games, and basically do whatever with your computer. If you want to play games on the big screen, either get VD or hope it works with Steam's thing. Since I already bought VD a while back, I'm just going to use it for games and Simple for media.<br><br>Compared to Whirligig (which I haven't nearly used as much as VD), it has:<br>+ Much, MUCH more intuitive interface. Whirligig has probably been the best 3D player out there, but actually using it is practically a case study in bad UX. Simple is...well, simple.<br>+ Plays everything, not just 3D. I THINK Whirligig is limited to 3D, but again, I haven't put a lot of use into it.<br>= All 3D controls you need. It's just easier to actually use them with Simple.<br>- A few research companies assume Whirligig, so they have config settings, etc, for it. They could be translated to Simple, I suppose, but that's work.<br><br>As you can see, Simple wipes the floor with the competition as far as pure media consumption is concerned. VD offers a more full-featured computing experience, but if you just want to watch some bloody movies, Simple's got you covered.
