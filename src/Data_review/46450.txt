you've warmed up to the game. The combat is fun and rewards planning. This game was a fun challenge all the way through. <br><br>The game doesn't hold your hand beyond the mere basics, but that made things a lot more satisfying once I figured out how everything works and started devouring quest after quest.<br><br>The only few negative points I can bring up are the art and how this game could have crossed the threshold from &quot;Pretty Fun&quot; to &quot;Strongly Recommended&quot; if not &quot;Must Have&quot; with just a bit of polishing. The 2d character art looks like something out of a teenager-otaku's animu sketchbook more than a professional videogame. In fact, it was quite a turn-off at first, but I'm glad I let it slide because I hardly noticed it once I got about three-to-four quests deep and started appreciating the game more for its gameplay.<br><br>The humor, for what the game is mostly promoted with, is rather hit-and-miss. Probably because of the translation. One particular joke, even if it was intentionally bad, was just too bad that I wanted my 32 seconds of life back.<br><br>As for the writing - it wasn't too bad. The story is bland as oatmeal mixed with tap water and a dash of salt in the middle of Ohio, but it was the character interactions that was just good enough to keep me invested in the game (Other than the gameplay itself). However, I've spotted at least one typo, and it was of the &quot;Semi-divine's Humble Self&quot;'s name. How can you possibly mispell one of the main character's name\/third-person referential name?<br><br>Speaking of which, the game is a bit buggy. There is a game-mechanic that allows you to freely roam over the map until you get close to an enemy, then you're characters are stuck into the grid when battles begin. Once the enemies have been defeated, you go back to free roam mode. Or so you should. At least twice I was stuck in battle-mode after I cleared the entire map of enemies. Luckily, this problem didn't prevent me from leaving the map and continuing the game. However, this change from free-roam to rigid-battle layout system bugged out once that made the game unplayable and I had to restart again from an earlier save. I was crossing a ford when forced into a battle, but because my characters where so bunched up, one character couldn't reach an open square to end his turn on, thus stuck in an eternal turn.<br><br>Also, this game crashed twice on me. Maybe because it's an older game on much newer software.<br><br>However, my biggest irk with this game, rather than the writing and bugs, was the sound design. Particularly the sound design on a few particular stages. There are things on the map that make noises, and no matter where you go on the map, you hear them. For example, there is a fool who constantly jumps in and out of the gallows, and it makes an irritating noise each time he does it. And it doesn't matter if you are on the other side of the map -- It. Never. Stops. It's even worse on the graveyard level where you come across a skeleton swinging about in a cage on rusty chains. You here the chains squeak and groan and clatter mercilessly over the other audio -- And it. Never. Stops. I just gave up and nearly muted the sound in the latter case.<br><br>In all, this game has a heart that shines through its unpolished and amatuerish exterior.
