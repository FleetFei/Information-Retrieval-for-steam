Now one small, quick (but important) note: the game description is too positive. &quot;Features the biggest, most detailed and alive open world city&quot;. This is <u>not<\/u> true. There are about 5 models for pedestrians, all wearing meme-shirts. You can drive straight through them, no interaction at all. They don't fall over, jump out of the way, you can't even push them around. Then what about traffic? The traffic consists of a bunch of cars (with even lower resolution!) that seem to be on Scalextric tracks. You can push 'em around all you want, but the front wheels will always point to the direction they're going, That doesn't mean they're completely inanemate, you can cause massive pileups, send them flying and make them go all sixes and sevens.<br><br>Now let's talk about gameplay, shall we?<br>The handling, let's just get on with it straight away. Ehmm, boring and obvious is the only way I can describe it. No, most definitely not. As a manner of fact, I'd rather have someone just take a premade string of code, rather than creating their own code from scratch and messing up.<br><br>Races<br>This game comes with a few racemodes. Time-trial and checkpoint races. It's pretty straight forward, timetrial, you drive on your own, through one of the few courses. Finish before the timer runs out, you win. When the timer runs out and you haven't yet finished, you lose. Checkpoint races: you drive around a course with other opponents. Finish first and you'll win, and you know the rest.<br><br>Bugs<br>This game is riddled with bugs, so many in fact, it's somtimes a guilty pleasure to just play a game of 'How to break a game in as many ways as possible'. Just take this example: When you go over the bridge away from the city, you'll arrive at a teeny tiny, almost roundabout-sized island. in the middle: a bunch of rocks. Hit those rocks at high speeds, you'll jump, do massive flips with an incredible amount of air-time, and with a bit of luck, you might just land on the next bridge. It is however, way more likely you'll miss the bridge, and land in the water. Big problem? Not really. Underneath the surface of the water, or I could just say texture of a surface, there is a bonedry, hard floor. And now you can endlessly drive around in the water. But watch out, if you manage to find the edge of the map, you'll fall down into oblivion. And don't expect the reset-button to help there. When your car resets back onto the road you are closest to, the car will just freeze. You're able to bump into it with other cars, and it'll move, but you can't drive it. And once you're out of it, you can't get back in.<br>Just a small example of a bug.<br><br>Final verdict:<br>Alright, that was a long, long review. Buy my final verdict is... 6\/10.<br>Surprised?
