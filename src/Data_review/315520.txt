MP3, 128 kbps<br>Number of tracks: 18<br>Total size: 22 MB<br>Total length: 0:23:56 hours (~24 Minutes)<br><br>The soundtrack offers chiptune-styled music, fitting to the theme of the game.<br>Unfortunately by the tracks being relatively short, the (perceived) repetition inside the tracks gives a feeling of getting even less unique music than expected. That aside, the variety of genres is fitting for a video game, but fails to set new accents.<br><br>The happiness of the third track &quot;The Lamplight Proclamation&quot; is carried over to the fourth track &quot;The Mushroom Jam&quot;, which sounds dark and happy at the same time, but not crazy enough to really remind of the other mushrooms.<br>Track 5, &quot;Old Haunts&quot;, uses a musical principle that was used in the infamous Track 4 from the Tropico 4 soundtrack: Repeating the same thing and then repeating it with a changed note. Unfortunately here it does not get quite that pop feeling, but could have been a great track with more variety. Please compare for yourself:<br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"https:\/\/www.youtube.com\/watch?v=ruxmkh6FQsE\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_0\">https:\/\/www.youtube.com\/watch?v=ruxmkh6FQsE<\/a><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"https:\/\/www.youtube.com\/watch?v=-wiiSjb5lhI\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_1\">https:\/\/www.youtube.com\/watch?v=-wiiSjb5lhI<\/a><br>Track 7, &quot;Old White&quot;, uses the same musical scheme again. If J. Kyle Pittman was inspired by the Tropico track above, he did a nice hommage without actually copying it. If not, a good choice to use that kind of scheme.<br><br>Track 9 must be the highlight of the album: &quot;Subterranea&quot; is good chiptune pop, reminding of techno\/trance music. Unfortunately, again, the length of the track cuts the enjoyment short.<br>Track 10 is &quot;Growing Up&quot; that reminds me actually of punk music \u00e1 la Green Day, but only lasts for half a minute.<br><br>&quot;Arc Evil&quot; follows which spreads a dark mood indeed, giving me even goosebumps, while still not being a memorable melody. This also sets the tune for the coming track which is in a darker mood: &quot;Pity Wings&quot;. Unfortunately &quot;Bored and Bled&quot; has a dark name, but sounds too happy for its name. It is no surprise then that &quot;The Razor's Edge&quot; is also happy, sounding like a ballad without a singer.<br><br>Track 15, &quot;The Hollow Heard&quot;, is mistuned while poppy - fitting to the place in the game where it occurs, but partially unpleasant to hear on its own. Track 17, &quot;Hall of Speedruns&quot;, delves into rock music, but is over too fast. The final track just delivers normal poppy music once more, like the other three not mentioned tracks (1, 2 and 16), although all in different styles.<br><br>What remains is a fleeting glimpse, like a box of chocolates.<br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"https:\/\/www.youtube.com\/watch?v=0acEl97ZBME\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_2\">https:\/\/www.youtube.com\/watch?v=0acEl97ZBME<\/a><br><br>It is pleasant but the enjoyment is too fleeting, too few. The soundtrack is by no means bad, it is enjoyable in parts, but as a separate DLC I can really only recommend it to hardcore chiptune fans. All others will get enough out of it from hearing it ingame.<br><br>Unfortunately one can only buy the standard or the soundtrack version, without any chance to upgrade, to buy the DLC extra. In this case I can only say if you get the game on a good sale (75 percent or more) and collect music, or if you want to support the developer, you might want the soundtrack edition. If you are a &quot;normal&quot; player, the standard edition of the game will suffice for you.<br><br>Recommended: Only for chiptune fans, as you will hear almost everything ingame aplenty.
