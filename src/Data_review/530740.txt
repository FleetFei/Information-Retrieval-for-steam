class=\"bb_h1\">Introduction<\/div><br>Spareware is the debut Steam game by Rusto Games. Rusto Games are based out of Kajaani. Kajaani is a small city of just shy of 40,000 people and located in central Finland some 560km (348 miles) north of the capital, Helsinki. You can imagine just how small a populous the city has to lure game developers in so it's a mighty effort for a developer to get a game published on Steam. <br><br>Spareware is an arena twin-stick shooter that was released in a year where the market was flooded with them. For the game to have any chance, whatsoever, of breaking into the market and getting an audience it was going to have to be special. It would have to offer something unique. But most of all it was either going to have to be so good as to be unmissable or so bad that it lures a crowd like Sharknado did for the B-movie scene.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Does it hit the sweet spot?<\/div><br>Unfortunately, for Spareware, it's neither bad enough nor good enough to capture a sizable audience. It's a slightly better than a mediocre game that will find some people becoming fans of it and much more turning away from it rather quickly. Therefore, it doesn't hit the sweet spot that small indie games need to to be successful. <br><br>It's a bit of a shame because it's clear that the developers are passionate about what they do. Perhaps that is the nicest thing that can be said about the developers at Rusto. It's a reasonable effort from what I am certain is a small studio. They can be proud of their achievement. And it is a solid title that I am certain they can use as a learning experience should they continue working on further titles which I encourage them to do.<br><br>The standout feature of Spareware is the destructible body parts system. Players have three layers of protection. These are your head and your arms. Enemies begin by attacking your head. If that layer of a shield is completely depleted your head explodes and you lose that body part. Being a mech you are able to continue and the enemies will target your arms. If this again is depleted you lose an arm and finally, if you lose your other arm you will die. However, you can shoot vehicles that are a part of the game's environment and replace your parts or pick up shields to regenerate health. It's a very easy system to learn.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Better With Teammates Than Solo<\/div><br>Spareware is a much better title when playing with friends than playing solo. This is, I believe, the intention of the game. A number of the better skills in the game are balanced around team-based play. The game is so much more enjoyable when you have friends playing with where you take down enemies very quickly, power up together and destroy the unending supply of respawning enemies.<br><br>In the time that I played solo, I quickly found myself becoming bored. This is because the presentation of the game is relatively basic. The assets are relatively basic and the reskinning becomes obvious. Of course, I wouldn't have expected much more than what the game delivers given a clearly limited budget. This isn't a game from a major studio, after all. The other thing that becomes particularly noticeable when playing solo - more so than co-op - is that the level design doesn't differentiate itself nearly enough from level-to-level. It often feels like you are repeating levels. The aesthetics and environments are largely repeated even if the maps are marginally different.<br><br>The game started to shine a little when I put in a little time with friends. The game's deficiencies become less significant and I focused more on kicking \u2665\u2665\u2665 with my friends by my side. This was the intention of the game and therefore I have to suggest that the devs did a good job in this regard. If you have friends and are bored of the more highly regarded games on Steam then I would recommend Spareware. It's an enjoyable co-op experience.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Steam Features<\/div><br>The game is not short on Steam features and kudos must be paid to the developers here. Clearly, they have considered the overall Steam audience for their game and have done a rock solid job in adding them to the game.<br><br><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li>Steam Achievements (36 of them all of which can be unlocked quite easily within the game, no nonsensical ones here)<br><\/li><li>Steam Trading Cards (a small set of 5)<br><\/li><li>Steam Cloud Saves<\/li><\/ul><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Conclusion and Recommendation<\/div><br>It's clear that the Rusto has put in a lot of effort in their debut title. However, that doesn't necessarily make the game very good. I've long held the opinion that the worst thing that you can be on Steam is mediocre especially if you're lacking product hype. Players are generally attracted to can't miss hit games, games that are full of hype (whether they deliver or not is almost irrelevant) or games that are so poor that they attract the attention of the more popular YouTubers, podcasters, etc. who are more than happy to create a video or spend time on their podcasts discussing how bad a game is and subsequently peddle the game to their audience.<br><br>Unfortunately, by being a marginally better than a mediocre title, the developers have missed the mark. There's a reason why, currently, there are only 6 reviews on Steam with mine becoming the seventh. There are better games on the market within the genre and there are significantly worse games. <br><br>I wouldn't personally recommend this title to soloists. There just isn't enough quality content to entertain you. You'll quickly become bored and move onto the next game in your library. If, however, you have a group of people to play with you - especially locally - and you've already played the well-known genre titles to death then give this game an opportunity.<br><br>Consequently, this is the type of game that I wish Steam would incorporate a middle option. I can't recommend the game to soloists but can do so to people seeking a multiplayer experience. Given that the intention of the developers was to create a multiplayer experience I will reluctantly give the game a thumbs up. Soloists beware.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">INTRODUCING GAMEPHASIS<\/div><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"https:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/linkfilter\/?url=http:\/\/www.gamephasis.com\/\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\" >Gamephasis<\/a> is a new gaming website featuring a talented globally based writing team.
