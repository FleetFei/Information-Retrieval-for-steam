None are found here, or at least not as ridiculous as most. I won\u2019t say the story is mind blowing or  something like that, not at all, but enough to keep me going,<br><br>Other part to note is the technical side, which is surprisingly pretty good, it\u2019s stable, no game breaking bugs, and runs well.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"> Cons \t<\/div><br>Gameplay wise, it's mediocre at best. I mean, they\u2019re A.I, they are not as complex, dynamic, and unpredictable as humans, of course. How do one cover it up? Make an epic scale battles with wide variety of units, which Squad 22 is none of those 2 (I don\u2019t really mean it as a bad thing), or  make the A.I more dynamic and adaptive, which sadly is not the case. Playing with the A.I is like having a match of rock, paper, scissors, but the A.I decided to always choose paper and not even consider choosing anything else. They\u2019re basically on paper rusher, sending small waves of enemies over and over again that it becomes repetitive and easy, the difficult thing is because they are constantly spamming them, and I can get overwhelmed at times.<br><br>In a more positive note on the gameplay is the research system, you collect canisters to upgrade your research tree. Not something big but it helps with the overall sense of progression. It\u2019s not too much of them that you will be underpowered as the mission progressed, and not too many that it makes you OP in the end. I won\u2019t say it\u2019s bad, because it is not, its just lacking and restrictive. For a single player game, the A,I is too predictable. Thankfully, the research tree is well done and that helps adding the depth it needed. The gameplay, is way too balanced and \u201csafe\u201d, contrasting the imaginative visual.
