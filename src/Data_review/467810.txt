classic character. Heck, the last Bomberman title I played was on the N64. The developer of Splody sought to bring this style of gameplay back for family and friends who enjoy it. For those who do not own a Nintendo Switch or do not plan to get one, Splody is a perfectly good alternative for some fun multiplayer bombing action.<br><br><b>Gameplay<\/b><br>==========<br>In Splody, your goal is to be the last man standing. To accomplish this seemingly impossible goal, you need to gather powerups from destroyed objects and use your endless supply of bombs to blow up the opposition. The player and either bots or other players are dropped onto a random map filled with objects to blow up and obtain powerups from, along with other random obstacles. Powerups range from Bomb Ups, which increase the number of bombs you can place at once, to a DINOSAUR THAT YOU CAN RIDE. This dinosaur is actually more useful than you think, as it can take a hit for you should you fall into the line of fire.<br><br>Online multiplayer was pretty stable from what I played. If no other players are available, the game will fill in bots for you to play against until other players join in. I actually got the opportunity to play with the dev for awhile (jimbly), as you'll see in the gameplay video further down!<br><br><b>Graphics<\/b><br>==========<br>Splody is super simple in this area. It's not great by any means but it's good enough to just jump in, not worry about it, and just have a good time. You can clearly tell what the powerups are, what\/where each obstacle is, and where every bomb is placed. There should be no confusion as to anything on screen. Except maybe when you see the dinosaur mount for the first time and think to yourself &quot;WTF?!&quot;. You can even customize your avatar with up to 6 different styles and even more colors.<br><br><b>Sound and Music<\/b><br>==========<br>The few music tracks I actually noticed were pretty good. Nothing too distracting, but nothing overly ambitious either. Perhaps there's some room for improvement? Maybe...maybe even an option to load up your own music? Sound effects were pretty decent. Explosions were alright, and the little effects when picking up powerups were nice and actually matched what you were picking up.<br><br><b>Performance<\/b><br>==========<br>You can probably play Splody on most any modern PC. Maybe even a toaster, with 60FPS and no lag or stuttering. All of my playtime was with my Logitech F310 controller, and it was pretty much perfect. There are plenty of display, audio, and control options to play around with to meet your needs.<br><br><b>Final Thoughts<\/b><br>==========<br>Want a fun multiplayer party game to play with your friends?
