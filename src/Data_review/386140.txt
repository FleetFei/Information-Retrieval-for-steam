class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt the moment, (version 0.07) this is a game that shows a lot of promise, but definitely is covered in &quot;construction&quot; signs.  <br><br>You are The Wanderer, which seems an odd title, as you clearly weren't a wanderer before the start of the game, unless you just wandered around town.  Maybe all the other people in town are wanderers too? Anyway, you're a character sprite you choose from a list of character sprites with no backstory because the plot's not really written yet.  A Beautiful Woman chased by The Bad Guys crashes near your house, so you naturally do the heroic thing, and steal her ship for yourself so you can go joyriding and see the universe, meet strange and fascinating new people, then murder them and loot their corpses for their stuff! Ostensibly, you are doing this to find The Beautiful Woman's soul because she's apparently now your Love.  (So what if you're a girl, you apparently swing that way!) You have to rescue her soul because The Bad Guys... umm... think it would taste great with jam? Or maybe just put it in a lava lamp or something? Anyway, you have to go to The Centre to find it and beat the game.  This amounts to fighting several giant Bad Guys, and one slightly bigger giant Bad Guy who shows he means business because he's a different color than the others and deals marginally more damage. <br><br>Along the way, you'll meet several alien races, most of which are hostile because The Harvest swept up all the good races, and took them to a Higher Plane of Existence.  Basically, it's the Rapture, but with dinosaurs and little gray men instead of the Antichrist.<br><br>The new version brought with it a &quot;sci fi&quot; pack of portraits, so the little gray men no longer use standard RPGMaker elf portraits, and now means the weapon dealers have a face that looks like a discarded cicada shell, and the catgirls look like goth girls with witch hats.  The dev assures us that none of the art is final, but it's somewhat amusing in its current state, anyway.  <br><br>The game really only has about 3 required missions, and the rest are basically you just visiting random places, being asked to pick a side in some battle between two opposing races, and murdering whichever side strikes your fancy or didn't give a bid.  In some senses, it's a bit like Skyrim in that regard, although also like Skyrim, it tends to wind up with me walking into some alien base, killing everything, then accidentally hitting a self-destruct button, and then being told &quot;Congratulations, I just completed a quest I didn't actually accept!&quot;  Gee, I hope I didn't miss any important lead-up to that.  I wonder who I just killed?<br><br>Enemies range from wolves and lizardpeople and skeletons you kill by the dozen with a couple strong attacks to giants that take shooting for a full minute while dodging their fireballs and chugging potions like crazy while trying not to think about whether the junk made by those red imp guys are really without any side effects when you take 40 times the reccomended dosage.  The fireballs are slow enough to make them easy to dodge if you stand further than 4 tiles away, but many weapon attacks tend to force you to stay still for a second or so, and so it's best to dodge, then shoot. A lack of ability to aim your character without stepping a tile towards your enemy is also an annoyance, as there's a rather small finite number of tiles on the screen to start with, and the player character is such a cool customer, (s)he never bothers to go at more than a walking pace.  So what if you're being followed by a flaming boulder rolled by a living volcano, you didn't pick the character who wears leather pants and shades at night to break your cool!<br><br>Weapons range from projectiles with various blast patterns to melee weapons you sell to buy more ammo for the projectile weapons.  The low number of frames of animation mean an enemy is already hitting you by the time the axe appears, so melee combat is essentially impossible to dodge and comes down to spamming the attack button to kill it before it kills you.  Save your potions, abuse your ranged weapons. <br><br>Currently, a few imbalanced weapons make the game fairly easy, as stun weapons can paralyze even a titan that by all rights should squish you like a bug.  Magic and projectiles in general need reworking, as they tend to be either easily evaded or, in the case of some spells, are impossible to evade and hit 20 times in a row with enough damage to kill you if you aren't spamming the potion button like crazy.  <br><br>It takes some clear references from Secret of Mana and Legend of Zelda. Some of the monsters are clearly in the art style of Secret of Mana. You have a &quot;grappleshot&quot; to cling to posts, bombs to open caves blocked by rocks, etc. You can even find outright expies where &quot;Dink&quot; is asked to fight &quot;Cannondork&quot; to save &quot;Princess Xena&quot;...<br><br>While it undoubtably has helped speed development, as there are a lot of scripts available for RPG Maker &quot;off the shelf&quot; at this point, the biggest limitation of this game seems to be its engine.  It would play better without the RPG Maker engine, whether it's because the RPG Maker engine is so badly optimized that even these graphics lag the game, or because it forces the game to stick to a grid-based system, when free-form movement would better suit the game. <br><br>Early access games should generally be rated on how they are, rather than what you hope they might someday be.  What it IS is still clearly under development at a pre-alpha level.  (There are some towns that just don't have doors because the shop interiors haven't been made yet...) That said, for the dirt-cheap price they're asking for it at this early state, it's more than enough entertainment to justify itself.
