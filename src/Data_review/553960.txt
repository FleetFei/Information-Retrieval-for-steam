Buy it whenever its around 12$ or so. That said, let's go into the review.<br><br>While the game certainly has its flaws, I have really enjoyed it. Let's break it down, beginning with the atmosphere. This is amazing. While I expected a lot more jumpscares or basically interaction with the user (instead of walking), I'm still satisfied because the atmosphere allows to add your own kind of fears (I mean come on, dark  spaces with chilling music, I easily go 'oh \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665'). The environment also plays a huge role in this atmosphere. This game truly shows the possibilities of Unreal Engine 4. If you have an end-game rig, the ultra graphics will blow your mind.<br><br>The second point is the story and I have to be honest here: I was hooked throughout the entire game, until the final chapter (the finale) came around. In my opinion it left a fair few questions unanswered and it seemed a  bit rushed. That said, story is still bareable.<br><br>But let's get into the problem of this game: the combat\/stealth system. This is pretty much the biggest negative point of this game. It simply isn't good enough. Combat is so generic and wacky (seriously, melee attacking is kind of annoying because sometimes it just doesn't register). Using the gun is pretty okay I guess, except you can't really steady your aim.  The monsters are also pretty generic, nothing special, one kills instantly, others don't. Bullets needed to kill them differ among the type of enemy. Next up: taking the stealth approach. In most cases, forget it. It just doesn't work the way  you want it to.<br><br>Last but not least, bugs. While I haven't experienced a lot of bugs (most gameplay was pretty smooth), I did encounter 2 monsters that just hit me through a wall.  Kind of weird, but not gamebreaking I'd say (more annoying).
