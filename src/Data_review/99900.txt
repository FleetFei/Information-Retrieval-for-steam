stuff on Steam, found this cute little Nintendo-ish MMO, and gave it a spin.<br><br>Spiral Knights is an action MMO set in a world that feels like some kind of cross between Legend of Zelda and Metroid. Alien knights fleeing a dying world crash-landed their starship on a strange planet called Cradle, which is a patchwork of places taken from other planets, with many rotating layers of mazelike levels within called the Clockworks. Granted sanctuary by the mysterious Strangers who run the surface town of Haven, they begin their search for the massive energy source that led them here in the first place. But they're not the only life on Cradle: Fiends, Slimes, Beasts, Undead, Gremlins, and Constructs all compete for territory and resources here. An elite team of Spiral Knights reached the Core, and were never heard from again; retracing their steps to find them forms the bulk of the main questline.<br><br>The game uses a sort of &quot;twin stick&quot; control scheme, I used mouse and keyboard for this. WASD moves you around, and the mouse controls a pointer for aiming your attacks and thrown objects. Left Mouse Button attacks, Right Mouse Button blocks. Shift+ Left Mouse Button while attacking does a shield dash that bashes monsters out of your way and stuns them, using up half your shield bar. Shift + Right Mouse Button does a quick hop in the direction you're moving, or in the direction you're facing if you're not moving; this helps you dodge area attacks, but has a ten second cooldown.<br><br>You start with a Helm, Armor, and Shield slot, and two Weapon Slots. Two additional weapon slots can be purchased, as well as two trinket slots that let you equip some Trinkets for a slight boost, but these only last 30 days before expiring; they're probably aimed at PvP players looking for an edge. You can equip any other Helm or Armor as a &quot;Costume&quot; over the Helm and Armor you're actually using, giving you some control over your appearance. There are also special Costume items which can be used just for this purpose, and Accessories which can be attached to Helm, Armor, or Costume items for decoration., A big source of income for the game is Prize Boxes which contain random Costumes and Accessories, many of them are reskins of existing items in new color schemes, others are bizarre original creations.<br><br>There are three kinds of weapons available: Swords, Guns, and Bombs. Holding the attack button begins charging your weapon's charge attack, which does more damage and has stronger effects than the normal attack. Bombs can only use the charge attack, but cover a wide area; you're immune to your own weapons, so you can bomb away at close range.<br><br>The game has four damage types, and every type of enemy is partly resistant to one type and vulnerable to another. There are also status effects that can be inflicted by some weapons, like Fire, Freeze, Shock, Poison, and Stun. Different kinds of Helm, Armor, and Shield give different status and damage protections; this is vital in the deepest Clockworks.<br><br>After a few missions you get to pick a Battle Sprite from the three different types, each of which has various special powers on a cooldown. They're very difficult to level up to full power, but they can make some situations much easier.<br><br>You have a heath bar and a shield bar, any hits taken when not blocking come straight out of Health which doesn't regenerate, so blocking with your Shield is absolutely essential to survival. Your shield bar recharges in a few seconds.<br><br>Getting KO'd triggers your emergency revive, which gets replenished at the start of each level. Any KO after that requires a Spark of Life to revive you, or you can wait for your team to finish the level, but then you get no experience.<br><br>Experience is tied to your gear instead of your character. In addition to collecting coins and crafting materials, you collect &quot;heat&quot; crystals which are added to your weapons and gear at the end of the level. When a weapon has a full bar, you can then use &quot;fire&quot; crystals to &quot;forge&quot; the weapon to a higher level. Gear also comes in different &quot;star&quot; ratings, from 0 star to 5 star, and crafting a 3 star or higher weapon usually requires the previous version as a material. Crafting your own weapons also requires 3 Orbs of Alchemy of the appropriate star level, and you also need to know the Recipe for it.<br><br>This is where the game starts to run into problems, as you can see there are at least five resources involved in character progress. This was changed from the original system, which alienated a *lot* of players, and effectively paywalls most of the game's interesting content. The amount of grinding required to do this without paying soon gets utterly ludicrous.<br><br>The part where free-to-play Knights usually &quot;hit the wall&quot; is the The Sovereign Slime, since it's very hard to finish the Royal Jelly Palace without either grinding for a lot of custom gear, or having a guild or a more experienced Knight carry you through. If you can make it through that, you can probably muddle through the rest of the game eventually, but there are literal &quot;gear checks&quot; (the Hall of Heroes missions) which require you to have gear of a certain star level before you can attempt the next level of missions. This will take a while to save up for, since the recipes you get as mission rewards are mostly for default normal damage gear that is not competitive past the middle of the game, and orbs are rare drops and not cheap. Many crafting chains branch, but some underpowered branches are effectively dead ends.<br><br>There is an Auction House which allows a player economy of sorts, you can buy or sell most unbound items for a small fee. There is a also a trade chat channel for people who would rather deal directly with other players through the trade system. However, any gear you have ever equipped (or crafted from something you have ever equipped) is &quot;bound&quot; and can't be traded to other players. There's a way to unbind weapons and armor, but it costs more than an item of that star level would cost from the store, so it's pointless unless it's a weapon with rare Unique Variants (extra powers that you have a slim chance to gain from crafting) or a weapon from a long-gone Prize Box like the Overcharged Mixmaster.<br><br>All of this adds up to leave the game in something of a typical &quot;MMO death spiral&quot; as new players hit the paywall and bounce off, and old players get burned out and move on to other games. PvP is also mostly dead for the same reasons.<br><br>My recommendation is that you give it shot and see if you like the basic playstyle of the game. Fight your way up to the Royal Jelly and see if this is something you're enjoying. There are packages available for purchase in-game, like the Deluxe Starter Pack or the Battle Pack, which can only be bought once but give some collectibles and\/or costumes. You're probably better off spending the money on energy instead, since the extras inflate the cost and are only useful to some players. (The Krogmo Coin Boosters in the Battle Pack are only useful if you play a lot of PvP, for example.) By this point you should have a fair idea of what you need. Check the game's wiki to make sure you're on the right track.<br><br>&quot;This sounds terrible, why are you giving it a thumbs up?&quot; Partly because I feel ridiculous giving a thumbs down to something I've played for over 4,000 hours, it was my gaming comfort food in a very tough time in my life. Partly because, once you get past the grinding and get better gear, the gameplay is actually very challenging and pretty satisfying. The real game starts at high level, but most players won't tolerate the grinding long enough to get there.<br><br>TL;DR: Get your Steam Wallet out, do an epic grind through the procedurally generated
