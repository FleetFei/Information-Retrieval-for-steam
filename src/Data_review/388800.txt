Good amount of content, especially for the money.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Graphics: 9\/10 (PC Version)<\/div><br>The graphics are great, but with the PC version being a near-direct port of the 3DS version, the graphics are identical, with possibly some exceptions here and there- just scaled up to fit a larger screen. I definitely give the PC version slightly lower praise in regards to that. The double-screens being optional for the most part was nice. It made the in-game experience more enjoyable, especially for myself, since I have trouble reading the 3DS's smaller screens (including the New 3DS XL's much-larger-in-comparison's screens). (*Since I played with Japanese Voice Mode on, I heard the entire game in Japanese with the original Japanese text translated into English, as opposed to the English localization.) All of Lumen's songs had nearly the same vibe, likely to keep the gameplay hype going, but the lack of variety in AZURE PHOSPHORESCENCE (as a result) caused me to lower my opinion just a little bit. Other oddities include the &quot;Boss Defeated&quot; jingle, which feels strangely lackluster compared to the rest of the soundtrack.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Story: 6\/10<\/div><br>The story was great! I really like all of the characters in the game, and I'm honestly a little disappointed there isn't much more of (most) of them to see. Speaking of, there is a Bad \/ True ending system in the game, where even if you meet all the necessary requirements otherwise, you can't access the True ending until you've seen the Bad one- and have been subjected to the game credits once already. (Almost the exact same credits reel repeats when you complete the true ending, and all though you can make the text scroll faster, you can not skip it.)<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay: 7\/10<\/div><br>The in-stage gameplay feels fantastic at almost all times. Everything controls tightly and even though your fingers will become a little tired after a few hours, I have very few complaints about the in-stage gameplay. Some checkpoint placement is weird. The seven jewels- which you need to access the True ending- are almost always nearly in plain sight or completely overlookable. (One that is easy to miss, upon looking it up online, suggests speed-run-like strategies to acquire it before explaining that it is, in fact, a puzzle.) Dying is sometimes your best weapon, as Lumen (also known as Joule) will occassionally revive you and boost your abilities ten-fold.<br>The out-of-stage gameplay is often a blend of visual-novel like interactions with Joule, reading mission briefings, accepting &quot;challenges&quot; for when you replay stages, and trying to figure out what materials you need in order to synthesize new gear, which can be equipped to Gunvolt to add a variety of additional, generally useful effects. This creates a &quot;material farming&quot; grind, which unfortunately is not my cup of tea, but the optional challenges give each stage more replay value and make the grind feel less grindy.<br>Since I've mentioned the True ending requirements a few times, I feel like I felt it was a tad odd that in order to access the True ending, you have to sacrifice your access to one of the game's staple mechanics (or, if the player has already ditched it, an item that provides some other boost in capabilities), in order to equip essentially nothing in its place. I understand that the developers might have felt the need to have the player &quot;earn&quot; the True ending- and maybe story-wise it makes some lick of sense- but it felt poor, considering accessing the True ending means you get the mechanic back before the credits roll again anyways.<br><br>I've watched players speedrun the game using the speedrun mode.
