I know FG is designed largely for online play, but I prefer face-to-face-roleplay and toe-to-toe-combat! My review is in that context and no other.<br><br>First: My requirements were to have a system that worked off-line.<br><br>Next I wanted to stop having to carrying around massive books as the game sessions were at a player's house<br><br>It would be cool if it  gave me maps and character placements on them<br><br>It needed to allow for story elements and descriptions, <br><br>I wanted as much as possible of the game mechanic to be automated <br><br>I desperately needed cross-referencing so I could easily access spell descriptions, item stats, monster stats, etc<br><br>Finally: If any player was unable to make the session I wanted a means of them joining in fro ma distance.<br><br><br>A tall order. So how did FG2 stand up under such pressure?<br><br>Off-line: FG2 runs perfectly fine off-line. I have found that starting it up at home where I have web access, then driving to the session helps if there's no internet available there. In the past I've had problems - this is most probably because I use the MAC version in Steam.<br><br>No Books: t allows me to leave most of the books at home. I say 'most' because a lot of the modules I'm running are non-SRD and not yet available as DLC. But with the community (currently) providing loads of extra modules and extensions for my preferred RPG (Pathfinder) and FG's inbuilt module creation facility I can overcome much of that. I still take a rulebook in case a player needs one to refer to but hasn't brought their own. I sometimes bring the Adventure Path with me for just-in-case backup.<br><br>Maps and Characters: Others have said that FG struggles in the maps area. This is a shame, as this is such a cool feature. However, other than a non-intuitive way of handling images, I've had little ofr no issues with maps. As I'm using them for reference purpose I suppose I'm using low-quality versions. (See later.) I could import maps, place a grid, and place characers on the grid. I still haven't used this feature to it's fullest for a number of reasons. Mainly because I play at the table, I'm forever drawing the map and then the players control their figure. At the end of the session, if the encounter is still active and the positions of pieces is important then I'll take a photo of the table. (Ore even a video!) I'd like to be able to transfer the pieces  to the map in FG and then I wouldn't need the photo! But this means extra work for me - especially as I sometimes change the map as I GM....<br><br>Characters: FG has a sweet set of Chaarcter sheets that are nicely laid out. I get my players to use PCGEN exports of their characters after they level up and then import them into FG. As we play at the table, I let them use paper copies of tablets. In fact I get them t maintain their own sheets all thetime. If they forget something and suffer for it, they'll learn for next time. If they 'cheat' then they have fun, but will get found out soon enough when I get their elecrronic version. My main point for having characters in FG is to keep tabs on initiative order in the combat tracker - which is superb.<br><br>Tokens: Placing characters and npc\/monsters on the map is simple enough in FG. However, the issue of token images is still a bother for me. I balk at having to pay for these as DLC - but getting my own set up is fine. Caveat: As I play at the table we use mini's or markers so tokens on a map are not such a great deal at the moment. And with FG not providing Line-of-Site or Fog-of-War this part of FG isn't so important.<br><br>Story: FG has a superb system for story elements allowing for descriptive parts, encounter groupings, and more. It takes a while to get used to, but it's great once you work within its quirkiness. Setting up story elements can be time consuming - DLC saves a tonne of that tedium - but you can add your own encounters, locations, and more. This is one part of FG I rely heavily on. <br><br>Automation: This is an area of FG that is fantastic and frustrating at the same time. Cross linking to spells, counting wand usage, rolling hit and damage, etc - all is great. But sometimes I want the character sheets and stats to update automatically and FG doesn't always do it. I want to be able to speed through the tedium of combat and skill rolls - sometimes I can, sometimes not. I get frustrated by it at times, but only say 15%. Mostly the players are unaware of what I struggle with. And my GM stye is laid back - sometimes if it's cool and fun I'll allow it. Sometimes if a player is pressing my buttons the wrong way I want a system to put the brakes on.<br><br>Cross-Referencing: This is great - as long as the info is in the system. You can set up a load of modules that can contain all you want. You have to load the module up before you get to see the info. I sometimes forget, and FG will just sit looking at me and do nothing until I remeber - then it's quick and easy to overcome. Creating your own items, spells, feats, etc is a bit awkward and I'd like to be able to easily insert cross references. As I said earlier, FG has its own system of hyperlinking that could do with some work - but it works. I just want more flexibility.<br><br>Remote Player: This is an area I haven't had success with. We have one player who is miles away and plays by Skype. Fortunately he's honest enough to own up to his fumles and crits! The major sticking point in FG for me here is connectivity. As I have no access to the modems at the player's house where we meet I cannot set up the connectivity FG wants. Also, the price point for multiple players using FG is prohibitive. Yes, I should get the ultimate version, but I can't justify the expense for one remote player (everyone else is at the table!)<br><br>Other comments: FG has a great interface but gets cluttered real quick. I want to be able to turn the chat box off altogether, as well as the dice, as they're not used at the table (I roll real dice - the players get jittery if I say I rolled a hit and either they don't see it or even hear the dice clattering!) Map movement and scrolling is awkward. Using a Mac trackpad is a pain as FG will open 'windows' when all I want to do is scroll. Showing players images and handouts is awkward, so I have to prepare those pefore hand. One day I'll get a flat screen set up and lay it out instead of my chessex mat, but then we'll loose the fun of saying 'the white counter with the feather is the undead-orc, remember?!' (And I like the spectacle of the table.)<br><br>So there we have it. I'm very pleased with FG at the moment and would rate it 9\/10. There's room for imrpovement. There's loads I use it for. (If it doesn't run at the table - one week it failed to launch on my MAC notebook - then I'm lost. That's how much I've come to depend on it.) I'm watching the future of FG development closely, especially with the Unity version and their recent partnering with Paizo. Will the price of modules increase? will the community generated material be banned? will we finally get line-of-sight and a decent fog-of-war? will there be a TPK button?
