relatively short, however I think the price is just right, if the game is played properly you will get much more out of it, the aim (FOR ME) is not just to get to the end by rushing through it (there are two endings) also 'memories' to collect as you progress through, personally I think these should have been a necessary part of completing the game, also giving you the freedom of revisiting past locations and perhaps offering a much more open world (just a suggestion) <br><br>To a point I understand why the developers have made it so you can just race through it not actually needing to collect anything, it's very hard to please everyone and I can see a good few people on here that would just give up on the basis of not being able to find a certain memory, plus some people don't appear to like backtracking.<br><br>This game has a lot to offer, the challenges are there should you choose to take them on, most of us older gamers were sat playing happily for hours on a commodore 64 when we were younger, accepting the simplest and hardest of challenges, and believe me one short game could take hours when setting challenges for yourself, my point being at the risk of sounding old - some of todays gamers don't like challenge because 'it's to hard' or 'time-consuming' do the achievements here to get the most out of the game, there's definitely a few extra hours of playtime to be had.   <br><br>Also, I would like to point out that In Shelter 1 and 2 food is a must! but in this game it really doesn't appear to matter if you eat or not, you never seem to get tired, weak or hungry but seeing as there is plenty of food about anyway this statement is slightly irrelevant but it would have added a little more challenge to the game should you have needed to feed at certain points throughout your journey.<br><br>As I said there is plenty of food around coupled with some achievements (if you catch the required amount of whatever meal) so again if you take some time to do a little hunting eventually you will be rewarded with an achievement, this is another reason to replay or to spend some extra time on your first run though.<br><br>The camera as in all the shelter games can be annoying at some points making perhaps the simplest of tasks rather tricky but far from impossible.<br><br>Take your time with it and really soak in the atmosphere and beautiful surroundings, annoy the angry swan, chase the birds and frogs and try leaping at the butterflies, these are the things a naughty baby lynx enjoys getting up to in the wild!
