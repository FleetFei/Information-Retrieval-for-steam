I found it hard to give this game a genre, but it\u2019s a 2D action game, and I think you could get away with saying it\u2019s got platformer elements to it. Though, as the name suggests, everything in this game takes place in Zero Gravity, so you don\u2019t jump from platform to platform, but there is definitely an importance placed on navigating the world and avoiding environmental hazards -- which is a pretty core platformer skill that will be useful if you\u2019re good at platformers.<br><br>So I really liked the visuals; as I mentioned, the blue vs purpley red was pretty clear nice and early on; don\u2019t touch the purple \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665. Each level could last from 10 - 20 minutes for me, and while there are plenty of checkpoints, there are things that can make you have to start the whole level over again. If you run out of energy, or respawn too many times at the same wormhole (which are the checkpoints) the game will just send you to the very beginning of the level. And I\u2019ve gotta say the level of frustration I experienced while playing much of this game really ruined the beauty of it. There was one moment that is so well done in terms of production. The music and atmosphere was just right for me in this moment, and I loved it -- until I began to get frustrated. I don\u2019t think the punishing nature of the game is the problem, but it often feels unfair. Though I will give the developer props for introducing new mechanics and enemies continually throughout the game. It always felt fresh.<br><br>I liked the story. And, I don\u2019t mean that in a bad way, it was obviously intentional that the world feel lonely.
