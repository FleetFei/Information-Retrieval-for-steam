girlfriend from the evil Dinosaur God. It is a retro styled platformer.<br><br>In each level you must cross the map before dying from hunger. There is a hunger meter at the top of the screen that is constantly decreasing but you can fill it up by eating food lying around. You don't have much time to think in game. There is a large variety of creative creatures trying to stop you like mexican skeletons, pooping birds, lazer firing bears, exploding bats and so on.<br><br>To help you out, there are weapons and dinosaurs. There are four weapons each with it's own damage, trajectory and recharge speed and five dinosaurs each with it's special ability. Dinosaurs also have their own life, so if you die when you're riding a dinosaur only the dinosaur dies. You can find weapons and dinosaurs lying around in each level or you can buy them in a store in some levels. When you finish a level you get to keep the weapon and dinosaur and choose which weapon and dinosaur to use in the next levels. However if you die while holding a weapon or riding a dinosaur you lose it and cannot chose it anymore.<br><br>There is some variety of maps like forests, deserts or under water maps. Sometimes you get to chose the path you want to go but not often. It's a nice little feature but doesn't really make much of a difference. The game is almost entirely linear. There are also bosses at the end of each section. The bosses are funny but also very easy to defeat.<br><br>The graphics style and feel in general is simillar to Alex Kid. Very simple pleasant graphics. The game isn't hard at all and it is in fact relaxing. You can beat the game easily without the dinosaurs or more powerful weapons. The only real difficulty is that the controls are kind of stiff and you don't know what's next. It is a trial and error kind of game so you will die but you can easily avoid the obstacles in your next attempt. One annoying aspect is that when you retry you must go through the weapon and dinosaur choice everytime. I don't see the point of getting to chose the weapons\/dinosaurs. Such a feature just makes the levels you want easier in an already easy game. Perhaps it would have been better if these weapons\/dinosaurs only belonged to some levels or if it was a permanent upgrade system.<br><br>So this is an easy, relaxing and short game. It's not a bad game but not an amazing game either. I think that was a perfectly fine decision to have made for this kind of game. On the surface that seems simple enough and perhaps for the first few levels it is almost that simple, however, as you make your way through the game new elements are added that increase the challenge. To further arrest your advancement the ground becomes even more broken up. This forces you to have to make more leaps of faith and then of course new enemies will also appear to hinder your progress. Those are the fundamental elements of a standard classic platformer. Now that you have the basic idea on what kind of game we are talking about, let's focus on Dinocide itself. <br><br>I mentioned Dinocide was an Adventure-Platformer so let's talk about the Adventure portion. As stated above, the game's entire story is that the Caveman wants to rescue his significant other. He begins his quest with a never ending handful of stones and ventures out in the dangerous wilds. As he travels through the levels he will start to encounter items that will make his journey a little bit easier. First off, a handful of stones is great in a pinch but there are far more effective weapons to deal with enemies. The throwing axe will likely be the first one of these valuable weapons you will aquire. It basically works in the same manner as the stones but it does more damage as it arcs through the air. The boomerang will fly in circular pattern around the screen near the hero and then return to him. It has the possibility to hit multiple enemies or hit the same enemy multiple times before returning. The last one is the arrow that you can throw straight across the screen and isn't affected by gravity like the stones or axe. Having only four weapons at your disposal might sound a little disappointing but it really isn\u2019t. It\u2019s more than made up for by giving you the ability to ride one of 5 dinosaurs (and a secret mechsuit) too! Each of these have their own special abilities and also increase your chances for survival. <br><br>Most games typically have a health bar that displays how many more hits you can take before you will die. In order to prevent you from taking your time and being meticulously careful as you progress through a level, most classic games will also have some sort of timer that will cause you to lose if it runs out. Dinocide combines these two effects into a Hunger Meter. This Hunger Meter will deplete naturally over time in order to prevent you from standing still too long. It will be further depleted every time you take damage. This makes Dinocide rather challenging. Not only are you having to carefully make your way from the left side of the screen to the right side, you are also having to ensure you grab all the food  along the way in order to avoid starving to death. It's a hard call to say if this adds to the fun of the game or detracts from it. There were many times when I was within striking distance of the exit or food and yet died of starvation just because I happened to have got hit a couple of times on my journey to that point. This caused me to have to repeat some levels far more times than I am willing to admit to. It is separate from your own Hunger Meter. All food sources you touch will go to refilling the hunger of the dinosaur. Once the dinosaur starves to death, you can continue on with your own Hunger Meter. Hopefully its sacrifice won't have been in vain! However, just sacrificing dinosaurs all willy-nilly isn't exactly in your best interest. Any weapons you buy or find during the level, as well as any dinosaurs you find, will be stored at your camp if you successfully manage to keep them around until you reach the exit. In fact, if you are super careful you can reuse the same weapon and dinosaur until the very end of the game. Even if you find a new weapon or dinosaur on the level, assuming you don't die, your old weapons and dinosaurs along with your new weapons and dinosaurs will be found waiting for you at your camp until you are willing to use them again.<br><br>There is another meter in the game that in my opinion is a little on the short side. It's the Stamina Bar. To prevent you from just spamming your attacks, each time you use your weapon, your bar depletes a bit. If you do multiple attacks in short succession, it leaves you unable to attack further until the bar refills itself enough. This made fighting the enemies much more challenging, and arguably made the game a little more enjoyable.<br><br>As you play through Dinocide you will cross between multiple different biomes each with their own enemies. You will also encounter multiple bosses on your way to rescue the kidnapped cave princess. Luckily, with bosses, the Hunger Meter turns into a standard Health Meter and no longer depletes over time. As an added bonus, if you really enjoy fighting the bosses, there is even a Boss Rush mode which will let you focus on just that part of the game. In order to help promote replayability, there are alternate routes you can take through the game. Each level follows the all or nothing format. Either you make it to the very end in a single attempt, or you lose everything you had with you and have to start the level over again from the very beginning.<br><br>Rather than collectable coins, gems are used as currency. Many levels offer a shop, which will sell you a weapon, a dinosaur and\/or a bit of food. If you have the gems to do so, I'd highly recommend collecting the dinosaurs and weapons. I ended the game with a surplus of gems and was really wishing I\u2019d purchased more weapons when I got the end boss.<br><br>Pros:<ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li>Classic Adventure-Platformer that stays true to the genre<br><\/li><li>Dinosaur mounts<br><\/li><li>Game length is appropriate for the genre.
