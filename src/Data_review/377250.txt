class=\"postedDate\">Posted: September 27, 2015\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t<div class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\t<div class=\"bb_h1\">Introduction<\/div>Subaeria caught my eye just recently when it was first released on Early Access. An action puzzle game where you have to use the enemies against each other and the environment sounded promising and like a unique experience. And the fact that the levels would be procedurally generated made it sounds even better. So is it what I hoped it would be or did it fail to meet my expectations?<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay<\/div>The gameplay consists of one simple objective really: move through the labyrinth while destroying the Cleaners, robots that are after you and who will try to attack you because you have no more credits left in your account. It\u2019s a simple story, but it gets the job done and like many other games it doesn\u2019t need a good story in the first place for it to be enjoyable.<br><br>Your character will be able to talk to other characters in some of the areas you visit, but none of them have anything particularly helpful to say, although they sometimes do give you a hint about where you need to to go. Problem is, however, that you usually have to head in a certain direction anyway and you\u2019ll most likely get there even without having talked to them.<br><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=525756987\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_1\">http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=525756987<\/a><br>Each area consists of a square layout, a block if you will, and the entrance and exit will be locked until the Cleaners have been defeated. This usually means you have to trick them into moving through the lasers or by letting them bump into each other. But for this to happen they must be of the opposite color. So a yellow enemy can bump into a yellow fellow without any problems, but let them bump into a red or blue enemy and they\u2019ll both blow up. Same principle goes for the lasers, of course.<br><br>You will do all of this with the help of a little drone, who can be used to help defeat enemies. You can let him pick up powerups that can be found throughout the levels. You have two slots, a left and a right one, and if both slots are full then the powerup you pick up will replace one of them (depending on which trigger you pick it up with). Unfortunately it\u2019s very unclear what the powerups exactly do without opening the proper menu. During my playthroughs I just started using them randomly, hoping at least one of them would be helpful. And even then it was hard to see what they would exactly do. Note that the game is still in Early Access, but this is definitely something that needs to be addressed; let it be a small tutorial, (paused) textbox or just a floating name\/description above the powerups itself.<br><br>The destroyed enemies will usually leave credits for you to pick up that you can sometimes use to upgrade specific skills, but since the labyrinth is randomized this isn\u2019t always possible (in my experience so far). You\u2019ll also be able to find some sort of \u2018perks\u2019 from time to time that will give you things like extra health and increased speed (for your drone).<br><br>There are multiple endings per character, though, but unfortunately the endings just aren\u2019t terribly interesting. I\u2019m not saying they\u2019re not worthy of your time, but if you\u2019ve finished the game once you probably won\u2019t play it again with that same character, unless you\u2019re absolutely in love with her (\/him?).<br><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=525756901\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_2\">http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=525756901<\/a><br>One other thing I\u2019d like to mention is the camera, which shows everything from the top. But sometimes it\u2019s hard to see where you\u2019re standing or walking and since you can\u2019t rotate the camera this can lead to frustration, since you\u2019ll bump into enemies or lasers regularly. It\u2019s also hard to see depth at times, especially when you have to jump onto boxes to get to higher places.<br><br>But despite the frustration it\u2019s still a unique experience. At the moment it needs a lot of work in order for it to be brilliant, though, but then again, it\u2019s still an Early Access game. Who knows what we\u2019ll see in a few months time?<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Audio &amp; Visuals<\/div>The audio is fine: it feels a little minimalist at the moment, but let\u2019s just assume it\u2019s still a work in progress and more music and effects will be patched in later. No real complaints here.<br><br>What isn\u2019t fine is the performance, which is absolutely horrible. It\u2019s not unplayable by any means, but there\u2019s a bit of lag and the framerate fluctuates a lot. Something I did not expect to see in a game like this. Again, it\u2019s a work in progress, but I\u2019ve played tens of Early Access games that were way better optimized than this. It sometimes took over 2 minutes to load a level: that\u2019s just insane.<br><br>The graphics themselves are simple. There\u2019s not much style. Every area has a lot of metalic objects laying around and they don\u2019t have any personality. I personally felt like every room was pretty much the same, with (some of) the objects moved to different places. There is a lack of variety in the level design and it\u2019s a shame, because the setting is great (although unexplained) and there\u2019s so much more room for added details and different locales.<br><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=525756873\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_3\">http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=525756873<\/a><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Length\/Replay Value<\/div>I won\u2019t sugarcoat it, but the length right now is just not worth the asking price. A playthrough will take about 1-2 hours, depending on your skill. You can repeat the game as many times as you want, but it won\u2019t show you anything new once you finished it. I\u2019m sure the game will be much longer by the time the final version releases, but right now I would recommend to wait for a sale or at least more content, unless you\u2019re intrigued by the concept and want to experience it all for yourself.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Conclusion<\/div>A somewhat unique experience that could\u2019ve benefited from some extra content and more polish. Right now it\u2019s a good demo (not technically, though) that partially shows you what they hope to achieve eventually, but in my opinion it\u2019s not worth the current price. Does that mean the game sucks? No, not at all: it\u2019s just that the price is too high at this very moment.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Too long, didn\u2019t read<\/div>Pros:<br>+ Multiple characters (although only 1 is playable at the moment)<br>+ Multiple endings<br>+ Replayability because of the procedurally generated labyrinths<br>+ Unique way to defeat enemies\/progress<br><br>Mixed:<br>+\/- Too many rooms are repeated (and therefore don\u2019t feel unique)<br>+\/- Not enough variety in map design<br>+\/- Despite using the same mechanics the boss fight is still a little unclear and could benefit from more (visual) feedback<br><br>Cons:<br>- Unoptimized performance<br>- Skills have unclear descriptions<br>- Too little content, even for Early Access<br><br><table class=\"bb_table\">    <tr>        <th>Please note: a Steam key for this game was provided by the developer.<\/th>    <\/tr><\/table><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"><b>Rating: 6.1 (out of 10)<\/b><\/div><br><br><table class=\"bb_table\"><tr><th>Want to read more of my unbiased reviews?
