Half of your time may be spent watching the story progress with the occasional choice that isn\u2019t flavor dialogue, but the other half is spent walking around in sometimes the most horrible camera angles I\u2019ve ever seen looking for prompts and actions you can take to y\u2019know...get yourself the hell out of this point and click stuff and get to the story, the only thing anyone ever talks about with this game. The point and click portion is not bad, in fact it\u2019s cool if you enjoy the point and click genre, but if you\u2019re in it for the story, the vagueness of what you have to do and lack of direction can get mildly annoying. Also, there\u2019s a lot of quick time events in this game where if you fail you die or someone else story-critical dies and then you have to go back checkpoint...those were...unexpected for a game like this. I\u2019ll let you all decide how you feel about that.<br><br>Now this next thing can be considered a pro or a con really. The writing of dialogue in The Walking Dead is on par with that of some emotional hollywood experience. From a creative writing perspective what this basically means is that it\u2019s GREAT at evoking emotion and getting you attached...but the actual quality of what\u2019s written for these characters to say? It doesn\u2019t sound like realistic conversation and doesn\u2019t even sound like good on-paper conversation material. If you like generic hollywood writing with zero depth but maximum emotional captivity? You\u2019ll love it. If you wanted a bit more realism and depth to the way characters interact with each other? You won\u2019t.<br><br>The graphics are alright. They\u2019re exactly what you\u2019d expect from this company in 2012 and maybe more than I expected from this genre from my previous experiences with it? More of a pro than a con unless you\u2019re someone that NEEDS graphics. The con here is the animations. The animations are bad, which makes sense for this genre. It\u2019s worst with the facial expressions. Some of the expressions characters make are hilariously disgusting and ugly at how failed of an attempt they are to mimic real life expressions and reactions.<br><br>The sounds that are there are good. They\u2019re impactful and realistic. They help add to the immersion of the game and reinforce what happens in the story. The sounds during the point and click portions of the game though? There could have been more ambience as everything seems rather empty when you\u2019re not pushing through a storyline in this game.<br><br>And now my BIGGEST gripe with this game which I have saved for last for those of you that are willing to make it past my lesser critiquing of this overwhelmingly positively received game. My biggest gripe is this. \u201cThe game series adapts to the choices you make. The story is tailored by how you play.\u201d This is the biggest lie I have read all year. The game lets you make choices sure, but it certainly doesn\u2019t adapt for more than a few minutes to any of them. If someone was supposed to die and you don\u2019t let them die or kill them yourself? The game does it for you in every single occasion. That\u2019s why by the end? And this might be a slight spoiler so I\u2019ll leave out specifics- Every single person will find themselves with the SAME exact group of companions give or take one very uninteresting side-character. There are end-of-episode screens that show you your choices compared to the choices other made and reactions in-game to the choices you\u2019ve made plenty of times. But none of them matter because there\u2019s no consequences for any of them. Even if you do what the game considers the right thing? It will still punish you for doing the wrong thing if that\u2019s what the writers have decided should be the choice you should have picked. The point I\u2019m trying to make is that this story never diverges for longer than one conversation. This is not a dynamic choose your own adventure game. This is a linear story that tries to give you the illusion that you have a say in anything that happens at all, which you don\u2019t.<br><br>Now that being said. Assuming you aren\u2019t already writing a hate comment or haven\u2019t already disliked this and left because, \u201c\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665 this guy he doesn\u2019t know what he\u2019s talking about. This game is amazing.\u201d. I STILL LOVE THIS GAME. I am so happy I played this game and I can\u2019t wait to play The Walking Dead Season 2. That being said, there\u2019s a lot of design choices that hold this back from being a perfect game. I still recommend it, and I\u2019m going to have to give it a 7\/10. 8\/10 is too generous for everything that\u2019s wrong with it. 6\/10 is too harsh for everything that\u2019s right with it. This is quite simply a good game. And in my 10 point review score that\u2019s what a seven means. \u201cGood, but with plenty of room to improve.\u201d. If you want a really damn good interactive story to follow? Get this game. You\u2019ll love it, I did. Just make sure you\u2019re willing to look past all the flaws that the game has that I had to learn the hard way.<br><br>Now as any of you who have watched or read any of my previous reviews know I like take on a very, \u201cIs it worth the price\u201d mentality at the end. Full price? $25? For a 10-11 hour game for completionists and a much shorter game for those that are rushing through the story? With a $5 DLC tacked onto the side to bridge the gap between this game and it\u2019s sequel? With zero replayability to the story due to the linear nature and how your choices don\u2019t matter? No. Don\u2019t get it at full price.
