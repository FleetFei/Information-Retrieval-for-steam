Running Man in space is the best way I can think to describe <i>Watch This!<\/i>, a sci-fi, horror\u2019ish game that has you running through a space maze, avoiding obstacles and collecting coins, with the goal of getting your family to like you enough to help you escape.<br><br>And how do you get your family to like you? By buying them items, of course! Perhaps a dinner. Or a game system. Because nothing says \u201cI love you and please don\u2019t let me die\u201d like a video game.<br><br>You begin in a random room, with a camera on you, where it is safe. This room changes every time, but there aren\u2019t a lot of different spots to spawn, so it\u2019ll become pretty obvious where you are rather quickly. And like the room itself, the maze doesn\u2019t seem to change (at least from what I\u2019ve told). If you spawn in a particular room, take a left, then two rights, then two lefts and you wind up in a room with a pool of water, then that will always be where you end up. There is no procedural generation here.<br><br>The game looks very nice. It\u2019s nothing to write home about, but the graphics do the game justice. Lights sparkle and reflect, coins have a neat shimmer to them. Sound is a similar story. You can pick up on environmental sounds pretty easily, and will be able to tell if the enemy you hear is to your left, right, or if he\u2019s behind you and you\u2019re dead. The announcer is very annoying. I imagine he\u2019s supposed to be extremely over the top, but it just becomes grating. Controlling your character is simple enough, but the game does feel stiff at certain parts, especially when jumping off from the top of a ladder.<br><br>Once you\u2019ve collected enough coins, you can buy items for your family at one of the panels spread throughout the maze. Each subsequent item will cost more than the one before it. Once you\u2019ve gotten a family member on your side, you then work on escaping the maze. Obstacles in your way will be water (because really, what would a platformer be where the character is incapable of swimming?), spikes that push down from the ceiling, spike pits, fleshy monsters, and monsters with a television for a head.<br><br>You\u2019ll be able to collect drinks to restore your health and hamburgers for I don\u2019t know what purpose. You can\u2019t eat them, and when I throw them at enemies, they do nothing. Good chance there\u2019s a purpose to them and I\u2019m just being dumb. You\u2019ll also be able to collect two powerups which will be random. The panel that gives you these look like it\u2019s going to be a memory match game, but whatever two cards you flip over are what your specials will be, and that\u2019s it; those will be your options until you die or escape.<br><br>Family generation is probably one of the \u201cbest\u201d features. Before every game, you\u2019re shown a picture of your family, which is randomly generated. My favorite, by far, was having Doc Brown as my father, a woman in a hijab as my mother, some trashy looking Kardashian (so any Kardashian, really) as my wife, and Kim Jong-Un as my son. Family dysfunction at its best.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Pros<\/div><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li>Interesting concept with a ridiculous story<br><\/li><li>Excellent visuals<br><\/li><li>Fast paced gameplay<br><\/li><li>Slight replaybility in terms of powerups and spawn location<br><\/li><li>Cheap, and has trading cards<\/li><\/ul><div class=\"bb_h1\">Cons<\/div><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li>Not much replayability, as once you\u2019ve escaped, you\u2019ve seen what the game offers<br><\/li><li>Occasionally stiff controls<br><\/li><li>Extremely annoying announcer<\/li><\/ul><div class=\"bb_h1\">Summary<\/div><i>Watch This!<\/i> is well worth its asking price. It\u2019s a fun jaunt through the maze, avoiding enemies and obstacles designed to kill you horrifically, but it\u2019s over rather quick, and doesn\u2019t have much incentive to do it again. Still in early access, I see a lot of potential for this to expand into something much more, and will be following the game\u2019s progress. Pick it up!
