class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tExcellent foundation for a game, with a lot of potential!<br><br>Overview: Essentially it's an arena deathmatch in a space-ish environment. Right now some maps have land, some don't, but all maps have some degree of cover one way or another.  Where this differs from usual arena DM rigamarole is in the crafting of your own vehicle to take to the fight (a la Robocraft(Grrr) or Crossout). The building is done by placing a number of different kinds of blocks (hulls, armor, weapons, engines, abilities, energy generation &amp; storage, etc.) around a central core, then take it to battle after which you will recieve XP for the vehicle as well as some &quot;junk&quot;(in-game currency) to upgrade with. There are currently 4 different types of ship you can build, plus a sort of &quot;starter&quot; ship to learn &amp; earn on. Each different class has it's own total block limits, it's own strengths and weaknesses and it's own set of abilities you can equip it with.  The matches are short and sweet (avoids any tedium) and accompanied by a pretty nifty little rock track throughout. <br><br>Some finer points: Game IS early access, so expect changes. Also, as such, it's continuously being tweaked in almost all aspects; balance, abilities, limitations, everything. The developers on this one seem genuinely interested in making a good game, and the changes reflect that. As you would expect with an E.A. game lots of it needs work, like balance, for instance, but things are continuously being tweaked to improve the experience as well as new features being added to same effect.<br><br>The game DOES support an in-game market (for skins, and premium time) and DOES support a real cash currency.  Some (me included) find some of the prices outrageous, but developers have already stated that they are working to make them more realistic (some proof of dev. involvement).<br><br>Great game, great potential, hope it goes far, hope they don't go the Robocraft route and drill it into the ground with money-grubbing and forum pandering and screwing their own playerbase.<br><br>Plus, it's free.
