Fifteen minutes later, which is a standard round, and the following screen shot was the result:<br><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"https:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/linkfilter\/?url=http:\/\/images.akamai.steamusercontent.com\/ugc\/450733824050078155\/6DE79C12422F16121EBAEB62428326AD3CD7F63D\/?interpolation=lanczos-none&amp;output-format=jpeg&amp;output-quality=95&amp;fit=inside|1024:576&amp;composite-to=*,*|1024:576&amp;background-color=black\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\" >link to screenshot<\/a><br><br>I am used to playing with that type of ping being from Australia. If we Aussies don\u2019t have local servers then the ping is going to be 200-350 on a good connection so please ignore the ping result. What I am specifically asking you to look at here is the scoreboard particularly \u2018kills.\u2019 This is a standard result of games with bots. The results were similar when I started a game with just one other person. You only lose \u201ckills\u201d when you commit suicide (-1 to kills each time you commit suicide). Rounds are fifteen minutes long. The \u201cbest\u201d bot in this round killed itself at minimum fifteen times. The other two were in the twenties for a total of 63 suicides in a 15 minute round. That equates to 4.2+ suicides by the computer AI every minute. It\u2019s more than that really if you want to get into the mathematics. There were 129 total kills in my game. I had 14 total kills and I may have killed myself more than once by accident as well. That type of AI is just unacceptable in a game like this when there are only 4 players\/bots on a map.<br><br>When playing with just another player the bots are just as bad at killing themselves. The game turns into a bit of a farce as you are constantly chasing your real life opponent around the map and taking the cheap bot kills when they\u2019re not too busy killing themselves. So how do they manage to kill themselves? In the most stupid way possible. There were multiple occasions where I would think that I was getting close to killing a bot and it would turn to face a wall, shoot a grenade on the wall directly in front of its face and literally stand there as it blew itself to smithereens. The game is called Grenade Madness and not Suicide Madness.<br><br>Unfortunately when I returned to play the next morning the servers were once again empty. The devs were awesome enough to provide a bounty of keys so I ran a quick giveaway on the Steamified Steam group in order to get some community members to play the game with. What I discovered was that you really need three or four players to have a meaningful game. A single bot wont kill you but it\u2019s a pretty cool game when you have a number of real people to play with. It\u2019s an easy game to learn \u2013 if you\u2019ve ever played any sort of shooter you\u2019re going to be set before you start \u2013 so it makes rounds easy to get into and a lot of fun. If you have a group of competent players it\u2019s going to be some of the best fun that you can have and even more so if they\u2019re a group of friends. So as much as I\u2019ve \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665ed about the shortcomings of the game there are a lot of strengths and the game is a ton of fun.  The action is fast and furious, the kills and suicides occur very quickly, and given the difference in weapons (knife, grenade and block placing launcher) there is room for quick thinking strategies to be employed.<br><br>My favorite part of the game play are the knife fights. Though grenades can be launched and that is fun there\u2019s nothing quite like being in the midst of close combat where you knife your opponent to death. And if there\u2019s another player in the distance who shoots a grenade into your area he can get a smart double kill. You can even use the block grenade to put an obstacle in the unsuspecting way of your opponent. Although I must note that one time that I tried this I got my opponent stuck in the wall. The assorted arrangement of power ups are also quite handy and well thought out.<br><br>I didn\u2019t investigate this myself but apparently you can change a number of the settings within the game. A member of the Steamified community established his own random room and changed the base settings of the game. At one point he, and the bots, were running around three to five times faster than what I was capable of. Therefore if you are to play on a random server I would be a little skeptical as to what the settings in that particular room are.<br><br>I must note that I loved the voice overs. When you commit suicide, for example, the robotic computer voice over will say \u201cWhat have you done?\u201d which is quite funny and when somebody else kills you you\u2019ll occasionally hear \u201cTry harder\u201d which made me laugh quite sadistically. It\u2019s small touches such as these that really can aide the game to reach its potential. The graphics are suitable but you\u2019ll be sure to notice that there are currently clipping issues within the game.<br><br>I think that about does it but I\u2019d like to close this by thanking the Steamified community for being as fantastically awesome as you are.
