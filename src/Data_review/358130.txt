best games in its class, a Roguelike-inspired strategy\/adventure game that will leave you wanting more. Easy enough to beat several times in one day if you're dedicated, yet hard enough to make every victory feel like you clawed your way to it from the depths of hades.<br><br>If you're looking at this game and you're even half sure you want it, get it. If looking at it fills with you a warm and gooey sense of longing for the hours spent huddled around CRT monitors taking turns playing The Oregon Trail, buy it. Even if you're a more modern gamer, but you're a fan of FTL, Caves of Qud, the Binding of Isaac, or similar, buy it.<br><br>Need more convincing? Read on.<br><br><b>The Main Ramble<\/b><br>Sometimes when a game developers look back to the past for inspiration, the results may drip with nostalgia but ultimately come out uninspiring. At other times, however, a game can look back and learn from both the past and the present to create something truly special.<br><br>This is one of those latter games, the ones that make us remember why we still like pixel graphics well into the 21st century. <br><br>The Curious Expedition is a delightfully fun exploration game set in a very &quot;Pulp Action&quot; Historical Fantasy setting. Think Indiana Jones, or the works of Jules Verne. You control one explorer plus a handful of companions (usually 4) as they trek through uncharted wildernesses in several flavors, always looking for a Golden Pyramid. Along the way way you'll meet native tribes, encounter wild animals, explore caves, investigate ancient shrines, and more. You will need to survive six such expeditions, each one longer and harder, but with greater rewards than the last, in order to finish the game. In order to &quot;win&quot; the game you will have to, on top of that, be the most famous explorder in the world, and there are four other explorers working hard to take that title from you.<br><br>What makes The Curious Expedition shine is the way it perfectly captures the 90s adventure game feel, without falling prey to being so obsessed with a bygone era of gaming that they can't make significant improvements on the model. The strong Roguelike influences give the game more replayability than most older adventure games (some of which are really only good for one playtrhough), and both the interface and the mechanics are a breath of fresh air.<br><br>One fair complaint about the game is that it is a bit of a one-trick pony. If you are looking for a deep sense of accomplishment such as filling up particular collections of rare artifacts will provide, you may not be happy here. This isn't a game about, long-term goals, it's about short-term survival and short-medium-term treasure hunting. Instead of the long term approach, The Curious Expedition instead opts to give you a fairly digestible experience, one in which a winning game can be finished in under 3 hours without breaking a sweat.<br><br>The end result is that, each game, when played well, ends up feeling like solving a good puzzle. If you think of TCE as being almost like Sudoku rather than trying to think of it as a game primarily about exploration, you may find yourself very satisfied. That's not to say there isn't still plenty of theme, however, the game is rich with little surpries that will continue to delight you many hours in. What utlimately makes the puzzle worth coming back to though is the complexity of it. There are so many variables, so many ways to solve the problem of how to survive six expeditions while staying the most famous, so many possibilities hidden within the map generation, ready to delight and baffle you.<br><br>All in all, The Curious Expedition is a delightful game about making hard choices and then either cheering at the results or laughing because you can't believe how wrong things went. It's a game about being too broke to afford enough food for your expedition and then lucking your way into enough supplies to get you through. Then on your next expedition, you stock up on tools and supplies, burn through them all like they're water in the desert, and die with the pyramid in sight.<br><br><b>Thoughts on the Future<\/b><br><br>Still not sure? Maybe some information about where The Curious Expedition is headed will push you over the edge:<br><br>The recent Arctic update has proven to be the breath of fresh air the game needed, and the new challenges and rewards of arctic environments are incredibly fun. The fact that the development team has made it clear that they would like to produce more new content on a similar scale is very exciting. The game is already a great value that will entertain you for many hours, so any expansion of content is only going to increase the longevity of a game I can already see myself playing for a long time.<br><br>On the note of improvements, the fact that modding is also a planned feature is huge. TCE's fantastic framework opened up for anyone to modify will create infinite possibilites for adventure.<br><br><b>Okay But Seriously Haven't I Said Enough?<\/b><br>I paid full price for this game and I don't regret it, so even if you're still unsure at least think about getting it on sale.
