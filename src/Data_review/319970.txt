a veteran detective and an ex-criminal trying to solve a murder and investigate other criminal activity. They won\u2019t do this as a team; you\u2019ll control both independently. The developer states that this experience was made for fans of HBO\u2019s hit series The Wire and Telltale Games\u2019 The Walking Dead. Obviously, these weren\u2019t the only inspirations and the game is actually meant to be played by anyone who\u2019s even remotely interested in it, or by anyone who\u2019s interested in adventure games in general. Read on to see what I think of it.<br><br>In the first episode (called Where The Dead Lie) you first control a veteran detective who\u2019s looking into a murder case. During the game you\u2019ll eventually swap with an ex-criminal who\u2019s forced into helping out with the investigations. I\u2019m not going to tell you more about the story; as a story-driven adventure game it\u2019s important that you experience as much of it by yourself.<br>During this first episode you\u2019ll be clicking on several points of interest. These are all clearly shown with a yellow border around them, so there\u2019s no pixel hunting involved like many, many other adventure games out there. Click on a border and you\u2019ll learn more about it. Looking\/reading is your primary action when investigating an object or person and sometimes it\u2019s possible to switch between actions by scrolling the mouse. You\u2019ll be able to easily pick things up and talk to people this way.<br>By playing you\u2019ll also be able to unlock character information of people you meet and even though it\u2019s not necessary to collect them all (or at all, really) I can see people replaying the game just to complete their \u2018collection\u2019. It\u2019s not quite similar to The Wolf Among Us\u2019 fairytale creatures, but in a way it is.<br>The puzzles are made with a casual audience in mind, meaning anyone can pick up and play this game without having too much trouble completing it. To be honest I doubt that there are people that won\u2019t be able to finish it at all; in fact, I don\u2019t think you can even call them puzzles. It\u2019s all so subtle that it all feels part of the story, which is good, but if I\u2019m going to play as an investigator I want to be challenged, even if it\u2019s for a little bit. In this case the gameplay is very similar to The Walking Dead, which was basically non-existent. It would be nice to see future episodes have a little more depth, but I also hope they\u2019ll keep the gameplay consistent. Otherwise it might just get messy.<br>So, the gameplay adds nothing new to the genre but still, what is there works as far as the story is concerned.<br><br>The graphics, unfortunately, are a bit of a mixed bag. The black and white comic book-like graphics are done extremely well. In fact, these are just excellent and if the entire game would\u2019ve been made with this style in mind it would\u2019ve been one pretty game. But for some reason (maybe budgetary or artistic reasons) the game uses several art styles, which gives the game an inconsistent look.<br>One of the things that comes to mind here are the portraits (i.e. conversations). These just aren\u2019t very pretty (putting it a bit more mildly here), and overall they kind of distract from the game. <br>The environments and characters (in this case not the portraits) on the other hand get the job done, but the characters could benefit from some extra animations.<br>There\u2019s no voice acting in the game, just text. Of course, there is music; this is very moody and atmospheric, and while it doesn\u2019t make voice acting obsolete, it didn\u2019t make me miss it either. <br>I agree with the choice (?)  of not using any kind of voice acting; if you have a small budget you don\u2019t want to ruin the immersion with poor and\/or cheap voice acting.<br><br>For a first episode The Detail has an average length. It took me somewhere between 60-90 minutes to finish it. Clearly it isn\u2019t very long but you can replay the game again and make different choices that will affect the story. I suspect the consequences will be even more obvious during some of the later episodes. I thought the length was good. It was long enough to keep me interested in the story and short enough for the gameplay to become too shallow.<br><br>The Detail isn\u2019t quite there yet when it comes to being a classic, but at this stage it\u2019s too early to tell how the story will even unfold. There isn\u2019t much known about the rest of the episodes, except for the fact that the entire game will consist of five of them. There aren\u2019t any release dates so we\u2019ll just have to wait and see. I personally expect an episode to be released every two months, but that\u2019s just me guessing. In any case, The Detail is an interesting game that\u2019s extremely light on the puzzles but it is its story that actually makes me want to keep playing. The first episode ends with a nice cliffhanger and I\u2019m anxious to see and know what will happen next.
