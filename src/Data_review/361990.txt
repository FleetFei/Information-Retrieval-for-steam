fire, shoot your friends into the face, run away from the horror in the sewers, fight off an alien infection and throw the dead bodies of your former friends towards Rednecks to defend yourself? Then this may be a game you should take a closer look.<br><br><b>Lakeview Cabin Collection<\/b> is a collection of four plus one short games (plus another very short drug induced fever dream game) centering around well known horror movies. You start the game in front of the cinema which is at the same time the main hub to choose which episode from Lakeview Cabin III to VI you want to watch\/play and also a mini-level itself. All movies have in common that there is absolutely no hand-holding. It is a sandbox experience where you have to figure out everything yourself compirsing with what an object does, where you can\/have to use it and actually starting with what object you are holding since it is not always clear (due to the simplified graphic). This is often fun and interesting, mostly refreshing and sometimes really, really frustrating. Also in common is the fact that in every episode you can switch between four playable characters. But let's look at each episode separately.<br><br>Lakeview Cabin III is the &quot;first&quot; movie you can choose and also the simplest one. You and your friends are counselors in a camp and everything is fun until something murderous will start hunting you. The goal here is simply surviving and killing what is hunting you. Every item is always in the same place so you can slowly refine your strategy. Find the shotgun, try to chop it down with the axe or just burn it with fire. Or feel the despair losing one character after another.<br><br>Lakeview Cabin IV is the second movie. You and your friends ran out of fuel in front of a mansion somewhere in nowhere. You have to explore the surroundings and find four canisters and bring them back to the car. In contrast to the previous episode there is some randomization including the order of rooms and where what items\/enemies are placed. Sounds easy, right? Did I mention the bloodthirsty Rednecks? Well, still easy. Did I mention the rat mutants?<br><br>Lakeview Cabin V is both fascinating and frustrating. Like episode III everything is always in the same place. Goal is to put an undieable killer to its final rest with the help of some demons. In contrast to all other episodes you can't really die here. It is more of a puzzle episode. But here the sandboxing gets really frustrating because the puzzles are really hard to figure out. Episode V appeared some time after the initial release. As such there was a community waiting for it and figuring everything out themselves. Brilliant idea on the paper but frustrating in reality since you have nearly no chance to get everything alone.<br><br>Lakeview Cabin VI is more action orientated again. It is more difficult in the puzzle aspect then episode III and IV but a lot easier than V. Problem is that you may be burnt out after episode V and not paying much attention to the obvious hints. In this episode you play a group of janitors, cleaning the space station after a party. Places are always same but item placement is randomized. This would be an easy job but someone might not have read the memo about not getting infected by alien parasites. And if you don't find it fast enough hell might break loose on the station in no time. And even if you find it ...... there is still good chance to die.<br><br>All in all there is much diversity in the episodes but you need to have a small experimentor gene in you (because using guides from the beginning would be boring as .....). In every episode there is also a small mini-game which can occupy you for some minutes. You can unlock those by completing the episode the first time.<br>The complete package is in total very enjoyable but you may find yourself burnt out slightly by episode V. Also do not be fooled by the cute graphics. The game can be - at times - surprisingly suspenseful and violent.<br><br>PS: If you have any question just write in the comments and I will try to get back to them as fast as possible.\t\t\t\t\t<div
