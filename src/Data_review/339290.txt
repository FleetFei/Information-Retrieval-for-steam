Overview<\/div>Castle is a simple unique puzzle game that has a steep learning curve, low replay ability and some interesting mechanics that really bring the game to it's name. I enjoyed this game for a certain amount of time, then it just got boring because of the difficulty. I am however recommending this game because even though it has low replay value and does get boring at times, there was a nice session of when it was fun to play. I do suggest playing in short sessions though as you might get bored of this game if you keep failing.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Game Play<\/div>This game is very unique in the sense of the puzzle side of things. You have to create a castle with the tiles of the castle at the top of the screen as the scroll from right to left. All you have to do is drag them into the correct places and make the castle. Simple enough right? That's what I thought. The games concept is great, don't get me wrong. I haven't seen other puzzle games that are like this. There are a few problems with this though. First off, it's easy to misjudge what piece of the castle goes where. There where many times in the game where I would put a piece on the wrong slot just because I wasn't sure if it fitted there or not. This is not a puzzle factor of the game, it's simple misjudgement. If you combine that with the enemies you have to take care of, the time limit and the lives that you have then you are always on edge of this game. There are so many things to take into consideration that makes this game so difficult. This then makes you play a single level over and over again for like half an hour and that isn't fun. That also ties in with the repetitive nature of the game as each level is basically a harder version of the previous level. They add more enemies, and bonuses you have to buy every now and then. Despite all the bad things that  have said there are some fun elements to the game that I want to talk about. They do produce a small and pretty basic graph of how many of each enemy will be on that level. This means that you can plan in advanced and buy the things that can stop them from you building, each enemy has a different type of item that you can use to get rid of them. A fireball for a bird, anvil for a knights, dynamite for a mole ect. Along with the variety of enemies there are also 3 different variations of castles to build, plain, desert and ice which kinda separates the art style.<br><b>Score (Out Of 10): 7 - Would like to see more variations of the castles and less of a difficulty curve.<\/b><br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Graphics\/Art Style<\/div>The Art Style to this game isn't all that good or bad. It has nice, crisp and clean graphics for a cartoon like game. I personally don't really like the way this game looks, the Art Style looks too childish for me to really like it. I like some cartoon art styles but this just seems to go a bit overboard for me. I will score it fairly as I know some people do like this kind of Art Style<br><b>Score (Out Of 10): 7 - I can see why people would like the graphics, but for me personally I don't like them. They look too childish<\/b><br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Music &amp; Sounds<\/div>The music to this game is great. This means that there are only 3 different soundtracks that play throughout the entire game. Again gets very repetitive if your playing in a longer session. As for the sounds they are okay, the sound of a anvil hitting a knight on the head is pretty satisfying to say the least, and others like when you hit a fireball on the bird.<br><b>Score (Out Of 10): 8 - Would like to see more variation for soundtracks.<\/b><br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Conclusion<\/div>I would recommend this game to people who like puzzle games, I suggest playing in short periods to avoid not really having as much fun as you did the first time you played it.
