Now we question this little game, we belittle and ask it, &quot;Are you really what you claim to be?&quot; And that little game rear its Early Access head and said. &quot;Yes&quot;<br><br>And that my good man and woman of steam and of the internet is what Zotrix claim and manages to land for. An arcade shooter game with elements of modern day gaming mechanic instead of sticking to retro style graphics alone to earn free nostalgia point. It's uhh... fun. In a rather brutal and uncompromising way.<br><br><b> Gameplay <\/b><br>I wish I was joking but in this game of arcade shooter it actually has elements of trading and resource management. To give a little insights we human have basically conquer the world and made colonies on mars when suddenly *poof* aliens came and destroy us. So you, another faceless grunt who ate the propaganda the world leader told you must now defend the colonies and cargo ships from further incursion. For mankind!<br><br>You start with a peashooter and a weak ship then you must choose a mission, look longingly at the upgrades and then shoot all the enemies that come in your screen and not die! Basically thats it but the added gameplay elements made for a more different experience than the rest, for example powerups are limited only to upgrade your main gun but you can add other side weapons, drones and shields to your ships so it can take on the bigger foes that plague the later missions. Speaking of which missions have difficulty number with higher being more difficult and adding more varied enemy type. <br><br>Your peashooter cant last long without upgrades and Zotrix allow you to do just that with the money and materials you have earned! The inventory of each station is randomized when you arrived so some important upgrades like shields might not be available. Speaking of which you also get material needed to actually make those upgrade. I suppose you're not really buying them but rather employing the people on the station to make those upgrades. Make sense.<br><br>Eventually you start getting harder and harder missions but i manage to finish a level 10 threat mission with nothing more than my peashooter craft and a pair of very powerful side arm so that counts.<br><br>You can also nullify shots by shooting at it as it comes or if you're about to crash, pressing space ducks you into 3d space for a few moment so you can avoid all those 2d shots from the other spaceships.<br><br><b> Sound <\/b><br>Good, good. Lots of custom track in the game and most of them are techno beat which i like, hopefully the dev release the soundtrack. A minor complain is while the sound effects and music are awesome the difference a level 2 shot makes and a level 3 shot make is rather jarring at the moment.<br><br><b> Graphics <\/b><br>Nice retro graphics there, background is a clear contrast color along with the shots thus making it easy to see which is a common issue in a shoot em up game. <br><br>Graphics for the rest of the game is is functional and does not detract from the overall experience.
