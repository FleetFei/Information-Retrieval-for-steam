if short, first person puzzler with some original mechanics. Its main problem are probably some obvious similarities to Portal (you traverse a sequence of abstract, test-chamber like levels while a female voice talks to you), due to which it will automatically get compared to that game - and Portal was such an amazingly well designed, highly polished product that a decent little indie effort like MP will look a bit insipid next to it, especially since there isn't really anything that it does better or very different.<br><br>That said, if you take MP for what it is, you'll find an enjoyable first-person puzzle experience with some 3d platforming elements thrown in.<br><br>Gameplay consists mostly of finding your way to switches, which open doors or activate other elements in the chamber you're in. These other elements can be trampolines that let you jump higher, catapults that launch you in a certain direction, teleporters with coded targets, or so-called &quot;recall pads&quot; which allow for some interesting momentum-based puzzles when you teleport back to them while in motion. All this is embedded in a &quot;two worlds&quot; setting, where you have keep alternating between two different worlds to find your way. Most of these elements are well known from 2d platformers, but I haven't really seen them in a first-person puzzler before, so they still felt fairly fresh. I found the levels and puzzles competently designed, if a bit linear.<br><br>Most of the puzzles were fairly easy and straightforward, but solving them still felt like an accomplishment. The 3d platforming elements were a bit annoying in one particular level, but enjoyable in the others. Unfortunately you can easily fall into bottomless pits though, and then have to restart the level from scratch, which makes gameplay quite repetitive in levels that require precise jumping. I think it would have been more enjoyable to let the player just fall to a floor and let him try the jump again.<br><br>The narration starts out interesting, and benefits a lot from the excellent voice actor, but unfortunately peters out towards the end. Various references to quantum physics evoke a sense of mystery, but in the end they do not matter. While there is some sort of conclusion to the story, it doesn't really explain anything. Which is a pity, because some of the story ideas hinted at something interesting, and the voice actor would definitely have deserved a role that mattered more.<br><br>Graphically the game is rather bland. All gameplay takes place in abstract rooms with white or black walls, only towards the end there's a bit of neon aesthetics thrown in. The sound design is plain, but fitting for such a sterile environment.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Altogether, I recommend Measurement Project for fans of first-person puzzlers that also like some 3d platforming. Just don't expect Portal quality, and you'll probably enjoy what the game has to offer.<\/div><br>.<br><br>(Side notes:<br>1. As usual, please disregard my displayed playing time.
