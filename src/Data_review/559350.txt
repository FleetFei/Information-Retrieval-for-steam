- Fight for Haaria for free.\">Product received for free<\/div>\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t<div class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEurgava:- Fight for Haaria by Ibrahim Indra Baskara, is a very unique take on the RPG\/Strategy genre. It feels like your playing the original Diablo or Diablo II but in a story book mode, with a distinct Middle Eastern feel. This is not a bad thing, I did actually like the movie Sahara! The 1943 version, no the rubbish they made in 2005.<br><br>The story is told from the prospective of Heizi a man coming to the end of his Pilgrimage of Knowledge, when an unexpected letter reaches him.  Heizi\u2019s oldest brother and chieftain, gets the seven year itch, packs his bags and buggers off on a journey, leaving no schmuck in charge.  In the absence of a leader, the village is attacked and taken over by raiders.  <br><br>Heizi is too late to stop the raiders but instead plans to go all Freedom Fighter like and retake his village with the help of friends. At the start you get to chose whom of Heizi\u2019s three friends, Kin, a Labourer, Lashala, a Botanist (has some funky ears) or Patash, a Scribe (and a smurf), gets freed first (looks like you unlock additional friends later in game) and you get to use them as fighters against waves of enemy (Also first fight is your tutorial as to how the game works).<br><br>These companions are weak to start with and need to gain kills to then shed as points towards training (that\u2019ll be fifty ears for a pint sir). Fighting is reaction based, you click LMB or double LMB to block or counter attack an enemy (when a green half moon bar shows) and repeated RMB is to attack the enemy (when a circle button appears). Clicks MUST be on the bar or circle or it\u2019s a dirt nap for you. You can stay on and fight each wave and use Heizi or another person called Nisle (looks like Poison Ivy has joined the fight, Suicide Squad II) as Battle Aids but be warned, they take up resource points each time. Or you can opt out at any time between waves and retreat to you camp (Run Forest, Run!) with any goods you have required. Die and you loose everything you have picked up.<br><br>Back at your camp you use your plundered loot as resources to start rebuilding your village (Hey look, found this cool ring, I\u2019ll see if it fits\u2026\u2026.My Precious!), train or heal your friends, or go through logs (quest &amp; item information when found). The three resources are bronze, food and other is a spice\/herb called Shem (make it hash and I\u2019m in!).<br><br>It is also here at camp you get to listen to your companions and villages talk about the village and their life and what other evils are happening in the region. The story is nicely carried along in this form but it gets a little repetitive when you are continually retreating from battle, trying to build your resources. At these lower levels you are mostly Guerilla fighting with Guerilla Fighters, it all boils down to monkey business. Until you get your fighters strong enough to make it through several waves.<br><br>There is little in the way of options in-game or at menu, just sound and music volume. Music is a nice Middle Eastern style that even though is looped, is very subtle and pleasing. <br><br>The reaction base fighting works with this game, but I do find that double LMB is a bit flawed unless you are right on the sweet spot, very annoying when in a four or more fight. A bigger click zone would be appreciated or even clicking on the actual enemy would suffice.  Another bug in the game is your item belt, won\u2019t work by pressing any of your number keys as it states in tutorial but will work by mouse. <br><br>The hand drawn environment and characters are also well done and pleasant to the eye and the story is solid and being well told by the characters but it is repetitive at first till you Bling your team to advance the story.<br><br>A little frustrating at times, Ibrahim Indra Baskara has made a gem that needs a few tweaks. Eurgava:- Fight for Haaria is one that is good to have in your RPG library.
