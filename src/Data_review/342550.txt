&quot;Potential&quot;. Solid foundation to build on, set on becoming great by the end of the journey.<br><br>The setting is the star of the show. Kowloon City is an excellent location to base a horror game around. If you watch documentaries on it, you'll know how crazy the construction was in the Walled City. A fact which surprisingly fits the procedurally generated maps element quite well. Justice is done to the setting, fits the atmosphere quite right in my opinion. With more variety potentially added down the development road, it can be one of the most interesting places to visit in a video game.<br><br>The presentation itself takes a little getting used to. The sound design is excellent but visually, the motion blur and flare effects can prove to be irksome initially. However, once past the barrier, you do start to appreciate it. It ties quite well with the gameplay elements and provides for a more immersive experience.<br><br>The gameplay is good, with a decent amount of variety already in the game. Primarily, the focus is on stealth; the objective of each level being to find the exit of the map. Though, you do have some leeway and can kill some of the monsters if you chose to. Be warned though, make too much noise and you'll awaken the Sleeper. You cannot hide or fight him once he's awakened. Your only chance is finding the exit, fast! A prospect which from my experience, was seldom met with success xD. <br><br>A neat feature which I'm a huge fan of is the possibility of you losing your sanity. Light can be your best ally or your worst enemy. You have to use vision smartly. Flash your torch at the wrong creature and you will go crazy, leaving you disorientated, fast. Doom is almost certain in these moments. There are light switches to turn on and flares to throw (Can distract certain monsters), so you have a lot to think about each time you turn a corner! <br><br>Exploration is encouraged as you may find written notes that help build the picture of the tale that explains what caused the Walled City's doom. Plus, you'll have a chance of finding items or weapons that'll aid you in your ways. Though, with you being constanly being on edge due to fear, I rarely found myself taking the adventrous route xD. With the generated maps, each area of each run you play will hold something different! Things may turn out in your favour one time but completely screw you over in the next moment xD. Keeps the experience fresh every time.<br><br>I will reiterate that I am playing this at early access launch. I did encounter a game bug or two, but generally nothing major which is worth mentioning. I'm confident the little nitpicks will be fixed as development goes on. Though, I would like to see a better options menu in the near future.<br><br>To sum it up, I can safely recommend this game to people interested by the premise. As of right now, it has good value attached to it.
