developed by Headup Games and Silent Dreams while being published by Headup Games. It\u2019s less of a sequel and more of a spin-off that provides the entire trilogy a closing note as crazy as the series\u2019 initial premise.<br> <br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay<\/div>Unlike Grotesque Tactics 1&amp;2, you no longer take control of Drake, the goth wannabe knight. Shifting the focus from him and onto the trilogy\u2019s true protagonist: His semi divine humble Self, The Holy Avatar. This paladin of nearly antihero status is once again a literal running joke and a tasteful parody addressed to that particular typecast within western fantasy role playing games. If the first two games in the series addressed tropes and somewhat genre conventional locations such as forests and dungeons, in Holy Avatar vs. Maidens of the Dead, the aforementioned protagonist is apparently on a tropical island vacation, sporting obvious sandy beaches, palm trees and\u2026zombies? Indeed the game takes a few jabs at the Dead Island franchise, more or less subtlely. As far as the gameplay formula is concerned, the entire trilogy has been fairly standardized with very little room for streamlining. In truth, the turn based combat was only a tad sloppy in the first part, since Grotesque Tactics 2 used a far more responsive game engine which is found in a slightly modified iteration in HAvsMD as well. <br><br>Outside of fighting, the rest of the game is standard RPG, with all the mechanics you\u2019re probably accustomed to already. Inventory, multiple choice conversations and map navigation by either point \u2018n click or by keyboard. Camera rotation is by default turned off, but I had to fiddle with that setting and turn it back on. Here\u2019s how you can do it yourself: Go to Documents\/Holy Avatar vs Maiden of the Dead\/Settings folder, open config.xml with Notepad and change \u201cCamCanRotate\u201d from false to true. Apparently the camera was locked into place to give the game map more utility. Why not use both? The game seemed a bit more liniar than its predecessors, so it\u2019s not like you can get lost without constantly keeping an eye on the map.<br><br>Taking nearly a three year hiatus after the release of Grotesque Tactics 2 \u2013 Dungeons and Donuts, in Holy Avatar vs. Maidens of the Dead we have expanded game engine assets and even a different general look. I\u2019ve always been a fan of cel shading in video games (Jet Set Radio was the first commercially successful title to use it, but the Borderlands series has made it truly popular) since it\u2019s an art style reminiscent of comic books and also a well deserved break from \u201cregular\u201d 3D. I\u2019m glad Silent Dreams opted for this approach and I hope they may explore it further in any future project they might consider releasing on Steam. What I didn\u2019t really like, was the lack of English voce acting, the game giving you two options: either accept the German dubs or receive the silent treatment and just read the (error-free) English subtitles. Of course I opted for the voices as well, even if I can\u2019t understand nearly 90% of what they are yapping about. If I could enjoy the S.T.A.L.K.E.R. series with Russian dubs, I\u2019m sure that German can\u2019t be that bad either. This might be an important lesson for all gamers out there. Learn to appreciate diversity and embrace the English subs, but don\u2019t neglect the dubs either, even if you couldn\u2019t understand a single word of what they\u2019re saying on-screen.<br> <br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Pros<\/div>+ Beautiful cel-shaded graphics<br>+ As amusing as the first two parts in the series<br>+ Steam Achievements <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Cons<\/div>- Only German voice acting (with subtitles in English)<br>- Feels more liniar than the previous two<br>- No Steam Trading Cards<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Result \/ Final thoughts<\/div>Give this spin-off a chance to entertain you and it will. Who knows, perhaps you\u2019ll even learn a new word in German or two! In all seriousness though, the game is worth purchasing separately or as part of the Grotesque Tactics Complete Pack. It\u2019s been quite a ride and even if I haven\u2019t finished them yet I\u2019m sure the clever use of satire, leaves few chances for boredom to set in, if at all.<br><br><b>Rating 7\/10<\/b><br>
