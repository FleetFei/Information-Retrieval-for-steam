Setting<\/b><br><br>Let\u2019s start with the first thing you encounter when getting into Torn Tales: The story.<br>An old man, known as The Bookbinder, is basically upset that no-one reads his stories and he gets no fame, money or adoring fans, and so he sets out to rewrite the classical fairy tales. If this story is ever made into a movie, his character will clearly be played by Shia LeBeouf.<br>The whole story is told in narrative segments inserted between the various levels, and only sparsely supported by encounters in the game; Most of what the player will be doing is completely unrelated to the story. This is also the main failing of the game.<br> <br><b>Gameplay<\/b><br>You control a trio of fairy-tale characters, as they walk through the twisted lands in The Bookbinder\u2019s world, and kill everything they meet. Yup, not really much dialogue or anything to support your actions, just walk until you find a group of hostiles, and chop-chop.<br>The combat itself even feels disconnected, like no actions you take really matter. To test this, just ignore the computer when you get into a fight, and then check back a couple of minutes later to see that you\u2019ve won. Some fights benefit from you moving around a bit, or position the Dr Jekyl\/Mr Hyde character as a tank, but since it\u2019s trivial to revive characters, don\u2019t worry if someone goes down for the count.<br>Even when you do everything you can, it is a somewhat empty feeling, as there\u2019s no real visceral or kinetic feedback. The characters hit an enemy, but nothing really happens beyond a number changing somewhere.<br>The result of this is that combat, exploration, and basically most everything in between the narrative quicktime events seem nugatory, as if only 1 side of the game was fleshed out.<br>I should point out that while most encounters can be done blindfolded and juggling green apples, the boss-fights generally deserves or even requires a bit of attention. If nothing else, then because there may be a need to react to a narrative segment right after them.<br><br><br><b>Roleplaying<\/b><br>As a roleplaying game, even if it is an ARPG, it includes the usual stats, skills and loot, as one would expect. Here you\u2019ll do well to simply accept that Dr Jekyl is the tank, Robin Hood is the \u201cDPS\u201d and preferably at a range, Snow White is healer and ranged damage-dealer, and then select skills to match that. Eventually, you\u2019ll have gone through and selected every skill, so there is not too much to worry about in terms of end-game setup.<br>As for affecting the story, i.e. playing a role, there is less of it than some might like, hence the reference to it as an ARPG. It would have been interesting to have more character options, and see the story develop depending on who you pick up as opposed to the current setup of the 3 characters.<br> <br><b>Sound and Graphics<\/b><br>Some of the graphics are very well done, especially during the narrative segments, and the style is overall quite interesting and competent. Snow White is neither a teensy girl, nor a hyper-sexualized female fighter, but instead looks like a competent fairytale character. Robin Hood and Dr Jekyl look largely as one might expect, and the game avoids outright grotesqueries.<br>The sceneries, the maps, are generally OK, even nice, but a bit bland and repetitive; unfortunately, due to how the characters\u2019 levels progress relative to those of the enemies, you\u2019ll find yourself traversing each map multiple times and it all quickly blurs out. The game could definitely benefit from more, smaller maps, and more variation in the decorations on the maps, incl better tombstone descriptions.<br>The sounds, ignoring the game-music for a moment, are best described as almost-functional. Sometime there appear to be glitches keeping some sound-effects from playing, but when they do play, they are not really descriptive. You cannot tell what is going on simply from listening to the game, and this leaves out an important venue for providing feedback to the player. Sure, wolf-howls means there are wolves, and you eventually pick up what some of the combat sounds mean, but it is less than intuitive overall.<br>Music in the game follows an orchestral or ensemble setup, with ample drums to set the mood of combat and challenge, but isn\u2019t particularly exceptional in any way. The occasional lone violin, however, does a very good albeit common job of setting the scene at certain points.<br> <br><br><b>Platforms<\/b><br>The game is out for Windows PC, macOS, and Linux, but playing the game, I\u2019m constantly thinking that this could just as well be played on a tablet \u2013 the UI comes across as designed at least in part for touch screens, and the gameplay is simple enough to be played during a commute to work. That said, it might be too much of a stretch to run it on a smartphone.<br> <br><b>Conclusions<\/b><br>Torn Tales is functional as an ARPG, the story and setting is interesting in itself, but it is let down by average-at-best implementation in some areas. If you like classical faery tales and ARPGs, definitely give the game a try, and I\u2019m certainy hoping that TwistPlay will revisit the world with new characters and settings, more dialogue and options for the players.<br><br>Bammsters\u2019 Rating: Design 6, Challenge 4, Fun 5, Replay 5, and Price 7, for an overall rating of <b>5.4 out of 10<\/b>. 2017<br><b>Type:<\/b>  Singleplayer <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">General Impression<\/div>This game reminded me of The Pagemaster. An animated film from 1994 about classic fantasy protagonists embarking on a common quest. I could say the same of Torn Tales\u2019 plot line but if you know me or my reviews, the only thing I hate more than spoilers, are the dimwits which spread them. At least the dev team isn\u2019t formed out of Steam rookies. They have released two other games which portray certain aspects of strategy. So far, so good.<br><br>I enjoyed Torn Tales and not just for giving another spin to established fairy tales. It\u2019s essentially a squad-based RPG at its heart, yet still manages to hint at more than just another hack \u2018n\u2019 slash. The term \u201cARPG\u201d is used more often nowadays, with \u201cA\u201d standing for \u201cAction\u201d.  Ironically, while I\u2019m usually an outspoken critic of multiplayer-centric titles, I find the complete abscence of an MP mode from Torn Tales, to be a missed opportunity. At least local co-op if nothing else, since the AI isn\u2019t making things any easier, that\u2019s for certain. Not much of a challenge though, as I was able to breeze through hordes even as my two AI-controlled companions were more likely to cheer me up from the sides, than take active part in the actual combat. The fact that you can switch between them at all times, represents the saving grace of an otherwise atrocious artificial intelligence.<br><br>So who are the three legendary heroes that form an unlikely alliance? None other than Robin Hood (everyone\u2019s favorite highwayman with a heart of gold), Snow White (somehow, a mage in this game) and Dr. Jekyll (also known as Mr. Hyde, if any of his friends would have survived the encounter). Archer, Wizard and Melee Warrior. Is this Trine on steroids? At least no obnoxious or fruity puzzles in sight! Instead these three companions are in the business of killing. As you can imagine for any self-respectable ARG, business is booming. <br><br>Some quests and boss fights try their best to break the monotony but Torn Tales still suffers from too much repetitiveness, in both assets and the action itself. Victor Vran and the Van Helsing trilogy never gave their players a chance to get bored since the enemy types and scenery were constantly shifting. So it\u2019s not a question of much more variety, simply in game design choices and how well can the assets on hand be used, in full effect. One might argue that the production costs and price tag of Torn Tales, cannot rival the aforementioned similar titles. And that would be correct.<br><br>Overall, Torn Tales is a decent representative of its genre and one that isn\u2019t overpriced either. Plenty of Achievements and Trading Cards sweeten the deal and you won\u2019t feel sorry for your bucks if you buy the game outside of a Steam Sale or bundled deal. Just have a lot of patience until you pass the first few levels and you will surely enjoy the experience of hacking away minion after minion. Game progression is given sufficient attention, in the form of upgradable skill trees for all three protagonists as well as an inventory\/load-out system, for maximum fighing effiency. You can't ask more of this game and I don't want to hear another word on DLC.
