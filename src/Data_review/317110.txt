I'll get right down to what I like and don't like.<br><br>Pros:<br><br>- The three classes (adventurer, soldier, trader) are distinct, with their own unique abilities. Further, the subclasses (jobs) that you can choose from promote skills, which encourages finding your niche and working on it. This makes for a good sense of progression, character uniqueness and a broad selection of playstyles. For combat there is naval and land, ranged and melee; and adventurers have a range of playstyles, from collecting data around the world, to hunting down shipwrecks, and going on long voyages to make discoveries. Apart from freeform gameplay, there are also semi-randomised quests.<br><br>-  The world is vast and there's a lot to see. You won't see all the content for hundreds of hours of gameplay. It's not derivative, either - it's awesome when you unlock a new part of the world to explore.<br><br>- So many different ways to play. There's something for everyone. Adventuring is about long voyages, discoveries and treasure-hunting; maritime and land battles have some options and depth to them; trading is for more patient money-hoarding players with good knowledge of the trade lanes and goods, as well as inventory management.<br><br>- Plenty of depth in the gameplay, you're always learning more tricks of the trade such as which consumables you're going to need to combat events at sea, or you can pick up skills to do that, or even set an aide to do it for you if they have the appropriate skills. You have many options in character-building and higher levels open up more possibilities.<br><br>Cons:<br><br>- The grind. Yeah, getting to the ''endgame'' (top-of-the-line ship and most advanced class) will take many, many hours. Although in this case, it's more about the journey than the destination. This isn't the type of game where you have to be endgame to get access to the worthwhile content. <br><br>- The tutorials take hours. Seriously, you're looking at 10+ hours to get through all the schools. You don't have to, and you can go your own way at any time and come back later. Still, they are worth doing for the rewards despite the backtracking and awkward dialogue.<br><br>- Documentation is poor. A lot of things in the game are not self-explanatory, and you won't find written help ingame. I've played for dozens of hours and I still couldn't tell you all the differences between Recog, Search and Observe skills. The good news is, it's not necessary to know every game mechanic. You pick whatever playstyle you want and you learn as you go. Also, the ingame community is very friendly and helpful.<br><br>- Pay2Win elements. Thankfully, you can chart a course that takes you far from the major sealanes and the coast, for a good chance of avoing player pirates. Still, items like that are disconcerting. Also, buying a monthly subscription (about 15 dollars. Or Euros... hm...) gives you a fat paycheck in ingame currency. I don't know whether to put this under pay2win or not, but I guess if you're seeking pvp, yeah it would allow the subscribed player to get expensive gear sooner. <br><br>So in my experience, UWO is great if you're after a PVE experience with a Renaissance maritime flavor.
