Backer!<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">&quot;Rad N' Ready!&quot; <\/div><br>Rad Rodgers: World One is a retro inspired 2D platformer by the developers Interceptor Entertainment, the same devs that brought us the Rise of the Triad 2013 remake... Don't worry though! This game is NOT glitchy and buggy how ROTT was on launch. As someone that grew up playing games like Wolfenstien3D, DOOM II, and Commander Keen, it was easy for me to get into this and enjoy playing it. I did not play it too much while it was still in development, as I knew it would be slightly different when the final game was released.<br><br>You traverse some very detailed and beautiful levels. Now my I built my PC on a small budget almost 4 years ago. Playing new games tends to stress the old machine out. I can run this game on high settings, but at like 10FPS. With everything set to lowest as possible I was able to keep it running at around 60FPS most of the time. The game still looked good though! The animations were good, the sounds were great, and that god damn mother f*cking SOUNDTRACK! <br><br>So I pledged $17 in the kickstarter, so I can get early access, artbook, 3D Realms Arcade Collection, some other little useless things, my name in the credits, and my main reason, the Soundtrack! It's no lie, I'm a huge fan of Andrew Hulshult and his work. I loved his remastered work of the ROTT soundtrack, love his work on the up coming game DUSK, and this games soundtrack is f*cking amazing as well!<br><br>Now the dialogue is corny as hell, but what did you expect? It's all funny in it's own way, and takes quotes\/sayings from other classic games. There is also lots of swearing, so don't be letting the kids play this gory fowled mouthed game (or do, I won't judge.)<br><br>Now if you are BAD at platforming games, you won't enjoy this. There is lots of platforming in this game, and sometimes it can be confusing on where you are supposed to go, or what you are supposed to jump\/climb on. This game does not hold your hand in traversing the levels. Your only help is an arrow sign in the background. This also gives you chances to explore and try to find secret areas in the game. You will also go through puzzles called &quot;The Pixelverse&quot; where you go through the games &quot;coding&quot; to fix a glitch in the game to progress. None of these were ever hard and are straight forward to complete. The final boss battle is also very interesting to fight, though it does lack in powerups during the fight.<br><br>Having completed the game, I've only been able to 100% complete the first level, and get about 80-97% on the other levels on Normal Difficulty. The game says I've completed about 81% of the game my first go through in the 5 hours that I've played. (2 hours was early access for 2 levels.)<br><br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">&quot;You know those games that go beep when you're about to die. BEEP BEEP BEEP!&quot; <\/div><br>Now here are my issues.<br><br>Notice how that game is called &quot;World One&quot;? That's because that is ALL IT IS! It's only the first world. That is 6 levels, and 1 boss level. You can complete this game in about 4 hours or less! As someone that paid $17 for this, I feel a bit cheated, but like I said I mostly wanted the OST. For $11.99 ($12.99 CAN) it might be a little too high for some people for how short the game is, and I fully agree on that. Most of the actual time that you would even sink into this game would be getting 100% on every level, on all 3 difficulties.<br><br>A minor issue I had was I constantly lost track of my aim cursor in the maps. It's just a basic white cross, that when you experience some explosions or lots of action on screen, or the camera moves or shakes, I lost my cursor, then sat there for a few seconds just wiggling the mouse around trying to find it again. Like I said it's just a minor issue and could just be me.<br><br>Another minor issues, that could just be full well my fault on me sucking at platformers, is I've had some issues with the platforming and grabbing onto ledges. Now sometimes you can grab onto a ledge and pull yourself up, and sometimes you can't, even though both ledges looked the same. This tended to make some platforming sections frustrating when you were like a micro inch off the platform, and could not grab onto the ledge, but then the next ledge you jump onto you were like a foot away and you grab and pull yourself up. This also made trying to find some secrets hard, cause you can't grab that ledge. Some ledges also seem IMPOSSIBLE to get to, as they are too far or too high to jump up to. Again this is probably just me sucking at platformers, and not seeing the obvious ledge I'm supposed to jump to.<br><br><br>PROS:<br>- Classic Retro Platformer.<br>- Combat is fun, but can be easy.<br>- Platforming feels solid most times.<br>- Levels have tons of detail, and very high quality.
