class=\"bb_h1\">Introduction<\/div><br>Polychromatic is a colourful, twin-stick arena shooter from Brushfire Games. You, a small blue shape, must fight off waves of other shapes in order to get as many points as you possibly can. Polychromatic is very much about trying to get progressively better scores to not only beat your previous personal best but also in order to get higher on the leaderboard which creates a real sense of competitiveness even though you\u2019re playing by yourself.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Presentation<\/div><br>Polychromatic is very simple in its appearance, relying on the vibrant colours of the different shapes to make it interesting. While this does work relatively well for the game as it makes all the shapes really stand out, I was very much left with a sense of blandness due to the grey background of the game arena. One thing I did really like about the presentation was the fact that information about your current game is displayed in the middle of the arena, rather than on any UI elements around the screen.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Game Modes<\/div><br>Polychromatic is split into three different game modes; Endless mode, Timed mode and One-Life mode. The major things that stay the same across the game modes is that you always have access to three blast power-ups and three dash power-ups (more can be earned by beating waves) and each consecutive wave is progressively longer and harder to beat.<br><br>Endless mode consists of you beating as many waves of shapes as you can before you run out of lives. In Endless mode you start with three spare lives though more can be earned by beating waves of shapes. As far as I\u2019m aware there is no \u2018wave cap\u2019 so in theory you could play forever but since the game\u2019s difficulty does ramp up a fair bit I don\u2019t see that being at all possible.<br><br>Timed mode gives you infinite lives but only two minutes on the clock to beat as many waves as you can. Each time you beat a wave ten seconds is added on to the clock. It\u2019s worth remembering that respawning takes around two seconds so while you may have infinite lives it\u2019s in your best interests to keep yourself going as long as possible.<br><br>One-life mode is identical to Endless mode but instead of starting with three lives and getting more every few waves you only have one life so you better hope you\u2019re good at dodging!<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay<\/div><br>There\u2019s not much to say about gameplay other than what I\u2019ve covered in my description of game modes. As you whizz around what appears to be a petri-dish of shapes you fire tiny circles at said shapes in order to eradicate them! As well as these tiny circles you also have the two power-ups I mentioned earlier at your disposal. The blast power-up sends out a ring of the little circles to eradicate shapes all around you, of course these circles spread out the further away from you they get so it\u2019s best to use it when there\u2019s a high concentration of enemies very close to you. The dash power-up sends you shooting around the little arena at a faster than usual speed while also making you a lean, mean shape-squishing machine, i.e. any shapes you touch while the dash power-up is active will be destroyed.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Audio<\/div><br>The sound effects are very good in Polychromatic and fit with the game nicely. The soundtrack is also good but I felt like it didn\u2019t really fit with the game. With a game like this I\u2019d expect quite lively music, something along the lines of electro or dubstep to really get you into the game for the more frantic, later waves. However, the music we\u2019re presented with, while very nice, has a more relaxing feel to it and just felt out of place to me.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Miscellaneous<\/div><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li>For the amount of content included with the game I feel like the \u00a36.99 price tag may be a little too high, I think it comes down to how much you like games similar to this as to whether or not you should be willing to pay that price.<br><\/li><li>The only graphical option is a fullscreen toggle.<br><\/li><li>While the game is playable with a keyboard &amp; mouse it\u2019s a twin-stick shooter so it\u2019s definitely geared more towards players with controllers, I for one enjoyed playing with a controller much more than with KB&amp;M.<\/li><\/ul><br><b>For a brief look at the three different game modes, check out my gameplay video:<\/b><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"https:\/\/www.youtube.com\/watch?v=qun-eFfBeQU\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_0\">https:\/\/www.youtube.com\/watch?v=qun-eFfBeQU<\/a><br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Verdict<\/div><br><b>Worth Purchasing on Sale<\/b><br><br>Polychromatic is a fun little twin-stick shooter, with a simple aesthetic, good gameplay and a real sense of competitiveness due to the leaderboards.
