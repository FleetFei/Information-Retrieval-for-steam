A crossover between a vehicle simulation game and a real-time strategy game, players were tasked with colonizing multiple islands in an archipelago and destroy the enemy aircraft carrier before they can colonize the archipelago themselves. The game was well received receiving high marks from various publications, and gaming magazine The Games Machine even awarded it the number one game of 1988. In 2012 Bohemia Interactive released Carrier Command: Gaea Mission, a modern remake of the original Carrier Command. Did the game hold up over all these years?<br><br>For starters, I\u2019ve never played the original Carrier Command, so I can\u2019t really compare the original to the remake.  From what I can tell, the main goal of Carrier Command\u2019s campaign is to take control of various islands on the planetoid Taurus in a war between two factions: the United Earth Coalition and the Asian Pacific Alliance. The Campaign portion of the game starts out with you leading an attack on a moon called Taurus in an attempt to take control of it for its resources, to help sway the battle against forces who have taken over Earth. Unfortunately something goes wrong and your squadron crashes on the moon and only a few survive. From there you set out on foot with another soldier who guides you as you roam about in a FPS-style game. Your first goal is to make your way to a damaged carrier. You will venture through a very linear path with just one gun, shooting at very generic looking robots. <br><br>After this short and dull FPS sequence, I came across the first vehicle in the game. My squadmates started directing me how to control the vehicle and what I could do with it. I found that controlling it was difficult using a mouse and keyboard, so I switched over to using a game controller, where one analog stick steered and the other aimed my gun. Playing with a controller honestly felt more rewarding. Your ground vehicle\u2019s health, fuel and ammo could be topped up by just driving up to various refilling stations shown on the map and pressing the right button. Topping off the vehicles various items is quick, which came in handy when you are on the run. You have two viewing choices when moving your vehicles: a first person or 3rd person view. I stuck mainly with the third-person camera angle because you need to see more of the surrounding area so you don\u2019t suddenly find yourself sliding down a hill, out of control.<br><br>After becoming familiar with your new toy, you eventually make your way to the damaged carrier, dock at its side, and make your way inside. Once again you are back on foot in a short FPS segment taking out more robots. After all the robots are cleared, the surviving soldiers join you on the carrier, and you take command of the ship which turns out to be completely remote controlled, so you don\u2019t need to leave your command chair. However, the carrier is badly damaged and in need of repair. So from your new seat you are able to remotely control the vehicle that brought you to the carrier.  You wil then drive it to the nearest island on your map to search for supplies. This is where the game\u2019s focus shifts to the main campaign where you will be taking over various islands you come across by hacking into base computers. <br><br>I quickly found that this is not that simple because all the islands have defenses in the form of ground roaming robots, vehicles, as well as small and speedy airships. Not to mention most of the bases have gun turrets. Much of the time you will be running around activating switches to shut things down to gain access to areas and bypass enemy defences. Different islands have different properties: some have production facilities which will produce new equipment or vehicles; others are rich in resources that you need to repair your carrier and produce your equipment. There are up to 33 islands in the game all with various environments. Some can be swampy, others more temperate and others more desert-like. In the swampy areas it is really hard to control ground assaults as you slip and slide in the mud and wet grass. <br><br>This is where a real time strategy-like gameplay element comes into play as you dive deeper into the game. Some islands get tricky and require you to have more than one vehicle on the go. You can have up to four ground and four air vehicles executing orders at the same time, each equipped with various guns and devices that you produce to aid you in taking over the island in question. I found the AI for the game as a whole to be very hit or miss, with spots of brilliance followed by some completely bizarre behaviour.<br><br>Other than the main campaign, you have one other mode called \u201cStrategy Game\u201d. Here you are pitted against the computer in a large randomly-generated battle, but you have various settings at your disposal like the number of islands, enemy strength, what resources you start up with and how fast islands generate the materials you need. You can also set winning conditions too, like a number of carriers destroyed, or islands that need to be taken over. It\u2019s a fun mode if you want to avoid the single player story and get into the thick of things quickly. <br><br>In terms of the visuals, the game is nice to look at, but nothing blew me away. Weather and environmental effects are nicely done and you can see a bit of influence from the ARMA series in it. I did experience some weird clipping issues while driving vehicles. I would be driving along and suddenly part of the screen would flash like I drove through something. The music in the game is odd. It\u2019s very big and orchestra-sounding with a large choir, but it only plays at certain times. The rest of the time it\u2019s just environmental sound effects mixed with sounds of vehicles and gunfire. class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is very under rated... It is a mixture of tactical gameplay and action gameplay and works really well. It falls short of being a masterpiece because of a few issues, but it wasn't too far in my opinion. I mostly play the Campaign so haven't done many Skirmishes. The story and voice acting is quite low budget, but the focus is on the gameplay anyway. The music is great and the graphics are nice. The controls and physics are good in this game, vehicles feel solid, weight affects their speed so what you equip them with has an impact, they slip on mud, they struggle up steep hills and whizz down the other side etc. It is realistic and handles well. <br><br>The gameplay overall is good but not great. You have to capture a series of islands, so you travel there in your uber Aircraft Carrier, and then you launch some flying pew pew pew craft to scout the island and work out where all the resources are etc. The flying craft are weak though so you have to use them to scout carefully and then get them back to safety. Later in the game you get to use them more to shoot or bomb key targets, but early in the game they are only really used as scouts. Then you launch your tanks which will do all the capturing. You can have 4 tanks and each one can have a few gear upgrades of your choice, heavier armour that makes them slower but tankier, a variety of weapons, a hacking device, etc. The campaign is slow to get going, the first dozen or so islands you have to do with basic gear, but it gets more interesting later on when you get to start using your carrier's big guns, and your units get more interesting weapons like the gattling gun and your can drop bombs from the flying units etc.<br><br>Capturing the islands is done by driving around and capturing mini bases all over the island, and killing anything that gets in your way - preferably by waiting for your squad to keep up with you so you can get the benefit of all their firepower at once. Sometimes there are fixed turrets shooting at you, sometimes a bunch of robots come at you, and sometimes some vehicles or aircraft come at you. Occasionally you have to deal with all of those at once, but you can tend to pick stuff off bit by bit if you go slow and steady, especially earlier in the game. Also you can park your flying craft nearby and you can use those to help you finish off stuff once the main defences are dead. This capturing of the islands is the bulk of the game and it is fun, but this is the part of the game I think should have been a bit better. It isn't bad by any means, but it could have been better, and there other bits of gameplay I think they should have ignored and instead just focused on this part of the game. <br><br>You also have to manage your carrier in the meantime, building gear to replace whatever you lose in battle, and generating more fuel, etc. There is a little bit of strategy with the island capturing too, because you can choose if the island should produce stuff, mine stuff, or be a defensive island, so depending where on the map it is, you can use this to your advantage. Although the game doesn't seem to focus on this enough in the Campaign at least. <br><br>Overall it is fun and actiony, but it does fall short from greatness in several ways and I'll try to explain. First off, as you can see from a lot of the negative reviews, some people try to play the game as a tactical RTS type game. Technically the game should have let people do that, but the AI just isn't up to it. The way I play is to take direct control over the main tank, and set the other 3 tanks to follow me, and the game plays fine like that. You somtimes need to wait a moment for them to catch up, but mostly the pathing is fine and the AI does ok at following you and shooting whatever you shoot. The problem is if you want to sit back at the carrier or fly around command the ground units from afar, like a real strategy game, bad things will happen due to pathing and AI failures. They AI and pathing is not terrible, but it isn't good enough to just send things off on their own and expect them to make it. Often they will get whooped by the enemy or stuck on a tree, tumble down a cliff etc.. It is a shame because it could have been a bit more RTS. Personally I don't really care about this though, because if you lead the troops into battle (surely this is the most fun thing to do anyway?), they will follow you just fine. <br><br>Also the actual capturing of the islands (which is the bulk of what you do) is quite repetitive and shallow and could have been better. Each island might have a different shape, but they all work the exact same way. There are several bases on the island and you have to hack a building or clear out the enemies in each base, and on to the next one, and then you can capture the command center in the final base. Do that then it is on to the next island to repeat, in exactly the same way. It is a bit more interesting in that you unlock better gear as you make progress, and in the campaign especially, this can make the game quite addictive. You start with very basic basics, and gradually you unlock many little upgrades. Also the enemy bases start getting tougher and more interesting as you progress through the islands. At first it is just a handful of guys who die with 1 shot. Then you start getting occasional tanks, but later you get fortress style bases with many turrents, ambushes of several enemy tanks, and flying craft all over the place. The game is repetitive, but at least you are repeating fun things, so not a major problem, but to me this is the bulk of the game and it could have used something to jazz up this basic combat.<br><br>Lastly, there are some wasted opportunities. Clearly they just didn't have the time or budget to do everything they wanted, but it is a shame because there are things that could have fleshed the game out more. Like I said you can customise the islands to do what you want, but there should have been more to this. Also you can actually shoot things from your carrier, but you very rarely get to enjoy this because most of the stuff on each island is inland so it is out of your range. Also you can choose priorities of repairs on your ship, but this hardly ever gets used so it is like a wasted feature. Also you can put different weapons on your tanks but the game doesn't really require you to play smart. The sensible option would be to have 1 of each weapon, so the lazer can blow up stuff, while the anti air cannon guy can you protect you from flying stuff, etc.. etc.. But really, just the lazer alone is enough to deal with everything. Also there are a few FPS on foot sections in the game but they seem a bit weak and gimpy compared to a real FPS. They are not terrible, but a far cry from Halflife 2 or System Shock or something. So there are a lot of under developed features, and I think the game would have benefitted hugely from the devs scaling back all this stuff, and focusing on the key gameplay and making that great instead of ok. <br><br>Overall it is good game though, and quite a unique experience. If you want deep strategy, or visceral action, look elsewhere. But a blend of both those things is not often seen so this game is worth thinking about. I wish it had better sales because a bigger budget sequel to this game would be a big deal in gaming, especially nowadays.
