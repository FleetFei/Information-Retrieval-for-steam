Moon?<\/div><br>Miaou Moon is what I would consider being a physics-based puzzle-platformer. It has been developed by Stephanie and Leslie Valverde of games publisher Agent Mega. The pair is based in Vientiane which is a city in Laos.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Expectations<\/div><br>I didn\u2019t have any expectations of Miaou Moon going into the game. I thought that it would prove to be just another cute cat-themed game. The cat theme has become an increasingly popular one on Steam over the past year or so. This is yet another game that I\u2019ve reviewed that has received little to no press coverage and generated almost as few user reviews on Steam. Of the five user reviews on Steam, all but one of them are private reviews.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Early Difficulty<\/div><br>It is clear to see that this game has been a labour of love for the developers. They are clearly cat obsessed people who have a shared love of gaming. This makes me feel disappointed in my first impression of the game.<br><br>There is a tutorial within the opening level of the game. It does a good job of explaining the basics to the players. However, the first level itself is rather difficult. In fact, to be honest with you, I died before I completed the opening tutorial. From speaking to a couple of friends who are platformer fanatics who have played the game they did as well. Given the number of games that I play I fear for more inexperienced and casual players who are likely to give up before progressing past the first level of the game.<br><br>I feel that the opening level for most games \u2013 certainly those within this genre \u2013 should be easy enough that your most casual of players should be able to complete the opening level without much thought. It should almost be trivial. The focus should be on teaching the player a base mechanic required to progress to the next level.<br><br>In my experience, the high level of difficulty continues throughout the game.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Progress<\/div><br>Thankfully enough I progressed through to the next level in my second attempt. However, I never learned much about how to play the game \u2013 even with reading the instructions provided at the different tutorial checkpoints. In fact, I am disappointed in myself. Food \u2013 energy \u2013 simply passed me by. The controls are more than a little floaty and unresponsive. My button presses do not have the intended effect but they do sap me of their energy cost. Consequently, I cannot honestly say that the control scheme is as accurate as it should be.<br><br>Furthermore, while on the topic of controls, the game is played purely through the keyboard. The default keys are left and right arrow keys to move with the down key representing an attack (cat scratch). The H key is pressed to use a health pack. Enter confirms and escape is used to cancel. However, be warned, that on the level selection screen pressing escape will automatically exit the game.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Upgrade System<\/div><br>As you progress throughout the game they can utilize the game\u2019s upgrade system. There are six areas where players can upgrade. These include energetic capacity, visor, armour, claws and the health pack. It costs three spanners in order to upgrade one slot. There is a total of 21 different components to upgrade. In my experience the high level of difficulty continues throughout the game.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Solid Presentation<\/div><br>My personal highlight of the game is the graphical presentation. This is perhaps the one aspect of the game that I did have expectations before I played the game. Cat games need to be cute and cuddly. Thankfully, the developers have delivered with a pleasing aesthetic that utilizes a delightful colour palette. Each level has its own colour scheme and the characters and other features are well drawn. In conclusion, the graphics certainly meet the brief required of the game\u2019s subject matter.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Is it Steamified?<\/div><br>Miaou Moon is Steamified by the combination of 15 Steam achievements and a set of Steam trading cards.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Verdict<\/div><br>The game is very challenging and will arguably prove to be too much of a challenge for a targeted casual audience. However, there is enjoyment to be had if you persist with the game long enough. The presentation is on point.
