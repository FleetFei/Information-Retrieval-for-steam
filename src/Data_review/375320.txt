The first few levels could be seen as tutorials to show you the different features and ways to beat puzzles within the game. Everything within Colortone is linked with using colors, whether it be to finish the level, destroy a wall, or to make a platform. There are 6 colors within Colortone which are the 3 primary colors (blue, red, and yellow) and the 3 secondary colors (purple, orange, and green). There are 2 types of object you can color according to which dab of colour you put it in, a small sphere and a board. You can dip these in individual colors or you can dip in one, then another, to create a secondary color. The game uses an interesting platforming idea where you place your boards on certain colored rods and if the board matches the colored rod then it have created a platform for you to jump on. There are also machines which use the sphere colored with primary colors to create its secondary colors (Example: blue would create green and purple), secondary colors create their individual primary colors (Example: green would create blue and yellow), and a machine which will take the color of the sphere and put it down so you can use it (Example blue means you get blue).
