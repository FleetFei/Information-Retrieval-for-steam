of living. Our task is to communicate with the mother-ship, however the communication system is broken. As we try to fix it, it turns out that we aren't the first human-like creatures to live on that planet.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay concept<\/div><br>The game puts emphasis on storytelling and atmosphere. As developers mentioned, the game also consists of exploration and survival, but don't take these as major elements. Survival itself isn't demanding and player is guided where to go in order to continue. Unless we want to explore deeper, optional areas - to find out every secret hidden in the game - we won't have any problems.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Pros<\/div><br><b>Music<\/b><br><br>Beautiful, ambient music composed by Jonas Kjellberg accompanies us through the entire journey. It changes depending on where we are, what we are doing and whether we are in danger or not. I have to admit that without so magnificent music this game would be much worse experience.<br><br><b>Graphics &amp; visual performance<\/b><br><br>Congratulations to developers! Smoothly rendered and good quality graphics are hard to achieve, but here it couldn't have been done better. I haven't experienced any lag issues so far and had all the chunks loaded before entering a certain location - perfect!<br><br><b>Story<\/b><br><br>Rich in secrets, complex environment pays off for rather linear story with no side-quests. There are plenty of references to famous alien-like movies, books, etc., but I won't mention them here (find them!). Story idea itself is quite genuine and well told. It also does contain some horror-like elements, which, together with magnificent music mentioned above, creep you out very well. Great!<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Cons<\/div><br><b>Poor fauna\/flora<\/b><br><br>While locations like alien structures or caves are really detailed and diverse, flora and fauna could be much more improved. It simply lacks variety, even for the planet with surface created of rocks. Why not add bird-like creatures, if we already can hear them in the game, some fish living in the ocean or stone-like plants chilling at the beach - huge field to fill the game with details, yet not used for anything.<br><br><b>Survival<\/b><br><br>Unlimited torch fire. Unlimited water source in every cave. Unlimited food and water every checkpoint. Multiple full food\/water recovery snacks found everywhere. Plenty of rockets filled with resources dropping from mother-ship. Come on guys, how is it survival? Even while exploring some deeper areas never was I even close to dying from thirst or hunger. And that's almost the harderst difficulty level. In addtion, nearly all puzzles could be solved in seconds and multiple traps found both in caves and alien buildings definitely couldn't be described as &quot;deadly\u201d. I actually used the fire ones to warm myself in cold areas. Smart, isn't it?<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Overall<\/div><br>To conclude, The Solus project is a beautiful, astonishing game. Its main strengths are story and atmosphere, yet it lacks diversity in planet nature and survival aspects.
