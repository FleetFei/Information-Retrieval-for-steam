class=\"bb_h1\">Start with nothing, build an empire. <\/div><br>Void Destroyer 2 is a space sandbox game (and the sequel to Void Destroyer) developed by Iteration 11, a one-man dev \u2018team\u2019. In Void Destroyer 2 (VD2) you start with nothing, in fact you start as nothing. Confused and disoriented you wake up in a small, fighter class with two men talking you through how to fly your ship, it acts as an introduction to the premise of the game as well as a tutorial. After completing this fairly short but useful tutorial section, you\u2019re free to explore the sandbox doing as you please (within the game\u2019s limitations of course). <br><br>Getting around in VD2 requires the use of VD2\u2019s \u2018Overworld View\u2019, it\u2019s pretty similar to how the Mount &amp; Blade games work. You have an overview of the whole map and you move around between spaceports and other points of interest using it. The reason you\u2019ll want to use it is because Overworld View has Time Compression, in other words you\u2019ll get around much quicker than just flying there normally. The Overworld View displays everything from ships (different classes of ships have different icons) to asteroid fields and of course it also displays all the different spaceports. The Overview View is only available if you\u2019re a set distance away from certain objects, the distance away you need to be is usually related to the size of the object or how dangerous it is, for example a cleared out mine-field only requires you to be 1000 units away whereas you have to be 10,000 units away from hostile ships before you can enter Overworld View. You\u2019re also pulled out of Overworld View when you get too close to an object.<br><br>Outside of Overworld View most of your time will be spent actually controlling your ship (there\u2019s also a tactical view which can be used to control your fleet). VD 2\u2019s controls are good and all the ship handling felt good to me. There\u2019s also a good amount of difference in handling between the different ships I\u2019ve flown so far. How well a ship can maneuver during flight depends on how fast you\u2019re going, your ship\u2019s cruise speed or lower is the optimum speed to turn at as the correction rockets are able to manage your momentum in different directions so you keep going in the direction you\u2019re actually facing. You can of course go faster than your cruise speed (as well as turn off auto-correction) but generally you\u2019ll only want to do that if you\u2019re trying to quickly leave or get to an area. <br><br>As with most space sandbox games, there are three main ways of earning money in VD2: trading, mining &amp; completing jobs picked up from spaceports. From what I\u2019ve seen so far, trading is the best way to earn large amounts of reasonably quickly (especially if you have fleet or hired ships, more on those later). Mining is fairly self-explanatory, I\u2019ve never been a fan of mining in space sandboxes so I haven\u2019t messed around with it too much though there are ways of having ships automatically mine for you. As for the jobs, there\u2019s currently a decent variety of jobs available to you, ranging from simple courier jobs to escort missions and bounty hunting. Each spaceport offers different jobs to you, some of the jobs will require that you use a faction ship. Completing jobs (of a certain difficulty, the level of difficulty required depends on what your level already is) will increase your relationship with the faction. Some ships available to buy at spaceports require a certain faction level before you can buy them.<br><br>The main idea of the game is that you start with next to nothing and as you progress you become more powerful. You do this in two ways. The first, and most obvious way, is by purchasing increasingly better ships when the money becomes available to you. The other way is to purchase new ships (or use older ships you\u2019ve upgraded out of) to create a personal fleet of ships, as it stands there is no maximum fleet capacity or any upkeep costs. If you can\u2019t afford to buy ships but need some backup or extra cargo capacity, you can also hire ships. Hiring ships is pretty cheap right now and the way it works is that you pay a set fee for each ship you have hired (you can hire a maximum of three) each time you dock at a spaceport. Each spaceport has certain ships which are available for you to hire and you may not be able to find what you\u2019re looking for at each spaceport. <br><br>As you\u2019d expect with an Early Access games, there are some bugs. I\u2019ve had a few crashes so far as well as some minor bugs where my ship spins out of control when colliding with something or having my ship change positions randomly when trying to enter Overworld View while targeting a moving object such as debris. There have also been some issues with the way the achievements have been set up but chaosavy (the dev\u2019s Steam name) has been working on fixing that. Speaking of chaosavy, he\u2019s super active in the Steam forums (in fact posting in the forum is the easiest way to get a hold of him) which is great to see, especially with an Early Access game.<br><br>My main issue with the game as it stands is that the trading side of things feels a bit unbalanced. When I started trading I had roughly 60,000 credits and through hiring ships and after figuring out a good trade route I was sitting on 600,000 credits. It took me an hour to get to that point. I don\u2019t want it to be like it is in Elite Dangerous where you have to grind for tens of hours to afford what you want but I feel like the game would definitely benefit from it taking a tad longer to get the best ships. Alternatively, trading could be more difficult by having groups of wandering pirates who might attack you while you\u2019re travelling. If this is already in the game, then I didn\u2019t see any of them during said hour so I was able to go without upgrading my very basic ship.<br><br>Furthermore, VD 2 felt a little bare to me in some areas. Not necessarily lacking content (though more job variety would be welcome) but it just didn\u2019t feel like a lived in game-world at some points. For example, when travelling close to spaceports I never saw any ships coming in or out of the spaceports. It\u2019s a relatively small detail, and at this stage it\u2019s definitely not a priority, but I would like to see it addressed at some point down the line. Another improvement in this department would be more ships <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Verdict <\/div><br>Void Destroyer 2 is off to a good start, it isn\u2019t without issues and it can feel bare at times but it's still a very enjoyable edition to the space sandbox genre.
