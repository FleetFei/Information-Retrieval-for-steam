It takes the concepts behind the world's most popular competitive fighting games and translates them into a strategic card game.<br><br>There's four different types of moves (Attack, Throw, Block, and Dodge) which form an interacting triangle that should be familiar to fighting-game veterans: <b>Attacks<\/b> damage the opponent and blast through <b>Throws<\/b>, which also deal damage and wreak havoc on <b>Blocks<\/b> (which give you more cards) and <b>Dodges<\/b> (which let yoou counterattack), which in turn are great counters to <b>Attacks<\/b>. Understanding how strong your cards are in a given situation is key to winning Yomi.<br><br><b>Character playstyle:<\/b> each character plays very differently, and you have to understand what your character's gameplan is--as well as your opponent's character. This is the hardest part of the game to get a handle on, and it's a skill you'll have to hone very carefully over time, after you get used to different characters.
