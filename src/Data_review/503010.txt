control of spacecraft.  LoA has some neat features going for it - ship hulls and stations are completely constructable from prefab parts (so it's not really a voxel game) granting an element of creative control that you don't get in a lot of similar space strategy games.  It's also free, which basically means &quot;stop reading this review and try the game yourself.&quot;<br><br>However, the game in its current Early Access form is very incomplete.  Pop-up notifications need to be click-exited, which is annoying when your mouse is used for steering and shooting (so just to get the notification out of the way, you have to steer and shoot at it).  There isn't currently any kind of targeting system; you literally steer your ships in the general viscinity of targets and they shoot at whatever is closest, which gives you very little control in combat beyond the single ship that you control directly.  The ship and station editor is very hard to work with, at least at first, as nothing seems to want to place quite where you want it.  The Tutorial gives you exactly zero help on how to work with the editor controls.<br><br>Further, the game crashes constantly, mostly on loading screens, or more annoyingly, as you hit Finish in the editor.  While the visual assets are very good and well-detailed, it would be even better if I could run this game at higher quality without the client crashing at startup.  Somebody else said &quot;push every button until it works&quot; and that applies to pretty much everything in this game, including the resolution and quality options (i.e. &quot;try everything until one works&quot;).  <br><br>I'm giving this game a Yes recommendation because 1) it's free and 2) there's a lot of original content that will be very fun to work with once the controls and camera get a little love.
