That being said, Guerilla is far from a perfect game and definitely shows its age in some respects.  <br><br>But first, the positives.  Like I said before, there is a lot of fun to be had in Guerilla and this is due in large part to the Geo-Mod system the game employs which essentially allows for you tear down damn near every structure in the game, brick by brick.  There\u2019s an extremely visceral amount of fun to be had simply roaming around the Martian landscape plowing through buildings or bashing away at a building with your trusty sledgehammer before hearing that telltale creak that signifies a building is about to come crashing down all around you and scrambling to get out of the way of the debris. I tested the game on a mid-range laptop (8 GB RAM, i5 processor, and integrated graphics card) and it ran with very few problems (there were a few frame rate hiccups here and there when the destruction started to get really crazy but even still it was absolutely playable) on low to medium settings.  When I moved over to my desktop though (i7, 16 GB RAM, with a GeForce GT 730) I was able to get silky smooth performance at higher settings.  For what it\u2019s worth, I\u2019ve also played Guerilla on my 360 and while performance there was closer to what I saw on my laptop, I would still recommend it on that system to anyone who asked.<br><br>Unfortunately, a lot of what\u2019s surrounding the actual gameplay in Guerilla leaves a fair bit to be desired.  The story of the game is utterly forgettable and extremely self-serious which is quite disappointing, especially in light of what Volition would ultimately go on to do in the Saints Row series.  I honestly couldn\u2019t tell you the name of more than two or three characters in Guerilla and to say that the characters who are present in the game lack definition would be putting things lightly.  The Witcher 3 this game is not but then again not every game has to be. <br><br>What\u2019s a bigger issue though, is that the game is unfortunately afflicted with an issue present in a lot of open world games in that there simply isn\u2019t a lot there to make the world feel alive.  Much of the time you\u2019ll simply be driving between the different \u201cGuerilla Actions\u201d (essentially side quests and events) or EDF (the game\u2019s primary villain) bases looking for more targets to destroy with little points of interest in between.  Furthermore, I found the actual combat present in the game to be frustrating at times as there\u2019s a surprisingly low ammo ceiling for the weapons you can carry and Alec Mason (the game\u2019s protagonist) proved to be somewhat fragile in terms of survivability.  There is a cover system present in the game, but it\u2019s laughably simple at best and actually detrimental to your play at worst as you can pretty easily get stuck to walls and find yourself swarmed with EDF goons in pretty short order.  I typically found it a lot easier to instead just get in whatever vehicle I could find and ram through as many enemies as I could before actually getting out and engaging in a direct fire fight.<br><br>That being said though, I would still recommend this game purely based on the amount of fun there is to be had with the destructibility of the environment.  Just know before going in that there are some gameplay frustrations that you\u2019ll have to push through and that there really isn\u2019t anything waiting for you at the end in terms of a satisfying narrative.
