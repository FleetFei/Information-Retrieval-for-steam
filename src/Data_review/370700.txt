Warfare Studios that I got my hands on to play, and reading many a good reviews about the developer in Aldorlea forums beforehand, I had decent expectations to begin with. All it's pros and cons gathered to make a last call, <i>Black Home: Vermillion Monastery<\/i> is a good RPGMaker game, yet not a good RPG.<br><br>It all begins during a heavy rainstorm, at the ruined tower halls of a forsaken monastery. We witness a battle for life and death between a Victorian gentleman, and... An undead priest and a pretty nun??!?! As the scene changes, we meet Lecia - or rather Elecia, who is a prim and proper lady with a chemistry degree. Through her diary, we learn that Lecia's husband, Lesnar left to search for an expedition arranged by the university, for the forsaken Vermillion Monastery. Said researchers haven't been heard from since and Lecia hasn't heard a thing from Lesnar thereon either. As the story starts, Lecia dreams of Lesnar calling for help and decides to go investigate the matter herself. What she will discover shortly after, is beyond her most foul nightmares.<br><br>Okay, okay. Pretty yet smart girl, lost relative, someone calling for help to an obviously dangerous place and as the protagonist follows, a dreadful occult catastrophe threatening to end the world occurs? Yup, we have seen this one many, many times before. It's the baseline of all HOG and most RPGMaker games out there anyhow + even some Hollywood movies. Yet, what makes <i>Black Home<\/i> notice worthy among others?<br><br>First of all, I should say that the game presents one of the better uses of RPGMaker out there. Environment design isn't terribly rich, yet it is gathered into a logical and consistent collection to present you a really, really big dungeon crawl and a decent enough atmosphere. The original midi soundtrack is pretty and the setting in enriched with even proper sound effects. The story itself is a bit lame though, but characters are enacted properly with appropriate personality undertones to make them somewhat memorable. Their story interactions are well-written. It is very much Henry James meets Arkham Horror out there.<br><br>As an RPG though, it is pretty much a straight railroad on a dungeon crawl. You recruit a party as you play, adorn them with items, defeat monsters for the experience; etc. I didn't notice any level cap, but I ended the story with my characters around level 20, and I am quite sure there was nothing else left to slay within the huge main map. If you have an idea about the prime function of any and all JRPGs, battles will become boringly easy within half an hour, and character skills are not various enough to challenge you into battle strategies. Monsters do not differ that much in their abilities either. Thus, you play for the story.<br><br>Well, there are 2 distinct endings - one happy, one not so happy - for you to discover according to your actions nearing the end. I'd say the whole thing packs 7-8 hours or gameplay if you don't mind grinding a bit and visiting the same areas within a huge map to collect loot and experience. Oh, and it has also achievements.<br><br>Last verdict? It's nothing brilliant, but a decent time kill with a story for a day. It depends on your mood. It was a bit fluffy on the &quot;Old Ones&quot; concept if you ask me, but who am I to judge? Overall, the game is not bad at all. Don't forget to fetch it on a sale!
