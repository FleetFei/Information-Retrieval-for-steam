Deathtrap is a tower defence game where you also control your hero Diablo-style, except there are tons of deadly traps you\u2019ll use also use. <br><br>Once you choose your hero you\u2019ll be fighting through the 13 included campaign maps, but because the campaign has so few areas, you\u2019ll start to become accustomed and familiar with them quite quickly. Choose from one of three classes; Sorceress, Mercenary, or Marksman. Luckily this isn\u2019t the case and even in the later stages, there are some interesting mechanics to help with this balance of player and trap gameplay. Throughout the map are Ink portals that when used, can teleport you to any of the other Ink portals, so traversing the map one end to the other isn\u2019t that daunting and honestly, not needed if you placed your traps strategically (unless you\u2019re playing Hard difficulty). <br><br>On the map you\u2019re able to see the portals that the enemies flood out of and also their predefined path to get the safe area that you need to guard them from reaching. First is the inclusion of a level editor along with Steam Workshop integration to make things easy to find and download from friends and others in the community. Even with these extra chests, the loot flow isn\u2019t nearly enough to progress your character in leaps and bounds.<br><br>If you finish the campaign quick and don\u2019t feel like grinding endlessly, you can play some 4 player cooperative gameplay where your character progression will continue as normal, just as if you were playing the campaign. I was pleasantly surprised and had an enjoyable time playing with multiple characters and building their skill trees to suit my play style. Without the cooperative mode, I would have been done with the game quite quickly, but being able to play with friends and strategize in the harder difficulties is worth the price of
