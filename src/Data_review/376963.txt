2016\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t<div class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAh, the Saint. I've been patiently waiting for this one since Victory Works posted the technical demo of the working TPO on their Blog back when I was still just a wee lad, or, at least, it felt like I still was. The Saint Class and TPO has been on the minds of the developer since 2009, and they should be very proud of the product they've set out to pasture for us. Even though I do have some little niggles, here is what I think of the newest DLC from Victory Works at the time of writing.<br><br>The Saint Class itself is an absolute blast to drive. Packaged with the latest iteration of Victory Work's Advanced Driving mode, the Saint really has been sent down from Heaven. The newest features of the mode include &quot;shovelful&quot; firing, and a fireman who actually gets a little winded like the rest of us mortals. If you work the fireman too hard, he'll start to slow down and, eventually, will stop for a minute and take a breather. GWR fireman are real stalwarts, but being human and all, do have realistic limitations. Another newer feature is the &quot;Head-Out&quot; camera view, a new standard that I look for in all of my steam purchases. Even though I'm partial to Right-Hand drive, this GWR class is made a lot easier to drive with this new view. Also featured is a Steam Sounds Supreme sound set based off of other Churchward locos, since there isn't a working Saint in preservation, currently. It's very well done and seems to be well suited for the Saint and it's hungry two cylinders.<br><br>Those aforementioned niggles would include the whistle sounds, which I find underwhelming. The long high whistle is not very well looped, and the short whistles are just too generic for my taste. I don't think it does the iconic GWR shrill justice. Also, while the loco model itself is beautiful and extremely accurate, the Saint Class was a very simple design, and there just aren't a lot of moving parts to be found around the loco. It reminds me of the Hall in that respect, wich makes sense, considering the Hall and other two-cylinder GWR classes were largely based on the Saint. <br><br>The TPO, on the other hand, has so many moving parts it completely makes up for the comparatively dull Saint. If you haven't heard (or seen), the TPO picks up and drops off actual mail bags, and it looks so realistc! This is where the heart of the DLC lies, and makes for so many awesome opportunities for amazing scenarios. With the included mail exchange points and signage, you can add the TPO workings to any route. I'm so excited for all the user-made content about to be made for this!<br><br>But, even though it picks up and drops off the mail, it doesn't do it in the most graceful of fashion. The moving parts on the TPO are jerky, and not at all natural. When you come within about 200 yards of the exchange point, the gates swing down with such force, I fear they'll brake off. It happens almost instantly. While I find this breaks the realism, it's something I can deal with. I also find that the other included stock, mainly the GWR Sunshine Composites, don't seem to match well with the TPO stock. The Chocolate and Cream livery has two different flavors of Chocolate, and the undercarraige of the Sunshine stock is quite a bit greyer than that of the TPO. I'm not sure why the sets are unmatching, but it sets off a portion of the otherwise wonderful look of the DLC. <br><br>PROS, SAINT CLASS:<br>Advanced mode<br>SSS Sounds, a requirement in my opinion.<br>New firing mechanisms, just wish I could cheer the fireman on!<br>Auxiliary steam Injector (Allows for Exaust Steam injectors to work while there is no exaust steam)<br>General attention to detail, as per the usual for Victory Works.<br><br>CONS, SAINT CLASS:<br><br>Simple, but accurate model<br>Extremely loud first safety valve, my ears were ringing at the end of the scenarios, literally.<br>Lackluster whistle <br><br>PROS, TPO:<br>TPO.... Need I say more?<br>Working lineside equipment<br>Great running\/standing sounds, just listen as the doors slide open and shut at a station.<br>Vast scenario potential<br><br>CONS, TPO:<br>Awful robotic animations of the exchange equipment on the TPO<br>Glaring color variations between TPO and Sunshine stock<br><br><br>Overall, this DLC is an absolute must if you consider yourself at all serious about the GWR or steam in general. This is yet another example of what is possible with Train Simulator and it's neglected, simplified use by DTG. If Dovetail put out advanced, accurate, special models like this one, we'd all be in a very different boat when it comes to things, currently. But, politics aside, the Saint Class is an iconic piece of machinery, and TPO trains served millions of people in their 150 years of service, with sorted mail still being carried by rail in the UK to this day.
