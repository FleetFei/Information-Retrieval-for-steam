Society as we know it has vanished! The year is 2150, you are the leader of a group which is determined to survive. Your focus is exploring, scavenging, fighting and building the best shelter for survival! <br><br>Forsaken Fortress Strategy is a survival \/ team management \/ strategy game which takes place in a post-apocalyptic area. At the start of each game, you will have to choose 2 out of 6 available characters, each of them has a specific skill set (crafting, close combat etc.). The girls will start with weapons but are restricted to how much they can carry compared to the blokes. Your camp is basically a house and you will have access to a stash of necessary items, a construction workshop and a bed to be built.  When you are ready, you will have to explore the world so you can scavenge important items the like planks of wood, metals, medicine, food and more in order to build useful structures. You can build a kitchen for cooking one of your favourite meals (roast with chemical gravy, what a treat), beds, workbench, medical station and more to make sure you survive as long as you can. <br><br>Early in the piece, you will be able to recruit the other 4 characters, which will be very handy so you can carry a lot more of the items that you find out there. You will come across bandits: some have great firing power (AK47) and you will probably relive the famous scene from an Iconic movie, where Indiana is face to face with an enemy waving his sword impressively to scare him. What does Indiana do, get out his gun and shoots him! Well, you will not be Indiana in this case. So, make sure you get guns as early as you can! You will also encounter zombies; some are your height and some will be twice as tall and will be quite a handful to kill.<br><br>Make sure you look at the state of each your characters as low morale and energy could sink them. Feed them well so they can still walk about. <br><br>The combat system is easy to get used too and I really like the fact that you can pause and direct your characters to attack a specific enemy. The audio is well done in my opinion; it really project the atmosphere of being in a sorrowful, empty, scary place with no way out.<br><br>In terms of graphics, they are sound. The camera view is not as smooth as I would expect; I think it needs to improve in that regards.<br><br>3 modes available on this early access: Survival (survive 20 days), Challenge (clear the city gate to win) and Free mode (Survive as land as you can).<br><br>Positives:<br>~~~~~~~~ <br><br>-\tGood graphics &amp; audio<br>-\tEasy combat and crafting systems<br>-\tInteresting enemies (zombies wise)<br><br>Negatives:<br>~~~~~~~~ <br> <br>-\tCamera mode need improvements<br>-\tNo achievements or trading cards<br><br>If you like survival \/ team management \/ strategy blended together, as I do, this game will be a great addition to your game library.<br><br>7.5 \/ 10<br><br><i>Key provided by developer\/publisher for review purposes. Review<\/div>\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\t\t\t\t\t<div class=\"postedDate\">Posted: October 7, 2015\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t<div class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\t<div class=\"bb_h1\">Introduction<\/div>Mixing games - and genres - like This War Of Mine and Dead State generally seems like a good idea. This isn\u2019t always the case, though, as we sadly all know (and have usually experienced ourselves). So, is this altered remake of the originally-crowdfunded game worthy of your time right now or does it need to be in Early Access a while longer? Let\u2019s quickly find out.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay<\/div>You start off by selecting two survivors from a group of six. All of these survivors have their own skills; one of them may be good in building while another one might be better with firearms, et cetera. Once you have chosen two people you will be dropped off in a house and your goal will be to survive for 20 days (by then it\u2019s already 19). <br><br>If you ever played This War Of Mine then you will likely already understand how it all works. <br>For those who haven\u2019t (yet) played that game; the game expects you to expand your hideout by building and upgrading useful furniture. This is done by looting places close by. All the while trying to survive, take care of your survivors and actively defend yourself from humans and zombies alike.<br><br>Your survivors have several meters that you have to keep an eye on: sadness, energy, hunger, wellness and injury. You can increase the meters (or, at least, try to let them decrease at a slower rate) by building campfires and kitchens while also making sure they don\u2019t get hurt too much\/get sick and by just letting them sleep every now and then.<br><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=531283415\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_1\">http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=531283415<\/a><br>Besides carefully watching over your group you can also let them craft weapons (which you should absolutely do if you want to make any progress) and other tools that will help you to (easier) survive those 20 days. <br>Naturally weapons and gear can be equipped to make your characters more powerful but they won\u2019t suddenly turn into superheroes. These are still (virtual) human beings, and even though they can be shot multiple times before they die they are not invincible: once a survivor is dead you will have lost him or her forever (unless you apply a first aid kit in time) and you can then try to recruit a new one. But it will never be the same as they all have unique skills, which I already wrote about a moment ago. Still, it\u2019s better than nothing. At least with another survivor you have a better chance at surviving.<br><br>So once you\u2019ve been dropped into your house you can choose where to go to with one or both of your survivors. By opening the map you can choose which way you want to go. It\u2019s recommended to start in the Easy or Normal areas because otherwise you\u2019ll most likely be killed within seconds. Each area is different; there might be an alley with a house where you can loot of items from (but you still have to be beware of enemies!). In other areas you might be able to find survivors that you can recruit while in others you will most likely get shot by untrusting survivors if you\u2019re not too careful. These are the places you can normally get a lot of loot from, however. The risk\/reward mechanic works very well in a game like this, especially considering the setting and tone.<br><br>There are a lot of places to loot\/scavenge but depending on how many survivors you bring with you you might not have enough space in your inventory to grab just about everything. This results in having to make some tough choices along the way; do you bring a first aid kit with you in case someone gets hurt during the next mission (maybe even the same one?) or will you just grab a few pieces of wood instead so that you\u2019re at least able to build or upgrade that next piece of furniture (that might prove to be even more useful (in the long run)). Life <i>really<\/i> is hard.<br><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=531283452\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_2\">http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=531283452<\/a><br>While Forsaken Fortress Strategy isn\u2019t a roguelike you do have to be careful that not all of your survivors get wiped out. Otherwise it really is game over. Thankfully you can save in-between missions, in your hideout. But only here! You can\u2019t save during actual missions. So if you have a feeling that things are going well make sure and save once in a while. It might just save your life (no, not literally, of course). It will save a lot of stress and frustration in the end.<br><br>Your survivors can also use stealth and it\u2019s recommended to use it as much as possible in order to prevent unplanned battles. You can\u2019t avoid everything, but sometimes it\u2019s clear when you might just lose a battle.<br><br>There are multiple gameplay modes, too. You have Survive, which I have discussed in a little more detail just now, Challenge, where you goal is to get out of the city and Endless, which speaks for itself.<br><br>One other thing I\u2019d like to mention, though, aside from the actual gameplay is that the controls do take some getting used to, but so far this - for me at least - only applies to the keyboard controls.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Audio &amp; Visuals<\/div>The music is moody and it definitely fits the game\u2019s atmosphere, although it\u2019s a little more up-tempo during battles. There doesn\u2019t seem to be any voice acting and the sounds effects are rather basic, but they get the job done I guess.<br><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=531283489\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_3\">http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=531283489<\/a><br>The visuals are okay and could definitely use a little more detail, especially as far as the animations are concerned. Keep in mind, however, that this still is an Early Access game. While it clearly lacks polish at this very moment, it\u2019s possible that the gameplay is the main focus of attention right now. So it\u2019s likely we\u2019ll see graphical improvements and more polish during the upcoming months.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Length\/Replay Value<\/div>I can be really short about the length. Since it\u2019s still in Early Access some content is currently still missing: bigger maps and more enemies, for example. Therefore it\u2019s hard to exactly calculate but you can easily get 10-15 hours or more out of it right now. I think, with some luck, that the story can be completed even faster than that, but I don\u2019t see any reason to rush through it. For \u20ac10 this is a pretty good deal!<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Conclusion<\/div>Forsaken Fortress Strategy won\u2019t set the world on fire, but with just a little more polish it is and will be absolutely worth your time and money. If you enjoyed games like Dead State and\/or This War Of Mine then there\u2019s nothing to dislike here. The gameplay is solid and while the rest of the game is still in a relatively early state I expect the final product to look and play much better than what is currently being offered.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Too long, didn\u2019t read<\/div>Pros:<br>+ Different gameplay mechanics<br>+ Six survivors with their own unique skills<br>+ Multiple gameplay modes available<br><br>Mixed:<br>+\/- Graphically it doesn\u2019t look terrible, but there are still some bugs and glitches that need to be fixed<br><br>Cons:<br>- Might be a little intimidating if you\u2019re new to the genre<br>- Lacks polish<br>- Keyboard controls can be a little awkward<br><br><table class=\"bb_table\">    <tr>        <th>Please note: a Steam key for this game was provided by the developer\/publisher.<\/th>    <\/tr><\/table><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"><b>Rating: 7.0 (out of 10)<\/b><\/div>(Ratings aren\u2019t calculated based on a formula, but on my personal feelings)<br><br><br><table class=\"bb_table\"><tr><th>Want to read more of my unbiased reviews?
