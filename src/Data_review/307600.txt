Let's see now...<br><br>   My first hesitation is the setting.  While I completely applaud the decision to not just set this in a post-apocalyptic New York - AGAIN - choosing to situate the continued adventures of our titular hero in an Aztec-ian jungle suggests, to me at least, that we're slowly moving towards more Serious Sam-styled territory (though all of these characters ultimately owe their lineage to Ash of &quot;Evil Dead&quot; fame, suffice it to say). (Oh, and there's also a completely needless and irritating &quot;punch&quot; noise every time you enter melee combat, even if you choose to use the chainsaw or what-have-you; other than this, though, the sound effects are perfectly dandy.) The 2013 game was VERY fast-paced, with cards turning over at lightning speed and enemies defeated just as quickly.  This time 'round we have ARMOURED enemies, who effectively take &quot;damage&quot; and gradually weaken rather than simply being defeated outright.  There are also some more &quot;tactical&quot; combat situations, such as a giant statue guardian who you can either attack directly, or firstly choose to target the stone hands of to reduce its ability to defend itself.  The overall effect of all of this is that combats seem to take longer, and therein so does the game.  Again...personal preference.  But for better or for worse, that &quot;jump-in-jump-out&quot; \/ &quot;I'll just play one more quick round&quot; quality of the original game seems to have been lost somewhere in the shuffle.<br><br>   Things are also arguably slowed down (read: overcomplicated) by the fact that the devs have opted to make this a tad more of a full-blown &quot;video game&quot; this time around; and thereby less of a &quot;board game adaptation&quot;.  Simply put, there are less cards to look at (equipment is just a list this time 'round), and the cards which ARE portrayed are visually less varied in content (one type of zombie crops up repeatedly, as opposed to the original, in which we had a healthy rogue's gallery of the nasties in question).  On a purely personal note, I also simply don't &quot;like&quot; the artwork as much (a very important factor in ALL games, be they video \/ board \/ card \/ whatever).<br><br>   There are also BOSS FIGHTS - some optional and some unavoidable in nature - which again, slow the pace for a moment whilst you \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665 yourself wondering whether or not to waste precious Laser Lance ammunition on something other than Darkness himself.  And I'm also pretty sure that all the new-'n'-improved &quot;card-decimation&quot; animations (chainsaws cutting through enemies, cards exploding, etc) take longer to resolve than the more modest equivalents from the first game...though this may just be a figment of my imagination (but even then, still contributes to my overall argument of things generally &quot;seeming&quot; more sluggish). <br><br>   So the overall feeling of hurtling-towards-the-inevitable-final-confrontation has been hampered somewhat in favour of more conventional video game dynamics, and that oh-so-modern notion of the &quot;side-quest&quot; (in the form of temples and the aforementioned optional boss fights); again, and I can't stress this enough, PERSONAL \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665ING PREFERENCE.<br><br>   I don't know...perhaps I'll warm to this one more in time, but I think I found the charm of the 2013 game to lie predominantly in the speedy &quot;casual&quot;-ness of its nature, and that can't quite be said this time around.  And I'm sorry, but I really just don't dig the jungle as much (hell, I'm a bit of a John Carpenter fan, as you may have guessed from my non de plume).  Having said all this, it's still a good game, especially at that price, and anyone who appreciates board \/ card game adaptations is liable to get at least SOME jollies out of it.  I dunno, maybe they should have set it in post-apocalyptic London, and had zombies in bobby hats?
