class=\"postedDate\">Posted: October 9, 2014\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t<div class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTri Of Friendship And Madness is yet another puzzle game shown from a first person perspective. I love these kind of games.  But is it as memorable as the Portal games or Q.U.B.E. ? Maybe. Let\u2019s find out.<br><br>Tri drops you into the world of the Odd Gods where you have to complete 16 stages in order to find out what happened to two of the foxes that once inhabited the world. You\u2019re being guided by a ghostly figure that gives you cryptic advice and messages. In each of the levels presented your goal is to collect the three hidden fox statues and to put them on their pedestal. Once all three statues have been placed on the pedestal you\u2019ll be able to exit the current level and progress to the next one.<br>The game has a soothing and relaxing atmosphere. You can take your time; there\u2019s no rush, but if you want you can beat your previous \u2018records\u2019 by replaying a level as quickly as possible, by collecting all of the hidden idols or by using the minimum amount of triangles. These triangles are an important part of the gameplay, so let me tell you a little more about it.<br>Your character carries a device that allows him to create triangular platforms. You can do this by pointing and clicking the mouse. The game will automatically complete the platform once three points have been placed but you have to take into account that there are many rules that make it impossible to \u2018just\u2019 create a platform. No, that would be too easy. For example, you can\u2019t create platforms on wood. You also can\u2019t create platforms in \u2018corrupted\u2019 spaces, and since the platforms created are in 2D (remember that you can only place a maximum of 3 \u2018entry\u2019 points) there\u2019s no real depth or thickness in them. There are more factors to take into account, but I\u2019m not going too far into this. It\u2019s something you\u2019ll experience and see for yourself.<br>Each level also has an amount of golden idols to collect. These are usually hidden in places you normally can\u2019t reach so you have to carefully set up your platforms in order to be able to collect these. They don\u2019t add anything to the experience, but it\u2019s a fun side activity nevertheless.<br>A new \u2018power\u2019 will also unlock every couple of levels. The first one obviously allows you to create the platforms themselves, but the second one will allow you to walk on walls by carefully placing your triangles. There\u2019s also a power that will let you redirect light and teleport.<br>The first couple of levels are very simple and can be finished in a matter of minutes. Halfway through the game becomes a bit harder and completing a level can then take anywhere between 15-60 minutes. Since you\u2019re able to walk on walls after just a few hours of playing you can imagine it can get quite complicated; the camera will rotate often and some people might experience motion sickness because of it.<br>The puzzles are actually very fun - and sometimes also very challenging \u2013 to solve. You can\u2019t die, so you can experiment as much as you want. You won\u2019t fall to your death and you won\u2019t get killed by enemies (because there are none). The mysterious story and characters make you want to keep playing, just to see what might\u2019ve happened. The story isn\u2019t 'in-your-face'; you have to think for yourself here, so it makes for a good incentive. A job well done, if you ask me.<br><br>The graphics are simple with relatively basic geometry. Graphically it reminded me of the game Kairo; it uses the same kind of soft graphics. But there certainly are more colors to be found in the world of Tri. Some of the levels also have a Japanese atmosphere, because of the lighting, use of colors and some of the objects in it (like lanterns). The visuals won\u2019t blow your mind, but they definitely get the job done.<br>And the music: well, the music is\u2026 different. I thought that there were a lot of \u2018world music\u2019 influences in the game, with music similar to African and Japanese \u2018tunes\u2019. It\u2019s subtle and not \u2018in-your-face\u2019, but it\u2019s definitely something you\u2019ll notice while playing. There are practically no sounds, with just a \u2018hnnng\u2019 sound for jumping and sounds for creating triangles. It\u2019s all very basic stuff.<br>The voice acting is also limited (so far) to only one character. But this person does quite a decent job at pulling off a convincing, yet mysterious, character.<br><br>The length depends on your skill and intelligence. Some might finish it in 10 hours I think, others (like me) might take a little longer. Some levels are harder than you might think, and even though I\u2019ve been stuck on the same puzzle for a few hours now I\u2019m still enjoying it. Maybe because there\u2019s no time limit or the fact that I can do what I want, trying to figure things out at my own pace. In any case, it\u2019s a good sign. The length is perfectly fine for a game with this price.<br><br>Yes, yes; Tri is yet another game that can be added to the list of already excellent first person puzzle games like Portal and Q.U.B.E.  It introduces a new gameplay mechanic that\u2019s original and one that works surprisingly well, even in puzzle format. The mysterious atmosphere makes you want to keep playing and the increasingly difficult puzzles are(mostly) fun to solve.<br>All in all this is a great game for puzzle enthusiasts.<br><br>[Rating: What a Pity.<br><br>The game, TRI: Of Friendship and Madness, is possibly the best First Person Puzzler I have played in 5 years (since Portal 2). Of course, as to artistic merit (which is clearly not the main focus of this game, anyway), this game falls far behind titles like Talos Principle or Antichamber. However, when it comes to gameplay, especially level design, this game is definitely among the best ones in the category.<br><br>Like every other hidden gems, this game is flawed, every outstanding aspect of the game, is accompanied by something not properly done which I'll break into details in the following paragraphs. But not in this game, the refreshing experience you would have when walking upside-down on the ceiling, is as inspiring as Portal provided years ago.<br><br><b>Bads:<\/b> While the core mechanism gives you a large range of freedom, it can also be easily exploited and overused. This might sounds like a downside for modern gamers, but undoubtedly a diamond for nostalgic gamers like me. The game has all kinds of surprise in full stock waiting for you here and there , which simply gives you a sense of adventure and exploration, in your full control, unlike any other roller coaster experiences modern games would provide. It's hard not to relate this game to those old school classic FPS of the 90s with identical elements, such a sweet d\u00e9j\u00e0 vu.   <br><br><b>Bads:<\/b> Although I speak highly of its overall level design, the first few levels of the game are rather tedious and uninspiring, which I'm afraid is the major reason that prevented more people from knowing the game. And what's worse, is the vocal sound effects of the player controlled character, which is laughably bad. If by any luck the developers can read this, I seriously suggest you guys to take a second look on that.
