in check.<br><br>As someone who has been waiting 6+ years to play Owlboy, it\u2019s a bit unfortunate to say that I have mixed feelings about this game. It deserves a thumbs up simply because the good outweighs the bad, and I'm sure there are people who'll love Owlboy to pieces despite its flaws. It\u2019s great for what it is, but there\u2019s some untapped potential here that I hope will be improved upon if there\u2019s ever a sequel.<br><br>Personally, I loved the universe, the amazing soundtrack, the beautiful graphics, and the protagonist (despite the fact that Otus never says anything). Interacting with the NPCs is always interesting and a lot of the time they say or do genuinely funny and adorable stuff. <br>Otus\u2019 gunners\/companions aren't as likable as I feel they should be, considering that they're always around (since you need them for pretty much everything). Well, the exception being the last one you meet - at least he's got a bit of backstory and is somewhat relatable. I wouldn't mind a bit of dialogue\/banter from them when you're flying around doing whatever needs to be done, since Otus (being a mute) is obviously silent all the time and the cutscenes are few and far between. Sometimes it feels like you're just doing levels for the sake of it and not to achieve anything, at least until you bother to read the reminded to see what your actual objective is.<br><br>My only real problem with this game, though, is the difficulty. It's literally so easy that I don't think I needed more than five tries on the hardest boss - and that wasn't even the final one. There's no learning curve or need for tactical thinking in this game, you just shoot enemies and make the slightest effort to dodge their attacks - and that's the solution to winning every fight. You sometimes have to switch your gunners mid-fight, but you can usually win with whichever one you're currently carrying.<br>Puzzles aren't challenging either, and I say that as someone with sub-par visual intelligence. Even finding every single coin in the world was fairly easy.<br>The most difficult part in Owlboy is actually completely optional, and that is the mini-game that you only have to succeed 100% at if you wish to get every achievement (you'll use WASD to move Otus normally, but I couldn't complete the mini-game until I tried the arrow keys instead. Too bad I discovered that after 2 hours of messing up).<br><br>I guess I hoped for more challenging gameplay to go with the story, but I enjoyed the game nonetheless. The world is extremely detailed and feels very alive, so getting immersed in it was no problem at all. Level design is clever and there's even a day\/night cycle that makes the environment even more beautiful.<br>Completionists will get more out of the game as the story is only about 7-ish hours long, but I\u2019d say that Owlboy is definitely worth picking up regardless of what type of gamer you are; just don't buy it believing it's anything other than a casual, easy-going
