You start off with a deck of cards, which you use to remove cards on the board. If you fail to remove all cards after finishing your deck, you lose a life. You start off with 5 lives; when you lose them all the game ends. Some degree of strategy is involved. <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Game mechanics<\/div><br>The game contains many stages, shown on a map. You must progress through them all to win the game. Stages are locked until you gain enough stars to reach them. Every cardgame you complete awards you with a maximum of 3 stars. To get all 3 stars, you should form as long as possible a &quot;combo&quot; as you can: i.e. by linking up as many board cards as you can, using a single card from your deck. If you don't have enough stars, you can always replay games from any stage where you didn't do that well.<br><br>As you progress through the levels, you gain &quot;companions&quot; who can help you: e.g. a wizard who will allow you to retract your last move, a knight who will randomly assign you a new deck card, a bedouin who will re-shuffle cards on the board, an elf who can remove one face-up board card for you, etc. These companions add a new dimension to the game and so you should change your strategy accordingly to make the best use of them.<br><br>You also have an energy bar. Making use of companions requires energy, which is deducted from your bar. If you don't have enough energy, you can't use them. Energy can be replenished by randomly finding energy bottles as you remove more board cards, or by simply matching board cards with deck cards. <br><br>Lastly, you are given some coins when the game starts. The coins can be used to buy more energy or life. When you run out of coins, you can buy more, using real money. And that's how the game profits.<br><br>As you play on, you will &quot;level up&quot; after completing a certain number of stages. At every level up, your energy bar's maximum level expands, and your energy and number of lives are replenished, so it is fully possible not to ever have to pay in order to win this game, though you'd need a lot of luck of course.<br><br>As the stages increase, so does difficulty. For instance, more board cards become inaccessible, some only become accessible after certain obstacles are removed, and some require more than 1 deck card to be removed. This makes you consider gameplay strategy very carefully.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Conclusion<\/div><br>Quite a fun game. I didn't spend any money and managed to last a long time before I finally ran out of lives. New strategies must be thought of, which keeps the player engaged.
