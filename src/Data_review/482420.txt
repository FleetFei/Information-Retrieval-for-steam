For the most part the buildings are just re-skinned versions of the Renegades from the base game, but the units themselves are different. The buildings being re-skins makes sense because each side needs to build and utilize those basic structures. As for the units, a notable difference right off the bat is the starter tank. The Renegades had your run-of-the-mill tank that shoots projectiles and could take out enemy buildings with ease. The Guardians have a flame tank akin to the Command and Conquer Nod faction of days of old. It is basically the reverse of the Renegades, rather than being extra effective against buildings and enemy vehicles, it is only really effective against enemy infantry. Tanks can run over infantry, smash things much more effectively, and generally last longer in a fight. Now that my tanks are less effective against other tanks and buildings, I am forced into using infantry more. This time around, rather than using a fairly weak gun, your base infantry throw vehicle and building destroying grenades. Typically, one of the bonus objectives involves destroying or collecting or protecting a certain amount of a specific thing\/item before finishing the mission. The other bonus objective tells you to complete the main mission and the bonus objectives in a certain amount of time. The bonus objective mission timer feels very tight, almost too tight at times. I often find myself winning a mission with literally only seconds to spare. The first mission with a timer gave me a mere five minutes to visit all the locations and destroy the main base. I finished that mission on my first attempt with a mission completion time of 5:01\u2026. Failure. Tried again and this time I beat it with a completion time of 4:59\u2026 success! The same thing happened again on my next mission. I was more than a minute over allowed time before redoing it and squeaking by. Now you might be thinking to yourself, why bother with that challenge if it is so hard? Well assuming you played the base game on a higher setting than easy, you will know that completing those challenges unlocks permanent bonuses in all future missions. So unlocking them as early as possible makes your life easier and more fun down the road. I have to admit, that I have mixed feelings about the timered missions in general. In most base builder games, I have a tendency to \u201cturtle\u201d where I build my base up, get massive amount of units, higher tech available to me and explore the map looking for goodies. That timer forces me to pump out basic units as fast as I can in order to complete everything in a short period of time; it even gets to the point where I don\u2019t even have time to build up a proper base due to spending all my time and effort spamming unit construction buildings to speed up the queue. <br><br>Since this is Downloadable Content for a game I already reviewed, if you want more gameplay details, you can see my <a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/id\/Psygineer\/recommended\/427250\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\" >other review<\/a>. No not really, but variety is the spice of life so if you are expecting everything to be brand new, you might possibly be disappointed. <br><br>There are also less missions available for the Guardians than the Renegades. This is a bit disappointing, but the DLC is about one quarter the cost of the base game, so this is forgivable. The missions themselves are variations on those found in the base game but the story behind them is different. <br><br>You are also now pitted against both factions so you will need to adjust your strategies accordingly at each level in order to effectively counter the enemy\u2019s defenses and assaults. <br><br>Other than new units, which are free already, this DLC just adds 15 new missions that utilize the new units. If you want, you could go directly into a skirmish and play with all the new units without ever buying this DLC. However, if you like playing through missions and unlocking units as you go, you will still want to buy the Campaign DLC. Admittedly, I did go to Skirmish before I played the campaign to try out the new units.  It didn\u2019t take long for me to realize that just having them all thrown at me at once was a little overwhelming. Learning what each unit does on the fly and all at once just wasn\u2019t as fun as the excitement of trying out the brand new swanky unit you just unlocked last mission.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Concluding Thoughts<\/div><br>All in all, if you enjoyed the base game enough to bother reading through my review and the other reviews that are available, you likely should pick up this DLC.
