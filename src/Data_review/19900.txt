probably be the only game i'll ever review on steam. This is also going to be a little biased and you'll see why in a second.<br><br>Okay first off, the world itself is amazing. For a game made in 2008, the level of detail in the world and atmosphere is phenomonal. The music that comes along with it is great, changing whether or not the game detects you being in a fire fight or not. The character models are also good, no two seem to look the same although they do have similarities. Only gripe would be the voice acting; characters mouths dont always seem to move in sync with the dialogue. The map is massive, and you'll spend a good amount of time driving to locations or maybe just walking like I usually do. If you're not a patient person, and need to have some kind of action 24\/7, this won't be for you. The action is there, and explosions and gun fights happen frequently, but if you're expecting the rushed delivery from something like CoD or Battlefield, stray away. <br><br>Theres a good selection of weapons to choose from once you've done a bunch of the convoy missions. Some include different snipers (Dragunov, AS50), submachine guns (MAC-10, UZI), Assault Rifles (AK, AR-15) and theres rocket launchers, a dart rifle to sneak around with, a mortar and a 6 round grenade launcher. Theres more aswell I just dont remember everything. All will deteriorate and jam once used too much, so you've got to keep an eye on your loadout or you'll run into an outpost empty handed and get slaughtered. You can only carry 4 weapons at a time, and choose wisely or you'll be stuck in long range engagement with a shotgun, pistol and a flamethrower. You can choose how aggressive or passive you want to play depending on your loadout which gives the game some replay value if you went through the campaign once and only played a certain way. <br><br>The game is a ton of fun once you get past the tutorial (which only lasts about 10-20 mins).<br><br>If i have to mention some cons, here are some. The respawning enemies at outposts can be annoying sometimes I suppose, but if they didnt respawn youd clear out all of them and then just be driving around an empty map that sure is well designed and beautiful, but wouldnt have any of the action youd expect from an FPS. Sound design from gun sounds to weather to just everything in general is really good, but if you're bothered by a characters mouth not being in sync with their dialogue then...yeah. AI can be a little finnicky sometimes, you can be on a rock in some cliff sniping down on an enemy outpost, put a bullet in a guys head, and his friend right beside wont even notice or react. Other times they'll find your location right away and immediately start firing at you. <br><br>Enemies seem to take a little too much damage sometimes. When I played this game before I always carried a sniper so i could kill most people from a good distance with a headshot and its a one shot one kill, but if youre going in with an AR and thats all, you can put around 15 rounds into someone and they wont die. Other times they will. Its weird sometimes, but it doesnt really take away from the game in my opinion. It's just something you get used to. <br><br>One thing I was disappointed by when I bought this was there not being any multiplayer. I try to create a <a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"https:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/linkfilter\/?url=http:\/\/Ubi.com\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\" >Ubi.com<\/a> ID and it just says theres a problem with the Far Cry 2 servers. Not a big deal, for only 3.50 its a good pickup especially for a great campaign. <br><br>Theres some things i've probably missed, but for just 3 dollars, please just try this game. Its an absolute masterpiece. I played this game for YEARS literally when I bought it on PS3. Had thousands of hours invested online and beat the campaign dozens of times. Havent played in a while so I figured I'd pick it up on PC and give it another go. That's why this review is biased though, I have so many memories with this game and it's what got me into FPS games which lead me into COD4 and then into the cods after that. It sounds nerd as \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665 but Far Cry 2 had a special place in my heart and I wont forget this game.
