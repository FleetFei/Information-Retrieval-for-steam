Battleborn is equal parts PvP and PvE.<br><br>On the PvP side, you will find game modes that resemble MOBAs in that you are usually trying to push minion waves through one or more lanes until you've gained enough ground to ultimately destroy the enemy's sentries. This will feel familiar to anyone who has played Leage of Legends, DOTA 2 and\/or Smite. There is also a capture mode, which is pretty straight forward.<br><br>On the PvE side, you will have access to the PvP modes listed above (vs. bots) which offers a very casual, light-hearted experience of the PvP modes. Additionally, there is an entire campaign that can be completed solo (on normal difficulty) or cooperatively (on advanced difficulty). Loot improves with difficulty. The campaign consists of various story missions, which feel like dungeon runs. Each mission offers unique loot, which provides incentive for replaying them later. (I'll mention the significance of loot later). After you've had your fill of story mode missions, you also have the option of completing Operations (DLC\/Season Pass), which are essentially hard mode missions that scale in difficulty and reward the more times you run them. Again, each Operation offers the chance at loot unique to that mission, which is incentive to play them multiple times.<br><br>Loot:<br><br>The loot in this game presents an interesting overlap between the PvE and PvP content. In both the PvE and PvP modes, you are able to create gear loadouts, which is essentially a buid utilizing three items of your choice. Unlike the typical MOBA experience (where builds are involved), rather than simply choosing items from a predefined item set, you only have access to items that you have personally looted during your adventures in PvE or loot packs, which are awarded periodically as you progress through the game, whether in PvE or PvP. Furthermore, similar to the Diablo loot system, each individual item's stats are randomly generated within a certain range. For example, you may have two &quot;Super Huge Fists of Smashing&quot; which has attack damage and cooldown reduction as stats (which might be between +3% and +6%), but one's attack damage and cooldown reduction are +5%, whereas the second copy of the same item, might have +3.5% to the same stats.
