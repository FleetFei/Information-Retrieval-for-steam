When it is discovered that she exists, many local residents decide they want to capture her, some with nefarious reasoning and others with slightly less nefarious reasoning. Thus, away we go. <br><br>The problem I had to start, is that I didn't realize this is the SECOND game in a series, and nowhere in the game's store page or the game itself is this explained. I quickly began wondering who Lisa was when no one in this story seemed to have that name, and even worse, began confusing Lisa with the adopted daughter of the main character, primarily because the two have very similar sprite designs early on. Without having played the first game, or even known of its existence to read up on the story, the impact of the opening scene was not nearly as significant as it should have been. The player can easily enough grasp the situation at hand, but completely loses the importance of it in relation to the titular character. <br><br>The second issue I have with the story is, many characters seem to go from antagonistic to penitent at the drop of a hat, with one character's conversion being entirely pointless in the grand scheme of things. This seems to happen to prominent figures in the game so often that it becomes a game-specific cliche, and made me think the writer didn't know where to go with these characters, or simply didn't care anymore. <br><br>Lastly, I didn't feel much connection with the characters. Certainly the protagonist of this game inspires the most sympathy, as he is essentially an &quot;everyman&quot; character drowned in tragedy; and while that increases over the course of the game, I can't say the same for anyone else. There is a conflict that emerges later in the game involving the protagonist's adopted daughter, and while I can see a legitimate reason for the conflict, it actually made me feel more negatively towards the side I'm supposed to agree with. I say this because this conflict is not ultimately resolved until the DLC, LISA: The Joy. <br><br>Speaking of which, the DLC is a must if you want to wrap up anything story related. Without it, you'll be left with a rather open ended game that doesn't explain much. <br><br>So why do I still recommend this game? This game, for me, epitomizes the need for a &quot;Mixed&quot; recommendation option on Steam. Make no mistake, my problems with the story-related issues above were significant enough that I really questioned if I should give it a thumbs down. In the end, however, it's a very well-made game that, problems aside, looks like it was given considerable care. The humor was entertaining, the world was immersive and the creepiness was memorable, and for all of that, I would feel it unfair to give this game a thumbs down. The conflict of not realizing this is the second LISA game and from that, who exactly Lisa is, is still a little glaring. Undoubtedly I would have enjoyed this game much more had I known about or played the first game, instead of discovering this fact AFTER completion of this one, but there was simply nothing to give
