I wasn't sure it will last me for more than few hours, and I usually don't approach games thinking that I'll stay up all night playing, but I got lucky here. This game is all about management and choices. It'll start innocent, but quickly becomes pure evil the more you hold on.<\/div><br><b>Gameplay<\/b><br>You control most of the things indirectly. You'll start small, and you'll make a lot of mistakes while trying to get bigger. I can't promise you that you'll get hooked the same way I did, but after having few tries on my own, I've started seeing the game differently. It is based around trying it over and over, and I didn't get sick of it. Despite what I've heard, every part, even the beginning, becomes an endurance run. Since you have to control everything, you can't stop.<br><br>I've played it at least 15 times and every time I've just tried better. This game isn't mainly about discovering new things, but about mastering the logic it's based on. Sure, sometimes I damn the developers to hell, but I think the fundamental gameplay is good for what it is. <br><br><b>Controversy<\/b><br>There are some people that are divided about some choices, or more precisely, the lack of them. They have usually played the game for few hours and then stopped, complaining about basics, like AI, or inability to control some things. While they raise some good points, I don't think they apply here. Because the game is so simple, it is essentially based around what they don't like. And that's fine. I think what they're asking for is a more complex game overall, because this is like asking for having advanced control over Lemmings. Also, I would love more complex version of Kingdom, so I'm hoping for sequel or expansion already.<br><br>Perhaps it could include an easy mode with some additional choices, because the way I look at it, this game is supposed to be beaten by trying over and over. I can't tell if it'll last 30 hours, but there are many people that have been mesmerized by games like Binding of Isaac, which is also based around playing it over and over. I've found out the hard way, that you can overcome the odds, if you just get prepared. The more you play, the more you plan ahead - that is the way I more forward here.<br><br>Don't get me wrong. The game is difficult. But it's difficulty doesn't come from poor design decisions or blatantly unbalanced gameplay. People that drop the towel early might not be into this, but if you just hold on, and give it few more tries, where you learn how to be more efficient, you'll be rewarded with progress. When something limits you, it's made so you can work with it - just like in chess, the more planning you do, the easier your progress will be. And yes, sometimes the random generators can screw you over.<br><br>And to be honest, there are problems with the late game, where you're mainly forced to run from one corner of the map to another. Apparently, the longer you wait with destroying the portals (your main objective, after you're comfortable with your defences), the more difficult it becomes to take them down, as you're limited with numbers of your attack party, but enemy defence spawn is infinite and increases over time. This is still being discovered by many invididuals on forums, but so far it seems like you can't take your time, which adds another layer of difficulty. You have to be efficient, and really fast. Once you get past day 30, portals become almost impossible to take down - it's doable, but frustrating and time consuming, not to mention that nothing significant happens at that point and you can play for hours, just running back and forth, without any tactics you had to employ at the beginning of game. I don't recommend trying to complete the game this way.<br><br><b>Presentation<\/b><br>It's pretty damn good. That is something we all agree with. Music is captivating and what more, along with the graphics it creates a great atmosphere, which I rarely see, especially in such simple game. Overall, great presentation helps you push forward as well.<br><br><b>Verdict<\/b><br>This is not a mobile game. It is a PC game, and games like this are rare. The simplicity mixed with indirect influence over your growing settlement is challenging and addicting.
