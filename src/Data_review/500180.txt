just bad on PC, or just an average game that can't compare to what is already on offer on the platform.<br><br>Epic Flail's option menu does not escape the rule of shoddy ports. Want to leave the game? No button, press Exit. Want full screen? No checkbox to quit, press Alt + Enter. Where the game redeems itself admirable is that it lets you rebind your keyboard keys. Light at the end of my tunnel.<br><br>Let's get into the game proper. It looks mostly like a &quot;wave survival in an arena&quot; type of game, in which you have to dispatch a certain number of opponents before moving on to the next arena. So far, nothing new under the sun. Until you set yourself in motion. There is no jump button, but instead the little gladiator can perform a dash attack in any direction, including upwards. And again, and again, and again. Screw gravity, you can remain in the air indefinitely and the only limit is the sky (technically, the top border of the arena).<br><br>Super fast and empowering. Bring it on, give me something to slash and... WOW THAT GUY KILLED ME. Yes, enemy troops have the same moveset as the player. Dash against dash results in a parry, so you will have to be cunning to be able to consistently kill opponents. The start of the game is very challenging. Keyboard controls are perfect for ground combat and rolls, while the controller is better in the air but not very good for rolls because they are not on a dedicated button, you have to move the stick down while maintaining a direction which can be a little bit fiddly.<br><br>Once you get better and graduate to the next arena, you realise that your mastery of the game mechanics was just an illusion. Now you are tethered to a chain, with very little leeway to dash around. You will need to refine your skills. Then the next arena introduces deadly spikes. There is not much variety in the enemy types, but the different levels are really well crafted and each proposes its own quirks to challenge the player. I only found boss levels unenjoyable.<br><br>Where the game also shines is in its way to manage health. You die from one hit, but you can steal armor plates, shields and helmets from your opponent. Equipment basically act as health points that can be lost, retrieved and exchanged again. The AI seems to handle this quite well as the enemies opt for survivability by choosing to pick up gear rather than blindly attacking the player. However, they are less at ease around deadly traps. To smoothen the progression, the player can earn armor upgrades and start the levels with everything equipped. On top of that, the unlocked equipment cannot be stolen by enemy troops. <br><br>Longevity is not the strongest point of the game, as it can be completed within a few hours depending on your skill level. At least it does not outlast its welcome, even though a few more enemies and a multi-player mode would have been greatly appreciated.<br><br>Epic Flail has a surprising amount of depth in its levels and game mechanics, thus it ends up being really unique and original. The fast-paced gameplay is super fun\u2026 really a blast to play. However, the difficulty coupled with awkward controls could easily put off many people. If you want a challenging sword-fighting game and short bursts of intense fun and violence, Epic Flail is for you. As a mobile game it had no right to be this good, and yet it is.<br><br>Want to see it in action?
