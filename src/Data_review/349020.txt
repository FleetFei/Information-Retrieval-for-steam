&quot;Hyperspace Pinball&quot; yet, but as there is only one review, I suspect something is in order. The physics of \u201cHyperspace Pinball\u201d will most likely leave players of the aforementioned trio of pinball tables cold. It just doesn't feel much like a ball rolling, or struck, or arcing, or pretty much anything you're accustomed to. But as this is &quot;Hyperspace Pinball&quot;, quite likely this is a deliberate choice to do things differently. If you're accustomed to playing 15-minute games in FX2, be prepared to be thrilled if one of these one-ball games lasts over 30 seconds. (Some are 4-ball.)<br><br>Though you hit balls with flippers, it just doesn\u2019t have the feel of pinball, but rather of something else, something that deserves thoughtful consideration. <br><br>While pinball is traditionally a game of developing the skills to hit primarily stationary targets, the goal of \u201cHyperspace Pinball\u201d is to hit both stationary and moving targets. The table field is almost empty in all modes, with the edges containing the stationary targets, and the otherwise empty center consisting of moving targets. <br><br>The only way to lose the ball is down the center between the lower flippers. There are in-lanes, but no out-lanes. <br><br>The balls are frequently blocked from going down the center, often explosively, by barriers created through player actions.<br><br>All tables are essentially symmetrical, with a second set of flippers just above the halfway point.<br><br>\u201cHyperspace Pinball\u201d has three modes: Campaign, Boss Run and Endurance. <br><br>Campaign Mode has more the look a traditional pinball, but not the physics. It\u2019s very easy to get extra balls, and involves leveling-up within each game. A 4-ball game, averaging perhaps 2 - 3 minutes.<br><br>Boss Run Mode is basically an attempt to get to a larger block that is protected by other blocks or barriers, and has eight choices in the menu. Each of the eight unlocked selections offers a different objective and\/or targets, but on similar fields. The menu previews each table, some of which are quite complex and interesting. Possibly the strongest feature of &quot;Hyperspace Pinball&quot;. A one-ball, win or loose game unlikely to last a minute.<br><br>Endurance Mode has the physics that seems most similar to the pinball we all know. Apparently the goal is to hit the highest number of moving targets on an otherwise almost empty field, and that total number of targets hit is your score. A one-ball game averaging only seconds.<br><br>But even though this doesn't FEEL like pinball, that's no reason to write it off, especially since this is clearly meant to be pinball in name only. This is no sloggy, &quot;wait 5 minutes for the ball to land&quot; game. It's very fast paced, the leveling within a single table in Campaign mode is interesting, and the methods of obtaining points are creative. I won't go so far as to say the price is reasonable (even on sale), but I still have much of the table yet to play. And with bosses and 20 levels, there is certainly plenty of potential to explore.<br><br>I\u2019m having trouble getting used to the erratic ball movement in places, though it seems possibly to be deliberate. And the table is sometimes dark, and not always easy to see just what\u2019s going on. While I can appreciate the bright flashes that express some accomplishment, they can be very distracting in the midst of a game, so much that balls must frequently be artificially saved in the aftermath. It's not always possible to understand cause and effect in a completely &quot;flashed-out&quot; screen. I suspect with player input we will see a better game soon.<br><br>\u201cHyperspace Pinball\u201d does have a leaderboard, but I didn\u2019t recognize any of the names. Perhaps at this early stage they\u2019re mostly in-house, but in this short time I did get as high as #31, and I didn\u2019t look much beyond that. There are very few controls, and \u201cportrait mode\u201d is not among them. Unfortunately, this game makes very poor use of portrait mode should you change your PC's setting. The achievements affered are primarily for beating bosses, or cummulative actions that can only be accomplished over time, but basically by any patient (or obsessive) player.<br><br>&quot;Hyperspace Pinball&quot; is a very challenging game, more in the arcade mode than strictly as pinball. Each pinballer will have to take a chance with this game, as it may not satisfy a traditionalist. IF you don\u2019t mind spending the money, this seems likely to be worthwhile, but ONLY if the price is acceptable to you.
