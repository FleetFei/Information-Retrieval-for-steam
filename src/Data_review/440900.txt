I am looking forward to what develops as it is balanced by playing, and think it is a good chance for community involvement to actually make a game better - instead of just grinding it into oblivion. It is discouraging to see so much critique based on mechanics shared by other games, especially when most examples given to contrast Conan Exiles are similarly made in the survival style made popular by Minecraft. Common sense should dictate that realistic tools have realistic purposes. As someone who wanted a mature sandbox I find building can be frustrating still at times with tile placement such as wedges and triangles. However, I was surprised by the realism of the combat. It is reminiscent of Chivalry in first person, and hearkens back to days of Bushido Blade when one or two well-placed strikes can easily prove fatal in combat. When so many games involve ridiculous amounts of hitpoints with no-sold animations simply dropping HP, even animals in Conan Exiles won't hesitate to throw you on your hind end and deliver a fatal blow. Without gods nuking the map every 3 days, you can actually build large bases or complexes that take a fair bit of work to raid effectively, which I am sure is exactly what the devs had in mind. The devs have been responsive and open to feedback, even going as far as giving exploit hunters $100-$500 USD for uncovering serious glitches and exploits. They seem to truly care about the game, and also have a rough roadmap on how to make it great. I think this has the potential to grow into something big.<br><br>PVP and creative building aside, right now there is rougly 100 hours of content, although this number could very well triple in the coming months. Overall, the game is great fun, albeit a bit prone to instability due to constant tweaks and updates. I find myself wanting more, and can definitely recommend it even in its current state.
