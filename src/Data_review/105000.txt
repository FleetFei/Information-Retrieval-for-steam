I felt the game was about to end while I played chapter 6, and compared its length to Day of the Tentacle. A guy says &quot;I can't hear?&quot; Try the megaphone. Your character says &quot;not with my bare hands?&quot; Try using an item. If you play the first three chapters thinking the game is a walk in the park, though, just wait until the later chapters - it gets much more difficult!<br><br>* Although it is now pretty much standard for the genre, I do appreciate the fact that there is no way to &quot;screw up&quot; and ruin your game by using the wrong item somewhere, or by dying.<br><br>* The game's humor, although used much too infrequently, is top-notch and intelligent.<br><br>* The ending is really, really, really good. I won't spoil it for you, but if you didn't enjoy the game, the ending might make it pay off for you.<br><br>* Goes for as little as $2.49 during Steam sales.<br><br><b><u>The Bad:<\/u><\/b><br><br>* Some puzzles are a bit weird. With no way to go back and solve the puzzle legitimately, I shrugged and moved on.<br><br>* Because of its hand-drawn graphics, the game only runs in a 4:3 aspect ratio.<br><br>* No pause feature that I could find - I missed two semi-important cutscenes because I accidentally pressed escape, thinking it might pause the dialogue.<br><br>* There are a large number of bug reports on the Steam Forums. I stumbled upon the correct solution by accident. Some words in the game were never translated to English at all, or were incorrectly translated to Russian or Spanish. The developers could certainly have done a bit better with localization for the English version.<br><br><b><u>The Verdict:<\/u><\/b><br><br>Give this one a play if you are into point 'n' click adventure games. It is not as good as Daedelic's other big game, &quot;Deponia,&quot; but still a solid game and avoids some of the mistakes that Deponia made.
