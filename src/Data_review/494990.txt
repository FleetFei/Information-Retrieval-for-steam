to Space?<\/div><br>Too Angry to Space is a retro styled 2D side-scrolling action platformer that is reminiscent of many of the classics. Games such as Duke Nukem come immediately to mind. The developer, AK84C promises in his 'about' section that the game is not one that holds your hand yet simultaneously he assures potential purchasers that the gameplay is simple to learn.  Players assume the role of Red Madson, a pure gunslinger, as he prepares to kill aliens and robots that have attempted to take over your base.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Expectations<\/div><br>My expectations heading into the game were low due to multiple reasons. Firstly, AK84C is a first-time developer on Steam. Young developers tend to make quite a lot of mistakes in their debut games that players are hypocritical of. Secondly, the genre is one that has many more misses than hits. Finally, as of writing, the game had yet to receive any hype. As much as the first two are true people are generally willing to give cheap games an opportunity thanks in part to the Steam refund system.  Therefore, seeing just the three reviews - again, as of writing - was a little worrisome.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">More 80's Than 90's<\/div><br>In the description for the game, the developer tells you that you miss the 1990's. Yes, the first game that came to my mind that the game reminds me of was the original Duke Nukem, which was published in 1991. However, there were countless games that I played on the Commodore 64 and other computers of the 1980's that are reminiscent as well. These include the first four games in the Wonder Boy franchise, The Legend of Kage and of course the great Contra.<br><br>This is arguably the game's greatest weakness and strength. The problem here is that the game suffers a little from a lack of originality. That I am constantly reminded of games that I have played before provides a 'been there, done that' type mentality that can, at times, override the serious enjoyment I was having from the game.<br><br>However, that valid comparisons and memories can be drawn to the classics are a testimony to its quality. I'm not going to sit here and tell you that the game betters those experiences. However, what I can honestly tell you is that I did enjoy a similar experience. I experienced similar feelings of exhilaration, excitement, and adrenaline in taking on the various levels within the game. If you're highly skilled in the game you're looking at a few hours of very solid and well-executed entertainment.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">A True Challenge<\/div><br>One of the key components of a successful game in the genre is creating a significant yet achievable challenge. If you create a game that is too easy players can and will lose interest very quickly. However, if you set a game that feels impossible to complete players will just as quickly complain that the game is unfair. As a result, this is one of the first things that I look for even before I begin to judge a game's graphics or sound.<br><br>I can happily report that the game possesses the prerequisite amount of challenge necessary to make a quality game. Even the easy mode will represent a sizable challenge to many gamers. Most are not going to be able to breeze through the game as if it's a piece of cake. The challenge is based on a series of fast and furious gameplay along with some thorough level design. The boss fights are tough and require quick thinking and strategy. They are powerful enough that if you allow them to strike then you will die at the first hit. The game demands precision and skill.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Presentation<\/div><br>A review of Too Angry to Space would be incomplete without making specific mention of the presentation. Though the artwork is solid and looks good it becomes a little overly repetitive. However, I feel that this has much to do with the likely low production costs of the game making the developer repeatedly use the same assets. They are well drawn, however, and do look good.<br><br>Where the game shines from a presentation perspective is in the narration. I loved the constant voice over work of the hero whenever he killed something, acquired a weapon, got a health pick up &quot;I needed that,&quot; etc. It's very funny and reminiscent of the action movies of the period in which the game is inspired. There is a lot of colorful language and cursing that is utilized throughout these voice overs. There is, however, a profanity filter for adults who allow their children to play action games.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Verdict<\/div><br>Too Angry to Space is a worthy entry to the side-scrolling action platformer genre. It is worthy of your time and money. Developer AK84C deserves recognition for delivering a highly entertaining debut title. I have no problem in recommending this game to fans of the genre and those seeking immediate gratification and challenge.<br><br>For similar reviews and your chance to win great giveaways, including
