class=\"postedDate\">Posted: March 30\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t<div class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCave Story is a two-dimensional platformer developed originally by a single person and released for Windows as a freeware title in late 2004. It has since gained incredible popularity on the internet for its stellar gameplay and excellent story, as it rightfully should have. This release of the game, Cave Story+, has quite a few extra features compared to the 2004 original. Among these are selectable difficulties, an option to play with remastered graphics and sound, and many challenges and extra game modes. I consider this the definitive version of the game due to the aforementioned additions. There are some known issues, however. Users have reported this came to not run properly on the Mac operating system (OS X). Some are also dissatisfied with the new localization this version received. I disagree with this notion, though if you wish, you may download and apply the original the original fan-translation to Cave Story+.<br><br>The gameplay consists of you running and shooting enemies, in a simplistic sense. This is what you would expect from this sort of game. It does not end there, however. The world that you are presented is extensive and will leave you playing and exploring for hours. The weapon system is quite unique. Your weapons are charged with experience points collected from fallen enemies and they can be upgraded from level one to level three, each level making the weapon more powerful than the last(or the opposite in one particular case). This being said, you can also lose weapon experience by taking damage. This presents a unique challenge that is not present in other games. There are many different weapons that the player may use, some being much more powerful than others. There is also a dialogue system that serves as your means of communicating with other characters and to understand the story and the environment you are in. All of this is backed by well-implemented controls that feel natural and responsive while being customizable.<br><br>The music is, for lack of a better word, awe-inspiring. The music, both the original and the remastered soundtrack, compliment and fit perfectly with the story and the world itself. Each character has their own memorable theme that you surely will not soon forget. The game gives you the option of choosing the original, remastered, or &quot;new&quot; soundtrack. I personally prefer the remastered soundtrack, but the original is quite excellent too. This is the kind of music that I have found myself listening to both in and out of the game. <br><br>The narrative is very well-written and interesting. You awaken in a cave on the floating Mimiga Island with no recollection of who you are or why you are here. As your traverse the caves, you eventually find Mimiga Village, home of the Mimiga, a race of adorable rabbit-like creatures. In their initial forms, they are harmless. But if they eat the infamous red flower, they turn rabid and become incredibly strong. Though, they eventually consumed by the strength and are killed. The initial antagonist of the game, simply referred to as &quot;The Doctor&quot;, has seized ownership of the Demon Crown, a relic that allows him control over some powerful beings. He plans to use the red flowers to make an army of rabid Mimiga and send them to the surface and conquer the world. You and a small group of allies have the task of defeating the Doctor and to restore peace to Mimiga Island. The rest of the story is slowly revealed to the player throughout the course of the game. Some plot points require some thought to fully understand. You meet many interesting characters throughout your journey that aid you throughout the game and become your friends. There are many shocking moments and revelations in the story where you perhaps would not expect. The story is not what you would call &quot;predictable&quot;. There is also a choice system. Some are minor, such as those involved in having a conversation with a character. Others are more meaningful, for example, the life of a certain character depends on your previous choices. By the end of the game, I found myself attached to the characters(<span class=\"bb_spoiler\"><span>particularly Curly Brace<\/span><\/span>). This motivated me to achieve the greatest ending.<br><br>While we are on the topic of endings, some things must be addressed. There are three main endings to Cave Story, though there are variations to some endings. First is the &quot;bad&quot; ending, which is the easiest to achieve, but the worst, and saddest, to achieve story-wise. Next is the normal or neutral ending, which is the ending that most players achieve. It involves you completing your mission, but at the cost of the lives of some of your friends. Lastly, we have the &quot;good&quot; ending, or the greatest ending. This is by far, the hardest of the three to achieve, and rightfully so. The narrative concludes with all of your friends alive and well, thanks to your actions, along with defeating the Doctor and restoring peace to Mimiga Island. When I say achieving this ending is difficult, I truly mean it. You must carry out a specific set of events to save the lives of your friends and gain access to the true final stage and boss of the game. Cave Story is a fairly difficult game, especially if you are playing on the higher difficulties. It is however, very possible and forgiving. There are many save points strategically placed throughout the game for the purpose of making the game fun. This being said, the final mission necessary to achieve the best ending will show no mercy. While this stage is by far the most difficult, it only makes the ending you worked for that much better.<br><br>In conclusion, Cave Story is the greatest platformer, and perhaps the greatest indie game, I have ever played due to its unique weapons system, great gameplay, and impressive soundtrack. It presents a fantastic story that can bring someone to tears and has many different endings to maximize replayability. Because of all of this, I am sure I will find myself replaying Cave Story many times in the future. So if you want to achieve the best ending prepare to keep a keen eye for details or rather even look for some guides.<br>From the perspective of game mechanics it\u2019s the usual run-jump-shoot trio, but with a few twists. There is Polar Star which fires plain bullets, then there\u2019s Fireball which produces bouncing deadly balls of fire. Then there\u2019s Spur which has chargeable beam; the longer you charge it, the more powerful the resulting shot will be. Plenty of choices results in very different strategies player can use. Though you\u2019ll tend to have favourites among your arsenal, keeping them all leveled up and ready will be invaluable. To make things even more complex some of the weapons can be traded off in exchange providing you with other guns which have different qualities. Unlike your main interaction tools your character doesn\u2019t really level up, with the exception of few valuable health upgrades.
