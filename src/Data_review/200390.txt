The most precious commodity is now oil, used not only for keeping the navy ships moving but for producing electricity and other necessities. You play a young navy commander named Kevin who is fresh out of school and ready to kick raider \u2665\u2665\u2665 to protect your people (and grab oil supplies while you\u2019re at it).<br><br>Game play is simple in Oil Rush. You usually start with a factory that automatically builds units until a limit is reached. You can send these units to any other \u201cnode\u201d such as another factory or oil derrick. If the node belongs to an enemy a fight breaks out and whoever has more units will win and gain\/keep control of the node. You cannot send your units into the middle of nowhere, they can only be sent to another node. And since the max limit of units is pretty small, the action is pretty fast paced (it\u2019s hard to build up a massive army and crush the enemy because they\u2019ll probably have the same size army as you). If your factory is heavily defended with bunkers you can feel safer sending all your troops out to attack. Oil derricks function as nodes on the map (you can send troops to them) but they cannot be defended with bunkers and they only produce oil, which is kind of like mana for your special powers. The special powers aren\u2019t especially awesome but they can turn the tide if you have almost enough units to conquer an enemy node. <br><br>Pros:<br>+Simple gameplay where you\u2019re really limited in what you can do. Don\u2019t go in thinking it\u2019s a real time strategy game like Starcraft, this game is all about capturing and holding map nodes.<br>+The graphics are very good for an indie game. My video card fan was spun up the whole time. <br>+Unlike the Galcon games that I\u2019ve played, Oil rush has an actual story campaign. You follow Kevin\u2019s progress through hardships and an increasing number of enemy factions.<br><br>Cons:<br>-The simple gameplay may turn some people off. As you progress in the campaign you get access to a lot more units and more special powers, but the core gameplay remains the same.  Capture and hold as many factories as you can until you reach your unit max, then send them to attack an enemy node. <br>-The gameplay may be simple (moving troops around), but the game itself can be extremely difficult. Even on easy mode, some of the campaign missions are against overwhelming numbers of enemies. <br><br>Overall, if you like virus type games you should enjoy Oil Rush.
