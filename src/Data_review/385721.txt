stories of his past to his granddaughter, Gwendolyn. We are transported back into his stories and play the young Graham through his adventures. The game is point-and-click, more similar to a Tell-Tale game than a traditional adventure game, but deceptively deep. I played this game part way through before realizing that my two youngest children (ages 6 &amp; 8) would probably enjoy sharing this experience with me. So, I started the game fresh with them beside me. Making new choices this time around, I found subtle changes that altered the story. After we completed the story (about 5 hours of gameplay), we immediately started yet another game in order to catch achievements we missed the first play-through. Again, we altered our choices and saw scenes that we had completely missed previously. Although you would be able to fly through puzzles again when you replay, it wouldn\u2019t be tedious in order to see alternate endings. What I love about this is that now my children can play, and we will each have a different experience to talk about.<br><br><br>King\u2019s Quest was a joy to play. We hated to stop playing when it was bedtime, and was eager to get back to it the next day. The puzzles were challenging, but not impossible. Death, although possible, was light and without consequence, keeping with the theme of the game. And the humor was delightful, providing plenty of laugh-out-loud moments for the entire family. Puns and witty dialogue abounded. Characters were hilarious and interesting. There is a Princess Bride them that runs throughout, all the way down to a Battle of Wits with Wallace Shawn\u2019s character. I giggled over these throughout the entire game. The part that really drew me in, however, was the art. The art is simply gorgeous. \u201cIt\u2019s so beautiful!\u201d was muttered often, even by my little boys. It really captivated the imagination and, at times, left me in awe.<br><br>Any complaints about the game would revolve around things like the inability to skip dialogue, the false hope of seeing an area I thought I could get to, but in reality couldn\u2019t, and it being perhaps a bit too easy in parts. Of all these, having to listen through dialogue I\u2019ve already gone through several times is the most irritating.
