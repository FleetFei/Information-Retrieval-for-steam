nothing about the topic. I mostly watched this because the tight formation of 3 bikes looked like a crazy sport and I was curious how it worked. This ended up being an extremely engaging and inspiring documentary.<br><br>First, some faults. The performance tracking technology using was interesting, but sometimes it felt like an ad for that stuff. My gut kept telling me this coverage was funded by the celebrity scientists that want to sell those products. I also found it difficult to follow the story of the 3rd and 4th team mates. They keep making a big deal about one getting cut, but never fully explain what that means or when it happened. They also do very little show their personalities outside of their athletic history and strengths\/weaknesses. I want to know more about them and feel like there was nothing to grab on to except that they have great relationships with their husbands.<br><br>The strongest point is how they set up the premise very early: they have a completely impossible task and no funding to solve it. The only thing that will take them to victory is creative technology and hardcore training. They really showcase the small breakthroughs and stress as they struggle to keep up with their goals. You can see the pain involved and any gamer or dev can recognize it from completing difficult game to shipping a game. And watching 3 bikes in formation was as strange and fascinating as I'd hoped. I learned a bunch and that's always the mark of a good doc.<br><br>The female angle is there and awkward at times, but for the most part it just focuses on how to tune the human body in to a superhuman machine.
