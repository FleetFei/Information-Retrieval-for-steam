It's very innovative and I'm surprised that nobody else picked up on it. If it were to be put into another game with more spells with modern graphics and effects, the game would definitely win many awards. <br><br>The dungeons can be confusing, for there are many tunnels within a dungeon and you have no sense of direction where you're supposed to go. You have to keep up with the story to know what you're doing. If you aren't going to use a walkthrough for this game, you may find it quite difficult. For example, for one of the quests you need to get back a Troll idol. To do that, you have to go to the Goblin fortress. You then, without an actual direct hint, after a long time of just wandering around in there, may decide to move a pillow to find a key to continue the quest. Where does the key go? Just try it on everything that's locked there. It's very confusing. Although, the dungeons do have quite a dark ambience. The only thing that may give you fright is when you don't have sound on and are not expecting a monster to creep out of the dark or attack you from behind, then making you panic as you're trying to gesture the correct runes to cast a spell and kill them. Another example is when you didn't know you had to stake zombies to actually kill them and they end up creeping up on you from behind. I mean, it's not scary, but the fog, darkness, and old style graphics and sound just give you anxiety (not that it's a big deal).<br><br>The combat is pretty good. It's not too easy or too hard. The runes, in my opinion, are not hard to do and I only have 5 hours into the game. I don't see why people are complaining about it in their reviews. It most definitely does not take long to get good at casting. It may take some practice for some people, I guess. Melee weapons are nice and take some skill to use. One must avoid being hit whilst trying to go on the offensive, and the only real way is by hit-and-running or attacking from long range, such as with spells are archery. I have yet to use a bow, so I can't really go into that. Along with magic, you can precast spells. You can precast up to 3 spells so you have them ready when you're actually going to fight so you don't have to gesture a spell while you're trying to stay alive against some monsters. <br><br>The story is pretty good. There is plenty of humor in the game, such as goblin kings with digestive issues and horrible goblin mom jokes. The lore behind the entire story is quite interesting. Humanity and monsters live underground, due to the sun disappearing and the freezing of Earth, making the ambience darker yet, knowing that your character has probably never even seen the sun. Although, with that, there is a loophole. You can find and buy plants that somehow feed themselves without the sun and the fact that humans need the sun to survive. If the lore means that there is no sun, at all, then their &quot;solar&quot; system would just be flying wherever they want in space or attracted to another planet's gravity. I guess it makes level design easier, simply needing walls, a roof, and a ceiling, not a background of trees and hills and a sky with clouds.
