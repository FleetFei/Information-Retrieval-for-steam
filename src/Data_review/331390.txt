There are enough levels of challenge to be satisfied with, and the game rewards you with more items for higher difficulties, although sometimes higher levels must be revisited after acquiring stronger skills and equipment. You unlock new summonable units and skills by progressing the story and leveling up.There's plenty of skills to get and the equipment upgrade and crafting is fun and satisfying to go through. <br>Units level up permanently, getting stronger and equipment is also permanently assinged to each unit type until you decide to change it (rarity is a thing which adds a little depth.)<br><br>The problems with this game... there are plenty. First off, the UI and game art for the most part resembles that of the average flash game and leaves much to be desired, it also has a lot of inconsistency. For example, the art on the game start screen is wildly superior to everything else in the game, like not much work was put into it. There's a few issues with the items, for example rarity is indicated purely by a colored text, which I feel like could've been more clear if ordered properly and named differently, for example all ores are &quot;Ore&quot; with different colors and prices, and everything is ordered from high to low, so browsing the store for items felt a bit unintuitive at first, it would've been nice to have things like &quot;iron, copper, etc&quot; or at least a word expressing quality, such as lesser or superior.<br><br>The writing and narrative are BAD at best, there's absolutely ZERO exposition or description of the situation, the area, or story the characters find themselves in, and you must pretty much guess what's going on from the unintelligible babbling of the characters that ranges from &quot;shut up&quot; to &quot;doors 1 and 5 are under attack&quot;. You're pretty much thrown into the story without knowing what's going on, who anyone is, or why things are happening, I found myself playing 9-10 stages which took me a few hours without even knowing what a &quot;sakura&quot; character is supposed to be, even though it was mentioned repeatedly and is a core concept to the game. There's pairs of characters named the same and their names differ simply by one letter, and this causes some confusion as to who is talking, specially since the writing is poor to begin with. It felt like the dev liked the idea of visual novels so they tried to shoehorn something similar to the storytelling but with little effort.<br><br>The music score is decent, but it is limited, gets reused a lot and gets repetitive quite fast. Sometimes the &quot;tension&quot; song stops playing at any given scene when moving to a different room instead of continuing with the story, and will return to the normal &quot;calm&quot; lobby song when you go back, which kills any sense of urgency or relative immersion.<br><br>The strategy... There is simply none, this is the biggest gripe I have with this game so far. Basically you are supposed to do certain moves at certain waves in certain places, otherwise you WILL fail. For example, a big wave of shield-using enemies is coming, you have no option other than using your own shield-unit to pile them up and then a mine skill to take them all out at once,  that you may or not have the energy for if you used it carelessly before, making this kind of mistakes rarely allows you to make a comeback and will result in a restart\/game over. The game expects you to have certain skills at certain points in the game so the skill tree gives a false sense of customization that's not there, for example at one point (quite early on) when deciding to go for the next level the game quite bluntly told me that the next stage was NOT clearable without a skill I opted not to take, so it felt forced and shallow. If unable to clear a stage and getting clearly overpowered, you're either not using the right units\/skills at the right time, or need to grind for some levels and better equipment. <br>To add to the last point, there seems to be an optimal\/unique way to do your skill tree. At a certain map I found myself FORCED to take level 2 in one of the skills, otherwise it'd prove impossible to clear the waves as fast the game expected me to. I think the illusion of choice is a really big problem with this game so far that and it would be nice to see that improved on.<br><br>All-in-all, it's a decent game if you're not expecting a masterpiece, the gameplay is mostly dynamic and and feels satisfying because of the short timers, but this may also not appeal to everyone. And here we come to Winged Sakura: Mindy's Arc: A fast paced anime-styled tower defense game by Winged Sakura Games.<br><br>I'm sure I've lost you already, so allow me to clear things up now. This is not the type of tower defense game many people will be expecting. It's beyond fast paced, it's hectic, and though I've yet to actually find a need for it, the game comes with a &quot;Strategic Pause&quot; (This is a normal pause, but allows you to catch your breath, refill your drink, think about your next move, or run out into the rain and question your current life choices). The game is structured very similarly (so far) to the first levels of Plants versus Zombies, another tower defense game that this one, like many others, so desperately wants to live up to. Except this time around, it's cats versus....the same exact cats, and your cats are on a timer. By this, I mean they fade away in seconds, or even after they attack ONCE. So you will be constantly reapplying the towers and struggling to keep yourself from being overrun by.............cats. Oh and a chicken! And I *think* I've seen bunnies by now. <br><br>The gameplay is honestly pretty enjoyable. Fast paced is a nice breath of fresh air in the genre, and I'm always willing to try something new. So let's move onto...the...story......<br>Hm.<br><br>If you're looking into buying this game, or at least have read the review up to this point, you're probably an anime fan. And most anime fans have enjoyed anime on television, from Adult Swim to Toonami, all the way to Anime Unleashed by from Techtv. Think back to those good old days--remember when you'd turn it on, and it would be an anime that you've never seen before, but the story was a good ten episodes in already? But you watched it anyways, not really trying to get into the story? Mine was .hack\/\/sign. God save me, I had no bloody idea what was happening, but I watched it anyways. Point being, that's what this story is. You get into the story, and CLEARLY, you're in the middle, but the game makes no attempt catch you (or its characters) up to speed. They all start out like they know a bunch of secrets, act all chummy, and guess what? You're not in on the joke.<br><br>The art style is charming if cliched', and there seems to be a lot to do for your characters. There's a skill tree, item crafting, upgrades, so on and so forth. I use the word upgrade despondently because Claws turns into Claws, but with a green name and a bunch of upward pointing green arrows on the stats. I would have liked to have seen the artists come up with cooler weapon and accessory upgrades akin to Drakengard style, where each upgrade looks a little more menacing. But since you only see these items in the inventory between rounds, I'm sure I'll get over it.<br><br>This game is still very recently released and Team Winged Sakura Games seems to know what their doing when it comes to patching the game. But for now, don't be surprised when you have to grind the same &quot;rounds&quot; (all the levels are the same) over and over again. Which is probably a good thing, because the STORY mode, a term used VERY loosely, looks to be very very short from where I stand, at just around 5 hours, grinding included. However, I HAVE NOT BEATEN THIS GAME, so this shortness may and or may not be true. <br><br>EDIT: Good news. It's infact quite a bit longer than I expected! This is a good thing.<br><br>There is a neko maid added about an hour in. I am still waiting for an explaination for this beyond &quot;because anime.&quot;<br><br>All and all, I'm still playing the game, and it knows how to keep my focus. If you like anime and tower defense, you'll probably enjoy this game.
