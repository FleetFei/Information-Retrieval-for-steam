2015\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t<div class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\t<div class=\"bb_h1\">Introduction <\/div><br>Planet Diver is an action game about diving through planets using a wing suit. Diving through these planets will be dangerous and you\u2019ll find many obstacles in your way but fear not, for you have Buddy with you! Buddy is your robotic companion of amusement and will be of use to you on your perilous adventure.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Presentation <\/div><br>Planet Diver\u2019s graphical style is quite hard to describe, it\u2019s almost as if it\u2019s being displayed on a relatively old analogue TV, well not really but you get the idea. Anyway, this graphical style not only fits the game well but also looks damn good! While fairly simple, all of the models in game look great, particularly some of the more colourful enemies you\u2019ll face.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay <\/div><br>Taking place on three planets each with three different biomes, Planet Diver\u2019s 75 missions is reasonably varied and with an endless arcade mode too there\u2019s a good amount of bang for your buck. The core mechanics of the game are incredibly simple. The levels have different lanes your character can travel in. As well as having the ability to move left or right into a new lane you can also speed up or slow down (the speeds and number of speed levels vary depending on what Buddy mods you have but the default is three different levels of speed). Speeding up (boosting) just before hitting an enemy will allow you to fly through them, i.e. kill them. Slowing down (braking) is used as a way to dodge as well as a way to recharge your boosts (default number of charges is three).<br><br>Okay so it\u2019s not quite that simple. For a start you have two health bars, meaning you can take three hits before it\u2019s game over. A health bar is depleted whenever you hit or get hit by something without dodging or boosting. For example if you fly straight into one of the many unfriendly creatures found in the game you\u2019ll lose one health bar. But fear not! Health bars can be recharged by killing creatures or by collecting star stuff. Star stuff is a resource found throughout the game, the amount found depends on your speed and how far into the level you are, the more you collect the more your health bar is recharged. Star stuff is also the game\u2019s currency and can be used to purchase new skins, Buddy mods and soundtracks. As well as all of this the tunnel you fly through isn\u2019t smooth with nothing in it, there are often obstacles in the level which you\u2019ll need to dodge if you want to survive.<br><br>Back to those pesky creatures I mentioned earlier. There are some. You\u2019ll find them throughout each level and more often than not they\u2019ll do their damn best to make your life a misery. What they do, of course, varies with each critter. Some of them will follow you and then dive bomb into you while others will sit on the wall of the level and shoot webs that slow you down at you. As well as these, you\u2019ll find yourself in boss fights at certain points in the game. The bosses are damn well challenging I\u2019ll tell you that, even the best Planet Divers will struggle a little here.<br><br>The last thing worth mentioning about the gameplay is the fact that each level has a challenge you have to complete in order to progress with each challenge having three levels: bronze (the minimum needed to unlock the next level), silver and gold. Generally these are relatively easy to do without going too far out of your way in any given level though some of them are rather challenging. While I did like these challenges I did feel as though some of them were a little too harsh considering the fact you can\u2019t progress without completing them. For example the first boss fight\u2019s challenge is to only get hit three times for bronze, something that\u2019s incredibly hard to achieve. Now you can of course avoid the challenges thanks to something I\u2019ll mention in the next section but I didn\u2019t want to take the easy way out and I imagine most people won\u2019t.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Miscellaneous <\/div><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li>While there\u2019s not much of a story in the game there is dialogue between Buddy and the diver before the start of each level and it\u2019s often rather amusing.<br><\/li><li>Store prices felt a little steep to me, I wasn\u2019t able to purchase my first item until about two thirds of the way through the first planet.<br><\/li><li>The soundtrack fits nicely with the game but does get a little repetitive after a few hours of gameplay.<br><\/li><li>This should be obvious but the game relies pretty heavily on your reaction times so if you\u2019re particular slow when it comes to reacting to games you might not have the best time while playing.<br><\/li><li>If you fail a level a fair few times in a row you\u2019ll be given the option to do a super easy run. You won\u2019t be able to keep any of the star stuff you collect but you\u2019ll be able to complete the mission.<br><\/li><li>The price of Planet Diver is considerably less than I expected when I was playing and reviewing the game, at this price I really can\u2019t see any reason for you not to buy it.<\/li><\/ul><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Want to know what the game looks like in action?
