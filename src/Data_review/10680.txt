Review<\/div><br>The highly acclaimed British developer Rebellion is striving to scare the hell out of you again with the new Aliens vs. Predator game. Re-capturing the pulse pounding game play from the original PC gaming classic, players once again get to play three different campaigns from three different perspectives. Aliens vs. Predator is a thrilling gaming experience no matter what part of the campaign you play. The clash this time around takes place on the deep space frontier world being colonized by humans. There on the the colony on a far off planet an ancient pyramid is found. Of course, scientist and archaeologists can't help, but go exploring this historic find. This of course triggers the ultimate confrontation between Colonial Marines, Aliens and the Predators.<br><br>The single player of the game is a rather unique playing experience. You can play any of the three species, each with strengths and weaknesses. The Colonial Marines, while rather fragile, they have a heck of a lot of fire power. The Predator has it's stealth and cloaking device, and the Aliens have the claws, the speed and numbers. Each character type requires a different skill to play to it's full potential. It all comes down to the skill of the individual players really.<br><br>The Colonial Marine makes due with the human technologies, from pulse rifles and pistols and auto turrets to motion trackers. The over all feel of survival horror and thrill when dozens of Aliens start clambering at you from every possible angle is exhilarating. Moving along through the darkness with only your beam of light illuminating small portions of an area is such a thrill.<br><br>The Predator is pretty awesome to play, I mean really who did not long for a game with this character after seeing the films? All the Predator equipment is at your disposal here, from the personal cloaking device which aids you in your stealthy approach to human targets, on to the unique ability to target a Marine and then throw a recorded mish mash of a voice to a point where the target will go investigate. This is perfect for luring target's out of a much more dangerous group. They will not fall for this tactic though if they are on alert. Predator also has it's view modes, each species has a special view mode that can spot them best. For instance if the player is in a view mode for hunting Marines, they have to take care because the Aliens are next to invisible in that mode. Getting around as the Predator is achieved by the standard running around...but what's fun is that the Predator has a focus jump mode - a circle representation of where you can jump to shows up, then hitting the right button and off you go. One would think the Predator would be the be all end all power player of this game, but there are things in place that balance it out pretty well. Some of the Predator's weapons have to be recharged, so just blasting away with the shoulder mounted plasma cannon is not advised.<br><br>Playing the Alien campaign the player is the creature that the scientist named \u201cSix\u201d - a poor captured Alien. The Wayland - Yutani company has some special plans for Six, oh the greed and nastiness of this company, who is the real monster here? Playing as an Alien requires a lot of tactical skill to fully utilize this character types natural defensive and attack capabilities. Claws, teeth and spiked tail and special senses. Sort of a natural enhanced sense mode that aids in seeing the prey. Blending with the darkness and easing in on a target is what the Alien does, or can attack in great numbers. Of course, the Alien can run along walls and ceiling, making it a very cool character to control indeed. As noted, darkness is your friend so, the Alien can also break light emanating sources, to plunge an area into darkness. Getting in close and personal is what the Alien does best. It's a good balance of character abilities and environment based survival abilities.<br><br>Other than the atmospheric single player campaigns there are several different multi player matches to be had in the game and all of them have something different to offer:<br><br><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li>Deathmatch \/ Species Team Deathmatch \/ Mixed Species Team Deathmatch<br><br>A staple of the online gaming, there are three variations of deathmatch featured in Aliens vs. Predator. There is the standard free-for-all deathmatch mode where no species allegiances matter. You\u2019re on your own here and it\u2019s every man\/extra-terrestrial for themselves. Species Deathmatch is basically team deathmatch. The players are separated into three teams based on the three species and pit against each other. I figured I'd give it a go, see if its as good as I remember it to be.  To my surprise it is just as good as I remember. The graphics still hold up really well on my laptop and the gameplay is just as engaging and intense. Whether you are the rough and tumble Marine trying to save him and his friends while you discover some secrets about your employer Weyland-Yutani, the stealthy Predator cruising through the jungle as he tries to restore honour lost to the humans or the ferocious Xenomorph dodging and ducking through vents as you slaughter human and Predator alike.  <br><br>The game really gets the universe of AVP. The smart guns and sentry guns from Alien are here and ready to use and all of Predator's gadgets and gizmos are present as well. Everything from his cloaking devices to his smart disc to his Combi Stick are here. <br><br>While the Marine's campaign is very much similar to your standard FPS, it does try to spice things up with by adding a motion tracker from Aliens. The tracker will quickly become your best friend as the Xenomorphs will  know your location and try to sneak up on you. I would have liked to see more variation of firearms though. There isnt many in the campaign. Maybe only 6 or so are there to use. Thankfully the flamethrower and smart gun keep things interesting. I loved watching the Xenomorphs stop, drop and roll as they tried to put out the fire that was rapidly burning them to death. Other than these few things, the Marine campaign isn't that memorable. Thankfully this doesn't really take away too much from the game.<br><br>The Predator's story is much better. They did a good job on this section of the game, in this section, you truly feel like you are the extraterrestrial hunter as you leap from vanatge point to vantage point and stalk your prey with precision. The story isn't very compelling here as you try to kill an abomination and remove the stain upon your honor that the humans have placed. But the finishing and stealth kills here are gory and satisfying, It is great fun to use your Predator strength and snap a human's neck before you remove his head, spine and all or to impale a Xenomorph on your wrist blades as your tear it's inner mouth out. The gadgets here are done well and fun to use. The plasma caster is capable of completely vaporizing enemies from long range and the smart disc is able to decapitate and injure multiple enemies in one throw.<br><br>The Alien's campaign is great fun. While the story here is pretty much absent, you don't really seem to notice as you crawl across ceilings and through sewers and ambush your prey. It was really something else to sneak up on a civilian and pin him down while a facehugger comes in to finish the job. The Alien doesn't really have any gadgets here other than her  tail, claws and teeth. But combat is better to be avoided as the Marines will turn you into swiss cheese at the drop of a dime. It is much better to use the shadows and vents to your advantage and take them out one by one.<br><br>So all in all, the game is good. The gameplay is fun, the kills are gory and the graphics still hold up really nice for a 7 year old game. The only real complaints I have is the the AI can be absolute idiots sometimes as they just stand out in the open and stare at a wall. But other than that, the game is solid and just as fun as I remember it to be. The final score is 8\/10.
