It's a story we've heard before many times and a genre we've seen perfected already, and Vaccine doesn't exactly do it any favors here (bad localization abound) but it does seek to shake the scene up a bit with the procedurally generated levels and items made popular by the roguelike genre. It might not be the same engaging story we know from Resident Evil or the same evolved gameplay, but Vaccine manages to create a frightening DIY vibe of its own that is worth the trip for those with patience for its archaic and old school ways.<br><br>It also has the difficulty of a genuinely old school and retro survival horror game. Everything you loved and loathed about the classics is here, from the clunky and cumbersome tank controls to the hard to manage limited item space that required planning. Every challenging and confusing imperfection in the game's design is almost definitely deliberate in order to keep in fashion with the originators it pays its homage to. Rainy Night Creations has the aesthetics and atmosphere of a 90's era survival horror down pat, and gives us an authentic trip down memory lane in both visuals and frustrating challenge.<br><br>Later enemies become seriously deranged and grotesque, and much harder to take down and dangerous. From rats and bats, to zombies and.. freaky crawling flesh dudes, Vaccine has plenty of grotesque oddities to die to.<br><br>When it comes down to it luck will be your greatest ally as the trick to grabbing a truly excellent run is having good enough chances to find the random placement of key items as early as possible, because honestly if you run into one of those flesh-y crawly creatures without ammo or a gun (prefereably a shotgun) you can kiss your \u2665\u2665\u2665 goodbye. This isn't uncommon with games of a procedural 'roguelite' style, and it isn't to say that Vaccine doesn't still require some skillful gunplay and careful consideration with stat distribution.<br><br>An addictively quick &quot;coffee-break&quot; take on the survival horrors of old. The rogue-lite twist of having a whole mansion that is procedurally generated along with the location of important key items and equipment keeps each quick playthrough exciting and unpredictable. Many of your runs for the life-saving vaccine will end quickly and humiliating as you die to stupid mistakes and oversights, but when you make it further and finally reach that destination it couldn't feel more rewarding.
