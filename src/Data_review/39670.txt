The game has many obvious and troublesome downsides, it is a real pain in the back for any casual nowadays gamer.<br><br>However, if you happen to know the drill of the old-school shooters (where, for example, you need to hit crouch button in the middle of jump to reach higher altitude) - I suggest you to buy and play the game. If you manage to beat it now, without all those modern features like flashing directional arrows, checkpoints and all-mighty recovering chest-high wall - well, welcome to the club, man!<br><br>Let me tell you what was interesting in the game.<br><br>First, it's the space. Not the out-world nothingness we call cosmos, no, I'm talking about really big levels. Almost every level here is huge, by every FPS standard. Sure, it's still a shooter on the rail, the most of the times there is only one possible route from start to end. But, that means kilometers of walking. Or driving. Or diving (there is an underwater level). Yeah, the most part of those wastes will be filled with primitive copy\/paste textures - same trees, same buildings with the same posters, same decals inside buildings... But that practice is still all over the industry! And even brighter in that parade of uniformity looks some noticable cool things on the background, such as actual rotating of the space station in the intro chapter, with different stars and views of the Earth in the portholes. Or the funny snowman somewhere in the middle of taiga.<br>All those big areas were made for the game to add some sense of realism. That kind of great achievement, when you to obtain some new thing have to actually do something, walk somewhere, elaborate, strive... and not just click two buttons on your phone to get all in your place - right away.<br>Sure, not too many people would like to change the comfort of modern games which telling you what you should do, for something odd-looking and not providing you with the safe and quick routes.<br><br>Second thing I liked in Chaser was the variety of missions. There is a sniping mission, where you need to protect a transport on its way through hostile military base. Exoskeleton mission, where you simply shoot your miniguns and rockets, defending the base. Stealth level, where you need to sneak around some goons to pick up some valuable conversation between some mobsters. Underwater level, where you swim... Swim, and then swim more, to get to some harbor &quot;undetected&quot;. Well, on the way there are hundreds of aggressive droids which you need to shoot, and the route isn't too obvious even with markers, so maybe, that would not be the best entertainment in your life... But the whole game isn't! It's about to see of what you're made from. A challenge for your reaction time, coordination and patience. And your navigation skill.<br><br>Finally, the story. Yeah, it's trivial for the most part, and its major pieces you'll recognize much faster than the game plot shows them to you. But, it's a long story, really comparable with the Half-Life by its length and number of events and changes in the pace and alignments. It's much better than modern &quot;don't ask questions, just shoot'em all!&quot; standards.<br><br>Downsides are plenty, as I've warned you before.<br><br>The levels are so huge, that the most amount of the game time you'll be wandering, where to move next. In theory, it's quite simple - just follow the blood trail, more precisely, go where there are enemies waiting. But, in every level, there are some switches to click, holes to jump in or crumbled ladders you're still gotta climb. Well, train your wits about it... or just watch it all on the youtube, like all weaklings do.<br><br>The AI of your enemies... They aren't gonna find the cure from cancer, that's for sure. But their reaction time... Even if you catch someone off-guard and will try to shoot him in the nape, in a split second he'll be knowing where you are and shooting at you. If you ever played Counter Strike 1.6 (or earlier) with tough bots - that's them. Which is more - sometimes game spawns them on the cleared territory, behind your back. That's tough, unfair... but manageable, if you have your guts around.<br><br>Finally, the proposed layout of the game keys is outrageous. Just change it right before the game start. You'll need your nightvision, 'cause it's usually night time around. You'll need a quick button for &quot;reflextion mode&quot; aka bullettime\/slomo feature. Yeah, it's not that cool as in Max Payne or at least in FEAR - but it's still useful, especially on the hard difficulty level.<br><br>Let's summarize again. The game is bad, by modern standards, it's not worth your time and money. It will hurt you badly.<br><br>On the other side, if you're a tough son of a gun, who have many old shooter games as kindergarten toys... try this thing. For example, it's much more variable, than Die Hard Nakatomi Plaza. More realistic, in many ways. And when it's not - it's a challenge for you to overcome. So come - if you're ready.
