While I still have an issue with the fact that roughly half the levels are strictly linear, the missions that open up to you (like &quot;Stonemarket First Bank&quot;, &quot;Life of the Party&quot; and &quot;Masks&quot;) offer a wide array of alternate routes and secret passages to accomplish your goals, as well as give a far grander sense of the City and the threat Karras poses to its inhabitants.<br><br>Likewise, the variety of weapons and items you can acquire is great and allows for lots of experimentation, although (since I'm a purist) I still stuck to water arrows for the majority of the game.<br><br>Admittedly, I found much more joy in the levels where I could pick off guards at will with hit-and-run tactics. I wish more of the missions had those kinds of additional areas or features to make them worth replaying, because I didn't feel much compulsion to go back and do much besides replay the missions that were either &quot;loot everything&quot; or &quot;steal and\/or kill everything&quot;.<br><br>All in all, though, Thief II is still a standout in the stealth genre.
