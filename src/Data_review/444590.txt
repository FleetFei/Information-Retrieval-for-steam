recent one-man project that claims to be inspired by Dark Souls, Resident Evil, Eternal Darkness, Bloodborne, probably Hellboy and Prey at this rate. Throw in Pride and Prejudice while we're at it, why not.<br><br>Like most indie games, Shrouded in Sanity isn't so much inspired as it is brewed in a beaker Powerpuff Girls style with its roots wholesale vomited into the concotion. Chemical X in this case being &quot;lack of money&quot; resulting in lowest resolution for pixel art since the original Wolfenstein.<br><br>Unlike most other examples of hyper-deriviative titles, Shrouded in Sanity is fun and well worth playing. <br><br>Fast-paced action is the name of the game. You've got your i-framed roll, which you'll be using a lot less than you think, guard, which you'll be using a lot more than you think, two types of parries (although only the sword parry is particularly useful), and just the one weapon because it's a one-man project and some slack is going to be cut. <br><br>With the enemy variety in tow and the gamepad in hand, Shrouded in Sanity easily stayed entertaining over multiple playthroughs. You've got tools for every situation, enemy placement changes throughout the game and the enemy variety is more than I think anyone expected.<br><br>It's not without caveats though. One major caveat that has permeated a lot of its ilk.<br><br>First, a little history:<br><br><i>King's Field is a 1994 first person aRPG released by FromSoftware. In it, you had only one attack and could easily stunlock enemies. To balance this, they incentivized charging up attacks for much more damage. This charge was dictated by a stamina bar. This worked well, since it replenished almost immediately. <br><br>In 2009, Demon's Souls, famously originally just a third-person King's Field for modern consoles, continued this tradition with the stamina bar, and as a cute twist made it horrible. Now, every action depleted stamina. Rolling, blocking, attacking, and it took forever to replenish. Multiple seconds of doing nothing after depleting it.<\/i><br><br>Now, 2016, Shrouded in Sanity takes this system and may be the worst iteration of it I've seen yet. Replenishment time has been slowed to a crawl - more if you &quot;Exhaust&quot; yourself - and multiple times I found myself landing a parry just to stand there like a lemon and wait for this arbitrary meter to recharge. <br><br>This isn't fun. This has never been fun. The idea that you just walk around after every clash is dumb, it kills the pacing of the fights. Action points, stamina, whatever Transistor uses, it's the dumbest idea I've seen to work itself into action games. We have ways to balance player damage - combos with recovery times, &quot;pushback&quot; on enemies that allow them to retort, these have been around longer than the stamina system. <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">&quot;Do what you were contracted to do, and slay the fog. Get to it&quot;<\/div> - Waltham<br><br>After finishing the game once, you'll have the choices of NG+, which adds a billion more traps, enemy placements, and otherwise alters stats predictably (you'll want to be like Rank 20 before taking it on) and the recent Boss Modes, which are fantastic. <br><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"https:\/\/www.youtube.com\/watch?v=oAUxhHz-LCM\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\" >From the First Knight Len,<\/a><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"https:\/\/www.youtube.com\/watch?v=fCKDvjVzpNQ\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\" >To the Second Knight Gray,<\/a><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"https:\/\/www.youtube.com\/watch?v=bd4uNizKAic\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\" >To the Third Knight Hito,<\/a><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"https:\/\/www.youtube.com\/watch?v=L53gjP-TtGE\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\" >To SARAGAT<\/a><br><br>Well worth it, and they give you more reason to explore the fantastic map. Built around a single checkpoint, the manor and grounds is riddled with shortcuts, multiple routes (allowing you to tackle any major boss whenever you want, even when you think you can't) secret rooms, easter eggs, and lore for the story. <br><br>While it more or less gives the game away almost immediately with enemy upgrade chances and the open-ended nature of the map itself, Shrouded in Sanity reserves a few surprises on both the domestic character motivation level and the cosmic level for the overall story. Some of which require maxing out the sanity meter.<br><br>The best way to do this is to die. Which leads to some hilarious endgame hijinks of barrelling straight into the same enemy's most powerful attack. <br><br>Art is exactly what it looks like. Aliased, binary character art and anti-aliased background art continue to jar to the end of time, although there are a few instances where it's used well-ish. Animations, some poor\/nonexistent telegraphs aside (look at you, basic Hunter slash) are well above what you'd expect for a one-man project.<br><br>Which about sums up Shrouded in Sanity. Well above what you'd expect. I went in expecting a mediocre deriviative product and came out with something that leaked effort from every orifice.<br><br>If you can stomach low production values and the pricetag, this is a game
