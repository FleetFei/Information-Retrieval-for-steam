It won't win any awards for its concept, but it does offer a surprising number of unique ships that play somewhat differently from each other. The result is a rather replayable shooter, if only to test the functions of each ship, which I found enjoyable. <br><br>Gameplay is a standard shmup. Shoot anything that moves, avoid bullets, grab items. The items dropped by enemies are mostly random; it is thus possible to have a bunch of missiles before you ever get a laser upgrade. The game lacks a &quot;focus&quot; mode, where you can slow down your ship to maneuver tight spaces. Thus I found faster ships, which often have less armor, work against me.<br><br>There are 14 ships to choose from in this game. Surprisingly most ships have a good balance about them, though a few stood out as being much better or much worse than the others. The majority of my time spent on this game was comparing each and every ship by fully upgrading them, and finding out which ones I liked best. Every ship possesses a unique &quot;limit break&quot; of sorts. As you grab points, your bar fills up and you can unleash a powerful attack or defensive ability that is based on your current ship. These occur often enough that your ship's special power is worth taking into consideration, especially on endless mode.<br><br>There's a rudimentary plot about a cold war between humans and aliens, and every ship has a bit of backstory, which is cool. On top of that, enemies occasionally drop health items to replenish your life. You can even heal yourself by getting grazed by enemy bullets.
