Originally you could take pretty much any card from either game, translate the keywords (Taunt becomes Screen, Deathrattle becomes Revenge, Spell Power becomes Firepower, Secret becomes Cipher etc etc) and play it in the other with almost no incompatabilities at all. Even some of the heroes were just remixes of Hearthstone ones- the Hajir-Gog are basically Warriors with the Mage's hero power, the Consortium with their random mercenary summon and cards with &quot;credit&quot; (coughOverloadcough) were Shamans etc etc.<br><br>However, as the game developed, it began to distinguish itself from Hearthstone in 2 significant ways. Firstly it began to introduce new core gameplay elements that indicated it was simply using Hearthstone as a <i>base<\/i> and planned on expanding on the core gameplay Blizzard had laid out. Basic &quot;hero powers&quot; were obsoleted with the introduction of the Module and Energy systems, giving each class several times as much tactical depth as Hearthstone heroes. Combined with some seemingly minor gameplay differences that had been there from the start (most notably the option to make your deck up from between 25 and 40 cards, rather than having it fixed at 30, with your deck size setting your life total), Star Crusade began to really stand apart.<br><br>The second difference ties in to how I opened the review, when I described the game as &quot;sci-fi Hearthstone <i>but better.&quot;<\/i> I can quite cheerfully say that Star Crusade is simply a flat-out BETTER GAME than Hearthstone because its developers actually <i>give a crap.<\/i> Blizzard's management of Hearthstone has been absolutely miserable- treating their players like idiots who can't grasp any but the simplest of gameplay mechanics, an agonising overreliance on total uncontrolled RNG as a substitute for proper card design, a refusal to make any meaningful developments to the game's design, and a miserly approach to balancing that sees them do at most ONE round of brutal, heavy-handed nerfs <i>per year<\/i> and NEVER buff anything, ever (after all, why would they ever want to increase the value of any cards you already own?). The devs of Star Crusade, on the other hand actually <i>care<\/i> about making the game a competitive CCG. There is a MASSIVELY reduced RNG element in the game (nothing like a Doomsayer popping out of a Piloted Shredder and killing everything or your own Yogg-Saron killing you outright from full HP), they constantly keep a close eye on the game's balance and are willing to change cards to deal with significant meta issues rather than just &quot;we'll balance it out with the cards we include in the next set release&quot;, and they're willing to both nerf overpowered cards <i>and<\/i> buff useless ones. Even the little things put it ahead of Hearthstone (you get 6 cards per booster rather than 5; dailies all give a fixed amount of credits -30 each, with boosters still costing 100- but you get 2 per day instead of 1; the Raid system has much more depth than Heartstone's Arena system, with you being given the option to aim for different prizes on an adventure map rather than just challenging the next opponent; ranked season rewards are FAR more generous than Hearthstone's etc etc).<br><br>It may strike you as odd that this review has apparently turned into &quot;let's all rag on Hearthstone&quot;, but if you think about it, that's completely relevant. Hearthstone, despite its HUGE amount of flaws, is the #1 digital CCG in the world- and Star Crusade is basically the same game but objectively better, so shouldn't <i>it<\/i> be the #1 digital CCG in the world? For what it's worth, I DON'T think Star Crusade is the best digital CCG on the market at the moment (that's either Infinity Wars or Spellweaver), nor that it should be #1, but it certainly deserves it more than Hearthstone does. The two games are basically directly competing with each other by offering the same kind of simplified, easy-access CCG gameplay, but Star Crusade has far more depth and variety, despite being much younger and having a much smaller card base. So basically if you liked Hearthstone (OTHER than just for the Warcraft setting and fancy window dressing) you'll <i>love<\/i> this game. If you didn't really like Hearthstone, maybe Star Crusade will be able to win you over where Blizzard's game failed. Now that it's finally out of early access and still improving (they finally added hero voices recently) it's the best time to hop on board.
