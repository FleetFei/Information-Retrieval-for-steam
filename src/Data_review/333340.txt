class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStar Rangers is a surprisingly interesting game.<br>Space exploration games are slowly demanding the spotlight, and this title is successfully shaping its path to success.<br>The game is still in a very Early Access stage, with many features and details to be added and fixed (which have been planned by the devs). Due to its current status I am unabe to properly label the negative aspects as such, hence they will be listed at the bottom of the review as a mere feedback and advice.<br><br>You start off by creating a character, modifying his appearance and picking a class, which will give you a bonus on several stats, along with unique skills and perks.<br>There are various missions available, accessible from a text based computer (you actually have to type the commands, a little feature that I found brilliant in terms of involvement), and they range from space exploration ones (planet discovery, debris cleaening etc) to planet related ones (build a particular structure, collect materials etc).<br><br>The game is vast, hugely vast. You have the chance to explore your favourite solar system planet and much more. In your aid there are different vehicles with their own particular function.<br>In my current game experience I only visited Mars so far (I've always been fascinated by the Red Planet). Reaching it was a wonderful experience and the landing process is brilliant, with speed balancing being a vital factor to properly reach the planet without destroying your ship.<br><br>Without further ado, I would like to talk about the exteremly positive aspects of Star Rangers, which also are the reason why you should definitely give it a try:<br><br><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li><b>Space Immersion:<\/b> who wouldn't love to explore the planets we could only see in books or tv channels? Star Rangers offers this incredible opportunity! While the majority of space exploration games are based on fictional planets and galaxy, this one takes place in a realistic universe.<br><\/li><li><b>Survival:<\/b> I am fond of survival games, I like the mechanism and the thrills that such titles offer to the player. You normally have to survive in an island, a forest, a city, or in any other place on planet Eart. Imagine how could it be to survive in another planet? You can build structures, inflate pre-made ones, gather materials, explore, but always remember to have a shelter nearby because oxygen and suit stability can be replenished by staying inside your base.<br>Every electronic apparatus needs to be powered with a battery (which has its own durability), making every decision important, as they aren't that common to have.<br><\/li><li><b>Updates:<\/b> as for every Early Access game I review, I put the stress on the updates, which are the reason why a title is labeled like that. The devs do actually listen to feedbacks and they constantly keep the game updated.<br><\/li><li><b>PC Requirements:<\/b> my PC isn't an high-end one, thus I feared I couldn't run a game of this calibre. I could easily set the maximum graphic options without having an issue. Yes, we aren't talking about extremely realistic texturing, and visually speaking it may not look like a 2016 title, but it still looks great! <\/li><\/ul><br>In the end, I would like to point out a few aspect that many need an optimization (which I am sure will be fixed as I have read them being planned in the future).<br><br><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li><b>Tutorial:<\/b> the in-game tutorial currently still feels a bit incomplete as it looks like a real campaign without a real lead to follow. It's not a great issue though, as the missions accessible from the computer clearly shows what to do. What it lacks is a proper control scheme tutorial.<br><\/li><li><b>Voice Acting:<\/b> even though it's just a minor thing, I think it may need a revamp, since it is a game about immersion (and trust me, it's very immersive). <\/li><\/ul><br><br>Am I recommending the game?
