with. There is a truckload of good and bad about it, just within the boundaries of a perfect balance. Considering it is the first ever game of the developer, I think congratulations are in order. Aside the questionable humor, game dynamic is more than decent for an adventure game.<br><br>It all begins with probably the most disgusting and pathetic excuse of a human being with an IQ level below repair: our protagonist, Bjorn Thonen. He is a genuine slob who enjoys eating food off the floor and lives by the extent of toilet humor every second of his life. He is rude, immoral and perverted. Oh, and he is an antique dealer that collects random junk hoping to sell them at excruciating rates to some tourists in Paris. There is literally nothing to make Bjorn sympathetic for us - alongside our other starring characters: the exaggerated bimbo next door neighbor Sandra and her insufferable + evil daughter Caroline. For being out loud, I think the primary negative about the game is the lack of either sympathetic or relatable characters.<br><br>One night, Bjorn hits home dead drunk and receives a strange phone call in the middle of the night, warning him about a grave danger approaching. Following the warning, he gets knocked out by an intruder in his own home and later discovers that he's been robbed. Beginning his investigation with little to nothing at hand, Bjorn will fast find himself in an adventure that one should indeed be cynical about hearing. Through a series of blatantly idiotic encounters - and a surprisingly well-written background story - we'll take on a journey only probably to define as a European version of Indiana Jones crosses over the worst American Pie movie ever.<br><br>As you can so far guess, the humor of the game is crude and downright obnoxious with sexual remarks, toilet humor and random absurdities. Thus, it misses the point of being funny quite often and ending up being simply gross. With that warning in mind, if you are to endure the humor you'll soon discover a genuinely interesting story with naively creative elements and a collection of brain scratching puzzles. The second half of the game, where Bjorn will take a journey abroad to continue his investigation was way more interesting both in dynamic and conceptual humor.<br><br>Contrary to your usual P&amp;C adventure, we have a first person camera and a static environment that can be investigated very much like a hidden object game. Aside that, you'll gather items, use them in proper places, converse with NPCs. etc. All the expected. There is an interesting hint system in the game: you'll be collecting cookies in each scene - very similar to a hidden object game - and later eat those cookies to get a hint about what to do next. Also, you are free to discover countless obscure ways to kill Bjorn through &quot;not so wise&quot; story and environment choices - which will be a mandatory stress relief for you. They are mostly a dedication to Bjorn's horrendous IQ, thus his low survival rate anyhow.<br><br>A game time of 12-15 hours are to be expected which is more than decent for a game in this genre. Aside the narrative tone and insufferable characters, the game dynamic was surprisingly interesting and entertaining. I've been informed that the developer came with the idea of working on a &quot;toilet humor filter&quot; that will present an alternative narrative mode for the game which will be included in a patch coming this summer and will possibly make a cleaner gaming experience on the humor department.
