for a lot of reasons. There is just so much it does right and so much it does wrong that I can't really say without a doubt &quot;this is a good game&quot; or &quot;this is a bad game&quot; without feeling like I'm not doing it justice.<br><br>At it's core, Evolve is an interesting concept. One powerful player versus many smaller but more agile hunters. A battle between a bear and a wolf pack essentially. Seems fun and like it could go either way, but in practice its less so.<br><br>When the game is at its best is when everybody involved is around the same skill level. When the monster is just strong enough to seriously harm your team, but to not entirely beat you. When the team is just skilled enough to put the hurt on the monster, but he is slippery enough to get away. During these matches the game absolutely shines. Every player is important. Every move the monster makes has him making calculated dashes and wondering if its worth it to stop for food with the hunters hot on his heels. Its intense, visceral, and feels great no matter who wins or what side you are playing as.<br><br>Unfortunately that isn't always the case. The game's matchmaking isn't perfect and unless you have a 5 friends who all play at the same time you aren't all going to be on the same skill level. This can be absolutely catastrophic. If you are veteran hunters and you get a newbie medic on your team, you are 100% dead. Nothing you do can change that. The monster will eat your face. On the flip side if you are newbie hunters and the monster is a veteran, you'll probably be killed on his second form before the game really gets underway without you knowing what you exactly did wrong.<br><br>The game's fun lies solely when you hit that sweet spot of everybody being at the same level of play. Too good of a monster and the hunters get frustratingly curb stomped while the player being the monster is bored out of his mind. Hunters that are too good become like exterminators rather than hunters. They just go put the monster down and move on to the next job because its boringly easy.<br><br>This is where the game fails, hard. Matchmaking without a full group often lands you in the curb stomp or be curb stomped spectrum because random players just can't be accounted for. Its frustrating, and I didn't have more than 2 friends I played with so the odds were against us. We would either completely annihilate the monster before he could even get to his second form or the monster would be so ludicrously good he would wipe us out minutes after the game started. The worst part is that when you do find those excellent matches where everything works out excellently, the game doesn't have a rematch option so unless you quickly add everyone involved into a group you'll probably never see them again.<br><br>Even with all its flaws I'd say give it a try. Its free, and when you do find those perfect matches, theres really nothing else out there that gives you quite the same feeling as Evolve. Just try to find 5 friends to bring with you if you can.
