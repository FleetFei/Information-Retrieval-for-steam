have the most glamorous hopes and dreams or the desired budget to make a blockbuster game that otherwise wouldn\u2019t be possible. Meet Unterrainer Informatik OG a small but experienced team of three software developers led by Psilo, the company\u2019s lead programmer, software engineer. They have a well developed philosophy that in today\u2019s age of sandbox games and user generated content that there is still time for old fashioned games. They believe in the fundamentals of gaming that are being thrown out of the window in favor of glitz and glamour. Those fundamentals being that a \u2018game, above all other things, needs to be based on a good idea and needs an interesting story. I agree with this appraisal. This is of vital importance because if you are lacking in these things then it is impossible to create something that people want to play for an extended period of time. Even in today\u2019s pick up and throw away world you still need these fundamental things.<br><br>So what exactly is Throbax TD? That\u2019s an easy one to answer. It\u2019s a tower defense game in which you, the player, have to survive waves of enemies otherwise your planet is destroyed and thus the game over. But it\u2019s not your standard tower defense game. That would be too simple and although the production isn\u2019t glamorous what it does offer is something fresh and challenging. As the Steam description aptly states there are two phases of gameplay involved. The first phase is an RTS where you use your strategic thinking in building your defense systems in order to avoid the incoming onslaught. The game then transitions into what would be considered a typical tower defense game. These two phases work wonderfully well together. You can clearly see where the developers draw their inspiration for the game from \u2013 which they unabashedly state is the tower-defense map in Frozen Throne, one of the most popular maps in gaming history. They have big shoes to fill if they intend on living up to their aspirations but thus far the development choices have been intelligent.<br><br>Arguably the most fascinating aspect of the project is that there is multiplayer. Unfortunately, in my time with the game before going to print I never got involved in that component of the game. However, people that I have discussed the game with me advise that the current status of the multiplayer is weak but there is more than sufficient grounds to believe that the multiplayer as it is developed will likely become the lifeblood of the game.<br><br>The graphics and sound are not going to blow you away. The team have concentrated on giving the best playing experience possible. The team have not offered any of the bells and whistles that you have come to expect from modern gaming. This would work against everything that the team stands for. If you\u2019re looking for your smoke and mirrors then you\u2019re best to look away.<br><br>Throbax TD is definitely a throw back to an older age in gaming but it\u2019s a delightful visit and one that is highly recommended.
