unique and risky RPGs yet.  Made in a style similar Knights of the Old Republic, Jade Empire is, at its core, a Bioware RPG.  If you've played them before, you should know the formula.  Tutorial, minor thing goes wrong, first mission, even worse thing happens, and then things open  up where you're given multiple objectives at once and it's up to you to decide what order to do things in.  There's a slew of side quests and different party members to drive your progress forward as you make your way from locale to locale to stop the bad guys (TM).<br><br>In spite of following the basic Bioware formula, there is almost nothing like Jade Empire out there.  It's closer to Mass Effect than KOTOR in that the combat is more active and there's not an abundance of stats.  You level up your character by putting points into health, mana or &quot;focus&quot;, which is spent to either slow down time or use martial weapons.  Fighting styles level up seperately and are generally made stronger, faster, or cheaper resource-wise.  You can swap styles mid-combat, giving you a number of ways to approach any given combat scenario, though in a realistic scenario you're likely to only have 5 to 6 fully leveled up.  Party members aren't too useful in fights, but you can set them to a passive mode which provides you with buffs or to regenerate one of your stats.  As a result, there's likely one character you'll use most of the game, since you can spend mana to regenerate health as well to fuel your magic powers.<br><br>Jade Empire's strength comes from its environment and story.  There are relatively few games that exist in an Eastern Asian setting.  It also has one of the most intriguing villians and pulls off a twist almost as good as the first KOTOR.  There are certainly a few flaws in story telling however, the most obvious being the karma system.  Rather than follow a strict good vs evil system, it's more of an order vs chaos meter.  In practice though, most &quot;chaotic&quot; choices fall under dumb choices that are nonsensical and hurt people for no reason, which is unfortunate since the main villian definitely follows the &quot;order&quot; school of thought but is most certainly a bad guy.  With this karma system poorly thought out, it typically just devolves into good vs bad, and if you're like me, a bad ending just feels like a non-standard game over and tend to be avoided.<br><br>Overall, I recommend Jade Empire if you like Bioware's formula, but want to try a different setting.  Expect a lot of interaction with NPCs and a slow start, but there's a lot to enjoy.
