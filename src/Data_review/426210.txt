2017<br><b>Type:<\/b>  Singleplayer &amp; Multiplayer<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">General Impression<\/div>Dragon\u2019s Games is a developer that\u2019s striving to improve themselves and the quality of their games. Mad Nords: Probably an Epic Quest\u2019s a far cry from their now second Steam project, Tyran. From a  fairly generic 2D title built in an RPG Maker lookalike engine, they have now went all in and managed to create a decent 3D competitive game through Unity 5 assets. A success story, if you ask me.<br><br>But there\u2019s always a pesky fly in the ointment, right? I know I can be pretentious at times and that I put the singleplayer experience above all else. Frankly speaking, you shouldn\u2019t buy Tyran for its SP even when it shall be polished and ready to be fired up. At its core, this will always be a multiplayer-centric game and I\u2019m perfectly fine with that. This isn\u2019t even a case of partially misleading advertising since the dev team states very clearly, the exact contents of their Early Access stage. The problem stems from the fact that it\u2019s not that much to play for now and it gets boring pretty fast when you can only spar with the AI or with a buddy on just 3 maps. And those aren\u2019t even that big to compensate at least in a vast open space perhaps. At least the Artificial Intelligence can put up a fight well enough. Game may lack in current content but definitely not in scope or challenge.<br><br>It shows promise beyond just beautiful effects and graphics. There is lots of potential from a campaign perspective once it gets sprinkled with lore and a convincing cast of characters, which players might relate to. For now though, you\u2019ll have to contend with an almost arena-sized fantasy brawler that draws at least some of its inspiration from Dota 2. Tyran still manages to be a competent role playing game with real time strategy elements sewn into its gameplay. The RPG side is dominant while the strategic aspect comes from the ability to summon and control several auxiliary troops which shall ease the burden off your main avatar. Consider them more like \u201cmeat shields\u201d than actually aides in combat, yet you will find them crucial to your victory against seemingly insurmountable odds.<br><br>At the very least, those few maps already made available have scenic diversity and the presence of exotic wildlife to farm for XP. The magical and general skill progression is nothing new on the horizon, but it still conveys purpose to your constant in-game grinding. I\u2019m certain that Tyran shall prove even more challenging once you can compete against skilled human players since the AI has its obvious limits. For now you may only play a small portion of what Tyran wished to exibit, but it won\u2019t be a dull playthrough if you decide to pick it up now instead of waiting several more months. You have Cards to it and I can only hope Steam Achievements won\u2019t make us wait much longer for their inclusion.<br><br>The reasons for which I\u2019m willing to recommend this game, are related to its creative use of the Unity Engine and the fact that it\u2019s an honest product as far its price tag\u2019s concerned. You get what you pay for and that is a feature seldom found in Early Access titles of the past two years. Dragon\u2019s Games has a lot of work ahead and I expect them to deliver on the many promises which Tyran made to its current playerbase and future customers.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Strong Points<\/div>+ Decently priced for an Early Access.<br>+ Beautiful graphics and sounds.<br>+ Steam Trading Cards.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Weak Points<\/div>- No SP campaign or lore, present in
