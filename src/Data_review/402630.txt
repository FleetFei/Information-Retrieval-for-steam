Gist of the Story <\/div><br>The Coma tells the tale of Youngho, an average high school student with normal worries such as controlling his raging hormones and stressful cramming for exams because of procrastination. His seemingly tame life takes a turn for the worse after discovering a strange pendant that belongs to his favorite teacher, Ms. Song. Unbeknownst to him, dark forces are at play, and soon during mid-exam, Youngho faints. <br><br>As soon as he wakes up, he finds himself in a twisted alternate version of his school with grotesque figures of his classmates and teacher roaming around its halls. Puzzled by the sudden transformation of his school, he wanders around until he meets up with Yaesol, an aloof young woman with a penchant for secrets. She knows something but isn\u2019t straightforward with Youngho. Yaesol advices him to keep a cool head and that he should begin retracing his steps. It is the only way he will escape \u201cThe Coma.\u201d With nothing else to do, he follows her guidance and realizes the cause of all this is closer than he thinks.<br><br>---<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"> Gameplay <\/div><br>All Youngho can do is run and hide. If he brings attention to himself, all he must do is run around long enough until Psycho Ms. Song (The Coma\u2019s demonic version of his teacher) stops chasing you. You can actually get her to chase you for an indefinite amount of time if you keep circling around the two exits of a classroom (it\u2019s funny watching her go!) I found it easier to maneuver around the school when she\u2019s right behind you because most of the time, she\u2019s camping around the spot you need to go to (As long as you keep track of her whereabouts, you can let her chase you, otherwise, you might accidently bump into her.) It can be challenging at times, especially when Song doesn\u2019t leave her spot. At this point, it\u2019s just the waiting game. Listening for her footsteps and growling are her audio cues and wearing headphones will let you know which direction she is coming from. Her chasing segments become repetitive and can disrupt the tension of the game.<br><br>There is an inventory system and thankfully there is a separate slot for key items and consumables. Unfortunately (or maybe fortunately for you), Youngho has a limit to how much he can carry and I swear, I don\u2019t know how many times I\u2019ve picked up water (it recovers stamina), but it\u2019s too many to count. I never needed to drink water because the stamina bar runs out very slowly and recovers as soon as you stop running. By the time Song stops her furious chase after Youngho, the stamina bar has already replenished. <br><br>Debilitating conditions like bleeding and poison is present but I found myself unconcerned by them. Bandages and antidotes are found in the world enough that you may never need to buy them at the store (there is a vending machine to buy all your curative needs). Really, they don\u2019t do much damage and in fact, I played the entire final round of the game poisoned and nothing really happened. The only times I become overwhelmed by my worry is when I run out of healing foods because they\u2019re rare. Unless I just have terrible luck, I almost always bolt towards the cafeteria (the safe zone) and buy a hamburger or chips to keep Youngho all fattened up.<br> <br>There is also manual saving, in the form of writing your name on a chalkboard. No auto-saving. NOPE!<br><br>---<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"> Did ya scare me? <\/div><br>The scare factor of the game, on the scale of 1-10 (1 being, \u201cme looking for something to scare me but couldn\u2019t find it, no matter how hard I tried\u201d and 10 being, \u201cI \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665 my pants, I should\u2019ve worn a diaper\u201d), I rate The Coma a 4, \u201cthe game tries.\u201d<br><br>I have to admit, it is unsettling during the beginning when Song is first introduced but after numerous chase scenes of her aggressively swinging her knife at you, you become desensitized of her. It becomes tedious and this is my main gripe about the game. It\u2019s easy enough to avoid her since she always loses interest in Youngho when he hides in a closet or bathroom stall. <br><br>Although the enemy itself isn\u2019t frightening, the sights and sounds immerses you into this dark, brooding world filled with hostile apparitions waiting to swat at you, hanging corpses waiting to crush you and soft moans and groans waiting to deceive you. I enjoyed the setting of the game and it is small reminder of games like \u201cWhite Day.\u201d <br><br>---<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"> Graphics and Technical Issues <\/div><br>The graphics of the game is pleasant. It has an anime-style look to it. There isn\u2019t much in terms of graphic options (it\u2019s on the Unity Engine, by the way) and the game seems to be locked at 50 fps. I don\u2019t know why. I\u2019ve just accepted it. I\u2019ve seen it drop to 40 at times but the drops are hardly noticeable (I have a fps counter at all times). For perspective, my specs are specs are GTX 970, i7-4790S, 12 GB of ram\u2026so on and so forth.<br><br>I had several crashes. Additionally, I discovered some bugs like the screen turning black but I can still hear the in-game sounds and see the HUD. I had a glitch where Youngho would go to a room, exit and then when he tries to exit\/enter other rooms, he teleports back to original room he came out of. For the most part, restarting the game solved these problems. I\u2019m not saying it\u2019s a glitchy game but these are the ones that would conveniently manifest from time to time when I haven\u2019t saved in awhile. Oh, my poor. poor mouse. How abused you must have felt.<br><br>---<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"> My Observations <\/div><br>I like the story. It is told through interactions with several characters and notes scattered in the world. It\u2019s important to read the notes to understand the minds of the staff and students of Sehwa High School. It ties to the plot of the story. The characters are quirky, especially Youngho himself, with his smooth talking (\/sarcasm) and his amusing side comments (\/notsarcasm). <br><br>It\u2019s a shame that running away and hiding from Song got tedious in the long run. I had to soldier through the game and at one point, I had to stop because it got very aggravating.<br><br>I believe the game might have two endings (?). I will have to play it a second time. I think I left something undone, so you can miss certain events if you\u2019re not careful. I will update as soon as I finish my second run. <br><br>Lastly, game time isn\u2019t too long. I completed the game at around 6 hours and that\u2019s only because most of the time, I\u2019m hiding and avoiding that stupid hobag Song\u2026yes\u2026hobag! Go ahead Steam! Bleep me out!<br><br>---<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"> Wrap Up <\/div><br>Even though I enjoyed the story, some of the game mechanics and bugs that were mentioned really hold me back from fully recommending it at full price.
