class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere's a lot to like about Racket:NX.  The graphics are outstanding, the mechanics and physics of hitting the ball are superior to other games I've played.  It's a lot of fun and gets the blood pumping.  <br><br>Unfortunately there are more things that I disliked about the game than things I did.  For starters, there's almost nothing that tells you what you need to do to play.  I know, I know. Hit ball with racket, right?  Except there are the times and how fast you hit the ball determines a lot of things.  The learning curve is steep, and it took playing through a few times to understand what I was even supposed to be doing.  <br><br>Then there's the portals.  The devs seem to REALLY love those portals.  Unfortunately they destroy the flow of the game play.  &quot;Oh here! You have to hit this tile.  Let me surround it with portals that spit the ball back out from another location!&quot;  As the difficulty increases, the game uses the portals to an absurd degree.  Which, maybe some people like, but it leads to the next issue I have.<br><br>The game favors very slow accurate shots rather than allowing you to really get the ball going around the court.  Because of the aforementioned portals, it becomes nearly impossible later in the game to play fast.  Instead, hitting with any sort of accuracy to avoid the portals means taking single, slow shots, which becomes boring.  I get that the portals are meant to make the game more challenging, but instead they just make it more frustrating by forcing you to play a specific way.  <br><br>I'm not sure whether to recommend this or not.  I'm just not sure it's worth $20 at this point.  I know it's early access, but honestly I think I'd recommend waiting until they've made some changes.<br><br>***Update to Review***<br><br>Because of the passion with which the Devs, who reached out to me after I posted my review, and the Racket:NX community have shown, I continued playing and have 12 hours of gameplay in at this point.  One of the developers suggested that it would take 12 to 18 hours just to master the basics.  Unfortunately, further time playing hasn't changed my mind. And, in fact, only reinforces my belief that the learning curve and increase in difficulty are simply too steep for the majority of players.  While I enjoy a challenge and find many aspects of this game enjoyable, I believe the 12-18 hours required to simply pick up the basics is more than most casual players will want to invest.  I think this game absolutely will appeal to those who want to spend 4-5 hours a day practicing to get to the necessary skill level, but for someone like me who just wants to play a game a couple of times a day as a way to break up my work, it's simply frustrating. As it is an Early Access game, I'll check in with it periodically to see if any changes have been made.<br><br>***Second Update***<br><br>I'm changing my recommendation from No to Yes because of the most recent update which improved the physics and, more importantly, added a Zen mode which allows new players to practice without the timer.
