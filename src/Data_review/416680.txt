Second, Zombie Night Terror outshines Lemmings in its horde management. For instance, stairs are already built (it is not Zombie Minecraft!) and the player can simply choose in which direction to take them. But the real improvement comes from a brilliant concept: the classical \u201cblocker\u201d that forces the horde to move in a specific direction can be given additional powers that will translate to the Zombies that the \u201cblocker\u201d (or Overlord here) manages. In other words: the Overlord can make the whole Zombie army jump to the next building, not only a few chosen Zombies. Sometimes a bit hard to figure out (often through trial and error - maybe the possibility to save the game would have alleviated that aspect). Difficult yet nothing impossible, so succeeding is very gratifying. Just that some (thankfully rare) levels have a timer and can be frustrating. So it\u2019s really good to be able to pause the game to give orders. On top of that, understanding the different powers very well is required to progress through the game, for most levels it is not possible to just wing it on the first try. Additionally, the game mechanics allow for some really interesting tricks that are hinted at in the achievements or optional challenges.<br><br>Another difference with Lemmings is that some levels are more focused on micro-managing one or a few Zombies, making use of the \u201ctemporary powers\u201d to navigate the levels as if it was a turn-based platformer, and take the upper hand in gunfights. Those pesky humans dare oppose the Zombie apocalypse with dangerous guns and rifles! These tactical levels are also very difficult and remind me a lot of how I felt when playing the commando missions in Command &amp; Conquer and Red Alert. Very action-packed and yet very tactical. And then the next level goes back to a nice massive bloodbath so that the game is not repetitive.<br><br>The graphics are really cute, a nice horror movie feel with black &amp; white colors but for the bloody red. The animations make the pixel-art work really well. Add to that really funny lines of dialog, whether during cutscenes or random chatter in the levels, and you have a very cool package.<br><br>In conclusion, Zombie Night Terror expands nicely on the concept of Lemmings, with great concepts and good execution.
