Overwatch. Well, when you actually play the game it's totally different. It's a shooter game with cards system and in-game items where 2 5-players teams fight for a sweet victory.<br>Four modes actually exists:<br><br>- Siege - one capture point, almost symetrical map. Being played until either team reach 4 points. First objective is to capture the point (NOTE: number of players from one team standing on point doesn't make capturing faster). Team which will do it, earn 1 point. After capturing, during the same round, the same team has to push spawned payload to the checkpoint (which is close to enemy spawn point), they have about ~2:30 to do this. If succeded, team earns another point, when failed - enemy team earns 1 point (until they have 3 points already, then they earn nothing). It's possible to run into overtime during a round: in capture phase - by standing on point with opposing team, when any of team bars shows 99%; in pushing phase - by pushing\/standing near payload when timer reaches 0.<br><br>- Payload - One team is Defenders, while other is Pushers. Pushers have to push the payload all the way from starting point (near their spawn point) to capture point (near Defenders spawn point). Their progress is shown by a % of distance that payload was pushed. They have 8 minutes to do it. Round ends when Pushers will make it to the end or timer reaches 0. Then second round begins, and teams sides are switched (Defenders are now Pushers, and vice versa). Winner is determined, who did pushed payload to the end faster (when both teams had 100%) or further (beating other team by 1% during 2nd round ends the match automaticly). No overtimes.<br><br>- Survival - just team deathmatch with no respawns during the rounds. Like in Siege mode: first to 4 points wins.<br><br>- Coop vs AI - Special mode where &quot;rules&quot; are being changed every few weeks.<br><br>There is also another queue, but dedicated for testers who would like to test upcomming maps to the game.<br><br>Champions are categorized in: Front line, Support, Damage, Flank. Each has unique playstyle and different role.<br><br>Every champion can be customised (from sprays, through MVP Poses and Voices to outfit).<br><br>Each champion has 3 legendary cards (1 is available by default, other 2 can be bought by Essences) and a basic loadout called: Basic [insert champion name here] which is pretty good and universal. You can have in total 6 loadouts, each contains 5 cards (selected from 20-cards deck) where each card can have different levels (from 1 to 4). Each card has rarity which determines its price in Essences. You have to buy a card before you can use it in your loadouts. NOTE: each loadout has to have exactly 12 loadout points (these are being calculated by summing up used cards levels, f.e.: 1+4+3+2+2).<br><br>Before every match in any game mode you have to make a selection, which legendary card and loadout you would like to use. As soon as match didn't started, you can change your selection by pressing U.<br><br>In-game shop offers 16 items in total in 4 categories: Defence, Utility, Healing, Offense. These items can be bought using Credits that you earn during the match passively, eliminating enemies, capturing objectives and by doing your champions role specific things (f.e. when playing as Damage you will be rewarded for doing high amount of damage in short period of time). You can only buy 4 items max, but every item has to be from different category, so you can't have f.e. 2 Defense items. Each item can be upgraded to max 3rd level.<br><br>Overall, it's a great game where shooting is important, but not the most one.
