class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is my last review of this game. I took down previous reviews because they were mostly written out of anger towards many of the (at times baseless) negative reviews and forum trolls. I'll try to keep it more objective, as a traditional review should be. This will simply be my honest review of the game, from the viewpoint of a long time tactical shooter fan, NOT a Resident Evil fan.<br><br>Umbrella Corps is a thinking person's shooter (think SOCOM, not CoD); you win by outsmarting your enemy, not by skill alone. There are two main gametypes; One Life Match, which is essentially single-elimination deathmatch, and Multi-Mission. Multi-Mission allows you to respawn and plays first team to win 3 rounds of constantly changing objectives (ie. Round 1 is simple TDM, Round 2 you have to find a special zombie and kill it before the other team, Round 3 etc. etc.) At first you might get away with simply running all over the map looking for kills, but the more you play the more you come to appreciate the freedom with which you have to approach your objective. Many of the walls and buildings can be scaled by the player with the use of your climbing gear, adding another level of verticality to all of the maps that ensures no two rounds play out exactly the same, even on the relatively small maps. Rather than take the staircase you know the enemy is watching, simply scale the banister and come up behind them. Rather than swing open the door that you know the enemy is waiting behind, you can fluidly open it while rifle-checking at the same time. When climbing ladders, you can make a quick sweep with your weapon before finishing the climb. The game even comes with a wheel of customizable pre-made strats to set up with your team at the beginning of each match (though whether or not your team actually follows the strategy is another story :P).<br><br>There's a fairly decent amount of primary and secondary weapons, though later unlocks simply allow different playstyles and aren't necessarily better than the weapons everyone starts out with, keeping it balanced. There is a relatively robust selection of grenades, from normal frags to smokes for advanced strategies, semtex and even grenades that disable and eventually destroy any zombie jammers in the area, leaving the enemy vulnerable to zombie attacks. Pistols are more fun to use for me in this game than in any other multiplayer third-person shooter I've played, and they are just as formiddable as any of the primary weapons in the hands of someone with good aim and quick clicking. There are NO sniper rifles; this game has a strong focus on CQB (close quarters battle) and as such the weapons featured are either PDWs (personal defense weapons, sort of like submachine guns), pistols, and shotguns. The weapons are all modeled on real world guns with (what I'm assuming are) Resident Evil universe based names. The only aspect I found lacking were the brainers. The brainer is the melee weapon every character is given that lets you run faster and get insta-kills if you can sneak up behind them, because otherwise there is a good chance you will get brainer-countered and killed. By holding right click (or right trigger) while using the brainer you can heat it up, making it impossible to be countered but completely eliminating any element of stealth by letting out a distinct powering up noise for everyone to hear and prepare for. My issue with the brainer is there are only 3 types available in the game, which is a stark contrast to the other equipment selections. They each have different characteristics, taking longer or shorter to heat up and staying heated for longer or shorter, so that may be why the selection is so small, but it would have been nice if they had added other effects as well.<br><br>The zombies roaming throughout the map actually add a tactical aspect; it isn't like Turok for the 360 where the AI was more of a nuisance than anything. You can distract the enemy team by shooting their zombie jammer and suddenly forcing them to deal with the hordes of zombies that were ignoring them before. You can grab attacking zombies and use them as a temporary shield (though they will eat you if you hold it for longer than a few seconds). They can be used as concealmeant, allowing you to blend in with the horde while your jammer is still active (for this reason, you'll find a lot of people decide it's easier to crawl everywhere so it's always good to keep your eyes both high and low), and in some gametypes they are part of the objective itself. I believe the zombie AI concept was overall executed well, if not for a lack of variety in enemy types (walkers, crawlers, dogs, birds are the 4 types you'll see in each level). <br><br>Customization was one of the biggest draws of this game to me, in all honesty. There aren't enough shooters that allow you to customize your character in my opinion, and this one gives you a good amount of options from the start. The weapon customization is also fairly deep, letting you change the paint scheme, optics, ironsights, and suppressors, and even aiming reticule of all the weapons that allow attachments. If you are like me and are a sucker for cool looking tactical \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665, you could probably dump a good amount of time in the customization options alone.<br><br>Now, on to the bad. This game is far from perfect. Oddly, you can't rebind certain staple FPS keys (such as CTRL) and are stuck using the spacebar to crouch\/prone (though you get used to it after a while). The playerbase is nearly dead, although there is a dedicated group of around 100+ players who have created steam matchmaking groups to help find players. Even with this, it can sometimes take 30+ minutes to find one match (You may think differently, but in my opinion no one would be willing to wait that long for a maximum 12 minute match unless it was worth it). As one could expect of a game who's player base consists of a small bunch of people spread all over the world, sometimes the lag is unplayable(but not all or even most of the time). Why was this game dead on release? Well, it honestly depends on who you ask. I personally feel that if this game had be released in its own universe with zombies and special forces instead of Resident Evil's it would have done much better. Resident Evil fans and tactical shooter fans can be two completely different groups of fans. This game is a tactical shooter, dressed up as Resident Evil and marketed to Resident Evil fans; it was a recipe for disaster. However, that doesn't mean the core gameplay is bad or broken, certainly not deserving to be removed from steam. For half the price of a full-priced game, I have put more hours into this than many of the $60 multiplayer games I own (*cough* Battleborn). And I will be honest, I refunded the game when it first came out because, at the time, the lag truly was unplayable. After seeing it go through several updates, I caved and re-purchased it. EDIT: However, the development team has been silent since last August and this game may fairly be considered 'abandoned'.<br><br>tl;dr I recommend Umbrella Corps to fans of tactical shooters who want to play a game with a bit of a SOCOM-esque feel with a zombie twist. Don't buy this just because you're a Resident Evil fan, as it isn't a Resident Evil game (Resident Evil is nowhere in the title).
