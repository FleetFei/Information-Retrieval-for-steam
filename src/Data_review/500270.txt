Together with a group of forgettable NPCs, Walker traverses sewers and metro lines to find and join up with the resistance undetected.<br><br>In the ways of gameplay, VoF is linear and somewhat barebones compared to the main game; the setting makes for a dark and claustrophobic journey, and a limited supply of weapons and attachments (only a fragment of the arsenal available to you later on) forces the player to turn over every stone he comes across. Still, both Rambo-style assaults and stealth are viable options as the level design has allowed for numerous ways to approach enemy encampments and setpieces.<br><br>Compared to the main game, VoF has a little bit more in the way of enemy variety with a rival faction showing up in addition to the Korean army, but lacks weapon variety and length; the regular campaign will take upwards of ten hours for sure, where VoF will take two hours at most (if players take the time to look around). Nonetheless, it switches things up (usually in a good way), and has some narrative relevance to it. And what about dem Metro vibes, man?! Still, you should definitely not buy this at full price; you get way too little for your 6 Dollars\/Euros\/whatever.
