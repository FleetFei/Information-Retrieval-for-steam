game, but I think it\u2019s better than a lot of the RPG Maker junk cluttering Steam, though it\u2019s also not as good as a large number of freely available RPG Maker games out there. I didn\u2019t mind finishing it, but I loathed some parts of the game (core hunting, specifically). There\u2019s also a new game + mode, apparently, and I\u2019m not too thrilled about the prospects there. If you want a TLDR, you just read it - if you want to know more, I\u2019ll start by discussing what I felt was poorly done in the game, then finish on the positive notes. <br>(Also, item farming spoiler below.)<br><br>The first real issue that I want to bring up is that the plot makes itself scarce. The game sort of hinges on being a simulation of an open world where the main character has no idea what\u2019s going on (in a style similar to .hack and its game series), but that makes for very little character or story development. I\u2019m pretty sure most of the game\u2019s story-related dialogue would fit on 3-5 pages, depending on your font choice. It really reminded me of playing .hack on the PS2 because you really don\u2019t get much back-story\u2026 but .hack had the breadth of the manga and anime to draw from, where this game sort of leaves you in the dark because you know nothing about the world going in.<br><br>Another flaw, or host of flaws, is the lack of polish that the game has in certain areas. To be fair, there was actually a massive graphics overhaul done on this game and it did make it much easier on the eyes\u2026 but it made it the spots in the game that were missed in the respriting effort and the quirks caused by bad QC during respriting much more apparent. For example, you can often find NPCs that are a few pixels misaligned on their sprite sheets, which causes floating pixels around them while they walk. Additionally, a number of the sprites weren\u2019t updated to the larger side afforded by the graphics update, so they look *really* out of place when they show up.<br>There are also a number of typos in item descriptions and even one in the battle status ailment prompts. It\u2019s pretty off-putting.<br><br>To wrap up negatives, I\u2019ll bring up what is probably a personal issue brought on by my playstyle in both .hack and Data Hacker: the data core collection. I didn\u2019t really spend a lot of time hacking enemies in this game (or .hack) when I realized that you don\u2019t get experience for battles afterward. In .hack, there\u2019s always the possibility of grabbing an interesting piece of equipment, but you usually just get data cores. In Data Hacker, all you have to look forward to are data cores. When Jaque, a main character to the plot, tells you to sell him your cores the second time you meet, it even makes collecting them seem pointless unless you need money - I can see people that haven\u2019t played .hack making that mistake. But I digress. Farming for cores is time-consuming and rather boring. I spent several hours trying to locate a good dungeon to grab the final \u201cTz\u201d cores from with any regularity when it came time for their use. In that vein, to anyone who reads this:<br>     Core Farming Dungeons<br>          AlZo Dungeon, second floor near the two doors: Al, Zo, Ki Cores<br>          ZoTz Dungeon: Ma, Ge, Tz Cores<br><br>But, now, on to the good: this game is playable. If you like RPG Maker games, Data Hacker doesn\u2019t disappoint because it\u2019s very clearly an RPG Maker game. There are some interesting tweaks to the engine and some clever uses. Even a minigame (which I did manage to lock up the game with - don\u2019t walk up to the tournament NPC from his right side) that\u2019s\u2026 pretty similar to that card game from FFVIII. The creator obviously put a lot of work into it and, as long as you don\u2019t focus on things you might want to nit-pick, you should be able to see this game standing quite a bit higher than many of
