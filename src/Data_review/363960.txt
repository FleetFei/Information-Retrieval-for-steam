2015\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t<div class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\t<div class=\"bb_h1\">Introduction<\/div>\u2018That looks like a fun game.\u2019 Those were literally the first words that popped in my mind when I saw some of the screenshots of Devils &amp; Demons. It\u2019s not uncommon for us gamers to think that, I assume, but still; I was intrigued to say the least. And I somehow naturally assumed it would be a mix of a few other games (like Diablo, Puzzle Quest and turn-based strategies) but then combined into one, amazingly fun, little package. So, was I right or\u2026?<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay<\/div>Not entirely, but yes. The game certainly has some influences from aforementioned games. In my opinion, at least. It has the atmosphere from Diablo and Puzzle Quest, the same casual feeling of Puzzle Quest and\u2026 turn-based strategies that can be found in many, many other games. Thankfully Devils &amp; Demons still has an identity of its own. Even if it is at times (seriously) flawed. <br><br>First of all, let\u2019s discuss the most important part: the combat. \u2018Combat\u2019 in this case means turn-based (semi-)strategic battles. You start out on a big map where you can move freely along a grid. Downside is that you always have to use turns, even when there are no enemies around. This gets tiring fairly quickly.<br>The battles themselves consist of multiple characters (who you can each control individually) using specific skills to destroy the enemies around you. For example, you have a Thief who can backstab enemies and a Demon Hunter who can travel great distances and inflict damage by shooting arrows. There are multiple classes, most of which can be unlocked by just playing through the game (so far), but there\u2019s also an option buy classes. Here it becomes painfully clear that the game was built around in-app\/game purchases\u2026<br><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=530372336\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_0\">http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=530372336<\/a><br>And I can\u2019t tell how sad this makes me. When I first started the game I was enjoying it very much. And then it suddenly goes overboard (just a few hours in) by making some of the enemies feel overpowered or just by throwing some extra enemies at you. Now, there\u2019s no need to actually buy lives or potions with real life money, but you will have to use earned gold (in-game) to buy potions or revive your allies. A few hours into the game I started spending much more gold than I originally wanted to. But I had no choice; it was either this or trying it again and I\u2019m not crazy enough to start the same battle again and again: I play games to relax and\/or for fun, not for frustration.<br><br>Now, you can argue with me that the battles require tactics\/strategy, and I couldn\u2019t agree more, but if you start to get that feeling that the game is playing unfair then you know something\u2019s up. What a wasted opportunity this is; the game is actually very fun until your characters start dropping like flies. It seems they haven\u2019t been able to find a good balance between leveling up your characters with the acquired gold and using that same gold to buy things like extra potions. You need to do a lot of grinding if you want to earn enough to get you through the game. Enemies drop gold as well, but it\u2019s hardly ever enough. Ultimately you just have to depend on world events and other quests.<br><br>The setting is great, reminiscent of Diablo. Cities and villages are on fire and demons, skeletons and other creatures are waiting for you in the shadows. I would\u2019ve liked it to be a little darker even (the title isn\u2019t enough I\u2019m afraid) but it\u2019s not a big deal. It\u2019s a personal taste.<br><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=530372372\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_1\">http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=530372372<\/a><br>Besides the clearly visible mobile roots there are a few other things that bothered me. One that dragged the experience down, one that didn\u2019t, but as always they\u2019re worth mentioning:<br>I think it was a bug on my system but the tutorial dialog windows were shown in big colored boxes that filled the entire screen. I\u2019m not sure if it\u2019s intentional \u2013 I don\u2019t think so \u2013 but it\u2019s incredibly ugly and distracting.<br>And after each player turn there\u2019s a load screen of about 1-2 seconds. It\u2019s slightly annoying and it\u2019s more frustrating due to the fact that when your enemies have made a move none of this happens: you get to play again immediately. Every time it happened it became very clear that I was just playing a game. It ruins the immersion completely. <br><br>On the maps you\u2019ll also be able to find treasure chests and other interactive items that you can click on if a character gets near. These usually contain health potions, mana potions and gold. But exploring also brings out other dangers (which are usually still part of the story, mind you): more enemies. And then the whole ritual begins again. These enemies usually aren\u2019t so tough, but if you have to fight 10-15 more just a few minutes later then it\u2019s a different story. Now, this differs per situation so it\u2019s not always the case, but it has happened to me at least a few times.<br><br>So the game is basically ruined by the terrible economy that should\u2019ve either been removed completely or tweaked to make things more fair (by handing out more gold, spawning less enemies or receiving less damage). There really is a good game beneath it all, believe me.<br>If you\u2019re a patient person or like hard(er) games in general then by all means go give it a try. You won\u2019t be disappointed, trust me. <br><br> <div class=\"bb_h1\">Audio &amp; Visuals<\/div>The music is bombastic and worthy of a strategy RPG. The lines, while sometimes a bit corny, are generally well spoken but they are repeated a few many times in my opinion.<br><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=530372301\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_2\">http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=530372301<\/a><br>Okay, we\u2019ve come to the part that reminded me of Diablo and Puzzle Quest in the first place: the graphics. They consist of nicely animated characters (although not fully) and environments, but in this case they\u2019re unfortunately a little too pixelated\/blurry when run in full screen. If you run it in a window and don\u2019t zoom in too closely then it all looks nice and sharp, however.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Length\/Replay Value<\/div>I haven\u2019t been able to finish the game yet and I don\u2019t think I ever will. Right now it\u2019s practically impossible or just extremely time consuming. I think I\u2019ve played it enough the last couple of days to write a little bit about it, though, hence this review. But I assume that finishing the game will take you up to 30 hours, maybe even more. That\u2019s plenty of value.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Conclusion<\/div>What could\u2019ve been a great, fun game is now nothing more than a good, but frustrating game. The game isn\u2019t all bad: the graphics are mostly magnificent, the audio sufficient and the gameplay is solid. All of it is just ruined by the now-changed economy system that should\u2019ve been either reworked or removed in its entirety to make it less punishing and all in all just fairer. Despite all that it\u2019s still a good game.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Too long, didn\u2019t read<\/div>Pros:<br>+ Great atmosphere<br>+ Plenty of classes and skills<br>+ World events<br>+ Decent XP system<br>+ Amazing, detailed art\u2026<br><br>Mixed:<br>+\/- \u2026which is just a little too blurry<br><br>Cons:<br>- Roots of the original IAP system are still clearly visible<br>- Why is there still turn-based movement if there are no enemies around?<br>- Load screen after every *player* turn<br>- Very unbalanced economy which makes it feel unfair<br><br><table class=\"bb_table\">    <tr>        <th>Please note: a Steam key for this game was provided by the developer.<\/th>    <\/tr><\/table><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"><b>Rating: 6.9 (out of 10)<\/b><\/div>(Ratings aren\u2019t calculated based on a formula, but on my personal feelings)<br><br><br><table class=\"bb_table\"><tr><th>Please feel free to join my group, <a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/groups\/djs_reviews\/\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\" >DJ\u2019s reviews<\/a>, or follow me directly as a <a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"http:\/\/store.steampowered.com\/curator\/10483089\/\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\" >curator<\/a>. <br>\nDon\u2019t feel like it?
