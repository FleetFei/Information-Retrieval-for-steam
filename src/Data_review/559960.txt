Single-player<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">General Impression<\/div>Power Hover was a fun albeit difficult ride. It\u2019s just what you\u2019ve come to expect from an indie hybrid between an endless runner and a racer on Steam. Its developer has literally hit the ground running since they made a more than decent debut for a title sporting Unity Engine 5 assets in a minimalist style. More and more devs seem to find new uses for that game engine and we should all be grateful indeed.<br><br>Surprisingly, this is a story-driven experience and not just a random succession of levels, even if the themes never repeat themselves a lot. You initially control just one robotic courier on a mission to restore electrical power to various parts of the game world. A task easier said than done since collecting the batteries needed to unlock different structures which produce electricity, will have you travelling at breakneck speed across desert plains, oceans, cityscapes or even purely abstract levels. And in order to make the challenge truly worthy for the veterans of this genre, obstacles either static or moving, will try to stop you constantly.<br><br>Why is the game that difficult? Well for starters, I found that the game progression requires that you max out on the numbers of batteries you should collect during each individual level, if you really wish to power-up as many structures as possible and thus reap the rewards. Powerful boons, that will include even the possibility to unlock new controllable avatars or at the very least provide you with more \u201clives\u201d or chances to start a level section at a checkpoint instead of restarting from the beginning of the stage. It certainly adds to the overall difficulty, the fact that you have a limited amount of retries as it is, and quick reflexes will have to be complemented by nerves of steel. More often than not, you may skip on grabbing a battery since you were too focused on the obstacle in front of you.<br><br>Ironically almost, but for such a fast-paced game, patience is of far greater importance than the ability to quickly shift your hoverboard in the general direction of the scattered batteries. There are fortunately, many checkpoints along the routes you traverse. It won\u2019t help by much but at least it gives you the chance to memorize some of the battery locations as well as the traps and occasional bombs which will instantly rob you of a precious life. Not even all obstacles can be learned by heart since in the \u201cboss\u201d levels, you have to quickly travel through elaborate laser beam patterns and vent blades for example. Not really something that you can rely on replicating with the same results, at a subsequent restart of the stage. <br><br>So at least some degree of luck might also be necessary. I\u2019m open to any gaming genre even if the ones that constantly require quick reflexes, aren\u2019t among my favorites. I will still try to improve myself and overcome the not so favorable odds since rage quitting isn\u2019t a worthy practice for any type of gamer. So even if a particular level might seem harsh, rest assured that there is most certainly at least one that\u2019s far worse, as you advance the story. Keep playing and you might get good enough at it.<br><br>The \u201cLess is More\u201d motto befits Power Hover wholeheartedly. The minimal looks are perfect for the type of gameplay that\u2019s all about speed and precision. Game sounds are indeed repetitive, but considering  its legacy (initially a smartphone title), that is weak point we can safely overlook. I was expecting more achievements, but even as they are, I\u2019m sure that collectors won\u2019t be displeased. If you consider the game to be overpriced, then wait for a bundle or a Seasonal Sale.
