class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStellar Overload: AKA Gravity is Trying to Murder My Brain.<br><br>My first impression of Stellar Overload was that it looked very good, had a great take on the block-based style and would surely be a lot of fun. After more than half an hour of talking and fetch quests though I was beginning to tire and really wanted to just dive into survival a la Minecraft. So far in my misguided quest to save the village I had managed to fill in large amounts of the well with dirt when I got frustrated trying to climb out of it, destroyed all the farms (and much of the surrounding area) whilst trying to harvest carrots and finally ripped down half the wall of a quest giver\u2019s house by tunnelling straight through the berry bush I was harvesting and into their front porch. Compare this mass destruction to the tree punching start I was expecting and it was quite a culture shock.<br><br>I don\u2019t know if the grabber has the ability to specify the type of block you are trying to break rather than it\u2019s default WMD setting... but if it does I haven\u2019t found it.<br><br>So for the sake of my sanity (and for the good of the villagers) I quit out to look for a survival mode without a story but only found Creative, where I was immediately overwhelmed by the sheer number of block types and items in my inventory and realised I\u2019d likely have to play through the story to understand half of them. At the moment it appears there is no Survival mode and this is sorely lacking since repeating the story over and over is unlikely to be all that enjoyable.<br><br>I jumped back into the story and was almost immediately given a jet pack after one quick chat and then I found a hover bike cunningly concealed behind a semi-transparent bush. So much for the usual grinding and saving of resources to build something cool. Apparently I would \u2018require\u2019 the jetpack for cave exploration which as explanations go is kind of on a par with \u2018I require this armour-piercing-high-explosive ammunition for pest control\u2019.<br><br>Texas state law aside, it sounded like after completing the quest to find 20 Moonstone I\u2019d probably get a rifle and armour too. Apparently I needed an upgrade... despite so far having only used my 200 rounds of pistol ammunition pot-shotting peacoc ks for their (assumedly) delicious meat that I have no way of cooking. In fact I had yet to encounter anything more scary than a squirrel who jumped out of a bush and startled me.<br><br>It is quite a long way from those early Minecraft days of rationing your torches and being terrified to go outside in the dark because you have no health or food but need three bones from a skeleton in order to make fertilizer... in order not to starve... in order to continue on to actually make a sword rather than trying to fight with a spade.<br><br>Needless to say I just wasn\u2019t exactly feeling the sense of survival and achievement that similar games have offered...<br><br>Nonetheless I persevered and after crashing the hover bike into one too many squirrels I found a nice big hole in the ground to jump into. Almost instantly I was overwhelmed by dozens of glowing crystals in various colours, none of which were the illusive Moonstone and still I found none by the time I\u2019d reached the bottom of the cave.<br><br>So out of frustration I decided to see what would happen if I attempted to tunnel directly through the centre of the Earth.<br><br>For the first minute or so I wasn\u2019t really aware that I was actually making any progress on my journey down as my screen was basically solely occupied by a single block which had constant particles coming out of it and then occasionally changed colour. Much like mining obsidian I thought it was simply taking ages to break the blocks. After all there was no way it could be so easy to go so deep, so fast at the start of the game right?<br><br>It was only when I looked up into the black abyss that I had apparently created above me that it dawned on me that even blocks way down deep in the planet break instantly and how deep down I could go was basically just equal to how long I held the mouse button down for. It seemed that my suspicion was correct and that like some other games mining was just going to be unchallenging, boring and somewhat of an obligatory requirement tacked on to every block-based resource gathering game.<br><br>Then I reached the middle of the planet and proceeded to spend the next half hour in a battle against the laws of physics.<br><br>I assumed one of three things would happen when I dug straight down: A. I would die in lava, void, a rock monsters gastrointestinal tract etc. B. I\u2019d hit bedrock and it wouldn\u2019t let me reach the core. Or C. Gravity would get all screwy, it is after all a cubic planet.<br><br>C is in fact correct however it is C to the power of \u2018Ow my brain hurts\u2019 screwy. I assumed (if I survived) then gravity would simply flip but I had failed to factor in the fact that I was not drilling through the centre of the quadrilateral planet and merely skirting through a layer of the cubic crust, nowhere near the magma and iron cube core.<br><br>If there was an audio transcript of my experience it would be something like this:<br><br>\u201cDig, dig, dig, down I go... what the... *incomprehensible screams*... WHERE IS GRAVITY? ...ok I\u2019ll go this way then... *muffled*...what is happening? ...I\u2019m scared. Help me.\u201d<br><br>Etc.<br><br>I won\u2019t spoil the fun of saying precisely what happened or how I got out of the middle of the planet as the first time you try it it\u2019s like an escape the room puzzle... only where Newton\u2019s first law of motion is plotting to murder you.<br><br>However I will dictate the things that went through my head at the time:<br><br>\u201cI understand why they gave me a jetpack now.\u201d<br>\u201cHOW? How do I have thirty seven thousand limestone blocks in my inventory.\u201d<br>\u201cI hear birdsong. The surface! At last. I wonder what side of the planet I am on? Wait is that my hover bike?\u201d<br><br>...<br><br>\u201cI still have no Moonstone.\u201d<br><br><br>----------<br><br><br>To give some slightly more helpful feedback and information however...<br><br>Currently there isn\u2019t a huge amount of content in the game past the first part of the story. Hence I spent a large amount of time finding ridiculous things to do in creative mode instead...<br><br><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"https:\/\/www.youtube.com\/watch?v=ra9xaRtGjOQ\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_0\">https:\/\/www.youtube.com\/watch?v=ra9xaRtGjOQ<\/a><br><br><br>Resources are so easy to find in huge quantities that there is scarcely a need to bother mining, farming or anything else. Most containers dotted around in the little hamlets, crashed ships, observation posts and the robot fortress contain stacks of hundreds of all the common elements and more medicine, food and ammo than you would need to survive against an entire armada of angry machines. Not that it matters because even if you die you don\u2019t lose anything in your inventory and the only penalty is returning to your clone machine.<br><br>If you do decide to mine then you can rack up thousands of coal and ore by just digging straight down for five minutes and jetpacking back out of the hole so you quickly end up with dozens of storage containers filled with stuff you have no use for other than building ten foot high carbon steel statues of yourself everywhere...<br><br>I flew to most of the planets in creative mode and was impressed to find they actually exist even if at the moment they are basically just different biome templates with nothing much to do there. It would be great in the future if you could play on large multiplayer servers with people flying back and forth between planets in cobbled together spaceships. At the moment though the only way of getting a craft into space it seems is to build an extremely high pole that reaches space and launch the ship from the top since it wants to just hover ten foot off the ground otherwise. This works but the ship remains fixed to the gravity of the face of the planet you launched from so you are limited to two dimensional movement and can basically only reach planets in line with
