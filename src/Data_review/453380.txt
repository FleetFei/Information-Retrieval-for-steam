add-on and my personal favorite of the lot. It has more new dialogue in it then most of Battleborn's vanilla story missions put together, especially from Attikus but also Deande, Ambra and for some reason Oscar Mike, has variations in that dialogue and in the mission structure, and offers rewards for every existing character, all of which encourages and enhances repeated playthroughs. I have done at least fifteen runs of it so far and It has not gotten stale. The same can be said for the later ops, though I am quite weary of Battle School. It also has my favorite skins of any Op, and the most convenient level design- at any point prior to triggering the final boss you can return to earlier areas to look for Ops Points.<br><br>Toby's Friendship Raid has some of the best music in the game, and features plenty of dialogue from Reyna, my favorite Rogue, as well as then-newcomer Pendles, ISIC and a certain penguin. The OPs points are easier to find then in Attikus' DLC, but it is possible to miss points in a run, as there are several points of no return. It's also less \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665t hard then Attikus, but not by much- you are inundated with Ronin bots instead of Thrall Brutes. Have fun with that. There's also these annoying hallways where you are conitnually knocked back by waves of projectiles, which they for some reason decided to also incorporate into the boss fight. It's villain is also one of the least likable characters in any game ever- though I suppose that's the point.<br><br>Oscar Mike's Battle School is probably the funniest of the Story Ops, and impressive considering the entirety of the dialogue is one voice actor. It is also the most difficult, and not in a good way: It throws large numbers of the games most \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665t enemies; Brutes, Ronin, and high-damage Varelsi at you in a confined arena space, and there are a whopping four side objectives in each run, many of which are incredibly easy to screw up, whether you meant to or not. It is by far the least suitable of any of the Ops for farming Commander Packs.<br><br>Montana and the Demon Bear is also a winner for dialogue, especially for fans of Alani, Shayne or the big man himself. It also features Thorn angsting over her parents and Boldur bawling about it. It is fairly difficult, featuring obnoxious numbers of Thrall and the most protracted boss fight of any of the OPs, with many ways to instantly die. Thank goodness you have a lot of extra lives.<br><br>With Phoebe and the Heart of Ekkunar, they saved the best for last. It's a treat for a Beatrix fan like myself, with additional dialogue from Rath, Miko of course Phoebe, and notably features the only real examples of Kelvin actually conversing with the other characters. It has some excellent level design featuring a generous allotment of shards and plenty of cover to hide from the overpowered Brute attacks. Most of all, it has some extremely significant plot revelations that outstrip the entire rest of the game put together.<br><br>I look forward to the rest of the story operations. I heartily recommend this season pass if you have enjoyed Battleborn's cooperative play or its character interaction in the slightest.
