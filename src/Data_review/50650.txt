While the first game focused on the character \u2018War\u2019, this title picks up the threads and introduces gamers to \u2018Death\u2019 - the \u2018Grim Reaper\u2019 in human form - a masked, twin-scythe wielding specter, rippling with muscle and barely contained rage. Large, varied and well conceived maps do well to convey a sense of immense size and scale as you traverse the Realm\u2019s on both main story missions and side quests \u2013 with a good mix of Platforming, Puzzle and Combat sections providing for great overall variety along the way. Loot from hidden \u2018Chests\u2019, \u2018Gold\u2019 and earned \u2018XP\u2019 can be exchanged for a variety of clothing, armor, and weapon upgrades, as well as to unlock increasingly devastating powers organized into 2 Skill Trees \u2013 \u2018Harbinger\u2019 and \u2018Necromancer\u2019 - featuring powerful QTE-driven Attacks, Health Bonuses, the ability to Summon Minions, transformation into \u2018Reaper\u2019 Form etc. Combat mechanics are fast, fluid and bone-crushingly satisfying as you utilize primary, secondary and tertiary weapons in a variety of combinations to devastating effect. Perhaps its most egregious offence however is the inescapable sense that you have played large portions of this game, verbatim, somewhere before \u2013 and if you\u2019ve played legendary titles like \u2018God of War\u2019, \u2018Prince of Persia\u2019, \u2018Legend of Zelda\u2019 etc - you will instantly recognize likenesses in character, combat, weapons, platforming, wall-running, puzzle sections etc from those classic games. <br><br>Overall, \u2018Darksiders 2\u2019 is an entertaining adventure game on a grand scale, boasting a memorable lead character, fantastic art style, fluid combat and great gameplay variety - and much like a summer \u2018Popcorn Flick\u2019 - it\u2019s a fun, action-packed distraction for its average 30-40 hour playtime.
