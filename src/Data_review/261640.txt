Depending on how you play Borderlands, it might be justified.  Since it's impossible to talk about this one without comparing it to the other games in the series I'm going to assume if you're reading this, you've played the other ones.  If you don't already like Borderlands, I see little reason why you'd like this one over the others.<br><br>While there were minor graphical and engine improvedments between BL1 and BL2, this one had made effectively no improvements over BL2 from a technical perspective.  The main draw here is the different environments and characters.  <br><br>As far as characters go, TPS offers easily the best skill trees in the series yet -- very few skills do something boring like &quot;x% less recoil&quot;, or those that do offer it as an additional effect on top of something else. All in all, the lowered gravity makes for a higher emphasis on movement and active play.<br><br>The game falters when it comes to end game. honest, I think it's awesome. Now, some people might think that starting right in the middle of something can be confusing, and I agree (I did have to do some reading about some of the characters), but even then, the game does a great job at storytelling, since you get to know the characters and all. While I wasn't too familiar with the Borderlands universe, I did know about Handsome Jack, and it's nice to see how a person with noble goals descends into villainy; and it isn't just a &quot;he's a good guy who becomes crazy with power&quot;, there's different events throughout the storyline that start showing his true nature.<br><br>Like I mentioned above, the storytelling is pretty good. The main story seems somewhat short when you think about it, with a few missions that feel like filler; mostly because of all the walking around, not so much the writing, which is great, with lots of hilarious dialogues, and some serious ones when the situation demands it. If there's something I enjoyed about the writing is that; it can easily go from the characters making jokes and such, to a really dark moment, and it still feels natural. The game also has tons of side missions, which unlock as you progress through the storyline, and offer some more gameplay (as well as some great rewards, depending on the mission).<br><br>The level design is mostly good, with great open areas, and some really cool visuals. Still, there's a few sections (mostly during main story missions) that feel like they were designed in order to extend the game time, as there's lots of going back and forth in order to open a few doors. Also, while most of the large maps have vehicles for you to use, there's one near the end of the game that's really big as well, but doesn't have any vehicle station, which means you must walk around.<br><br>Now let's talk weapons. From what I know of Borderlands, this game is practically Diablo with guns, or a shoot 'n' loot, as the game says. That means that a great deal of the gameplay involves killing enemies, finding better weapons and gear, and so on, until you can defeat stronger enemies, to get even better loot, all the way up until you finally get a broken-\u2665\u2665\u2665 combination of loot, which you'll then proceed to show off on the Internet. It's a good system, I'd say, especially when you defeat some tough boss and see all that sweet, sweet loot dropping from them. And the variety is impressive: from pistols and submachine gus, to laser weapons with different elemental effects, not to mention the legendaries and uniques, which have their own quirks (I love Miss Moxxi's Vibra-Pulse, for example).<br><br>The soundtrack is mostly ambient sound, which changes depending on the situation (combat or exploration), it's good enough, but it's not something you'll pay a lot of attention to.<br><br>As far as playable characters go, there's four of them, at least in the vanilla game: Athena, Wilhelm, Nisha and Claptrap. I haven't played Wilhelm and Nisha that much, but their skill trees seem interesting. When it comes to Athena, she's easily my favourite character, her Aspis shield is great for soaking up damage and throwing it back at the enemies, and one of her skill trees greatly improves elemental damage. And Claptrap, well, he's fun to play, but the randomness of his action skill can sometimes be frustrating.<br><br>Well, I think I've written a fair bit about Borderlands: The Pre-Sequel. There's a lot more I could write, but this review looks long enough.
