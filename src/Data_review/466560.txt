class=\"early_access_review tooltip\" data-tooltip-content=\"This review was written while Northgard was marked as Steam Early Access\">Early Access Review<\/div>\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\t\t\t\t\t<div free.\">Product received for free<\/div>\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t<div class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\t<div class=\"bb_h1\">Basic Information<\/div><b>Title:<\/b>  Northgard<br><b>Status:<\/b>  Early Access<br><b>Developer:<\/b>  Shiro Games<br><b>Publisher:<\/b>  Shiro Games <br><b>Genre:<\/b>  3D Real Time Strategy<br><b>Release Date:<\/b>  8th of March, 2017<br><b>Type:<\/b>  Singleplayer <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Introduction<\/div>You might say I\u2019m a bit biased in Northgard\u2019s case. This title fondly reminds me of The Settlers IV, one of the first strategy games I ever played. The game I\u2019m reviewing today is still in Early Access stage, but it is a stable and entertaining experience nonetheless. The dev team have already proven their creativity on Steam with their successful Evoland series and even now with their third project, they still seem to focus on fusing two or more gaming genres into hybrid titles with few flaws between them. Northgard attempts to revive not just the long lost glory of the Settlers franchise but also Cultures, among other similar titles.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Story <\/div>Sadly I cannot discuss about a storyline since it hasn\u2019t been implemented yet in the Early Access version. From what I could gather in-game and by examining the game\u2019s Store Page, I\u2019m sure we shall have a captivating story filled with danger and adventure. We are talking afterall, about Norsemen. Scandinavian colonialism is uncontestedly accurate as both Iceland and Greenland were settled centuries before other European cultures would set foot on them. <br><br>Vikings or not, Norse explorers such as Erik Thorvaldsson and his son, Leif Erikson have left their mark upon history and their exploits most certainly deserved to be adapted for video gaming. So Northgard can currently be played in sandbox\/skirmish mode and doesn\u2019t include a campaign or a set of individual scenarios. Once it gets fully released though, expect a coherent plot and a natural scale of progression. Evoland I and II haven\u2019t disappointed in that regard and I have no reason to doubt Shiro Games now.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Graphics<\/div>The game looks great, with diversified assets and the option to zoom in, quite a lot. Fully 3D graphics that show clear signs of attention to details, from animation and even the dynamic weather effects. I do wish the dev team might also include a day\/night cycle eventually. One thing I noticed and bothered me a bit (not enough to count it as a weak point) is that all the settlers are male and once they get assigned to a certain profession, they look like clones of one another. 2002\u2019s Cultures: Northland had female settlers too and even children since it fully simulated the family life as well. And they didn\u2019t look exactly the same. <br><br>It\u2019s a game design choice, I know, but when addressing the topic of colonial life at least aim for a bit more realism, ok? The current building roster and the jobs they imply, offer sufficient variety for now but there is always room for improvement. The islands players get to settle could sure use more terrain diversity and perhaps more wildlife as well. I think that most of the minor issues I mentioned above can be ameliorated within several more months in development.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Audio<\/div>Both the soundtrack and sound effects are nothing spectacular yet they alternate pleasantly between a relaxing tone and a more alert one, once combat is being initiated by either the player or AI opponents. Honestly, very few strategy titles I played over the years could rival the sound scores of the Age of Empires or Blitzkrieg series and unlike action-packed shooters or role playing games, I know that real time strategy doesn\u2019t need music to \u201cassist or further the plotline\u201d.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay<\/div>I already mentioned The Settlers and Cultures, as clear inspiration for Northgard\u2019s gameplay, yet the title uses one of its most crucial mechanics in an entirely different manner from the aforementioned models. Territorial expansion is more close to the Civilization series, with (non-hexagonal) plots of land which first need to be individually explored and only afterwards, colonized if your settlement can afford the ever growing Food requirement. <br><br>Since Norsemen were known as both ambitious and resilient people, it should come as no surprise that the ultimate goal in Northgard isn\u2019t simply to annihilate your rivals but to outsmart and especially, outlive them. Survival plays center stage, in a game which simulates some of the harshest winter conditions seen in strategy titles. The kind of unforgiving weather which will deplete both Food and Wood stockpiles if you weren\u2019t paying enough attention to the rapidly shifting seasons.<br><br>If you think you can win in this game by mustering a large warband and swarming your opponents as soon as you see the window of opportunity, I have some bad news for you. Northgard is more static than it seems at first glance. You need to \u201cturtle\u201d not Zerg Rush the other clans which might not even offer a stiff resistance if you learn to properly defend before trying to attack them. Trade shall offer you both the ample supply of goods to survive and prosper, so long as you focus on the resource gathering closer to your Town Hall (the HQ, if you will). <br><br>Land patches offer different opportunities as some may contain rich soil (which could enable wheat harvesting) while other colonized parts might host the type of woodland which shall never leave you short of timber for construction, survival, and even trading with the other clans. A skill tree handles the unlockable perks which can considerably decrease the challenge, so I rarely bothered with them.<br><br>I keep mentioning rivals, but it\u2019s time I explain the strengths and weaknesses of each \u201cfaction\u201d, so to speak. The current game stage features four not-so-distinct clans so far. The combat-oriented Clan of the Wolf, prefer the aggressive approach and shall gain bonuses to their warrior training and damage they can inflict. The more balanced option is being represented by the Clan of the Stag, which prefer the more peaceful way of Fame towards the ultimate Victory. The \u201ceasy mode\u201d appears to materialize within the Clan of the Goat, which have very useful bonuses to food production and consumption. Lastly, the Clan of the Raven represents the master traders of Northgard and can hire mercenaries to bolster their armed forces.<br><br>Indeed, I have won on Normal difficulty on a randomized scenario (procedural generation is implemented every time you start a new game) with the Goat Clansmen by trading food to the J\u00f6tunn (Giants) tribe in exchange for kr\u00f6wns, the monetary resource in Northgard. Not all in-game creatures from the Norse mythology are as friendly and open to trade as the Giants though. Draugr (undead), valkyries and wyverns can lay waste to your warbands unless they have received adequate training and the leadership of a warchief unit.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Verdict<\/div>Northgard\u2019s a promising and polished game already, while it also proves that Steam didn\u2019t fail completely with the implementation of the Early Access program. Being a hybrid of multiple genres and with an equal number of inspired gameplay mechanics, the title I had the chance to review today couldn\u2019t have failed. I consider its price tag justified and I urge all strategy fans who prefer slower paced progression, to purchase Northgard even in its current stage. I personally can\u2019t wait to see the campaign, Shiro Games has in store for us!<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Strong Points<\/div>+ Steam Achievements.<br>+ Pleasant graphics and relaxing sounds.<br>+ Interesting mix of RTS combat with colonial settlement &amp; survival.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Weak Points<\/div>- Not enough cultural\/technological diversity between the rival Clans.<br>- No campaign or story missions in this Early Access stage.<br>- No Steam Trading Cards.<br><br><b>Rating 85\/100 <\/b><br> <br><blockquote class=\"bb_blockquote\">This review was submitted While I believe this is a straight-forward fix, I think it is one that will take time. There are three clans, and they each have one thing that is different, 2 of them get a unique building and the other gets an additional hero to fight with. There is only one troop type (warrior) and one hero (Chieftan), with the Wolf Clan getting a Berserker, and an opportuntiy to befriend an NPC clan called Jotunn, that grant you a VERY powerful hero at the cost of lots of food and lots of time (passive time nonetheless). There is also just one defensive structure, a simple tower that shoots arrows, and you can only have one per map tile (which seems fair), although the Sheep clan is capable of building defensive towers that do not consume building slots.<br><br><u> Gameplay Bugs <\/u><br>Literally haven't found one. Northgard still has no campaign and no multiplayer (yet. Both listed as &quot;Coming Soon...&quot;). So, when you consider that this game manged to deliver at least 10 hours of gameplay from just Single Battles, and it's been released as early access for less than a month, that's pretty freaking great. This means that the campaign really only has to be 8-9 hours long (not factoring potential multiplayer time) for Northgard to be worth $21.99 CAD by my personal formula.
