I may not have that much time so far (15 hours at time of writing), but I can already tell it is much better than Civilization. Since Civ is such a giant in the 4X genre, I'd like to compare Endless Legend to Civilization VI in this review. <br><br>Despite the fact that Endless Legend is now 2.5 years old, it's far superior to Civ VI.<br><br><br><u>Unit Stacking -<\/u> Seriously, why doesn't Civ have this? War is one of the most tense parts of these kinds of games, and in Civ VI you must take what seems like hours to move your army across the map to attack a city. In Endless Legend, you simply stack your armies and move them swiftly across the board.<br><br><u>Movement -<\/u> Most units have at least 4 movement points, meaning they can cross 4 tiles in a turn. In Civ, you get 3 tiles per turn at most, and much of the time your movement is hindered by hills and forests, such that your units rarely get to move even 3 tiles per turn. Yawn. <br><br><u>Artifical Intelligence and Diplomacy -<\/u> Damn does it feel good when diplomacy actually works! There are more items to trade (and that are indeed worth trading) in this game than Civ, and there is more information to trade (trading map knowledge, mutually increasing currency from international trade routes, etc). One AI will not start a war with you, leaving you to defend yourself, only for the other civilizations to call YOU the warmonger (if you don't know, this actually happens frequently in Civ VI, great AI coding Firaxis). As far as I can tell from Youtube comments and forum posts, this kind of silly AI behavior used to happen in 2014-2015, but since then the devs seem to have fixed this issue. Conversely with Civ VI, we're now 5 months out of the gate and have yet to see any AI improvement.<br><br><u>Strategic Resources -<\/u> It's not terribly hard to get the resources you need, and if you can't get access to land with them, you can access the neutral market and purchase them. In Civ VI you would have to trade with another civilization in order to get them (if you don't have access yourself), and that's hard to do when the AI seems to hate the player by default as is the case in Civ 6. <br><br><u>History\/Lore -<\/u> In the Civilization franchise, a lot of the interest comes from history and the humanities which is built into the game. I love that stuff too, and initially when I tried Endless Legend I wished that it was also a historically-based game. But since I've gotten into it, I came to appreciate the lore of Endless Legend. It's well written, and every item and Anomaly (non-generic tiles that give bonuses) have interesting lore. There's also a backstory for each faction you play.<br><br><u>Technology -<\/u> This game's tech seems to be a lot more flexible than Civilization; whereas Civilization uses a linear tech tree, forcing you to get techs that you don't need or won't ever use, Endless Legend allows the player to choose exactly which tech he needs. The only requirement for progress to the next Technology Era is that you have 10 techs per era, which isn't an issue because each tech in the game is useful - some are still better than others, but at least you won't be actually wasting any techs. <br><br><u>Graphics -<\/u> For probably the first 3-4 hours of my time with the game, I really didn't love the graphics. To me the map wasn't very readable. But once I understood what I was looking at, I really enjoyed the graphics and general aesthetic. The game blends a nice mix of computer generated graphics with what seems to be (beautiful) hand-painted pictures for diplomacy. Nobody really plays 4X games for graphics, but nonetheless this is a beautiful game. <br><br><u>Factions -<\/u> Factions in Endless Legend feel very different from one another. Different civilizations in Civ don't feel very different or authentic. Sure, you get a unique building or upgrade, but overall it just doesn't feel super different. In Endless, there are significantly less factions, but each one feels unique to the rest and each come with significant gameplay considerations. <br><br>So that's my review. No I don't hate Civilization; I really like it, but I wish it was made better.
