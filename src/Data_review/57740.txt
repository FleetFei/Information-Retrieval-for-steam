My taste in PC games mostly includes the strategy (especially turn based and pausable RTS) genre. I'm a fan of the X-Com Games, The Total War Series, Close Combat, etc. I played all of the Jagged Alliance Games and enjoyed them all. In my opinion, JABIA is a good game if you like this type of game; a game that requires a lot of management (inventory, strategy, assembling teams, et.). I found the planning and execution of my plans in this game a lot of fun. There are a few rough edges. They could have included a way to look at items ordered after you ordered them, as in the old JA Games. As it is now, you place the order and you have to remember what you ordered until it arrives and the only place it can arrive is at the Drassen Airport, requiring a lot of 'mule' trips by your mercs who will spend much of their time running back and forth for supplies and using newly hired mercs as mules when they come in. The end result of this is that I found myself spending much of my time organizing logistics for reenforcement and resupply. If, like me, you enjoy this in a game, then you won't mind it and may even have fun with it. If this kind of micro-management isn't to your taste, skip this game.<br><br>The other part of the game is the tactical combat. This is done using a real time pausable system that requires many pauses and movement order updates during combat to be succesful (survive). With a small group of mercs (one or two teams of 6 each) it can be very exciting and great fun, but as the game goes on, and especially when you are getting ready for the final assaults on the enemy strongholds, you will probably have several dozen mercs going against hundreds of bad guys in just a few sectors. At this point the tactical combat becomes overwhelming as it is virtually impossible to be everywhere at once and are therefore forced to send a small group of mercs at a time against many enemies to whittle them down slowly, and it becomes a drawn out meat grinder of attrition. Not much sneaking around, tactical planning here. Just a whole bunch of shooting against very long odds. I think JA2 did much better by forcing the player, VIA limited finances and merc contracts, to run fewer teams and keep the game managable, while not requiring quite so many mercs to succesfully complete the game.  When you hire mercs in BIA, you pay the fee up front and they are yours for the whole campaign regardless of the length of time they work for you, and the money you find and earn in this game will easily run into the hundreds of thousands, so there is a balance issue here. Also, militia are pretty innafective, and when they die the weapons you equip them with dissapear. I found it much more effective, once you've been in the game for awhile to just leave teams of mercs within striking distance of primary towns and mines that you have captured and send them in when enemies try to attack. Mercs can move amazingly fast along roads and, as mentioned before, you'll have the money and therefore can afford the extra mercs to do the job.   <br><br>Some things I really like about this game:<br><br>-Friendly fire is a threat. This adds some realism that I welcome, especially with grenades and explosives. You don't have the automatic grenade placement of the new Xcom. &quot;Watch out for that tree branch...Hit the dirt!&quot;<br><br>-The strategic logistical and organizational planning and the tactical planning for each sector. A lot of fun here if you like that sort of thing.<br><br>-The great fun of fast moving firefights once you get used to the interface, especially when the skirmishes are small.<br><br>There are some annoying bugs and design flaws, a few of which are:<br><br>-In close combat you can't shoot at an enemy when he gets close to you. You'll hear comments like &quot;Can't make the shot&quot; and your merc will just stand there as the enemy soldier cuts you to pieces, even if you have a pistol or SMG.<br><br>-Unlike the other good games in this series, you can't repair any equipment but weapons. Body armor just takes damage until it's useless, and it is very expensive.<br><br>-Sometimes when told to fire repeatedly at one target, your mercs will cycle through the count-down time for aiming, then cycle through again without firing. They seem to be able to do this indefinately so you have to watch them carefully.<br><br>-When in a firefight, mercs are often forced into a kneeling position (frequently exposing themselves to enemy fire) when doing certain tasks, like first aid, clearing a weapon or disarming a mine. This also happens when you try to arm a LAW (Light Anti-tank Weapon). This unfortunate glitch will get you hit fast!<br><br>-Game stops working occasionally for no apperent reason ('JABIA has stopped working'). This doesn't happen often, but just when you are about to win an intense twenty minute firefight, it's a bit annoying.<br><br>NOTE: There is a very good mod that helps this game quite a bit called the &quot;Realistic Rebalancing Mod for Jagged Alliance - back in Action&quot; by KILLATOMATE. I highly recommend using this mod to help some balancing issues and decrease the frequency of some glitches within the game.<br><br>As it is, I find this to be a very good game that has a lot of replayability. It's interesting to start off with various combinations of mercs to build different teams, then progress theough the game by taking areas of the map over in different order, etc. If they had taken a To my regret, I wasn\u2019t impressed with that cover-version, so I\u2019m not gonna persuade you to buy it. Game\u2019s decent, even engaging sometimes, but really not that much as original JA2. Even with the all downsides that original Jagged Alliance 2 have, it is still a much better game than its remake Back in Action.<br><br>Short audition of features:<br><br>1) 3d view works fine enough, but I can\u2019t say same thing about control above the camera view. Due to real-time gaming pattern, I often was in the not-so-fun position where I can see neither enemies, nor my own soldiers in the middle of the shootout. Somebody\u2019s shooting, but who? On whom? It was not obvious way too often. The biggest throw-down of the given 3D engine is not a bad camera, though. It is very limited destructibility. You have plenty of C4 there, but you only allowed blowing certain points at every sector. You can\u2019t even open closed door with the shotgun!<br><br>2) All controls are giving the feeling that developers were in need to invent a wheel anew. Or at least, a bicycle. Even moving a map from side to side with the sliding of mouse was added after some patches. And when you\u2018ll make yourself familiar with the basic controls, you\u2019ll still be wandering with some \u201cdecisions\u201d about user interface. Like management of your mercs in teams, or management of inventory of taken sectors.<br><br>3) Smart pause mode itself is good, comparing only with the best examples in the genre. But crude AI behavior and sometimes ridiculous camera makes that modus not so desirable, so to say. It is still a good option, but not the essential, like in games on Apeiron engine (7.62, Marauder) or even in the Dragon Age titles.<br><br>4) AI of the enemies\u2026 they shoot precisely, even with the weakest of weapons, but instead of taking the advantage of those superb skills, they always tend to rush forward, by the shortest path available. And they don\u2019t give much attention to the dead bodies of their comrades, if they don\u2019t heard a shot or seen the shooter. In short, they are quite dumb. There are several maps in the game, where some additional rules were added to enemies behavior, and it is there you\u2019ll probably meet some troubles, and get an urge to plan something ahead. 3-4 serious fights in the whole game, I\u2019d say it\u2019s not enough, but that\u2019s me. Someone likes easy games, and the majority of games now are making for those casual players. So take a hint: buy some PSG-1 rifles and collimators, to shoot far and precise enough, and quite fast at the same time. With those rifles you\u2019ll be having the upper hand almost in every situation that game has for you.<br><br>By the way! The AI of guards you\u2019re hiring for defending your sectors\u2026 it\u2019s the same as with soldiers, but even worse! They don\u2019t use any tactical advantages of the defending position; they just rush on the bullets! And, unlike the Jagged Alliance 2, you can\u2019t just hire new guards via global map. You need to get some guns, go to the sector where the fight with Deidranna forces was, arm manually each new volunteer\u2026 And if he dies, he won\u2019t leave his gun on the body, you need to find or buy a new one. So you need to participate in every fight by yourself, and it will be faster that way.<br><br>I can keep up with that list, but that\u2019s major things: the game went in 3D, but became much plainer in the process, if we compare it with the vanilla Jagged Alliance 2. Back In Action took almost everything that wasn\u2019t so necessary and fun, like over-pompious music themes, and constant transitions of items between sectors. A 3D perspective is not bad to have, and smart pause mode can be tolerable. But illogical and un-informative camera views, clumsy controls and blunt behavior of any opponents are not giving the taste of satisfaction even when you prevail above them.<br><br>Take me right, I\u2019m not thinking that the Jagged Alliance is a perfect game, even if I\u2019m calling it the king of the tactical genre. Were it up to me, I\u2019d cut or remade some features from it as well, and all that constant fuss with the inventory items, which need to fix, optimize, transfer from sector to sector, from drop zone to battle lines \u2013 in the first hand. I\u2019d add some really fitting and atmospheric music to the background, just to fight the urge to fell asleep over the keyboard, while I\u2019m planning another phase of ongoing war conflict. And to think more of it\u2026 Maybe the most challenging thing that was made under the brand of Jagged Alliance was not that 2nd game we\u2019re so much talking about? Maybe it is the add-on to the first Jagged Alliance, the Deadly Games? There you have a line of separated missions for your squad of mercs. You have one map, where you fixing all your tasks, and after it, that\u2019s all, you\u2019re victorious! It feels much more refined, than questionable economic system of the JA2 game. <br><br>The developers of Back In Action thought in that direction as well. They made two DLC with such maps, in every case you have some quest to get in somewhere and kill there somebody, via some sub-stages on the way. Sadly, but both of those DLC\u2019s are just shown how amateur those developers are. There are different reasons for this conclusion, but hear this: in the mission Shades of Red you need to storm some embassy where hostages are being held. The developers make the several ways to get to that embassy, and the cleverest one was from the basement, which you can penetrate via sewerage tunnel. That way you don\u2019t need to dodge the rockets, which the insurgents will be shooting in case of direct assault. But, but, but\u2026 In the end the devs decided it is not good for balance, so now you can\u2019t get to embassy via tunnel. You see it from one end near the river, you can get there with a C4 blast through the wall. But you can\u2019t go further to the tunnel. After you storm the embassy in the hardest, direct assault way, you get down to the basement, and then you see another end of that tunnel, and you realizing, how far Back In Action from the real complete state. Even now, after 3 years after release, with all after-market fixes, like adding the possibility to use mouse to move the map\u2026 it is still far from complete, not only in the DLC maps, but in the major game as well.<br><br>Finally, so many words after, a conclusion: you CAN buy the Back In Action, if you have a thing for tactical games.
