<\/div><br>Vehicle Simulator is, well, a vehicle simulator that was originally released in 2009 by Quality Simulations. Six years later, Vehicle Simulator has made its way on to Steam bringing all the original content as well as some free DLC packs which include some of the best mods which have been released for the game. Vehicle Simulator allows you to take control of aircraft, naval vehicles and cars in single player and multiplayer. I haven\u2019t tried out the multiplayer and I\u2019m not too sure how it actually works so this review is based entirely on the singleplayer portion of the game. It\u2019s also worth mentioning that for aircraft I\u2019ve played the game with a T-Flight HOTAS and for naval vehicles I used mouse &amp; keyboard.<br><br>Full disclosure: A review copy was provided by the developer.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Presentation <\/div><br>Being a six year old game, Vehicle Simulator obviously isn\u2019t the best looking game. If you\u2019re used to playing older games such as FSX (though FSX does look better than VS in my opinion) then it shouldn\u2019t be too much of an issue for you but if you\u2019re a bit of a graphics nut you might not enjoy it so much. The base game vehicles are all great, looking about as good as they can, given the game\u2019s age, however the vehicles included in the add-on packs are very hit and miss, some of them look just as good as the base game vehicles with other ones looking pretty rough. Obviously, how good the add-on vehicles look hasn\u2019t really got anything to do with the devs but since you will want mods to expand the library of vehicles in the game I feel it\u2019s worth mentioning. <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay <\/div><br>As with most simulators of this sort the main appeal of the game, in my eyes at least, is the free flight (or the equivalent for boats\/cars) but VS does feature scenarios for you to try out, you can even make your own! The scenarios are usually pretty simple, such as fly from point A to point B but some of them are far more interesting such as cargo runs or mid-air engagements. Free flight is self-explanatory, you just fly (or boat, is that a verb?) around any area you wish at your own pace for the simple purpose of exploration!<br><br>VS has several different locations for you to explore, mainly in Southern Europe but there are a couple in the US and the Caribbean. Each location has its own scenarios, the number of scenarios depends on which location you\u2019re looking at, with some having more than others. While the number of locations available in the base game with the free add-on packs isn\u2019t particularly impressive (locations in particular, there are a reasonable amount of scenarios) there are plenty more to be downloaded from the VS add-on library website.<br><br>One of the really cool things about VS\u2019 gameplay is that you can create and run scripts in the game, VS also includes three pre-made script scenarios and more are available as mods. If you know how to create them you really can do some cool things but the scenarios available are also pretty cool to watch, such as an F-86 taking down a little Cessna. <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Realism <\/div><br>As with all simulators, the realism is incredibly important and while I\u2019m no expert, VS feels reasonably realistic. The aerodynamics seem to be pretty good and for the most part aircraft handling felt about right for the size and mass of the aircraft in question. Naval vehicles also seemed pretty realistic to me but I know a lot less about boats and submarines than I know about planes and helicopters. Sound effects also contribute to a game\u2019s realism, sadly VS\u2019 sound effects are pretty hit and miss, with more misses than hits. While some of the sound effects, mainly the engine sounds for base game vehicles, sounded reasonably realistic other sound effects weren\u2019t so great and I often found myself wondering what the sound effect I was hearing was actually supposed to be. For a couple of aircraft I also felt that the variation of sound effects depending on where the chase camera was positioned was off though that may be dependent on the mod-makers rather than the game itself. In some cases the engine sounds would completely cut out unless the camera was positioned directly behind the aircraft which of course, is not very realistic.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Miscellaneous <\/div><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li>In case I haven\u2019t made it clear, there\u2019s not a huge amount of base game content if you ignore the free DLC packs. While it has been out for six years so there are a fair few mods\/free add-ons out there for you to use I would have expected there to be more base game content for the price tag. If you don\u2019t mind using the slightly (and sometimes more than slightly) inferior community made mods this isn\u2019t too much of an issue but if you do mind then VS might not be for you.<br><\/li><li>The game\u2019s performance isn\u2019t great even when running it on a PC well above the requirements. For most vehicles I was getting between 50 and 60FPS with reasonably regular, albeit short, drops to around 20FPS. For larger or more complex vehicles I was getting between 20 and 30FPS with some drops down to around 10FPS. Rendering the game in a window greatly increased my performance to sitting between 80 and 100FPS but again this was pretty unstable and I still experienced drops. As well as this I found that the game often had to pause for around a second on some of the maps to load scenery, now that\u2019s not much time but when it\u2019s happening every ten or fifteen seconds it becomes very noticeable and rather frustrating. <br><\/li><li>Weapons in the game are fully-functional, they can be pretty fun to mess around with but they\u2019re also used in some of the scenarios.<br><\/li><li>You can edit vehicles as well as create your own in-game, I haven\u2019t messed around with this side of things too much since I\u2019m not particularly good at that kind of thing but it\u2019s rather interesting and in the right hands could be used to make some cool things.<\/li><\/ul><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Verdict <\/div><br><b>Worth Purchasing on Sale. <\/b><br><br>If you can look past its dated graphics, VS is a solid simulator with enough free add-ons to keep you entertained for quite a while. While the non-sale price is a little steep for what the game offers I\u2019d say it\u2019s well worth purchasing at 25% off or more.<br><br><i>No drama, <a class=\"bb_link\"
