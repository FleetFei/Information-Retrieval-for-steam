<\/div>A sheriff, a stranger, and Cold Winter Farm. Three words from the stranger, the only three words, and three months in the saying. An insufficient answer for why he was wandering the road in the outskirts of Deacon Oaks. Sheriff Truman wants \u2013 needs \u2013 more. Cold Winter Farm and its stately home beckons.<br><br>A snowstorm, a tree fall. Trapped in a basement, a door is revealed. A door to a corridor. He can\u2019t go back, so Sherriff Truman goes forward. What he finds he cannot have imagined.<br><br>And so begins Corrosion, a self proclaimed \u201cdark, shocking, and fear-charged mystery horror game\u201d.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"> The underview <\/div>A large part of Corrosion\u2019s thrill is discovering what the hell is going on, with tantalising hints dropped in at a satisfying pace, leading you down that dark and terrifying path towards the inevitable truth. You\u2019ll spend a lot of time hunting down keycards and codes to access locked areas of the lab. This does get a little repetitive, but the excitement of exploring a new area keeps you going - you\u2019ll be dying to see what\u2019s in next room, despite - or maybe because of - the potentially nasty things you may find.<br><br>The game is packed with journals and recordings, which fill in the story of Cold Winter Farm\u2019s past and flesh out the characters. It\u2019s a well-worn but trusty method of driving the narrative along, and pretty much the only option, as this is one of those games where you\u2019re on your own 99% of the time. Of course, the occasional self-locking door and mysterious noise hints that there\u2019s someone - or something - else inhabiting the labs, but to say any more would spoil the surprises.<br><br>Aside from tracking down keycards, you\u2019ll have to solve the occasional brain-buster to progress through Corrosion\u2019s plot. Puzzles are about 50\/50 on logic and inventory ones and they tend to lean towards the brutal side in terms of difficulty. These aren\u2019t brainteasers. These are migraine-inducing hard puzzles. There are moments where there isn\u2019t a logical connection between where you are and where you need to go next. You\u2019d expect a hint as to what to do next, perhaps one of those handy keycards for accessing another part of the lab, but three or four times I was left clueless. It was only after scouring the entire building that I discovered a previously locked door had been opened, with no warning at all. It\u2019s a simple case of patiently covering old ground, but I could\u2019ve done without it. With that being said, there\u2019s a cool little maths conundrum, which I thoroughly enjoyed, and you\u2019ll need to work out times and dates of specific events that occurred in the lab, then play back recordings using the handy security archive.  <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"> Is it scary? <\/div>After several hours of creeping through Cold Winter Farm\u2019s eerie corridors, I sat back and considered the key question: is Corrosion actually scary? It\u2019s surprisingly tough to answer. \u201cFear-charged\u201d is probably an apt description. Psychological horror might be another. Things don\u2019t go &quot;boo&quot;, and I have already said it wasn\u2019t frightening, in the scary sense of the word. But there is fear in the game, as an emotion expressed in a variety of ways. People who were here before you were clearly fearful, and some frightening things went on. After unlocking the door to the medical lab or morgue, I hesitated before plunging inside, for fear of what I'd stumble into. Some of the contraptions I saw, including a particularly memorable chair, definitely surprised or shocked. Those with weak stomachs will also balk at some of the diary and computer entries, while the sinister music gets under your skin and creeps you out. This understanding builds, and by the time you get to the chase, all the little parts \u2013 the music, the setting, the sounds \u2013 may well have come together to produce a tension waiting to be relieved. Or run away from.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"> Final thought <\/div>Corrosion: Cold Winter Waiting isn\u2019t an easy game, so it ended up being a good length, and it kept me engaged and wanting to play some more. It suited a lot of my preferences, and what didn\u2019t appeal was outweighed by what did. In a way, the theme that revolves around this game reminds me of Exmortis.
