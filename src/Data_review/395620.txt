Player's tools in combat<br>I will praise the enemies and bosses in this game for having attacks that feel fair, and being easy to overcome once you have the pattern down. Just to note, I'm not talking about the actual items which you can find, but their applications in combat. <br><br>Every single tool is one dimentional in its use; for puzzles each item can be used in conjunction, and has unique properties when two techniques are combined. I feel this is the largest problem of the combat; your techniques never develop through the game, and each weapon only has face value in usage, so combat gets stale fast.<br><br>4. Boss health<br>To me, I entered each boss room with the mindset of &quot;prepare to die.&quot; As Jim Sterling has said, this is not just a catch phase, but a tactic on how to overcome a challenge. Throughout the entire game, few bosses felt frustrating, and once I was at a boss room, I never left until I beat the boss. Most of them felt pretty easy to me, but I'm more used to the counterintuitive tatics and knowing when some options are inferior, like rolling into projectiles instead of reflecting, since it gets rid of it faster and safer.<br><br>With that in mind, I still feel like the bosses health scaling is way off. I knew how to dodge each attack, and the patterns themselves were fairly balanced, but each death meant so much time was added to the boss fight, I was just bored.
