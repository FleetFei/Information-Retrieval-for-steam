If you're new, then please come in playing with a different mindset considering that the control scheme and graphics were much different than games of the present day.<br><br>Cons:<br>The input for the controls was a minor problem for me at first. I had to spend time adjusting the controls to my liking to determine what would work best for me. So, the default controls, I felt, weren't very good at all. Look at the trailer or in-game pictures on steam and decide for yourself about that. Searching for certain pieces, solving puzzles in certain temples, and going back to places you originally weren't able to get to but after gaining new abilities will be able to get to. That's kind of the main concept of the game, in my opinion. I don't mind it, but I know other people hate going back to places they've been to since the beginning of the game, probably forgot about that location entirely, and are basically stuck thinking, &quot;Where the hell do I go?&quot;<br><br>The gameplay is linear. I didn't think this was a real problem, as the matter of choices and decisions in-game weren't really popularized back then, but some people like having an option to make decisions and perform choices that matter in the game. The main antagonists are Legion and five serial killers known solely as &quot;The Five.&quot; Together they plan on taking the dark souls and unleashing a terrible dark army onto the earth. As shadowman you must collect the dark souls before they do and prevent the apocalypse from happening. So the premise itself is good, and gives off a somber and harrowing feeling overall. The item pickups were neat too, considering there are many different weapons and useful tools that will make your life easier when confronting baddies and getting from point A to point B quicker.<br><br>The soundtrack and the atmosphere were both pretty mindblowing.
