Earth Space Colonies<br><b>Status:<\/b>  Released<br><b>Developer:<\/b>  Persona and Pixel Studio<br><b>Publisher:<\/b>  Persona and Pixel Studio<br><b>Genre:<\/b>  3D Colony Simulation<br><b>Release Date:<\/b>  1st of July, 2016<br><b>Type:<\/b>  Singleplayer <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Introduction<\/div>Ever since playing Startopia in 2001, I became fascinated by video games which portray the hardships of outer space colonization. Earth Space Colonies (ESC) does not feature playable alien races or a giant donut-shaped space station, but Startopia\u2019s principles and general gameplay, remain firmly in place. Survival has a steep cost and resource management is paramount. For a Steam debut, the developer left quite an impression on me. I had been following this game\u2019s progress for several months and I contacted the developer asking for a review copy. It\u2019s not something I do often nowadays, but some games most certainly deserve my undivided attention. A mixed Steam rating spells a polarizing subject, but you shall find more details below.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Story <\/div>The campaign underwent the most radical changes from the one present in Earth Space Colonies\u2019 Early Access stage. You can still find several YouTube clips showcasing the initial lack of polish in both the User Interface and voice acting along with a limited space on which structures were built from the start. Once fully released, ESC now truly looks like a 3D colony sim worthy of being reviewed by yours truly. The story per se, is farily simple. You\u2019re the nameless administrator of humanity\u2019s colonization efforts on Mars, Ceres (dwarf planet) and Ganymede (Jupiter's largest moon). <br><br>Ah, the <a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"https:\/\/www.youtube.com\/watch?v=7HqHZTad6Kc\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\" >Red Planet!<\/a> Subject of so many video games and films, that it should come as no surprise that Mars is the focal point of your expedition. Your ultimate goal is to somehow transform that obviously inhospitable environment into the ultimate tourist destination. Or just terraform it enough until the red sand and gravel get replaced by green pastures. As you can imagine, it\u2019s still a Sci-Fi topic since Mars shall probably take a few more decades until humanity attempts to colonize it.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Graphics<\/div>Unity Engine 5 in all its glory. The assets look great and the attention to detail is obvious. It\u2019s a shame that some structures still have flickering textures to them, the closer you zoom in. Biggest culprit seems to be the hydroponics facilty, but the shadows cast by most buildings also seem a bit jagged around the edges. Disappointing, since playing at 4K res should have eliminated those annoying artefacts, even without further antialiasing settings. I still like the graphical diversity on most of the structures you shall build and the fact that you may rotate before placing them, improves the visual variety of the prospering colony.<br><br>The astronauts themselves can be distinguished by the different colors they wear, corresponding to their jobs. Cyan for scientists, Green for maintenance workers, Yellow for miners and a few more categories which you have to unlock later in-game. Sadly, you may not interact with the colonists at all and clicking on them is pointless. A wasted opportunity in ESC, if you ask me. They are just nameless NPCs that cannot even be issued any commands and whose AI isn\u2019t anything noteworthy.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Audio<\/div>Soundtrack is decent, albeit repetitive. The sound effects and voice acting are pleasant enough and as a result I wasn\u2019t entirely unimpressed. There are even a few attempts at humor through the interaction between your AI assistant, Sinclair and a few subordinates that offer you advice from time to time, in the campaign mode.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay<\/div>Since we\u2019re not gonna focus only on Mars, you can actually agree with the game\u2019s title that states \u201ccolonies\u201d instead of just one. Your Martian exploits shall have to be supplemented by similar efforts on the mining facilties of Ceres or the colonial exploration &amp; defense of Ganymede, from alien threats. How you design and manage each of those three colonies is up to you. In all honesty, it takes some time getting used to the building layout and the many pathfiding issues encountered by the maintenance crews you shall have to train fast enough.<br><br>These colonial structures seem even more flimsy than the ones in Red Faction: Guerrilla (I\u2019ll let you guess where that game\u2019s action takes place). Unless they constantly receive the attention of your astronaut grease monkeys, they will explode within 15-20 minutes. That is a serious issue considering the not so stellar (pun intended) Artificial Intelligence which has your spacemen wait in front of the very buildings they should be repairing. I had to restart the campaign and colony designing several times until I realized that the key to success, is to leave avenue-sized spaces between anything you erect.<br><br>The AI really takes away a lot of the charm from this otherwise entertaining simulation, but at least after you spend several hours perfectly planning and crafting the necessary materials, you can trigger all sorts of disasters to test your colonists\u2019 mettle. Everything from earthquakes, deadly meteor showers and even hovering UFOs that blast several buildings in one shot. You can\u2019t complain from a lack of activities in Earth Space Colonies since you\u2019ll have to micromanage a lot, even if some of the crafting resources can be given an automated process of harvest and trading. It\u2019s best to keep a constant eye and expand across the large maps, instead of building each structure close to one another. Soon enough you\u2019ll be employing rovers and drones, instead of just relying on snail-paced astronauts.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Verdict<\/div>Yes, I know what you\u2019re thinking. The game has a few lingering technical issues and then there is also Planetbase on the Steam Store. A more polished 3D planetary colony sim that is also priced slightly higher than ESC. You have your answer right there. For better or worse, Earth Space Colonies\u2019 is a title quite decently marketed for what it offers and its developer deserves all the support it can get since creating simulations with Unity Engine assets is neither that common or as easy as developing yet another platformer. Steam surely features enough of those already. Colonial simulations on the other hand? Keep them coming!<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Strong Points<\/div>+ Beautiful graphics.<br>+ Steam Trading Cards &amp; Achievements.<br>+ Implementation of disasters and alien threats.<br>+ Complex class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEarth Space Colonies is a building outer world pods simulator. I recommend the game for the reason I was enjoying such things right from the Moonbase Commander (which is quite old now). But - I see some mishaps so far which is why not everyone happy about this project, so - I urge you to think before getting yourself into.<br><br>Generally, there you build some structures, and then build more. And more. Some of them are very basic for survival (giving your colonists energy, water, fresh air and so on). Other will be gathering materials for the further growing, and once you'll have enough - you get more structures to build (like bigger powerplant or even a factory to build stuff from another stuff). That's it! Build - wait to collect some materials - build further. There will be other tasks, like maintenance of your buildings in the unfriendly environment - but those elements are quite crude at moment. In theory, you can build military base, and even fight some foes there... But principally, it's about building and developing, and not raging wars. And that's a good feature! Too many games are theming bloodbaths now and too few - prosperity and well-being of the all humankind.<br><br>What I liked was the overall atmosphere, which returns us to the romantic novels, like Ray Bradbury used to write. You know, about blooming gardens on the Mars surface...<br><br>What I didn't liked is the lack of content - but the developer promises to make more. What I didn't like even more is the unfriendly interface. Considering the facts that your buildings wears off quite fast, you need to build new ones and hire maintenance stuff even faster, right? But to build new pods you'll need materials. Which of existing structures produces them - you'll learn only when click all of them. If you'll need more stuff later - again, you should manually find that specific building and make an order of producing. Even on the point, where you'll be managing dozens of materials, you'll be doing the very same basic moves by finding the producer, ordering him to do some work, and controlling all such producers... Jeez, I need &quot;yellow pages&quot; there! <br><br>In the bottom of the hud there are 5 special buttons to Destroy your colony. I'll not be arguing, that they can be of some use... yet I don't need them so often, even if I forget about Bradbury completely. I'd preferred to see on that place some other, much more useful elements, like special buttons for producing all aviable materials from one place!<br><br>To make a final statement, this is a building sandbox simulator, which is not quite good with the building so far... and considering its sandbox nature, not so much fun. Yet... it is still early access, so there is still hope. You know, of the better future. So far, it is just another indie game made by one man, where you need to tune with the developer's logic - like Zigfrak, for example. I'll mark it as 6\/10. Certainly not a sleep-taker. Yet it deserves to be on Steam much more than many other scumming junk pieces, which people buying only for cards. As of price, though... Sol 0 is two times cheaper and delivering more fun at moment. class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEarth Space Colonies, developed and published by Persona and Pixel Studio, is a Steam Early Access title that fits within the resource management simulation-strategy genre. The ultimate object of the game is to terraform space colonies and to save the human race. The first planet that you have to work your magic on is Mars. You have been selected to create the first self-sustaining colony on the red planet. You must terraform it into a natural, fertile wonderland that is beaming with life. Getting there will not be easy as you\u2019ll have to manage your resources expertly and balance between growth and development and maintaining the stations, buildings and equipment that you already have at your disposal lest you kill off your colony.<br><br>I have to suggest that for an early access title the game is already at a very playable level. There are three planned zones but the current game is limited to Mars. There is more than enough content to satisfy your gameplay needs for at least several hours and I\u2019d suggest that there would be a lot of room for replayability. I know that with my first playthrough that I wasn\u2019t necessarily happy with a few things that I did, for example I should have utilized my space much better, and doing things differently would likely have provided me with different outcomes. Most importantly what is there absolutely works. It shows off its potential, ultimately leaving you wanting more but allowing you to have a taste of what is to come. It\u2019s exactly how I would hope an early access title should be.<br><br>The level of micromanagement in this game is something to be praised. I feel that many games within this subgenre fall short in the accessibility of micromanagement. While I know that not everyone is a fan because it can become tedious I can appreciate when a developer puts the work in to find the right balance. I believe that that balance is met extremely well here. Of course, balances can change throughout the development of a game but for now its a major pro.<br><br>I quite enjoyed the objective structure of the game. There\u2019s always something new to build and develop towards and there is seemingly many ways of doing that thanks to what appears to be a very diverse marketing system. The research into how many of each structure do you build to amass a colony that suits your needs will leave those who like to build data sheets spellbound for a long time. Do you target experience points rather than finances or vice versa? Is there a way to capitalize on both targets at the same time? What is the quickest and best way to attain your next actual objective? There is room for a ton of strategies that could work. Some will fall short, obviously, but therein lies the beauty of the genre.<br><br>The game is currently priced at $13.99 and is going to be raised as many early access titles are when it is released. If you\u2019re a fan of city and base builders and resource managers then I would highly recommend jumping in now. It\u2019s already what I would consider to be a good game and knowing that more is to come makes me very eager for its retail release.
