Fighting enemies that are in rooms, and just trying to get to your objective will become a real challenge with the random weapons you will find that may (or may not) help you along the way.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay<\/div>Disposable Heroes is a fun local co-op game that has a few issues here and there with some balancing issues with the enemies and the way that you get hit which I will explain later. It's a very content-rich sort of game that doesn't focus much, if at all, on story but more of the gameplay itself. There is a nice variety of levels, weapons, special powers and such that you can find throughout the entire game. This does mean that you might be constantly switcing weapons and having to adjust to how the new weapon works in the next couple of seconds of obtaining it. Obvioulsy some weapons are better than other, and some of them are just damn OP, but we as gamers like OP stuff. The way that you will play through the game is rather simple. Your objective is to clear out each room in order to progress to the next one. You will continue to do this, with some RNG deciding what rooms you will encounter, until you get to the end of the area. You will face countless amounts of different types of enemies along the way, some of them throwing swords at you while others are just walk to you. This does however take me to a few of the issues that I had wit the game, and what it set up to be in my opinion to what it really is. When I first started playing Disposable Heroes I assumed that the game would run along the lines of a Dungeon Crawler. However, the game itself doesn't do that unfortunatly. You only have to kill the enemies in the room and then you get teleported to the next room. I found this a little odd, as there are sometimes 2 doors that will open after you have killed the enemies in that room. I also found out that you can't actually grab the dropped loot once you have killed all the enemies in the room because you get instantly teleported to the next level. Nothing too major, but the next point is one of the things I really don't like about this game. Once you get hit I would expect to get a small amount of invinsibility to get out of whatevers attacking me. You don't get that here, which leads to a lot of cheap deaths as your stuck in a corner and get continously hit by enemies.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Pros<\/div>+ A nice co-op game<br>+ Content-rich<br>+ Variety of weapons<br>+ Trading cards &amp; Achievements<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Cons<\/div>- Some cheap deaths<br>- Get teleported to the next room automatically, meaning you can't grab any gold or weapons once you have killed all the enemies<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Result \/ Final thoughts<\/div>Disposable Heroes is a fun co-op game that has some fairly difficult gameplay.
