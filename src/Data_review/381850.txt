class=\"bb_h1\">Introduction<\/div>Sometimes you\u2019re not looking for something deep or meaningful. Sometimes you just want to casually play a game, without thinking too much and without having to worry about things like complex control schemes. If you\u2019re in the mood for such a game right now then My Paper Boat might just be the thing. Read on and let\u2019s find out together.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay<\/div>The fact that the game is called My Paper Boat lets you know what kind of game you should expect here. As I mentioned it\u2019s nothing deep or complex, but if you\u2019re looking for a relaxing, fairly mindless, game then My Paper Boat is ideal, but in my opinion, only in small sessions.<br><br>The game has 3 modes that can be played right from the start. You have: Aim, where you try to hit targets with your cannon, Rally, where you go through the rings\/checkpoints (which add a few seconds to your timer) and Collect, where you try to collect all of the 50 coins that are scattered throughout the level.<br>Unfortunately, the levels are rather linear. Ocassionally you\u2019ll be able to go one way or another but in the end you\u2019ll always end up in the same place and it\u2019s usually just for a few metres.<br><br>Each level lasts about 10 minutes, give or take, and there are 5 different locations. There is Garden, Desert, Cave, Canyon and Forest. The later levels have to be unlocked with your earned stars, but fortunately these levels can practically be unlocked after one try, maybe two at the most. In each of the levels you\u2019ll find obstacles that have to be avoided. In the garden there are rocks and waterlilies, which can be easily avoided, but later levels might give you a little more trouble; the desert level has whirlwinds that will toss you away and the caves for example have swinging shackles\/hooks. None of the levels are particularly interesting, but I do have to admit the Canyon level was kind of fun, because here the boat will go downstream and you\u2019ll have to avoid rocks while going at a faster pace <br>than normal.<br><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=525011398\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_0\">http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=525011398<\/a><br>In the levels you will also find a powerup in the form of a lightning bolt that, when pressed, will give you extra speed for a few seconds (or get you off land whenever you\u2019re stuck)), and a health pickup that slightly restores your boat when it\u2019s become damaged. That\u2019s right, even though it\u2019s a fairly casual affair your boat will still take damage when it drops from a high distance or if you hit a rock or other object with more speed than normal (or just hit it the wrong way). A nice feature is that your boat will start to crumple whenever it takes damage, like real life paper.<br><br>Sometimes you\u2019ll go to fast or get tossed around and you end up on the land. Whenever that happens you have 10 seconds to try to return to the water or else you\u2019ll have to restart from the checkpoint. Each level usually has 2 or 3 checkpoint so it\u2019s not a big deal normally speaking, but there were a few times were I had to do a section a couple of times because I kept getting thrown on the land. During my first playthrough I also encountered a bug that got me stuck in the environment. With no option to reset your boat you then have to either start from a checkpoint or from the beginning, depending on how far you had already progressed.<br><br>If you really can\u2019t get enough of the game then you also have the ability to unlock parts for your boat. These are purely cosmetic and won\u2019t give you any extra abilities or anything, but it\u2019s a nice touch, especially if you really enjoy(ed) the game. You will have to unlock them with stars, though, so not everything is available the first time, but that\u2019s the reason why they\u2019re called unlockables, of course.<br>A few examples of what you can customize and unlock: flags, anchors, spyglasses and patterns for your boat (regular, white, paper or newspaper, et cetera). Fun stuff and a good reason to try and unlock all of the stars.<br><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=525025561\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_1\">http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=525025561<\/a><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Audio &amp; Visuals<\/div>I can be very short about the musical aspect of this game; except for the main menu there doesn\u2019t seem to be any music. I personally enjoyed that, because the goal seems to be to provide the player a relaxing experience. It manages to do this perfectly fine this way. Naturally there are sounds effects, like when you pick-up a coin, or shoot your cannon. Nothing extraordinary, though.<br><br>As for the visuals; it\u2019s a pretty game, but very demanding. I had to turn down the \u2018resolution scale\u2019 to get a good performance. Unfortunately, though, it also seems to use a lot of filters and other effects and they mess up the clarity and image quality. As you can see from the screenshots a lot of the objects look (and are) blurry. There\u2019s also sun\/light reflection but somehow the developer went overboard with this. Once again, this can be seen by taking a look at the screenshot below.<br>If only the image quality would\u2019ve been sharper this game would\u2019ve looked perfect. Right now it\u2019s not what I hoped it would look like in motion. Don\u2019t get me wrong, please, because the game is still very pretty, even if the water sometimes also looks a bit oily.<br><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=525011502\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_2\">http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=525011502<\/a><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Length\/Replay Value<\/div>The downside to casual games is that they\u2019re usually meant for short sessions. This applies to My Paper Boat as well. It\u2019s fun in small doses, but play it for a few hours and you\u2019ll most likely will have seen everything by the time you\u2019re done. The available modes didn\u2019t invite me to replay any of the levels, since all of them start out the same, and have the exact same routes. I think most people will have finished it (or at least will have seen what it has to offer) in roughly 2-3 hours of playtime.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Conclusion<\/div>I can be short about this: My Paper Boat is a fun experience if you\u2019re looking for a small game to kill your time with, or if you\u2019re not in the mood for something complex. It\u2019s relaxing and fortunately has enough gameplay to keep you occupied for 2-3 hours, but after that you most likely won\u2019t start it again anytime soon. It won\u2019t win any awards, but it\u2019s still a somewhat unique little game. Therefore I recommend to grab it when it's just a little cheaper, or if you have some extra money to spend and desperately want to add a +1 to your game collection.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Too long, didn\u2019t read<\/div>Pros:<br>+ Graphics<br>+ Relaxing atmosphere<br>+ Funny unlockables<br><br>Mixed:<br>+\/- Levels are too linear<br><br>Cons:<br>- Too many filters and effects used for the graphics<br>- Little replay value<br><br><table class=\"bb_table\">    <tr>        <th>Please note: a Steam key for this game was provided by the developer.<\/th>    <\/tr><\/table><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"><b>Rating: 6.5 (out of 10)<\/b><\/div><br><br><table class=\"bb_table\"><tr><th>Want to read more of my unbiased reviews?
