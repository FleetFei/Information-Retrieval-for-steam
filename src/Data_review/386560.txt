for a game like Tyrian, my favorite space shooter from the late 90s. I didn\u2019t find anything. There were tons of space shooters, sure, but Tyrian had a great customization system, and over a decade later I couldn\u2019t find a game that compared, much less improved on it. Turns out Psycho Starship Rampage is the game I was looking for.<br><br>PSR is a customization-heavy rogue-like game built on the skeleton of a standard space shooter. Gameplay is divided into two parts. Stages are pretty standard shooter levels with waves of enemies, terrain hazards, and a final boss. But, after each one, you go into \u201chyperspace\u201d where you can use parts picked up in the prior level to customize almost every aspect of your ship. Thus the game cycles between design and battle until your eventual demise.<br><br>What makes PSR special is the ship customization. This isn\u2019t merely a matter of selecting weapons or some such\u2014PSR gives you the ability to design a ship from the ground up. You build the body of your ship of basic geometric blocks and give it functionality by installing devices on them. You decide the number, location, and type of engines, power generators, batteries, repair units, and weapons. You have control over the precise angle of guns and, in a very clever feature, over key bindings of individual components, meaning that you can map weapons systems to keys in whatever combinations you like. Your customization is only limited by what you pick up in the levels: technologies are rolled like RPG weapons, meaning that even two lvl 1 blaster patterns won\u2019t be identical. Upgrades must be paid for with scrap salvaged from your exploded foes.<br><br>This system is the game\u2019s strongest feature. Your design decisions are significant, noticeably altering how your ship plays. The interface is clear and easily learned. And there is opportunity to test your customizations out before embarking on another level\u2014a smart feature I wouldn\u2019t have known to ask for. Designing ships is what I most enjoyed about this game.<br><br>The actual shooter is well-executed, but unremarkable and a bit plain. Enemies are rather abstract looking and all come in the same color. The only other element of a stage is random space debris such as meteors. Backgrounds are nice looking but simple. One noteworthy feature of play is the variety of ways to fight. In addition to projectile weapons, you can install impact weapons (energy spikes) or tractor beams which allow you to bludgeon enemies with debris. (This is the only game I\u2019ve played in this genre where terrain obstacles hurt opponents as much as you.) My only real complaint with play is that in some stages small debris, background elements, and scrap look enough alike that, in the thick of things, it can be hard to tell which is which.<br><br>There is a story mode which honestly I\u2019ve yet to thoroughly explore. (It was not implemented yet for the beta period where most of my play took place.) It\u2019s mechanics are largely the same as those described above, though. Still, I can say that the game\u2019s quirky, somewhat morbid humor, present in both modes, actually succeeds in being good for a laugh. Also, parts of the game\u2019s sound track are quite catchy.<br><br>On the other hand I was a bit bothered by the progression mechanic. In typical rogue-like fashion technologies, including fairly essential ones, are to be unlocked with play. Now, right up-front I should say that I dislike grinding. Having to play many games at a handicap while you unlock everything is my least favorite feature of current rogue-likes. In the beta, this frustration was compounded by extreme difficulty. The most recent release seems to have mostly fixed the problem; there still is the need to run survival mode many times to unlock everything though. Also, the devs are listening, and this makes me hopeful that whatever tweaks are still needed will take place.<br><br>At this point, the only really major complaint I have is that the game involves insufficient documentation. I had to figure many things out by trial and error, and in a few very opaque cases I had to consult the devs for answers. <br><br>I really like the core of this game. Building your own ship and blowing things up with it is good fun. There is still room for some polishing and tweaking, but this is a solid implementation of an interesting concept. Not a really big deal on the lower levels, but at higher levels, the bosses can surprise you... rather quickly.<br><br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">I see a &quot;Rogue-like&quot; tag up there, what about it?<\/div><br>When you start PSR, you only have basic equipments : Cannons, power generators and power storage. When you progress through the game, you permanently unlock more stuff for your next game by doing specific actions (kill X enemies with a certain weapon, reach a certain level, etc). Basically, the more you play, the more things you can do, and the more fun you have. Some unlocks are easy, some others are not.<br><br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Game modes, campaign?<\/div><br>PSR has a campaign, and it has a slight FTL feel to it (Faster Than Light). By that I mean, you need to reach a certain point by going from system to system, and bad guys will steadily catch up on you and block some routes. You have to take some risks to grab some unlocks to make your campaign progression easier, but you can't waste too much time either, or the bad guys may catch up. Again, each person will have it his\/her way!<br>As for the survival... well, it's like its name suggests. If you want to build the mammoth ship of your dreams, that's the mode you want! I always die because I like to take risks on my ship setup, but that's part of the fun.<br><br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">What about the cons?<\/div><br>IMO there aren't many, but if I had to name a few :<br>\u2022 No way to change the color of your ship.<br>\u2022 No way to change the projectile\/lasers colors.<br>\u2022 Could use more game modes. Survival is a ton of fun, but something like a boss rush could work too.<br>\u2022 Even more weapons! The game has quite a number of them, but you can never have enough.<br><br>Well, the game just got released, so I suppose there's plenty of time for stuff to happen.<br><br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Closing words<\/div><br>All in all, I believe PSR wanted to focus on the &quot;freedom of build&quot; to distinguish themselves in the genre.
