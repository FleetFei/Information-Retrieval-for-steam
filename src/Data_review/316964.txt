2014\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t<div class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo far, the MiG-21 has mostly lived up to my expectations...mostly.<br><br>First thing you need to realise is that the MiG-21, being Soviet, has a very different philosophy to systems than any Western aircraft, so most of what you know needs to go out the window.  Having said that, being a '50s vintage design, it is a fairly simple aircraft at the end of the day.  Getting the MiG going from cold and dark is pretty easy; it's on about the same level as the F-86 Sabre, a bit harder than the P-51D and a LOT easier than the A-10C.  No complicated electronics here, apart from the radar.  One thing to be mindful of; the MiG-21 needs ground electric power to start, so unless you are at a friendly airfield, you're not going anywhere.<br><br>The second irritation is the flight model, and sadly it's a bit of a limitation in DCS at the moment.  The model is great if you're flying a subsonic aircraft like the A-10, but once you start flirting with the sound barrier, things go a bit haywire.  Comparing acceleration times to the official flight manual (and USAF intel reports), the DCS MiG-21 is basically correct from zero to about Mach 0.9, and beyond Mach 1.3.  In that troublesome transonic range, the DCS MiG is hopelessly slow, which is kind of a dealbreaker - the aircraft struggles to exceed Mach 1.1-1.2, even clean, when it should sail straight on to its top speed without a huge amount of drama.  Also, some of the other features, such as boundary layer control, are kind of fudged in and as a result don't work all that well.  The dev is looking into this, but my understanding is that this is a model limitation (many missiles are similarly hurt by poor transonic modeling) and a fix could be a long time in the making.<br><br>Finally, the landing gear of the MiG-21 is (for the moment) hopeless.  It's as if they decided to use superballs instead of hydropneumatic dampers, making the aircraft very unruly on its (alarmingly fast) takeoff and landing runs.  Fortunately, the dev has said this is a high-priority fix.<br><br>Even with the problems (and bearing in mind that this module is still technically in beta), DCS MiG-21 is already pretty much my favourite DCS module, and is well worth the price of admission.<br><br>19 November UPDATE:<br><br>The developer has fixed the issue with both the landing gear and the flight model.  The aircraft is controllable on the runway, and it can reach Mach 2.05 as it says on the tin.  Any reservations I had about this DLC have been addressed.
