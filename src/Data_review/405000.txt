Description<\/div>UTOPIA 9 is a procedurally generated rogue-lite with plenty of flavor and excitement. Unique for its take on the nemesis system, this colorful twin-stick shooter provides loads of vengeance seeking fun. Even though the solo experience is praiseworthy, support for local coop with up to 2 total players raises the experience to a new level.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay<\/div><u><b>Utopian Travels<\/b><\/u><br><i>Dear Guest,<br><br>Take a vacation with Utopian Travels to the best resort in the galaxy, Utopia 9! Filled with unique and exciting experiences, rest assured that our ever evolving staff will take care of you. The only thing you need to bring is a friend. Be prepared to have a blast!<br><br>Sincerely,<br>Utopian Travels<\/i><br><br><u><b>Combat<\/b><\/u><br>The traveling protagonist is allowed the use of two sets of primary and secondary attacks. The weapon or item in either hand can be switched with ease, creating a personalized playstyle with constant on-the-spot switching.<br><br><b>Weapons<\/b><br>UTOPIA 9 employs the use of many different melee and projectile weapons, each with a unique style. The classifications are: bullet, energy, explosive, melee and shell weapon types. Some weapons will release a charged-up blast, others will shoot scattered, focused, explosive or rapid projectiles. Depending on the weapon or consumable item, two-hands may be required. This encourages the player to focus and improve on active weapon switching. Upon mouse-over, weapon stats are also provided, such as: cooldowns, damage, spread, etc.<br><br><b>Enemies<\/b><br>The enemies in this game are extremely varied, although many of them will utilize the same character model. Some of the creatures are aggressively fast melee vanguards, while others are focused snipers. Some of them will even retreat to backup if the player is starting to dominate. When an enemy is killed, ammo, health, and soylent will be randomly dropped. These loot items will disappear after a period of time.<br><br>UTOPIA 9\u2019s take on the nemesis system is what sets it apart from many other twin-stick shooters. Upon dying, the enemy that landed the death strike will: transform itself into a nemesis enemy, take your gear, and earn itself a place on the wanted list. It will also be given a name and profession to add flavor to the newly promoted target of revenge. Defeating the nemesis will allow the player to reclaim the previously lost gear. It's also possible to have multiple nemesis enemies on the map, but only one can be present in a single level.<br><br><u><b>Mutations<\/b><\/u><br>Mutating is a major form of character progression in this game. Upon collecting soylent from downed enemies, the character may level up and choose a mutation (ability). The mutations are classified below, along with an example. The mutation options are also randomly generated each time\/playthrough.<br><br><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li>Physical: Projectile Parry - attack with melee weapons to deflect projectiles.<br><\/li><li>Utility: Eagle Eyes - 20% more ammo from ammo crates.<br><\/li><li>Mastery: Painful Payback - when you receive damage, you retaliate with a blast.<\/li><\/ul><br><u><b>Travel Information<\/b><\/u><br>Upon clearing a location, the player then chooses where to go next by selecting another location on a map. The map is similar in design to a hopscotch court where travel occurs at specific nodes. The map also provides small bits of information to help the player choose a location, such as: health, weapons, armor, and shield indicators. If the player perishes, the death location is marked on the map and in the level itself. Upon death, statistics are provided \u2013 number of discovered items, score comparisons and leaderboard information (most soylent, most points, etc.). Although it is possible to progress by running through the level while bypassing enemies, doing so is not advisable as the character\u2019s mutation level and gear will be severely lacking for future endeavors. <br><br><u><b>Video Gameplay<\/b><\/u><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"https:\/\/youtu.be\/jrNHWJgIwGM\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_0\">https:\/\/youtu.be\/jrNHWJgIwGM<\/a><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Presentation<\/div><u><b>Graphics<\/b><\/u><br>UTOPIA 9 does an outstanding job creating an immersive and colorful alien world. Its use of vibrant purple, blue and green color palettes help sell the extraterrestrial aspect. The aesthetics, art style, and projectile animations are very appealing from a visual standpoint. Taking a laser to the face is less agonizing when it looks so pretty.<br><br><u><b>Level Design<\/b><\/u><br>UTOPIA 9 utilizes procedurally generated levels which help break up any monotony experienced from rogue-lites of lesser quality. A more in-depth explanation of the game\u2019s level generation is explained by the developers <a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/app\/405000\/discussions\/0\/364040961442176781\/\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\" >here<\/a>. An important point mentioned within details how a level takes into consideration the time of the day, and generates fauna and lighting effects accordingly.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Controls<\/div><u><b>Controller Support<\/b><\/u><br>Although there is full controller support, the game doesn\u2019t seem to favor incorporating it. A few examples of what this means include:<br><br><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li>The beginning introduction presenting key-bindings using a M&amp;K.<br><\/li><li>Not allowing the selection of travel locations using a controller without first hovering over the map with a mouse.<br><\/li><li>A &amp; B or X &amp; O, by default on a controller, perform the same action in-menu which is extremely confusing.<\/li><\/ul><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Conclusion<\/div><u><b>Current State of the Game<\/b><\/u><br>Even though UTOPIA 9 was only released last month, there seems to be an active player base, as seen in the Discussions board and community content. Despite the lack of reviews, which are all positive at the time of publishing this review, player support seems to be relatively strong. The developers have officially responded in every thread with a directed question, and Whalegun is currently working on multi-lingual support for the game. <br><br><u><b>Advised Suggestions<\/b><\/u><br>There are a few suggestions that may improve gameplay listed below.<br><br><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li>Provide a method to view items\/descriptions without using a mouse.<br><\/li><li>Indicate AOE range or blast radius of an explosive object.<br><\/li><li>Introduce a currency system - purchase boosts, health, ammo, etc.<br><\/li><li>Introduce a bonus objective system with rewards for each level (soylent, currency, etc.).<\/li><\/ul><br><u><b>Verdict<\/b><\/u><br>UTOPIA 9 is an extremely light-hearted and immersive game, despite the plethora of alien guts spread all over the place. The game\u2019s aesthetics and nemesis system earn it a recommendation for any fan of rogue-lites or twin-stick shooters. Unfortunately, I didn\u2019t manage to experience this vibrant game with other couch buddies, but I will say that the solo experience is still a worthwhile one. <br><br><table For an Early Access game, it's already very good.<br>If this review is too long for you, I suggest you to only read the first paragraph about the game's content and the final verdict at the end.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">What is happening in the game:<\/div>You arrived to Utopia 9 as a tourist, but your vacation doesn't really go as planned. The world is full of enemies, explosive barrels, weapons and other deadly things. You have to progress through the environment to get off this planet to sue the agency that sent you here.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Pros:<\/div><ul class=\"bb_ul\">    <li><b>Gameplay<\/b> | I really like how the vacation is divided into four parts. You can choose different paths where certain types of weapons can be found. If you get killed by an enemy, he picks up your weapons. You can reclaim those weapons by getting to the same area and beating the enemy who killed you.<br>    <\/li><li><b>Weapons<\/b> | You can have one primary and one secondary weapon equipped and two stored for switching. There are a lot of weapons, and each one of them is fun to play with, in my opinion. You can find weapons in weapon crates (which weapon you get depends on the level you're currently in) and from previously mentioned enemies who killed you.<br>    <\/li><li><b>Upgrading System<\/b> | After a certain amount of gained experience points, you will have a chance to select a so-called mutation which pretty much just gives you some kind of a bonus. It can be an ability, pickup bonuses, damage blasts and a lot of other different things. You can have approximately 4 to 5 of them in every game.<br>    <\/li><li><b>Graphics<\/b> | The game is very colourful and I like how they chose different colours for different weapon types. Although the options regarding graphic quality of the game are not included, I think that this game is not as demanding as some games are so you should have problems with performance. Animations are also made pretty well.<br>    <\/li><li><b>Game Sounds and The Soundtrack<\/b> | I really love the soundtrack (you can listen to it if you watch the trailer on the game's Store page) and together with all the game sounds like weapon firing, damage taken, explosions and other things. There's really nothing they could add to it in the final release.<br>    <\/li><li><b>Procedurally Generated Levels<\/b> | Every time you start the game, levels are different from your previous round. Nemeses stay on the same level, but level shapes and weapons you can find there are different every time. This provides you with a lot higher replayability. I have to say that even after many hours of playing this game, I'm still not even a bit tired of it. <br>    <\/li><li><b>Achievements<\/b> | There are a lot of achievements in this game, which is great. In fact, acquiring every single weapon for the first time grants you an achievement! There are also some achievements for beating your nemeses, traveling to certain stages for the first time and others. I have to say that this is one of the most enjoyable achievement systems and it definitely helps the whole experience.<br>    <\/li><li><b>Looping<\/b> | I didn't know how exactly to name this paragraph, but every time you die, your corpse will be shown in later playthroughs. Your crashed spaceships on the crash site will also be stored. I know this doesn't really help the gameplay a lot, but it's a little detail I like.<br>    <\/li><li><b>System Requirements<\/b> | This game uses an extremely small amount of system resources, which is great if people with weaker computers want to try it out. Loading, however, probably takes a bit longer time for such computers. It's not a big deal, though.<\/li><\/ul><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Cons:<\/div><ul class=\"bb_ul\">    <li><b>Playable Characters<\/b> | It's far from being a real problem, but I sometimes wish there were a few different characters to play with. You currently only have one, which is fine, but multiple characters would bring a lot more diversity and would possibly require different playing techniques. They could have different stats like speed, shooting rate, melee power and others. Characters could be unlocked by beating the game. Just an idea.<br>    <\/li><li><b>Linux Support<\/b> | Well, there isn't any. The developer still has time to build it in, though,as the game is still in Early Access.<\/li><\/ul><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">The final verdict:<\/div>For an Early Access game, Utopia 9 is extremely well polished and since they're still planning to upgrade certain things, I'm looking forward to upcoming builds to see what more can even be done. It's pretty useful if you got some good weapons in your previous run, but you played really badly, so you have a chance to reclaim those weapons.<br>This game runs very smoothly and uses very few system resources, which is definitely a big plus. Graphics are also surprisingly nice-looking. The soundtrack is, as mentioned, really good and they should probably make a DLC for it.<br>If you're into indie casual shooters, definitely give this game a shot.
