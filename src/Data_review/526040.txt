this and judge the dlc on its own merits it is really good.<br><br>First off, invasions, the orbital watch now sends ships in to attack and you have to fight them off. This brings back an element that many people loved about the previous games. Though, like many things in anno, it is 100% optional, but even if you do not want invasions you can still have your fleet in your sectors now. Not only does this look cool but the fleet in your sector can kill ships that spawn for escort missions. Before this update you just had to avoid enemies but now that you can kill them, escort missions are much easier.<br><br>Secondly, this dlc addressed an issue that I had for a long time...too many workers in the temperate regions. Now, one could argue that it never hurts to have too many workers the problem is I liked to be challanged in planning my econmic and civic development. Before this dlc there was no thought involved in what modules to use. Energy was the obvious chose for factories and cost cutting ones was the choice for powerplants. Now I have to think and find myself quite often using drone modules to reduce workers in the temperate region. Additionally how many workers you get is tied to happiness. This gets rid of the idea that you can just upgrade your houses an additional tier even if you have no goods for the higher tier. Essentially tier 2 citizens with everything they need will have more workers than a tier 3 citizen with nothing that tier 3 citizens want. Again this makes you have to plan more carefully.<br><br>With all this they also increased the amount of goods your citizens consume. This may sound like a bad thing but again, it, like all other things is 100% optional. I actually like it for one simple reason. You see I have described anno as a business management game with a city-builder coat of paint. These changes make me feel more like a business than ever before. With workers consuming more goods themselves you make very little profit from your own people, but instead need to export and sell resources. That was an element I always found missing in 2205 but now it is here.<br><br>Another thing this update\/dlc brough with it were changes that reduced the grind greatly. Up until now you needed rare resources to expand your factories with modules. This meant having to constantly do the combat missions or wait around and hope that a competitor would offer you a mission with the resources you needed. Now you can have the option to build modules with standard building materials. So no longer do you have to prioritize completing the rare resouce sector projects or wait around for missions. You can actually be playing the game. I find myself upgrading all I have resources for in one region then jumping to another to further optimize and expand it and then keep going back and forth. It is a great option to have and it means that if you are like me and never really liked combat in Anno games you can even further avoid it and just focus on building and managing. This isn't to say rare resources are useless, you still need them for spaceport upgrades, bridges, tundra pumps and expansions to your starbases etc... However, the game has had the &quot;grind&quot; reduced greatly by this option.<br><br>Then there are the new regions...they are massive with lots of islands and their own sector projects. This greatly expands the game and the amount of content on offer. Also, you do not have to worry about the new system messing up your old saves. All changes it brings are optional with backups being created if you make changes after the dlc. This I find to be a huge deal because I had 70 hours in a game on the veteran difficulty as I was working towards that statue you get with 1 million investors. With the new changes to keep it on veteran my cities all collapsed within minutes as now litterally hundreds more goods where required in each city with my available workers reduced by roughly 100k. So yeah the fact that I was not forced to change the save game in order to keep it on veteran was nice. I wish more games made the changes to games after updates entirely optional. Granted if you want to start a new game on veteran you need to have the options enabled to be veteran, which is fine because you can build from the ground up knowing it and planning for it.<br><br>All in all, when you judge this dlc on its own merits it is really good. I know some people are upset about having to pay for it, and they have a right to their opinion, but in my opinion it was worth it. Before this dlc I had only 163 hours in Anno 2205, now I am up to 175 and it just released a few days ago. I see myself easily getting 100 more hours out of this game with the dlc. I had two previous saves with around 60-70 hours each and only made new games because more updates and dlcs came out. With this being the final dlc I will be able to actually finish it while going back to my old save on veteran to finish that. So essentially this is roughly a $12 dlc that will easily add over 100 hours to my gameplay time. Worth it! My only gripe with the dlc is that they didn't tell us about it in enough time. I was only able to get one day off work to play it :( Oh well taking one day off work to play it was worth it.
