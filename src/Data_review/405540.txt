(PC, PS4 &amp; PS Vita) was originally published on Excitehype<\/a><br><br>Over the past few years there have been a lot of 8-bit revival indie games. Some just have the appearance of a game from that era, others fit the mold perfectly and feel like they belong on cartridge (i.e. Shovel Knight, Undertow, Cave Story). Enter Tribute Games, who pride themselves in creating pixel art games based on the 8-bit and 16-bit era. I\u2019ve been a fan of these guys prior to them becoming Tribute Games with XBLA &amp; PSN release of Scott Pilgrim vs. The World: The Game from Ubisoft and their first official Tribute game Wizorb. They later brought the Metal Slug\/Contra metroidvania Mercenary Kings to PC and PlayStation consoles and followed up the next year with the arcade style 8-bit survival beat em up, Curses N\u2019 Chaos. So of course I was excited for the release of their next title, Ninja Senki DX. Much like Scott Pilgrim, the original Ninja Senki was a pre-Tribute game and was created by Tribute co-founder Jonathan Lavigne and released on PC as freeware back in 2010. So to celebrate the 5th anniversary of the game, Tribute decided to re-release the game on PC, PlayStation 4 and Vita and included an arranged soundtrack, a challenge mode, 2 secret modes, multiple endings and more.<br><br>Ninja Senki DX at it\u2019s core is an old school platformer that feels like something out of the Famicom\/Nintendo Entertainment System era regarding gameplay and difficulty. You play as Hayate a ninja set on revenge after his lady friend, Kinuhime was killed by a ninja demon. Simple enough, right? The game has you go through 16 stages filled with enemies that you take out with your Mega Bus\u2026.I mean Shurikens and various death traps which pretty much feels like a Mega Man game mixed with the fast action pace of the original Ninja Gaiden Trilogy. Much like those games, the controls are super tight and at times take precision to avoid certain death or to strategically take out your enemies. That\u2019s pretty much it gameplay wise. There are no secret powers you obtain or anything like that, it\u2019s a straight forward playthrough the stage and try to collect all Korban (coins) and kill all enemies as fast as you can for the best score. You have a boss fight every now and then but it\u2019s nothing special but that\u2019s okay. As simple as the game seems, it\u2019s challenging as hell and trust me, it will cause you to rage in certain areas of the game. (Especially the last boss fight)<br><br>It\u2019s definitely a title geared towards hardcore retro game fans and speed runners. Aside from replaying the main story at harder difficulty (hardcore mode), the only other replayability the game provides is the challenge mode and the 2 secret modes Ronin mode which allows you to play the story as one of the samurai enemies (Musashi) and boss rush. But comparing all that content to the price point ($5) it seems like a steal.<br><br>In closing, if you are looking for something to get your old school gaming or speed running fix ,especially if you are a fan of Mega Man, pick this one up. Ninja Senki DX is an awesome plate of retro goodness as well as the rest of Tribute\u2019s catalog. Ninja Senki DX is available for PC via Steam, PS4 and PS Vita.
