class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShadow Warrior (1997) is a 2.5D Build Engine FPS by 3D Realms, the same guys who made Duke Nukem 3D, which was also a 2.5D Build Engine FPS. Because of this, Shadow Warrior and Duke Nukem 3D have a lot in common. Like DN3D, Shadow Warrior has some pretty good old-school shooting, varied yet annoyingly mazy level design with a good sense of place, a nice range of weapons that's kinda unbalanced, lots of personality and little touches, pop-culture references, and weird attitudes towards women. 3D Realms clearly had a comfort zone in the mid-to-late 90s.<br><br>There are, however, some marked differences from Duke Nukem. The protagonist of Shadow Warrior is Lo Wang, a ridiculous Asian caricature. I say Asian because he's Chinese, but also a ninja, which is a Japanese profession. Now, I'm not saying that Chinese people can't be ninjas, but the main reason for that being the case here seems to be so that 3D Realms can hit every broad Asian stereotype possible. Lo Wang speaks broken Engrish, has a name that sounds like genitalia, brings up his nationality every 5 seconds, finds lots of fortune cookies, kills gassy sumo wrestlers, and keeps running into naked anime girls who are scattered around for no identifiable reason (at least Duke Nukem 3D had some flimsy pretense relating to mass kidnapping). However, despite all of this, he manages to be a fun character as well. He makes lots of puns (most of which revolve around his name, and a decent amount of which are actually funny), has some good one-liners <b> that aren't stolen from films, <\/b> and seems to be incredibly enthusiastic about everything, from finding a grenade launcher to slicing a guy in half to watching rabbits \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665. Also, he uses the heads and hearts of certain enemies as weapons, which is fun. He grows on you after a while, and though the racism is incorrigible, it doesn't feel as bad as it would otherwise.<br><br>The Build Engine has also been improved, mainly by the addition of sector-over-sector at last. However, I'm not sure why they bothered, because Quake had introduced full 3D the year before, and the entire industry was quickly shifting over to that. Still, it allows for some level design that wouldn't have been possible otherwise, so that's nice, I guess.<br><br>The gameplay has been improved from Duke Nukem 3D in a couple of ways. There are visible meters for air (when underwater) and boss health now, which is useful. Melee has also been made a more viable option, as Lo Wang's katana can insta-kill a few enemies and do serious damage to others. It rewards charging into the fray and hacking away like a maniac, which is fun every now and then. However, like Duke Nukem, Lo Wang dies pretty quickly, so melee rampages aren't always the best option. Additionally, primitive vehicles have been added, although they're annoying to steer and are only really used to open up a couple of pathways with their ability to shoot arbitrarily harder than you can.<br><br>Lo Wang's inventory items aren't as useful as Duke Nukem's, and I barely ever used them, with the obvious exception of the Medical Kit.<br><br>Some enemies are just terrible. There's a suicide bomber enemy that you can't bait into exploding prematurely, which would be annoying enough by itself. However, these enemies also have a good chance of spawning a creature when they die that floats around erratically, occasionally becomes invisible, and fires damaging projectiles, which is a \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665 to take care of. You can prevent the spawn by hacking at the suicide bomber's corpse with your sword, but it's still very irritating. Also, there are some bees that are like the Snarks from Half-Life, but are worse because they can fly, they don't die after a short period of time, and you can't have them for yourself. There's also a boss at the end of the shareware portion of the game that gets away, leading to a future encounter where you kill him, but after that he shows up again several times. What's the point of setting him up like that then?<br><br>You can't rebind any of the controls, which is annoying and additionally leaves four different buttons for activating the Medical Kit, three of which could have been used to make other controls more convenient.<br><br>The game seems a little harder overall than Duke Nukem 3D, which I guess makes sense considering that it's sort of a spiritual successor.<br><br>The levels are quite long.<br><br>It's annoying how your aim is shifted whenever you move onto a sloped surface, causing you to miss a lot.<br><br>Overall, Shadow Warrior is pretty good as 90s FPS go. It's fun, it has variety, its protagonist's enthusiasm is infectious, it improves on Duke Nukem 3D in several ways, and it's clearly doing its own thing, which is always admirable. It has its share of annoying parts, but they don't get in the way of the good stuff most of the time. I recommend it.<br><br>EDIT: I played the classic version, not the one with the slightly upgraded visuals, and it's a bit unstable.
