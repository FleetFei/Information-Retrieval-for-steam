game, set in an office where you play as one of the many workers who have just been told they are being made redundant. The game was released in December 2015, being developed by Black Lime Studio, who has previously had some not so popular releases on Steam. <br><br>The game play is a very simple idea. You are in control of one worker and your boss has come up with a great idea to let everyone fight for their jobs. You pick up the nearest weapon in the office, don on your keyboard as a shield and of course, reach for the cardboard box in the corner as you step out into the hall ready for battle with your co-workers. You are rewarded with experience and gold for taking down your foes, as well as completing the stage. There are a couple of different stage modes you can take part in, these are a last man standing mode, a domination mode, and a survival mode.  The game\u2019s talent spending system is really easy to get your head around too, only two talent trees to learn, meaning you can pick and choose which ability to aid you in your current situation. The in game shop is where you will spend a lot of your time, as you try and buy the most recent new weapon or piece of armour to take into fight. <br><br>Visually, Office Battle is again, very simple. No mind blowingly good graphics here, just your generic looking cartoony style game. It does look better than your standard game though, with it on part with I would say a few Sims games. The office furniture is well modelled as well as quite a few unique character models throughout.  The animation is where this game is let down slightly; the game\u2019s hitboxes are a little bit off making positioning a little difficult at times. The UI in game is clean and easy to understand too.  A really cool addition to this game, is the sound track, some very high tempo upbeat club mix soundtrack is in the background as you run around the halls of the office. Whilst it doesn\u2019t quite make sense totally, it does fit the game play really well as it keeps you on your toes without you realizing it. <br><br>The usual levels offer no real difficulty to get through, where Office Battle really shows some challenge, is on the bosses. The first boss is a real challenge and forced me to replay the last level probably about 10 times in order for me to grind up enough levels and gold in order to just overpower him. A mistake I made was making my character too defensibly minded; I think it would have proved a better move to go offensive right from the start with the talent points. Whilst I am not a huge fan of a game forcing you to replay previously completed content, I don\u2019t feel so bad doing it here; I think it\u2019s because the stages last no longer than 2 and a half minutes usually and the rewards are often worth the time it takes anyway. <br><br>There is a substantial amount of content on offer in Office Battle; I have been playing for almost two hours now and I am only about 1\/3 of the way through the main game. There is a free roam style mode but this offers no real benefit other than \u201cjust for fun\u201d as you don\u2019t get any experience or rewards out of it. Whether there is much replay ability, remains to be seen. If things carry on the way they are so far, I don\u2019t see there being any reason to play through this game more than once unfortunately. <br><br>Technically, there are a few issues here and there. Firstly the game will run at 1080p as a maximum. Whilst that is the PC standard at the moment, a 1440p or 4k option would of even better especially since they are becoming more popular formats.  Secondly, the game claims to have partial controller support, sadly neither my Xbox nor Steam controller worked at all. The keyboard controls are really weird too; you control your character with the directional arrow keys and you punch\/kick with WASD. Personally this is completely wrong, it would be much better to control the character with WASD and attack with the mouse. It makes navigating the character very tricky at times and I got stuck behind office furniture more times than I would like to admit due to the controller problems. <br><br>Despite the technical gripes I had with the game, I can\u2019t really complain too much since it is priced ridiculously low at only 79p. If you want an evening\u2019s worth of fun for less than a pound, then Office Battle will certainly do that. An unforgettable tale of isolation, survival and getting your \u2665\u2665\u2665 handed to you by your 70 year old boss. All joking aside, Office Battle is a Beat \u2018Em Up\/RPG where you play as a customizable character and survive level after level of fighting your co-workers. You have a button to punch with each hand and one to kick while weapons can be picked up once an enemy randomly drops one or you purchase it.<br><br>Weapons range from newspapers to belts and other out of place things though they all handle the same and have the same reach and animations so you'll be sticking to whichever causes most damage. Other than weapons you can also pick up shields and protection for your head such as trashcans and caskets. Sadly it all amounts to nothing but boredom regardless of how wacky this all sounds since the combat itself is extremely sub-par. This plays akin to something like Diablo where positioning and timing are nonexistent as you keep clicking the attack button like a madman. Aside from the few levels that require you to capture certain points of the map there is no reason to move your character other than finding a choke point to easier deal with the enemies and avoid getting surrounded.<br><br>You cannot dodge or get out of reach of attacks, once an enemy is an attacking animation it will hit you regardless of how far away you are. There is nothing in the levels like health pickups or anything of that sort, all items randomly fall from defeated foes and the AI always run directly to where you are as soon as they spawn. They always spawn near you and even right behind you occasionally. So no enemy placement, no items around the level and still taking damage from attacks that struck several feet away makes this game feel like an unholy combination of both the Beat \u2018Em Up and RPG genres worst aspects. Whether you win or lose ultimately comes down to luck of item drops and how high your level is instead of any skill on your part. Well it does the same damage as punching, is slow to use and range is the same, not that it matters in this title. It could have been used for staggering an enemy or something of that nature but it is just an inferior attack that you'll likely never use in Office Battle. Defeating foes and completing missions grant you both money and experience which can be used to buy items and to level up your skill tree. In the main menu there is a store where you can purchase said weapons and gear to take into a mission. You can also sell whatever gear you picked up from missions for cash. Weapons do break down so the benefit of buying them is that they are in 100% condition as such will take longer for it to break in the middle of fighting. Protection for the head and shields wear down from taking damage so constantly replacing your gear is necessary if that is the way you built your character.<br><br>Leveling up grants 1 skill point that allows you to upgrade one of the abilities in the skill tree such as regenerating health, better hand to hand and weapon usage increases to name a few. There is a skill for blocking which oddly enough cannot be activated, instead works like an RPG where it's pure chance if it activates. There are 4 types of missions. Survival where you must stay alive for a certain amount of time and Last Man Standing where you must defeat a certain amount of enemies. They feel pretty much the same to be quite honest. Office Capture is the one mode where you must move since you have to take control of three circles scatted on the map but once you are there it's back to being stationary and mashing the attack buttons so not a huge departure. Finally we have Big B missions that has you facing your old boss in a fight.<br><br>Big B missions are without a doubt the worst part of the game. The old man has a ton of health and packs quite a punch making it so you can only beat these missions once you grind enough since skill isn't a factor in this game. It's not a one on one affair either, you will still be under attack by the endlessly spawning coworkers and with no way to refill your health other than the random pickup they drop beating the Big B missions require luck as well as being high leveled. There is an editor where you can create levels which is sadly ultimately pointless considering you will just head to a choke-point and not see anything else like in the developer's levels due to the nature of the gameplay. This game has partial controller support so you will need your mouse to navigate menus and you will have to bind the keys before being able to use it.<br><br>Another thing I would like to mention is that the games highest resolution is 1920x1200 which is less than ideal for someone with a higher resolution but a minor annoyance overall. It's not a serious game by any means so if you want to have a green skinned, freak of nature type of character you can. Also the physics are pretty hilarious and landing a critical hit will send them flying as if this were Dragon Ball Z or something. The music is pretty solid though the loop is a bit on the short side. Most importantly I feel it's important to remember that this game is just 99 cents (or national equivalent) and it is stuffed with content.
