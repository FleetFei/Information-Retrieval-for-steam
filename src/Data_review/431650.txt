Home<br><b>Status:<\/b>  Released<br><b>Developer:<\/b>  ION LANDS<br><b>Publisher:<\/b>  ION LANDS<br><b>Genre:<\/b>  3D Adventure<br><b>Release Date:<\/b>  7th of February, 2017<br><b>Type:<\/b>  Singleplayer <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">General Impression<\/div>When I first saw screenshots of this title, I was expecting it to be just another cute robot-themed game that\u2019s geared predominantly towards a younger audience. Phoning Home\u2019s mechanized protagonist sure looks a lot like WALL-E at first glance yet the similarities end there. ION is a far more resourceful robot and even braver than most Star Wars droids. Between repairing his crash-landed ship, protecting his fragile friend ANI and contacting his homeworld, the players will guide our hero through an equally fascinating and perilous journey.  Hard to believe that such a polished and refreshingly authentic game, is the Steam debut of a dev team that has had no previous experience on the platform. I guess I became almost numb from so much mediocrity within recent indie games, that I forgot Steam can still offer a surprise every now and then.<br><br>While ION\u2019s forte certainly ain\u2019t combat, he\u2019s more than capable of outsmarting his few (but deadly) foes on this new strange planet he\u2019s stranded on. Even more tragically, the subsequent crash not only left ION without the means of communicating with his base of operations but also separated him from ANI, another robot and his only palpable friend since the AI guiding him along the way is hardly the company he so desperately needs. So \u201cphoning home\u201d is a priority, but ensuring ANI\u2019s structural integrity is also paramount. A complex but still intuitive crafting system, plays a central role within the title\u2019s gameplay since players shall have to produce everything from the repair modules, ship components and even fuel for the robots to press forward. Using the individual thrusters may help speed up the resource gathering and exploration parts a lot, but they shall take a toll on ION and particularly on ANI. <br><br>Fortunately, the developer understood the frustration most gamers have when trying to protect a non-combatant ally and thus, ANI can be ordered to stop or follow at the players' discretion. Enemies aren\u2019t in large supply but whether we\u2019re talking of giant golems, abstract lava creatures or flying dinosaurs (pterosaurs), ION is far better off simply avoiding them than to engage directly. Especially with the intial weapon that is just a pipe. So Phoning Home feels more natural as you roam the faily large open levels in search for new basic resources from which you shall craft more complex ones. It doesn\u2019t drag on or imply any degree of boredom, but I was still annoyed by the constant backtracking to the crash site. At least dynamic weather effects plus day\/night cycles are also implemented and the game itself is beautifully textured and designed. Fields filled with benign life forms such as huge butterflies or flowers are a stark contrast to the barren wastelands you shall also explore. From volcanic to arctic landscapes and even abandoned structures in the desert that clearly point out to previous civilizations. If you didn\u2019t have a near constant companion with you, it would have really been a bleak exploration game, such as Lifeless Planet.<br><br>Phoning Home manages to simulate the stress and perils of being stranded in a foreign place, but since out protagonist never loses hope and even manages to escape, the game most certainly has the happy ending it deserves. From a graphical perspective, eye candy\u2019s aplenty and the sounds + voice acting are also of high quality and diversified enough. The slow-pace given by ION\u2019s own lack of speed is a game design choice which might not be very popular but still doesn\u2019t warrant the far too many negative reviews this title has been receiving. Achievements (including ones that imply you must never heal) and Trading Cards sweeten the deal but the price tag might still be a bit too high. If that\u2019s the case for you, wait for a Steam Sale or bundle and you won\u2019t regret purchasing and playing Phoning Home. Come for the exploration and crafting and stay for the story. ION and ANI depend on you!<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Strong Points<\/div>+ Beautiful graphics and sounds.<br>+ Interesting storyline and setting.<br>+ Excellent mix of crafting and exploration.<br>+ Steam Trading Cards &amp; Achievements.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Weak Points<\/div>- Slow-paced gameplay also involving backtracking.<br>- Slightly overpriced.<br><br><b>Rating 85\/100 <\/b><br> largely beginner mistakes.<br><br>The premise and presentation is interesting. You play as an explorer robot named ION who crashlands on a planet that is rich with resoures and you need to survive and find a way to phone home(roll credits). The game certainly has personality, the character designs are pretty neat, a cross between Wall-E, Star Wars and ET most of the time. Your ship has a personality that brings a lot of Portal characters to mind and while not entirely original, it is one of the better takes on the humorless, business centered, arrogant robot companion archetypes and he is one of the games highlights. Especially when you find and repair another ship with a completely opposite personality and the 2 starts bickering. The companion robot, ANI whom you find later is a bit cliche'd. A feminine talkatative and somewhat impish robot who instantly falls in love with you. imagine if Wall-E was female and EVA male and that's pretty much their releationship. except ION is silent. At least they have an in-game excuse for this in that his speech chip is supposedly damaged, but still. Seeing ANI make several attempts at romanticising someone who can't even respond was initially cute, but after a while it became almost sad. overall, pretty good setup and characters.<br><br>Now the gameplay is a mixed bag.  it IS a survival game so crafting and maintenance galore. the game isn't too stingy with crafting items and you do have a compass that shows you nearby items. ION isn't too hard to maintain either, even though he has 3 needs, fuel for rocket thrusters, power for sprinting, using weapons and staying alive and hull damage, maintaining them isn't too hard, they last a decent amount of time and items for restoring them are fairly abundant. i haven't had any issues there yet. however, when you find ANI, you not only have to repair her when damaged(no power or fuel needs, thank god) but she does corrode and you do need to make coatings for her to prevent that. and you want to because if she corrodes, her movement slows to a crawl and to my knowledge you can't undo corrision! and she doesn't do enough to warrant your attention. she can fight, but other than that, she is basically an escort mission. she can't teleport to you if she falls too far back, if a sandstorm appears. you need to find shelter or constantly craft coatings for her and you need her to progress. why can't she find random crafting items for you, perhaps give you fuel or power or teleport closer if she gets too far away, just something to make her more self reliant. how could she ever think she could even survive on the planet, if she was smart she would have stayed on her ship.<br><br>Combat is fairly basic, fire a relatively slow moving projectile at usually slow moving enemies, movement is pretty good overall, the thruster is a bit wonky as it only seems to add upward momentum, aka don't use it if sliding because you will keep your momentum, a forward thruster would be great could have speed up the slow walking too. which reminds me, the walking is slow. it feels like the world is large and characters slow specifically to pad out the gameplay. note to indie devs, don't do that. most pc gamers already have a library filled with unfinished games due to a lack of time to play them. a short and sweet game is much better than a long sluggish one. fortunately, the devs are aware and have increased sprint speed slightly, but ideally there should be an upgrade or a forward thruster that significantly speeds you up. <br><br>The gameplay has a good framework but isn't handled as well as it should. i'd love to see a sequel or a redux where all of these issues are fixed because there really is a good survival game hidden in there somewhere. <br><br><br>as far as graphics and audio go, there's no issues. i've heard people complain about it being hard to run, i'm rocking a gtx 1080 and playing at 4K max with a near locked 60fps so i don't see any issues. but i'm also rocking the 2nd best video card right now period so. graphics themselves are well polished. not the best of the bunch, but art is quite coherent and some particles really impress. sound effects are also very pleasant, impressively so for an indie title. now graphics options are fairly basic. so is input options. gamepad support is shoehorned in there and just kinda works. it plays best with M\\K.<br><br><br>I think in summary, this is a potential indie gem that has it's appeal already, but a few dumb decisions, especially mid game, holds it back. Fortunately, the beginning 2 hours is a good taste of what the game has to offer so you can safely refund quite fast if you don't quite feel like it's up your alley. The devs have done a good job listening to feedback and patching, but i think a redux of sorts is needed to really leverage the game's potential. it's a recommend from me, but a cautious  one.
