I dare say that there is almost a Skyrim level of content here, as far as exploration and secrets go. Not only are the locations, puzzles, and monsters more varied.. They've also GREATLY improved the monsters' AI, to the point where it's DOWN RIGHT SCARY how intelligent they can be. Kiting around monsters is still an important aspect to combat, but it's no longer an easy-win strategy, by any means.<br>There's also a few non-standard enemies, who's AI seems to be on a whole separate level from the rest.. They soon find out, however, that they are not alone and to make it off the island alive they have to overcome the challenges devised by the ominous mastermind of the island.<br><br>After playing around 10 hours <i>(and counting)<\/i> of Legend of Grimrock 2 I can tell you without the shadow of a doubt that the game has managed to take everything that I loved from the first Legend of Grimrock and somehow make it better. Almost Human Games have given us extra flexibility and variety when creating and setting up our party with new races, classes, abilities and perks, an expansive and open world to explore and survive in with a full day and night cycle, new multi-tiered zones ranging from tropical shorelines to rat-infested mines, lush jungles, to creepy graveyards, overgrown ruins to long forgotten tombs, ancient temples to poisonous swamps and then the freedom to move between them all at will and with ease. The game's engine handles the great outdoors wonderfully, too, and it all looks much better than I thought possible for a game of this type. There are now more secrets, puzzles, riddles and items than ever before and then, most importantly, the same great, addictive and thoroughly challenging old school style of gameplay and combat. Progression through the main plot of the game is done in a non-linear fashion that rewards players that take their time, explore and immerse themselves fully.<br><br>Grimrock 2 also features a built-in Dungeon Editor that seems a lot of fun. I've only briefly tested it but in the 15 minutes I spent using it I was able to create an actual dungeon I could walk around in. I plan on sinking some time into making my own content for the game once I've completed it. <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Well, Is it?<\/div><br>All of that said and after playing an unhealthy amount of the game over the past couple of days I can say with much surity that <b>Legend of Grimrock 2 is well worth your time and definitely worthy of a grand adventure!<\/b> This game will appeal to long-time fans of the series, and now almost extinct genre, and newcomers alike. Although, it must be said, both of the aforementioned groups will still find themselves struggling with some of the enemies, challenges, puzzles and riddles presented to them during their time on the Isle of Nex.<br><br>Check my likes and dislikes below!<br><br>Legend of Grimrock 2 was developed and published by Almost Human Games and is the sequel to 2012's Legend of Grimrock. Legend of Grimrock 2 has a total of five races, eight classes and a large number of abilities, traits and more. You get to create a much more varied and fun-to-play party.<br><br> <\/li><li><u>Freedom<\/u> - After a brief introduction area you are thrown into a incredibly (for this type of game) large open world to explore at your own pace. This game is as open world and free as a first person dungeon crawler and grid based game can be and it works great. Is one particularly challenging puzzle or fight getting you down? No problem! Just leave it for later and try something else in the mean time. You get to go where you want, do what you want, fight what you want and loot what you want when you want. <br><br> <\/li><li><u>The Great Outdoors<\/u> - While playing through the first Legend of Grimrock I couldn't help but wonder what it would be like, and look like, if we could actually exit the dungeon and see the great outdoors... Wish granted! As I said above in Legend of Grimrock 2 we get to explore tropical shorelines, lush jungles, creepy graveyards, overgrown ruins, , ancient temples, poisonous swamps, long forgotten tombs, dungeons and mines, the beds of rivers with actual water in them (yes, underwater exploration is now a thing... see below.) and much more.<br><br> <\/li><li><u>More Depth<\/u> - The level design in Legend of Grimrock 2 has been improved upon greatly and levels can now be multi-tiered both indoors and outdoors. Why is this important? It all just looks that much better and feels that much more expansive when exploring. Makes for great, and confusing, puzzles!<br><br> <\/li><li><u>Day \/ Night Cycle<\/u> - You thought walking around a dungeon or mine was scary in the dark? Try a cemetary, or a poisonous swamp or even the bed of a river you just accidentally fell into... Night time in Legend of Grimrock 2 is great and terrifying all at the same time. <br><br> <\/li><li><u>Secrets<\/u> - There's always something more to find out there, trust me. With the game now being as open world as it is there are just so many more opportunities for hidden caches of treasure or secret areas than ever before and it truly is satisfying uncovering all the no doubt long-lost secrets. <br><br> <\/li><li><u>The Challenge<\/u> - Legend of Grimrock 2 is not easy. Even on the easiest difficulty setting the game can be brutal at times and I personally think it is for the better. Whether you're being teleported into a room full of face-eating zombies or whether like five swarms of killer wasps are spawning on top of you... It is always an exhilirating and ultimately rewarding experience to try escape with your party, and sanity, in tact. <br><br> <\/li><li><u>A Better Map<\/u> - In a game with as many secrets, hidden areas, puzzles and riddles as this it really, really helps to be able to put your thoughts down somewhere before losing them and the updated map is really great for that. The new visual style of it and the fact that you can have the all of the sprawling open world visible in one place is also kinda great. <br><br> <\/li><li><u>The Sound<\/u> - The Legend of Grimrock 2 sound and soundtrack is just absolutely brilliant. I honestly don't understand how they do it. I recommend playing the game with a headset turned up nice and loud. Also, the main theme.... Chills! Every time I listen to it.... CHILLS!<br><br> <\/li><li><u>The Dungeon Editor \/ Custom Dungeons<\/u> - SO GOOD! Even a map-making newbie like myself can actually throw something relatively decent together in no time at all and then, conveniently, share it on the Steam Workshop!<\/li><\/ul><b><u>Dislikes:<\/u><\/b><br><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li><u>The Island Master<\/u> - He's a real jerk.
