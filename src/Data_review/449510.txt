class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\t<div class=\"bb_h1\">Basic Information<\/div><b>Title:<\/b>  The Crow's Eye<br><b>Status:<\/b>  Released<br><b>Developer:<\/b>  3D2 Entertainment<br><b>Publisher:<\/b>  Nkidu Games Inc.<br><b>Genre:<\/b>  3D Adventure<br><b>Release Date:<\/b>  20th of March, 2017<br><b>Type:<\/b>  Singleplayer <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Introduction<\/div>I\u2019ll start by stating very clearly that The Crow\u2019s Eye is every other genre you can imagine, but definitely not Horror. In the several hours of playtime I have accumulated so far (and reached the end of the story), I wasn\u2019t startled or scared by anything this game has thrown at me. No jump scares, no implied terror or even tension at the very least. You have to be aware of this and if you can come to terms with a relatively toothless \u201catmospheric horror\u201d as it is being marketed, you might enjoy playing it. I sure did, despite the mislabeling. The game represents the Steam debut of its developer and I am pleased by their first project.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Story <\/div>While I never make a habit out of spoiling the storyline or even subplots in any of the games I review, I can safely offer you some small but crucial details, which you may learn anyways within the first 30 minutes of actually playing The Crow\u2019s Eye. The title is clearly linked to the location you\u2019ll be assiduously exploring throughout the game: Crowswood University. I could draw several stylistic and plot similarities to BioShock 1. Rapture is also seemingly abandoned at first glance and things quickly take a turn for the worse as you arrive. Crowswood has William Holtwick as main antagonist. A mad and equally deadly scientist in the vein of Andrew Ryan. <br><br>Both aforementioned titles feature silent protagonists whose identities are only revealed near the end of their stories. I have finished the game and I can point out to story or visual style influences from gaming cult classics such as The Suffering series and the good ol\u2019 Call of Cthulhu: Dark Corners of the Earth. In other words, you\u2019re preparing for a very gripping storyline filled with challenges and unsettling characters.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Graphics<\/div>Sadly, the graphical details cannot keep up the pace or level of polish, present within the gameplay mechanics. I am always pleased to see the Unity 5 Engine being stretched to its limits by ambitious developers, but the textures and assets used in The Crow\u2019s Eye felt both overused and lacking in sufficient details. Even when running the game maxed out, the textures weren\u2019t very crisp and their patterns could be found on all the game\u2019s levels. Gameplay repetitiveness only gets exacerbated by the constant use of just a few assets. <br><br>Even more weird was the resolution option which never seemed to change as far as my screenshots were reporting. Screen resolution was thus unchanged and just the game objects were affected by the switch from 1920x1080 to 2560x1440 or 3840x2160 options. Frame rate was all over the place when running both the screen and object resolution at full 4K. Some performance tweaks or patches are quite mandatory in this case. Overall, the game does look decent but nowhere near as pretty as the standards for quality 3D indie titles in 2017 demand.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Audio<\/div>At least you\u2019re in for a treat in the audio department. Top notch soundtrack, which can also be purchased separately on Steam and contains 33 tracks ranging in style from smooth jazz to eerie instrumentals. In-game use of the OST is spot on, as some level sections feature dream\/nightmare sequences that make good use of songs not typically found  in titles within the  horror or thriller genres. The really pleasant surpise though, was the voice acting. <br><br>For a game in which you rarely interact directly with other characters, at least their spoken words shall guide, tease, mock and attempt to trick the protagonist in a skilled manner, from both the pronunciation and voice tone perspectives. Hearing Holtwick\u2019s self-righteous drivel and incessant laughter throughout most levels, he reminded me of Mark Hamill and his brilliant performance as The Joker in the Arkham video games or the Batman animated series.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay<\/div>Pure 3D Puzzle Platformer with the occasional creepy atmosphere which I shall summarize as \u201c3D Adventure\u201d. Can\u2019t really call it Horror since you\u2019ll only witness 4 direct threats to your health during the entire playtime. Three of those potentially deadly instances contain some type of facehugger\/bug that you need shake off your head through rapid movement of the mouse peripheral. The fourth and my favorite such tense moment involves, finding an antidote for your unexpected poisoning within the time frame of less than two minutes.<br><br>As you can imagine, such examples of unexpected action from a game that has you wandering countless rooms and corridors, provide well deserved distractions for a fairly repetitive core gameplay. The Crowswood University once hosted students of Medicine but now it\u2019s a near literal ghost town. You\u2019ll be constantly on a fetch quest for one object or another. A crafting system is implemented and thus you may combine various inventory items into, bandages, lockpicks, maps or a powerful electromagnet which will prove crucial to late game puzzle sections. <br><br>Even without a looming threat per se, the game does its best to offer a creepy ambience. It didn\u2019t really click for me, but your mileage may vary.  The puzzles you\u2019ll need to solve are intuitive and increase in difficulty as your character also descends ever closer to madness. Alas, I found them diversified enough and with just the right amount of challenge to boot. The presence of so many bandages and static save game stations, tricked me into thinking that this title might feature combat of some sort. That expectation never materialized, unfortunately.<br><br> <div class=\"bb_h1\">Verdict<\/div>The Crow\u2019s Eye shares more of its gaming DNA to the Portal series than Silent Hill, for example. If you\u2019re okay with that, the game can be quite entertaining albeit misleading from its current Steam Store description. You have no foes to face head-on and I won\u2019t spoil you the ending, but suffice to say it offers a cliffhanger large enough that I expect a sequel eventually. And I do hope The Crow\u2019s Eye fares well with its potential buyers and players. If you would judge the developer just from the perspective of a Steam newcomer, you would be impressed by what they achieved with their first and so far only, released project. For what it offers, I consider it more than decently priced.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Strong Points<\/div>+ Challenging puzzles.<br>+ Captivating storyline.<br>+ Excellent voice acting and soundtrack.<br>+ Steam Trading Cards &amp; Achievements.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Weak Points<\/div>- Doesn\u2019t quite live up to the promised \u201cpsychological terror\u201d.<br>- Level design
