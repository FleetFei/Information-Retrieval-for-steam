II- Jedi Outcast is pretty cool, I guess. <\/div> <br>You play as Kyle Katarn, a name that carries as much weight in the legacy universe of Star Wars as Luke Skywalker or Han Solo or Gartogg. <br><br>Sent out to investigate the Imperial Remnants of several planets, Kyle, and his partner, Jan, get caught up in a plan by the Remnants that leads to pain, adventure, mystery, and cool lightsaber fights.<br><br>Jedi Outcast came out in 2002 when it was hip to make everything on the Quake III engine. And when it came out it looked really good. It still does, though you need to fiddle with the options just to not make it look all wonky on your new intergalactic space computer. <br><br>As Kyle, the coolest guy with the lamest name, you get a variety of weapons. Outside of the lightsaber that even the original game box advertised back in the day, you also get blasters, bowcasters, rocket launchers, space shotguns, space sniper rifles, thermo detonators, trip mines, and even that little orb thing that shot look in the original movie. <br><br>The shooting is nice, all of the blasters have the iconic Star Wars sound effects. They're crunchy, fun to fire, and since most of them have a delay like before hitting their target like a blaster in Star Wars would, you usually have to predict where your opponent is going to move before you fire.<br><br>While the shooting is fun, the lightsaber begins to take center stage as your powers grow and you become one of those intergalactic murder hobos known as a Jeddy. Not only can you murder fools with the space knife but you can also block incoming attacks and throw it like a very dangerous boomerang. Like the one from Road Warrior but not thrown by that kid with autism. <br><br>There's a big power trip in just clearing a room with your blue stick, dodging or deflecting incoming blasters, throwing people around with force powers, and leaping over fools before cutting them down. <br><br>The levels in single player are impressively large. Each area is unique considering most places you go to are in different parts of the galaxy from the last. \u201cAbandoned\u201d outposts, mining facilities, crime city, and Lando's crib are places you will visit on your journey to hit people with your laser sword. <br><br>\u201cThat's fine and all.\u201d You tell me as you kick me in the junk. \u201cBut tell me about the really cool stuff.\u201d <br><br>That was very rude of you.<br><br>Well if a really tight first person shooter with cool third person combat and sword fighting doesn't appeal to you then check this out. Jedi Outcast was released at a time when Modding was at a all time high. I remember about a year after it's release someone made a total conversion mod that was a single level where you play as a Gladiator in Rome. Not only was it kind of neat, but it was actually pretty educational as well.<br><br>Not only that, but there are skin packs, like one that returns Kyle to his original appearance in Dark Forces that was released in 1995. Fan made gameplay tweaks exist as well. If that doesn't tickle your pickle then there are also an assortment of console commands that can add replay value or just flat out break your game.<br><br>One console command that stands out actually increases dismemberment from your lightsaber swings, adding things such as cutting off heads or flat out bisecting people depending on high you set the value. I feel like the original idea was to always be able to dismember people, but maybe Lucas Arts didn't allow anything other than hands or arms? I don't know, I don't actually have any info on that.<br><br>But how friggin cool is that? You just type out a console command, slap in a few numbers, and suddenly you have the most brutal Star Wars game ever made. <br><br>So what else is there to cover? <br><br>Multiplayer, duh. Pay attention.<br><br>So I went into multiplayer with the intent of screwing around with some bots and covering the game modes. But to my surprise I actually found a few people still playing? <br><br>Back when I first played in 2004, I went into multiplayer ready to smash heads and immediately found a bunch of people just kind of standing around and chatting while other people fought in makeshift arenas around the map.<br><br>There were a handful of servers that focused on deathmatch, team deathmatch, etc, but a good majority of them were about dueling. Jedi Outcast's lightsaber combat, which was mostly about positioning, movement, and timing, was just complex enough to gather a cult following for dueling. <br><br>I remember challenging someone to a duel, then bowing before the fight begins, predating a certain other games dueling community by a few years. But back then it was fun to just sometimes sit around and chat with others, or jump and flip around the map. <br><br>There were clan wars that would break out on servers, rivalries that got pretty intense at some points. Jedi Outcast's multiplayer back in the day was something special, and finding it so empty actually made me feel...Sad, I guess. <br><br>I got a chance to talk to the 5 or so people that were actually playing at the time, with one person saying that he has been playing since 2004. I didn't stay around to duel, but I did ask a few questions.<br><br>They said it was weird to be part of a such a super niche community, remnants of what was a rather large nation back in the day. But the dedication of who was left was something to admire.<br><br>It's sad to see the multiplayer the way it is now. The last members of the Jedi Outcast community are an endangered species of true fans, even if there's only a few of them left. But hey, Jedi Outcast had it's run, it had a few years of fame and countless hours of good memories between thousands of players. And all good things have to come to an end, right? <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"> Should you play Jedi Knight II: Jedi Outcast? <\/div><br>The single player still holds up, coming out at a time before Disney decided to throw away thousands of stories in the galaxy far far away. The combat and exploration are still fun, even though you may get so lost in the early levels that you may as well set up camp and play campfire songs.  <br><br>But Jedi Outcast's multiplayer was one of it's big draws in the early 00s. It was special and stood out from many of the other games that were out at the time. But hey, I know a lot of you still have Jedi Outcast sitting around, either in a jewel case or on your Steam library. It's never going to revive, I'm not that optimistic. But if you ever feel like just reinstalling, if just for a little bit, maybe stop by one of the servers and say hi to who's left, maybe stop for a duel or two. While you still can. <br><br>Star Wars: Jedi Knight II- Jedi Outcast gets a score of 9 in a galaxy far far away out of 10. <br><br><br>Oh man, that last part really bummed me out. <br><br>The Curator:
