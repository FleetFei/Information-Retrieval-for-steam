open-world adventure teeming with things to do. The excitement can give way to boredom because the short story can\u2019t hide the required grind to advance. Still, there is a plethora of different dusty environments and strongholds \/ outposts, while the detailed landscape is full of things to do and discover.<br>Total size on my hard drive: 32.8 GB<br><br>++ Optimization is stunning. One of the best optimized games of the past year or two.<br><br>+ Graphics are great. Light, dust, haze is a great icing on a highly detailed cake.  Clouds look a little low-res at times, but the skybox looks fantastic in general (if you like sunsets \/ sunrises, there are some great screenshot opportunities).<br>+ Punchy sound design. Explosions have great impact.<br>+ Great story - gritty and grim - but it\u2019s short. Do yourself a favor and complete the Wasteland side-missions in addition to the main campaign. You\u2019ll get to know the factions a bit better and the game feels more complete.<br>+ The wasteland is wonderfully detailed. Rocks, caves, forts, pumping stations, strongholds, outposts \u2013 even though it is a dusty wasteland, there is plenty of visual variety.<br>+ Customizing the car. From the body style, to the suspension, engine, armor, and more, you can really make the car you want to play the way you want.<br>+ All the unfinished missions and quests return after finishing the main story campaign so you can take your time, explore the wasteland, and 100% the game if you want.<br><br>+\/- The hand-to-hand combat is a riff on the Batman Arkham combat system with fancy finishers but without gadgets. It works well enough, but nothing outstanding about it.  (If keyboard &amp; mouse is your preference, be sure to have a 3-button mouse.)<br>+\/- The game is a grind for scrap, which is used for much-needed upgrades. If you hate grinding, walk away. If you enjoy exploration and looting, then Mad Max is an amazing experience.<br>+\/- Child slavery content warning. There isn\u2019t much, but it is there.<br><br>- Checkpoint saving is the primary method of storing progress. Awkward in a sandbox game. You can save the game from the menu, but it does not hard-save the last checkpoint; it saves Max to the nearest fast-travel location. Not very useful.<br>- Driving mechanics are sub-par; floaty and imprecise, the smallest bump can send the car flying, leaving Max facing the wrong way in seconds as the enemy speeds off into the distance. Not good in a game that focuses so much on driving. The Gastown race almost made me rage-quit the game.<br>- Enemies are endlessly re-used.<br>- Grinding is required. Upgrades are sometimes hidden behind completely clearing a region of threats. <br>- Glitchy menus. Hitting Escape to view a new log entry would frequently result in seeing the menu headings, but no images or text. Switching to another menu then switching back would usually fix the issue.<br>- Messed up mission progression. Need to build the \u201cJack\u201d, a special class of car referred to as an Archangel. Well, the next part of the mission wouldn\u2019t trigger, no matter what I did. Checked online. As it happens, I had parts on the car that were a level HIGHER than what was required to progress. I had to un-equip all upgrades on the car, and re-equip only the upgrades the game told me to, and the rest of the mission triggered properly.<br>- Some quests that were supposedly optional disappeared without warning because I chose to continue with the main story first. All the missions return for completion after finishing the main campaign, though, so there\u2019s that.<br><br>It was a challenge to bring Mad Max to an open world game, and Avalanche delivered an incredibly gritty, detailed world and a mature story. While the story itself is not open-ended, there is clearly a chance for sequels - with possible franchise potential. Fans of Mad Max and open-world explore-and-loot games should give it a try \u2013 especially since it now discounts during Steam sales to $10 or less. There\u2019s a lot of value here.
