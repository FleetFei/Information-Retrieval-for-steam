and polish all work nicely to make Vran stand out. Players will find themselves wall jumping to find secrets and dodge rolling to avoid attacks all with the comfort of smooth directional input (WASD or controller).  Though Vran has loot (graded equipment with random qualities), sprawling dungeons, and grinding like many traditional ARPGs, a fair amount of the game has a tinge of Bastion to it. <br><br>For those who have not yet played Bastion, it is a whimsical ARPG that skips loot in favor of loadouts where weapons determine play style, difficulty can be shaped by modifiers, equipment is handled by a handful of set items, special abilities are separate from weapons, and character progression involves opening more ways to tinker with these aspects. Victor Vran follows Bastion fairly close in these areas, but with some great added twists in addition to loot. <br><br>Every weapon uses a kit of three abilities and powerful special skills use an \u201coverdrive\u201d meter which can be filled a few different ways. The weapons are finely designed: each type feels fun, unique, and balanced, so choosing any of the bunch is satisfying. The kits are engaging and reward players for stringing abilities or timing attacks carefully rather than button mashing. The game also tries to mitigate the burn of carrying favored-but-weaker equipment by letting players break down gear to boost weapons within limit. Despite the drastic visual shift, outfits mainly change how overdrive generates; the more interesting parts to tinker with are destiny cards. These cards, equipped by using \u201cdestiny points\u201d from a pool, offer everything from more damage to weapon specialization, and like weapons they act as loot. Once the basic mechanics become second nature, picking the right equipment for the job is a lot of fun. <br><br>Victor Vran\u2019s amount of build freedom houses a lot \tof potential for players, but comes at a cost. Character progression suffers a bit in order to allow players to tinker freely. After a few levels, leveling up is underwhelming because it only grants health or destiny points and an item. The lack of hard classes or skill trees means there is no Blessed Hammer moment or satisfaction of a full skill tree. Once you see all the kits and weapon cards, you have seen a large portion of builds. There is little reason to start a new game unless you plan on starting a fresh co-op run. To be fair, the game really tries to push towards an extended endgame experience with lots of challenges, an endless dungeon, and equipment boosting. <br><br>Put plainly, Victor Vran is bland outside of gameplay. Other recent ARPGs having some kind of personality: Bastion\u2019s narration, Diablo\u2019s legacy, and The Incredible Adventures of Van Helsing\u2019s chatty characters all succeed. Vran\u2019s story could be bottled as a sleep aid, and the characters are as memorable as that one movie with Kevin Costner and a non-white cast that came out last year- oh wait, there were two. What is strange is that Victor talks to the handful of NPCs (also forgettable) in the hub area but rarely talks when adventuring. While The Voice, some mask that tags along with Victor, blabbers on everywhere except the hub area. The Voice is rather unfunny: lazy references, mocking Victor who has no character or comebacks, and hit-or-miss observations. The Incredibly Long Title for a Game Helsing has protagonists that share banter. Bastion has a strong narrator. Vran has broody grumbles with an unamusing companion whom he ignores. Not a deal breaker, but it is certainly a misstep, and it is possible that the real villain of the game just sucked the personality dry.<br><br>Victor Vran is polished, fluid, and different. The combat, equipment and movement in the game craft an absorbing experience. At the time of this writing, the devs have released much of their planned post-release content with the notable exceptions of local co-op and a Mot\u00f6rhead expansion (which may be tied to the tragic loss of Lemmy). The base game offers a healthy foundation for continued support and I would gladly buy any content they decide to add.
