received this product for no cost for the purpose of reviewing. However, this does not affect my views and opinions of the game.<br><br>Beglitched is an interesting take on the &quot;Match three&quot; genre. It's a strategic 2D puzzle game that takes elements from games such as bejeweled and minesweeper, while adding a unique spin on them. You take on the role an individual who finds a mysterious laptop. This laptop belonged to the &quot;Glitch Witch&quot; a well-known hacker who has taken a leave of absence. In their absence, they have left you to do some tasks for them with the promise of sharing the net's greatest secrets. <br><br>--------<br><br><b> Graphics: 7 \/ 10 <\/b> <br><br>The graphic style is great. The game is about hacking, and despite the art style being cutesy and colourful, it does pull off the the technological theme very nicely. The simulated desktop that the player uses, along with the textboxes and circuitry within the game do give off a computer-y vibe. The non-playable characters in the game also have cute designs. The game is not demanding at all and should run find on any low-tier graphics card and CPU. <br><br>--------<br><br><b> Sound: 6 \/ 10 <\/b> <br><br>The sound assets are also well done and fit in with the overall motif of the game. There are dial-up internet tones, typing noises and a bunch of small circuitry noises that all contribute to the experience of working on a computer. The game's music is not bad though it does not stand out very much. However, it does not take away from the experience, and it is not annoying or repetitive. <br><br>--------<br><br><b> Mechanics: 8 \/ 10 <\/b> <br><br>The game's mechanics are really solid. The game is almost sort of like two in one. The over-world, where you move your character around, plays kind of like minesweeper. You move your character around on a set of tiles using the WASD keys on the over-world map. You activate a tile by hitting the &quot;login&quot; button that appears on the left side of your screen while you're standing on the tile. When you login to a tile you have the chance of getting an item, hitting a mine, finding a door, or finding a shop. When you don't login to a tile, and just stand on top of it, the tile will show you the contents of all the other tiles connected to that tile, however, you have no way of knowing which of those contents are in each of the connected tiles without walking around and standing other tiles. It is not as complicated as it sounds, but it is very satisfying to pinpoint exactly what is in each tile, and reaping your reward afterwards. Your character has a life meter, and loses one of these life units once they take damage from a mine. The items that you can discover are various power-ups or &quot;battle tiles&quot; that are used for the other part of the game, the shop allows you to use your money to buy power-ups or battle tiles, and discovering the door will let you move on to another area.<br><br>While in the over-world flipping through tiles you'll encounter other enemy hackers. You can see these enemies on tiles within the over-world as well. When you step on a tile with an enemy hacker on it, you'll be moved to a different part of the game. The screen changes to become a board, bejewel style, with a bunch of tiles (NOTE: These are completely different tiles to the over-world tiles). The game is a lot more than bejeweled. It's essentially a cat and mouse, hide-and-seek style battle where you're trying to use the bomb tiles to blow up your enemy. Your enemy is usually hidden behind one of the tiles on the board, and your job is find them, get a bomb tile in that area, and try to blow them up. <br><br>This may seem simple, but there's a lot more too it. You have a certain amount of moves you can make before the enemy will attack you. When you're attacked, you lose one unit of life from your life meter, and after you've lost all units, you have to restart from where you last saved your game. The enemy will only attack you after you'd used up all your moves, afterward you get a new cycle of moves before the enemy can attack you again. You can stall a little longer by matching CPU tiles on the board, which increases the amount of moves you have. There are other tiles on the board such as money tiles, which give you money to spend in the over-world, compass tiles which can direct you where the enemy is by clicking onto them, and of course the bomb tile which it needed to attack your enemy. Battery tiles are needed to power other tiles. Using a compass tile or bomb tile takes up battery power, and your battery life is indicated as a bar on the screen above your move count. You can recharge your battery power by matching battery tiles. <br><br>Matching three tiles of the same type doesn't always remove them. For compasses and bomb tiles, matching three will create an upgraded version of the respective tile. The upgraded compass tile will more accurately show where the enemy is, and if they're moving. The upgraded bomb tile will destroy tiles in a larger radius than a smaller bomb. More tiles are added as you continue to play the game, and more enemies with different abilities will challenge you as progress further. <br><br><b> Personal Final Remarks: <\/b><br><br>I enjoyed playing this game. The game is really cute and I do like the art-style. It can get very complicated and I expect there to be a lot more challenges within the game as you progress further. You really have to think about your moves and be strategic about placement, for both; the over-world and  the hacking battle portion of the game. The game doesn't take itself too seriously, though I feel that because of the graphic style, some people may not take a serious look at it. It does get complicated. Sometimes I found myself trying to desperately increase my move count so I wouldn't take another point of damage, and other times I've found the enemies location but didn't have enough battery power to blow them up. Knowing when to conserve and what tiles to utilize is an important aspect of this game. I'm not usually a fan of puzzle games but I did like this one. <br><br>Thanks to the Devs for giving me the opportunity to review the game, as well as anyone who took this review into consideration, I
