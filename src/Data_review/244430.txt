There is no risk of dying, no mechanics or stats to maintain, and this really free's the player to get lost and explore the diverse and creative worlds Cyan have imagined. You're not given a map or directed how to proceed, and certainly when I first arrived in Myst I was like \u201chuh?\u201d I had no idea where to start. This may sound like a negative comment but both puzzles and the lack of direction did actually add to the mystery and made the game more enjoyable. You get the feeling \u201cRight nobody is going to tell me what I need to do here so I better get on with it!\u201d. I found that quality of the game unusual and attractive.<br><br>Puzzles were mixed; some challenging, others I got immediately and others still I had to google (pathetic I know!). With respect to length I completed the game in roughly 8 hours but I imagine it largely depends how quickly you catch on. With respect to re-playability; I don't think I'd be able to replay until I've played enough games to \u201cforget\u201d what your supposed to do in each age. I think the game has replay value but after some time has passed.<br><br>The graphics are dated and do look like they were taken from a Civ IV trailer (tangent: why the heck are Civ Game trailers so inconsistent with gameplay??). but the environments are still aesthetically pleasing. Myst Island does feel like a mesh-mash of clutter but once you begin to unpick the puzzle it is an enchanting place. With respect to the \u201chints\u201d menu these are genuinely helpful and offer various levels of \u201chint\u201d rather than just giving you the answers. At times I even kicked myself for using them having actually taken the next step and not realised! I think if the edition didn't come with them at times you simply wouldn't have a clue (well I wouldn't anyways!)<br><br>Using my camera phone to take pictures of key bits of information was a must and I totally understand why some individuals historically kept note pads \/ journals on which to jot and sketch. Frankly unless you have an eidetic memory it's likely going to be more trouble without. Some areas in particular still have me confused but perhaps this was deliberate misdirection by the authors. For example what are those crystals for in the throne room? Or why is there a recording of a guy speaking a peculiar language in the last tree house? Given I still have 11 outstanding achievements I presume there is probably more to all this.<br><br>While at this point it is perhaps moot to comment on possible improvements I will. I should perhaps first add the disclaimer that 1) I never played this back when it was first released 2) I've never played any other games in this series, and 3) I can only comment on this as a game played in 2017 with all the prejudice and experience that goes with that.<br><br>The first was most obvious; the ages offer nothing more in storyline development. It isn't until you have collected all of the pages that you get the next storyline instalment from the brothers \/ father. It's definitely here a lost opportunity as the idea of books you can examine could have further developed each character were they found in the ages. Consequently the significant choice (red \/ blue clich\u00e9!) toward the end seems a little arbitrary because aside from what they have told us and what we have read in the library we have no other opportunity to learn anything further about the key characters.<br><br>There is also limited [meaningful] interaction within the environments, and no other characters emerge as you play (such as the tree people) that were mentioned in the library. Aside from father and sons you actually don't meet anyone else with whom to interact. I'm undecided if this is a good or bad thing. Likely a good thing given the age of the game and how difficult it would have been to implement well all of the relevant characters.  A remastered edition could have offered the opportunity to provide a more richer and interactive environment. That said I can entirely understand the authors decision to remaster as true to the original as possible (I can only imagine the colourful comments and reviews that would have ensued by the unforgiving internet had they done otherwise) and omit new sections.<br><br>With all of that said Myst Island and each of the ages offers a beautifully rendered environment to explore and get lost in. I found the game well priced, well balance and thoroughly enjoyable.
