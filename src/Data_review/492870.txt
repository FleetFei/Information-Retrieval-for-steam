Champions of Canaan for free.\">Product received for free<\/div>\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t<div class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\t<div class=\"bb_h1\">Introduction<\/div>FIVE: Champions of Canaan is developed and published by Kingdom Games. This is both their second Steam project and the second game in their FIVE series which is focusing on the ancient history of the Levant.<br> <br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay<\/div>Instead of addressing the heroic exploits of the second king of the united Kingdom of Israel and Judah like in FIVE: Guardians of David, in the apparent spin-off FIVE: Champions of Canaan, you witness an almost gladiatorial-style of arena-based combat. Thus, a more static type of gameplay that revolves around famous or less so, cities from Ancient Canaan and the fighting pits within them. The idea of a sequel that alters the initial gameplay formula is indeed a bold move. But I for one, appreciated this stylistic departure from the pure hack \u2018n slash to more disciplined combat that relies heavily on tactics. The confined spaces offered by an arena, serve as a testing ground for both pacience and skill, as the game\u2019s natural difficulty progression is obvious. Foes are increasingly dangerous as one wave precedes the other. Near the end of the rounds, you get to fight a boss-type of enemy that can provide ample challenge and your character\u2019s survival will rely on coordinated attacks and strategic retreats. Mindlessly charging at your targets will result in failure most of the time. <br><br>Champions of Canaan retains its role playing roots in the form of a well fleshed out inventory system as well as character skill and attribute upgrades. Speaking of that, the creation screen at the very start of the game let\u2019s you choose your lineage, an important aspect in this game and a substitute of more traditional hero classes. Since my historic interest never reached far or in-depth about Levant or in particular Canaan, I cannot say for certain where the historic truth (there\u2019s a fine oxymoron for you) ends and Biblical elements begin. And yet the Ammonite tribe\u2019s penchant for violence translated into arena combat fits the setting quite well.<br> <br>Pushing the Unity Engine close to its limits and the creative adaptation of new assets, really make Champions of Canaan stand out, when compared to other indie 3D RPGs. The soundtrack and voice acting were most fitting as well. In fact I would have found next to no cons to this game, if I wouldn\u2019t have been \u201creminded\u201d every single time I tried booting the title, that a crash report has been filed. Which would soon be followed by the obvious CTD. On several occasions it took three tries before I would be able to play this game in peace, without fear of random crashes right before I would finish a hard fought battle\/round. The culprit of all this senseless commotion? None other than the Steam Overlay I depend upon in all the games I currently play, whether we talk of Uplay, Origin or naturaly, Steam itself. I need to take screenshots and I want them easily linked onto my Steam account. It\u2019s not a lot to ask for by 2016 PC gaming standards, is it? Well this game appears to disagree. With the Overlay enabled, I would get the error and subsequent CTD: \u201cgameoverlayrenderer.dll caused an Access Violation (0xc0000005)\u201d. With the Overlay disabled locally just for FIVE: Champions of Canaan: \u201cgameoverlayrenderer.dll caused a Privileged Instruction (0xc0000096)\u201d. Alas I shall not deduct any points from my rating, since it may be an isolated case and the problems themselves might be solved with a simple update. Hopefully.<br> <br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Pros<\/div>+ Beautiful graphics and sounds<br>+ Unique characters and locations for a video game<br>+ Complex RPG elements<br>+ Steam Achievements &amp; Trading Cards<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Cons<\/div>- Steam Overlay causes random crashes<br>- Combat gets repetitive after a while<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Result \/ Final thoughts<\/div>While not as narrative-driven as it\u2019s predecessor, FIVE: Champions of Canaan still emerges as a breath of fresh air to the relatively stale faux-medieval setting that role playing games constantly exibit. Levantine mythology, sprinkled with elements from the Old Testament is not a subject you can tackle often at all in gaming. The developer deserves recognition for this aspect alone were it not for the fact that its games are fully functional, educational and most importantly, fun to play.<br><br><b>Rating 85\/100<\/b><br>
