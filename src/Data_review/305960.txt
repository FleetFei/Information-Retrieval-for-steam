Map doesn't unload and fail to load again when you come back. Rather odd coloring, kind of a neon green or off green for the &quot;grass&quot;. More trees. Trees look nicer now. Villagers do not appear to live very close together. Takes away from the feel of villages. Over all, makes the game feel like an Alpha so a step up for sure.<br><br>Mobs prior - Few villagers, chicks with swords and guys with pitch forks (and of course the villager of doom, but he was a bug\/glitch), cyclops, wolves, ents (or where they giants?). AI wasn't that good, simple see and attack. Passive mobs, cows, sheep. Easy to kill.<br><br>Mobs now - Same types of villagers, plus man at arms (or what i'd call them since they had shields), Didn't play long enough to find many other types of mobs. But i did take note there are now deer, and rabbits. Wolves are still in. AI is still rather basic. Though now they don't all attack on sight (but should in cases like the wolves who sat there and let me fry them for one of the quests)<br><br>Dragon Prior - Going to be honest here. The dragon we had access to when i first played was awesome looking. Really made me think Dragon. Handled ok, would go lopsided every once in a while. If i were to gage it's &quot;age&quot;, i'd say it's early to mid Alpha. It's gettingn a lot of factors together now. The map is for sure cleaned up. Just needs more TLC. Combat and UI needs some heavy work. The game does though seem to flow better. There are some real possibilities for this game if it ever gets finished. <br><br>Do I recommend the game? Review<\/div>\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\t\t\t\t\t<div class=\"postedDate\">Posted: March 11, 2016\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t<div class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI will update this review with each successive update to the game.<br><br>If you find this review unhelpful, tell me why and I will try to fix it.<br><br>Revised 7-8-2016<br><br><b>Overview:<\/b> It is evident from the blog and other resources that the focus right now is on game function and code stability, mostly under the hood stuff, the kind of stuff that matters much more than how pretty it looks. Nobody gives a damn about your Corvette if there is no working engine, and nobody cares about a game with underdeveloped code. In that regard I think Red Level has its priorities straight, and in the play-through my priority at this phase in development was to look at code stability, controls, and interface. Stability and Control score a solid OK, it took me only about five minutes to figure out the basics of movement and flight and after ten was able to fly around without crashing into anything.\u00a0<br><br><b>Movement &amp; Controll:<\/b>\u00a0For those thinking of buying now, the dragon maneuvers like you are flying a helicopter, gravity is irrelevant once you hit the space-bar and continues to be irrelevant after you let go, which causes you to slowly float back down to the surface. A The W key allows forward flight, with a momentum dynamic like you would logically expect. Turning is instant and mouse directed, but you will tend to drift like a Ford on ice while cornering even if your dragon faces the desired direction instantly so plan ahead. Be aware that the game does not bind the mouse to the game screen, so long turns in one direction will cause the mouse to drift off screen and will close the window when you click any mouse button.<br><br><b>Combat:<\/b>\u00a0Combat is very one sided, both in terms of damage taken, and physical damage dealt since you can only swing your tail to one side or your dragon, so make sure the thing you want to kill is on that side. Fire breath is hard to aim at first so be careful using it on the ground or you will burn yourself before anything around you. Think like an airplane when using this, you want to strafe a target with fire if you can, before landing to collect it.<br><br><b>Navigation:<\/b>\u00a0As of right now navigation is line of sight only, you get a look at a map sketch when you first drop in to the game but you have no mini-map in your interface so plan on getting lost. Right now having your bearings doesn't really matter since there is not all that much to do. .<br><br><b>Quests\/Tasks:<\/b>\u00a0The quest system did not load during my most recent playthrough and is likely being rewritten.<br><br><b>Interface:<\/b>\u00a0Currently you get three small circles in the lower right had corner of the screen which tell you what powers or attacks you have. There are &quot;Roar&quot; &quot;Tail Swipe&quot; &quot;Bite Attack&quot; and &quot;Fire Breath&quot;. Roar did not provide audio feedback when used, and I could not observe any notable effect to things on the surface. Tail Swipe\/Fire Breath controls are functional though there are some quarks to be resolved as the game progresses. A Three button mouse is needed to use both bite and tailswing. There is a hunger readout on the bottom right which gives access to the upgrade menu which is currently in development and not working. A new menu tree is in process which looks promising.<br><br><b>Graphics:<\/b>\u00a0Vast improvements to terrain and graphics quality were included in the most recent update. The framework is there for high poly models but they have not been applied yet.<br><br><b>Sound:<\/b>\u00a0Mostly placeholder sounds, they have good ambient noises but the looping soundtrack can be a bit annoying. Improvements to come, but likely not until after Alpha.<br><br><b>Secondary Effects:<\/b>\u00a0In shooters this would be like bullet holes where you shoot walls, or dynamic destructible vehicles. In dragon this is most notable with fire breath, where your fire can spread from one part of a building to the whole. You can burn down trees, or knock them over with your tail.<br><br><b>Physics:<\/b>\u00a0Physics modeling for a dragon in flight is helicopter based, you will drift in the direction you are leaning when not using the throttle. Glide dynamics have been added, but gravity is weak for your dragon and allows for unrealastic floating, and are now fall\/impact deaths for you or NPC's, but collision mapping is buggy and can cause models to drop through. The glide dynamic has improved somewhat with the most recent update, and is being dialed in progressively.<br><br><b>AI:<\/b>\u00a0Villagers become hostile when you get close enough, and are harder to kill. They will always take the shortest route to you, and will stack up beneath you if you fly just right. Animals are neutral, (except for bears) but will run away if you get too close.\u00a0The recent update has reduced the amound of ambient wildlife, making eating more difficult.<br><br><b>Stability:<\/b>\u00a0Beta, not optimized. You will have low frame rates regardless of your system power. I maxed out at 32FPS but dropped as low as 12 at near max graphics, which did not strain my system. Pre 3-29 update I was lucky to get 12FPS max, so there is improvement. There have been a series of small un-announced updates which have improved overall stability.<br><br><b>Glitches:<\/b>\u00a0A quick twenty minutes of game play did not result in any crashes or game breaking glitches. I did not get stuck in a cave, or get caught under the map. When grabbing objects you are almost guaranteed to experience a glitch known as the &quot;runaway rag-doll&quot;. This glitch can happen as a result of poor collision mapping, or can occur if the engine thinks the object its grabbing is weightless and therefore has no inertia. You get an explosion of flailing limbs that can be distracting, but that\u2019s about it.<br>Another prominent glitch occurs when two animated objects collide on uneven or akward terrain, resulting in one or both objects being thrown violently out of map or straight into the air. This glitch occurs in nearly every game in development, and was made popular by Skyrim's &quot;hammer spike&quot; in which being killed by an overhead swing from a giant launched your character straight up into the air. You will see this in Dragon if you land directly on a live deer or wolf. Sometimes the both of you are thrown, or sometimes only one of you. This is more likely to happen on hillsides or in rocky terrain. It also happens if you tail swing a sheep, deer, or wolf into another object without killing them, resulting in a violent but non lethal vertical launch.<br><br><b>Developer:<\/b>\u00a0Red Level Games as an upstart has an opportunity to fill the general void left by developers like Bungie, EA, and even Microsoft. All of these companies have had rather lackluster recent releases that have underperformed in some way, they have become victims of their own success and now sell titles based on their franchise hype.<br><br>Red Level Games has all the attributes of a good developer, they:<br><br>1. Operate an independent website that isn't an eyesore, and was reasonably easy to navigate.<br>2. Have an easy &quot;contact us&quot; section which allows anyone to email them directly.<br>3. Post blog updates to their website and Steam (recently on a monthly basis)<br>4. Allow the community to contribute to game mechanics where applicable.<br>5. Are honest and forward in their efforts.<br>6. Clearly communicate update goals and priorities.<br><br>Dragon: The Game has great potential, especially if it allows the community to generate content and assist in building the product. They have already partly done so by releasing a model dragon skeleton for hobbyist animators to play with, and to potentially contribute to animation in game. They appear dedicated, and work in a fantasy niche that nobody has attempted to fill until now. For that reason, and the ones listed above, I can recommend this title to anyone who wants to support beta development.
