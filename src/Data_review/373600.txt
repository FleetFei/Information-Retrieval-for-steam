Combining striking visuals, interesting weapons, and run &amp; gun shootemup action, Weapon of Choice is a pretty memorable experience.<br><br>There is a lot that's unique about this game. There are 7 stages, but your average play-through takes place over 2 or 3 of them. Admittedly, this decision is pretty strange. Usually action games - even if they have branching paths - consist of 5 or more stages. I suppose it works due to the variety of environments and enemies. Each branch changes things up, and the operatives you control can have different effects on the mission. The replay-value is further improved by a selection of difficulties. Since the good guys die in a single hit, the harder settings are a bit more creative in their implementation. You won't see boring changes like &quot;good guys take more damage&quot;. The harder settings have enemies exhibit different behaviors, fire more projectiles, drop menacing saw-blades, and so on.<br><br>Choosing the Operative that fits your play-style is something to consider. Each of them have their own weapons and special maneuvers. Everyone also carries a back-up gun. Unlike the main weapon, this machine-gun has an attachment that allows the player to move it around. So if you need to shoot an enemy that's around a corner, behind a post, or in some other hard-to-reach area, you can count on this gun. Experimentation is just one of the many keys to success. Xerxes carries a flame-thrower. I started with the easiest setting, played through all of the stages, then immediately jumped to the hardest. I've lost a few operatives on each path, but I never got a game over. Even without &quot;death-brushing&quot;, you'll survive as long as you take it slow and destroy everything. I think going for &quot;no-miss&quot; campaigns could be a worthy challenge though. Speedrunning the game would be interesting as well, especially with the unique movement abilities everyone has. <br><br>Weapon of Choice is a fascinating shootemup. Along with the vivid graphics, there are some cool weapons to play around with, and the bosses are impressively designed. If you haven't already purchased one or two of the other games, I advise that you should just grab the bundle. For a few dollars more you get four good games.
