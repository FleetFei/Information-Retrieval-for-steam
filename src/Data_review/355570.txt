So I got myself an account, and started browsing through the indie section.<br><br>I really wasn't sure about this game, since it only had one review, and I couldn't really find any information on it other than the description, but I figured $5 wasn't going to kill me, so I went ahead and got it. <br><br>I can say, without any sort of hesitation, that it was worth five bucks. <br><br>The story is interesting and just the right amount of macabre. The main character is afraid of the dark, and as you travel through the enormous mansion with a very limited field of vision, his fear gauge will go down. Of course, lighting a match alerts the murderer to your location if he is in the same room. It makes the game just challenging enough to keep you on edge as you make your way, trying to keep your family safe and get out. <br><br>I've finished one playthrough, and I have to say, the ending I got definitely surprised me. The mistakes are pretty frequent, but they don't interfere with the ability to understand, so they aren't a huge issue. But it plays into the story, so it's forgivable. <br>There was an occasional glitch, particularly on the second floor for me. I had to load my games a few times because of this. But it's not too hard to overcome. <br>Some of the puzzles aren't well explained. You are given vague directions and just kind of have to push buttons and hope. There was one that involved moving a bunch of stones to the right that I'm still not sure how to do, I just kept messing around until it worked. But you can eventually get it, even if you just mess around for a while. <br><br>For me, it was a fun game, and I'll likely play again soon to try and get the true ending.
