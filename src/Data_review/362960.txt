The graphics overall are much better than PoE, the scenarios are very nice, the 3D effects feel much better and the overall pyrotechnical effects related to weather, magic, explosions and so on are amazing.<br><br>The aesthetics doesn't follow the Baldur's Gate or Icewind Dale formula we see on PoE. A key and amazing aspect of the story setting is Kyros itself. Kyros feels distant, abstract and omnipresent which creates this oppressive feeling wherever you go. You feel propelled from rags to riches once you acquire those items. In a way, it helps building up the immersion of your ascension in this harsh, power driven world of Tyranny. <br><br>The economy is challenging, I bought almost nothing from the traders in the game and overall one always feel poor. Once you finally get some real cash after selling some items, the game ends... Money feels of little relevance. Again, everything in the world of Tyranny appears to orbit around power and power only. Money is of secondary importance in a world like this or that was how I felt it.<br><br>Finally, the game feels rushed up in the final stages. There are a lot of paths you may choose which gets access to certain places or not. This creates a lot of replay value but in the later stages of the game, everything feels rushed and linear to finish things and wrap up. <br>I also don't love the 4 people party. I like parties of at least 5 members, 6 feels more comfortable. I loved the characters but as the game rushes up in the later stages, I believe their backstories could be better integrated and explored in the game.<br><br>I really hope DLC or at least I hope to see some of this in PoE2. Lots of replay value and I think it's a much better title to dive in if you are not familiar with isometric RPGs like Baldur's Gate or Icewind Dale than Pillars of Eternity for instance.
