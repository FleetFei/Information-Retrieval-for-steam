metroidvanias or even bullet hell games. Yet, Rabi-Ribi managed to be one of my favorite games all the time. <br>Definitely worth the price!<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay<\/div>Rabi-Ribi is an action platformer game in which the player explore a massive metroidvania-like map. There are lots of bosses to beat, attacking the player using swarms of bullets which create a pattern (hence the bullet hell).<br>I am not an expert of bullet hell games myself, but I believe a good bullet hell game involves &quot;readable&quot; patterns (both bullets and attack patterns). By this, I don't mean an easy-to-read patterns. At least the player needs to be able to figure out how to dodge any kind of bullet patterns. Rabi-Ribi has them. They may seem overwhelming at first, but after several trials and errors you will eventually figure them out. That said, there may be times when you just can't dodge the bullets no matter what. But not to worry, the game provides a workaround for this. You can enter invincibility mode for several seconds just by a mere button push, but the amount is kinda limited. If you got hit it's not the end of the world either, so you should not worry about dodging every single bullet. Just don't get hit too much.<br><br>The combat mechanic is fluid enough. Hammer attacks may seem like a single button push at first, but as you advance the game a whole bunch of attacks to build your combo will be added. Uppercut, sliding attack, aerial rolling attack, air dash, carrot bomb, drill attack, etc. Your partner Ribbon covers your weakness in attack range by giving you a wide range of ranged attacks (pun intended) and some nukes.<br>Tons of upgrades also included in form of badges, items, and power-ups. Badges are meant to be used in a certain situation, while the power-ups are just straightforward buffs. But you should be aware that using them means fighting more advanced enemies. So they should be used sparingly.<br>Also, secrets. The game has lots of them secret contents. On your first playthrough it may be hard to get 100% of the items since many of them are hidden.<br>And the most interesting of all, you can even beat the game without any items\u2013including the hammer! <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Challenges<\/div>Do you think the term &quot;bullet hell&quot; is an abomination created by a cultist who worships satan for them hardcore masochists? Well... fear not! Rabi-Ribi includes seven types of difficulty for any kind of player. If you haven't played any bullet hell games before, you may try the casual difficulty first. But IMO the novice one provides more suitable challenge while also forgiving at the same time.<br>For those who are familiar with the bullet hell genre but finds touhou games too challenging, there is normal difficulty. It is a perfect difficulty for starters before you tackle the higher ones: Hard or Hell.<br><br>But maybe you're THE masochist, aren't you? Then step up to the challenge. Clear the impossible or even bunny extinction mode! Also don't forget that there are speedrun and boss rush mode. In addition, the game has a huge amount of achievements but around 50% of them are either challenging or need a lot of effort to achieve. For instance, the dodge masters (Take 0 damage while fighting a decent boss), Sequence-breaking, 100%-ing, and speedrunning achievements. And did I mention no-items run? <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Story<\/div>Lots of other reviewers said the story is &quot;meh&quot;. While I understand this point of view, I don't think it is all that bad. It may not have deep or intriguing plot points, and sometimes the plot doesn't even make sense. But for me it's entertaining enough to make me wanting to know more about the plot. It fits the theme (Cute magical girls in a magical world) and the gameplay (another day, another boss fight) too. I think the story feels like those moe slice-of-life animes. No depth, no plot twists, no sense made, just moe girls (and some humor).<br>The characters' role needs a rework, though. So many characters in this game, but they lack purpose. They exist only for the sake of... existing. While having distinctive character design and backstory, many side characters contribute almost nothing to the plot, making you question whether they are relevant to the story or not. <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Aesthetics<\/div>Oooh, boy! I love the aesthetics. The characters are so adorable, suitable to my taste. Call me a moefag if you want :p<br>On a serious note, if you like moe anime-styled games, then you may like this game. One may even say that it's the art that keeps you playing the game. And not to mention the design of the world. Both of them synergically creates an experience: Being in a cute, colorful world.<br>However, the pixel art style feels kinda off to me. This may be my personal opinion, but consistent anime style for both the characters and the world will fit better. Not saying that it's a bad design choice, though. At least it's not cancerous.<br><br>While the art is good and all, the only thing that hooked me up is the music. I once thought this game is just like some other anime games, cute anime girls but no engaging gameplay. Once I heard the music on that trailer, I bought it almost instantly. And I'm still glad that I did. The music fits the mood, theme, and world perfectly. Some tracks felt bland however, but it's not much.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Controls<\/div>Rabi-Ribi has controller support, and I believe it works best with controllers. That said, I'm a keyboard player, and the controls for keyboard are easy to master. The bunny amulet invincibility buff needs two keys to activate, though, which may be annoying if you want to release it in less than a second. Thankfully the game lets you define one shortcut key for not only the bunny amulet but also the air dash attack. <br>You can remap the keys if you don't like the default setting. I don't know about controllers, but one thing I don't like about this is that you have to remap ALL of the keys even though you just want one and only one. This is a chore even for me. <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Performance<\/div>Well, it's a 2D platformer game, so it should not have any issues on a laptop using integrated graphics card, right? Well, that's not the case for Rabi-Ribi. The game did run well in 60FPS on my rig. But I had huge FPS drop on my Pentium 2.13GHz laptop with Intel HD Graphics, resulting in slow-motion gameplay. My other laptop (Pentium 1.9GHz, also Intel HD) had the same problem. Those laptops run 2D and 3D games with DirectX 9.0 just fine, though. So I think those system requirements are pretty much underestimated... or this game just hates onboard graphics.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Summary<\/div>In short, I recommend this game for the fans of bullet hell or anime-styled games, no matter your skills. I don't play metroidvania that much, but I think metroidvania fans will like it too. Don't expect engaging story, since you won't find it here. And if you think anime is cancer then it would be best if you just move along and find other games that are not cancerous for you.<br><br>Final verdict: 8.5\/10\t\t\t\t\t<div
