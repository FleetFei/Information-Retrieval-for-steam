class=\"postedDate\">Posted: May 15, 2015\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t<div class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPrincess Battles is a charming, quirky, totally enjoyable little card battle\/visual novel hybrid that despite a silly story and simple game play stands as a shining example of how creativity and humor can make a smaller title more fun than flashier games in the genre.  <br><br>Life as a princess isn't all it's cracked up to be.  Between unwanted marriage proposals and commoners aiming to dethrone you through popular opinion, it's a tough life.  What's a princess to do when even her royal parents find her rival more praiseworthy than herself?  Card battling, that's what!  The premise is preposterous, but that's why I love it so much.  Princess Battles knows exactly what it is and plays to its silliness with perfection.   In this world, a lady's parlor game is no laughing matter. Tournaments, black market card trading, demonic overlords, card battle schools, all ridiculous ideas that click together and work mostly because the story never takes itself too seriously.   <br><br>I love Lillian.  She's a spoiled, lazy and entitled brat, but that is precisely what makes her so endearing.  We're given a caricature who acts more realistic than most other &quot;perfect&quot; protagonists.  Even though she's far from the ideal princess, through the challenges she faces in her rise to <i>Prima<\/i> champion, she grows and matures, but never entirely loses that impudent attitude.  Oddly enough, her love interests make more sense for it.  Perfection is boring, guys want a girl with character and spunk, and Lillian has it in spades.<br><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=428692777\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_0\">http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=428692777<\/a><br>Speaking of love interests, the boys are appealing, too, in their own ways, though we're not given nearly as much time with them as I would have liked.  Perhaps another scene or two of dialogue and a little more development of their characters would have fleshed out their stories a bit more.  But I admit, outside of satisfying my own desire for experiencing the &quot;feels&quot; of romance, it probably wouldn't have benefited the game much as it works so well simply because it's not as deep or complicated as it could be.  That goes for the game play as well.<br><br>The game consists of visiting various areas and selecting dialogue choices between characters to further the plot with card battles sprinkled in.  The battle system itself is simple enough to enjoy casually, but allows for a bit of strategy to keep the player engaged.  A race to max stats against your opponent, you play princess themed cards to either increase your stats or decrease your opponent's, all while juggling your gold and energy.  As with other card games, success often comes down to the luck of the draw which at times can be frustrating, but the battles are usually short enough to not feel like a total waste of time if you lose.  My sole complaint with the system is a bug that does not allow your opponent's attack cards to affect your stats.  While this makes the game easier (although success is by no means guaranteed), it removes some of the strategy from play and makes Free Play mode less attractive.  I certainly hope the bug is fixed as I'd love to build and play around with my own free play deck, but it's only worth it if the card battles are working the way they should.<br><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=428692853\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_1\">http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=428692853<\/a><br>Which brings me to game length.  Free Play mode would add several hours of game play if only the battle bug were fixed.  The main game itself is rather short taking only about 3 hours to complete.  There is plenty of replayability, however, with over two dozen achievements leading through most of the branching dialogue paths and in-game events.  It took me a good 12 hours to make 27\/28 with that last one eluding me, but I'll get it yet! <br><br>Despite a few shortcomings, I enjoyed Princess Battles immensely.  It's not often I am this impressed by a title that looked much less adventurous and entertaining than it proved to be.  The price may be daunting for some due to the short play time, but I feel I've gotten my money's worth given how much I enjoyed what's here.  If you're looking for silly fun with a dash of romance tossed in, pick up Princess Battles.  On sale, off sale - it's worth it either way.  I know I'll be supporting Nekomura Games in the future, especially if they continue to bring the same creativeness and humor to their other titles.<br><br>Bottom Line - Worth it for the humor alone, Princess Battles deserves a spot in any visual novel lover's library. The card game itself is fun and not super challenging, so if you want something you can come back to and play from time to time even after you 100% the game, you should pick this one up!<br><br>---<br><br><b>Overall impression<\/b>: Generally positive. It's a bit rough around the edges in places, but certainly is a game I'd recommend for younger players or those who are quite sensitive to more mature content. It's rather G-rated. About the only &quot;questionable&quot; content I can think of is if you fail a particular battle you'll be [SPOILER - locked in a cage and kept as 'pet']. Neither the art nor the text implies anything sexual or violent about it. Rather, the princess is just bored out of her mind. <br><br><b>Translation<\/b>: N\/A (?) (Olivia is herself an hommage to raising sim games, particularly Princess Maker.) She enlists the help of friends to become accomplished in about the only thing Olivia hasn't mastered yet - Prima, a card-battling game for (generally upperclass) ladies.<br><br>Along the way, she can fall in love with one of three gentlemen - her tutor (honestly, he's useless at his job), a thief\/shady card trader (you can only trade cards with him in Freeplay Mode), and the prince her family wants her to marry. There is also a fourth &quot;unlockable&quot; love interest, a demon. [SPOILER: You win him over in the game proper, don't end up with one of the other three love interests when you end the game, and then in FreePlay, spam &quot;Trade Cards&quot; until he shows up instead of the thief.] The gentlemen suffer a bit because they're not given enough time to show off their personalities. The two characters I liked most got the least amount of &quot;on screen&quot; time. The other two felt bland, just because they were of a similar character type and, again, didn't have much of a chance to develop, even though they had the most screen time out of the guys. Even side characters like Lina, Olivia, and the demon princess were more fleshed out and distinct in spite of having less time on screen.<br><br>Don't expect much more story than, &quot;I'm not letting Olivia take my place! I'll learn Prima and get good! (Maybe I'll fall in love too?) Oh hey, here I am at the huge Prima tournament and once that's over, that's The End.&quot;<br><br>Honestly, as a &quot;story&quot;, the game suffers a little for being just that. There's nothing in-depth about the romances. No real &quot;falling in love&quot; stage for any of them and even the bonus stories don't do that great a job of bridging the gap. It all seems quite abrupt. I'm also a little bummed that there was no ending where you and Olivia decided to get along.<br><br><b>The Heroine<\/b>: On the positive side, she has a personality of her own. She's quite distinct as a character. Unfortunately, I find her terribly rude (sometimes even in her &quot;good&quot; answers) and self-centered. She improves only very slightly throughout the story. Fortunately, she does have her moments, but I honestly thought for all her faults, Olivia really was a better heir for the kingdom. I'm not entirely sure whether I was supposed to admire the princess or not.<br><br><b>In closing...<\/b>: I sound quite negative about the game overall, but I'm really not. It's a cute game that's very light and fluffy. The Prima card game is the shining feature and clearly a ton of work went into making it run well and polishing the game flow. The fact that it's a game that I could let my nieces play when they're a little older is even better. <br><br>I know I'll be returning to Princess Battles from time to time just to futz around in Free Play mode.
