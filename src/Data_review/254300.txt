several times, unhappy with each. I have decided to stop doing that and just write how I feel instead of sounding professional. <br><br>As someone who found Dofus when it was first in beta, I feel almost ashamed to admit I've been playing this game as long as I have. In fact, It might be for the best that it's only now reaching steam, as I fear seeing the hours I've poured into this game might be traumatizing. Heck, the hours I might pour into this game AFTER this point intimidate me..... but alas, there's a reason I have stuck around as long as I have. THIS. GAME. IS. AMAZING. Why? because I said so! Okay, I'll be a bit more specific.... <br><br>Appearance - I start here, because I frankly see little reason to go over it. You see the top of the page, you saw the trailer (granted it's a mishmash of the movie and the game, well done, nonetheless). And it's the thing that will catch people's attention. I, for one, LOVE the artstyle and direction Ankama has chosen to go, but something I should mention. It looks cartooney, and childish, don't let that throw you off, this game is VERY much aimed at young adults and adults. It might be a bit too complex for young children, and the dialogue and....ahem...references... assume a respectable level of maturity. That is to say, there is adult humor all over the place (think condom sword, and rabbits vandalizing fox(tanouki) statues by placing a giant wooden log between their legs...) so don't be afraid. Also note this game isn't just a ton of sex humor, Ankama is pretty classy, after all. <br><br>Gameplay - The main reason you'll get sucked in...or turned off. Dofus is a turn based strategy MMORPG, which I can understand some people not enjoying, I do. The game is very skill intensive, almost no reflexes needed. There are currently 17 classes to chose from, and I can tell you, don't bother asking what the &quot;best&quot; anything is. There is no &quot;best&quot; character, &quot;best&quot; build, &quot;best&quot; equipment or the likes. Ankama has done a marvelous job at keeping the classes ballanced. Speaking of the classes, the 17 classes all play VERY differently, leading many people to play multiple (oh yeah, multiclienting is a thing, and is supported in this game. That is both good and bad, depending who you ask). If you're just starting out, don't worry about playing multiple characters, it's not necessary to succeed, just keep in mind someone using 6 accounts will progress faster than someone using 1 or 2. <br><br>Uniqueness - The only thing even remotely comparable to Dofus is Wakfu, which is almost a sequel to Dofus, and is the same company. There's nothing even remotely comparable in the MMORPG market. If you've ever played Final Fantasy Tactics, or a similar game, you should catch on pretty quickly. <br><br>Accessability - The game is flash based, making it so it's not particularly taxing on even weak computers. After major updates, the game can be a little less comparable (such as the recent interface changes) but even with a mid-range laptop with integrated graphics, I had little issue playing several accounts at once. I also run on a poor net connection (I'm talking average ~200-250 ping, with spikes regularly) and it causes little trouble for me. There is a respectable free-to-play area that anyone can try out to find out if they like it before paying a penny. The game is also multi platform, working on mac and I believe still linux. The learning curve can be steep, and the initial game overwhelming, but that can't be helped with something as unique as this. <br><br>Management - Ankama as a company is one of the companies I respect the most. They have been phenominal at maintaining ballance between the classes, They are good at storytelling (granted they could use some work in the presentation in game...) and their use of the cash shop (Also known as the Ogrine Market, their cash currency) has been great. The items you can buy on the cash shop are almost entirely cosmetic, with a couple minor convienances. Also you can buy the currency with in-game money, as is the case with many games, and it's pretty reasonable to do so, and you can also use the method to subscribe characters. Customer support is famously aweful, by american standards, but when you look at it with the realization that the company is french, where customer support is a totally different perception, it's actually not bad. Izmar (the community manager) is also incredibly helpful and friendly, and very good at making sure things are running smoothly (she deserves a big raise!)<br><br>Longevity - I've been playing it for well over a decade..... I think that's enough said. I admit I am not a crazy hardcore player, but there's still plenty I haven't done and still want to do. <br><br>Customization - Character customization is a weak point here. Hats and cloaks are the only displayed equipment, you can chose colors of your character by picking 5 predetermined areas and coloring those to your liking, and you can chose between m\/f and 8 different faces. Otherwise there's not a ton of options. The interface has recently gone through an overhaul and you are now able to move interfaces around to your liking, but even that could use a little more freedom. <br><br>Social - The guild system is O.K., the alliance system is interesting, and multiclienting people can play on their own no problem. With that said, You will meet some interesting people in this game, there are plenty of chat channels you can access, and the community is generally pretty friendly. In PvP obviously there is drama and hostility, but it's nothing comparable to what you'd find in arena games. <br><br>Bang for your buck - The game is relatively cheap for a subscription game, and the cash shop is nothing near necessary to keep up (in fact, you CANT realistically keep up with other people just by dumping cash into the game), and you can subscribe with in-game currency at a rate that is normally relatively decent. I would advise avoiding trying to use in-game currency for your first month or 3, but after that is when alot of people are able to transfer over, not that supporting ankama is bad by any stretch, and paying with real money makes the game less stressful, as you don't have to worry about upkeeping subscription. <br><br>Footnotes - Note that this game is oldschool. Level 200 (max) isn't when the game starts (or ends, for that matter), so you don't need to rush to high level. There is a huge amount of content. The official forums and subreddit are also great resources for newcomers, and those may help you avoid being overwhelmed. If you're into solo play, awesome, but keep in mind this game is designed for team content, and high level content will require you to team up at least some, or a ton if you find yourself questing.
