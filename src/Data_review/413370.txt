games in the DLC's. As it was for Deus Ex: Human Revolution and The Ship so it is with Deus Ex: Mankind Divided and A Criminal Past. This DLC easily constituted the most enjoyable hours i spent with Deus Ex: Manking Divided.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Graphics<\/div><br>If you've played the base game you know what to expect here: more of the same. The texture work is good, and the lighting is stellar if you can crank up the settings to the max. As always the aesthetics are on point. I really only have complaints about the animation work. Specifically the lip-syncing in most of the cut-scenes. Its usually not too noticeable but at its worst the mouths hardly match the dialogue at all.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Level Design<\/div><br>This DLC map is small. Small in that if you were to run from one side of the prison to the other its would probably only take about a minute or two if all the guards were dead and the doors unlocked. The map is also extremely dense. So dense that it took me about 16 hours to do everything on my first run through. The are many routes to take throughout the prison and I never felt forced down one particular path. There is a decent amount of verticality to the map, particularly in the area between the two cell blocks.  The dev team did a great job laying out the routes and areas so that getting from one area to another feels very intuitive. In general everywhere you think you should be able to go is accessible, including <span class=\"bb_spoiler\"><span>the roof area above both main cell blocks<\/span><\/span>. One notable exception is the inability to access the <span class=\"bb_spoiler\"><span>Admin Building<\/span><\/span> early. I suspect they don't let you in due to story reasons but you can get to the entrance well before the let actually let you in and that was mildly disappointing. If you've played the main game this DLC is comparable to a much larger version of the Bank. There are also almost no loading zones which was nice.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay<\/div><br>The game plays out very smoothly and in general just flows from situation to situation very well. This DLC is at its best when you don't have any augments and I would advise getting as few as you can or even none at all throughout the course of the DLC. Its very tense and satisfying to get by using nothing but your wits and good timing. When you get more than four or five augments however any sense of difficulty goes out the window. In fact without artificially limiting yourself I found the prison to be far too easily &quot;solved&quot; or &quot;broken&quot;. With no augments you can be inside a security office within about five minutes of the start of the game if you know what to look for. With augments the security is a breeze. This DLC really could have benefited from having more prison elements. Rotating patrols, regular code changes, not having both of the <span class=\"bb_spoiler\"><span>security offices<\/span><\/span> have the same damn code, having different sectors need different key-cards, etc. Its not a massive deal and it doesn't break immersion but after a certain point the prison seems less like a prison and more like a playground. Its also far too easy to get praxis points. There are <span class=\"bb_spoiler\"><span>two<\/span><\/span> praxis kits on the <span class=\"bb_spoiler\"><span>starting cell block<\/span><\/span> that required nothing to get other than attention to detail. You can get another <span class=\"bb_spoiler\"><span>six<\/span><\/span> all at once with one item that is just in the <span class=\"bb_spoiler\"><span>supply closet<\/span><\/span> of the <span class=\"bb_spoiler\"><span>starting cell block<\/span><\/span>. The augments are also not equally valuable. Hacking has the best value. You can blow through the game with only hacking level three and robot control. There are no level four locked computers and the few level five computers are easily bypass with multi tools, which are plentiful. On the other hand the Icarus system is literally useless, the punch through walls augment only has one use that I know of, and the lift heavy objects augment suffers the same fate. Overall its played well but there were some minor issue with balance.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Story<\/div><br>Not to much to say about the story. It was good but not great. It was paced well and never lagged or felt disjointed. Some parts were painfully obvious, such as the <span class=\"bb_spoiler\"><span>riot<\/span><\/span>. With the <span class=\"bb_spoiler\"><span>riot<\/span><\/span> in particular it was a little odd because the characters were being so blatant about it but Jenson seemed to not notice anything and then got angry when it eventually went down. Pretty good, small issues.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Overall<\/div><br><br>This DLC was quite enjoyable and was definitely worth $12. I had some small issues with it but nothing that stopped the game from being enjoyable.
