about this amazing game when it was in EA. Now I missed out on the exclusive units.<br><br>This game is amazing. From the fact that it's a steam early access game that actually got out of early access (I would have never seen it coming), to the incredible attention to detail in this game, it's obvious that the developers have given this game their all. That's something you don't see everyday.<br><br>The gunplay is pretty much perfect. Hipfire doesn't work, you need to aim, which is pretty realistic. Realistic is the name of the game here. The developers went for a realistic inmersion theme with this game. Not the kind of realism that makes games tedious. You could almost say the realistic mechanics are what makes this game challenging, fun and unique.<br>And the guns themselves? They're powerful. SMGs kill in two or three shots, rifles kill in just one. You really can't just run around like Rambo in this game.<br><br>The factions, well, I'd really appreciate soviets and italians (I understand we want to keep it in the european font, but those two played pretty big parts on that), but the germans, americans and british all play differently thanks to their different weapons and artillery options. For instance, the british have phosphorous artillery which leaves the ground on fire, damaging everyone who tries to walk through, but the german stuka penetrates buildings. It's quite interesting, and much like the gunplay, all the factions and their weapons are based on reality.<br><br>The classes are all pretty fun and well designed. My favourite (and I'm sure many would agree with me) is the Officer, who can use his binoculars to call in air support in the form of supply drops and bombings of all types (smoke bombing to cover a push, artillery to cause chaos, carpet when you just want people to die...) but ONLY if he's got a soldier equiped with a radio nearby. If noone plays radioman, the officer becomes nothing more than a mook without any special perks (although he does come with an SMG by default, he can be pretty deadly in close quarters). I won't go into much depth with the other classes (mostly cause I am still pretty noobish, I don't know everything just yet) but they're all pretty good.<br><br>There are I believe 10 maps, and a plethora of gamemodes to play. You can also play multiplayer skirmishes, cooperative (where a small team of up to 8 players take on an army of bots in unique gamemodes) and practice with bots.<br><br>While we're on the subject, the bots are top-notch. This is one of my favourite parts of the game. It begs repeating, the bots in this game are amazingly done. It feels fun to play with them. They are a bit less responsive than players (can't win them all I guess), but they know to capture objectives, they take cover efficiently, if you're an officer and you request a radio the nearest radioman will actually come to you and follow you, and in maps where you need to fall back and regroup to let everyone else respawn, the bots will go back and do that for you. The only thing I am not too sure about is if bot officers actually use artillery. I don't think they do (and it would be a bit complicated to make them use it) but don't take my word for it.<br>It might not seem like such a big thing, but I've played enough modern FPS to know bots this GREAT are quite an accomplishment (looking at you, payday 2 and TF2).<br><br>The graphics are great. They look good. That's enough for me, really.<br><br>There are only two bad things about this game that I must address. First, the difficulty curve is quite steep: the ammount of dying in this game might put you off. And it's certainly put me off a couple times. It's a fun game, but hard.<br>Second, it might just be a problem on my side, but I seem to crash frequently. This is made even worse by a really weird bug source games have in my computer (when I try to exit a source game, the screen gets stuck in a black screen until I change the display port and then back to the one my screen uses. I don't understand it either), which makes the crashing even more tedious.<br><br>Overall, I have just fallen in love with this game. My hour count so far is tiny and my skill is even worse, but I hope to improve them soon. It's a lot of fun, it's very well designed, and it's obvious that the devs are doing their best about this game.
