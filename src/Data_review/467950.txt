class=\"postedDate\">Posted: July 23, 2016\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t<div class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSurvive in Space is a space shooter that blends Defender's left and right controls with Asteroid's single screen presence. I like Survive in Space, but its still tough to recommend, because the entire game is a grind. The 50 levels that the game boasts are all the same. Each level consists of three waves of enemies, each enemy gives experience that you use to buy upgrades. Even with upgrades, I never feel like I'm making little progress, because each level feels the same.<br><br>Like all shooters, you have your basic machine gun, but to make things interesting, there is a fat laser that lasts a few seconds and homing missiles. There seems to be a color coated mechanic where when you're red, the four orbs get tucked close to your ship while when you're red they're far away. Once your a super weapon get used, you need to wait for them to recharge. Since enemies spawn left and right on a single screen, the game has mercy on you and lets you face left or right ala Defender. Because you push a button, that means you can fly right while facing left and vice versa.<br><br>You have a versatile set of controls and the game does a good job of explaining them. You can use mouse, keyboard or a controller, but some of the menus seem to need a mouse.<br><br>The enemies feel like a good variety and they feel different than other games of the same caliber. The problem is that you see them all on each level, making each level feel the same. To the game's credit, every new level includes a new enemy, but they're always hard to spot when mixed in with the same foes that you've seen for hours. Your foes come at you from all different angles, so you need to stay in front of them and destroy them before they can come at you from awkward positions.<br><br>To break up the monotony of what it is, there are boss fights every 10 or so levels. Each boss has several bullet patterns as you fight them one-on-one. These feel like a good diversion among a sea of the same. Its odd that I can enjoy a classic arcade game to no end, but when I feel like I'm locked into a 50 level campaign, it begins to feel like a chore. Perhaps if I took it lighter and not as a goal and just played the game over the span of years I'd feel better about it.<br><br>The upgrades come in tiers. You first make a few minor upgrades to each weapon and then make a few major upgrades before the next tier unlocks. The upgrades include shorter recharge, more experience, more damage, vampire health from enemies, the chance to kill an enemy with one shot or do critical damage. Everything makes the game a fraction easier, but its never enough to notice a difference. One of the double-edged swords is the upgrade to split beams. In theory that sounds great, but in practice you shoot in a V which means you need to maneuver around differently when enemies are coming toward you.<br><br>On the plus side, you can upgrade your vessel too to do more damage, have better shields, more armor and so on. Since this is a game about killing things, I recommend just pour everything into attack power. The quicker your foes die, the safer you are.<br><br>Some of your own attacks are questionable. The strong straight laser feels powerful, but the homing missiles are slow and detonate if you destroy the vessel they were aiming at. So by the time they get to their target, its dead, making the missiles almost useless. Good thing there is an achievement for using them. One thing that the game has going for it are the easy achievements that a low percentage of people have.<br><br>Perhaps the most aggravating part of Survive in Space are the cheap deaths that come in two forms. The first are the invisible ships that decloak. You can see them and they move in a slow, straight path, but when it gets chaotic they can decloak in on you. The other cheap death is something similar where a vessel will make a portal. The portal starts small so you can see it... unless you sit and shoot, then the portal opens wide and throws out a vessel. These two cheap deaths are the only time I ever die.<br><br>To the game's credit, you can make your own challenge for each level. Its more then difficulty, there are three check boxes for more enemies, fortresses that fly by and shoot at you and asteroids. Yes asteroids fly at you, which makes me feel the Asteroids vibe even more. I have gotten to the point where I am unstoppable so I add asteroids and fortresses. You get extra experience for the extra challenge. More enemies seems to spawn a little too much to handle and when this is a grind, you need to play it safe.<br><br>I have avoided perhaps the worst thing about the game and that is the art. In the game, the art is fine, but there are several cut scenes with art that just feels out of place. I am sure someone worked hard on the art, but it cheapens the look of the game. In the end, I'm just there for the arcade experience, so I skip the cut scenes.<br><br>Another downside to the game is with your ability to chose between red and blue on the fly means you shoot the same color of bullets. However, enemies fire their own color coated bullets, so if you're red and red bullets are being fired at you, then they just blend in with your own. Now that I've said it, you do have plenty of health and shields to compensate.<br><br>If you need a reason to stay in the game longer, there are other vessels to play. That might keep your interest, but after the sheer investment of time into one vessel, its best to just stick with what you know.<br><br>In the end, I'm still on the fence if I can recommend it. Its enjoyable, but a chore. Its fun, but there's just too much of not enough. There are other similar games out there, that feel better to play or have more charm or a better look. Survive in Space is still a fun time waster, but I'd like more diversity when I'm playing the same level over and over again with a new hat. received this product for no cost for the purpose of reviewing. However, this does not affect my views and opinions of the game.<br><br>The team at Just1337 must really love space and space themed games, as this is there 4th space game on Steam. Survive in Space is a 2D Space Shooter. The story is kind of interesting so I won't spoil it, but throughout the stages of of this game, you pilot a space fighter. You can choose between 5 initially, though I'm not sure if more unlock as you continue to play. Each ship has a set passive abilities that are different from the other ships, and you can choose a ship to cater to your playing style. You can also switch what ship you want to use before each stage. You have a basic attack and 2 other skills you can use in combat, and after each stage your character receives experience as well the ship that you are using. When your ship levels up you can assign more passive abilities to it, and the same applies to your character. <br><br>The combat itself puts you in a space battlefield for each stage. You are tasked with taking down all enemy ships as they appear on your screen with your various abilities to complete the stage. You can manage the difficulty of each battle from the classic presets of &quot;Easy, Medium &amp; Hard&quot; as well as choose to add more asteroids (which pose as flying obstacles) as well increasing the amount of enemies you have to defeat. Of course the more difficult you make it for yourself, the more experience your will accumulate. The game can be a bit bullet hell-y at times, and there are boss battles at certain stages. <br><br>--------<br><br><b> Graphics: 6 \/ 10 <\/b><br><br>The art style might be subjective. It's a 2D western animeish design for the characters, which isn't something I tend to enjoy but I could see how a lot of people would appreciate it. The back drop for the battles is decent, though I feel it does get a bit repetitive after awhile, although I haven't played all the stages to know if it's different later on. The animations are also decent, and the combat effects as well as the menus are designed pretty nicely. There shouldn't be any problems running the title on any graphics card or CPU as it is not a demanding title.<br><br>--------<br><br><b> Sound: 8 \/ 10 <\/b><br><br>Everyone has different tastes in music, but I feel like anyone who can enjoy EDM music; this title has a nice soundtrack for you. I personally enjoy the music in this game, it reminds me a lot of their other title Neon Space, which I believe some of the music is also from. I noticed in the music interface that they have various types of EDM styles like: Drumbass, Dubstep, Electronic and House music, and as someone who enjoys this type of music, I would rate it pretty highly. There is also options to skip to the next track as well as shuffle and repeat features. The in-game and combat sound effects are also decent and surprisingly didn't feel repetitive. There is also some voice acting within the game which is also very subjective. <br><br>--------<br><br><b> Mechanics: 6 \/ 10 <\/b><br><br>For a title like this, the mechanics are pretty solid. You have your basic attack, and your 2 other skills. You more around with either a controller, your arrow keys, or your mouse, and shoot down enemies on your screen while avoiding their fire. Unlike some shooters, you also have the option of flipping your ship and shooting in the opposite direction. I do find it a tad frustrating not being able to shoot up and down at times, though I feel the controls might be too messy if something like that was implemented. You can switch between two different modes while playing, attack mode and defense mode, which both offer benefits and disadvantages. The enemy variety is pretty good, there's quite a few different looking enemy ships with different abilities. You can upgrade your ship as well as your character with experience at the end of each battle as well as toggle the difficulty level. <br><br>--------<br><br><b> Final Personal Remarks <\/b><br><br>I think Survive in Space is a good title for beginners who want to get into 2D shooters. It's not as intense as some bullet hells but it can give you some rush and satisfaction when you're able to take down all the enemies. The art style isn't  something I personally enjoy but I do like the soundtrack and how you can upgrade your ships as well as your character. The story seems interesting but also kind of clich\u00e9, but I see it hooking a lot of people. In my experience with playing the game, I preferred to stay in attack mode rather than defense mode. It's a bit riskier when you take a hit because you take more damage in addition to having your health points already lowered, but you deal a lot more damage, and it's easier to take out enemies.
