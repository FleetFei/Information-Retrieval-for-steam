Now why would a gamer like me with several thousand games (some of which might actually be good) waste time to play this? For achievements of course.<br><br>Now that my lousy motivation is out of the way, let's get dirty with the details. Being a clicker game the gameplay itself isn't very complex. A monster appears on the screen. Clicking it will damage it. Once it gets enough damage it dies and bleeds gold, with which you can buy upgrades and train your heroes to be able to beat higher, more difficult monster waves.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">A game with a story<\/div><br><i>Tap Tap Infinity<\/i> becomes distinct from other similar games by having a story. It's very basic though. Some monsters have appeared and the world is in danger, so you have to beat them up. A catch is that beating monsters will &quot;bring them to their senses&quot; and then you can hire them to fight for you.<br><br>It's also 3D: what a time to be alive, huh? Models are fine but not exceptional. Some of them looks rather ridiculous but that's likely a personal preference and you really shouldn't make fun of stupid-looking monsters because that might hurt their feelings.<br><br>I disliked the sound effects that I found to be too sharp and having too many high notes. Music is fun though, but since its loop is not terribly long, I found myself muting the game 99% of the time.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Grind is real<\/div><br>Typically in clicker games there's a restart mechanism. This one is no exception: after a while you can go <i>infinite!<\/i> You'll be doing that a lot, since it gives you Infinity tokens that are used to buy permanent upgrades to e.g. gold farming and damage your heroes and clicks make. They also grant a fixed bonus to your DPS (damage per second).<br><br>But wait, there's more! Killing monsters and beating waves grants you experience, that increases your rank that directly affects all damage you make and also unlocks active skills.<br><br>Skills are a bit different from other clicker games on Steam, which is fun. <i>Boss Rush<\/i> is probably the best of them, allowing you to skip through several hundreds of waves by only killing boss monsters (usually every 5 waves). Since there is no skill to increase the DPS over a single run (no equivalent of <i>Dark Ritual<\/i> from CH), deep runs are less useful (which is good: I find them boring). But grind is real, so you can also upgrade the skills with Infinity tokens.<br><br>Finally, Infinity tokens can be used to <i>Ascend<\/i>, which resets the rank. Ascension roughly doubles the DPS in the long run.<br><br>Your heroes also get kind of experience tokens (i.e. Valor tokens), which are used with the in-game currency, gems, to upgrade the heroes.<br><br>The grind, while not infinite, is still great if you are going for all the achievements.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Convenience is the key<\/div><br>Fortunately one can play <i>Tap Tap Infinity<\/i> in many ways. Both casual playing of couple of minutes and long sessions are supported. Once you close the game it will progress through the waves until it reaches a spot where it no longer can beat the boss wave.<br><br>You can in effect return once in a while to use the skill combos. Still, it's slightly faster to proceed and you can get farther into the waves when playing actively, since click damage outdoes the idle damage. You could even play hybrid, only succumbing to clicking when it becomes too slow to progress by idling.<br><br>Game is generous with idling: you could click one moment and after just a few seconds the idle mode starts again, and of the skills only Clickstorm (which grants a constant, frantic clicking of monsters) breaks the idle mode.<br><br>Conveniently, there's a direct link to Wiki from the game so the optimizing players find their thing easily.<br><br>Finally, while the game doesn't have Steam cloud saves, it does support cloud saving through their own servers. I could still struggle to reach the final wave 5000 but I'm good, thank you very much.<br><br>A paying player could probably cut a few tens or hundreds of hours out of that, since one of the achievements directly depends on the in-game purchasable gems. Thankfully, even a non-paying player can grind them out and even succeed before doing the last of the super-grindy ascension cheevos.<br><br>It's not a terribly good game, but it's not bad either. I would recommend <i>Tap Tap Infinity<\/i> for a busy person who doesn't have that much time for gaming but who also likes to hunt achievements.
