Arma 3 Altis Life was great for this, and Enforcer: Police Crime Action is quite similar, only it is single player. You can drive around the town centre looking out for speeders (cars drive around 20mph and around 30mph is considered speeding) and you can pull them over, use the breathalyser or frisk them if you feel it is necessary. On the start of every shift there is a passive mission to complete by the end of the shift, for example setting up a roadblock with cones and checking 6 drivers for faults on their cars. There are also dispatch calls with generous time limits such as finding a person wanted for assaulting passers by. For this you would go and talk to the victims to get a description, then wait until there is intel about where the person was last seen. You would then go to the scene, find the person matching the description, and park up. Normally when you call out for them to stop, they will run off. You can pull out your tazer, and when you get close you fire. After that you can arrest the suspect, take them to the station or call for a transport. You get career points which help you rank up after completing every mission.<br><br>Off duty mode is really unique, and has a lot of potential. When you have enough money you can buy a car, and you can also customise your home with new furniture. It is also possible to level up your stress level buy going out with friends or your mother, and other people. Instead of actually interacting with your friends or mother, the game teleports you to their house and puts the time forward a few hours, and says that you have spent time with your friends \/ mother, without you actually seeing anything. Physically doing activities may well be something the developers add in the future. When off duty it is also possible to fill up on food and replenish your energy by sleeping. You have to be careful though as spending too much time off duty will reduce career points.<br><br>Conclusion:<br><br>This game as mentioned earlier has obviously been developed on a low budget, but they have made that budget stretch as far as possible. The gameplay is enjoyable even though the graphics and physics are not fantastic. Now it has been released, it is likely that if lots of people buy it and the developers have access to more money, the game will be better optimized, feel more smooth and have some great new features added. That will of course come over time so if anything, the money you put in to buy this game will be an investment, so they can improve it.
