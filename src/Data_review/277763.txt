I bought shortly after buying Train Simulator when it was TS2015 last year. I like the Grand Central livery as it does suit the Class 180 very nicely in my opinion. The cab view is one of my favourites on a passenger train as it is streamlined and modern which I like. The physics are very good and balanced as well as it doesn't take too long to get to line speed of 125mph (and higher to around 132 of so if you break the speed limit) and there is a speed set button which can be pressed which will hold the train at that speed or a couple of miles under. But it disengages when you move the power handle though. Which can be either a good thing or a bad thing. The sounds however are not so great and perhaps the horn could be calibrated better so that a smooth two tone sequence of the horn could be achieved better. The brakes a bit slack as well as it takes a while to come a stop from line speed (more than in a HST!). But apart from the sounds and the weak brakes, this is a very nice pack from DTG and if you own the London to Peterborough route. There is a couple of scenarios supplied for career mode that can be played. I would have liked some standard mode scenarios as I don't really play arcade mode much. But its certainly a train pack to consider getting and there is some reskins available of the Class 180 in First Great Western, GWR, Northern Rail and Hull Trains that have been made by the train
