decent HOG title and one of the earliest games coming from the relatively small Czech developer, Icarus Games. The game presents an interesting and well detailed supernatural storyline, ending up with a couple loose ends, convenient for developing future installation as series.<br><br>Amanda Waters is a mother of two young children, visiting the nearby antique train exhibition to see Runaway Express, a famous locomotive hailing from early 1900s. It's all fun and games until the appearance of a mysterious engineer and the train taking off with Amanda's children on board! Determined to find her children, Amanda catches the train in last minute. From thereon, she'll meet many strange passengers on the train - long dead and lost in time. If she is to find her children, she should solve their mysteries and shed light into their stories first. With each new clues she gathers, Amanda gets closer to saving her children. Can she solve the dangerous mystery of Runaway Express, revealing the dreadful affair of its last journey and return her children home safely?<br><br>As you can once again observe, a loved one in danger + a supernatural mystery dynamic never fails in HOGs. This game is no different, aside the remarkably irritable personality of our protagonist. Amanda seems to be immune to fear or surprise, and amazingly fixated on finding her children only. She is a mother first and foremost, okay. But she embarks on a train, meeting corpses and travelling back in time for God's sake! She keeps walking around, reprimanding living corpses (!) and demanding explanations about the whereabouts of her children from them! It's amazing to watch her reactions and listen to her comments. This whole eerie setting has literally no effect on her. Once she is declared that the only way to save her children would be solving the mystery, she gets into the investigation yet keeps challenging the rules, looking for shortcuts all the way. The mystery itself doesn't concern the mother of the year. Talk about one track mind. Imagine your mother reprimanding Frankenstein's monster - that is Amanda Waters for you. Now that would be hilarious.<br><br>Visuals are beautiful, and the atmosphere is appropriately rustic. Background artist knew what he was doing. Character designs are well done too. Talking animations of characters are a bit clumsy, but they have impressive facial mimics and body gestures. All characters are voice acted with clean cut accents. Listening to Evelyn's heavy Aussie accent was cute, for example.<br><br>The main attraction of the game is the investigation process for each new case about various passengers you meet. You walk around, gather clues about their pasts and try to solve their murders a century after their demise. You are expected to arrange your clues on a crime board, explaining the course of events. There are not many HOG scenes, but the ones that we have are short and easy to manage. Puzzles are mostly innovative and interesting. The train is huge - with a couple of visited stations along the way - and backtracking may become an issue if you are not playing in casual difficulty, but the map has a teleport function at least.<br><br>The ending was a bit anticlimactic, I won't lie. The mystery of the train is revealed alright - with a nicely arranged inter-relevant background story, but the nature of the engineer is not explained at all. It is hinted that this won't be the last time we'll be facing him as our enemy. Over all, <i>Runaway Express Mystery<\/i> is a good casual game for you to spare 3-4 hours and explore a nice story. Hit it when you'd feel like having a light reading on the mystery department.
