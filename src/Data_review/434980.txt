I was quite impressed by the game, and found myself drawn to it and it's very simplistic gameplay.  Onion Force makes for a nice casual game play experience, it's a game that can be enjoyed by people of almost any age and gameplaying ability.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"> Gameplay <\/div><br>The game play is simple, you select a stage, <b> (the first time you play it'll be on easy, after that you have the choice of med or hard)<\/b>, then you select which hero you want to use.. Each level has 5 stages, with the fifth one being the most difficult and containing a unique &quot;boss&quot; battle, although most of the bosses aren't too much more difficult than the normal enemies.  The game features three different heroes, the Warrior, archer and mage, each one has a slightly different approach on how to defeat enemies.   The warrior is as you may have suspected, the upfront meat head type, continually smash into enemies until they are dead, rinse and repeat, I personally found myself using the warrior twice as much as the other two heroes.  Personally felt the other two were slightly under-powered in terms of beast slaying, although they are viable choices albeit much more options.  The goal of the game is to protect your king from being overwhelmed and killed by the enemies.  <br><br>Throughout the game you'll get various drops from the enemies, the first of which are coins, which you can collect and use to build your various towers, the second collectible will be onions, which are used to purchase spell buffs prior to the stage. The game also has weapons which can drop for the various heroes, you can equip them prior to the stage, the weapons increase attack power and sometimes have a unique bonus added to them.  You also get coins by beating stages, which are used for upgrading your towers.  Although this proves to be a double edged sword as each new upgrade, increases the cost of building that particular tower.  The gameplay is relatively fun although can get repetitive after a while<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">  Combat <\/div><br>Each of the three heroes has their own unique brand of attacking.  The Warrior bumps into enemies and takes down the enemies health.  He has the most health and deals the most melee damage.  The archer as you may have guessed is given a bow which shoots at enemies from am distance.  You can also bump into enemies while playing as the Archer but don't expect to last very long, the archer can only take 4 or 5 bumps before he's killed.  The mage attacks with an AOE cone of damage, it damages and pushes back enemies, a great tactic involves standing just near a tower as the wizard and continually pushing enemies back so the tower can hit them.<br><br>The two bars on your heroes are health and stamina, the warrior loses health and stamina with each attack, the archer and mage lose stamina with each attack and only lose health if they melee an enemy. You have the choice of using 1 of 3 heroes - a warrior (tank), bowman (ranged damage), or a wizard (ranged AoE damage).  You can start with any of the 3 at the beginning of a map, and can swap in another if you happen to die before you finish it.  You're also given stars for completing maps, which can be used unlock and upgrade different types of towers to place.  Clearing maps also gives experience, so you can level up any of your 3 heroes.<br><br>I played this game when it very first hit Steam, and it had a few issues.  Controls were wonky, I lost my save data.. I gave it a chance, but it didn't work out.  I lost the willpower.<br><br>However, I recently came back to give it another shot, and learned that the developers had improved the control scheme to allow WASD controls.  This dramatically improved the gameplay.  Interestingly, I also found my old save data on my computer; apparently the save folder changed during one of the earlier updates.<br><br>As I write this, I've just finished the game on hard mode, and overall the whole experience left me with a positive vibe.  It's not without flaws, though.  Below, I'll share some of what I liked and didn't like.<br><br>PROS<br>------<br>1) The stat progression feels rewarding.  In this game, as you reach higher levels, you start to gain more stats (attack, defense, and health) per level.  This makes it so each level is exciting to gain - I wish more games recognized this and followed the example.<br><br>2) It plays well in general.  Bashing into enemies as the warrior, my favorite class, often feels like a pinball machine of pain (THEIR pain), as I bounce from mob to mob.  You can do this as the other classes as well, but they don't have as much defense and health.<br><br>3) The 3 separate difficulty modes are somewhat tiered, such that you're not expected to beat hard mode from the start.  This allows you to go back and replay levels for more experience.  Granted, you don't have to beat level 30 on easy to start level 1 on medium.  Each difficulty setting is a good nudge up in both challenge and reward, though.<br><br>4) My kids find the talkative fairy creepy, but I think she adds a nice touch to the game.  She never did tell me something about the last boss, though!<br><br>5) If there's a level cap, it's high.  I finished the game at level 40, and still have an experience bar filling up.  I like it when a game allows me to decide when I'll stop leveling up, not the other way around.<br><br>6) Stable - zero crashes.<br><br>CONS<br>------<br>1) The stamina system isn't fun.  This is one of the reasons I picked warrior as my primary.  He just bashes into things, and stamina loss doesn't seem to impact him much.  The bowman and wizard, however, are massively dependent on stamina.  Once you run out, you can no longer perform a ranged attack, and have to either use a heart or go to a healing tent to replenish.  This felt tedious, and I wasn't a fan.  The wizard can only perform 9 casts before having to refill.  Gaining more stamina on each level up would have been an easy fix for this.<br><br>2) Items with magical suffixes need tooltips.  I never really know what they do until I play with them.  If one replenishes stamina, that would be a fix for the wizard, but I didn't toy around enough to find out.  &quot;Of the gods&quot; gives health regen though, so that was a sweet deal for the warrior.<br><br>3) Class balance doesn't seem right.  The wizard in particular feels very underpowered, mostly due to the stamina system, and not being able to continuously cast.  The overall damage from her spells feels low as well, or maybe it's the fire rate that suffers more?<br><br>4) Very limited settings.  It's apparent that this game wasn't originally developed for the PC.<br><br><br>All in all, this game delivers what it promises - it's a satisfying tower defense \/ action rpg.  There are annoyances, but I don't believe they're numerous enough to warrant not buying the game.  5 bucks is a steal; I paid that much for my coffee this morning.  I would recommend this title to other fans of the genre.<br><br>Thanks for fun experience, devs.  I hope to see more of your stuff in the future, or possibly more updates to this game.
