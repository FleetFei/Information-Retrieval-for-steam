The Light and Dark side, if you will. Let's start with the former.<br><br>Droids is the most approachable of the tables, just requiring you to combo up some loops, though the modes get more difficult to nail. While there's nothing exceptional about it, the droid theme is fun and it has very good flow to it. The video modes stand out quite a bit, as well, in general, a good start.<br><br>Han Solo is one of my favorite Star Wars tables, though I'd recognize it's not for everyone. It has a generally odd layout and quirks that take some time to learn, and doesn't have any modes to it. However, I think the Cantina theme is a hell of a lot of fun, and I'll never, ever get sick of Harrison Ford's running yell when fleeing Stormtroopers.<br><br>A New Hope mainly has you doing relatively dull loops, and the slings will easily throw your ball into the outlanes. It's just not what it should have been.<br><br>Masters of the Force is probably of the hardest tables Zen has ever done, with some farcially difficult shots... and requiring you to pull off some aerials with a janky physics engine that just doesn't make doing that user-friendly in the slightest. Very little in this table could be deemed &quot;fun&quot;, save for trying to puzzle out what on Earth the designers were thinking.<br><br>Overall, Heroes Within is probably a more divisive pack and it's hard to say how one might feel about it. I have a deep love for the Han Solo table, but recognize its obtuse qualities probably don't have wider appeal.
