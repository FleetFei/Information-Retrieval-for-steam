class=\"bb_h1\">Intro<\/div>Goscurry starts with the game showing you a warning message. I don\u2019t know the exact words by heart but it pretty much comes down to the following: by playing the game you agree that you\u2019re going to play an extremely hard game which, of course, is both scary and exciting. So which one of the two is it really?<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay<\/div>When you start your first game you\u2019ll probably crash within just a few seconds, and if you\u2019re lucky it might take a few seconds longer, or, in the best case scenario, a few minutes. Either way it won\u2019t take long before you crash your ship. It\u2019s most likely the simple visuals that fooled you.<br><br>The gameplay is reminiscent to games from the arcades where the story usually didn\u2019t matter and the gameplay was shallow, but hard and addictive. Goscurry is exactly that; it\u2019s extremely simple in its execution and very shallow, but at the same time you can\u2019t help but restart the game every time you crash. It\u2019s very addictive but needs to be played in same doses. It\u2019s not a game meant to be played for hours and hours, but, in the end, that\u2019s still up to you of course.<br><br>This really is game of skill, not memory, because the tracks are randomly generated and you need to have good reflexes if you want to make it far. Tracks will get increasingly more difficult and the speed will also ramp up and if you don\u2019t pay attention it will take but a millisecond to make a fatal mistake. But you\u2019ll try again and again if you have the determination.<br><br>Score enough points in a mode and do it several times and you\u2019ll unlock new ships to should make things a little easier for you while also providing a bit of new eye candy. Problem is that it will take several hours before you\u2019ll have unlocked a second one. I only managed to unlock one and I had a hard time doing it.<br><br>The first mode is called Training mode and if you die here you probably have no idea what to expect from the Hard and Pro modes. The fact that it\u2019s called Training mode is discouraging to newcomers; it takes at least 30 minutes, most likely even longer, to get the hang of things. In Training mode the camera will follow you so that it\u2019s easy\/easier to turn and there are no obstacles you have to avoid. In Hard mode the camera will follow you but there will be obstacles and in Pro mode the camera will be fixed, making things even harder. Don\u2019t ask me what the Speedfreak or Paranoid modes do because, to be honest, I didn\u2019t want to find out.<br><br>Besides the obstacles there are some visual distractions. Distractions that bothered me. I\u2019m not sure if they\u2019re there for the fun of it or if they\u2019re really there to distract you because that is what it felt like to me. The subliminal messages, the messages on the buildings, the pop-in messages that show you the city and\/or tracks; it all distracted me. At first, at least, because later on you learn how to automatically ignore these distractions. Still, I wish this was done differently. It feels like an artificial way to make the game harder.<br><br>In the intro I also talked about the warning\/disclaimer message that the game shows you pretty much immediately after booting it up. It feels unnecessary to have this message in the game if people already bought your game. People looking for a way to relax might quit the game immediately and never look back. I\u2019d rather see this message posted on the product page, where it belongs in the first place.<br>Now, I haven\u2019t really talked about how the game controls. I\u2019m sorry for this; the game feels like a simplified Audiosurf where the game accelerates for you and it\u2019s up to you to move left, right, backward or forward while avoiding or jumping over obstacles and gaps. The controls are very good and responsive and make the game a joy to play (don\u2019t think this too literally, though ;)).<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Audio &amp; Graphics<\/div>Musically Goscurry is pretty good. It has some electronic, up-tempo beats that give off a lot of energy and keep you going. Other than that there isn\u2019t much to be found here as far as audio is concerned.<br><br>The visuals aren\u2019t the best in the world but they get the job done. You have a colorful ship and a colorful city with very basic geometry (I don\u2019t know if it looks any different\/better if you get any further. I\u2019m afraid I wasn\u2019t able to pull it off.) It\u2019s very simplistic but it\u2019s all very clear and one of the things that actually doesn\u2019t manage to distract. I talked about Audiosurf earlier and the game has that same kind of vibe, minus the neon (effects).<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Length<\/div>Here is where Goscurry really shines.
