It breaks away from many of the traditional stylings of the point-and-click adventure genre and offers a nice experience that will appeal to those who want to stay within the genre without having to settle for another clone of a clone of a clone of a hand-holding, linear game.<br><br>The aesthetics and presentation of the game neither add value to the game nor do they take any away. It doesn\u2019t have the prettiest graphics, the trendiest soundtrack, or the most refined atmosphere. And, as is always the case with the genre, it is very relaxing and casual. You are mostly free to solve the puzzles at your own leisure and should never feel any pressure to succeed. Most of the gameplay consists of gathering the correct inventory items and giving them to the correct NPC, however some of the puzzles are more like mini-games and there are also a number of hidden-object-pictures as well.<br><br>But what mostly allots this game its charmingly unique identity is the relatively large and very open world. Instead of being ushered from one plot point to the next as you solve puzzles and complete tasks, you are free to explore the entire city at your own pace and complete tasks in any order you choose (save for those which are locked behind other solutions). The main goal is to acquire a certain amount of money which unlocks the ending area and allows you to \u201cbeat\u201d the game. That money is obtained by completing the various tasks available.<br><br>Through this openness inherent in the gameworld, the quirkiness of <b>City of Fools<\/b> really comes to life. As you wander around and meet the various NPCs, collect many inventory items, and slowly build up currency, you will be exposed to all sorts of simple playfulness and silliness. You will quickly come to find that it certainly is a city of fools.<br><br>There are some noticeable flaws to the game as well, but nothing egregious in any way. For one, there are so many tasks to keep track of.  There is a journal which will point you in the right direction for each task, but it doesn\u2019t remind you of the location of the task-giver. And so you may occasionally find yourself struggling to remember where to find the NPC who wanted to buy the &lt;Insert Item&gt; you just obtained. Relatedly, there is a map which will help you out, but no fast-travel option. Not too big of a deal, because exploring the city is part of the fun; however towards the end of the game when you\u2019re just trying to finish up, it becomes more noticeable that a fast-travel would be appreciated.<br><br>In summary, <b>City of Fools<\/b> is an enjoyable and quaint little game. It won\u2019t appeal to everyone, but fans of the point-and-click genre will very likely appreciate the novelty of the game design and won\u2019t even have to leave their comfort zone.
