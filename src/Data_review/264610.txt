paid DLC to be released for PAYDAY 2, giving a go to a charade of downloadable content of varying quality locked behind a paywall. There are some borderline-necessary DLCs one should consider investing into, as well as ones better left out. This one stands in the middle of the two.<br><br><b>Heists<\/b><br><br><i>Armored Transport<\/i> does away with heists with complicated structure, offering in return a more straight-on approach to increasing your cash reserves - raiding GenSec armored cars much in the fashion of the famous truck robbery scene from <i>Heat<\/i>. This is further reinforced by the free &quot;Hockey Heat&quot; mask available to players, which resembles the one worn by McCauley and the crew.<br><br>The heists' payout differs along with difficulty: easier ones, such as Park and Harbor offer little challenge and a relatively small reward, while tougher ones, such as Crossroads, provide a hefty income at the expense of increased difficulty, with enemies attacking the players from all sides and snipers swarming the crime scene. The goal, however, is always the same: raid the trucks and get away with as much loot as possible.<br><br>There is, however the sixth, secret heist, which can be accessed by finding a military blueprint in one of the Transport heists. Situated in the vicinity of a mountain train station, this heist requires the team to steal a prototype turret <span class=\"bb_spoiler\"><span>which heavily resembles Engineer's turret from TF2<\/span><\/span>, as well as its ammo, either stealthily or loud. The diversity of the heist compared to its Transport counterparts, as well as an increased payout and a non-linear approach to it make stand out greatly among the heists in this DLC.<br><br><b>Weapons<\/b><br><br>This DLC provides three new weapons for the player to use: 1 assault rifle, Commando 553, 1 SMG, Swedish K, and 1 pistol, Gruber Kurz. Despite being in different classes, these weapons share the ability to be highly-concealable, making them viable for stealth or dodge builds, as well as them being available at any level, thus giving new or reranking players more guns to choose from.<br><br>Commando 553 is a well-rounded assault rifle that comes with a hefty ammo pool and potential for high concealment. This, however, is offset by the weapon's low accuracy and damage, as well as the fact that most mods can only be obtained through Card Drops or Continental Coins.<br><br>Swedish K is a hard-hitting SMG, offering a base damage of 80, as well as high magazine capacity. The drawbacks for this gun are subpar accuracy at range, full-auto lock and obscured iron sights. Still, it is worth bringing this SMG as a back-up weapon for Enforcers with OVE 9000 saws.<br><br>Gruber Kurz is a highly compact pistol with a lot of reserve ammo and low recoil, maintaining good concealment even with a supressor. However, the damage on this gun is mediocre without certain skills, like Specialized Killing or Fast and Furious, and so is the accuracy.<br><br><b>Masks<\/b><br><br>The masks that come with this DLC are modeled after four US presidents - Richard Nixon, Bill Clinton, George Bush and Barack Obama, which seem to be inspired by the masks donned by the protagonists of 1991's <i>Point Break<\/i>, as well as the Presidential masks from PAYDAY: The Heist.<br><br><b>Conclusion<\/b><br><br>It is a matter of good consideration whether to buy this DLC or not. The five Transport heists are repetative and do not offer high payouts compared to other heists, however the Train one is definitely worth digging through the deposit boxes in GenSec trucks. Keep in mind that everything I say here is my personal opinion.<br><br>The Armored Transport DLC adds the following:<br><br>3 new weapons:<br>-The Commando 553 assault rifle<br>-The Gruber Kurz pistol<br>-The Swedish K submachine gun<br>The weapons are pretty much reskins of other things we already have in the game, if not the free alternatives perform better than these DLC ones. However, the Gruber already starts at pretty high concealment and the Commando and Swedish K can be fairy modded to have some concealment, so you can utilise a dodge build with these weapons.<br><br>6 new heists <br>-Transport:Park; Harbor; Underpass; Downtown; Crossroads<br>-Transport: Train<br>These heists are really one set in 5 different locations(the former) and one unique Heist(Transport:Train). The 5 heists are standard traditional armored car hits where you ambush a convoy od 3\/4 cars. You have to drill through the doors (or use c4 with the right skills) and then colllect the loot from the deposit boxes in the cars(this can be done quicker with a saw). These heists are loud only. Transport:Train is a heist in which, true to the title, you hit a Train in an attempt to steal a new experimental turret and it's ammo. In stealth you drill through the wagon doors or use a keycard if you find one or saw if you have one. You can also use ECMs to open the doors. Drills and saws are a more risky method as they can be seen and heard. Once inside the three wagons, you will find three safes, two containing 10 bags of ammo each and one containing the turret that is bagged in 3 separate parts. You open the safe using the hard drive that you are provided with at the beginning of the heist, during opening the safes, the timelock will forcefully stop, to get it going again you have to lockpick and rewire the circuit boxes of which there are 4, one in each corner of the railway. You have to secure the turret parts before you secure the ammo. After that you're finished. Loud is similar, except you open the safes with thermal drills, of which you get two, so you can open two at a time. The train heist is both fun in stealth and loud. The other 5 transports can be a great challenge on higher difficulties seeing as there are spots with few cover on these heists. However, these heists are pretty basic ones and yoir opinion on them varies if you prefer traditional style heists like banks and armored car hits, or if your preference is some hollywood style heisting(and example is point break's Beneath The Mountain and Birth of Sky heists).<br><br>4 new masks, patterns and materials:<br>I won't say much about these as they are only cosmetic. The masks are themes around the most famous american presidents, and it's pretty funny to use them. The materials and patterns look good and more customization is always a plus.<br><br>Final thoughts:<br>This DLC was inpored by the movie Heat, and fans of the movie will like this DLC. If you also like stealthing, you will like this DLC, seeing as the train heist is fun, and the Gruber Kurz is a 30 concealment secondary. However, if you're a loud only player and haven't seen Heat, you can give this pack a pass.<br><br>My personal opinion:<br>I would recommend this DLC. The transports are a great challenge on higher difficulties and the train heist is one of my favorites  the weapons are not all thaf but they can be concealed pretty well.
