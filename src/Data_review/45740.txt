Full price would be a bit much, but it is a fun game to run around and kill zombies in.  It's reasonably fun and has a lot of gore and humor in it.  But there are a few problems that drag it down and make it somewhat tedius, and which keep you from really getting pulled in.<br><br>The biggest issue is how extremely repetetive the gameplay is.  It relies on you never getting tired of killing hordes of non-threatening zombies, and while there is a lot of novelty with weird outfits and strange found-item weapons, it wears off after a couple hours because of how monotonous the game is.  Every side mission is exactly the same: kill a hundred zombies, find an NPC, escort them back killing another hundred zombies.  Most of the game becomes an escort quest with poor AI, which is tiring.  The main story missions are more entertaining, but not because of any interesting characters or story, just because of how insanely over the top the voice acting and cut scenes are.  These are some of the best parts of the game, and all feel like they are out of some horrible B movie that doesn't realize how terrible it is, and where the actors are all either really bad, or are too talented for the material and are hamming it up for a laugh.  The actual game play in those missions is still pretty dull.<br><br>Another major issue is the controls.  Movement is choppy and sluggish.  Whether zombies can grab you or not appears to be entirely random.  Most of the ones that look like they will get you miss entirely, most that grab you seem to come from nowhere.  In the stages where you are driving it's even more skittish, and the gun controls are surprisingly bad.  Guns are almost worthless except for the shotgun, but even that is frustrating because of how hard it is to actually hit things.  You more or less move on a 45 degree turret, so any kind of precision turning is all but impossible, and aiming, unless you go down ironsights, basically consists of pointing at the enemy, not moving, shooting, and hopping the auto-target works.  Aiming manually is slow and not particuarly accurate either.  This barely matters against the zombies, who are easy to kill and avoid, but against the bossess, who have a sharp spike in difficulty from the regular game, it can get you killed a lot as you try to wrestle with the controls.<br><br>Another problem is that everything is done with time constraints.  For a game that derives most of its entertainment value from sandbox exploration, it's strange to constantly have timers counting down on all your quests, and to have an overall timer on the game at large.  This doesn't hugely impact the game unless you're a completionist and actually care about rescuring every survivor, in which case I imagine it would be a pain.  The worst part of this, though, is your stupid kid with her stupid Zombrex medicine.  You have to bring her the medicine every 24 hours, but you can't give it to her, you can't show up late, and you can't show up early, so you spend a lot of time running back to base, then waiting for her to accept her medicine, because I guess if she takes it ten minutes early she will explode or something.<br><br>I played the original Dead Rising when it launched and lost interest after 10 hours or so because it was repetetive and pedantic.  This one feels like it has most of the same problems, but is saved by co-op.  I would reccomend it at half price or less if you have a friend to play with who enjoys zombies and mayhem and whatnot.
