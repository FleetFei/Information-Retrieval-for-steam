I don't buy early access based on future promises, and it has enough to offer right now for you to enjoy the game. I think if you enjoy citybuilders its got enough for 15-20 hours of play, and the RPG elements make it unique from other entries in the genre.<br><br>Having said that, there are definitely some areas I'd love to see improved so that I can come back and play this game some more.<br><br><ol>    <li><b>A citybuilder and an RPG<\/b> - This is what makes the game unique. But I would have liked to see some difficulty sliders for each portion of the game. I prefer city builders, and doing the RPG part of the game was honestly a chore. I would have liked a difficulty slider that made the RPG part much easier, and the city builder part a little more challenging. <br>    <\/li><li><b>Level scaling<\/b> Leveling is a core part of the game. Villagers get better, heroes get better, and monsters get better. But the monster levelling can get pretty annoying, once I had levelled up my heroes to level 6 the monsters had gotten almost too difficult for my troops to take on. I had to rush my heroes around to every little combat event. Until this is fixed I'd like a way to turn off combat level scalling altogether on the village map (for everything except heroes), or don't include the heroes in the monster levelling calculations. To fix it long term I think there should be some kind of training building that slowly adds experience to your lowest levelled troops up to the level of your highest levelled troops. I also think there should be a global ticker that says what the monster level is, and clearly shows what effect you are having on it. I think what effects the monster level should be the size of your city and the amount of loot available in it. So if monsters successfully break your defense and destroy your buildings they would fall down to an easier level. This would also force larger cities to invest more in defense. <br>    <\/li><li><b>Building synergies<\/b> Right now all of the buildings are very independant, they have their own research paths, and they don't really have any effects on the buildings around them. I think the city-building aspects of this game would be greatly improved if each building also provided some synergistic benefits to nearby buildings. Some of the synergies I'd like to see:<br><ul class=\"bb_ul\">    <li>Cottage provides bonus to worker productivity of nearby buildings. Since villagers no longer need to walk around to provide for their needs, forcing players to keep the cottage closer could simulate the benefits of having their home close to their workplace. <br>    <\/li><li>Sawmill and hunting lodge next to each other. Animals like the tree cover so maybe provide a benefit to animal spawning near a sawmill<br>    <\/li><li>Towers that are garrisoned will increase the range of nearby buildings.<br>    <\/li><li>Walls near a gatehouse are strengthened<br>    <\/li><li>Blacksmith provides small productivity bonus when next to any building that it could provide tools for.<\/li><\/ul>    <\/li><li><b>Town improvements<\/b>It would be good to get a building that can serve as a research center for general improvements to all buildings. Like a town hall, maybe the supply wagon serves as the first level of the town hall. The town hall would have research available for lower fire chances, increased health of buildings, increased productivity of workers, starting level of new workers, improve xp gains for soldiers\/workers, etc. This building could also serve as a lock on tier improvements, like it has to be tier two before other buildings can be tier two. It would be neat if this building replaced the lamppost for fast travel. Maybe this building would also provide a special minor-hero unit 'the mayor' he could level up like other characters. He would be able to cast productivity enhancements on nearby buildings, if &quot;killed&quot; he would lose 10% experience but also hide out in the town hall for 2 minutes while he recoveres.<br>    <\/li><li><b>RTS improvements<\/b> - <br><ul class=\"bb_ul\">    <li>Rally flags for military buildings. I found it easy to create a choke point where new monsters would go and I would just leave a bunch of my soldiers there. It would be nice if military buildings could create rally flags so new soldiers would automatically go to the spot. Also there need to be a way in the barracks to auto create arbalists or city guards, right now it will default to auto-creating spearmen who are the weakest. <br>    <\/li><li>Patrols. I want to be able to assign military units to patrol a certain area.<br>    <\/li><li>Formation behavior. If I tell a large military group to go to a location, I don't want the cavalry charging in ahead without support from arbalists.<\/li><\/ul><\/li><\/ol><br>That is a long list of suggestions. I don't think they are all absolutely necessary, but they would all be nice to have. ARE GETTING INTO: because there are plenty of negative reviews and complaints about Folk Tale. Few are fair points but mostly a single sentence &quot;I no liek dis gaims&quot; sort of feedback(!). To combat this, Folk Tale community responds in kind with positive &quot;I liek dis gaims&quot; sort of reviews and I believe that is equally damaging. I personally like this game and yes it has problems but I believe they'll be fixed in time and this game will be awesome. However:<br><br>If you want a smooth experience right off the bat, even if you are into the genre, HOLD OFF FOR NOW and follow the game's progress a bit. You might say &quot;this is early access of course there'll be bugs, crashes and what not&quot;. I'm not talking about bugs and crashes; actually the game is quite stable, runs well on my 5 y-o machine at top quality. I had no crashes, frame drops or hickups. If you don't mind the stuff I mention below and\/or if you like the idea of this game and want to support it, GO FOR IT.<br><br>I bought this game when it was a showcase demo back in 2013 and just loved the idea of it. I've played it on and off and recently played it for a while in it's latest (I think Alpha 0.4.4.1) version, normal difficulty. I believe it'll give me my money's worth when it's finished, but that's just me.<br><br>Below text includes what I've ran into; call it a first impressions since I decided to let it bloom further for now. For some these issues on their own or collectively might annoy enough for a rage-quit and make you regret buying Folk Tale; so as I mentioned above: KNOW WHAT YOU ARE GETTING INTO. :P<br><br>Building models seem too big &amp; map feels like it has too many indestructibles: I like the graphics and overall art style of this game but it feels like you are building things on a minimap. Maybe it's just me but I can say space is a scarse commodity, has to be used wisely or you'll quickly run out of it. Alternatively you can strech farther into the map which  exposes you, makes defense kind of harsh. If you're OCD about putting buildings side by side in an orderly fashion to make it look like a proper village, it is near impossible. Too many obstructions, too little space. Spider nests, minotaur camps with walls and towers that are indestructable, lots of rock and cliff formations, slopes, even small pieces of rocks which you can not build on, limits you even further. Ground doesn't scale nor remove props for a construction, for now. They do look nice though. On the other hand there are people who manages to play in this state so it's probably just me. Also I'm guessing as defense buildings get introduced defending stretched out villages might be easier\/possible, time will tell. Alternatively maybe things are just big enough but maps need to be wider.<br><br>Managing plebs, finding idle plebs: Back then when, I remember an &quot;idle worker&quot; button was suggested. As RTS players would know, idle worker button is nice to have, not only a quality of life improvement. To find your idle workers and make them gather things or quickly summon them as quarterbacks a.k.a. meat shield is important. There is a sort of &quot;worker button&quot; now, but not an &quot;idle worker&quot; button. It only cycles through all villagers who are not assigned to a building. So this cycle includes plebs who are for example gathering berries (which is important because food variety keeps people happy), and if you want berries in your life but also want to assign a pleb to a building, at least for now, you'll have to depend on your luck then manually assign someone to berries. Idle plebs hang out at the supply wagon area, so I guess you can manually pick'em from there.<br><br>Production-consumption balance: Your villagers sometimes gobble a certain resource to zero and put you in a tight spot. Since you need villagers to produce and villagers won't come in (due to unhappiness) when a resource is spent, you get in a nasty loop. If you are getting attacked by something (which happens more than you think) and start to lose villagers it's pretty much &quot;gg&quot;. You have to be on top of the jobs and make sure everything is in order all the time. Exploring the map with your heroes, checking out researches; in general paying attention to something else always involves a risk of tipping the balance. Don't expect things to trickle up passively, regularly and obsessively check everything. There is also a market with which you can buy-sell all resources. If you sell something too much the price goes down and buy something too much price goes up. It has a neat supply\/demand kind of system and can help you balance things or drain your money, gut your resources if you're not precise. Again this might be just me.<br><br>No time stamp, game mode indicator on saves: Your saves appear only with the date and as auto or manual. You can't tell the difference between a save you made during tutorial mission or campaign. You'd normally expect your top save to be the latest but my last try had proven otherwise. So for now you have to manually try each save to find the right one.<br><br>Constant disasters and attacks: Your guys get diseased and there can be fires at random (I think they are intended to happen every thirty minutes(?), not sure). I don't know how diseases are cured so my guys just die, but you build a well for fires and then repair buildings. In addition there are regular attacks from any nest or camp at any part of the map (even a farthest, unexplored corner) and attack party size increases. I think it's intended to come from the nearest active camp\/nest but since they are indestructable I think they respawn and overlap maybe (I think attacks are also intended to be regular like disasters). Sometimes a level 6 spider queen casually strolls in your village (without triggering an alarm) attacks some guy minding his business and by the time you bring your soldiers or heroes to it, it mauls through 10 of your guys. As long as you have the happiness up and manage to replace the job slots you'll be fine but it does set you back a ton. Even the camps\/nests you've cleared may end up spawning a wave of mobs. Your soldiers and cavalry will die very quickly (at least for now cavalry seems the only useful soldier unit, I haven't managed to get a crossbowman). I have no idea how to level them up, I guess if they survive they eventually level up. Maybe hide them behind heroes? Not sure if they gain experience on hit or kill. Again, there are people who have figured it out, I'll wait for the release to explore it further. As a side note, best and only combat tactic for now seems to be to zerg and focus the enemy down 1 by 1.<br><br>Killer rabbits: This annoyed the heck out of me at first, because I thought hunters were attacking rabbits as game and getting killed for it. The hunters path out and hunt game (deer) with bows. Sometimes they walk near the critters and get murdered 1 by 1. Sounds funny, yes; is funny, yes, but can be very annoying when you are struggling. The thing I realized later is that the critters like rabbits and crabs are not game but hostile mobs. You see them as red dots on the minimap like bears or wolves and they attack anything that moves close. Maybe they are meant to be lower level mobs to help you train your soldiers? No idea. I'm not really sure what may cause this problem but hunters get out and get killed, and plebs get no meat. As I mentioned earlier, if your happiness happens to be not up to par for some reason losing plebs like that causes loss of production you desperately need to get more plebs.<br><br>To be fair I haven't been able to advance much with the campaign story since whenever I try to send my heroes to one of the points, something starts munching on my folk and\/or they get unhappy with something.
