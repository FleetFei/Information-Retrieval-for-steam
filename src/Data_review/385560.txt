critically acclaimed Shadow Complex, which was released on the Xbox Live Arcade back in August 2009. The remake was released on Steam in May 2016, with a console version of the game being released in March 2016. The Microsoft Windows store offered the game for free back in December 2015 for a brief amount of time. Having no exposure to the console version of the game, I went into this game only with the knowledge that it was one of the better games to come out of the Xbox Live Arcade. <br><br>Game play is done in a 2.5D platforming style which includes a handful of 3D elements.  Your movement is restricted to two-dimensional systems though.  You find yourself in the middle of a huge spy plot, as you explore an underground base which is holding an entire army ready for overthrowing the American government. Throughout this underground complex, you are faced with plenty of different puzzles to solve. The puzzles aren\u2019t massively complicated; they normally involve some sort of jumping mechanic. Other puzzles will require the use of other pieces of equipment which you will discover throughout the course of the story.  These pieces of equipment vary from different weapons such as rocket launchers, grenades, foam guns (question mark?), grappling hooks, all the way to supersonic speed for running through walls and boosted jumps for scaling walls. <br><br>The gun play in Shadow Complex is a little tricky to get a hang of. The game uses twin stick controls for aiming meaning you don\u2019t always get a huge amount of accuracy.  The 2.5D environment also causes a lot of confusion and hassle when trying to aim at enemies but you have to be in a certain position in order to shoot at enemies in the rear of the scene.  You will come across plenty of enemies whilst exploring, these can be dispatched in a number of ways, using range combat, or an easy to use melee combat system which changes style depending on where you are attacking for example, if you are jumping from above, you\u2019ll land on top of the enemy to knock them out, whereas attacking from behind will result in you being a little more stealthy. <br><br>Boss fights throughout the story mode differ a fair amount between them. None of them are your usual style fight where you just volley a ton of ammo in their general direction. A lot will take into consideration the environment you are fighting in, such as needing to throw grenades underneath a tank to hit its weak spot. I found the boss fights to be a nice challenge which you wouldn\u2019t otherwise get from the regular enemies you\u2019ll come across.  As you unlock new abilities and pieces of equipment, you\u2019ll often find yourself in a position to be able to backtrack to previously visited locations.<br>Even though this is a remastered game, which runs on the Unreal 4 Engine, I was a little disappointed with the graphical fidelity of this title. The environments look really poor, and at times you are able to see the textures quite close up and they are really pixelated which take the edge of the remastered effect. The character models look average, nothing special sadly. Animation in character\u2019s faces can be a little off-putting at times if you focus on them whilst they are speaking, as well as a few awkward movements in cut scenes to boot. <br><br>Plot wise, the game is fairly interesting but somewhat cheesy at times. The opening prologue of the game bares no relevance to the actual story itself so I was a bit confused as to why it was included. The main protagonist is Jason Flemming, who happens to be on a weekend away with his new girlfriend, Claire. Your new girlfriend gets kidnapped and you are tasked with finding her and bringing her to safety. As the plot unravels everything turns out not to be the way it seemed. Although a lot of it is largely predictable, I still found myself interested in finding out what happens at the end. The dialog between Jason and Claire can be quite cringy at times and often felt like it was written by a twelve year old.  Thankfully you don\u2019t need to follow the story too much in order to get the most out of Shadow Complex. <br><br>The voice acting across the game is nothing special, and you may even be better off turning it off as it did make me wince with awkwardness at times. The rest of the game\u2019s sound effects are as you\u2019d expect from an action game like this. There are moments where you really should be hearing a sound effect, such as background noise when you are on a factory floor, but you don\u2019t hear anything. This can really knock you out of the immersion of any game, and sadly it happened all too frequently throughout this one.  <br><br>Shadow Complex comes with a variety of different difficulty settings, most will be sufficiently challenged on the normal mode and however you can change the settings on the fly, and so I would advise start as high as you can to see how well you handle the combat. The easy mode is no challenge at all and you may find yourself getting bored of the combat system if you aren\u2019t being challenged. <br><br>I wouldn\u2019t consider Shadow Complex Remastered to have any replay ability unfortunately. There are a number of achievements which people may be after which will force a number of different play throughs, but the regular gamers amongst us will find themselves being satisfied with the single play through of the story. I did like the in game leader boards comparing your progression with people on your friends list who also have the game though; it adds a little competitive nature to an otherwise single player experience. <br><br>Other than the control issues being a bit of a pain in the 2.5D environments, the only other technical issue I had was that even when playing in full screen mode, your mouse does not get locked into the game window, meaning anyone who plays with more than one screen will find themselves minimizing their game more times than you would even believe to imagine. The only way I was actually able to play the game without this happening constantly was to move away from my preferred control method of keyboard and mouse to use an Xbox controller. Granted this got around this annoying technical problem that brought along its own problems with the lower accuracy when shooting due to using the joysticks rather than the more precise mouse movement. <br><br>Shadow Complex Remastered shouldn\u2019t really be classed as a remastered edition in my opinion. This is the first time the game has come to the PC platform, and it doesn\u2019t really show off the platform to its full potential.  The game play itself is enjoyable with a lot of interesting and unique abilities at your fingertips, but there is so much room for a potentially amazing game here that I was left feeling disappointed at the end.  The $14,99 price tag is probably nudging the boundaries of being over the average for this game so you may want to wait till the drops in price a little bit before picking it up. <br><br><b><u> Tom's Score Card<\/u><\/b><br>1) Stay away<br>2) Not Recommended<br><b><u>3) Only recommended when on sale<\/u><\/b><br>4) Recommended<br>5) Highly recommended<br>6) This is a must play<br><br>If you found this review helpful, please give it a thumbs up. playthrough, Shadow Complex is still an incredibly enjoyable game. <\/div><br>Having played Shadow Complex four times on my Xbox I was very excited to finally see the Remastered version on Steam. Shadow Complex is a 2.5D side-scroller developed by ChAIR Entertainment that originally released on Xbox in 2009. Jump forward seven years and the game has been remastered and released for PC on Steam. In Shadow Complex you play as Jason Flemming, after your girlfriend Claire is kidnapped by a strange organisation you venture into their base to find and rescue Claire. Unfortunately for you this organisation is anything but friendly and they have some serious firepower, you\u2019ll need to learn how to use it if you want to survive here.<br><br>Shadow Complex doesn\u2019t have cutting edge graphics but it looked good when it came out and it looks even better with the updated graphics now. The visual effects are also pretty good and all of the in-game models look good too.<br><br>The game features a large map with indoor and outdoor portions, it takes roughly 8-10 hours to beat the game. You\u2019ll see pretty much all of the map just by playing through the story but there are some secret areas where you\u2019ll find upgrades. These upgrades range from how many grenades you can carry to how much armour you have and they\u2019re incredibly useful, especially on the higher difficulties.<br><br>As you progress through the game it does get a fair bit more difficult, again even more so on higher difficulties. There are a few advanced enemy types but generally you\u2019ll just find more enemies, sometimes in awkward positions making it even harder to fight them. Every so often you\u2019ll also run into a boss. The boss fights are enjoyable and tough to begin with but once you figure out the main strategy to beat a boss they become less challenging. Generally these strategies involve the different equipment items you\u2019ll find over the course of the game: grenades, missiles and a strange foam which prevents enemies\/objects from moving. While the main purpose of these items is to break through part of the environment (for example using a grenade to get through a locked door) they can also be used to great effect in combat.<br><br>The other item used to get through things in the environment is your gun! Whatever gun you have (the first one you find is a basic pistol but you\u2019ll find others as you progress through the game) can be used to break through some objects, such as metal coverings for vents, allowing you to reach a new area. The gun is also what you\u2019ll mainly use during combat, though you also have a melee attack. The gun is obviously good at range while the melee attack is always your best bet when you\u2019re close enough to the enemy as it\u2019s an insta-kill. The melee attack triggers a mini-cutscene, of which there are a few variations, in which you pretty much beat the crap out of whatever enemy it is you\u2019re attacking. Of course, there are some enemies who can\u2019t be killed with the melee attack but the enemies you\u2019ll see most regularly can.<br><br>There are some other pieces of equipment in the game too. You get a flashlight early on in the game which is obviously used to illuminate dark areas in the game but, when shone on objects, it also highlights objects which can be destroyed by your other equipment, for example orange highlighting means it can be destroyed with gunfire. Without giving too much away, you\u2019ll also find some high-tech armour along the way, each piece of the armour adds a new mechanic to the game. You\u2019ll find the armour parts as you progress through the story.<br><br>Speaking of the story, it\u2019s actually quite good. The story of Jason trying to rescue Claire is reasonably interesting but it\u2019s the game\u2019s backstory that\u2019s really enjoyable. There are a few points in the game where you\u2019ll be able to listen in to the conversations that take place between some of your enemies in the game. These conversations give you an idea of what the organisation in the game is like and what their goals are. Missing or ignoring them won\u2019t make the game\u2019s story hard to understand but you will really miss out of some cool stuff. The story is based around ideas in Orson Scott Card\u2019s books. While I haven\u2019t read any of his work it seems that he has strong, often controversial right wing views. It\u2019s worth mentioning that Shadow Complex isn\u2019t politically driven so don\u2019t worry if you don\u2019t agree with his views.<br><br>Returning to upgrades, as well as the collectible upgrades you find you\u2019re also \u2018upgraded\u2019 through collecting EXP and levelling up (an automatic process). The Hardcore and Insane difficulties offer more EXP for killing enemies\/completing objectives, meaning you\u2019ll level up considerably faster, but these difficulties are also a lot harder to beat. When you level up, three things happen. Firstly, you\u2019re maximum health will increase. Secondly, you\u2019ll return to full health (this has saved me from death a fair few times). Lastly, one of your stats (such as accuracy) will be increased (some levels also have special bonuses, for example the full map is revealed when you reach level 20). The level cap is level 50 which will take an incredibly long time to get to (after 6 hours I\u2019m level 12, playing on hardcore difficulty) but you keep your level when you start a new game (though you lose your weapons, items etc.)<br><br>As much as I love Shadow Complex, it\u2019s not without its issues. The biggest problem I have with the game is the save system, there are checkpoint rooms every so often where you can save your progress and from where you\u2019ll restart if you die or quit and then return to the game later. I always prefer it when games allow you to save at any point and it can be quite inconvenient in Shadow Complex, especially on the higher difficulties, since when you die you\u2019ll have to walk back through a fair bit of the map (dying won\u2019t respawn enemies but when you quit and come back the enemies you killed will be there again). My second issue with the game is with the aiming controls. Being a 2.5D game, not all of the enemies are exactly to the left or right of you, sometimes they\u2019re off at an angle or horizontal to you. The problem is (at least when playing with a controller) that the aiming system is designed for a 2D game, in other words it\u2019s made for you to fire in straight lines in front, behind, above, below or anything in between. Because of this, when aiming at an enemy who\u2019s not in the same plane as you, you have to be aiming at just the right angle so that the gun aims into the scenery\/level rather than straight ahead. I realise that\u2019s probably not the best description of the problem but basically it makes some enemies incredibly difficult to hit which results in you spending more time than necessary trying to hit them, often translating into you losing a fair bit of health. Other than that the controls in the game are solid which is important since there\u2019s a fair bit of platforming in the game.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Verdict <\/div><br>Easily one of my favourite games, Shadow Complex is a great 2.5D side-scroller with enjoyable, challenging gameplay.
