truly appreciated for what it is...an ever evolving simulation game with a very solid foundation.  The developers gradually phase in content while delivering a great game in the process.  For example, the first release of the game was very basic compared to what the game is in it's current state by the additon of lots of new content.  This means instead of taking forever to deliver a game that has lots of content that's half baked, we get a solid experience that gradually grows with a quality feel to it.<br><br>One of the most impressive factors of this game for me is the visuals and performance.  The game looks great even on mid range systems and performs very well at the same time.  The devs are constantly fleshing out small details to compliment this as well.  For example, in the last major update, they added full planet visuals to the galaxy map.  That might sound like something small but in a galaxy with thousands of planets it's an amazing addition because you can completely rotate the planet and see it's surface completely.  Another example is the visuals they've added to the stations, again seemingly small but it's something that's been phased in gradually while we've had a very playable game.<br><br>I think my overall favorite aspect of this game though is the sheer versatility of the ships.  You start off with a small Sidewinder and graduate into larger ships as you progress but ships are never totally without worth.  Sure, a Sidewinder isn't going to take out an Anaconda on it's own but if you had a capable wing of four pilots in completely maxed out Sidewinders they'd put up a damn good fight.  A better example is the Cobra MK III...this is a ship you can get fairly early in the game but is a capable bounty hunter or smuggling ship even in the late game as you upgrade it.  The Asp Explorer...a great mission ship, smuggling ship, exploration ship or even miner.  Combine all of that with engineering (despite how some may feel about it) and you have an awesome level of customization.<br><br>The value for your money is also amazing because you can sink hundreds of hours into this game with a $60 package (which includes the expansion as of this writing) and not drop another dime on it (until the next expansion comes later if you want it) unless you want to buy the cosmetics.  <br><br>Now, I know there are some that feel like you should be able to earn the paint jobs and other things in the game without having to pay additional money; however, this game is effectively a sandbox MMO that requires a consistently maintained server back end, so I have no qualms in throwing $5-10 occasionally to further support the development of the game.  So basically, you can earn every ship in the game and see all of it's content and just pay extra for the shiny stuff to put on yoru ship.  Even with the base game there is a lot to do but if you're considering purchasing it, I'd highly recommend getting the Commander Deluxe edition since it includes the Horizons Season pass and some cosmetics.<br><br>The other thing that I love is the fact that you can play this game Solo, in private groups or in open play with everyone else that's in the game in Open play.  And no matter which mode you play in, your actions affect the universe the same way.  For example, if you go to a station and buy out all of their gold then this takes it away from everyone else that might visit that station.  This means you get to experience the same universe as everyone else but not worry about running into griefers.  To be fair though, I play in Open from time to time and rarely run into anyone that causes problems these days.<br><br>Lastly, this isn't just simply a space flight sim.  There is an entire Background Simulation running behind the scenes that all players impact.  So for example, if you join up with a group of folks they might have their own faction in a station and you can work with them to spread that factions influence to other systems.  You and others can also have a major impact on the economics of a system and can cooridnate these efforts.<br><br>So what all can you do in the game?  You can do missions for stations to increase your rep with them that helps with many aspects of the game, you can bounty hunt, mine, take missions to planets for all sorts of objectives, explore, become a trader and run trade routes, and all sorts of other things.  What's missing for many folks though is the lack of direction provided by other games.  You are basically dropped into the game with very little direction to start and are left to your own devices.  This creates a learning curve that can be tough for some but once you get the hang of it, the universe is yours.  <br><br>So is the game perfect?  No, not yet.  The party system is still buggy and unintuitive in some ways.  Most of the games story happens through meta and you need to keep up with it either by reading in game news or keeping up with it through their blogs.  The missions in the game are somewhat randomly generated and really have no bearing on the main story unless there is some event going on that they can impact.  Missions are largely for earning money and reputation.  There are community goals that are always available and those are part of what determines the outcome of the games storyline.  <br><br>So Elite Dangerous' greatest strength and weakness are the same, it's very open.  It's so open it can be hard for new players to understand what to do and direction in the game is often left to the community; however, the games mechanics and customization are so open it makes for a great experience for anyone who loves to constantly tweak and collect ships and equipment.<br><br>As of this writing, I have over 850 hours in the game and have enjoyed every minute.
