Unfortunately, the gameplay is not perfectly balanced, leaving some strategies much easier to use than others; however, despite some strategies being better, it is still fun making various decks to fight with.<br><br>Unlike many recent card games, this game does not have an automatic battle mode where you simply play the cards and the game decides on the moves. Instead, you are in charge of many different aspects from whether a card attacks or uses a skill to even what you attack. In a defense state, you have little control if a card is attacked directly, but if the player is attacked, you may choose to defend with a specific card or not at all.<br><br>Unfortunately, due to how defending works, it requires players to essentially know every card and with the ability to upgrade, this may become even more difficult.<br><br><b>Story<\/b>: 3\/5<br><br>This game has a rather simple story, you are part of an organization that is fighting to maintain the balance between three warring factions. Of course, your actions are supposed to be neutral with no favorites, but as can be expected, being neutral and unbiased all the time can be rather difficult.<br><br>Unfortunately, a large portion of the game is tied to the story with several unlocks and rewards being tied to story mode. That said, one may come to think of story mode as a long tutorial, which while somewhat interesting, it can feel tedious when the player wants to move onto other modes.<br><br>Fortunately for players who do enjoy story mode, certain story mode battles have mechanics that will rarely be seen elsewhere.<br><br><b>Graphics\/Audio<\/b>: 2.5\/5<br><br>Graphically, the game has a decent resolution, but is limited as is apparent if viewed in full screen mode with a higher resolution monitor. As this game was originally for the Vita, this can be understandable. Fortunately, the game's graphics are still decent despite the limited resolution. It should also be noted that while the game defaults to a censored state, the game features an uncensored option for those who prefer the original art.<br><br>There are two noticeable issues with the graphics, first is that the game defaults to window mode when it starts instead of remember the last state, which combined with having to right click to access full screen mode may be annoying to some. The second issue is the more limited FPS, which players who are used to PC's higher FPS may find annoying. Unfortunately, this is unlikely to change due to animations, which would look odd at a different FPS.<br><br>Some players have noted FPS drops, this may be system dependent or a side effect of battle animations.<br><br>The audio quality is decent for the game with voice acting and music to accompany the game. While nothing especially impressive, it is decent enough.<br><br><b>Play Time\/Replayability<\/b>: 3\/5<br><br>While much of the replayability of the offline game modes will be based on farming for gold in order to acquire better gear, the game can be a nice and calming to play. Story mode replayability will be primarily based on players who want a more static set of battles. Dungeon battles vary by the day, though there is still a limited amount of variety. PVC, or Player Vs Computer, battles are more randomized and can provide a decent amount of replayability. Challenge mode changes each week and players can try to make decks to get the best scores.<br><br>The majority of the replayability will be in online battles where players may form strategies to counter each other and fight in a more intelligent manner.<br><br><b>Final Score<\/b>: 3.5\/5<br><br>This game is worth trying out, especially when on sale.
