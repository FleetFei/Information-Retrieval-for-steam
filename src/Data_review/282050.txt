this game with a passion. But after having over 2k hours on reflex over the years, it was time to look for something else. 1st impression, no feel for the bike, couldn't turn, coudln't whip, could barely make any of the jumps. And why is the camera panning sideways in 3rd person? This of all things really screwed me over, this panning of the camera sideways in every turn, made it very easy to run off the track in every turn. I felt limited it what I could do. <br><br>Quit and played reflex some more, but the hours were wearing me down. Started playing Encore again when I heard KTM's were released as DLC. This time I gave it an honest try with an open mind, not allowing the sea of negativity to influence my opinion before I gave the game an real try. I would play to get to a point where I could get some clean laps in and start pushing, because this is where racing games come alive, when you are on the gas! <br><br>The biggest difference for me between liking and hating this game, (other than the KTM's), was forgetting about 3rd person and playing in 1st person. After I started getting some basic lines down it really felt like Encore was designed to be played in 1st person, not 3rd. The game just felt so much more fluid in this view, much closer to what I've come to expect from an MX vs ATV game. Looked way better and played better as you feel the bike more as you actually have a feel for the front of the bike. 3rd person by comparison feels very, VERY stiff and static.<br><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"https:\/\/www.youtube.com\/watch?v=Is-7gIsE3JY\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_0\">https:\/\/www.youtube.com\/watch?v=Is-7gIsE3JY<\/a><br><br>I came to realize that my initial opinion of the game was compounded by friends saying &quot;don't play this game it sucks.&quot; It was also influenced by the fact that the supercorss was different from Reflex, which is what I was used to. When I first started reflex I hated it, why? because it didn't play like unleashed. And reflex turned out to be one of my most played games of all time. Also bear in mind that reviews from early access are not seperated. It was a totally different game back then, and a lot of gripes have been addressed.<br><br>The tracks themselves offer a nice variety of content, though I do wish nordic would release the 2016 AMA supercross tracks which are all out there....somewhere. Not as much a fan of some of the outdoor tracks but they do offer a completly different challenge with those flat turns that require a completly different approach to how you attack the track. I do wish SX tracks would get rutted the same amount as outdoors though, Sounds are pretty well done, feels great when you open up the throttle, go though the whoops and rev the bike in the air.<br><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"https:\/\/www.youtube.com\/watch?v=qs-B3i9MH4I\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_1\">https:\/\/www.youtube.com\/watch?v=qs-B3i9MH4I<\/a><br><br>At it's core this still is an MX vs ATV game. Lots of huge lines to be found, still feels great when you land the rythms clean and you put down a fast lap - Physics are pretty much on par with what you would expect with an Mx vs ATV game, not ultra realistic but not over the top arcadey. Feels like there is much more traction than reflex and the clutch will actually give you a decent boost. Unlike reflex, you actually have to use the clutch and brakes in encore, as you can very easily blow the turns. I do wish, however, that manual shifting was a little more refined so that there would be a real benifit to manually working your way though the gears instead of staying in 5th and using the clutch. That said automatic transmission works just as well and is what you would expect from an mx vs atv game without manual shifting. In the end I feel both are on par with eachother. <br><br>Preloading can seem over the top in 3rd person as it seem like the bike gets popped up and launched forward, but I find it much more mellow playing in 1st person. In my videos above, I do preload quite a few jumps but you don't see that huge pop up and forward that you see in 3rd person.<br><br>At this point, after hating encore for so long, I'm playing it a lot more. (but I still hate it's implemention of 3rd person which I think puts a lot of people off). Its not a perfect game, it still has it quirks and still could benifit from some elbow greese, but what game is perfect? <br><br>So if you're thinking of trying this game, ignore the sea of negativity. Stop by my page and check out some of my videos and see for yourself how much fun this game can be.
