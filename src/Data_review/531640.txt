class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy current gametime is due to me playing entirely on my phone\/tablet before downloading it on steam.<br><br>I could not reccomend more! A fantastic split between Hearthstone and Magic: The Gathering, urging more towards MTG than any rendition of a CCG I've played.<br><br>In summary<br><br>Pros:<br>* Not at all pay to win, surprisingly easy to get into<br>* Very little RNG, more skill required<br>* Interactive gameplay that leads to a lot of high moments<br>* The draft system towers over any CCG I've played<br><br>Cons:<br>* Slower meta at the moment, hopefully future sets flesh this out as it is still early access<br>* The introduction is slow paced and can take a couple hours to beat (Strongly suggest pushing through it, highly worth it. The game completely changes when you can make your own deck)<br><br>Many CCG's tend to focus more on paying in order to aquire cards, and while there are benefits to buying the ingame currency, it's quite easy to make top tier decks without the norms associated with CCG's. (It won't take months, nor would it take much money whatsoever if you decide you don't want to wait the week or two)<br><br>After playing for less than a week, I have a fully made Tier 1 deck that I can flex in various options for. I'm on my way to make a second deck, more than halfway there.<br><br>The gameplay focuses more on skill than RNG, which I appreciate - you don't feel nearly as screwed over by a lucky roll of the dice as you do in other online CCG's. I can count on one hand (a few fingers) the number of cards that say something along the lines of, &quot;Deal 3 damage to a random enemy&quot;. Which, after the resolved play, ends up destroying a 1\/1 creature that allows the enemy to draw, rather than the 7\/3 towering monstrosity threatening to rampage through your soul.<br><br>Another nice thing that I like, in regards to the lack of RNG, is the mulligan system this game has. Rather than traditional mull's, when you mull your hand, your next hand is gauranteed to have at least 2 power (mana) and up to 5 max. Takes away those feast or famine hands you can get.<br><br>The mechanics of this game mimic many of MTG, but there are quite a few of its own. It definitely takes advantage of the digital aspect very well. The interactions from card to card are incredibly fun, and more are being found out often.<br><br>Even with just one set, drafting in this game feels incredible. Not to mention, it's easy to build up the currency to play drafts. My favorite part? It works like a traditional draft. Everything you draft, you KEEP. That means you can easily greed draft to get the best bang for your buck. So even players that are not too confident in their drafting abilities, can get more than their gold worth from greed drafting. (Always draft, don't buy packs out right)<br><br>The only negative I feel I can truly give this game is the restictive meta at the moment. Most every deck in the top tiers are either midrange, a midrange control, or a couple aggro decks. There are very few full control decks, let alone combo decks, that can fair too well against what's currently being played. However, this is common among CCG's that first come out. This is mostly due to card pools, and how many are available. I've found, usually, that as games progres and release more sets, the meta responds well. (Come on aggro decks!)<br><br>On that note, they are coming out with patches, and the last one made a direct impact on the meta. Very fun to see and be apart of.<br><br>Aside from that, I do dislike the current way they introduce you into the game. It's a long process that is not all that fun. You play through every faction for a number of games, and it can feel slow. It does teach you a bit, and for every faction you beat you get a starter deck (strongly reccomend) That being said, you don't have to do this. You can skip straight to ranked play, drafting, or the other AI matches (One that's a one shot challenge, giving stronger prizes until the end. The other being a AI Draft system that costs less)<br><br>Eternal Card Game is one that I suggest for any TCG\/CCG player that's looking for a new experience!
