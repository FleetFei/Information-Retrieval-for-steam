There are spoilers, but I engineered the review to not reveal anything to ruin the game, but to still tell you about it. It should be safe to read for anyone who hasn't yet played the game, but they are, technically, still spoilers. These English reviews are lacking, in my opinion, so mine is quite detailed.<br><br>~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~<br><br><b>Detailed Review - Some Spoilers Ahead<\/b><br><br>Overall, I enjoyed this game, but it does have its faults. It is also somewhat deceptive in its appearance. Though it looks rather juvenile and innocent, family-friendly, it does have some adult elements in the game; prostitution, sexual assault, murder, and others. You do not partake in those adult elements yourself, it doesn't happen to YOU, but they are mentioned enough times to influence your perception of the story world. Decide for yourselves if you think that is a good or bad thing, but, against the appearance of innocence and juvenility, it does seem out of place. I think that was the authors attempt at some humour, perhaps. Regardless, it is still quite odd.<br><br>Anyway, the music is great, the artwork is good, and the story can be rather interesting. In all honesty, the story can drag in parts, and I found myself getting bored as I crawled through the Slice of Life stuff. It can be charming, SoL stuff, but this game overdoes it, in my opinion. Like the interactions with Selene, in one scene, where you agree to help her. Helping her does nothing for the story, its just filler, and rather dull filler at that. The story also does not have much in the way of content about the story world itself. You never really come to learn much about the world and its inhabitants; countries, creatures, history, magic, and so on. You learn about individual people, like Dee and Nen and so on, but don't learn much about the story world itself and, when you do learn about people, you only learn so much; too little, in my opinion. I found that quite disappointing.<br><br>It also doesn't help that the protagonist is an illiterate and uneducated peasant living in a solitary village, and you spend most of your time in that mindset; isolating and without curiosity of the world. Rhea just doesn't seem realistic to me at all. If you find out that there is so much more to the world than what you ever imagined while living in that tiny village, wouldn't you want to explore? Wouldn't you want to learn all about it? Rhea doesn't. When you go to Alune, for example, you stay in the Amarell estate and never explore the city, and you remain there without complaint. I would want to explore and experience everything I could before I had to leave behind such beauty and splendour, I would want to soak up that world and life as much as possible before I had to go back to that damn village; learning absolutely everything I could about those people and their world of high-society while I had the chance. Like I mentioned, you don't get much in the way of content, and the author almost uses the whole 'country bumpkin' routine of the protagonist to not bother fleshing out the story world. It is the same with Dee and Nen and Viral and Jackel, all very interesting characters from an array of fascinating backgrounds that you almost never explore or learn about. Very, very disappointing.<br><br>You also have to suffer through a number of spelling and grammar mistakes, which I found enough of to damage the immersion of the story. And you also don't get much in the way of choice. You can make a number of choices, but you are more of an intrusive invisible influence than you are the protagonist herself; like the hand of God, if you will. I found much of the protagonists interactions disagreeable since I would have liked to decide how to react to certain situations, especially when it came to the character routes. I just thought her reactions were not natural in some scenes. By giving control to the player, it allows for bad endings to happen, and I have only found one bad ending.<br><br>~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~<br><br><b>Conclusion - Still With Some Spoilers<\/b><br><br>All in all, however, I did find the game enjoyable. It is far from perfect, certainly, but I would say it was a good try and a good effort, overall. Despite its problems, I did find it charming and endearing in parts, as well as funny and gripping in others. You can find something to like about each character, and you can find something to dislike in them, too. Well, maybe not Sion, he is a thoroughly unlikable character, but you get the idea.<br><br>I do hope the author does make more games and, should they read this review, I hope they take note of their failings here so that they make better games in the future. I would also hope they make more games with this story world in mind, for it was rather interesting; I would like to learn more about it.<br><br>I would recommend this game, but do not get into any illusions of it being any kind of masterpiece; it, flatly, isn't, but it is worth reading, at least. Just make sure you set aside the time to read it through, however.
