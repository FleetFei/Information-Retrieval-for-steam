At first I was appalled at the graphics and horrendous job of optimisation, but it didn't take long for the cartoonish style to grow on me.  The zombies themselves are quiet varied, and the game throws plenty of easy bosses at you along the way.  Unfortunately the majority of combat is just bash the attack button and then throw out your special attack once its ready  (there are multiple special attacks, but when you get the best one right off the bat, that being the jump and stomp move, there really is no need to mix it up). There is only one type of powerup, beyond health items, which lets you speed up noticeably for a decent period and hit harder. There are stores where you can trade the coins you harvest from the zombies but you'll rarely bother as the zombies have healing items coming out their ears.  In terms of weapons there isn't a huge variety.  You'll end up hitting zombies with a baseball bat for 80% of the time, or your fists if it breaks on you. They range from flooded church basements to ravaged towns, to mines, to army camps, to a haunted house.  There isn't much to do but run across them and kill or outpace zombies as you go, clocking up one objective after another.  It's very linear, and the most the game has you doing in terms of &quot;puzzles&quot; is pick up the key\/explosive to open door\/clear barrier.  There are a range of other collectibles but as none of them appeared to do anything they quickly fell by the wayside for me, and it just became of soothing journey of zombie whack-a-mole.
