As such the graphics are dated and the engine is not the most robust around so this should be taken into consideration.<br>It is an isometric game, with single-player mode only, a funny (sometimes overdone) story, in a post-apocalyptic setting. Fallout meets Mad Max, kind of thing.<br><br>Gameplay:<br>Contrary to most RTS games around, since the inception of the genre, this one does not feature base-building. What has come to be a key component of the genre.<br>That in itself, at least to me, is refreshing. The game plays in typical RTS fashion controls with the exception of not having the ability to add units one by one to a group using a modifier key (usually shift). Everything else as far as controls go is like every other RTS.<br>What is new on the table is the use of vehicles. Your units can enter and exit vehicles (and buildings) and can survive if their vehicle gets blown up. A unit switches their active actions menu depending on if they're on foot or in a vehicle.<br>The game has a funny backstory that you get to watch in between chapters, in the form of a comic book and there are funny, sometimes to the point of absurd, dialogues inside the campaign missions as well.<br>The story, as far as I've seen, is full of cliche and sexual innuendo. It is funny enough though that it works for me.<br>Worthy of note is that the engine feels quite clunky. One may think that it's only reasonable in this time and age, the game is 17 years old, by the time I write this, but there have been older games of the same genre with more responsive engines (a wink to Dune here).<br>The pathfinding AI routines are also not very advanced. Prepare to micromanage and use the pause button a lot. Not only to think about your strategy but to correct pathing as well.<br><br><br><br>Graphics:<br>The game is dated 2005. With that in mind, the graphics are not bad at all. Notice though that there is no support for resolutions higher than 1280x1024 and although there are widescreen resolutions available, the game is not really made with widescreen in mind.<br><br>Music\/Sound Design:<br>On this department it is doing ok. The music sounds good and so do the effects. If you are easily offended by sexist stereotypes you may want to avoid it. Also it is quite wacky.<br>-It is an older game and it shows.<br>-Single-player only. Some may like that, some may not.<br>-No base building. Same as above.<br><br>Cons:<br>-Engine and interface feels a bit clunky.<br>-Pathfind could be A LOT better.<br><br>Conclusion:<br>It is a decent game which perhaps did not age very well.
