the hidden object genre. While these games do sometimes make their ways to handhelds and consoles, the bulk are on the computer. With so many to choose from and more coming out each day, it can be hard to decided which title to play. <i>Portal of Evil: Stolen Runes Collector's Edition<\/i> was in my backlog of games and, at random, I decided to give it a shot. While it may not be the most memorable or groundbreaking HOG on the market, I enjoyed playing it quite a bit. It\u2019s a good game for veterans and new players to the genre and offers a fair amount of challenge.<br><br>Players assume control over a young woman named Vanessa Helsing who receives an invitation to St. Peter\u2019s Cathedral in Italy in regards to information about her missing grandfather. However, once there she becomes mixed up in a secret order that her grandfather was a part of and a mission to stop a gate from opening and releasing evils upon the world. The plot is pretty generic and straightforward, there is a traitor amongst the order who stole the magical runes that held the gate locked so Vanessa can trust no one. But there aren\u2019t many other characters in the game and my first guess on who the traitor could be was right from the beginning so any real sense of mystery is minute.  The story also ends very abruptly and you don\u2019t even get any resolution unless you play the bonus chapter.<br><br>Gameplay is pretty standard for a game of this nature. It\u2019s all point and click so you\u2019ll only need a mouse. You have an inventory system and a map for easy teleporting and backtracking. The game has a good mix of hidden object scenes and puzzles, though I wouldn\u2019t have minded a few more of both actually. You\u2019ll be visiting most scenes twice, however. This isn\u2019t so bad because the game offers two types of gameplay for the scenes. One is the usual list of items in word form, you search the scene to check off items from the list. The other is you\u2019re given items and you have to find where in the scene the item goes, an example being you\u2019re given a glove then you find another glove in the scene and put it there. I enjoyed this different take on things. Most of the games are your usual fair though they do offer a decent challenge as do the hidden object scenes. A small complaint I have is the item menu, it\u2019s at the bottom of the screen and there was no way to lock it so that it would stop minimizing. I know this feature isn\u2019t always in every game but it\u2019s one I like, I like my item inventory always visible. <br><br>Graphics were good, very pretty scenery that fit the story of the game. Colors are bright when they need to be but dark and unsettlingly as well helping to bring Vanessa\u2019s journey to life. The items in the hidden object scenes were well rendered, not too hard to find. I found some to be a bit tiny or just too obscured by darkness, however it was so infrequently it wasn\u2019t a big issue. The cutscenes are well animated, lip movements may not match the lines being said but again, it\u2019s not a big issue. All characters are expressive. The music sets the atmosphere well and there is also full voice acting. The quality of the acting is passable, not the best, but I\u2019ve certainly heard worse.<br><br>I would say <i>Portal of Evil: Stolen Runes Collector's Edition<\/i> is a good game, a bit middle of the road but still better than a lot of other hidden object games I\u2019ve played. I found it fun and enjoyable, I liked the story and I felt satisfied when the game was over. There are Steam trading cards and achievements as well. Older reviews say that two of the achievements are broken, this isn\u2019t the case anymore. The issue was fixed in an update so no need to worry.
