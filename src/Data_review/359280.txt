basic elements isn\u2019t necessarily a difficult task, but doing it well absolutely is. Just cutting gameplay and other mechanical elements out at random won\u2019t do; it really requires surgical precision. And yet, Orangepixel pulls it off so well with Heroes of Loot they make it look effortless.<br><br>Heroes of Loot, at its most basic, is what happens when a game like Gauntlet is combined with Roguelike elements. The four adventurers (Elf, Warrior, Wizard, and Valkyrie) are in it for the money and not much else. Once players select their character, it\u2019s on to adventure! Or in this case something akin to an arcade dungeon crawl. Randomized dungeons, quests, and special items make an appearance along with permadeath, but it\u2019s all been streamlined to the point that dying is merely a temporary setback.<br><br>The most miraculous thing about Heroes of Loot\u2019s simplicity is that Orangepixel managed to trim the fat but kept all the fun intact. Not only that, but they seem to have made the fun even more fun. Running through the halls and chucking weapons at enemies is incredibly quick and twitchy, but the controls never get in the way because characters will automatically target whatever enemy is closest to them. On its own, something like this could start to become dull but thanks to the occasional quest room, complete with random quests, there\u2019s always some sort of goal to strive for. Aside from the mass destruction of a dungeon's ecosystem, I mean.<br><br>The second most miraculous thing about Heroes of Loot is that pretty much any complaint that could be leveled at it has already been addressed. Stages are big enough to enjoy a bit of exploration\/enemy hunting but small enough that they don\u2019t require any sort of mini-map. The RPG elements never get in the way of the gameplay, but they make a difference with a character\u2019s increased strength and improved weapons. Shops only ever sell one item, and it\u2019s random, so there\u2019s no real pressure to save up for anything in particular so players can focus on the adventuring. Virtually every single aspect of the game has been designed to compliment the core gameplay, and it\u2019s a spectacularly impressive feat.<br><br>And Heroes of Loot has a gorgeous style. The pixel-art is nice enough, but the way the game carries itself is just sublime \u2013 from the short and genuinely hilarious \u201ccutscenes,\u201d to the rousing soundtrack, the fun characters, and the silly dialogue, it\u2019s very much a neat package if ever I\u2019ve seen one.<br><br>I\u2019m not so naive as to think that Heroes of Loot will appeal to everybody. However I believe it will pull in both dungeon-crawling fanatics and twin-stick obsessives in equal measure, with its fast-paced, randomly-generated action.
