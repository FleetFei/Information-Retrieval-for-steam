class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\t<div class=\"bb_h1\">Preview<\/div><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gone with the Demon<\/div><br>Gone with the Demon is a first person melee based game with RPG elements. Does it provide a scary encounter with the demons or falls flat on every aspect, let\u2019s find out:<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay: <\/div><br>Gone with the Demon puts you in the shoes of a unknown man who starts out in a broken elevator. From here he has to search the various rooms in the building and has to eat, sleep, open doors, fight, rinse and repeat his way to the basement. At the time of writing, there is barely any sort of story present in the game.<br><br> There are a few notes lying here and there which provide a few bits of lore but the game seems to be begging for a good, fleshed out story. You start off unarmed with the game requiring to go and investigate the various rooms and take anything you find of importance. You fight off against various zombie like creatures with red eyes who are intimidating at first. Initial versions of these guys are essentially punch bags with them doing almost nothing to actually hurt you.<br><br> Slowly as the game starts to picks up the pace, the difficulty rises. Enemies start holding different melee weapons and start to block your attacks. The combat though somewhat slow and clunky, slowly turns out to be actually interesting as you have to time your attacks and blocks to fight off against oncoming hordes of enemies. There is a learning curve to the combat and you will need to bear with the game for its starting half an hour to actually get going. Gone with the Demon is a game that relies on looting heavily.<br><br> If you try to rush the game and leave without looting the bodies or exploring the rooms, you will soon have the odds stacked against you. While the AI is dumb and needs improvement, when you come across a lot of armed enemies you can\u2019t help getting overwhelmed quickly. Now the RPG system present in Gone with the Demon is one of the more interesting systems I\u2019ve seen in quite a time. While exploring the rooms, you find rooms with beds in them. You can sleep on the said beds and upgrade various aspects of your characters. While there is a very certain logic fail in sleeping in a strange bed in a building which is crawling with zombie like demons, they act as very important checkpoints. <br><br>When you are killed by enemies you respawn on the last bed you slept and your enemies grow a little tougher. To turn them back to normal you will have to find a totem in your way and spend some essence points to do so. This seems to be inspired by the nemesis system in Shadow of Mordor and is a nice little touch to them.<br><br> The game is very repetitive though and the combat is slow and clunky. If you are looking for melee combat like Warhammer End Times; Verminitide or something like that, you won\u2019t find that here. Here the combat is far more methodical and requires you to time your attacks and blocks carefully. There are a few bugs present in the game and it crashed on me a couple of times but it is kind of expected from an early access title.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Visuals<\/div>Gone with the Demon runs on Unreal Engine 4 which is a good thing for the game. Visually the game creates a claustrophobic atmosphere and looks generally good throughout. The enemy designs are all right but the boss designs truly stand out and visually create an impact. Though the game looks good and the animations of combat looks fluid, the game suffers because of repetition and the movement of c haracters.<br><br> The way the enemies move simply make them non intimidating when they are alone. The rooms are repeated way too often and there is a certain of lack of distinguish between the said rooms. It is not helped by the fact that these rooms are mostly empty and lack enough details.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Sound<\/div><br>There are two parts to the sound design of Gone with the Demon. While the main soundtrack is good and compliments its atmosphere by creating a creepy vibe, the grunts and other sounds made by the player and the enemies are absolutely trash. They need some changing to feel actually scary.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Overall<\/div><br>Gone with the Demon is a game with huge potential. It is still in early access and has a long way to go before it is ready to release. But it does have a very interesting combat and RPG system and requires to think before you start mindlessly swinging around your knife. It is in dire need of some polish but it can turn out to be something special in the future. <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Pros:<\/div><br><b>Solid Melee combat<\/b><br><b>Interesting RPG system<\/b><br><b>Creepy atmosphere<\/b><br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Cons:<\/div><br><b>Lack of proper story<\/b><br><b>Difficult learning curve<\/b><br><b>Lots of repetition<\/b><br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Rating: 65\/100<\/div><br><br>This review was brought to you by SaveorQuit.
