Review<\/div>\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\t\t\t\t\t<div class=\"postedDate\">Posted: June 17, 2016\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t<div class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNOTE: This game was given to me by the developer for review purposes. This did not influence my review in any way.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">==========================================================<br>\n---------------------------------------OVERVIEW-------------------------------------<br>\n==========================================================<\/div>Note: Johnny Graves - The Unchosen One is currently in Early Access. The version reviewed was the latest as of the date of this review.<br><br>According to the information on the Steam page for this game, Johnny Graves is an action RPG and the first game in a planned trilogy of games set in an original urban fantasy \/ horror setting called the Night of the Creatures.<br><br>In Johnny Graves you play Johnny, a former agent of hell with a psychic link to Ivy, a woman who offers advice and assistance in your quest. Your quest itself, it seems, is to rescue someone called Paddy, but this is unclear: in its current state, the game doesn't provide any introduction or background information at all, so it's hard to work out just what the plot of the final product will be. The Steam page offers considerably more information on the game world.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">==========================================================<br>\n----------------------------------------QUALITY-------------------------------------<br>\n==========================================================<\/div>Graphics - The 3D graphics are a mixed bag. Most of the environmental graphics are very nice, if touch heavy on the browns and greys. Character and zombie graphics are rather blocky, though well animated. The dynamic lighting and shadows are excellent. Exploding zombies look terrible when they explode, and the fire from the fire-spitting spiders (WTF?) is not wonderfully convincing either, but it's passable. There's no separate torso turn animation, so Johnny's gun does not follow the crosshairs used to aim.<br><br>Given that the game is essentially an action game, it also seems to be missing some visual indicators of damage, as well. Without the sound effects it's impossible to know whether a zombie is hitting Johnny or not, and Johnny's gunshots only make a very unsatisfying temporary spray of red pixels that are presumably supposed to be blood. Killed zombies collapse like ragdolls (which is cool), without any visible effects of multiple gunshot wounds (which is not cool). The graphics give no real 'feel' of taking or dishing out damage.<br><br>The game so far features a couple of conversations between Johnny and Ivy, and these include quite good 2D character portrait overlays, which is a nice touch.<br><br>Standard Unity graphics options are provided in the launcher: resolution, full screen \/ window, and a graphics quality drop-down allowing a selection between Fastest and Beautiful. I played in 1920x1080 in Beautiful and the game mostly performed well, although some of the larger scrolling sections had noticeable tearing. A vsync option might be effective here.<br><br>Sound - Johnny Graves features a well-produced and suitably spooky music track in the background, but so far there's only the one. Sounds, too, are limited: a grunt when Johnny is hit and a very loud gunshot when he shoots were all that I heard, I think.<br><br>At present the lack of ambient environmental, movement, and enemy sounds makes the game feel rather empty.<br><br>Gameplay - Although the Steam page claims that Johnny Graves is an &quot;action RPG&quot;, I currently see no evidence of the RPG part; instead it plays like a fairly standard isometric third-person shooter that I suspect may end up including some platforming elements (given the inclusion of a jump control).<br><br>You move Johnny around with WASD and independently move his pistol crosshairs and shoot with the mouse. With a controller I can see that this could be controlled like a typical twin-stick shooter, although given the speed of movement of the zombies included in the game so far, I suspect the added precision of the mouse might be required.<br><br>The game is currently VERY short and only includes one level. After finishing this level (which should take around 20 minutes), you're shown a brief message that this is only level one out of seven, and level two will be released &quot;in the summer 2016&quot;. As mentioned previously, there's also no introduction or background story provided. You're dumped from the main menu into a graveyard, Johnny and Ivy exchange some banter that doesn't make a whole lot of sense without understanding why Johnny is there, or who Ivy is, and then you start walking through the graveyard and killing zombies. According to the Steam page, there is a fairly clear plan for the rest of the content that will be included gradually as the game goes through Early Access. In its current state, though, it's barely a taste.<br><br>The in-game text conversations between Johnny and Ivy are well written so far, with mostly good grammar. I hope the final game includes more of these (with a bit of editing), as they really add some interest to the game.<br><br>In its current state, Johnny Graves also has a number of issues that make it a bit clunky to play. The worst of these is that there's no way to rotate the view; camera control is automatic, and often not ideal. Even in this short demo level I found myself being beset by enemies from very close by off one edge of the screen, and with no way to rotate the view to move a tree out of the way of the camera, I couldn't see them until they were on top of Johnny. There's also no zoom, which is a bit sad, but nowhere near as important as the missing manual camera rotation. Finally, it's very easy to get stuck in open graves, requiring frustrating jumps to get out, or up against a lamppost or other environmental object, making positioning Johnny in the heat of battle much more frustrating than it should be.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">==========================================================<br>\n-------------------------------------PROS &amp; CONS------------------------------------<br>\n==========================================================<\/div>Pros:<br>+ Original modern horror \/ fantasy setting that could be quite interesting: demons, spiders, zombies, and guns<br>+ Existing level has nice atmospheric graphics and excellent lighting and shadows<br>+ Good music<br>+ Clear plan for Early Access progression<br><br>Cons:<br>- No introduction, so although the story seems like it could be interesting, it's hard to tell<br>- VERY short at the moment: I died once and it still only took me 27 minutes to get through the current single level<br>- Some clunkiness: it's easy to get stuck on level geometry, making the game more frustrating than it should be<br>- Character graphics are quite blocky and small, although well animated<br>- Bland character and enemy textures<br>- Very few sounds<br>- No way to manually zoom (minor) or rotate (major) the view; automated camera positioning is not always ideal<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">==========================================================<br>\n--------------------------------------CONCLUSION-----------------------------------<br>\n==========================================================<\/div>Johnny Graves - The Unchosen One has the potential to be a very fun game. It's not there yet -- it needs some work on the core gameplay and the developers need to finish their planned roadmap for the game -- but it's on the way.<br><br>As it stands at the moment, it's more of a demo than a game, and most definitely doesn't pass my personal value test at its current price point. But it is an Early Access title by developers with a clear plan, and I think that's worth a chance. I'd love a &quot;maybe&quot; button in Steam; I'm hesitant in recommending it now, but I'd expect that recommendation to get considerably stronger over the coming months as the game matures.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">5\/10<\/div><br><div class=\"bb_code\">For more recommendations by LL's Game Giveaways and Reviews on quality indie games or ones with high potential follow our
