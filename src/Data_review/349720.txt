(yes this game is that old(er) ) As I read a lot of the reviews below me, I've seen that many of the reviewers had come into the game with their mindset thinking that this was supposed to be DDR Online, or an OSU competitor. I can honestly tell you that this game is neither. <br><br>Audition Online is a heavy rhythm based online dancing game with heavy social aspects. Originally made in Korea, the game has very heavy K-pop culture references (fashion mall\/ K pop dance moves), and many Korean songs. If you are annoyed by pop, electronic, or music that is in a foreign language, you most likely will pass on the game. There are enough English songs to pass for an American server, but a vast majority of songs are in Korean. <br><br>As said above, the Korean culture for the game is quite immense. The fashion mall (cash shop) is full of Korean Pop star references and clothing that would mostly make sense to the Kpop audience. The cash shop is completely optional. NOTHING in the cash shop will enhance your skills to the point of unfairness. There is a non-cash currency. (beats). These beats can be earned by playing with other players in the server. The selection of Beats items is quite small compared to the cash items, but there is enough to satisfy one's fashion sense to an extent. <br><br>The Gameplay for Audition is very different from DDR or OSU online. This is something that many new players fail to understand when they write the game off as a DDR clone. Players are given a combination of arrow keys that correspond to the 4 directional keys on the keyboard or the numpad, and must press those keys within the 4\/4 metronome displayed, and then hit the space key once the bar reaches the 4\/4 on the metronome. The metronome's speed is determined by each songs BPM. Often new players assume that they are really button mashing, but every arrow key combination is attached to a certain dance move. The fact is that this is just ONE of Audition's many different game modes.<br><br>Audition has roughly over 10 UNIQUE game modes, with about 15-20 different variations of certain game modes. Each mode has a different concept, a different way to play, and win. Some are team games, some are solo (story mode), and some are social modes (wedding, love party, club). I'd advise the DDR fans to start off with Beat Up mode which utilizes the players numpads using the keys (741\/963) as the keys are tapped in direct relation to the rhythm, unlike &quot;Normal Mode&quot; where only key combinations are pressed followed by the space bar at each fourth beat. <br><br>The game has a VERY steep learning curve. If you are prone to quitting easily, or not interested in taking the time to learn the different modes, I'd not recommend the game to you.  The game is currently in need of new players and a revitalization in its community. However, if you'd like to try something different, make some new friends, and even improve your playing skills.
