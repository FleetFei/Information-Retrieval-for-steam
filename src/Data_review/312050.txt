game very similar to games like Puzzle Agent and Professor Layton, although it has a more serious tone and puzzles require a bit more effort than just pure logic. In fact, it blends together some of the better elements found in other (point-and-click) adventures games and does this successfully, but ultimately (and as expected) not without any flaws.<br><br>Red Johnson is a private investigator who\u2019s investigating a murder at Drownedman\u2019s Bridge. One clue leads to another and before you know it, you\u2019re in the middle of it. The story is actually pretty clich\u00e9, and the final payoff isn\u2019t as good as it should be. Furthermore, the game ends with a cliffhanger, one that wasn\u2019t even necessary to begin with, in my opinion. Still, we got a part two because of it, because Chronicles is not just one game, but two. So you get two games for the price of one. Well, two chapters is more like it, but come on, that is a good deal.<br>But let\u2019s not get ahead of ourselves and let\u2019s just focus on the gameplay for a minute. You view the world from a fixed (first person) perspective and with your mouse you can click on points of interest. Some of these are useless (thankfully, there aren\u2019t so many of these) and others are useful. The useful ones will usually zoom in on the object and let you solve a puzzle, or puzzles, next.<br>Puzzles range from slide puzzles and \u2018detail\u2019 puzzles (rotating an object in search of a clue, item or answer) to character confrontations where you have to pick the right answer.<br>Solving puzzles will earn you money and this money can later be used if you don\u2019t know how to finish one. Hints are relatively cheap to buy so you could get through the game by just using one of the many, many hints. It clearly needed a bit of tweaking in that area.<br>There\u2019s actually a good variety of puzzles here. Unfortunately, the game doesn\u2019t always use checkpoints. Sometimes it does, but sometimes it doesn\u2019t and when the latter happens you have to start the puzzle all over again. This is awkward game design and something that shouldn\u2019t have happened to begin with. Some of the puzzles are tediously boring or hard to solve and if you have to quit because of a lack of time or because you don\u2019t feel like playing it anymore you shouldn\u2019t punish the player for this. Same goes for quitting the game while trying to solve a puzzle; the game registers it as one attempt if you quit halfway through.<br>Sometimes puzzles reward you with evidence and that evidence can be analyzed and compared by a device set up at your headquarters. These results can then be used to progress even further, by finding new suspects and places to go to, or interrogating suspects.<br>Every now and then a QTE will trigger and it\u2019s because of the cool controls that these encounters become even remotely interesting in the first place. The mouse is used to create a motion so let\u2019s say that you have some screws you need to get rid of then you have to rotate the mouse in a circular motion to unscrew them. During encounters the game will ask you to press the left button and move up, down, or anywhere else depending on the given situation. But, yes (there\u2019s always a but), the controls just don\u2019t work. I\u2019ve failed many encounters because the game didn\u2019t register them properly. Unnecessary and evil, because the game ranks you based on the number of tries it took you to finish an event or puzzle.<br>While you\u2019re not doing any of the things mentioned above you have to search apartments and areas for clues. Unfortunately, some of the objects you have to search for are too hard to see because the graphics are either too dark or because the object isn\u2019t instantly visible to you because it\u2019s tucked away in a corner. So in the end it comes down to pixel hunting and that\u2019s again something that shouldn\u2019t be needed in this game, although it\u2019s something we see often in the point-and-click genre.<br>The story is the usual \u2019whodunnit\u2019 and even though it\u2019s clich\u00e9, it\u2019s executed pretty well. The characters all have the right motivations and the whole thing connects together nicely in the end.<br><br>The graphics consists of static backgrounds mixed with some small animations but on the PC they\u2019re fuzzy and blurry and anything by hi-res. They\u2019re also mostly brownish but that\u2019s just the style of the game. Character animations are fine, but never great, and they\u2019re also a bit unsharp. Lip syncing doesn\u2019t seem to be there, characters just start moving their lips as soon as you enter a conversation.<br>Overall, the style of the game is pretty good, but it could\u2019ve used some improvements here and there.<br>The voice acting is actually pretty good (which surprised me) and same goes for the jazzy music.<br><br>The game is pretty long. I think it will take anywhere between 4 or 6 hours to complete the first chapter and after that you still have chapter 2 to complete. My estimate is that it\u2019s roughly the same length; I still have to finish it. But it\u2019s good value for your money. You get a good amount of gameplay here.<br><br>Red Johnson\u2019s Chronicles isn\u2019t the game I hoped it would be. I expected another Professor Layton\/Puzzle agent type of game, but got a regular point-and-click adventure instead. It mixes some fine gameplay elements found in other adventure games, and although that doesn\u2019t make the game awesome or great it\u2019s still become a somewhat enjoyable game in the
