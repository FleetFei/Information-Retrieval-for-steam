TL;DR version: Cool game, its just fun to fight multitude of orcs and the personality\\diversity of captains will keep you entertained.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Story:<\/div> I am not a LOTR fan, read the first book and stopped in the middle of the second due to having so many characters and couldnt remember who was who. Eventually watched the movie and liked, but still not really a huge thing to me as it is to some people.<br><br>The game has some classic characters from the series, and I think they fit the story well. But after all said and done, I wasnt really impressed with it, and after two lame battles with quick time events, the ending left me confused since it didnt answer my question, and was a cliffhanger.<br><br>The voice acting of the characters in the story is good, but they really excel as you listen to the random orc banter in game, they are very varied and fun, the writers and voice actors did a great job in making them interesting.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay:<\/div> I am going to separate this part in multiple sections.<br><br>First of all, if you want a challenge, disable <b>all<\/b> Runes DLC that you may have gotten, they are really overpowered. Granting a chance of avoiding a fatal blow, increasing slow-motion time when aiming with a bow or doing extra damage when you have a hit-streak can help a lot in the beginning, theres even one that makes the character immune to poison, eliminating a game mechanic right from start. Another one that grants those unbalanced runes is the free Defiance DLC, but it also includes a game trial mode and a new character skin. So just avoid using those runes and earn your own.<br><br>Most of the game you fight Uruks and it starts off frighteningly simple and easy, you just have a attack and parry button and use them all the time. When a ranged enemy attacks, you do a roll (which I fail to do often, and the vast majority of my deaths were due to ranged captains who have an attack that removes 70% of your health striking me as I was focused on the enemies nearby). Eventually the captains you find with certain invulnerabilities and new kinds of soldiers that appear some time later will require a little more diversity in your moves.<br><br>One of the interesting points in the game is the amount of foes you will face at the same time. As you battle with a small group of orcs, the ones nearby will investigate the noise and join the fray. If you invade a fortress and let them sound the alarm, be ready to fight 30 or more enemies and more will keep coming. At the beginning this will be a little difficult and time consuming, but as you unlock skills it becomes easier since they allow you defeat multiple foes in a short amount of time.<br><br>Talking about special skills, one you acquire later in the game allows you to <span class=\"bb_spoiler\"><span>command the enemy orcs, by branding them. You can easily build a army by conquering them and making them fight the enemy for you. It also plays an important part in the plot.<\/span><\/span><br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">-Nemesis:<\/div> The much talked about Nemesis system, is where random uruks assigned to be captains and warchiefs, the game equivalent of bosses, rise and fall in strength and rank due to your action or inaction.<br><br>They all have different faces, strengths, weakness, armor (that improves as they raise in rank) and personality. There are more than 20 of them spread around the map and you will constantly find them even if you arent looking for it. Its very common for you to fight a random group of orcs, and they randomly appear and greet you before a fight. You can even be hunting for a specific captain, and in the middle of combat one or two more show up to make things harder.<br><br>They can also be only available to fight in specific missions. When they are recruiting orcs, having a feast, battling a beast to prove his worth or fight another captain he isnt fond of. You can interrupt those events or just watch them unfold, with them gaining strength if they succeed.<br><br>Some are bodyguards of the Warchiefs, the top bosses who answer to Sauron, who will only appear when special conditions are met, such as making a orc he is fond of squeal, or killing multiple followers with fire to get his attention.<br><br>The lines of captains and warchiefs can be menacing, sophisticated or just plain crazy, and they all have unique speech for each situation. They can remember if you run away from them calling you a coward, or if they ran from you, for a strategic retreat. They comment if you are surrounded by enemies, sneaked around to attack him or if you kept &quot;killing&quot; them but they came back (one captain I killed 3 times, the last fight removing his head. But he later appeared with a mask, swearing vengeance... surprising and amusing me to no end).<br><br>They all have strengths and weakness that you should pay attention to, specially at high levels. You can find a captain\\warchief who is afraid of nearly everything and make him a easy target exploiting his weakness, as a example by burning him, or insta-killing him with a stealth or ranged attack, or fight against one who is a warmaster with fire weapon and do a devastating grab attack and have a sequence of blows that you must parry each, coupled with invulnerability of stun or regular strikes. They can become stronger if you enrage them. One of the possibilities is a hate for a beast called Caragor, if you bring one to battle (or they accidentally appear), you will make him angry and stronger.<br><br>They are a great addition to the game. In one case I fought a captain, almost beat him but he got enraged for &quot;fear of losing&quot; but  immediately started to run because a captain he dislikes appeared soon afterwards, triggering his &quot;fear of rival&quot;. You can get a lot of unique situations that you cant predict due to this.<br><br>Remember the special skill I mentioned before? With it you can <span class=\"bb_spoiler\"><span>dominate captains who are bodyguards of a warchief, and make them betray him and fight along your side when you finally meet him. And if you brand a warchief, you will be able to make him do a riot and along with the orcs he commands attack another warchief<\/span><\/span><br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">-Stealth:<\/div> Stealth is another part of the game. Even if you dislike this, fret not. The ones that are essential to missions are rare and quite easy if you dont aim for the extra objectives. I found it to be a good way of dispatching Orcs in fortresses, but if you just prefer to go <span class=\"bb_strike\">guns<\/span> swords blazing, thats completely doable. The optional stealth missions can be a little challenging for people who arent used to sneaking around, but I find them to be fun and forgiving on the difficulty scale.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Expansions:<\/div> The game have some challenges (trials) and two expansions. The challenges are interesting but unfortunately they give a bonus if you are fast enough, and I hate fighting against the clock, so I didnt have much fun trying to hurry up and kill the captains and warchiefs as quickly as I could. My brief review of the expansions will be from memory of 3 years ago so I wont give much details on it, sorry. Look for them in the comments.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Verdict:<\/div> Overall this is just a fun game to play, and if you are a LOTR fan it will probably be even better. Like all games it will be repetitive, specially if you aim for doing everything available on it, but you dont care because you are just mowing down orcs and having fun finding the weakpoints of the captains\\warchiefs.
