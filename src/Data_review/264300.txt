The game definitely has its problems but it\u2019s worth it to play if you enjoy horde survival games and are looking for a cheap one to kill some time.  7\/10<br><br>Guns N Zombies\u2019 story is pretty thin, and is told through short cutscenes at the start of each mission. This means you need to pick up the ones on the ground if you want more to appear, otherwise you\u2019ll be killing enemies, and nothing will drop. Between missions you can spend your crystals on upgrades such as body armor, better weapons, and upgrades to your turrets. Also bear in mind that you have a character level, which limits what equipment you have access to. Most of the gear you can buy has a level requirement, and the levels don\u2019t actually have any other benefit (you don\u2019t get stronger when you level up, you just get the ability to buy better gear).<br><br>The \u201ctower defense\u201d part of the game is actually pretty limited. Each mission only has a few places where you can actually place a tower (referred to as turrets), and you have a \u201ccool down\u201d between placing and upgrading the towers. This means you can\u2019t just lay down a huge number of towers and expect them to protect you. You place a tower, and you can\u2019t upgrade or place a new one for about 30 seconds. This means that you\u2019re going to need to fight for yourself about 90% of the game, don\u2019t count on the towers to save you.  There are 2 character classes that you can choose from at the start of the game, a fighter class and an engineer class.  The fighter has more health and can have 3 special abilities (you buy these). The engineer can create more and stronger towers. I\u2019ll grant you that some of these high level engineer towers are pretty awesome (black hole cannon!), but the 30 second cool down is a real party pooper. In many levels the enemies spawn endlessly, so you\u2019re not just waiting 30 seconds, you\u2019re fighting for your life while waiting for it.<br><br>Pros:<br>+Nice graphics and some cool enemies and weapons.
