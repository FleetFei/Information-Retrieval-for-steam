that I, personally, effin' hate the concept of soulmates, or at least the notion of it that most people (and media) seem to have. So, when I first stumbled upon this game, I had zero expectations for it. I decided to play it anyway since I do so with any free VN I get my hands on, but I honestly thought it would be just another clich\u00e9 thing that would have me rolling my eyes at stupid idealistic notions of romance the entire time.<br><br>So it was a lovely surprise when I found myself constantly laughing at the humor and smiling at the cute scenes rather than rolling my eyes at sappy overused ideas. The way the &quot;red string of fate&quot; issue was handled was also unexpected due to how down-to-earth it was. In the game, Valerie starts off obsessed with the immature ideas usually associated to the concept of fated lovers, and rather than fostering her delusions or including some plot twist that would prove them right all along, the game crushes all of them down by making Val realise (either the &quot;easy&quot; way by noticing it herself or the hard way by chasing her soulmate to no avail) that not everyone has\/needs a perfect predestined relationship to find love and be happy. It is, overall, a surprisingly realistic take on such an idealistic topic.<br><br>The characters are funny and endearing. Rather than sticking to heavy stereotypes, you just get an extrovert with a light route and an introvert with a deeper story, and both are well-written. I like how the guy whose route you don't go for still sticks around as a good friend, and rather than a good or bad end, you basically get a romance end and a friendship end. Furthermore, instead of relying on how much you appease your guy of choice and whether he will stay with you or dump you, your choices only shape the way that Valerie herself handles her (and the guy's) already existing feelings. After the first choice, which is the one to set the route, you are the one to choose whether Val will move on from the red strings and embrace her love, or wallow in her obsession and refuse to acknowledge what she feels for the guy of choice. And even when you don't achieve a romance end and make Val turn down the guy, they still stay as very close friends, which also shows that (unrequited) love doesn't need to be the end-all-be-all and friendship is just as good <span class=\"bb_spoiler\"><span>yep, they crush down the concept of ~friendzone~ too, and I freakin love them for it.<\/span><\/span> Despite its simplicity, I found this otome to be very innovative in that regard.<br><br>The technical side doesn't disappoint either, despite the art being a little subpar. There is a good amount of choices, most of which are intuitive enough, and a good number of CGs placed just in the right moments (although narration mentions changes in characters' poses\/expressions that don't always reflect on the CGs, which may get kind of weird). The GUI looks cute, the music is pleasing and you can check a gallery with 2 extra illustrations besides all CGs.<br><br>Despite its flaws (the start was too fast for me and there were minor inconsistencies in writing), I liked this novel a lot. I recommend it without a doubt, whether you're into the cheesy fate thing or a more realistic person who still enjoys some cute romance.
