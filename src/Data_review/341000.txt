But after having put some real time into Mad Games tycoon, I can say quite frankly that I'm probably never playing it again. It's not meant to be a diss toward them -- it's a great game, through and through -- but MGT is better in almost every sense of the phrase.<br><br>MGT is a tycoon game wherein you build a game studio, release games (no, you don't actually make them\/ can't play them), and eventually work your way up to producing consoles, MMOs, and more. If you've played GDT, MGT probably sounds pretty familiar. But in this game, you're actually doing floor layouts for your buildings. Your options when hiring workers are better. The gameplay feels smoother and more polished. You can self-produce your games and go into publishing the works of others. There's even an admittedly-dodgy stock market system where you can buy out your competition. All told, MGT is a lot like GDT... only there's more of everything, and everything feels better-refined.<br><br>Well, almost everything.<br><br>MGT does come up short in a few aspects, most notably with its failure to include a Steam-like distribution service that would cut out physical media production altogether. When was the last time you bought a game in a brick-and-mortar store, with a disc and everything? 2005? Yeah... this game needs to offer players the ability to publish on\/ create a service similar to Steam. GDT has something sort of like this, so why doesn't MGT?<br><br>Another problem, one that befalls most tycoon games in general, is an utter lack of creative throughput. When designing a game, you can basically that game a name, a broad genre, and a couple of basic topics... and that's it. Where are the sliders for making stories more intricate? Where's the mad-libs-style topic input where I'm defining protagonists, antogonists, and setting? Where do I design the game to feature open-world? Offering these sorts of customization options can make a game considerably more fun and addictive, even if your choices don't have real consequences. Rock God Tycoon lets you write music. The Movies allowed you to make films and watch your creations (why the crap isn't that game being remade?!?!). I want more options when making games, even if I can't play them!<br><br>All told, Mad Games Tycoon is a very fun, addictive game. It has its drawbacks, but every game does.
