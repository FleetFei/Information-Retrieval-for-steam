class=\"postedDate\">Posted: May 1, 2016\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t<div class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\t<div class=\"bb_h1\">Do not anger the gods, humaniform. <\/div><br>MegaSphere is a futuristic 2D platforming action game developed by AKGames. In MegaSphere you play as ATAI, a special ops combat unit that has been sent to investigate some strange goings on with a few nearby stars. You find yourself in a strange installation filled with deadly robots and strange references to something known as the Half-Dead god. <br><br>Full disclosure: A review copy was provided by the developer. <br><br>Up first I\u2019m going to talk about my two biggest issues with the game (which will be addressed in the next update though it currently has no expected release time). These issues are the lack of a save system and the lack of any real direction in the game. The lack of a save system is pretty self-explanatory and it means that you have to start over again every time you quit. It can get pretty frustrating (even with the current amount of content) if you have to keep restarting the same area, plus the levels are randomly generated so if you were trying to do something in particular and you have to leave, you might as well give up on doing that since you may not see it the next time you load that area. As I said, my second problem is with the lack of direction in the game, hopefully this\u2019ll be fixed by the addition of quests as well as a chunk of story in the upcoming update I mentioned. As it stands though, MegaSphere ends up feeling like an exploration game more than anything else. I enjoyed wandering through the six different areas currently in the game, killing enemies and crafting new weapons, but after looking around all of the different areas I feel like I\u2019ve seen all there is to see, even with randomly generated levels and that\u2019s a tad disappointing. Once these are addressed in the next update I\u2019ll update my review and re-write or remove this paragraph.<br><br>On to what is undoubtedly the game\u2019s greatest strength, its visuals. MegaSphere is incredibly good looking, it has some of the best pixel art I\u2019ve seen in games and its design is consistent throughout. It\u2019s definitely one of the best looking games that uses the Unity engine. The visual effects in the game are also awesome. There\u2019s good variation between all the different weapons and there are some particularly cool effects for when you get close to dying, though with any luck you won\u2019t be seeing much of them! The menus and UI are also pretty good but they\u2019re being streamlined in the next update which should make them even better.<br><br>Next to walking around, combat is what you\u2019ll spend your time doing, thankfully it\u2019s pretty good. There are a few different types of enemies each with very different ways of engaging you which makes combat with more than one type of enemy particularly interesting. A couple of the enemy types also have different variations, the different variations have different appearances as well as different attacks, further diversifying the game\u2019s combat. It\u2019s pretty challenging on normal difficulty (there is an easier difficulty if you do find it a little too hard) but once you figure out how all of the different enemies work it does become reasonably easy to predict how they\u2019re going to attack which means you can try and avoid their attacks. Once you\u2019ve figured this out you can also start using the environment to your advantage by standing\/hiding out of range of an enemy and shooting at them from there.<br><br>Killing enemies always drops health but sometimes you\u2019ll also get weapons, upgrades and shields. You begin with three health segments on your health bar but when you collect health it just adds new segments if you have full health. I\u2019ve only made it as far as six full segments before losing health so I\u2019m not sure what the maximum amount is. A shield simply goes on top of your health bar and offers you more protection, they don\u2019t recharge though so you\u2019ll have to find a new one once yours is fully drained.<br><br>There are quite a few different weapons in the game, ranging from the burst rifle you start with to a grenade launcher and even a flaming bear gun! All of the weapons have advantages and disadvantages so they are mostly balanced though the flaming bear gun does seem to be considerably better than any of the other weapons. Sometimes the weapons come with upgrades (the flaming bear seems to always come with all three upgrade slots filled) but they can also come with no upgrades which means you\u2019ll need to find upgrade tokens as well as upgrades to fully upgrade them.<br><br>Upgrading weapons currently takes place in the game\u2019s inventory menu. Each weapon (providing it doesn\u2019t already have upgrades installed) has three slots for new upgrades, one of which can be attached \u2018for free\u2019 while the other two can only be attached using upgrade tokens which are sometimes dropped by enemies. There are quite a few different upgrades, including speed increases and upgrades that make your projectiles bounce off walls. However, not all of the upgrades are effective for all of the weapons and it does take a bit of experimentation to figure out what works best for which weapons. There are also different levels of upgrades (I\u2019ve seen up to and including three so far) and as you\u2019d expect, the higher the upgrade\u2019s level is the more it improves your weapon. <br><br>As I mentioned earlier, there are only six areas in the game. Two of them don\u2019t have enemies, one is a boss fight area and the other three are just \u2018regular\u2019 areas filled with all the enemies. There are visual differences between all the different levels as well as some things you only find in certain areas. Every time you play an area the game randomly generates the level so you\u2019ll never have exactly the same experience twice though the levels will look pretty familiar. The reason they aren\u2019t completely random is because there is a story running between the different levels, I don\u2019t want to spoil too much (and to be honest I don\u2019t understand it enough to spoil it anyway) but the game\u2019s story seems to revolve around strange gods worshipped by whatever created the installation you\u2019ve found yourself on. There\u2019s not a whole lot of story in there at the moment but what\u2019s there is most certainly intriguing. <br><br>Last but by no means least, controls. For combat and generally moving around they\u2019re pretty good (I played with an Xbox One controller) but they did feel a tad bit clunky for platforming. There were several times in the game where I had to attempt jumps between platforms a few times before I actually made it. It\u2019s not a huge problem (though it\u2019s still pretty frustrating) during regular gameplay but you could quickly end up dead if you run into a particularly tricky platforming section during combat.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Verdict <\/div><br>MegaSphere is a beautiful game with good gameplay and a mysterious story behind it but the amount of content in the game at the moment, as well as the lack of direction, does, for me at least, let the game down a little bit. It\u2019s still an enjoyable experience but I\u2019d recommend that you wait for a sale or at least see how the upcoming update improves the game. <br><br><i><a class=\"bb_link\" <br><br>If you remember the old Crack Dot Com game ABUSE on the PC or Bungie's port of it to the Mac OSX, then you'll remember what a fantastic game that was. Tight controls, frantic action, great atmosphere and amazing weapons. Megasphere is very clearly taking some lessons from that classic, and this is absolutely a great thing.<br><br>Bear in mind though this game is in early access so it's nowhere near as polished as Abuse was. It plays more or less the same, though a touch slower. At first this annoyed me but then I realised it's because with so much flying at you, you need time to strategise as well as react. Because the environments are partially destructable (yes you can actually tunnel through walls... within certain limitations which are fairly logical from a gameplay design standpoint, more on that later), and because there are hazards in the environment that can help or hinder you, you need the additional time to respond to be able to make proper use of them.<br><br>There's some great touches here and there, some fantastic spritework and animations, levels are dark and brooding but still clear enough to see what's going on, enemies are lit up rather nicely without glowing like beacons and cluttering up the gameworld, though it would be nice to see some kind of targeting reticule and if the aiming cursor could JUST stand out a little more...  Enemies drop life pickups when they die, but they don't stick around long and other enemies can pick them up too (which is pretty neat actually), so there's a bit of a risk-reward thing going on there, do you get right in the thick of it and risk tons of damage or do you hang back and obliterate them then pick up what remains if you're quick enough?<br><br>Given that the only two languages are russian and english, and the english is somewhat dodgily translated at points, I'm gathering the game devs are Russian, but they've done a pretty good job of the translation all things considered. It's certainly not the worst.<br><br>If I have any criticisms it's that the game realllly doesn't teach you anything about how to play, making some of the gameplay mechanics ridiculously obtuse, like how am I meant to kill the boss, and how do I upgrade my weapons... The latter point there I managed to work out through fumbling about, but killing Rosyanka-1 still eludes me. I assume it has something to do with those buttons that drop exploding fire...things... which you can grab and throw wiht one of the wepaons I picked up but... I'm still confused as to whether I'md oing any damage or not, could do with more feedback.<br><br>Okay so, basic rundown of the destructable environments then. Basically if two rooms neighbour each other, you can tunnel through the walls into them. If there's no room nearby you can &quot;dig&quot; like three or four blocks out in any direction that doesn't neighbour a room before you hit unbreakable terrain. These blocks can occasionally fall down and make steps or block progress, there are cables which are phys-enabled hanging from the ceiling that you can shoot, and yes they will slow your fall, or block your jumping progress, or trap certain enemies to... There's exploding... things that you can shoot to make them pop into firey painful stuff, which I believe can damage enemies too, though not much. OOne gun I found can pick up and throw items like detatched blocks, enemies and explodythings too, which was neat.<br><br>The upgrade system is pretty fantastic, you can find modules which you can plug into existing gun-types that you pickup to grant them additional power. Granted once they're set, that's it, you can't pull them back out again so choose wisely.<br><br><br>OKAY, so, pros and cons then:<br>Pros:<br>Gameplay is rocksolid, but then it basically plays much like ABUSE, so that's a given.<br>Nice variety of guns and a great weapon upgrade system keeps things interesting and fresh.<br>Dynamic soundtrack responds to threats.<br>Great visual effects when you take damage and are at critical levels of health lets you know when to bug out (if you can) to reassess your situation.<br>Destructable terrain and phys-enabled objects<br>Great sprite-based visuals that don't clutter or get massively confusing in huge combats.<br>Nice wide field of view allows you to tell when combat's about to happen (something ABUSE Lacked, sadly)<br>Cutscenes are now skippable (hold Space!)<br>Neat little (semi) hidden hint to give you a clue as to how to beat the first boss (check your map in the first actual level when you enter, should be easy to find).<br>Invisible-immortal foes\/foes in walls bug seems to be fixed!<br>Devs respond rather quickly to issues!<br>Relentlessly challenging! (though also could be a downside if you're easily frustrated)<br>Every run is different! Proceedurally generated levels!<br><br>Cons: (REMOVED UNTIL I CAN VERIFY EXISTENCE OF ANY BUGS OR ISSUES)<br>Only two languages to choose from, English and Russian (Translation to English now appears better!)<br>(have not tried dying yet with a save point reached so don't know if that bug still happens)<br>Mouse cursor still kind of tiny.<br>Brutally, brutally difficult. Though also a plus because it's nice to be challenged by a game, and this game certainly does not handhold you. It lets you make your own mistakes.<br>Still could do with just something to run you through how to upgrade guns (not what combos are the best or what they do, just how to do it) and maybe something to let you know what level each upgrade module is before socketing it. (also slight b ug with weapon upgrades displaying wrong level and name when you hover over them)<br>Game ends shortly after first boss, but that's to be expected, we are in Alpha :)<br><br>Even at this stage of Early Access, the game is fantastic and well worth paying for and playing! Its difficulty is high enough to make you work real hard for your wins, but not so punishing that it'll drive you away.
