Just as soon as <b>I<\/b> stop dying.<br><br>Lamia Must Die is a game of adventure wherein you are not just one person but rather four people. You can change two of your party members and their styles are definitely unique. You adventure into a dungeon and skip all the trash tier monsters completely. The only game play is a big epic boss battle.<br><br><b>Graphics and Art<\/b><br><br>The graphics are not that good, but it's excuseable. This game runs mainly on artwork and pictures anyway and it's played through various GUI's, which all look and feel rather fluid and work well. The artwork itself is rather nice and sometimes kind of mediocre, though certainly better than I could care to draw. I'm not completely convinced that some of the pictures of the characters drawn were drawn by the creators of the game or anyone they hired. I don't think they were commissions and rather just something free. If they were drawn by the creators then I ask why there is no consistency in the art work. Some of it looks great while others look like it was drawn by a complete ametuer who had way too much interest in \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665. I of course refer primarily to Lamia. <br><br><b>Story and Characters<\/b><br><br>The story is pretty alright. I give it a 6\/10. Basically, there is a snake-lady (Naga?) monster attacking the village and eating their children. Many people have tried to slay this foul, big \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665d beast but all of the soldiers were eaten. They probably couldn't tear their eyes away from her \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665. So now you and your vagabond party of adventurers\/heroes\/whatever-the-f*cks embark on a perilous journey to which you get little to no part in as far as interaction goes to venture into Lamia's cave and slay the bodacious reptilian babe. A bit sexist, if you ask me. No one's asking me so now we continue. It gives a campfire scene and talks about what they're going to do and how they're going to get to Lamia. This kind of bugs me because &quot;You&quot; say that it's not as easy as just getting to the big bad monster, but it skips any gameplay that could have happened anyway. You're treated to a dialogue between yourself(s) and Lamia to which you learn a little more about her and then combat is engaged in. Overall, it's a fairly &quot;meh&quot; storyline.<br><br>What I do like is the characters. They all have their reasons for being there, whether that be royal responsibility or just straight up revenge. They have different personalities and lives, as you would be able to see if you beat the game. The outro for each character is different depending on if they died or not. Try and find them all! I found myself at least a little bit drawn into their characters and those characters' lives, despite how little I'm told about them and how little exposure I get. <br><br><b>Sound and Music<\/b><br><br>The sounds are what you'd expect from a game like this. For the most part, generic. Though they are satisfying to some extent. You've likely heard most of the sound effects before. Something nice is that they voiced the character's themselves. My favourite is the Male Paladin, as he does the best job. I also quite enjoyed the narrator's voice and I'd encourage them to dub anime or something as they have fantastic voices. Unfortunately, most of the women don't seem to be so gifted and they seem to be trying a bit too hard to sound Fantasy-esque and it just doesn't end up working for them, though I wouldn't say they're terrible. Just average at best.<br><br>The music, while I'm unsure if it's stock music or composed music, was fitting to the theme and sounded good. I didn't find myself getting bored of it at all. That being said I've only played about an hour and a half so far.<br><br><b>Gameplay<\/b><br><br>The gameplay consists of nothing other than a big, epic boss battle. I use the word epic and I mean it. It's challenging - hard as my \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665, in fact - and leaves you seething just enough to be \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665 at Lamia for kicking your \u2665\u2665\u2665 time and time again but not enough to make you rage quit. In fact, I found myself being driven to win. You have to manage many things at once. I recommend starting out playing the &quot;Turn&quot; based mode before trying the &quot;holy\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665ing\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665twtf&quot; mode.<br><br><b>cough cough<\/b> Oh, sorry! I meant &quot;Active&quot; mode. In active mode you have to know what you're doing, how to do it, and when to do it and you have to do it <b>fast!<\/b> If you don't you're simply a sitting duck until you make up your mind. So plot out a strategy in turn based mode and then try it out in Active mode after that. If it fails miserably, don't do it again. Simple concept, difficult execution.<br><br>While I enjoyed the gameplay I still have no idea what half the spells, specials, and items even do exactly or what some status effects mean. What would have been nice is some sort of brief tutorial or a manual menu to look this \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665 up. Sometimes it's fun to figure out what something does, but sometimes you can't. Cut out the middle man and just let us know. I look forward to some kind of update regarding this though I don't expect one since the game is completely free.<br><br>It's relatively short, depending on your skill at this sort of thing plus your reaction time and how fast you win. I can see myself giving this at least a few more hours of play time and when you get the game completely free with no catches, hooks, or microtransactions - not to mention the game takes all of 2
