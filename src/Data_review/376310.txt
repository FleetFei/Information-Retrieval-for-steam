Yes, this game feels old and even for standards in 2008 it would've been antiquated. However, I used to play literal text mmorpgs and I don't have any issues with it feeling old. Not everything has to be an action mmorpg these days. Eventhough you can tell that Otherland MMO tries to appeal to that audience.<br><br>Anyway, having gotten that out of the way, the main reason I even have tried this is because as of right now, it stands at 5% positive reviews. The main issues of people are that it feels old, clunky and weapons not damaging enemies instantly (Projectiles have a travel time etc). Not a huge deal to me.<br><br>I have to slice up my opinion into two different parts for the game: the tutorial\/prologue and the game outside of that. Since it feels like those are entirely different. I was playing the Equalizer class or whatever it is called.<br><br><br><br><br><b><u>The Tutorial\/Prologue<\/u><\/b><br><br>The assets for the prologue are all over the place: You have somewhat decent looking things and then completely muddy objects. They also heavily reuse single models with the same pose over and over. Because the quality is all over the place, it feels like total garbage.<br><br>Biggest critique of the prologue though? The design of it. It tries to teach you the advantages of your 3 different weapon types, but ultimatively it feels like it's artifically padding the gameplay. You have enemies that respawn in a matter of 2-3 seconds, while you require 15-20 seconds to kill one of them. Not only that, but they also respawn around some NPCs and quest objects which only adds ontop of the frustration. All you have to do is hold the left mouse button and wait those 20 seconds for mobs to die. You don't gain any exp for them or at best you get 1. <br>I came out with around 170 mobs killed.<br><br>As if the Prologue wasn't already a huge \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665 move to the player already in itself, there's a big one waiting at the end of it. In order to escape, you have to enter a portal - which needs to be used in order to progress. Now you might be saying that this isn't a big deal, but it's on a narrow platform that is completely littered with enemies. Around 20 I'd say. You can't use this Portal on your own, you have to wait for players to team up with you who tank all the mobs in order for you to use it, or do it like me and just die infront of it, then mashing F to use it while you're reviving on the spot.<br><br>Never ever put hard mobs into your prologue that are aggressive and especially not a ton of them next to objectives or npcs that are required to progress. The only way this whole scenario could even work, is if you have a lot of players rushing through the prologue but I have a feeling most of them just quit before they even get there. I can't write enough how that whole setup is a huge '\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665 you' to the player and I don't understand why it's in the game.<br><br>I think the only merit the whole thing has is that it tries to tell a story through 3D stills, it's an interresting concept of story telling and points to them if it persists outside of the Prologue. I doubt it though. The whole stuff about NPCs being there and a ton of mobs respawning ontop of them, are a complete disservice to your game. Let the player walk through that in their own pace without the persistent \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665 mob comming from the corner of the map to slap you while you talk with an npc.<br><br><br><br><br><b><u>Post-Prologue\/The Real Game<\/u><\/b><br><br>The Prologue actually had me right on the verge of quitting, but right as I got out it (and thrown into the prison) it changed for the better. It teached me the usage of my AMPs in a fun manner and how to interact with things, it was fun because it didn't last a \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665 hour like the Prologue does and not having to kill dumb damagesponges helps a lot. You can already tell that the game feels entirely different than it did and right as you hit Lambda Mall it kinda makes you want to explore more. It's aesthetically pretty and the assets all seem consistent now. I even was tempted to almost just crank it up to 4K and make screenshots. There's even themed bars, such as one that has flying saucers with cows inside their tractor beams. Quite a lot of neon, it really suits the world. You get to hang around a little and get an introduction into customizing your own player housing. It's a decent, lively hub area, albeit devoid of any real players because I don't think any of the 8 people I started out with didn't manage to get through the prologue.<br><br>Quickly you get sent to your first island, which is thematically also pretty and nothing like the prologue. There's some annoyance with the collision detection inside caves, though. Quests are your average run of the mill mmorpg quests and you will have to grind a lot I feel. Also be prepared to be teached the same things you've learned in the Prologue again.<br><br>There'S also some quirks with the enemy AI that you don't need in other mmorpgs. It made me question whenever this is a bug or if it's there to actually promote switching your weapon (They have advantages and disadvantages depending on the situation)<br><br>I don't have much complaints beyond that and it certainly is a hell of a difference beyond the Prologue.  But I can definitely see that this doesn't appeal to many people and you really need a lot of tolerance to go through that prologue. The UI isn't very streamlined, but it's also very reminiscent of older stylized cyberpunk mmorpgs so I got used to it. Sometimes there's also no background music or sounds missing and as many people know, sound, feedback and music make up a huge portion of a game (Try playing Overwatch with music to off). Lack of feedback (Either visual or an audio cue) is also something I feel that leads to a lot of misconceptions and frustration.<br><br><br><br><br>Generally I think the game is playable just fine if you get beyond that point - if you're into this kind of theme and gameplay. However, I personally wouldn't continue because Anarchy Online would serve as a better cyberpunk substitute eventhough the aesthetics of Lambda Mall and beyond that are very very tempting. As far I remember, Otherland MMO was announced as a sandbox in 2008 eg. No character levels, open world, all sorts of skills. The way SWG, EQ, UO etc used to be. But now it seems to be just your typical mmorpg experience.
