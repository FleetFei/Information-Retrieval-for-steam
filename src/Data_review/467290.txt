If you are a Douglas Adams fan, or a Monty Python fan, and especially if you enjoyed the Hitchhiker's Guide text-based adventure game, you should certainly consider picking this up. If you're not a fan of that sort of humor, you probably won't enjoy Starship Titanic very much. <br><br>It really says something about a game when, nearly 20 years after its release, it still manages to do something that seems impressively creative by modern gaming standards. I won't spoil it, but there is a puzzle towards the end of the game that does something that actually made me say 'oh, wow' out loud. That being said, most of the other puzzles in the game range from 'decent' to 'practically unsolvable'. <br><br>If you're familiar with the text-based Hitchhiker's Guide Adventure Game (also written by Douglas Adams), you probably know that many of the puzzles in that game (particularly the infamous Babel Fish) are nearly impossible without a walkthrough. In a good environmental puzzle, the player should be able to logically determine the sequence of actions that must be taken to complete the puzzle. The Hitchhiker's Adventure Game is so completely illogical that one almost wonders how anyone ever managed to solve it at all.<br><br>Unfortunately, Starship Titanic makes the same mistakes. A walkthrough is essentially necessary to complete the game. (Later reprints of the game even included a walkthrough that was originally published on GameFAQs; it is very useful, and can be found easily on Google. This review originally contained a direct link to the walkthrough, but Steam didn't seem to like that.)<br><br>Despite all that, Starship Titanic has a lot to enjoy. The graphics are very nice for 1998, and the designs of the ship and robots are lovely to look at, despite the game's age. I particularly like the DeskBot, who folds out from a bell. The robots all have very distinct personalities, and are very fun to talk to. The voice acting is hilarious, even featuring appearances from Terry Jones and John Cleese. The story is what makes the game worth playing, and it is very, very funny. <br><br>The game creates an excellent atmosphere that feels very similar to the Hitchhiker's books. The ship is enjoyable to explore, especially after upgrading above SGT class. I almost found myself wanting to stay longer after completing the game. <br><br>Overall, the story, characters, and atmosphere are what make this game memorable and worth playing. If you can overlook the poor puzzle design, and appreciate Adams' humor style, this is definitely a game to check out. Even if you're not familiar with Douglas Adams' other work, consider checking this out if you like good old adventure games and fun stories.<br><br>Note that you will need a pair of anaglyph (red\/blue) 3D glasses to complete the game.
