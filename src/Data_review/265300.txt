Open-Minded Admirers of Action RPGs <\/div><br>I think the main problem people have with this game is they compare it a little too much to and hold it up to the standards of multimillion masterpieces like those that From Software makes. Sure, this game has similar systems to Dark Souls and may remind you of it from time to time, but this is its own game and should be reviewed as such, not how it holds up to the game of another developer. <br><br>On its own, the game is moderately entertaining in how it approaches the combat system (which does feel very different than that of a Souls game), but in the end suffers to its lack of enemy variety and formulaic liniarity. After all, I was a little dissapointed to see that the first 2 bosses were guys with shields and swords, a standard knight archetype, and how you will see the same 5-6 enemy variants most of the time in the opening hours. This may or may not improve in the late game, and ill push through to the end to find out, but the goal of a game should be to grab the player's attention early on and maintain that interest, building on its systems and mechanics. Lords of the Fallen fails in this aspect. <br><br>Some commendable early-game attractions would be the combat and loot systems. The combat feels much more Kingdoms of Amalur: Reckoning esque than Dark Souls, be that good or bad is up to you. It feels slightly more fast paced and less methodical than Dark Souls too, giving the impression that this game is a little less about careful life or death combat choices than its counterpart. Though don't take this the wrong way, Lords of the Fallen is still a difficult game and at times, especially during boss fights, you will feel closer to the Dark Souls style of calculated blows and dodges. The loot early on feels a little like a Diablo game. There is plenty of loot (armor, weapons, such) in the first couple levels allowing great customizability in playstyle, especially when it comes to the weapon variants and the variety they give to your approach to combat.<br><br><b>Over all, do I reccommend this game? For the price of $19.99,<u> I think any open-minded admirers of Dark Souls or other difficulty based Action RPGs will enjoy this game<\/u>. Idealy, find it for a deal, but the asking price is fair. At the very least, it's a good play so that you may have an example of how Action-RPGs can have shortcomings and how to avoid them. I respect From Software so so much more now and appreciate their arts now that I see how much work it had to be for them. <u>I look forward to a sequel, where maybe they can iron out any flaws in the
