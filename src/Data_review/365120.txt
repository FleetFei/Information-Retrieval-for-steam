Now in The Curse of the Crescent Isle DX, the graphics have been vamped up &amp; players can choose between playing as the queen or the king &amp; either venture into the perilous kingdom by themselves or with a friend via local co-op. What\u2019s even better is that this indie game can not only be played with a keyboard, but also automatically recognizes one\u2019s controller without issues. However, co-op buddies should keep in mind that the camera follows player 1 &amp; doesn't prevent player 2 from wandering off-screen. The twist this time around is that, after the introduction stage, the whimsy monarchs can use the monsters\u2019 abilities by holding on to them.<br><br>Since most of the monsters, save for the boss enemies, are citizens of the Crescent Isle, throwing them against one another won\u2019t kill, but merely stun them. The bosses, however take just the right amount of analytical thought to figure out how to defeat them. I really miss having to think in video games.<br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Bugs<\/div>Since Curse DX is an independent production made by a small team &amp; with all programming done by one man, Adam Mowery, it is only natural to have some charming flaws. I played this on a 32-bit, Windows Vista Home Premium, Intel Core 2 CPU, Nvidia GeForce 9500 GT, 3070 MB RAM, DirectX 11 &amp; had an overall great experience with some minor issues here &amp; there, but generally nothing that I hadn\u2019t seen on old Nintendo machines either.<br><br>When carrying the icicle shaped enemy on one\u2019s head &amp; falling into water, players should be pushed above the water surface, but it did happen twice that the game thought that I was still underwater, despite standing on ice, which caused the character to die from drowning. During one occasion, I threw a stalactite shaped enemy at a bunch of rocks, &amp; upon its return, it catapulted me back into the ground at the beginning of the stage, which also caused the character to die. Then, one time, I encountered an amusing error. During co-op play, both players were playing as the king, when instead one should be the king, the other the queen. Finally, I suggest playing in the game in fullscreen, as I had some screen tearing &amp; missing frames in windowed mode. Despite these smaller bugs, I had a great time playing.<br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=818204080\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_2\">http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=818204080<\/a><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">A minor Dent<\/div>The entire game is child friendly, cutesy, &amp; if your little ones are in grade school The Curse of the Crescent Isle DX will be just the right balance between tricky\/challenging, fun, &amp; length. In short: They\u2019ll have a great time with this game. Still, as Curse comes with achievements, there is one thing that puts a minor dent into things, at least for me. The \u201cGive it Up \u2013 Find one of the special messages in the password screen\u201d requires players to enter a four letter expletive on the screen to acquire it. Now, I don\u2019t mind cussing in video games, but it is still a minor nuisance that an otherwise family oriented game requires one to do this to get an achievement.<br> <div class=\"bb_h1\">Conclusion<\/div>The Curse of the Crescent Isle DX is a refreshing sight in the indie game market. It is still a bit unpolished around the edges, but wow, it\u2019s a great game. Just a few tweaks &amp; it\u2019d be perfect. I am 100% certain that, save for that minor dent with the cussword &amp; the occasional bug, families &amp; jump\u2019n\u2019run enthusiasts throughout will enjoy this game.
