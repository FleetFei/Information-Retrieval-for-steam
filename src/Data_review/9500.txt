Pretty standard fare.  The twist is, you have no legs.  Or arms.  Or\u2026 anything except eyes, a mouth, and a body.  You\u2019re a 12lb ball of tar.<br><br>Gish\u2019s lady friend Brea has been kidnapped and taken into the sewers, and it\u2019s up to you to save her.  Fight your way through five worlds and reclaim your love.  Now, to be honest, this title has not aged well aesthetically.  The graphics are mediocre at best, and the audio is not very memorable.  The five worlds are made distinct by little more than sprite-swapped square bricks with new lighting colors.  There is a charming bit when you open the game  where the loading screen shows a poster of a pop culture icon with the main star replaced by Gish.  The Gishorcist, Gishheart, Gish Fiction.  But the real draw here is the unique and simple-but-deep gameplay, which still shines through today, making this a game worth a look.<br><br>Gish can jump, but it\u2019s a pretty ineffectual maneuver.  To reach a ledge, you\u2019ll need to gain momentum by jumping, compressing him down, and then releasing all that energy into a jump again.  Gish can also modify his body in a few important ways.  You can slide through narrow passages, increase your weight in order to crush enemies or obstacles, or make yourself sticky to cling to objects or surfaces (even ceilings).  The whole game is based around learning these different modifiers and applying them through each (relatively short) level.  This is a deep physics-based platformer, and you\u2019ll develop a working understanding of momentum, trajectory, and crushing things with a tar ball.  Pushing a box onto a pressure switch is a much different experience when you can\u2019t pick it up with hands.  You\u2019ll need to stick yourself to it and propel it, or make yourself rigid and heavy to push it along.<br><br>Some of the levels can get extremely frustrating, and a single mistake can cost you quite a bit of time spent retreading platforming sections.  Some jumps have very little margin for error, and the unique controls take a while to get used to.  This leads to situations where you know exactly what you need to do, but getting Gish to actually accomplish that in the physics engine can be difficult.  Especially frustrating is when objects like catapults or swings, which you need to use to progress, end up killing you randomly and draining you of precious lives.  A few bugs like this are irritating, but not game-breaking.<br><br>Besides the main quest, there\u2019s also a \u201cCollection\u201d mode and a Versus mode.  Collection mode tasks you with collecting all the amber pieces on a level within a certain time limit, and can be a fun challenge, and a great way to learn the systems at play before diving into the main story.  Versus mode is exactly what it sounds like.  You and up to 3 other friends compete to kill each other using different tar balls.  A fun little diversion, but not much else.  For as long as the game has been out, it seems the Steam achievements haven\u2019t worked, but it appears that recently they have been fixed!  Finally, validation from Steam of my success in this game!  They\u2019re still really difficult to get, however.<br><br>While the game shows its age, and there are a few irritating bugs present in the systems, this is a title worth checking out for the sheer novelty of its control scheme.
