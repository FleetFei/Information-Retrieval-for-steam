In the first two games, there was frankly too much random-generated BS that would kill you when it's not even your fault. In FNAF4, you have to rely entirely on learning sound cues and knowing where to go at the right time. You have to adapt to a new kind of game. You check outside your door and see something you've never seen before, and immediately die. Night 5 changes, even while you play. You're constantly learning new things and adapting to them. Not to even get started on Night 6 (which I beat) and Nightmare (which I did not). Scott receives a lot of hate for putting out the games too fast, but that's just how he is. He works exceptionally hard, and he outdoes himself. He makes good games. Overall, it's a good game. The story to be uncovered is intriguing, and the gameplay has nearly been perfected. It's a horror game, but one you can have fun and enjoy yourself with.
