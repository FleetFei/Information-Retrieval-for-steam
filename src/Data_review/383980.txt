late, I only picked up the game in November, but watching Rivals grow has been an awesome experience. Dan Fornace, the game's creator, and the other devs are all supremely focused on creating a well balanced, competitive, fun fighting game. All of the characters are just as viable as one another in competitive play, all of the stages are designed with competitive play in mind, and yet it's still just so visceral and fun! Pulling off a sick combo, or landing an awesome set up, or hitting that optimal punish all feel so awesome. The game gives you nothing for free, but that means that you feel great once you've managed to actually land something.<br><br>The gameplay feels a lot like Super Smash Bros Melee, if you're familiar with that game, but with enough unique stuff in there that it doesn't come off as just another smash clone (even if we call it Furry Trash Smash). No ledges, grabs, or shields here, instead they've been replaced by wall jumps and parries, a new mechanic where you can block your opponent's moves and leave them in a state of hitstun for a brief moment, allowing you to follow up and punish them with (almost) whatever you want. The timing is tight though, you've got 2 frames to work with, and leaves you with a significant amount of endlag if you mistime the parry, so you can't just throw them out whenever. The game is very fast paced and combo-centric, with movement and spacing being vital for competitive play.<br><br>But the really interesting part are the characters. The roster currently holds 8 characters as of writing this review, with at least 6 more promised for the future (2 guest characters and 1 more for each of the 4 elements), and each of those 8 feel incredibly different from one another, and each have their own mechanics that interact with the stage in unique ways. From being able to create a destroyable pillar underneath you, to covering the stage in ice, to obscuring the screen in smoke, every character has a unique way to interact with the stage itself. But that's not the best part, the care and design that has gone into each and every character shows. While certain matchups may tend to favor one character over another, it's never more than a 60\/40 chance at worst, and no character significantly outshines any other. High level competitive play regularly sees almost the entire cast, so who you play just comes down to personal preference rather than being forced to &quot;play the meta.&quot;<br><br>The community around this game is wonderful as well, with several major Discord channels, including the &quot;Rivals Academy,&quot; a Discord created with the sole purpose of pairing new players with more experienced Mentors (including some top-level players) to help them learn, a hugely active subreddit (\/r\/RivalsofAether), and almost daily online tournaments, you can almost always find someone to play with. The community has been nothing but helpful and friendly the entire time I've been playing. The general attitude of the Rivals community is that we're all trying to get good at a competitive fighting game, so why not help each other out.<br><br>Finally, it's a small note, but the amount of polish and awesome small features in this game is just outstanding. Everything oozes character, from the menu music to the character select screen. Here are just a few small details that I really appreciate as a player:<br><br>- Being able to create a profile, set custom controls, and playtest those controls, all without having to leave the character select screen<br>- Having a music toggle on the stage select screen<br>- Stage banning in the stage select screen<br>- CUSTOM COLORS (no seriously, it's a lot more fun than you think)<br><br>If you are interested in picking up this game, I highly recommend it! I came from having only a minimal amount of experience playing a platform fighter, I wouldn't even call myself an amateur at Melee, but was able to pick up the game enough to start participating in online tournaments after only a couple of months (not saying I was doing well, but I wasn't losing out round 1 every time). A few of my friends who I recommended this game to came from Melee, Smash 4, and PM and all still loved this game. The barrier to entry is not high (the game even has tutorials to teach you pretty much everything you need to know to play the game), even if the skill ceiling is. Basically, if you're interested in a competitive platform fighting game with a fantastic community and devs that actually care about delivering a quality product, then you can't go wrong with picking
