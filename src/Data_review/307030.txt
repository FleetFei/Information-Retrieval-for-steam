on the 3DS, I jumped at the chance to own this masterpiece on Steam. As with most things of great value, it takes a little time to fully appreciate Fractured Soul. Initially, the gameplay, while razor sharp, will confound most players. The ability to navigate the levels over both screens is one which will take time to grow, but when it does, playing Fractured Soul is an absolute joy. The innovation behind the dual-screen mechanic is anything but a gimmick. With time, one will realize how carefully and intuitively the levels have been put together. While it may not take players much time to complete the earlier stages (particularly on the more forgiving &quot;normal&quot; difficulty setting), Endgame have done a wonderful job in steadily introducing additional mechanics. Just as you begin to feel comfortable with the shifting mechanic, you will have to deal with, amongst others, strong wind, inverse gravity and heat damage.<br><br>Visually, Fractured Soul looks great (the PC version has a smooth 60FPS), and if you are able to take your eyes off the action, you will appreciate some surprisingly varied and beautiful backdrops. While I very much like the aesthetic, and the story is nicely told through text between the levels (fleshed out from the brief text of the 3DS version), Fractured Soul is very much about its gameplay. The game actually develops a very specific skill set, taking the platforming genre, as trite as it sounds, to a new level. There are really two ways in which you can play this game through to the end. The first is by adequately mastering the shifting mechanic in such a way as to simply complete the levels, using as much health and time as necessary. This will probably be enough for the casual gamer, and will still provide a stiff challenge. The second way is by playing the game to the point where you have intuituvely mastered the shifting mechanic and &quot;understand&quot; the levels, and are thereby able to comple them within the par time, picking up all bonus items, without taking damage. To my mind, this is how the game is meant to be played. This is extremely challenging, but because the game is not &quot;cheap&quot; and the levels are built entirely around the effective harnessing of Entity's shifting ability, a combination of attention, care and persistence will eventually pay off. Fractured Soul is a game which at heart is built around the idea of speed running, and achieving five stars, particularly in later levels, is a truly rewarding sensation. I have recently managed to five star all thirty-one levels on the 3DS, and am looking forward to doing the same on this version.<br><br>Fractured Soul is a gem of a platformer. Obviously made with passion, it espouses real creativity and intuition without even a hint of pretension. Particularly with the low introductory price, it is a very easy recommendation, but even at full-price it is money well spent. For those who enjoyed the 3DS title as much as I did, it should be a no-brainer. The technical improvements are clear, and through over 100 brilliantly-conceived achievements you will further hone your capabilities and draw even more from this work of art. Fans of tough and true platformers should lose hours on this, and appreciate every second.<br><br>EDIT: I will echo another review that in order to appear on Fractured Soul's leaderboards, you must
