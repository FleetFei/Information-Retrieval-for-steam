The political background might bother some, but if you aren't one of them, you're going to enjoy the sandbox experience. One of the most defining aspects of the game is it's &quot;oldschool&quot; difficulty, even on the easiest difficulty I found myself confronting unbeatable enemies frequently. <br><br>In addition to focusing more on the RPG side of the game (vs Heroes of Might and Magic) Blood of November also has a striped down economy, you only have one resource (gold) and everything do you has that goal in mind. Equipment costs gold, healing your army costs gold, hiring new recruits costs gold, capturing buildings and castles gives you gold, etc. You get the point, far fewer resources to consider when coming up with a strategy. <br><br>But that's mostly just Eisenwald in general, what's different about Blood of November? To be fair, not much, the original game has a much more engaging narrative, Blood of November banks heavily on it's theme to shift units, it's a laugh at the U.S. 2016 presidential election but that veneer is very thin. It's mostly an excuse to choose a side and fight, sure you'll find some comical references in Taverns to some of the inane stuff the presidential candidates said during the race but it barely touches the gameplay. If you are worried about being bombarded by political propaganda, don't worry, this game really doesn't. <br><br>So that kinda comes down to 'do I recommend it'? With reservations, yes. I think it's a competent product for the cost and you'll get at least a dozen or so hours out of it if you don't cheat and use console commands but the original game is still superior. Sure it costs more and is a year older, but you get a much better thought-out campaign. If you ever wanted to take U.S. political sides though in a strategy game this is probably one of the few games that natively allows you to do that. <br><br>To hear more of my thoughts on the game,
