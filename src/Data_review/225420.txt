Save before any renovation projects.<br> - Some of the randomized tasks are either worded very unclearly or programmed in a buggy way. With all your vehicles coming from and returning to depots it feels a lot more realistic.<br> - Individual and monthly ticket pricing, transit zones, etc. lets you control rider customer experience to an unparallelled degree. You can tailor your strategy to the situation seamlessly.<br> - The ability to build your own roads and tramways is *not* to be underestimated. You can bypass high-traffic areas entirely by building a detour.<br> - Campaigns with a more free-form structure than the original. You can very much choose your own path to success.<br> - Game mechanics can be adjusted freely, tailoring the experience to what you want.  But it only works for sandbox and custom games, not the built-in campaigns.<br> - That same feeling of almost zen-like relaxation you can get from the first game is definitely present here. <br><br>Conclusion: More to wrap your head around than the first game. This one definitely demands more from your mind and your hardware, but it does start to pay dividends eventually.
