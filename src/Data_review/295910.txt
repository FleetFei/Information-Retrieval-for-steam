The game takes place in Ancient Greece where you play as Iola, a heroine who was killed, but has now been reawakened in the underworld, and she doesn\u2019t know why. You have to make your way through the underworld in order to get to Mount Olympus and find out why you were revived from Zeus himself. One of the most prominent features of Olympia Rising is the ability to chain kills together in to multiply the amount of coins you receive. Not only does this give you more coins, but it also resets your double jump.<br><br>This makes sections where you have to climb become a whole lot easier and a lot of fun as you chain enemy kills together to continue moving upwards. This makes descending a hazard as you can run into a lot of enemies suddenly.<br><br>Your life\u2019s represented with hearts, a la Zelda, and you also have a magic meter which can be used for different powerups or to give yourself additional jumps, which becomes critical if you want to keep your kill multiplier going. All the powerups are useful for different things; fire magic lets you hit enemies from far away, the water shield makes you invulnerable to projectiles, and the lightning blade lets you easily defeat enemies that take multiple hits. They all serve their own functions and no power-up really feels useless, although you do need certain powerups more than others in specific areas.<br><br>The game divides its levels into 3 different types of areas: There are the acid levels, where you must progress upward as fast as possible in order to avoid the acid that fills up from the bottom of the level. This is where killing enemies to reset your jump comes in handy, as you can use them as kind of stepping stones to move yourself upwards. There\u2019s then also what I like to call exploration\/coin collecting levels, where you have to collect a large amount of coins in order to progress and there are a ton of enemies and treasure drops to pick up. These rooms seem to always have enough coins to progress whether you chain kills or not, so retaining your life becomes crucial.<br><br>Even if you do die, you start back at the beginning of the current area \u2013 you don\u2019t have to worry about lives and can just have fun, which is one of the games strengths. It\u2019s easy enough that anyone can enjoy it, but difficult enough that it feels like a challenge.
