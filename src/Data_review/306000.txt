360 controller\/gamepad).<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Things I enjoy about this game so far<\/div>+ Simple to pick-up-&amp;-play.<br>+ Good visuals, audio, controls, action, atmosphere, &amp; functional quality\/stability for the price.<br>+ Vibrant comical fantasy art design.<br>+ Well-designed user interface.<br>+ The overall &quot;fun factor&quot; is there for me (though I know &quot;fun factor&quot; is very subjective).<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Things I find a bit frustrating as a new player<\/div>- Hordes of many types of enemies (close-range\/melee, long-range\/shooters, traps, etc.) often cover the screen, leaving you no room to safely maneuver and unable to attack\/escape without taking lots of damage (and\/or dying).<br>- When you melee (sword\/hammer) attack, you stand still momentarily, which leaves you vulnerable to the legions of slashing enemies, flying arrows\/beams, &amp; traps all simultaneously coming at you from every direction.<br>- I feel like there's a slight delay (extra\/long frame data?) between each distinct player action (attack, jump, grab-&amp;-throw, dodge\/roll, move, etc.) that makes combat less fluid than I wish it was. This brief delay continually leaves me vulnerable to more enemy attacks. A more rapid flow\/transition between my actions could really help deal with enemy mobs better.<br>- The game spams big mobs of enemies, but players have no effective &quot;spamable&quot; attacks or moves.<br>- The rate that summoners spawn new minions is too fast. After dispatching a round of summoned minions, there's no time to attack &amp; eliminate the summoner because he instantly spawns more minions infinitely. This makes an area with multiple summoners virtually impossible to pass (for me). After eliminating summoned minions, there should be a delay before the summoner can spawn more minions.<br>- Despite the twin-stick-shooter-style controls &amp; mechanics, strafing (moving one direction while aiming &amp; attacking in another direction) is significantly slower than normal non-strafing movement.<br>- Some enemies appear to have a damaging aura around them, and the sword's &amp; hammer's ranges appear to be too short to attack those enemies without taking damage yourself (or maybe I'm just going about it wrong).<br>- Ranged enemies can attack you from upstairs\/downstairs, but your ranged attacks cannot reach enemies that are upstairs\/downstairs.<br>- The initial crossbow's small ammo capacity, weak damage, &amp; eternal reload time makes the weapon woefully inadequate. It constantly leaves you helplessly running from mobs waiting to reload.<br>- Achievements don't seem to pop until you close\/exit the game.<br><br>-- The best way I can think of to try to describe what I think is the biggest fundamental problem with much of the combat in this game is what seems to me like (in fighting game parlance) unfavorable frame data... For example: you swing your weapon to attack an enemy &amp; (hypothetically) say it takes 30 frames for your attack animation to complete; you are locked into that entire 30 frames of attack animation &amp; unable to do anything else until after it completes entirely; but a few frames after you've initiated your attack &amp; before your attack connects with your target, the enemy you're attacking (or multiple other enemies in the screen-spanning mob) takes a shot at you with their own attack that (hypothetically) takes 20 frames to complete (10 frames faster than your own attack); you cannot avoid taking that damage because you're still locked (&amp; can't even move) in your own attack animation while you're being attacked. This puts you at a vulnerable disadvantage every time you attack, especially heavy attacks, and\/or attacking with hammers, and\/or when dealing with hordes of enemies. I would love to see this re-balanced somehow, either by speeding up (reducing) the player's attack frames, or maybe by giving players some way to cancel out of their attacks before they complete (perhaps with the dodge\/roll move), or maybe by some way of having player attacks interrupt\/cancel enemy attacks, or maybe even by giving the player some invulnerability frames while attacking, or by some other better solution.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">My wish list for improvements<\/div>\u2022 Faster flow\/transition between player actions (shorten\/decrease frames\/delay between actions).<br>\u2022 Faster strafing<br>\u2022 Ability to repeatedly dodge\/roll more before waiting (dizzy) to recharge the dodge\/roll ability, along with faster recharge of dodge\/roll ability.<br>\u2022 Instead of finger-cramping button mashing, let players hold down an attack button to automatically swing melee (sword\/hammer) attacks repeatedly.<br>\u2022 Ability to attack while jumping.<br>\u2022 Free pick-ups to recharge your ultimate attack (or auto-regenerating ultimate charge).<br>\u2022 More health pick-ups (or more frequently\/easily-obtained health\/armor power-ups\/buffs, or auto-regenerating health).<br>\u2022 More noticeable indicators when the player takes damage (the first couple times I died, I didn't even realize I had been taking hits).<br>\u2022 Longer &quot;magnetic&quot; reach\/distance to pull in coins\/pick-ups.<br>\u2022 Access to game options from pause menu while playing.<br>\u2022 Built-in configurable frame-rate cap\/limiter option.<br>\u2022 Built-in support for borderless full-screen window option. (I have not yet had any issues with Alt+Enter to switch between full-screen &amp; windowed, nor any issues yet with Alt+Tab to switch back-&amp;-forth between the game &amp; other apps.)<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Summary<\/div>So far, Super Dungeon Bros has definitely been fun enough to make me want to keep playing more. The game delivers on its claims, and it works without crashing\/freezing or suffering from major game-breaking defects (for me, so far). I've tried playing public\/online co-op, but I guess there aren't enough players yet (so soon after launch), as the game doesn't match me with anyone after several minutes of waiting in a lobby.<br><br>As it is (at launch), I think Super Dungeon Bros is a reasonably well-made &amp; decently fun little indie game... but if some simple improvements to some of the game's basic mechanics were to be updated\/patched in (like some of the items I previously mentioned above), I think this game could explode into something spectacularly addictive. :)<br><br>Of course, this is all just my opinion based on my own preferences. Others may have different opinions &amp; preferences.<br><br>(I may edit\/update this review later as I progress further in the game and\/or as the game evolves.)<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Quick beginner's tips<\/div>\u2022 Jump...
