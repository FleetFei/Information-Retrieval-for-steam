frustating but extremely addictive shoot em up with rogue like elements. <br><br>The game is a shoot em up in the conventional sense. You control a character riding a lollipop like a magic carpet?! Your only control over this character is moving up and down using the mouse and holding down the mouse button to shoot. Other than that the character moves across the screen at a consistent rate on it's own. <br><br>What makes Dandy so fun as compared to other shoot em ups? Well the enemy variety is probably the best part. The enemy variety is large enough without being too large. New enemies are introduced periodically as you progress through the level. Each enemy has their own unique style of attacking that you have to discover and try and figure out the best way to attack them. This implements are certain amount of strategy, especially when you have multiple different enemy types on the screen. Some times you are swarmed with a certain enemy type but there is a single of one enemy type that is hidden behind the other enemies that is the hardest to deal with. It makes strategic planning something that is necessary to make sure you can get through the level loosing as little health as possible. <br><br>It is not a rogue like but it does have many of the common elements that are present in the popular rogue like genre. Some people sometimes assume a game is a rogue like just because it has permadeath. Dandy has permadeath but also has many    other elements of a rogue like. The item pickup system is one example of this. At periodic stages throughout the level you reach a place where you can choose between three different powerups. All you see of these powerups are a picture. One powerup everytime is always a health pickup, the rest are random. When picking them up you get a brief, but cryptic description as to what the items do. You are really just left to yourself to figure out what the item does. There is a large enough variety of powerups that after a time you can learn what they all do and plan which you are going to pickup accordingly. <br><br>Powerups are very varied as to what they do. They always have interesting effects that make a notable difference to how well you can fight the enemies. These include things like larger bullets, a charge shot, shield and double shot. One interesting thing with picking up a powerup is that it styles your character. They can put very ammusing effects on your character and it really does add to the experience. Powerup effects stack on top of each other and so you can come up with interesting combinations. <br><br>The graphics style of the game is unique and cartoon like. The soundtrack really adds to this and provides a relaxing tune for you to shoot and kill your enemies to. The game is very styilized and really knows what it wants to go for making sure everything follows this initial style. <br><br>Each run is never the same. Or at least thats what they advertise. After playing the game through a lot of times I have to say that this wasn't quite the case. It's procedurely generated to an extent. There were a lot of times were I met the same combination of enemies and it was very noticable about the lack of different combinations. But this isn't necessarily a bad thing. At least I found that I could learn how to best deal with these combinations to progress more easily. <br><br>The majority of this experience I really enjoyed but there were just a few things that put me off a bit. The first being at the beginning of the level every time. There is what I believe is a mushroom at the front of the terrain. So many times an enemy seemed to appear from behind this out of nowhere and hit me without me having time to react. It isn't cool to hide enemies like that. Luckily this was the only occurance of that I found throughout the game though. Secondly is the options menu. There are resolution and audio sliders but nothing else. No other adjustments could be made to the game. So this was severely lacking. Luckily these negatives were only minor factors and didn't impact my experience too much. <br><br>Overall I greatly enjoyed this game even if at some points I felt like throwing my mouse at my screen! I will definitely be back for more and I will beat this game eventually, I promise! It is very reasonable I believe at a price point of \u00a36.99.
