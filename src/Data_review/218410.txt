You can more-or-less ignore it if you don't care, but if you take the time to read the journal entries, you'll find that the world and characters are very fleshed out. A lot of the characters and situations are very tongue-in-cheek, which relieves some of the tension a game like this can inspire. :)<br><br>+ Level design constantly pushes  you out of your comfort zone. You often find that you can't quite use units in the way that you'd want to because of the limitations on the terrain. Things don't get as monotonous as a result of this.<br><br>+ For a tower defense game, it's pretty long. If you're a veteran of the genre and can breeze through tough fights with effortless tactical accumen, you'll find that getting maximum stars on each level takes about 10-15+ hours.<br><br>+ New Game Plus (NG+) adds sidequests that aren't available in the normal game, which includes new story and cutscenes. The behind-the-scenes journal entries are also really interesting.<br><br>+ Hero Mode makes for an even more challenging game (you can only use the 6 story characters).<br><br>+ Lots of customization for loadouts, units, etc. Branching skill trees make the customization feel a little more fleshed out, although some options are clearly better than others.<br><br>+ The DX version of the game vastly improves the graphics. The biggest mark against the original game was that it was in SD and the art looked very amateur. With this update, the game supports HD\/Widescreen resolutions, and all sprites\/cutscenes look 1000% better.<br><br>+ You have the option of increasing the amount of XP and Scrap (money) you get after each battle by up to 300%. Scrap isn't all that useful, but XP is mana from heaven. This is great, because if you left it at 100%, it'd take a metric \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665ton of grinding to get to the end of the game. See cons for reasons why even this feature doesn't save NG+ from grindiness, unfortunately.<br><br>- In the normal game, Rangers are by far the best unit. You can win almost every fight by just using them, even on Expert. It seems that the developers were very aware of this, which is why --<br><br>- In NG+, the Ranger (the only <i>real<\/i> ranged unit in the game) is the subject of immense nerfs. Many enemies that appear in large numbers and have high speed either have 50% resistance to ranged damage, or 60-70% ranged <b>evasion<\/b>. These are the kinds of enemies you'd want an archer for because they can slip past your melee, but your archers can't hit them. This really hurts the unit's viability in a lot of fights. I feel like the challenge could have been increased without this frustrating change. There are some enemies that the Ranger is still really good at dealing with, but mostly you'll be relying on melee in Expert modes.<br><br>- Most of the added difficulty in NG+ is artificial. Inflated HP and stats, enemies that phase out so that you can't target them, etc. You're often forced to spam global spells to deal with certain situations because your units simply can't no matter how you have them set up. This mode just isn't very well done in my opinion. It's frustrating and tedious, not fun.<br><br>- The super sheep levels are tedious and not fun at all. You kill a slow moving sheep (that you can slow even more) that has about a bazillion HP and takes optimal tactics and resource management to kill before it reaches the end of the level. It is a very boring, very long fight even with the game sped up to 16x speed, and you have to do it TWICE in order to get all the achievements. If you're a completionist, you'll be subjected to several grindy annoyances, which brings me to the final gripe:<br><br>- If you complete the game in Hero Mode (can't recruit units), once you've finished those achievements (after finishing NG+), you'll want to recruit units because there are some levels that are impossible to get 3 gold stars on with only the main 6 heroes. Unfortunately, the recruited units at that point will be so low level that even setting the XP to 300% is an unbearable grind to get them to 60. Battles take a long time to fight, and the XP from a battle -might- give your units half a level. You can imagine how long this would take. This was originally a community challenge that the developers put into the game later (along with associated achievements), but they didn't really balance the post-achievement gameplay for it. I actually gave up with only a few achievements left to get. Not worth it.<br><br>Overall, if you're interested in a tower defense game that's challenging and satisfying in the base game, and aren't too concerned with unlocking every last achievement, then this game is for you.
