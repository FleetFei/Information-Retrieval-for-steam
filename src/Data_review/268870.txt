for a while, then bought it in a sale and installed it right away, but I did not start it.<br><br>Why, you ask? Because I was afraid to be disappointed.<br>I played the original Syndicate in 1997 as a kid and I loved it to bits. I replayed it so often that I knew every map like the back of my hand. <br><br>Last weekend, I was forced to cancel all my plans because of a flu and so I said to myself, lets find out if the critics are right about this one. <b>And boy oh boy did Satellite Reign deliver!<\/b><br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">REVIEW<\/div><br>The game is indeed best described as a spiritual successor to Syndicate. The world, the lore, the style, the music and the tech is much the same. The gameplay is similar but much refined which I want to elaborate. <br><br><b><u>Gameplay<\/u><\/b><br><br>While many of Syndicate's features can be found in Satellite Reign, their implementation makes for a better gameplay.<br>The biggest difference between the two is that there is no hard cut between the missions and briefing phases. Syndicate sent you on missions but every time you reached your goals you got to the extraction point and found yourself and your agents in a safe, far away briefing overview. In Satellite Reign, you get your briefing on the go, while your agents are still in the thick of enemy cartel controlled terrain. You can get briefed, buy additional intel, research tech or outfit your agents all while &quot;in mission&quot;.<br>This might seem like a small cosmetic change, but it changes the whole feeling of the pace. While in Syndicate you sometimes escaped into the extraction zone, while being shot at by dozens of gauss guns and it didn't matter, because the next screen was a safe place far away, Satellite Reign keeps you in the game and it feels much more interesting and immersive.<br><br>But there is more. There are some key elements that were added to the Syndicate formula that make Satellite Reign even better.<br><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li> Classes<br><\/li><li> Skills<br><\/li><li> Cloning<\/li><\/ul><br>While in Syndicate all your agents were identical and only what equipment you gave them made them take on a certain role, Satellite Reign has introduced classes.<br><br><b><u>Classes<\/u><\/b><br>You start with a <b>Soldier<\/b> which is the tank of your team who also can dish out good peak damage.<br>You get a <b>Support<\/b>-class agent next, who can scan the environment and augument the whole team.<br>You have a <b>Hacker<\/b> next, who can hack terminals, ATMs, and take control of civilians, enemy agents or drones.<br>And you get the <b>Infiltrator<\/b> who can cloak, stealthily kill with a sword and use ziplines without additional tech.<br><br>This leads us to<br><br><b><u>Skills<\/u><\/b><br>All your agents can be upgraded in Health and Weapon Proficiency, as well as learn to carry more Items.<br><br>Your <b>Soldier<\/b> can learn <br><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li> Rage - which for a short time amplifies all damage he causes.<br><\/li><li> Draw Fire - which for a short time causes all enemies to concentrate fire on him while granting him damage reduction.<br><\/li><li> Harden - which for a short time grants him damage reduction at the expense of movement speed.<br><\/li><li> Explosive Specialist - which increases carry capacity for explosives as well as damage and radius of explosions.<br><\/li><li> Hardwiring - Ability to short circuit energy couplings to disable doors, security cameras or turrets.<\/li><\/ul><br>Your <b>Support<\/b> can learn<br><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li> World scan - which gives you information about individuals in range, wiring of tech around the world as well as other useful info<br><\/li><li> Field Medic - which heals your team continuously.<br><\/li><li> Team Stims - which effectively slows time around you for a short time.<br><\/li><li> Leadership - which gives your whole team more health, energy and reduces cooldown of skills.<\/li><\/ul><br>Your <b>Hacker<\/b> can learn<br><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li> Hijack - which lets you take over the minds of civilians or, with higher level, enemy agents and drones.<br><\/li><li> Hack - lets you hack terminals and ATMs<br><\/li><li> Drone Puppeteer - which lets you summon 1-3 personal drones that fight on your side (you have to buy and equip them first)<br><\/li><li> Mark target - which lets all of your team focus on one target while the damage it recieves gets amplified additionally.<\/li><\/ul><br>Your <b>Infiltrator<\/b> can learn<br><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li> Melee Attack - basically a one hit kill with a sword against most targets. Higher levels increase the damage to meke it a one hit against higher end enemies.<br><\/li><li> Cloak - which makes you invisible until your energy runs out.<br><\/li><li> Master Assassin - Increases sniper rifle range, damage and accuracy. Also passively increases the agents speed.<br><\/li><li> Master Thief - which grants more and more money from infiltrating facilities. Level one adds the ability to use ziplines without ever falling. Level two gives access to high vents without the need for Leg augumentations.<\/li><\/ul><br>So with all these skills there is much more tactics involved, some tactics are slow and safe, some are bold and reckless.<br>And some are very risky and your agents might die. <br>In Syndicate if you took your Persuadatron into town, you had an unlimited supply of bodies to fuel your war. Whenever one of your agents died, you lost a bit of money for the cybernetic implants, but after a couple of missions money was plentiful and you did not care about your death toll.<br>Satellite Reign is similar, but different.<br><br>When your agents die, ther is a short countdown during which you can pay money to revive (reclone) them right away or wait till the countdown reaches zero and you can send a fresh clone of the deceased agent in for free. <br>But it still comes in at a price.<br><br><b><u>Cloning<\/u><\/b><br>Every individual in Satellite Reign has stats and every agent does as well.<br>There is a baseline for the stats:<br><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li> Movement Speed<br><\/li><li> Health<br><\/li><li> Health Regeneration<br><\/li><li> Weapon Aim<\/li><\/ul>Every time you reclone your agents, they degrade and get worse in one or all of these stats.<br><br>It is a good tactic early on to Hijack enemy agents, which have better stats than the average civilian and send them back to cloning. Then clone your Soldier, Support, Hacker and Infiltrator into these bodies with better health, speed and aim.<br>But when you find a nice individual with +55 health +20% speed and +15% aim, you think twice before you let him die.<br><br>This is a nice game mechanic to balance out the free of charge revive and it adds another layer to the immersion where you find yourself thinking in the same perverted way about human bodies like these syndicates do - as just assets to add to your company.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Conclusion<\/div>This game is a jewel. I only played it for a weekend and I found myself googling if there will be a sequel or expansion to it, because I already fear that I will play through it too fast.<br><br>If you loved Syndicate, you must play this.
