class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\t<i>Daily Chthonicle<\/i> is an indie investigation management\/RPG game by Sinister System, focusing on detective story narrative with a touch of horror literature. As a first development trial, it is spectacularly enjoyable with a great deal of replayability. This version here on Steam is the last version with many storyline additions to the original prototype. If you are interested, but would like to try the freeware version before purchasing, it can be reached and downloaded via Game Jolt.<br><br>Here in this dark little world, we take a pace in the horror journalism business. In each run, we are given six detective\/journalists to send out to follow different leads which will be eventually tidied up into one supernatural case with a series of concurrent events, dominated by a lot of characters and different supernatural groups. The story patterns change for each game and the resulting story mostly makes sense by the end.<br><br>In every 24 hour period, your journalists will follow leads, engage with enemies, make investigations and collect clues. According to your management abilities - mainly training and equipping your journalists properly - you'll gather enough evidence to publish your newspaper and make sales. You should become careful with your budget, considering you'll need every little bit to properly support your journalists for them to be able to face enemies and paranormal encounters if need be. Whence they are either injured or disturbed at the end of an encounter, they should either be hospitalized or sent to therapy appropriately.<br><br>Environment design and graphics are presented in quite basic noire culture. Some effects are presented in colored writings to take your attention, but everything is mostly enveloped in black and white simplicity. I\u2019m positive that the game should work in nearly all Windows systems without a problem. You are given a bulletin board to send your journalists on leads, a filing cabinet to collect evidence, a window pane to contact your informants and a small laboratory to compare blood samples and supernatural essences. That's about all. All you'll be needing to play is your mouse - preferable one with a middle wheel to be able to read the continuation of all encounter cards during investigations. Whence all is set, you click on your clock to speed up the passage of time, until an encounter pops up in your window to inform you about the ongoings of any investigation. The investigation process includes some mini games too - deciphering phantom recordings, sending informants after some leads with lore choices etc. As the time passes, you will gather enough information to publish your journal and close cases. When you manage to tie up a collection of cases centering the same event to reveal the whole story, you win the game.<br><br>The game comes with different difficulty settings, including a tutorial setting with an easy game that will teach you all you'll need to successfully complete one single gaming session. If you\u2019ll still have questions about the gaming process, you\u2019ll find a really useful manual about all there is to learn about the game in your game directory. When you complete a game and quit, it will ask your nickname to put on the score board. Your score includes all the statistics of your whole game for a single scenario. According to the difficulty, the scenario and its main evil constantly changes, presenting you decent variety for gaming sessions.<br><br>Overall, I can safely recommend the game for anyone who'd enjoy an investigation RPG with a piece of supernatural lore on the side. It\u2019s nothing groundbreaking, but a decent time kill for a couple of hours.
