class=\"bb_h1\">Overview<\/div>Wind Child is a unique type of RPG Maker game, which doesn't fall into the pile of rubbish RPG Maker games which just want to take your money. This one on the other hand looks like the developers have taken some care and attention to their game. There is a interesting story line, and great opening cutscene that just makes you ask &quot;what the hell is going on?&quot; The characters are really nicely done and the mapping is fairly decent, of course there are some flaws with the game which I will talk about as well.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay<\/div>Wind Child is a fun and great RPG Maker game that really does look like they have taken some care and consideration to this game. This game isn't a cash grabber like some other RPG Maker games are. According to the developer, the game is approximately 20 hours long, if this is true then this game really has been worked on well. The opening cutscene ireally pulled me into the story, I wasn't really sure what was going on and why I was these people I was playing as but it was intriguing none the less. As the story begins to unfold, the game really shows off what you can do with RPG Maker. The characters that you come across are very well done, it's not like other games where you have a character added to your party for no specific reason to do so. Wind Child really makes sense for this person to help you along your journey as you play through the game. Upon entering new towns and cities you come across, you will find people to talk to. They aren't all just welcome to the city, some of these characters give you some background information about the story or about themselves.This draws away to the basic defult character saying things like &quot;hello&quot; and such that just really aren't that interesting. Another thing that the developers dide very well is the custom start menu and the mission log. The mission log is rather helpful if you have taken a break fro the game and when you come back in you are not sure what to do. The mission log will tell you what your missions are. Although I have explained a lot of positives about the gameplay itself, there are also some negatives that I feel like I must cover. First off, some of the tiles don't work the way that they should. What I mean by this is that you can walk through a tile that you aren't suppose to normally, either because you aren't on the right level, or because the block is meant to be impassable. A example of this is when you enter your home town, upon entering you will have to walk under a bridge to get across, although you walk over the bride. If you then walk forwards you will then be on the next height part of the world. This kind of brings down the game a little, as I found a couple of tiles that weren't exactly acting like they where suppose to. Another thing that slightly annoyed me whlist playing this game is the text that breifly describes the mission you are currently doing. Sometimes a line of text will be bold and the other lines will just be in normal font, this slightly annoyed me as this game is really fun to play.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Graphics<\/div>The developers really went all out with the custom tilesets that they created for their game specifically. This really makes it look different from the other RPG Maker games that only use the default tiles with minimum editing. Wind Child really takes the game and makes it their own, this just makes the game more unique and well presented. There is also a very nice transition whenever you enter a new town or city, with the name of the town that appears on the screen. The mapping (which I guess is part of graphics) is really well done, although I found that in some places it did seem a little bit empty like there was just too much space. Also in my personal opinion I didn't like some of the designs of the houses, they looked weird and out of place. Most of the outside mapping was done really well, there wasn't overcrowding trees or anything that really didn't make the map look natural.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Music &amp; Sound<\/div>When it comes to the music and sound of this game, I feel like th developers really got it right. There is fast paced music when something happens, when you have to chase that guard. It's then very calm and collective when you are in town or city. Something that I didn't like is that some of the houses didn't have any sound at all, and that really was a little dull. I also didn't like the way that the music carried on from outside to the inside of the houses. To me that kinda lowers the game a little.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Conclusion<\/div>Wind Child is a very unique type of RPG Maker game.
