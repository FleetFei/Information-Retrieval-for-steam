set a fictional 2018 Japan. In this game you play as Yama, a lazy good-for-nothing with quite a few mental issues. A string of disappearances are happening, all of them students from the school Yama attends which is supposed to be highly secure. You\u2019ll have to juggle your school and social life while trying to find out what\u2019s happening to the students and who is doing it. Can you do it before someone you care for joins the list?<br><br>Unlike many people who found this story to be touching or sad, I found myself more indifferent and bored. The story drags on for a while, this due to the fact that our protagonist doesn\u2019t actually actively care or do anything about the disappearances until over halfway through the game. While players are fully aware that something is wrong, we experience this through Yama\u2019s eyes. A good chunk of the first game is spent just chatting with friends, taking tests and other mundane things, which is fine if this was a slice of life title. Putting that aside, the other reason why I found the game to drag on was Yama\u2019s pretentious nature. His inner monologues are long, drawn out and reek of someone trying to sound smart but failing miserably. That being said, the characters besides Yama and Rui are enjoyable and I enjoyed the scenes where you interact and get to know them.<br><br><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li><b>Yama<\/b> - Our hero of the story, a self-admitted loser who looks down on almost everyone. As a main character I found him utterly annoying. The game starts with making you wonder if he\u2019s the killer or someone else is since Yama has \u201clapses.\u201d These are when he blacks out but apparently can still move, talk and perform actions. He\u2019s depressed, self-deprecating and puts up a wall to avoid showing the world how fragile he is. His best moments in the game is when he\u2019s interacting with his friends and learning to understand them.<br><\/li><li><b>Rui<\/b> - The childhood best friend trope, she stands by Yama no matter what and tries her best to take care of him. She always defends him and knows most of his secrets, she\u2019s also in love with him. Wow, go figure. The game is very adamant about pushing Yama and she together, despite him not really showing any interest. All of the character\u2019s in the game push this and Rui refuses to confess because that\u2019s what the trope calls for. I also found her annoying and how she was pushed upon Yama, though I did respect her dedication to him and believe she\u2019d stay that way even if they don\u2019t end up together.<br><\/li><li><b>Misaki, Airi, Akira and Elizabeth<\/b> - These are the other females in the game that go to school with Yama and Rui, Misaki is their childhood friend. Akira is Rui\u2019s best friend who is also in love with Yama. Airi is a mysterious girl who is very quiet and Elizabeth is the smartest girl in the school who is probably also in love with Yama. The game lets your choose options with them, like call or text and even hang out. You can get to know each of them and doing so makes the game more well-rounded. Doing so also doesn\u2019t matter, the game ends the same way so don\u2019t expect a cute romance with any of them like I unfortunately did.<br><\/li><li><b>Yahiko and Masato<\/b> - Yama\u2019s two male friends. Yahiko is a ball of fun and laughs who is also very naive. Masato is an incredibly smart jock who is very competitive. They help round out the circle of friends often offer most of the humor present in the story. They both care about Yama very much, however Masato greatly dislikes Yama\u2019s laziness.<br><\/li><li><b>Shigure and Mr. Ryouta<\/b> - The two main adults in this game, Shigure is a private detective that hires Yama to be her assistant and opens his eyes to the rampant disappearances. She is snarky and at times quite rude but is a good addition to the story and helps move things along. Mr. Ryouta is the dorm manager at the school and quasi guidance counselor. He offers Yama support and an open ear while trying to motivate him to do better.<\/li><\/ul><br>Story aside, everything else about <i>Lucid9<\/i> is fine. You use your mouse to play the game, though there are two points in which you will need a keyboard. You can click to progress the text or put it on auto and relax, there is also a skip feature to zoom past text you\u2019ve already scene while trying to achieve other endings. The user interface is simple and offers all of the options needed for a visual novel such as the ability to hide text. One thing I did not like about the gameplay is how your choices mean very little. Other than failing the final steps against the villain your choices have no impact. Passing or failing tests is meaningless, getting to know a girl or not does nothing, you can\u2019t end up with her and in any deduction sequence choosing the wrong answer will just lead you to try and pick a different one. The ending of the story is always bad and you can\u2019t change it which I don\u2019t mind but it can be a downer.<br><br>I really enjoyed the music in the game, it was cheerful when it needed to be and had great sad melodies as well. The music always fit the scenario. The game also has sound effects which were fine, though I found the phone ringing one annoying since there is at least once you don\u2019t answer the phone right away because Yama is having yet another long inner monologue. The art is fine, I enjoyed it and the character models were cute. The game has superb CG art, even of the most unpleasant thing. My only complaint is that where weren\u2019t more CG scenes. The game is rather long and I would have enjoyed seeing more of the lovely artwork.<br><br>I may sound critical of the game but did like it on some levels. While some things dragged on for a bit it did build up the feeling that slowly but surely Yama was losing everything around him and it was satisfying when he overcame this. This satisfaction is probably why the lack of any happy endings hurt so much because in the end it seemed like it was all for naught. The game is free so you\u2019re not losing anything by giving <i>Lucid9<\/i> a go, the developers promise more content in the future as well. I love everyone.<br><br>THE ART IS SOLID AND THE UI IS WELL ORGANIZED, STYLISTIC, AND CONSISTENT. Sprites are very pretty to look at! I prefer the male character art as opposed to the female style in this, but it's definitely right for those who want their cute waifus!
