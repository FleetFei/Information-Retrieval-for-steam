get the whole dish from easy and well-balanced to devilishly hard and almost uncontrollable. Crunching through 4 of the most beloved (adult) cartoon series of the last years, this menu is tasty and makes for hours of play - if you are frustration-resistant and rage quit-approved.<br><br>The tables one by one<br><br>Family Guy is the easiest of this pack. It is a fairly conventional ramp table that lives off of its well ported theme. Sounds and visuals are spot-on and it sports some nice video modes. What kicks this table a notch above average is an breezy game flow that makes for (relatively) relaxed gameplay. A nice package for beginners or a fun round inbetween the heavy-weights.<br><br>American Dad is the weakest of the tables concerning gameplay. While the modes and visuals are funny (Long live Roger!), the table has a slightly awkward rhythm to it. A serious obstacle is the spinwheel that catapults the ball directly into the middle drain. So, much of the time this table is about avoiding the top middle to not produce a fast beeline ball to death.<br><br>Number three is Bob's Burgers and this one takes the cup. It really is a bit of a jack of all trades, catering to the casual player and the wiz crowd alike. Its strong point is a large variety of modes and good balance between sharp shooting and   play flow. In fact, it is almost two tables in one. By switching between day mode that starts all the burger-themed missions (including the customer main mode that is an intricate series of challenges between locking balls, time-limited ramp collecting and quick-shooting) and the night mode that bolsters up the jackpots, the whole table is quasi-doubled. A tricky loop to the cross-ramp and aggressive, yet controllable outlanes add a bit of bite. All in all a great table.<br><br>And finally there is Archer... this one will split the audience. Obviously aimed at the pros out there, this table brings together all that is hateworthy: high speeds angles everywhere, a rail that drips into the middle drain (over the &quot;p&quot;), the &quot;y&quot; bumper feeds directly into the right outlane and the left outlane is a highway to hell. The player is forced to continously sharp-shoot, yet severe time-limits on most actions leave no time to set-up and aim the shots. The table features a shop system that makes you earn money at the spinner to later buy kickbacks, ball saves, etc. in the armory (which in turn is only triggered by the ominous &quot;p&quot; and &quot;y&quot; bumpers cursed earlier...). But the prices rise with each sale, so kickbacks become a ridiculously hard affair after second buy. This table is an entry to the all-time b***ards of Zen pinball hell, comparable in speed to X-Men and Boba Fett. It really combines the erratic behaviour of Age of Ultron with the shot complexity of the Sith Masters table. Funnily enough, it also is perversely entertaining (at least if you can take a beating on Super Hexagon scales...)<br><br>So, there is something for everyone. Bob's Burgers rules. Archer will last you for hours before you can even control the ball. Famly Guy is a fun romp. And even the low point, American Dad, still ranks as an average table.
