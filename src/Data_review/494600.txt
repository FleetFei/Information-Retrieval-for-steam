Review<\/div>Aliens have invaded earth, and it\u2019s up to a couple hulking dudes and limber babes decked out in space armor and pimp shades to drive those \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665s back into outer space (Slow motion pimp shades zoom in with gansta music).  Tossed in a purdy coat of Japanese inspired comic-book art, you can pick it up on for $24 US dollars on Steam right now.  <br><br><b>Full written review below, or check out the video version (giveaway included)<\/b><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"https:\/\/www.youtube.com\/watch?v=Sn9NfNmzt04\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_0\">https:\/\/www.youtube.com\/watch?v=Sn9NfNmzt04<\/a><br><b>Story<\/b><br>Earth's Dawn isn\u2019t going to win any awards for storytelling.  You are charged with reclaiming Earth from invading aliens.  You\u2019ve hi-jacked some alien technology and now you\u2019re a superhero laser shooting, katana swinging badass capable of going toe-to-toe with some of the galaxies scariest creatures.  Sound like a Michael Bay movie?  You betcha.<br>(Insert Micahel Bay)<br><br><b>Gameplay<\/b><br>Gameplay in Earth\u2019s Dawn revolves around the mashup between it\u2019s boost and juggle systems.  You can mix together melee and ranged attacks in mid-air and dance around the screen with your dashes which replenish over time.  When you\u2019ve smashed your enemies down far enough, you can open up with finishers to execute.  <br><br>A dedicated combo system would have gone a long way to make the combat more satisfying.  Where are the abundant special abilities, special melee attacks and why isn\u2019t there a combo system to create links and cancels during combat?  With these features omitted, gameplay often feels a little disjointed as you will be at the mercy of simply smashing the attack button over and over until an enemy's lifebar is depleted.  Finisher...rinse and repeat.  Fun for a while, but then repetition sets in.<br><br>To counter that litany, the game does have a lavish loot and upgrade system.  Unfortunately, there is also shocking amount of missed opportunities in terms of this progression system.  Most unlocks are simply stat enhancers, sadly, and do little to vary up the gameplay save a few interesting movement unlockables.    <br><br>On the bright side, boss battles in Earth\u2019s Dawn are pretty epic and challenging, not to mention really enjoyable to look at.<br><br><b>Aesthetics<\/b><br>The beautiful manga-based art style is a treat to look at while playing, and while the characters are silly and charming, the environments are far less colorful and personality-driven than games like Dragons Crown .  Animations are flashy, however, and give a nice pop to the otherwise suprisingly grey and brown dominated environments.  Yeah, there\u2019s a lot of grey here, a lot of caves and rocks, and a lot of stone and fire.  I was craving green in Earth\u2019s Dawn.  Trees, grass, sunshine radiating through the screen as it exists in it\u2019s competitors games, but on the positive the game does offset this monotone environment style with effects that seem to light up those dark places.  Tons of spells and abilities burst and splash through the game space like fireworks - really fun stuff to watch.<br><br>It should be noted that the game has no english voice overs and the music is fairly uninspired.  <br><br><b>Cons and Opportunities<\/b><br>Due to the mission structure and lack of gameplay variety, Earth Dawn will begin to feel repetitive.  Depending on your tolerance, that could be a few hours, or 15 in to the game.  Whatever the case, you\u2019ll beg for Earth\u2019s Dawn to mix things up, give you interesting tools and really explore deep gameplay mechanics as you progress through the game.  Sadly, that never happens.  Games like Odin Sphere present far more depth to the gameplay, more satisfying chain mechanics, and really give us more personality overall.  <br><br>Also, I would like to the camera panned back a bit and some improvements made to your AI friends.  Most of the time they sit there like they are half-asleep - all they do is take up the valuable screen space that is already lacking in the first place.<br><br><b>Verdict<\/b><br>I love Japanese inspired games.  And I love sidescrollers.  So for me, I enjoyed Earth\u2019s Dawn for a few hours.  It sits in the bucket of \u2018average, yet competant game.\u2019  I can\u2019t slam it, and I can\u2019t praise it.<br><br>Buy this game if you already love the formula of Odin\u2019s Sphere.
