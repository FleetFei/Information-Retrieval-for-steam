There were some differences in the story details. But Microid mostly made changes where Hercule Poirot was simply not involved in the case.  However, there were some changes that were a bit odd. The novel makes it a point to keep the number of suspects open. But very quickly the game drops the suspect count down to two.  Now, this is done so that you could get a sense progress in the game but it makes the ending a forgone conclusion. Moreover, they outright cut out the last murder due to the fact the Hercule Poirot was not involved in its direct investigation. This, and some other altered scenes, actually robs the criminal of some vital planning they had done. I wouldn\u2019t say any change was a deal breaker, but some may consider the changes a bad interpretation of the story.<br><br>The game, however, does well enough with visuals and voice acting. It use a cell shaded style that\u2019s not to taxing on GPUs. Voice acting is nice and clear and doesn\u2019t leave you wanting. The failure is in game design. Gameplay is compartmentalized into about 4 styles. First, is good old fashion Point and Click evidence searching, where you look around a crime scene for items you might need. Can\u2019t say it was a fun part of the game though, there was more pixel hunting than I cared for. Next, there was an Interview type game element, not unlike L.A. Noirs interviews, where you first examine a person to determine their state then you get a choice of questions to ask. Depending on what you ask you could get the interviewee to cooperate well or to cooperate not so well.  Beyond getting some points, it really was just dialog. The Third game play style came in the form of a quiz.  You would take what you know to answer questions about the case, sometimes the answers where straight forward, other required a bit of a leap. The last gameplay style was \u201cRoom\u201d-esque puzzle solving. Everyone seems to have a locked object that requires a complicated means of opening it. This definitely felt out of place, but it\u2019s the main driving force of game play. None of the 4 styles of gameplay really meshed well with each other.<br><br>So with weak gameplay, it begs the question. Why not just make this a visual novel? Really, if you\u2019re interested in Agatha Christie or mysteries in general you may enjoy the game.  While the gameplay did nothing genre defining or captivating, the story was interesting enough to push me forward.
