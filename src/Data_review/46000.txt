While most of his co-workers don\u2019t share his enthusiasm for the less than stellar gig, he\u2019s pleased as punch to clock in each day and go about his normal work routine. Unfortunately for Bob, his commute to work is cut short one day when his ship is struck by a wayward meteor. And his day doesn\u2019t get much better from there.<br><br>Losing his hyper drive unit, as well as numerous other crucial chunks of his ship, he crash lands on a strange planet without help in sight. In order to get Bob back to work, you\u2019ll guide what\u2019s left of his spacecraft through different and interestingly designed areas of the planet to recover missing parts and the ever-important hyper drive system. What makes the explorative journey so fascinating is that you\u2019ll use the junk parts you recover along the way to constantly re-build and re-design Bob\u2019s ship to overcome the obstacles you run into.<br><br>Both above and below the surface, the mysterious, earth-like planet is rife with mechanical contraptions designed to impede your progress. Among giant locking mechanisms and puzzles that must be assembled you\u2019ll find trigger plates to depress, large objects to move, and force fields to circumvent, alongside more peculiar hurdles. While solving puzzles on the first few stages only require some basic common sense, these impediments grow increasingly complex and you\u2019ll have to make more drastic alterations to your ship to proceed.<br><br>Your core ship is a small orb with eight bolt points where additional parts can be attached. As you explore the planet, you\u2019ll find basic extender parts, various hinges, and several special functioning items that you can combine and assemble onto your vessel. Most levels have multiple hotspots where you can stop and freely play around with re-designing your ship using any parts you\u2019ve collected. Frequent experimentation is necessary. For example, one area may require you to assemble a long hook-like fixture to your ship in order to flick a hard-to-reach switch. Other areas require you to attach a pushing or pulling device to manipulate rocks, mechanical devices, and other gadgets you find.<br><br>But it\u2019s not as easy as just dropping anything onto Bob\u2019s ship willy-nilly. The game features a dynamic physics system, and the more you add to your ship, the more it affects how it handles in accordance with balance and gravity. Plunking down too much junk on your craft without beefing up your boosters will render you virtually immobile, and you can only move in certain directions if you have boosters located on the opposite end to push you. Bob Came in Pieces oozes creativity and rewards you for noodling around to your heart\u2019s content.
