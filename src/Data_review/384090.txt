There's fun to be had no matter how you approach the game - landscapes designed for exploration, quests to find and perform, structures to build, beasts to kill and loot to hunt. <br><br>I spent my first hour just wandering around in confusion with a goofy grin. I was chased by enemies who muttered daft things at me or played the harmonica. I laughed as I killed something whose body span comically down the hill. The drawing element is essentially an in-game paint programme, with the ability to add basic animation frames if you want. Right now you can edit\/draw anything you're holding in your inventory (if you have picked up the Witch Brush), plus you get to draw your character when you first start playing. <br><br>3. There'll be bugs - unoptimised graphics, a couple of  spelling mistakes, features that don't seem to work - but they'll get fixed. No full crashes, but did have to reload quite a bit to clear any built-up lag, though.<br><br>4. The building isn't as intuitive as Minecraft, with (simple) menus to select shapes and so on, but once you get the hang of it you can build blocky structures easily. You can alter the default cube to stuff like ramps, arches and flat bits too.<br><br>5. You don't get given all the building\/colouring tools at the start - you need to go out and find them in the world on scrolls. Plus, everything you build requires energy, which uses up very fast and then slowly fills back up again. You can find items that increase your max energy, but at the start of the game you're very limited (This applies to story mode - I don't know yet if sandbox mode will have this limitation).<br><br>6.
