I was hooked moments after playing the game for the first time. Best of all, it is free to play and, while it does have a cash store for gold, you NEVER have to buy anything to fully enjoy the game.  I have easily played halfway through the game, yes I have had to do some gold grinding, but I have never made a store purchase for gold nor felt like I needed to do so.<br><br>It plays much like the family card games one would play in real life where you have to have a sharp eye to quickly slap the correct card you need as fast as you can.  Only in this case you are competing against the computer for time instead of other players, and every card you slap sends out troops or battle effects in order to seige a horde of monsters in bloody mayhem.<br><br>Game play is simple: click on the &quot;card&quot; of a unit at the bottom of the screen to send them into battle, but you only have so much time to get as many units out as possible before you have to just sit back and hope your troops can achieve victory. However, there is actually a lot of strategy involved. The six cards that you can click on at the bottom of the screen can quickly change as you click on other cards, and the cards that appear vary from helpful troops, bombs, time gains, and gold to hurtful bombs, time losses, or just no action. So you have to pay attention and make quick decisions without adversely affecting your troops.<br><br>The action stays frenetic, and unlike other games where you have to hire troops with gold and must wait for an amount of gold to accrue before sending out your better troops, you can always send out any troops you have unlocked in the game as long as it appears in your bottom bar. I found the game pretty well balanced as far as what appears in your six card slots. Many times there are loads of troops to select from and all good effects, and sometimes there are only bad effects available, but not enought to feel like the game is slanted against you. It's not so easy that the game plays itself, yet it is also not so challenging that it scares away anyone who is not a master player.<br><br>After beating levels you accrue gold which you can then spend to upgrade your troop types, monetary gain from cards, and lower card cooldowns. While there is a cash store to buy gold for upgrading purposes, it is the same as the gold you make in game, and so you would never have to buy anything in the cash store to fully enjoy this title. This is an example of a Free-to-Play with a cash store done right. As you are buying the same currency as that which you make in game, nothing is locked behind real money payments, plus you can upgrade the amount of gold you gain while playing, and go on &quot;gold runs&quot; where you click on as many gold cards as possible instead of focusing on sending out more troops. The cash store is really there so that people can donate money to the game designer as it is a free game, while still giving something back to the player for doing so. And it's there if you are a really impatient person who just can't grind their own gold for upgrades.<br><br>All in all, this is a really fun game to play, it's free, and if it sounds at all like a game you might want to try, I wholeheartedly encourage you to give it a chance.
