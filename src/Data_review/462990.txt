Even at the cost of being almost completely disconnected from the rest of the visual novel, it ties in perfectly with Clannad, which is something the core story of Tomoyo After seemed to go out of its way to avoid at every opportunity. The premise is familiar, but not the roles. Characters regain the qualities that made them likable in the first place. The Tomoyo route comes full circle and gets as satisfying of a conclusion as possible given the nature of the setting. The only criticisms are that it's relatively brief and it wasn't better integrated into the rest of the story.<br>\t<\/li><li><b>Dungeons and Takafumis side-game<\/b> --- A small, turn-based RPG featuring the characters from the visual novel is available as optional content from the main menu.<\/li><\/ul><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Cons<\/div><ul class=\"bb_ul\">\t<li><b>Story<\/b> --- Uninteresting and at times forced by way of characters being blatantly written as unnaturally oblivious and passive for the sake of drama. One plotline is proven meaningless upon conclusion despite some stretching to justify the whole misunderstanding. Another passes by so quickly and changes absolutely nothing for the characters involved, it may as well not have taken place.<br><br>\tDuring the rare moments when the story is at its best, it's on par with the Clannad visual novel's weakest routes. The theme of family that is core to the series is there, but is severely underdeveloped. It's a poorly planned and executed story with misplaced focus on the wrong characters and problems. Up until the sudden turn of events towards the end, it was easy to be misled into thinking the story would conclude the same way.<br>\t<\/li><li><b>Characters<\/b> --- The main character from Clannad and Tomoyo return, although they sometimes act out of character. The brother that was a motivating factor behind the Tomoyo route becomes a permanent addition while receiving some elaboration on his past. The other few, new characters aren't compelling. One is an annoyance that not only lacks any sort of character growth but also actively detracts from the story at crucial moments, dragging it back down before it can accomplish anything.<br><br>\tNew characters receive poor presentation as they barge in with introductions all the while established characters put up feigned resistance to give the illusion of agency as the story pushes forward.<\/li><\/ul><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Conclusion<\/div>Having considered the Tomoyo storyline to be one of the best routes in Clannad, reading through the majority of Tomoyo After was disappointing. Given her situation in Clannad, it was natural for her relationship with the main character to grow impersonal as the story progressed. Retreading that path here with the role reversal could have worked, but without the engaging characters and story, it failed to be entertaining from the onset. That is up until the ending, where out of nowhere the story finally meets and exceeds all original expectations.<br><br>If the journey had been anywhere near as great as the destination, Tomoyo After would have been an easy recommendation. Looking at the work as a whole, the ending alone isn't enough to make up for the slog getting there. At a length of about <b>11 hours<\/b> without taking the optional Dungeons &amp; Takafumis RPG into account, only those that have at least completed the Tomoyo route in Clannad and enjoyed it immensely should consider picking up Tomoyo After. Even then, this visual novel is barely a recommendation and <b>should only be purchased on sale<\/b> when it's heavily discounted. Tomoyo After ~It's a Wonderful Life~ is finally on Steam! The tagline for this visual novel has been on my Steam profile description for as long as I can remember and while this VN is short, it has a powerful impact which I think is much more powerful than CLANNAD. Even so, CLANNAD and Tomoyo After still works on the same premise: the importance of family.<br><br>Tomoyo After ~It's a Wonderful Life~ is the spin-off to the highly acclaimed visual novel CLANNAD which picks up where Tomoyo route ended in CLANNAD. Developed by Key back in 2005, this VN was originally released as 18+ eroge thus why the voice actor for Tomoyo is different and the art direction is slightly different than how it was in CLANNAD. This time around Key didn't use Sekai Project to publish their game, instead they publish it under VisualArts brand while the translation is handled by another company using Doki's translation as a base (probably). Nevertheless, thx based Doki.<br><br>Tomoyo After picks up few months after Tomoyo's route ended in CLANNAD. Tomoya now works in a local workshop fixing home appliance while Tomoyo is now in her Senior Year. After the problem between Tomoya and Tomoyo in CLANNAD, the couple promise to be more forward with each other so they can overcome all the problem in life no matter how hard it is. One day, Takafumi, Tomoyo's younger brother, brought a young girl named Tomo which turns out to be the sibling's illegal stepsister. Takafumi, not wanting the Sakagami family to be torn apart again, decided to make Tomo live with Tomoya. Tomo is living happily with Tomoyo and Tomoya, despite that she still wants to be with her mother, thus began the journey to find Tomo's mother and the reason why she abandons Tomo to the Sakagami family.<br><br>Without giving too much spoilers, Tomoyo After follows on its tagline <b>\u300cLet's look for the treasure of life\u300d<\/b> by taking us on a journey of how Tomoyo overcoming her weakness with the help of Tomoya while Takafumi have to face the consequences of what he had done in CLANNAD and the burden that his choice has put upon him. Tomoya himself also wants to prove that he is no longer the person that he was back in his high school days and show the world that the love between him and Tomoyo are <b> eternal <\/b> and cannot be broken by anything for their power of love will overcome all troubles.<br><br>The art for Tomoyo After was directed mainly by Fumio and not by Hinoue, thus why Tomoyo looks similar but visibly different from how she was in CLANNAD. While Hinoue might not be the main art director like how she was in CLANNAD, she still helps with the character design. Everything else was handled in-house by Key with another notable thing to note is that Kashida Leo, a new scenario writer for Key, helped Maeda Jun in making the scenario for Tomoyo After.<br><br>The VN will take about 15-20 hours to finish depending on your reading speed. The VN alone is worth the current pricetag that it carry. But it doesn't stop there! As if the epic closure for Tomoyo story is not enough, they put an icing on the cake with Dungeon &amp; Takafumi! A tactical role-playing game centered around the game that Takafumi is currently developing along with his overseas friend. It is time consuming and quite fun once you get the hang of it plus it has its own story centered around Takafumi and Kanako, Takafumi's girlfriend. On that note, I kinda dislike the fact that the Steam version lets you to play D&amp;T even when starting Tomoyo After for the first time instead of unlocking it through the choice that you made while conversing with Takafumi in the VN.<br><br>Since this VN has many revision and re-release, I actually managed to read this VN multiple times across multiple version across multiple console. Retconned the &quot;bad&quot; ending in Original release because everyone hates it while adding voice acting for Tomoya. I prefer the Original release ending rather than the retconned ending since it delivers a much more impactful ending than the one in CS Edition but I guess everyone is entitled to their own opinion. Also I never platinum this Edition because I don't like playing RPG on my couch.<br><\/li><li><b>Memorial Edition<\/b>: Played on PC, this edition is basically CS Edition but for PC since the CS Edition only released on console.<br><\/li><li><b>Perfect Edition<\/b>: Played on PC, includes all the content in Memorial Edition with added 18+ scene, but removed Tomoya's voice acting.<br><\/li><li><b>English Edition<\/b>: Played on PC via Steam. This Edition is based on the Memorial Edition of Tomoyo After. <b><u>Remember<\/u><\/b>, this is <u><i>English Edition<\/i><\/u> so it make sense that it won't be translated to other language.<\/li><\/ol><br>Closing my review, I strongly suggest that you read CLANNAD before picking up Tomoyo After or at least have watched Tomoyo-hen otherwise you will be lost as to what is going on in the story.
