There are straightforward brawls, though enemies in those are usually pretty tough. While the rewards remain the same, the fights will be significantly more difficult. <br><br>There are also three new companions to recruit: a monk, a rogue, and a barbarian. Each of these companions are fully-voiced, have their own side-quests, and will also react to events in the vanilla game. Thus, it pays to recruit them early on. If you found some of the companions in vanilla PoE to be too solemn or flat, then you'll be pleasantly surprised by the new recruits, who manage to add some light-heartedness to the game without spoiling the overall tone of the story. These items have 3-4 different levels of power, which can only be accessed when bound to a character (which prevents other characters from using said weapon). Some of them are really useful, but most of them aren't quite as good as some stuff that you can get from the higher level areas of the game. But if you do the White March DLC at the recommended levels (7-10 or so), then these items will probably serve you well. <br><br>There's also a side-quest series that involves an NPC challenging your claim to your stronghold. This wonderful human being starts with legal action and harsh words, and then eventually escalates to more extreme measures. Be advised that making allies in both parts of the DLCs will go a long way to ensuring your claim over Caed Nua. Like many other of the in-game events, there is no strict time limit in regard to dealing with this wonderful human being, so you have all the time in the world to raise a suitable defense force.<br><br>I'd say that the entire White March DLC packs added about a good 20 hours to my playtime, with me largely speeding through the vanilla content, though I was largely taking my time during the White March content and doing all the side-quests and talking to NPCs to see if there were any consequences (good or bad) to some of my actions.<br><br>WM1 + 2 are solid additions to the base PoE game, and I found that it was definitely worth the full price.
