2017<br><b>Type:<\/b>  Singleplayer &amp; Multiplayer<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">General Impression<\/div>Since 2015, there have been four games in Fabio Ferrara\u2019 Heaven series. This is my second review for one of his creations, after Woodle Tree 2: Worlds. This developer\u2019s games are far from the \u201clow-effort\u201d label some haters and trolls would have you believe. First and foremost, all those foaming around their mouths after such affordable titles, miss the crucial point here. They couldn\u2019t code or design a game even if their lives would depend on it. Case dismissed.<br><br>On the contrary, given what they cost, such games have a niche of their own on Steam. Couple that with the fact that so many of these well-priced games host massive public giveaways, you can imagine that they\u2019ll reach an ever wider audience. Believe it or not, about one year ago I was more than happy and grateful for ANY free game I might gain in a public Gleam giveaway since I had a Steam game collection of close to 100. Before you disrespect someone\u2019s hard work perhaps think for a second that not everybody on Steam can afford  to pay 60 dollars for a newly released AAA title? Some modesty wouldn\u2019t hurt one bit, no matter how much spare cash you got at your disposal just for gaming.<br><br>Heaven Forest NIGHTS might not have changed much from it\u2019s \u201cdaylight\u201d version in the form of Heaven Forest - VR MMO but some minor tweaks have been applied to the fauna and flora. The core gameplay has you exploring a faily big open level in search for collectibles (mushrooms, crystals, souls, apples and quatrefoils) while you can read floating scrolls filled with metaphors and wise sayings. Those written words may not belong to Confucius or Lao Tzu, yet they still represent food for thought. Buddhism seems to be a central theme in the entire Heaven franchise since giant statues of the Buddha, can be found in all four titles. I may be closer to agnosticism than anything else, but at least I respect other people\u2019s cultures and beliefs. Religion isn\u2019t being promoted per se in this game which seems to focus more on Asian archtectural motifs.<br><br>Animals are also present on the map and roam freely. They don\u2019t seem to even notice your presence and thus no combat\/killing in this game. I hope you don\u2019t regret that. In fact you appear to be controlling an essence or spirit more than a materialized character. You can levitate and thus explore Heaven Forest NIGHTS with increased efficiency. It\u2019s a relaxing game that must be treated precisely like that. There\u2019s no hidden meaning behind anything and what you see is what you get. I was more annoyed by the contrast between low-res textures near objects that were very skillfully textured and the constant pop-in of objects as if I was playing this game on my PS3 or something. At least the Unity Engine assets deliver 4K resolution and stable 60 fps without a single crash to desktop. Unreal Engine 4 has left a sour taste in my mouth because of its poor implementation in Steam indie games. At least you don\u2019t have that stress in Heaven Forest NIGHTS.<br><br>As long as you keep an open mind and understand its low production value is being reflected in the equally low price tag, all will be well. And relax while playing it since that\u2019s its intended purpose anyways. It may have full VR support, but I\u2019m still a couple of years away from owning such a headset. Perhaps you might be more interested in these (still) overpriced gadgets but even without VR, the game is fully playable on a monitor and mouse+keyboard setup. You have Cards and Achievements too and all you have to do is buy it now or wait for a bundle or giveaway.
