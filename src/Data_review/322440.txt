So I'll try to gather both pros and cons.<br><br>Red Goddess: Inner World is a 2.5D side-scroller exploration metroidvania made by a 6-people team, where you take control of Divine, a young girl who lost her memories and mainly spends the whole game trying to figure out what happened to her and her godly family. During her journey Divine will find people who were relevant in her past life and that will help her recovering her memories. <br><br>PROS:<br><br>+ A game beautifully made, with more than a decent artwork and interesting exploring areas.<br>+ Relaxing soundtrack; not that memorable, but does its job.<br>+ As a metroidvania, in terms of platforming, it's a surprisingly consistent title. Enemies will have the same color as one of those transformations, and you will have to take it down with the proper one. This adds more interest into the battles.<br>+ A detailed map denying any confusion about where you are and where you have to go.<br>+ The game takes from 5-10h to beat, mainly depending on user's skills. Which is more than enough for a side-scroller.<br>+ Interesting story that keeps you entertained and waiting for more.<br>+ In terms of difficulty, the game grows pretty fast, being completely fearless in the first hours but challenging in the second half unless you're used to those kind of platformers.<br>+ Chilling achievements, mainly based on combat stuff and storyline. No achievements based on collectibles which is definately positive.<br><br>CONS:<br><br>- Even though the game features enough skills to play with, combat is not as soft as the platforming thing.
