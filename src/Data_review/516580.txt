Nucleons can be found on every corner of the level, but so can enemies. You can avoid killing enemies if you like, but the salvage gained from killing enough of them could net you a fancy new ship.<br><br>There are various power ups and items scattered about that give you an advantage as well as help unlock specific portions of the level. For example, bombs blow up glass, opening up new areas to explore.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Controls<\/div>Keyboard and mouse can be used and so can an XBox 360 controller. I used the controller, which is super responsive and traversing the level couldn\u2019t feel smoother. You fire with the right trigger, place down items with B, fire your secondary weapon with X, view the map of the entire world with right shoulder, and change the camera with Y.<br><br>Utilizing the map is crucial if you\u2019re a completionist and want to collect all the Nucleons and find the secret ship piece hidden somewhere in the level.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Music\/Graphics<\/div>The music is straight out of the 80s and fits perfectly with the chaotic gameplay. The colors are bright and vibrant in a neon style that\u2019s reminiscent of Tron. <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Overall<\/div>Dystoria is a wild, six-axis, technicolored ride.
