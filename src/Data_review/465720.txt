The best part is, while it\u2019s a direct sequel you don\u2019t need to play the first game to fully enjoy this one. The story of the first game is referenced in this one but you get enough of a recap to not get lost along the way.<br><br>This game takes place twenty years after Dawn\u2019s first adventure, she is now a respected -and experienced- demon hunter. However, her past continues to haunt her as she is called in for a case that connects to her adoptive father who was slain two decades ago. I did not find myself frustrated at all while playing and the game gave adequate instructions if you needed help. The visuals are much crisper and the animations a bit better. However, the characters still have weird lip movements when they speak, I\u2019m not sure if this is just a stylistic choice for this series or not but I find it off putting. Overall, however, things look fine. The colors are vibrant and even when the setting is dark and spooky you can see what you\u2019re doing pretty well. The voice acting is alright, some actors were pretty bad but as a whole I didn\u2019t find myself laughing at anything or wanting to mute the game. The music is fitting for the theme of the game but easily forgettable.<br><br>If you\u2019re a fan of hidden object games then I do suggest adding <i>Demon Hunter 2: New Chapter<\/i> to your library.
