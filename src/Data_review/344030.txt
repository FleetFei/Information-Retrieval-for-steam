And the telepathic link starts off interesting - you ask others about things you don't know about - but, given the counter-intuitiveness of most puzzles, this soon devolves into one more inventory item that you combine randomly with anything in the hope of advancing.<br><br>The authors of this game really, really should have played &quot;Discworld Noir&quot; first. They would have picked up a lot of very useful guidelines, such as how to do switching between different forms of the PC to great puzzle advantage. And exactly how much good voice acting matters, especially when you have bad 3D graphics.<br><br>Because the two main problems of &quot;Ghost Pirates...&quot; are the horrible 3D models, and voice acting. The 3D elements stick out like a sore thumb from the breathtakingly beautiful backgrounds, making you wish you could play this in FPP and just watch the amazing pictures without the &quot;3D models&quot; punching holes in them.<br>And since you have to watch the clunky, graceless things, they could have at least spoken well, because while the actual lines are often pretty funny, the voice actors speak as if doing extremely thick accents took up 100% of their powers and none was left for actual delivery. So the swashbuckling dashing spy talks as if she didn't understand her own words, and the bokor is permanently in &quot;me big black angry guy&quot; mode, even when waxing poetic about his lost love. The voices are so insufferable I mostly played on mute, and then a cutscene happened and I had to reload, because they have no subtitles for some inscrutable reason.<br><br>All in all, I still give a
