very niche market. You will like this DLC if:<br>-You love old timey western guns.<br>-you are sick of the car-4 and want something that requires a little more skill to be used.<br>-You want to be a total badass elf like legolas.<br><br>Onto the contents. This pack contains 4 weapons, these are:<br>-The Peacemaker .45 revolver (colt single action army): This is essentially a Bronco on steroids. It does an incredible amount of damage, more than the rattlesnake, which is a SNIPER RIFLE! It also has good accuracy and a nice amount of base stability. On the more negative side though, it's reload time is extremely long, as is has to be loaded bullet by bullet. It also comes paired with some massive visual recoil and only six shots when it's fully loaded (since it's a revolver), but then again 6 shots is more than enough to kill anything that moves. Probably the best thing about this gun is the fact  that when you switch to it from your primary weapon your character will twirl the gun as he brings it up. In my personal opinion this gun might not be &quot;the best gun ever&quot; as Ocelot might claim, but it sure is a lot of fun to use and it has been my favorite secondary since this DLC came out. Unforgiving at it's best, this gun is a true skill cannon. (warning: use of this weapon might make the user start uncontrollably quoting Revolver Ocelot.) <br><br>-Plainsrider bow: We've had miniguns, grenade launchers, flamethrowers and now... a bow? Yes, a bow. The bow is an excellent stealth weapon, as it's silent and has a concealment of 30, which is really quite nice for an unmodified primary weapon. It's also got pretty high damage, but that's to be expected from a weapon with such a low rate of fire. It's damage can be boosted by equipping the explosive arrows, but this will make the bow a loud weapon. The bows main problem is that it requires you to be really good with it, even more so than the peacemaker, as missing a shot can be lethal since it'll take a second to load another arrow and draw the bow again. The arrows shot from the bow also fly in an arching motion (unlike the bullets fired from any of the regular primary weapons), so you'll have to carefully aim to hit targets at longer ranges. It's ammo pickup system is quite different from traditional weapons, as you'll actually have to recover your own arrows after you've shot them. This brings up a problem when you equip the explosive arrows which explode on impact, thus keeping you from recovering them. All in all, the bow is most useful in stealth and has very limited use on loud jobs. Does that mean you shouldn't use it on loud though? No, not at all. There's nothing better than completing a loud overkill or deathwish heist while just using the bow. It's A LOT of fun to use and if you get good with it you'll feel like Legolas. And who doesn't want to feel like Legolas?<br><br>-Repeater 1874 (Winchester model 1873): The repeater is strangely enough classified as a sniper rifle, but payday has thrown realism out the window long ago, so I guess it doesn't really matter. When compared to the other sniper rifles it's really quite a unique case. Damage wise, it's right in between the R93 and the Rattlesnake, but the rest of it's stats are either significantly better\/worse than those of the other sniper rifles. Firstly, it boast an amazing 15 bullet capacity, that's 5 more than the Rattlesnake. This incredible capacity comes with a trade-off however, as every round has to be individually loaded, similar to some shotguns. It's accuracy is sub-par for a sniper rifle, but this can be boosted by equipping the long range barrel. it's stability is nice, but who really needs stability for a sniper rifle? It's rate of fire is really high (for a sniper rifle), which makes it very forgiving. lastly, it comes equipped with iron sights and only has one option for a scope. All these attributes  combined make for a very good close- to medium-range sniper.<br><br>-Dynamite: This is really nothing more than a re-skin for the grenade. The only difference between dynamite and grenades is that dynamite can't be detonated by enemy gunfire, making it slightly safer to use. Apart from that there's really nothing special about dynamite. And boom goes the dynamite!<br><br>This pack also adds 4 new melee weapons: You're mine (a branding iron), Scalper tomahawk, Arkansas toothpick and the Gold fever (a pickaxe). Honestly, who even really cares about melee weapons? in my book there are really only three reasons to use a certain melee weapon: high damage\/knockdown, high concealment or pure trolling. This pack doesn't really offer much in any of these categories except maybe the branding iron, which can be quite trolly. My personal favorite is the &quot;gold fever&quot; as it allows me to start smashing up the walls and floor in between waves while screaming: There's gold in those mountains!<br><br>onto the masks. This pack, once again adds 4 masks. one will make you look like an Apache, one will make you look like a dead bull, one will pack you look like a bad super hero and the last will make you look like a bad-\u2665\u2665\u2665 old timey bank robber. <br><br>In the end, you really don't *need* this pack, as it doesn't contain any real P2W weapons like the Thanatos. But if you're into stuff from the old west or like skill-based weapons, you'll love this pack. Also it'll make you feel like Legolas and Revolver Ocelot, which is always a good thing. This pack is just a lot of fun.<br><br>TL;DR It's fun, but not for everyone.
