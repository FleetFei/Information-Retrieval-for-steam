Bronze is unlocked by just playing through the story while the other two difficulties are unlocked by discovering creatures and progressing. <br><br>Before you start a stage, you can see the enemies you\u2019ll expect to encounter. If you\u2019ve seen them already, the game will indicate whether or not you\u2019ve researched one with a little star. If you have yet to encounter the creature in question, it remains a vague outline until you\u2019ve actually faced it in battle. It\u2019s important to note that the order in which you place your units\/skills matters. More on this is just a bit.<br><br>Battles consist of a single lane of your units versus the enemy units. You can pan the camera from left to right by hovering at the edge of the screen or by dragging the bar at the bottom that represents your field of view on the mini map. There are two types of resources used in combat: energy and gold. Gold is used to build rooms and units while energy is used to activate your skills. Both regenerate over time except for in special cases related to the story.<br>From the outset you are only able to create one unit, linked to the Q hotkey. By building additions to your base (B), you are able to create more units. There are three buildings available to you\u2014Workshop, Hatchery, and Den\u2014which are unlocked throughout your progression. Each allows a certain type of unit\u2014robotic, birds and bugs, and mammals respectively\u2014and also different skills. <br><br>Units become available to you in the order that you chose them. The size of the units\u2019 displayed portrait also factor into when you\u2019ll be able to use it: if the unit takes up one tile, you only have to utilize the build menu once; if it takes up two tiles, you\u2019ll have to build both rooms before the unit becomes available. Skills become available to you in the exact same way. Every additional unit you unlock is assigned to a different hotkey in order (q, w, e, r, t\u2014and so on, beginning again at a). You summon your units and they march across the field until they meet a destructible obstacle or another unit. Thus, the first minute or so of battle becomes a balancing game between lowering the cost of your units and maintaining enough units to stop the enemy from destroying your base.<br><br>Battle is won after you\u2019ve pushed the enemy forces back to their base and destroy it.
