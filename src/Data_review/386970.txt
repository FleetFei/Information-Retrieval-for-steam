After finishing the game, I was convinced that the main sci-fi focus was the former, but I can't forget how I wholeheartedly believed in the beginning it was about digitizing memories and its villainous uses and later felt a little betrayed. There was also a little &quot;inequality by genetic difference&quot; mixed in there, maybe it was part of the writer's inspiration. So, if you are looking for a game all about digitizing memories, this is not for you.<br><br>Although I liked them all personality wise, the side characters' purpose and design were of questionable quality. The biggest problems were Akane and Ema. Why were they partially cosplaying (real weapons)? What was this organization that they were in? Read on and plot holes linked to those two keeps appearing. The police officers group didn't make much of a sound and pretty sure was just there to pad for the development of the main characters. Loved Haruna's CV though; the fact that she looks like Misaka from Railgun is probably not a coincidence heh. The miko took me out of immersion as well. It felt like she was using a little knife chipping away at the 4th wall. However, I did enjoy Aoi's role, she was nicely done. Hopefully these plot holes will be addressed in sequels.<br><br>Near the ending, I was praying to the lord to not strike down and give me a deus ex machina, and BAM it happened, and now I am still troubled over whether or not it was done well. It provided a definite wrap for the ending, and it served some purpose in reinforcing central themes, but it did seem out of place.<br><br>WHY was there a dubstep track in the soundtracks? I loved every other track EXCEPT the dubstep one. Thank goodness it only came up during short action sequences. Out of everything, this was the most immersion breaking thing in the whole game.<br><br>The spellchecking team did not do their job perfectly. Probably rushed. C'mon guys, I paid over the listed price, and I didn't get a game with perfect spelling?<br><br>The engine was not friendly to me. First, I wanted to change the text box visibility, but somehow changing it made it permanently invisible (no box, white text against bright BG), so I just defaulted it and said screw it. Second, I don't know how, but clicking on the tips in some specific fashion locks the game up, and I had to reload previous saves multiple times. Plus there was also the time the text in the save\/load screen strangely appeared the reading log. <br><br><br>Onto the good points so let's turn that frawn now. Art 10\/10, loved it. Day\/Night environment CGs are a requirement in current day VNs, and this was wonderfully done. CGs at key points in the story also greatly induced immersion. Music 9\/10. I will probably use the OST as BGM while I'm doing school work now. Voice acting, 9.5\/10, only CV I didn't like was Sigma's, whose voice I found lacked enthusiasm. Other than that everyone was on point the entire way through.<br><br>One thing I want to especially praise is how the VN makes its branching choices tied tightly to the central theme. For mechanics I like the timer on the branches, making it even more of a test on how well you are reading and how well you remember. The choices in this game made me think back more than other VNs have, made me question why I made the correct or wrong decisions. You should quicksave, but make sure you know that making the correct choice is Not the point.<br><br>I enjoyed the storytelling and the main characters' development. I cared about all the characters, and the characters cared about each other.
