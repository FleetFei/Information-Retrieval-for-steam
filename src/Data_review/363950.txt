However, filling the Steel Boots of these epic titles is far from an easy task and drowning in a Lake Hylia is more then likely if one doesn't tread carefully.<br><br>From the onset Windscape is doing rather well for itself, currently in its early development stage. The moment you load up into the game your eyes are overwhelmed by the bright colorful world. Aesthetically, Windscape aims to have simple graphics, reminiscent of the classic Ocarina of Time but with more of clean crisp look. Needless to say, but the game is extremely polygonal, The people, monsters, caves, mountains, clouds, everything is polygons. At first this is going to take some adjustment, at least for me it did. Once you do adjust though you realize how beautiful the game looks with its vibrant tones.<br><br>Deeper areas of the Early Access show the versatility of this art style, as everything goes from bright and cheery to dark and sinister. I personally can't wait to see what future areas are going to be like and how far this style can go. Other aspects shine as well, such as level design, use of lighting and particle effects. The UI needs improvement, house designs become repetitive, and other little things here and there.<br><br>Another highlight we should talk about is the music of Windscape. As soon as you embark on your adventure you are greeted by a soundtrack full of levity and excitement. I could just sit there and enjoy the music for quite a bit. In short, the developer has nailed the overall feeling of the game and just needs to keep moving forward and keep things interesting.<br><br>Touching upon those Elder Scroll elements, we have the first person combat and crafting. Windscape takes the scheme of other survival crafting titles and simplifies most of it. Unlike in Zelda, here you can craft your own weapons and tools to use in combat. As of right now there are spells, bows, blunt weapons, and sharp weapons, all corresponding with a different damage stat.<br><br>How this is going to be expanded upon will be interesting to see, as there is no hunger bar or really any other survival mechanics outside of your health bar. While crafting in the game is simple, it ended up feeling a little bit like a chore for me. It especially sapped the joy out of opening huge treasure chests. which usually just contained materials to craft something or meat. So much meat. Crafting materials tend to pile up.<br><br>The reason it felt like a chore is because the combat is rather simple and easy to exploit.(Of course that will likely change) Using saving totems automatically refills your health, so items such as food or health potions only become necessary on rare occasion. The resistance to damage type potions, I never used once, despite them being a huge chunk of crafting. The crafting system though does have plenty of room to grow in good ways, from crafting armor, shields, and magic accessories. The only problem is going to be making it fun and exciting.<br><br>Combat is great but does suffer from quite a few bugs, AI randomly going brain dead and standing still, little variation in tactic beyond just charging up a shot, hitting and backing off. It becomes a very much rinse and repeat scenario. Situational awareness is an issue with no HUD as well. Boss fights on the other hand, when they do work, feel like epic show downs. You gotta move fast and play smart as they have far superior health and damage.<br><br>With currently about two whole areas to explore, you will get about four hours out of the game. That is stretching it if you want to craft everything. Obviously more content is on the way with a plethora of changes. I can see why people are excited about Windscape, the game does have plenty of potential, but it needs to build on itself in a pretty massive epic way. There are areas that need some tender loving care such as combat, bugs, AI. Ideas that can bring more life to the game such as voice acting, personal housing, and what I loved most of all about Ocarina of Time mini-games and secrets around every corner!<br><br>At $16.99 currently, keep in mind purchasing this title is mostly to SUPPORT THE DEVELOPER, as there is hopefully way more content coming. explored everything this game has to offer so far, and let me say this game has ALOT of potential. I loved what I did play, I experienced very minor glitches, riveting battle, a beatuifully crafted soundtrack, secrets arcoss the land, and a decent storyline. I will go over the Pros and Cons of this game and give my general opinion.<br><br>Pros:<br><br>Beautiful Graphics - Everything looked great for cel shading and it ran very smooth at 60fps. I loved the way leaves fell from trees, the effects when mining rocks or chopping trees, the poofs of smoke monsters disappear into. Visually stunning land scapes and trees are most of the game so far, however there are some caves and mines that looked great as well. Crystal glowing effects looked good and really added to the depth of exploring. The only bad part about the graphics is the water. No sound effects when you're walking in it, it is static and never moves, and it seems shoed in. <br><br>Soundtrack - This game has the music. Evergreen island has a beautiful overworld theme, however it becomes repetitive after awhile. I wish it would change depending on what section of the world you were in. Sky Harbor's theme has to be my favorite. It sounds like a lively town and I enjoyed exploring it with NPCs walking around, even if there wasn't much to do.<br><br>Sound Effects - They were good, but could have been better. I wish that when you harvested purple herbs it didn't sound like a hachet going to town on the leaves. It feels out of place, some of the sword hits on Rat Bandits as well felt a little weaker. The web slinging spiders sounded great, and I was disgusted fighting them. The honey pots were awesome, a lively hive awaits perfect honey! <br><br>Rewarding Exploration - There were times where I set off in a direction just to see what something was. I found a cave hidden in the far corner of the map with it's own story to tell, a man living in a tree fearful of his angry wife, and a mysterious shrine shrouded in magic. I felt like the areas didn't feel reused or tacked in with a couple different rooms, each felt unique with it's own story to tell. I would add more things around the world, a quick 15 minute swoop of the 2nd area in the game and I had completely gathered all the crafting materials.<br><br>Cons:<br><br>Weapons - There is a lack of weaponry, and after you get the bow the game becomes very easy. I would recommend not making a bow to have a real challenge. The one boss fight in the game is a breeze with the bow, halfway through I adjusted to the sword to have a real challenge. It was easy, but better than the bow. I wish there were more weapons for crafting, and that iron was easier to get as the iron sword and bow are really over powered. <br><br>AI - This needs work. Enemies would rush towards me if I was miles away. It felt as if their detection rating was too far, and that they would charge you from across the map. Sometimes I would be off exploring and the spider I saw across the hill far away from me would come to tussle. Most of the time they would miss their attacks as well if I slowly strafed to the right or left. Combat felt dull at times.<br><br>Crafting Materials - After you start exploring, you'll notice that it's just the same 5 or 6 things scarcely scattered across the land. The worst part is once you gather all of these items, you never really use them. Dry Wood, Ore (which is just copper and a rare ore Pallas that only has 3 on the map), and logs. Cutting down trees takes forever, and it only rewards you with four logs no matter the size of the tree. 4 logs is 1 charcoal bag, and the conversion rate is slow and grindy to get. There are only two tools, a picaxe and an axe. Most of the materials either sat in my inventory or were sold to buy more arrows. I never felt the need for potions as the game never challenged me enough to make them useful. There is only one cooking recipe in the game and it feels way too overpowered. I could eat a soup at 50 hp and go full, whilst still taking combat damage during the healing process.<br><br>Collision\/Detection - Finally, the last piece of the review before my rating. If you take a sharp turn around a wall or NPC, you will start to move very slowly and occasionally freeze wherever you are standing. This happended very frequently. If you turn a corner too sharp, try to pass grey rocks, or go around NPCs, you will encounter this bug. This happens as well if you try to jump up something you're not suppose to or if you go near any water. It really took away from the games otherwise polished movement. It was very frustarting going through the mineshaft at the end as every corner felt like a trap. The hidden secret inside the mine as well that requires a bit of jumping\/platforming was much more difficult than it needed to be. There was a puzzle in the mine as well that I almost got stuck in. During the three color step puzzle, I was walking inbetween one of the steps and my character froze in between steps, I broke free after multiple jumps and walking backwards slowly.<br><br>Sometimes I would charge a power attack with the copper sword to strike at the bees with, but the attack would not register. I would point blank hit the bee, but no damage would be applied. It was frustrating charging an attack several times for it to miss. I know, just use normal attacks, but that would not help the developer polish this game more. It is not all bad, the bow and arrows really felt dynamic as the arrow has drop off the farther it flies, phyics in how much power it took to draw the bow and how far it was going, and damage at certain ranges. The arrow never missed no matter what part of the hurtbox I shot at.<br><br>Overall Rating - As I stated at the start of this review, this game has ALOT of potential. I really did enjoy what I played, and I really have high hopes for this game. For the 90 minutes that I played I find it is obvious that the developer has put care into this game and has created something I believe a lot of people are going to enjoy. When this product is finished I think it will be one of the better indie games for your buck on Steam. Despite a few problems during play, the game ran very smoothly.
