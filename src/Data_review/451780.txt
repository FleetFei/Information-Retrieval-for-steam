But I had so much fun that I have to recommend it.<br><br>The concept of the game is very interesting : it's a giant boss fight and not much else. There are a few mooks here and there but they all get one-shot easily. And contrary to other RPGs, the boss isn't waiting in his castle until you've grinded enough EXP to defeat him. The prize up for grabs?  The Great Overlord's seat.  After a bit of reading and\/or listening, you get some tutorials about the various options your character can do before God of Destruction (Trillion) will awaken.  There is a fatigue bar to watch for as you are doing your daily tasks, and you can run the risk of injury if this goes very high, penalizing you some.  If you put together three straight poor or excellent results, you will also get a certain bonus with further traininng.  One can always save during each day, but there are a lot of different activities you can choose between as you progress from day to day.  <br><br>The main tasks that are used to progress through each day will impact various stats and numbers based on performance, and each one has importance.  You can trade these stat bonuses for passive and active skills as well, actives costing some mana to use.  The idea is to find out which ones and which types of activities you want to prioritize, and when, so you can be powerful enough to win against Trillion before it destroys Underworld's core.  You don't have to defeat this all at once, and you can remove yourself from the battle, or attack as much as possible before it leaves the designated area.  Just inflicting enough damage on it as you go is a key factor.<br> <br>I like all the options and possibilities there are.  Too much to go over with Steam's 8k character limit, or whatever it is now, but the tutorial explains quite a bit in the game itself.  Most mechanics are either automatic or random, aside from when you actually fight something or move around at certain parts, which could definitely be a lot more, in my opinion.  That is my biggest contention with this game is that there is very very little actual activity to be done compared to other similar RPG games.  That being said, I do enjoy the fresh mix of varying concepts going on to keep things less boring and grindy than a lot of similar simulation style games I have played.  Nice tidbits of conversations and story.  Stats and abilities to juggle and spend points on.  Earning coins for entrance into a little dungeon to fight baddies and gather treausure in, and more!<br><br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Pros<\/div><br>A pro I always mention in a review, or con, is controller support. I like their outfits, or in many cases, lack of, and the interaction and dialog is engaging.  Some of the voicing can be little bit over the top, but all in all they are tolerable for me to listen to.<br><br>I like all the random events that take place as you go.  There is a lot of reading and\/or listening throughout to help with some of the mundane daily grind.  You may be presented with a choice to make that can make an impact on your game, like new skills for instance, some you can't get any other way.<br><br>The random gifts you can give the Overlord are fun, and it's nice to see what you will get each day when you use the vending machine, or whenever you check.<br><br>This game is very unique in my opinion.  The combination of types of gameplay elements is refreshing.  <br><br>While it seems a little bit heavy on the simulation and strategy preparation as the days tick down to boss time, the addition of various random character interactions, as well as other optional events break up a lot of the monotony.<br><br>There are multiple endings in the game, which is always fun.  There is a bit of carry over into a NG+ mode.<br><br>While difficult, things are not always what they seem.  NG+ will net you some decent gains to get a head start on the next run, and also you don't (and I doubt you could) finish this boss in one try, due to several factors.  Progression is key.  You get several attempts at it with an Overlord though.<br><br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Cons<\/div><br>A bit of a grind, and the things you work on each day can get repetitive.<br><br>I had trouble with the battle mechanics at first, and I don't like having to change the camera angles to efficiently walk diagonally.  You won't do much grid walking on this game, but it is a vital skill for battles, and the turn-based dungeon.<br><br>I don't like everything moving each time I move.  One thing in particular bugged me: the easiest thing to do sometimes was to attack at the empty space in front of me when an enemy was one square away from a regular attack just so they would enter the grid area so I could hit them first.  I would have to take the first hit without doing this, generally.  AI is predictable.<br><br>A couple of the moves could use some tweaking.  Most are not very useful aside from certain situations, and others make you very overpowered with very little effort.  Experimentation or a little researching can significantly change the difficulty of the overall game.<br><br>I would have liked being a little bit more active as far as moving around and doing things in a traditional RPG sense.  There is a little bit too much focus on a daily routine, and making choices on that daily activity.  Not entirely bad the way it is done, but I would have enjoyed a bit more balance in a sense of third person movement, fighting, treasure hunting, etc.<br><br>The Steam page needs to show more actual gameplay related information.
