dumbest and most simple RPGs ever. It has nothing particularly special about it \u2013 it's made with RPG Maker, the story is laughably silly, and the gameplay is pretty much what you'd expect from a typical turn-based RPG. So, why am I recommending it? Well, read on and find out!<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">The Breakdown<\/div>The game \u201ccombines the atmosphere of Streets of Rage with the gameplay of Dragon Quest\u201d, according to the store page at least. The gameplay might be a little different than Dragon Quest, but the bit about Streets of Rage is pretty much spot on \u2013 this is clearly a silly game with a silly premise. I mean, the name says it all! <br><br>Perhaps one of the funniest parts about the story though is that the girl you're trying to rescue from the bad guys (ninjas, of course!) isn't actually the main character's girlfriend. Instead, she's just some random girl that the guy has a crush on. He decides to team up with 3 of 13 (4 of those are unlockables)  characters\/friends to stop the bad guys. Personally, I found the character designs for the party members to be pretty amusing. My party consisted of the main dude, a shady-looking \u201cdealer\u201d character, a little boy, and a frog. Yep, a frog. Each of the characters have amusing dialogue occasionally, yet what truly sets them apart from each other is their strategies during battles.<br><br>See, the gameplay of Girlfriend Rescue basically consists of a series of battles in a set area. The game throws you into a series of areas linked together by an overall location (like say, a line of train cars in a train). In these, you fight a variety of enemies, collect items by walking into objects (yeah, it's weird), and manage your party through equipping gear or using consumables like pizza, drugs, or \u201cred toro\u201d (Red Bull). The battles themselves are pretty much what you'd expect: turn-based attacks, skills, defending, and whatnot. Each character has their own special skills (sometimes with silly names) that sometimes apply status effects to enemies; certain weapons can also do this.<br><br>Now, a somewhat minor complaint I had is that it was a bit difficult to remember what most of these status effects actually do. There's a LOT of them \u2013 quite a bit more than most RPGs \u2013 and it became tough to try and remember what would happen if an enemy \u201cinfected\u201d one of my party members, made them \u201ctired\u201d, \u201cdoomed\u201d, etc. There's police officers NPCs that can be found every once in a while and explain about a few status effects, but it would be nice to have a help section or even just an item that gives little text blurbs about each status effect as a quick refesher.<br><br>Another part that bugged me about Girlfriend Rescue was how repetitive it got after a while. Each \u201cworld\u201d was basically the exact same \u2013 slap on a fresh new coat of paint for the scenery and enemies, fight a bunch of enemies in a mostly linear path, then rinse and repeat. There wasn't a lot of variety besides the pixel art, different songs for different worlds, enemy designs, and bosses. Speaking of bosses, they were pretty much incredibly easy, or at least all the ones that I fought were. I would have gotten farther, but.....well......<br><br>\u2026.the difficulty. This game has really strange difficulty spikes. At the beginning of the game, it recommends you play on Easy for a first playthrough, which I found a bit odd, so I decided to be rebellious and pick Normal. Now, for most of the game I had a good challenge, but it was nothing too tough. For the first few worlds, my characters rarely died at all. Yet, when I reached the 4th world, I kept dying to the enemies of that area, zombies. It wasn't too much, but it was definitely a noticeable difference in difficulty from the previous areas. The weird part though is that the 5th area was one of the easiest for me \u2013 I beat the boss (or rather 2 bosses) quite quickly and the enemies were similarly a breeze. However, the 6th location......oh jeez. It began simple enough: a town with snakes. So what? Well, it turned out to be extremely difficult, to the point that my characters were all either dying or nearly dying after every battle. Those damn snakes were crazy hard, and the weird part is that the difficulty spike sort of came out of nowhere \u2013 I had great equipment and a decent combo of powerful fighters and spell casters at that point. So, I don't really understand why that area was so incredibly challenging for pretty much no reason. Eh, oh well.<br><br>Anyway, why might I be recommending this game then? To be honest, I think a lot of it was how charming I found the game to be. The story made me laugh at its silliness, and pretty much everything about the game was dumb.
