overall effects and abilities. <br><br><b><u>GRAPHICS<\/u><\/b><br>The game has a nice overall design, however as far as graphical fidelity goes other games in the space genre (X series, Rogue Galaxy, etc..) will likely have impressed for their initial visuals more, however the ability to move the camera pretty much anywhere you want (simple pan, dolly, rotate features are present and easy to use..) you can frame the action anyway you want. A cockpit view for certain ships adds some immersion.<br><br>You will see the same missile patterns, explosions, and &quot;jump \/ warp&quot; animations, but the initial effects are all pretty cool. The game depicts desolate, never-ending space well, however their could be more planets and other outer space phenomena scattered around the sectors to appear more immersive... <br><br><b><u>GAMEPLAY<\/u><\/b><br>A rogue-like gameplay progression as you jump to subsequent sectors deciding whether to return to home base and salvage what you have acquired (and take a break to repair with your constantly-changing fleet..) or continue making jumps to different situations (akin to DarkStar One's sector jumping, arriving, being attacked immediately etc..) adds some panicked moments trying to take down an opposing fleet, as you wait for your abilities to level up. <br><br>To level abilities such as your fleet's shields or to unlock new fighter jets, repair freighters, and the ability to see a description of where you are next jumping to are all unlocked via &quot;X number of sector jumps&quot; so say I want higher shields on my capital ships, it will take 5 sector jumps to complete the ability unlock, to finish unlocking the ability I will have to endanger myself by doing a numer of sector jumps, and with no traditional save feature I may jump to a situation that wipes me out before the ability is finished researching.<br><br>The random encounters you have as you progress with subsequent jumps provide the opportunity to rescue other freighters and capital ships along the way, who join and expand your fleet.<br><br><b><u>BAD STUFF<\/u><\/b><br>The capital ships \/ corvettes \/ stations all move fairly clunkily it seems, beyond making sweeping moves toward their waypoints. Some enemy ships snag on one another goofily (it seems to be the same model that it happens to..) and then neither is able to move anymore so you can wipe them both out fairly easily. Suffice to say the game has its share of jankiness.   <br><br>I still find combat in the game a bit confusing overall as to whether my ships are actually attacking whom I'm telling them to, beyond a vague &quot;Direct (Unit) Fire&quot; option on each of your constructed fighter jets, there isn't much indication that ships are going where you've told them to beyond watching if the swarm of fighters approaches the other capital ships etc. Though I haven't played the game a ton, so maybe I am missing an indicator. <br><br>Some voice acting would have been nice, as it is present in the studio's other title Battle of Sol so its absence here is a bit strange. The UI is simple and effective.<br><br><b><u>OVERALL<\/u><\/b><br>Overall, for all the game offers and with its modest pricepoint, it maintains my interest for longer than many other space games. What is present may be a bit thin on complexity and the graphics ~could~ use a little more polish or higher rez\/more effects \/lights etc... what is present is quality entertainment, especially if you are a fan of deep space sci fi \/ capital ship battles and the like.<br><br>I picked up this game on sale for only 1.99 after having played its other separate \/ side game the Battle of Sol ( a game in the same universe I also recommend but with strictly space flight combat, no RTS, and chapter-based storylines..) . After finding the storyline behind Battle of Sol and the overall gameplay to be enjoyable I figured I would give this one a try as well. At first after playing briefly I feared the game would be way too complex and heavy into tutorials and micro managey \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665t that often plagues other &quot;Space Strategy&quot; games for me, as I prefer them to be a little more straight-forward ala Colony Wars\/Rogue Galaxy\/DarkStar One etc...<br><br>However, as I'm not often a big fan of RTS games I found this game to have the right pacing between letting the battles happen and just admiring the visuals of the units themselves. In many other RTS games I find I spend a ton of time zoomed way too far out just staring at bland icons meant to represent my massive fleet moving through space. Thankfully this game includes simple camera controls with mouse and keyboard that allow you to navigate the action and repair your capital ships as needed. Controller support seems limited and like a wack way to play this particular game, which is more a result of the genre than by bad programming from the developers per se.
