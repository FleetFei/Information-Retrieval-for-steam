must admit that I enjoy the variety offered by this Sega alternative.  Sonic &amp; All-Stars Racing Transformed offers customizable vehicles, three different vehicle modes for different terrains, and a large variety of racing modes.  The solo campaign features the expected World Tour, Grand Prix, and Time Attack modes.  Grand Prix and Time Attack are no different than a Mario Kart game, but World Tour has much variety. (Note, the last Mario Kart I played was Mario Kart Wii, so it is possible Mario Kart has since started offering this variety as well.)<br><br>All-Star Racing Transformed offers several different types of game modes in the World Tour campaign:<br><br>* Standard Race - 3 laps, use weapons to attack other racers<br>* Battle Race - at most 3 laps, use weapons to attack other racers, 3 hits eliminates a racer<br>* Boost Race - 3 laps, no weapons, lots of boost pads<br>* Boost Challenge - Race through a number of checkpoints before time runs out, time only counts down when you are NOT boosting<br>* Ring Challenge - Fly through a ring course before time runs out<br>* Traffic Attack - dodge through cars to reach checkpoints before time runs out<br>* Drift Challenge - drift through checkpoints along a set path, time only increases when you drift correctly<br>* Versus - 1v1 race, stay in the lead for a set amount of time or get far enough ahead to eliminate the opponent<br>* Sprint - solo race, beat the lap time of a ghost<br>* Pursuit - attack a massive tank that stays in front of you, dodge the tank's attacks, kill it before it kills you<br><br>Multiplayer retains only the Race, Battle Race, and Boost Race game modes, but it also offers two additional modes exclusive to multiplayer games:<br><br>* Battle Arena - drive around on a small open map using weapons to attack opponents, 3 hits eliminates a player<br>* Capture the Chao - pick up a Chao creature and bring it to a dropoff area to score, attacking the player with the Chao will cause the Chao to drop and another player can pick it up<br><br><br>Living up to the title, race cars will transform on different levels to account for changes in the course.  Levels feature a mixture of land, sea, and air racing, so you may find yourself driving a car, boat, or plane as you race to the finish.  If cars transforming wasn't enough, the race tracks will also change between laps, so you may find yourself flying in lap 3 even though you used a boat in laps 1 and 2.<br><br>In addition to the transformations, each driver also has several mods you can apply to the vehicle.  Mods are unlocked by leveling up a driver.  Drivers earn experience for each event they complete regardless of whether or not you win or lose, though you do get much more experience for winning.  The mods may affect each driver's vehicle differently, but in general they take a point away from one category and add it to a different category.  The mods are:<br><br>* Standard - default stats<br>* Balanced - usually takes a point off the highest stat and gives it to the lowest stat<br>* Handling - takes a point off Speed and adds it to Handling<br>* Acceleration - takes a point off Boost and adds it to Acceleration<br>* Speed - takes a point off Handling and adds it to Speed<br>* Boost - takes a point off Acceleration and adds it to Boost<br>* Console Mod - generally maxes out Speed and\/or Boost at the expense of other stats (this mod must be unlocked in the World Tour campaign rather than by leveling up)<br><br><br>But how does the game actually play? Is it fun?<br><br>Short answer: YES!<br><br>Long answer:<br>It will take you a bit of time to get used to this game.  There are several tricks you'll need to learn to help you race faster, such as knowing how to get the starting boost and when to perform aerial acrobatics, but you will quickly get the hang of things.  The car is by far the easiest vehicle mode to control.  The boat and plane take a bit of getting used to, but none are terribly frustrating after a bit of practice.  There are also plenty of characters with different stats, so you'll certainly be able to find something that suits your play style.  (In fact, there's more than enough characters, many of which you will likely never use.)<br><br>I enjoyed playing my way through the campaign.  The difficulty curve increased at about the same rate as my skill, so the levels got harder as I got better.  There were a handful of tracks I had to play several times, but for the most part I was able to complete each level in 4 tries or less.<br><br>Multiplayer is where this game really shines, assuming you have friends to play with.  The game supports 4 player local play and up to 10 players online, which can be a mix of local and network.  The silliness and chaos of casual races with friends is great, but there is also a small competitive community if you're into that instead.<br><br><br>One point of complaint is that the game artificially increases the game time by trapping you in non-skippable menus after each race.  It takes 35 seconds to restart a level after you fail, 55 if you want to change characters or difficulty.  You will certainly be button mashing that :XBA: button to get through these screens as fast as possible, but this still amounts to over an hour of your life wasted in menus if you play through the campaign.<br><br>Another point of complaint is that the ability to fully customize races is missing.  I'd like the ability to specify which weapons are available, or change the rules of the race.  Maybe put on low gravity, faster cars, or some other crazy mix of custom rules.<br><br><br><br>My Game Completion info:<br>I beat all World Tour levels on Hard difficulty.  This unlocked a 6th cup and Expert mode.  I then beat all the levels in the 6th cup on Hard except the last level (which requires you to beat the entire game on Expert in order to unlock. No thanks!  I've had enough of this game artificially increasing game time.)<br><br>I also beat the first 5 Grand Prix cups on Hard difficulty.
