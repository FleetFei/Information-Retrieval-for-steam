So be prepared for a top down third person view most of the time.  What makes it most enjoyable are the bawdy humor in the dialog and the lyrics to the songs.  <br><br>Our hero in the game, The Bard, is not really a hero; but a scounderal given to snarky comments and possessed of an ability to get himself into trouble. After that you'll have an opportunity to really wreak mayhem and get yourself into some interesting situations.  The game is full of cliche's on purpose and they spice it up instead of detract from game-play.  And of course there are always complications along the way to add dimension to the game.<br><br>The screen resolution choices are based on what was available at release time.  Don't fuss over them too much.  Pick the largest resolution with the same ratio as your screen and it ought to look fine anyway. It seems more like they just lost budget for it rather than intentionally added filler, however.<br>- Save points. This was particularly annoying as I never like this game mechanism. Maybe you will. I've been spoiled by save-anywhere games... <br>- Limited replayability. You may want to delve back in to try a different main weapon, but not likely. The collectibles you miss aren't game-breakingly important. It's a fairly linear game. Difficulty levels involve reduced save points (the most annoying &quot;challenge&quot;) and noticeably fewer starting points to invest in your attributes.
