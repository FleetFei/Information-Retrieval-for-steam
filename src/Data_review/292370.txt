You journey to the frontier to fight the DarkEnd, to progress through the DarkEnd you must complete individual floors and leave through the portal but in order to use the portal you need a key, the keys can be found in chests or dropped from enemies and the floor boss, through each level you will encounter enemies and rare enemies. As expected the rarer enemies are well rarer and will put up more of a fight and give you more exp as a reward for killing them. You should not underestimate rare enemies they can be deadly and in the event your party dies you will have a game over and have to start from the last time you saved and that is only at the frontier, don\u2019t lose all your progress out of pride. Do not fear you do not have to do the whole game in 1 run there are serpent gates which act as a checkpoint once activated you can then travel back to the point you left from the frontier after resupplying of course. The levels you progress through may change if you die and start them again they can change in that they have a boss rather than a chest or they can change all together. The game features several very catchy songs as all good RPG\u2019s should. Each character can act each turn at the beginning you are given the option to fight or flee, each character can attack, defend, use magic or use a special the magic attacks use mp and the special attacks use Ep which is generated by attacking and getting hit, not all characters have a special to start with, magic and other abilities can be unlocked using the upgrade system which involves choosing to kindle various crystals. Each floor will differ if you have to repeat it such as loot and if a key spawns in a chest or you have to fight the boss for it, when you characters health is low you can use a potion or a heal spell. All spells and specials have a cool down so be sure to time their uses correctly and choose the right target an enemy with a fire weakness might well die in 1 hit to a fireball and an enemy with a fire resistance almost certainly will not. Spells initially have a warm up period as well meaning at the start you ca\u2019t cast a fireball and destroy the enemy turn 1 but as you upgrade your spells the warm up will no longer be required.<br><br>Dying in DarkEnd can be very frustrating especially if you are having a good run maybe you found some good loot and then you got cocky and a rare enemy destroyed you, but if you didn\u2019t want a challenge you should have chosen a lower difficulty. Dying and retrying is how you learn anyway, although it can be sad to lose a good run it just encourages you to try again and not make the same mistakes.<br><br>The combat of the game can be very strategic with you having to time your magic and special attacks to when they are best used for example an attack that provides a stun effect might keep you alive long enough to use a magic that was on cool down next turn.<br><br>The DarkEnd is split into floors of 5 on the 5th floor you will encounter a boss and also get some story, after the set of 5 floors you will then enter a new area with new music, new enemies(far harder) etc.<br><br>The art style of the game is just lovely with the majority of the game having the usual 2D RPG look but during tutorials and cut scenes the looks of the characters truly show the time taken to create.<br><br>Overall from playing DarkEnd I highly recommend it to any 2D RPG fans it has a beautiful look, charming characters, good combat and a catchy soundtrack what more do you need?<br><br>The only negative is the game has a small resolution meaning if you are a graphics snob this probably isn\u2019t for you.<br><br>I rate this game highly and
