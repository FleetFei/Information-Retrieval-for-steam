However, the end of the demo is what definitely got my attention. Many of you who tried it already know what I am about to talk about: the boss fight. Got to see the game form as time went on. And its now the time that I finally review it. How exciting.<br><br>I'll start off and say that this is not a tech demo. That is very important. When I scroll through all the VR titles in my library, more than half are capturing the term  &quot;Experience&quot; rather than full game. Not to say there aren't full games out there for VR right now. There are a few. But I feel this is important to at least mention this is a full campaign game in every way.<br><br>Before I go into the actual game itself, I feel it is super important to talk about a few pre-game features. Or as you might call: the main menu. In most VR games, you are even lucky to have a menu that lets you change graphic settings... Here, you are given a lot. I can tell the developers worked a lot on this. You have things such as graphic settings, locomotion comfort settings, and even a way to change the color of keypads if you are inflicted with colorblindness. Very cool. You just heard me mention locomotion settings. I know this is super important to a lot of people who wear play VR experiences. Fret not, this game supports both artificial and teleportation methods. It is to be noted, however, that if you have the weakest stomach, there is a tiny room in the Tutorial mode of the game that you cant use the teleport tool.<br><br>Anywho, lets actually get into the game and talk about mechanics. Being as it is a full game, it doesn't capture a single mechanic it captures quite a couple. The genre I would describe it, would be &quot;Shooter with some puzzle elements.&quot; Each stage gives you a small mechanic you will quickly learn to use to continue through the game. They go from making sure you use a teleport machine at the right time, to figuring out how to take down a boss with your new aquired weapons. Stuff like that.  The puzzles aren't too difficult. In fact, I had an easier time figuring out the puzzles than I had actually completing them. But I will get back to that later. I want to talk about boss fights.<br><br>So I already praised the demo for having a fantastic boss fight. It showed a huge amount of potential for me. This game has an updated version of that terrifying boss fight, and more. There were at least 4 boss fights I can remember. They are all fun and require some handling of the new mechanic or weapon you were given in that stage. One of the boss fights is even just entirely platforming. And teleporting at the right time... or you drown. These boss fights are probably the highlight of the game. You play the game because you want to get to the next one. But are the bosses meaningful to the story? Well, I would say no.<br><br>So let me explain, the game clearly has a story in it somewhere. There are abandoned logs and voice recordings that piece some things all together. But they feel kind of clunky. You don't understand too much why you were teleported into the facility at the beginning, well, at least until the end. And even the ending &quot;cutscene&quot; feels out of place. But the light story doesn't take away from the game too much. I never cared where the monsters came from or why I am in this giant laboratory. I just wanted to keep going further. But some things would keep trying to stop me.<br><br>I mentioned earlier in this review that I had an easy time figuring out what I am supposed to do in the puzzle sequences of each level. The game is intuitive enough for the average user, who can complete a game such as Portal, to beat. However, I cannot hide the fact there small bugs may pop up in a few places along your journey. I've had bugs such as getting stuck in a teleport loop, clipping through areas, and boss fights resetting without me actually dying. It is strange. Most of these bugs did allow me to keep playing once I figured out how to deal with them. But I cant deny there is at least one bug I ran into that forced me to restart the game.
