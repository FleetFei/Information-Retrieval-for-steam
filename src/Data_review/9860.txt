If you enjoy the Riddick movies and don't feel like waiting until 2013 for the new installment in Vin &quot;I put the fury in Furyan&quot; Diesel's brutal sci-fi anti-hero saga, you'll enjoy this double pack of Butcher Bay and Dark Athena. If you enjoy innovative, intelligent, visceral, criminally (ha ha) satisfying first person games, you will also enjoy these games. If you're a fan of both, then these games might just shoot to the top of your list of best damn games ever, because they really are some of the best experiences you can have in gaming.<br><br>The remote prison of the first game will make you forget about Crematoria from The Chronicles of Riddick movie. If you always thought that place was lame (except for the planet itself and the sun with boundary issues) and the bunch of vaguely Russian prison guards were goofy screwups who couldn't keep a dead bird from escaping an open cage, then you will be blown away by the gritty, harsh, no-holds-barred world of Butcher Bay. It is a believable, effective vision of what sort of horrors one might expect from dictatorial incarceration in the draconian future of anarchic deep space, not only for all those horrors, but also for the portrayal of Riddick's fellow inmates, who are harsh and morally flexible as you might imagine but also very familiar and &quot;down to earth&quot;, if there can be such a thing in Riddick's unforgiving world. It elicits nothing so much as the thought that things may change, mankind may colonize the stars, but at the bottom, in the dark and desperation, we are all still animals. It is a masterpiece of storytelling and will stick with you long after you've completed the game.<br><br>Playing as Riddick is incredibly satisfying, challenging and rewarding. The stealth and shooting gameplay has a bit of a learning curve, due to the need for timing in Riddick's melee attacks and getting used to the way he moves, in addition to the AI being mostly smarter than other FPS games. Riddick doesn't level up or anything, because he doesn't need to - he's already Riddick. Instead, the game asks YOU to level up by getting better using Riddick's formidable skills and strength and thinking like a predator, and you have ample opportunities to do just that. Eventually you will find yourself grinning after taking out an entire group of gun-toting thugs with just a knife, each of them having no idea that the rest of their team is already dead. There are some times where the game allows you to go Rambo with firearms and it's like hitting a wide open road after tooling around in downtown gridlock: exhilirating! But when you do go back to the shadows and the shivs, it's never a disappointment, due to it being so much fun to engage in Riddick's brand of violence.<br><br>What sets these games apart the most, however, is perhaps exemplified best by the Dark Athena mercenaries and the technology that fuels their &quot;recruitment&quot; methods. No spoilers needed except to say that when you are introduced to the mercs, you may feel a little revulsion and possibly anger. I did, and while revenge may be a dish best served cold, you certainly can't say it's not filling. The story in these games is all about revenge, something at which Riddick excels, and at which the game devs excel at actually making you feel that revulsion and anger and the need to get even, as opposed to just giving you a bunch of targets to blow away helter-skelter.
