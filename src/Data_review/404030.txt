I picked it up somewhere else last year and got a Steam key when it crossed over. So I have a bit more time on this than is in my stats.<br><br>This is a pretty straightforward otome game with different routes, datable guys, friend-datable girls, day-to-day decisions and managing different attributes - it's also self-aware about the romatic-confession-at-the-end-of-school-term trope. In terms of sex, it's very tame. There's language to indicate these kids know what's up, but you're not getting much more than cleavage and abs. Sowwy. ;-)<br><br>Your protagonist (name and birthday of your choice) is a British girl going through her last year of UK high school at a new school, so she has to start from scratch making new friends. She does this in pretty short order with the prototype outgoing quasi-\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665 girl and the prototype shy-ish high-achieving girl. There are also a couple other girls that come along in time for friendship possibilities with pretty distinct personalities and stories (some of which don't become fleshed out until you get through a couple routes). As for the datable boys, you've got the prototype flirt, the sensitive musician, the jerk brain\/drama guy, the younger athlete from a nearby school and a young art teacher. While these guys fit common otome roles, they have enough twists to them to keep them somewhat interesting (said flirt is also a jock, the art teacher is also a model and tattoo artist). You go through the year day-by-day choosing different activities which get you points for different attributes which help your prospects with the guys. As you go on different routes, you can date on the weekends (which is also when you can buy clothes that certain people may like or not and also go places on your own), though if you've done well enough with a guy or girl, they may call you to go out while you're on school breaks. Along with all this, some routes have slight mini-games to test your memory (all routes have the one where you, that's right, are making pancakes).<br><br>There are a good number of routes, and a few of them can't be tried until almost halfway through, which makes them a bit challenging. The routes aren't as simple as racking up the points - certain decisions have to be made as well. While there are some repetitive aspects, I appreciate the variety of routes and what's needed to complete them. <br><br>As has already been discussed in some places here, the weakness on this game is visual. The character design is meant to be a riff on anime, though the shadows and body shapes will look really wonky to some. I think the look is pretty much the same as it has been through the years, with just a few tweeks here and there. Also the text is a straight and small Arial that people could actually have a hard time reading. But as you may have figured if you've gotten this far (thanks!), I don't care. I like these characters, I like the sense of humour they have and I like the variety of things they get to do. There are a couple other things (jobs! taking exams!) that I haven't covered through all this.
