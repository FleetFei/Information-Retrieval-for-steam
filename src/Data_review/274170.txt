If the free look had a longer reach (which was a special mask in the first one, but I'll get to that), this wouldn't be a problem, but I can't count the times I knew I didn't clear a level and I got blasted dead by some guy through four windows from the opposite side of the map. Difficult I have no problem with, I know when I messed up and I know what to correct on my next attempt.<br><br>Less open, free gameplay: while Hotline Miami had a roster of masks (that weren't all useful, but still), here you have a few characters that each have their own playstyle. In a way, this is good because it forces you to try different things, different ways of causing mayhem, but I think it limits your freedom in how to complete a level by doing so. For example, the Soldier character MUST wield a weapon, and has a non-throwable knife as a sidearm. As someone who relied heavily on melee, this is a nightmare and it would be cool if you had a &quot;vanilla&quot; choice as well. That way, you could remedy some shortcomings of the level design (for example, use the giraffe mask to lengthen the reach of free look).<br><br>Story...?: the first game didn't have much of a story, but to be fair, it didn't try to. This game attempts something akin to a story and while some elemenst are amusing, I ended up not caring for it because it lacks any particularly enthralling characters or plot lines, and honestly, it's a bit of a mess. It's not a bad thing, I just think it was a bit tacked on.<br><br>Although subjective, I think the soundtrack in this game is still a notch better (more mature, maybe) than the first game's offering (and that was already amazing, so that's saying something).<br><br>Overall, if you're not a fan of this series, this one won't win you over.
