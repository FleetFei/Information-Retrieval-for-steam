has finally arrived! But is it worth picking up, or should you avoid this all together?<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Story<\/div>Sitting in front of a desk and staring at a screen, you hear a door open. Your friend asks you to watch the room for him, making sure nothing happens while he's away for a short time. Turning back, you notice a button on the desk...but what does it do? Does it have something to do with the city being displayed on the screen? Do you even want to find out? That button does look tempting. One press won't hurt, right?<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay<\/div>Despite the title being Please, Don't Touch Anything 3D, you do the opposite for most of the game. While you can obey your friend and sit there till he comes back, it actively tempts you with the button. What's the fun with just sitting there if you can see what the button does?<br><br>The ultimate goal is to unlock all 30 endings, each characterized with a light beneath the table and a poster. One to tell you which numbered ending it was while the other to decorate your wall with posters. The red button, which is quickly revealed to be the main one, is the starting point. Other buttons, switches, and compartments are uncovered as progress is made. While there are endings that can easily be achieved, you will not go far without puzzling over various text and objects around you. In fact, one of the first puzzles have you find out a code that unlocks other interactions and puzzles. Once this first puzzle is solved, it quickly picks up as you learn where everything goes. While it does keep to it's roots, it has a whole room to work with.<br><br>But where are the solutions to the puzzles? What's stopping someone from constantly guessing? While there is no stopping someone from constantly guessing the solution, it becomes unlikely as each ending needs specific conditions. Some are number puzzles, and going through all the possibilities may take longer than figuring out the solution. The solution is all around you. All you have to do is determine where they could be and where to put them or how to decipher them. Some are in plain site, but others will become visible with interactions or seeing from a different perspective. Along with this, you must remember what you see, as it is often located in a different path. Each solution also opens up multiple endings that results from different interactions. <br><br>As pressing buttons is the main interaction, it is important to get the hitboxes right. For the most part it is, but sometimes it may register a different button than the one you meant to press. This doesn't happen much, but when you are going for achievements it can become annoying. If you do have trouble hitting the buttons, you can zoom in.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Sound<\/div>Throughout the game, music is constantly played in the background, characterized by a radio. Once you get used to the background music, it is easily forgettable until it changes. The sound effects, for the most part, is in the perfect middle. You can hear it and the background music without any problems. The distance between you and the sound is also taken into consideration. Taking away sound only to add to the atmosphere and the experience.<br><br>The voice-acting of the friend may be one of the things that need to have a higher volume. It seemed like it had a lower volume than the music and was almost drowned out by it.<br><br>The only sound effects that is not great is the phone's. Pressing the numbers can result in a high pitched beep, and hearing this over and over can be grating. The static when first picking up the phone can also be difficult to listen to. While the dial-tone and messages is not as bad, it does not help that it followed other displeasing sounds.<br><br>Please, Don't Touch Anything 3D does recommend wearing headphones during gameplay, but I would not. You can still hear everything without it, and it would be best to not have the high pitch phone closer.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Art Style<\/div><br>Two different art styles is most prominent in Please, Don't Touch Anything 3D. The realistic style of the room and the friend's arm is mostly seen and interacted with. Despite this, it is a simple room design, decorated to show it is an office but not too much to make it cluttered or showy. The other art style is the pixelated style that is displayed on the screen, which used to be the main art style in the original. This was a great choice, as it does let the player easily understand the town's state. If it did use a realistic style, it may have been more difficult to view and may not let the room nor the screen stand out.There are some other styles sprinkled in. Such as the posters having a paint feel and some endings using an exterior area to show the room is not there anymore.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Performance<\/div>Please, Don't Touch Anything 3D does generally run well. There are a few peaks where it does lag behind while it loads in dust or the ending you triggered. This is far and few between, and does not really affect the enjoyment. Only confusion on why you are suddenly staring at the ceiling at some points.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Replayability<\/div>The replayability lies within the achievements and the player. Did you get all the endings? Why not try to get all of them with no mistakes? This does get the player to find out the most effective way to achieve all endings. However, this can be tedious as the hitbox can press a different button, causing you to restart from the beginning. Unknown exceptions may or may not count towards the button count also.<br><br>Other achievements are interactions. Doing an action under a time limit, repeating an action, or finding hidden interactions. But this is all assuming the achievements do unlock, as I had two achievements that did not register until I reinstalled and did it over.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">3d to 2d?<\/div>Please, Don't Touch Anything 3D is a remake of the original that released around a year ago. While it does contain the same puzzles, it does not have the same solution. You would not be able to use the same numbers from the original to this version. New puzzles have also been added, along with a way to encounter the Red Baron minigame.<br><br>The new space provided with the 3D remake is not wasted. While it does have solutions and secrets all around, the room is also affected by the result of the endings. While there are some missed opportunities with some, it gives a different feel from the original.<br><br>The interaction between the friend and you may be weird, more so to those who played the original. You do not hear the reply the player gives to the friend in this version, where the original did. However, you do get a view of their arm. This may of been a way for the player to envision their own friend, while also saving their own time with rendering a whole body.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Overall<\/div>+ Simplicity<br>+ Multiple endings<br>+ The new space is used effectively<br>+ Plenty of secrets to see<br>+ Easter eggs towards movies<br><br>+\/- The silhouette left behind when picking up objects can be puzzling<br><br>- Price may be too steep for some<br>- The phone can make high pitched sounds<br>- Achievements have a possibility of not registering<br><br>While Please, Don't Touch Anything 3D is a remake, it is a whole different game. The space is used effectively, and does not seem it was wasted in anyway. The way it is designed seems like it had VR in mind. There is a current Oculus version on the publisher's site, but they are building support for other VR platforms for Steam.
