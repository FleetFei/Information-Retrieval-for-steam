Which made it average, but not great (mostly due to the large amount of AAA games that already use this formula over and over). The interesting character to character interactions and quips felt like they were cut in favour of the darker overtone and the story focus. <br><br>This brings us to the subject of the conquest ending. The darkest and edgiest of endings in a darker and edgier sequel.<br>So in conquest, the world once again needs saving, but now there's a great cost that one must pay to save the world. In most games, this generally means the thousands of faceless enemy mook that you killed to get there, or whatever. Not this time. A hefty price must be paid, as the characters are put in a situation where there are no winners. One of them must walk the path and suffer a fate worse than death. This is the incredible weight of emotional baggage involved in this tear-jerking emotional experience. The guilt from walking the path just to prevent the end of the world. Due to the setting, it tells a tale of a highly relatable, but painfully tragic story. (This is probably the reason why it's also regarded as the most traumatic ending. Frequently there are shows, movies or stories with downer endings that just feel like a cheap cliffhanger or a &quot;and then everyone died the end&quot;. Conquest sets itself apart by being different. There's resolution, but is it the one you expect? There's peace to the world at last, but will it hold? The world was saved, but does the end justify the means to do it? The beauty of the writing slowly opens up to you the further you get.<br><br>Conquest is though invoking. It makes you really think about the things we often take for granted or simply take at face value. What is saving the world? How tangible is saving the world? Does the world matter enough for you to go to ANY length to save it? Would you rather let the world end and enjoy the remainder of it with your friends? Why did you choose this path for the world? To prevent others from doing it? Or to ensure your own survival? Who's the real villian here? If you've lost everything you hold dear, does saving the world even matter anymore? The boundaries of gray morality and ethics are pushed in this ending to further make us question what's really going on. <br><br>Conquest is a masterpiece, a work of art, and a gaming experience like no other. It has changed my opinion on this game, and has shown that is far from mediocre. finally been released. And, as IFI's second PC port, I think they did a better job in terms of basics like optimization and even graphics.<br><br>Hyperdimension Neptunia Re;birth 2 is, much like Re;birth 1, a remake of the second game in the series, Mk2. Normally, RPGs nowadays require a run of the first game to understand some of the plot details without spoiling the first game, but because the first game for the PS3 was so bad, IF and CH decided to reboot the entire series, so Mk2\/Re;birth 2 is actually the first canonical game in the franchise.<br><br>Gameplay:<br><br>If you're familiar with the first Re;birth game, you'll feel right at home with the standard battle mechanics. You first do a basic attack, and then have up to 3 extra attacks with varying mechanics on their own. You have Rush, which deals multiple hits and is helpful for increasing your EXE Drive. Power is the heavy hitters of your standard attacks, and Break deals tons of damage to your Enemies Guard bar. When an enemies guard is broken (A Guard Break), they take more damage until your turns are up and they attack again. Partners still give you special attacks if your EXE Drive gauge is at or above the required amount along with their Lily Ranks. You also have Special Attacks that use up your SP. These attacks range from doing a good amount of damage, healing your allies, or Support by either raising your own and\/or your allies stats temporarily or lowering your foes stats. Finally, EXE Drives are your Super Moves. They deal tons of damage but you need to unlock them through the Story, level ups, or Events on the World Map via Chirper in the city menus and require 1 or more level of the EXE Gauge to be filled. You have 2 different types of EXE Drives: One that requires just the character using it and one that requires 2 or more characters who are partnered or out in battle along with everyone else as well as a specific Lily Rank number. New to Re;birth 2 is the fact that you now have 4 characters out on the field, giving you more options to bring the pain to your foes. Dungeons and the World Map are still the same, but Re;birth 2 was not built with the Jump button in mind. This makes the dungeons pretty linear and kinda boring, like Final Fantasy 13, but the battle system is still solid.<br><br>Finally, a new addition to the game is Stella's Dungeon. It's a real time, Mystery Dungeon\/Spelunker-like mini-quest of sorts that involves the personificiations of the remake's developer, Felistella, going through the dungeons you've unlocked via Plans or Story. You choose a dungeon, how many floors, equip Stella with a Weapon and Armor and Felis with an Orb and Cat Necklace to increase your stats before sending them off. They always start at Level 1 once you send them out, and they'll either fail or succeed after a certain amount of real time. Succeeding gives you new items, plans, and other goodies. Failing, much like Mystery Dungeon or Spelunker, results in all of your equipment you equipped for that dungeon to be lost. I cannot stress this enough: BUY THE HISTY'S RESCUE PLAN DLC! It makes Stella's Dungeon MUCH less annoying as, once you activiate the plans given through this DLC, halves the amount of time a dungeon requires and lets you keep all your equipment if you fail,<br><br>If you haven't played a Neptunia game since Mk2, the AP system is gone, meaning you don't have to think too much about what your next plan will be. I admit that I actually miss the AP mechanic as, while it did cause some rage from some pretty difficult fights, it made you think a bit more on what attacks and items to use at a given time. The battle system is fine as is, especially with Re;birth 3 adding more options, but the AP system added a bit more strategy to the battles. Otherwise, everything is the same.<br><br>Story:<br><br>Let's be honest, you're not really playing Neptunia for a &quot;gripping&quot; storyline. The story here, while decent, is not as good as Re;birth 1 and not even close to Victory\/Re;birth 3 or even V2 on the PS4. The Goddesses have been defeated by the leader of the Four Felons, an organization that promotes piracy to dwindle down the Four Nations Shares and gain enough shares to revive their own goddess, Arfoire. IF and Compa, sent by Histoire, go to the Gamindustri Graveyard to attempt to bring back the goddesses from a now 3 year sleep due to their shares now being next to nothing, but can only revive the Goddess Candidate of Planeptune, Nepgear. It's up to Nepgear to recruit the other Goddess Candidates of Lowee and Lastation (Poor Vert doesn't have a sister), gain back the shares and faith for each nation, and obtain the Mascots of each nation to bring back the Goddesses and defeat the Four Felons. <br><br>As a warning to those who complain about translations not being 1:1 to the Japanese audio or original text, this is not a faithful translation. It is a localization. I find that the writing itself is better than Mk2's, but if you want a 1:1 translation, you will not find it here. The Neptunia franchise is not one that honestly needs a literal translation. The games message is still the same and the comedy is still there. Either buy the Japanese Vita version, or wait until a fan translation or Japanese Text mod of sorts.<br><br>Audio:<br><br>The OST for Re;birth 2 and Mk2 are okay, but it didn't feel as good as Re;birth 1's original tracks or Victory's overall soundtrack. The most I can say is that the game has the best battle theme in the franchise. It gets your blood pumping, it's fast, and has a wonderful composition. As someone who's loved the English voices from the start, I have not played the games in Japanese, but from the anime, the Japanese cast is good. Neptune is a bit too high pitched for me for her age, and the Candidates are just....eh. The English dub track is a great dub track, and has at least 75% of the game fully voiced in English. Melissa Fahn is still the best Neptune voice, giving a zany but mature-like voice to Neptune whenever required. If you've played Skullgirls, you'll also recognize a lot of the amazing actresses featured in that game, such as Erin Fitzgerald being an outstanding Noire to this day, Christine Miller making Nepgear sound mature, but still young, innocent, and nerdy all at the same time, and Sarah Williams delivering great lines as Uni among all the other voice actors and actresses just being plain outstanding. If you have the chance, I highly recommend giving the English audio a try at least.<br><br>PC Port:<br><br>And last but not least, the PC Port as a whole. IFI still needs to work on some kinks like adding more graphic options other than resolution and Window type, make Borderless and Fullscreen different from each other, and better optimization for various cards, CPUs and RAM requirements, but I feel like this is a much better port than R;b1. The game stays at 60 FPS much more and doesn't lock up as much, and the character models look MUCH better in battles. The portraits during cutscenes are still low quality, but this is due to Tsunako drawing them for the Vita screen, not PC. Keep reporting to IFI if you have issues in the fourms and they should fix it quickly like R;b1. <br><br>Tl;dr I recommend buying Re;birth 2. While it's not as good as Re;birth 1 in some aspects, it's still a good game in its own right.
