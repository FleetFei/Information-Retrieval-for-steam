developer for the purpose of reviewing the game. Receiving the game for free has had no impact on my opinion of the game or its mechanics as I already have played this on mobile prior to this version.<\/div><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Introduction<\/div><br>I have always loved the tables that the Russian developer OOO Gameprom has made. Unfortunately, for a very long time, the only way to enjoy these tables was through playing on the tiny screen of a smartphone or on a tablet like an iPad. Thankfully, their best tables have been gathered together and this collection has landed on Steam allowing more people to play them. So the big questions that need answers are how did the move to Steam work out for the game and is it worth the price point that the developers have set?<br><br>Did the transfer to Steam go well for this game? Yes. The Steam version's menus and interface first of all are just as easy to use as the mobile menus and interface are but the two versions have differences. All of the tables also have quick guide pages that will give you the basics of what to do on each table which is great.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Game Mechanics<\/div><br>These tables feature something fun that most pinball tables do not have: the mission system. The table will give you a mission which is a shot that needs to be made or a place the ball has to go and if completed, gives you bonus points. These allow the player to always have something to aim for and try to achieve. There are also side pockets that will catch the ball if going down the left or right outlanes. After use, they must be reactivated by hitting drop targets to prevent future ball drains.<br><br>The physics that this title are known for are intact and great. While they might not be as robust as something like Pinball Arcade or Zen Pinball (Pinball FX2 on Steam), they are still silky smooth and very good. They are closer to how Zen Pinball handles physics with less realistic bounces on the flippers. Trying to use bounce-passes and similar techniques won't really work here because the physics system was developed prior to pinball games like Pinball Arcade, and since this title is aimed at a generally more casual audience, they have not changed the physics yet. It is worth mentioning that using the nudge feature does allow the player to perform bang backs to save the ball and it almost always works.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Visuals<\/div><br>The visuals are in general amazing. Tables like Da Vinci are stunning in their appearance and design. These tables are in general king in one area: creativity in design. Tables like the Arcade table with minigames to play while playing pinball are so original that it changes the whole game of what you're doing. The tables are visually striking and fun to watch with generally bright color schemes. There are a few different view options available with the flyover mode the best in my opinion.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Sound\/Voice<\/div><br>The sounds and voice acting are great. Most of them exceeded my expectations with only a couple being slightly less than expected. One of my favorite tables that I have played is surprisingly the Halloween table which has great voice and effects that are themed very well with the visuals.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Best Tables<\/div><br>The top tables that I have played that I think are stand out examples of their brilliant design are:<br><br>1. Da Vinci<br>2. Arcade<br>3. Red Planet<br>4. Halloween<br>5. Pirates<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Is It Worth It?<\/div><br>The fact that most people do not know about this title is that it is not a small collection. It has a whopping 15 tables with a very large variety of themes and designs. Obiviously some tables are lesser quality than the rest like Fantasy for example, but the others that are great make up for the shortcomings of the lesser few. The fact of the matter is that in my opinion, 15 tables all gathered together which are overall great with some tables even reaching awesome status, makes for a package that is well worth the price if you like\/love Pinball like me and you want more pinball on Steam. These tables are great additions to the pantheon of tables that are currently available on Steam for players.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Drawbacks?<\/div><br>The game is not perfect and does have some minor problems. First of all, the game does not have built-in controller support. This can be subverted using a program that converts keyboard keys to gamepad. I recommend the Steam application called Controller Companion. Using something like this makes for a much better experience. It is very much playable on keyboard but the catch is that your controls are left and right arrows for the flippers, holding down the down arrow launches the ball and the up arrow nudges up. These controls are not remappable at the time of this review. It is unknown whether this will be added in the future. If you have a controller and a program that can convert to gamepad like the one mentioned prior, you will have a good time here. There are also some bugs and issues reported but I personally have not encountered any of these reported bugs. It is possible that these reported bugs\/issues will not be patched because the developer has not made an appearance on the game's forums here on Steam.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Conclusion<\/div><br>This collection is a great gathering of many original designs that were priviously not available to Steam users. They include many fun to play tables with varying degrees of difficulty and design, with great sounds, voices and visuals. The downsides are that with no built-in controller support, the user must use a third-party application to make up for the shortcomings of the control options on offer here.
