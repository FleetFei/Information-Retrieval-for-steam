version of original Fallout gameplay, where there's point and click element to it but with real-time responses. You are able to freely move your character around wherever you point and click, as long as they're not obstructed by obstacles.<br>The main questline seem to consist of you trying to protect and survive from the atrocity that lurks in the wasteland, which itself is rather straight forward, with apparent tasks such as bringing the fragile population to safety, join forces, pass information, <i>etcetera<\/i>.<br><br><b>The ingame currency<\/b> is <i>nuts<\/i>, specifically <b>screw nuts<\/b>, as opposed to bottle caps in Fallout. These can be used for trading or paying for services, just as you'd expect. Each item you own has its value in nuts, and can be traded with most NPC in the game. Almost everyone that are non-hostile will have the ability to barter, where you can browse their belongings and eventually trade with them.<br><br>One thing that can be rather tedious for some players is that during the bartering there is no indication of the total amount of value in <i>nuts<\/i>, so you'll have to calculate the whole thing every time you barter.<br><i>Although I feel this may be an <b>early access<\/b> condition and will likely be an implemented feature in time being.<\/i><br><br><b>Now there's a strong emphasis<\/b> on early Fallout games in aspect of this game development, Krai Mira isn't as unforgiving as Fallout were. In Fallout you'd have to think for yourself and try to explore the wasteland, find its paths and venture into the dangerzone. While Krai Mira has more of a hand-holding -esque to it, but not necessarily.<br><blockquote class=\"bb_blockquote\">For instance, upon picking up a quest regarding the main questline, you'd be guided by large illuminating arrows showing you the paths between areas, as their exit tend to be in a specific point of the map. If you were given a task to head to a fort, you'd only be able to venture towards to the fort and encounter events in between. You cannot sidetrack or stray off, which prevents you from making any mistakes but also takes away your freedom of procrastination.<\/blockquote><br><b>It might be worth to mention<\/b> that there are mild nudity in the game such as topless women, who normally would be prost\u00edtutes. Those can exchange adult services for currency, and there'd be a potential side-quest given afterwards such as treasure hunting. The intercourse itself <span class=\"bb_spoiler\"><span>is not visible, the screen fades to black and you'd hear few moanings and then the screen becomes visible again.<\/span><\/span><br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Controls<\/div>One of the interesting features in this game is that you can move around the camera freely as you please. The mouse wheel zooms in and out, and the zooming range is rather large. The right mouse button can be held down and dragged around to rotate the camera.<br><br><b>The movements<\/b> feels awkward, the protagonist seem to slide around quite a bit, especially when you're taking a turn.<br>Their legs would be moving, but don't seem to have any impact on the ground, as if the surface is slippery. But again I believe this is yet another <b>early access<\/b> condition and may be reworked on as the development progress further.<br><br><b>The looting<\/b> is very easy, as you hover over something interactive it'll indicate with a cogwheel beneath the cursor, upon clicking on it the protagonist will move towards it and crouch down to loot it. A looting table will show up and you'll have the ability to click on an item to directly place it in your inventory or press the <i>take all<\/i> button for your convenience. If the item consist of a stack, then clicking on it will open an amount field where there will be up and down arrows, to increase or decrease.<br>There are still no ability to justify the amount by typing it yourself as of now, unless I didn't struggle hard enough.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Combat<\/div>The combat system is very clever I think, where your steps and attacks cost <b>action points<\/b> (<b>AP<\/b>), just like they did in Fallout. Despite the whole thing rules out in real-time, once a combat is activated, the environment would pause, until you have made your move. You get a highlighted line of sight striking between the protagonist and your mouse cursor, the further away you place your cursor the higher AP count will rise next to your cursor.<br>That way you'd know how much action points it'll cost you.<br><br>Clicking on an enemy will make you attack them, and the count of AP next to your cursor changes to enemy health during battle. The health indicator shows amount of health remaining out of max health. Every hit shows how much health is subtracted from the injured's health.<br><br>Unfortunately there's a slight control issues, where clicking on a hostile unit may not always cause you to attack it, but rather confuse the game whether you want to attack the target or simply walk past them. Thus makes your character move past the target and lose action points for that reason. Which can be infuriating. This applies to aiming for anywhere but the torso.<br>I think this could be solved if the target were highlighted in an outline each time you hover over them.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Dialogue<\/div>Now this is one of the crucial aspect of the Fallout franchise, where the dialogue has a major impact on the game progression. Depending on your attitude and intelligence you can diverse the mission to a different scenario.<br><br><b>In Krai Mira<\/b> the protagonist seems startled at all times. His responses are rather overreactive.<br>The protagonist doesn't seem someone who would be tough enough to survive in a wasteland, let alone to be a vigilante or a rebel.<br>- I personally tend to be calm in real life and conversate in more of a withdrawn manner, whether it be a joke, argument, flirt or small talk. <br>When playing a roleplaying game you'd want to connect yourself with your character, play yourself through them. I couldn't connect with my character as he was too melodramatic.<br><br>This might not be the most desired feature but I genuinely liked it in Fallout where I could select how my character would approach to someone. This helped to give our characters a personality to our likings.<br><i>Hopefully this may be reconsidered during future updates.<\/i><br><br>The NPC dialogue is rather vulgar, there's a lot of repetetive cuss words, but most of them seem to bring up sexual assault of some sort. It's just minor mistakes.
