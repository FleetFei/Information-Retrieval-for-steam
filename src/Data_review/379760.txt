Error?<br><br>Gravity Error is a short platform puzzle mix. You are playing in rectangular rooms that only fill a screen each, using placeable arrows to influence gravity.<br><br>Gameplay<br>The gameplay consists of the strategy and the action phase, as I will call them. In the strategy phase, you place the arrows. There are four arrows: Up, down, left, right. Each arrow makes gravity pull the protagonist in the direction it shows. You can place them on the whole playfield, with some exceptions. You can't place them into other objects (orbs are exempt from this). You want to collect all orbs and then reach the exit, so you must plan a bit ahead, but only in one level was it actually interesting to figure out how to do it. Many times you have more arrows than you will need.<br>The game consists of five sets of ten levels each.<br><br>Achievements<br>There are ten achievements that unlock as you progress while collecting all orbs. As the developer currently is moving, he will fix the achievements when he is able to, he says on the forums!<br><br>Music<br>The music is very nice, it's very catchy. The sound effects are sparse and fitting, but of a bit a low quality.<br><br>Controls<br>The controls are working fine, but have two big issues to me. First the protagonist does not steer well. He oversteers easily, running further than he should. This can become tedious later on.<br>Second the protagonist goes on the 2D plane in the direction of the key you press. Now this might be a matter of taste, I would actually like him to always jump when you press up and accordingly go for the other keys, so for example pressing left is going right when the gravity points up.<br><br>Graphics<br>The graphics are very basic, there are few different graphics. The game feels just like a series of chambers you go through, which it is, and does not build any graphical atmosphere.<br><br>Overall you get a small nice game, one I can actually recommend if you got it from a bundle or cheaply.
