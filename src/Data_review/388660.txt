strategy games, I thought I would give this game a try. Not all strategy games play alike and that is not a bad thing. In fact, its a distinguishing feature of the genre. For no other purpose but to create a catchy hook to this review, I want to compare strategy games to cakes. Yes, delicious, sugary, scrumptious cakes. Cakes, just like strategy games, are made up of some core ingredients. The basic ingredients of cake are flour, eggs, and sugar.  That's all you need to make cake. Strategy games also have core ingredients such as offering the player the ability to respond and adapt to situations while balancing certain requirements. No two cakes are alike in the same way that no two strategy games are alike. The whistles and the thrills we attach to our favorite cake are different just like the components a game developer can utilize to create a good strategy game are different. All this to say, Project Tarvotan is a game that succeeds on some ingredients whereas other ingredients could have used a bit more time cooking in the oven.<br><br><b>Overview of Presentation<\/b><br>For those who just want the short and sweet of it, this game exceeds at visually creating a unique image for itself. No computer is going to have a hard time visually displaying this game at its highest setting, but the visuals create a unique atmosphere that gives the game identity. I would go as far as saying that Project Tarvotan has the most beautiful game over screen I have ever seen in any video game. The gameplay music compliments the visuals and helps immerse the player into the environment of space. The User Interface is rather simple which I think is a bit of detractor. It also might take the user a bit of time to figure out what exacty is what on the screen. The developers have added the option to play the tutorial which helps a bit. <br><br><b>Overview of Gameplay<\/b><br>This is a turn-based strategy game through and through. I found myself having a lot of freedom to pick and choose a play-through style. I think that is one of the better things Project Tarvotan is good at. Most of the gameplay involves choosing where to navigate and what to do once you get there to best help you balance your science, fuel, materials, and such. Beyond the Milky Way, I think most of the solar systems are fictitious, but the maps are interactive. In the Milky Way, the sun expands swallowing up planets forcing you to navigate only outward. Other planets have different environmental elements that force the user to adapt their strategy to fit the solar system.  As I mentioned before, there are a few things that I think could have been given a little more polishing up. For example, we are asked if we want to accept a quest but there is really no reason why we would ever not want to accept a quest. This is especially odd since we are never told what the quest is before we are given the option to accept it. Another complaint I have is that the combat system is relatively simple and not very engaging. However it is a game that deals more in strategy than it does in action, so that is where the true fun of this game lies. The technologies you unlock are a bit lackluster in character and diversity. None the less, this game is worth a try in spite of these flaws. Its a good game to go back to because you know that no one play-through is going to be the same. To me, its not a game that I am going to spend hours and hours playing in a row, but it is one that I can see myself coming back to time and time again just to see what new challenges I will have to
