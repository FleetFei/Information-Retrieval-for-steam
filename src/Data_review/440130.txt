game. They have listened to feedback from fans and have implemented changes that have really made this game shine. While it\u2019s not the traditional \u2018anime\u2019 art style that comes with many otome games, the artist should be congratulated highly for such a beautiful style that highlights and captures the story in amazing ways. The characters are very well rounded and complement each other wonderfully. Character stereotypes have been broken apart and it\u2019s such a nice sight to see. The story is complex but well-structured; there are moments of confusion, which I believe is well intended because like Tsubasa, we are experiencing this world for the first time. While it does leave some rather big questions open, this is the first route of the game and I do expect that they will be addressed in future routes. <br> <br>Tsubasa is an amazing character who I hope many will fall in love with as I have. Unlike many traditional heroines, Tsubasa is a deeply layered and flawed character whose insecurities help shape who she is. She is an independent young woman who is usually very logic based and will dismiss anything that doesn\u2019t make sense to her. As we get to know her however, we start to see that things aren\u2019t so black and white. Tsubasa is kind; she is protective of those she cares about and will always try to do the right thing even if it means she\u2019ll get hurt. Her insecurities; being afraid of this newfound power and the added frustration at her inability to control it or understand it, makes Tsubasa a relatable and somewhat perfect for this role. While some may find it hard to Self Insert into Tsubasa as she is a completely character, the choices you make do somewhat change how she reacts to things. (I personally treat her as a character and not a self-insert, as I do with all otome games, so I can\u2019t quite comment on what it\u2019s like to self-insert as her.)<br><br>Initially I was pretty hesitant about Shou because that sort of character never really appealed to me, but since I had already paid for it I decided that I needed to give him a chance. After playing through his route and three endings, I am very glad I didn\u2019t trust my first assumption. Shou, like Tsubasa, is another amazing character who is defiantly more than what he appears to be. At first glance he appears just to be your typical confident happy go lucky guy who can easily bring a smile to your face.  He seems lazy and isn\u2019t too aware of his surroundings. Once he begins to open up to Tsubasa, however, we start to see a there is more to Shou than meets the eye. He lacks true self confidence and is very insecure about his magical abilities. As we get to know him, Shou displays some rather adorable quirks like being \u2018overly physically affectionate\u2019 with his friends and being rather shy when the attention is on him, but never quite enough to stop him from expressing how he feels.<br><br>Playing Mystic Destinies: Serendipity of Aeons will give you warm fuzzy feelings but it will also make you feel some angst as not everything is sunshine and roses.
