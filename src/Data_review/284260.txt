Syndrome inducing piece of art, that becomes David Kanaga\u2019s most ambitious work to date<\/div> <br>I should start this review by saying that after the first 20 minutes I spent with Fernando Ramallo and David Kanaga\u2019s new creation, I feel forced to stop in a state of anxiety while tears of emotion blurred my eyes.<br><br><i>Panoramical<\/i> puts me in the same state of euphoria that Stendhal felt visiting the Basilica of Santa Croce in Florence for the first time, as he became overwhelmed by the dazzling beauty found in Giotto\u2019s frescoes.<br><br>And you should take into account that I was skeptical about <i>Panoramical<\/i>, based on my prior experience with David Kanaga\u2019s first work, <i>Proteus<\/i>. Because, well, <i>Proteus<\/i> is one of my all-time favourite art pieces. A giant leap in the of use of the videogame medium to express mature and philosophical subjects, taking advantage of its interactive elements to maximize the spectator involvement with a natural and surreal environment. A contemplative but also narrative masterwork, that evokes Andrei Tarkovsky\u2019s existentialist films. And all of these elements were emphasized by the experimental approach of David Kanaga regarding musical composition. Using layers of sounds, like a painter that selects from a range of colors to fill a blank canvas with joyful and hedonistic emotions.<br><br>So, I was skeptical about <i>Panoramical<\/i> because contrary to <i>Proteus<\/i> and its narrative approach, now we\u2019re talking of a fully abstract creation, that also implies taking a lot of new risks in its conception. <i>Proteus<\/i> was a pleasant and absorbing experience thanks to its magnificent artistic and musical design. But it also succeeded in touching the player\u2019s heart with its psychological and complex structure, based in the recurrent art theme of using a seasons cycle as a metaphor to express an entire human life. The childhood innocence and sense of magical discovery of a new world full of stimulations in the spring. The fauna at its plethora in the summer, like a metaphor of the puberty. The quietness realization of maturity in an autumn envolved by sad and dissonant musical notes. The terrifying and ominous proximity of death in the winter, where the life forms are replaced with sinister tombs. And a final cycle of ascension to an elevated and deeper plane of reality, full of hope, where you finally close your eyes, with an emotional fade to black conclusion.<br><br>But after I can finally experiment <i>Panoramical<\/i>, and while I still remain exhilarated with its sheer beauty, I can say that is the most ambitious work created by David Kanaga to date. And that his ability to create pure and intense emotions in the spectator still remains intact. If <i>Proteus<\/i> was an impressionist painting based on the theme of nature, <i>Panoramical<\/i> is more close to Kandinsky abstract creations, affecting the player\u2019s perception in subtle and unconscious ways. And it has one of the most impressive virtual environments conceived in the interactive art medium since Tetsuya Mizoguchi\u2019s <i>Rez<\/i>.<br><br>Fernando Ramallo and David Kanaga\u2019s new creation will appeal not only to <i>Proteus<\/i> fans, but also to all the admirers of Toshio Iwai\u2019s underground cult classic <i>Electroplankton<\/i>. Like <i>Electroplankton<\/i>, <i>Panoramical<\/i> is a fascinating toy for musical creation, with an extremely original control scheme based in the use of a MIDI controller, that also fits perfectly a gamepad button layout. But unlike <i>Electroplankton<\/i> or the also similar <i>Cosmic DJ<\/i>, Panoramical has the ambition of being much more that a simple musical toy. Becoming a complex rehearse about the use of geometry forms and musical patterns to create intense and vivid emotions in the player, but also serving as a tool for intimate artistic expression.<br><br><i>( The writer of this review is not an English native.
