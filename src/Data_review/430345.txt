He made policies based on his idea that if the people had their needs met, their virtue would follow. Performed a series of practical reforms in areas such as public health and commercial stimulation, making Qi into a mighty nation. <br><br>Bao Shuya - Qi retainer during the Spring and Autumn Era. He was dead friends with Guan Yiwu and knew the extent of his talent better than any other. When Huan took the throne and wished to slay Guan, Bao suggested he employ him instead, &quot;If you wish to rule Qi, I am all you need, but if you wish to rule all the land, you will need Guan Yiwu.&quot; Though Guan Yiwu ended up as prime minister, Bao served under him without a hint of jealousy.<br><br>Zhang Yi - Scholar of Diplomacy. Served as prime minister in Qin and Wei. Studied with Su Qin under Gui Guzi, became a traveling orator and was taken in by the prime minister of Chu. When suspected of theft and being attacked on all sides, he told his worried life, &quot;I still have my tongue, don't I? That is all I need.&quot; So saying he returned to his public speaking.<br><br>Gongshu Ban - A denizen of the Lu nation in the late Warring States Era. A legendary craftsmen. Created siege ladders for Chu during his time that were used in their campaigns against the Song state. When he heard of this, Mo Zi visited Chu and challenged Gongshu Ban to a series of simulated war games and repelled every one of his attacks. Impressed by this, the King of Chu vowed never to attack the Song state. <br><br>Han Wu - Ruler of the Han kingdom during the Warring States Era. Since the Han kingdom was the weakest of the Seven Kingdoms, it found itself under constant threat from the Qin and Wei. Han Wu employed a bureaucraft, Shen Buhai, as his prime minister. Shen spent his 15 year term strengthening the Han kingdom, bringing it to its greatest period of prosperity, and did not allow Qin and Wei to cross the border. Died four years after Shen Buhai, criticized for his extravagance. <br><br>Huo Qubing - A cavalry general of the Early Han period. Grand marshal. He won the recognition of Emperor Wu when he was young and served at the Imperial Court. When he was eighteen, he followed Wei Qing to the front to put down the Xiongnu, and being gifted, performed some outstanding exploits. He fell sick and died at the young age of twenty-four. Emperor Wu mourned his passing and had a grave built for him next to his own mausoleum. <br><br>Kong Qiu - The founder of Confucianism. Given the honorific title, Confucius. A genius of the pen and sword, and a tremendous physical presence at 2.2 meters. Traveled the land to develop with his disciples to develop his insight. Served as an officer for Lu and Qi, but was unable to apply his ideals to the real world.
