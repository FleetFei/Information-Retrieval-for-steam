were fixed. After some thought I figured I'd try it out for myself instead of trusting the small amount of reviews and am glad I did.  <br><br> <br>Pros: THE LOCOMOTION MOVEMENT IS SO MUCH FUN!!! Also I never ran into any issues where it made me sick. I love the variety of weapons in the game! The physics are hilarious! Although caused by a bug if you throw a weapon at a zombie it will tend to continue it's momentum and bring the zombie with it (as seen in the first few seconds of my first time gameplay video) which provided me with bunches of lol. There is a nice variety of Zombies and weapons to destroy them with. The levels are koo lookings and the game runs very smooth with really no framelag that I remember. I was bummed when Unturned didn't offer VR support aside from a level builder; while not on near as large of scale this game gives me that experience. <br><br><br>Cons: I played it for approx an hour and did experience about 3 crashes. I noticed the first 2 happened whenever I tried to picked up the magnum looking pistol. Once I didn't try picking that up anymore they gameplay lasted significantly longer. There are issues with the Zombies where you will take damage from them if you make contact with their body on the ground after it is dead. Zombies will sometimes keep coming after you after the heads are blown off. There's also a bug where a Zombie or 2 will spawn underground and remain stuck there stopping the ability to continue to the next wave. In addition to this, there is not a way to quit the level and return to the remain menu (at least not that I could find) without just fully closing out the game through the Steam VR interface. <br><br>What I'd like to see: <br><br>Aside from the current issues; i'd love to eventually see co op added to the game. More interactive levels would be awesome too (i.e. being able to go inside buildings and destroyable debri). Being able to barracade yourself in would add to the fun. Maybe even a dedicated level (like a grocery store or abandoned house) that requires you to build barricades on the possible entrances and hold off zombies as long as you can. Difficulty levels would be neat too adding larger waves of zombies with each level of increased difficulty.   <br><br>Summary: <br><br>The game definitely needs some work but is still very fun to play! For the amount of content it offers I feel a $10  - $15 pricetag might draw in more of a crowd. I do not mind supporting games which I enjoy though so for me $20 for this game is cash I'm willing to part with and really look forward to seeing the future progress for this game!
