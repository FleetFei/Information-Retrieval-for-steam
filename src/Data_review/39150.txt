There will be additional rules that come into play to make this much more challenging. She will learn to become fearful of loss as well after her first confrontation with the Sorceress. As for Squall... because Squall's &quot;older sister&quot; left him at an early age, he isolated himself from the world to prevent himself from being hurt. In essence, you could say these two are the most paranoid heroes you will ever come across in the FF series. But because of this, the heroes in FF8 are also the most human you will ever meet. It makes them relate-able to the average person and requires a rather mature audience to play.<br><br>By the end of the game, you will discover that majority of the characters will have something personal that they struggle through. that I remember playing, and I got hooked and it currently sits as my second favorite game I've ever played (behind Baldur's Gate 2). I've played it many times, read all of the criticism, and just recently completed all of the quests, so I'll attempt to address the main points on every side, bad stuff first.<br><div class=\"bb_h1\"> Overall, TL;DR <\/div>What this leaves us with is a fantastic junction system that is built on the power and versatility of GFs, but is crippled by the forced grinding that is inherent in the broken Magic stock system. A small handful of characters shine, while the side characters prop them up and then fall to the wayside.<br>Final Fantasy is a deeply flawed game, and is not very forgiving to newcomers, but has some deep complexity with several rewarding points, both in combat and side quests.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"> Negatives <\/div><br><b> Progression System <\/b><br><br>The biggest complaint I've seen for FF8 is how your power level progresses, specifically the Draw + Junction systems as well as level scaling. The Draw system is the magic system in the game; each character has 32 total slots for magic and can &quot;draw&quot; magic from enemies or draw points, or have magic refined through a variety of abilities, with a maximum cap of 100 total stock of each spell. The Junction system works in conjunction with GFs (Guardian Forces, equipable summons that give you a bunch of skills and such) and is reliant on the Draw system in that you junction magic to your stats (HP, strength, magic, vitality, spirit for health, physical attack power, magical attack power, physical defense, magic defense), as well as more rare junctions like hit %, speed (how quick your turn comes up), evasion, status and elemental attack and defense junctions. Sounds all great and dandy with tons of customization right? And it is! <br><br><u> The problem <\/u> is that the effect of junctioned magic is directly related to how much of said magic you have stocked. 100 fires will give a lot more health than 10 fires, and the system very much encourages sitting in a super boring battle for 10 minutes to fully stock up on magic. <u> Even worse <\/u> is that using magic will deplete your stock, therefore reducing their effectiveness as junctions, and thus meaning that there is never a good reason to use good spells, which is sad considering all of the crazy interesting spells, from Maelstrom (reduces physical defense to 0), to Double or Triple (allows you to cast 2 or 3 spells in a turn, respectively) to more traditional stuff like haste, regen, meteor, etc etc.<br><br>The other problem that arises where power levels are concerned is level scaling. Every enemy in the game (with a handful of exceptions) has a scaling set of stats, skills, and draws that corresponds to what level your characters are, and they scale much faster than your characters do respective to their levels. That's right, you're better off never leveling up anybody, as using the roundabout ways to acquire junctioning magic will make you much stronger relatively speaking than gaining EXP.<br><br><b> Story Character Progression <\/b><br><br>As far as the story goes, the problem here is that the side characters have little to no character progression. Outside of the main ~3 characters in the story, the side characters just don't have much going story wise to flesh out their personalities, introduce conflict, or provide any meaningful way for them to grow as characters. Personally, I like Squall (the main character), but the fact that there are so few developed characters means that 1) seemingly interesting characters are mostly wasted and 2) there is less of a chance for the player to bond with any of the characters (see: FF9 with its varied cast of characters), so unless you like Squall, Rinoa, or Laguna you probably won\u2019t like the game from a story perspective.<br>*Note* I\u2019m not touching the plot, as I feel like it is good not great, with several somewhat questionable choices throughout, but it neither makes nor breaks the game in and of itself.<br><div class=\"bb_h1\"> Positives <\/div><br>Woohoo, stuff that makes the game worth playing! First up, Guardian Forces! Guardian Forces (or GFs) are summons that give you command abilities, let you junction magic, and refine stuff from stuff to give you more stuff! And you can summon them in battles to fight if you want, but that\u2019s far from their most useful function (with the possible exception of the coolest of them all, Doomtrain). Why are GFs so cool? Because they tie everything together! They can help you in battle in a pinch, can be mixed and matched to get the right junctions, command skills, and support buffs you need, and can learn refining skills that will turn the useless junk you find into stupidly awesome magic and weapons! On top of that, many of them are new. Quezacotl takes Ramuh\u2019s place as the lightning summon, while mainstays Shiva, Leviathan, Ifrit, Carbuncle, and Bahamut, in his best form of the entire series, all make appearances. New characters like Diablos, Pandemona, and Eden, and recurring characters that get their first appearance as summons in Cactuar, Tonberry, and Gilgamesh, all provide unique skill sets, as well as interesting ways to hunt them down (good luck with Ultima Weapon, Bahamut, Eden, and Tonberry without guides).<br><br>Next up, Triple Triad! Yes, it\u2019s a mini game. Yes, it\u2019s the easiest (and frankly, only realistic) way to become a God, but it\u2019s much more than that. It\u2019s a simple game with a 3x3 grid where each player has 5 cards with 4 numbers on them, one for each cardinal direction, and you flip cards but having a higher number than the one you place a card next to. Simple, right? But that doesn\u2019t include a rule like Plus, where you have to calculate what they can do with their cards so you don\u2019t get your super cards flipped. But then there\u2019s the Elemental rule which turns your only safe play into a losing play.
