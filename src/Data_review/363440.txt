The love letter to Mega Man fans was already written when Mega Man Anniversary Collection came out for the PlayStation 2, GameCube, &amp; Xbox. It never came to Steam. If you can acquire that title, do it. Be warned that the Gamecube version did swap the A and B button placement which made the game harder for those familiar. Regardless, that set included more games, and everything was much more polished, included more features like remixed music, tutorial graphics, etc. Some may whine about it not being perfectly NES accurate, but the pros outweighed the cons.<br><br>If you don't have those consoles, then here you are. Legacy Collection. You must understand, the devs misled us on this one. They hyped this up as the end-all, be-all MM1-6 set. They talked about how they were remaking these games for modern machines while keeping pixel perfect accuracy and even re-inserted the known glitches, exploits, and flicker we remembered. Yeah, that's all there....because this is literally just an emulator. They didn't do what they said. They just made an emulator. The original NES ROMs can literally be read from the EXE at a HEX level. <br><br>And you know what? It's not even a perfect emulator. There's actually some additional slowdown here not present on a real NES. There's unofficial emulators that run better and can actually provide superior visual filtering or superior pixel perfect upscaling.<br><br>I'm pooping all over this title, but I have to remind you, especially when it's on sale for $7, it's still a good deal. These are some of the best NES games ever made. MM1 is a bit rough to suggest perhaps, but it's important to see where it all started. MM2 and MM3 will always be the fan faves but I'll always praise MM4, 5, and 6 for being worth your time, despite so many people overlooking them. They are much more balanced, and less glitchy than the first 3 games, even if the boss and stage ideas were less original or logical. The chargable megabuster divided the fanbase, perhaps because so many people had an OCD about needing to keep it charged and suffer the noise which it puts out all the time. If you break free from that and use your arsenal instead, you'll enjoy them more. All 6 games have outstanding music, as well. Yes, just as good as MM2's. They all grow on you. This has a soundtrack player accessible from the main menu too.<br><br>Now besides emulating 6 classic games, they offer you up very high quality scans of most of the original artwork that could be find in the japanese manuals and other sources. You can even extract those images from the game's data.pie file using WinRAR with the password P091uWEdwe4lI6StDNMNlkodPGvJ38bL3HW6t3BCMYdFi83FXKu7k0NsHP8caDKS.<br><br>The only other noteworthy addition here is their attempt to include something like the Endless Attack mode that MM9 and MM10 had. Here they remix segments from the MM1-6 games in random sequences in a time attack fashion. But guess what? These are still just emulating the ROMs. It's actually clever what they did. They essentially created a series of save states from the games to load you into various positions. You run and once you touch a portal, it loads another save state to challenge you with. Only during this portal animation is the game even putting out any original visuals, essentially as an overlay for the background emulation going on. At least these time trials do have online leaderboars so you can compete with friends.<br><br>So if you're going to be a good boy\/girl and support Capcom and perhaps try to convince them that we still love Megaman and want to see more Mega fun on PC, buy this. Just know you're not getting the perfect experience as was promised during all the hype.<br><br>And Capcom, if you could bring over Megaman Zero 1-4 with the remastered CD soundtracks built into them, you got $50 waiting from me on day one.
