2nd of December, 2016<br><b>Type:<\/b>  Single-player<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">General Impression<\/div>Most of today\u2019s fairy tale adaptations for gaming, are effortless to say the least. Among the piles of cash grabbing tie-ins, certain gems still manage to shine through. Such a title I\u2019m reviewing today. Saved by its own visual style, which managed to sway me from taking into consideration its many flaws and be more critical of it. Little Briar Rose has charm, indeed. A Steam debut for its dev team from which I\u2019m certain they have learned enough to further improve their formula should they continue to grace us with future releases.<br><br>Now onto the task at hand. What is a \u201cbriar\u201d you may ask? The English language shows once again the proclivity of its native speakers towards gardening. A briar is a term used especially for rose bushes and occasionally for a few other thorny shrubs. Roses are more common though and at the same time a leitmotif in many works of literature. LBR draws an obvious inspiration from \u201cThe Sleeping Beauty\u201d fairy tale though apparently the writing itself seems to share a Franco-German legacy, being considered a Bothers Grimm classic under the \u201cDornr\u00f6schen\u201d title. Our game does take an artistic license from the source material and for the sake of originality does sprinkle it up with other elements and characters from the fantasy genre.<br><br>While the story itself is merely decent, the visual style truly stands out. Stained glass inspirations which convey a rather unique look to this game. Rare enough and at the same time a welcome departure from the far too common hand drawn 2D graphics we\u2019ve seen in dozens of Point &amp; Clicks. If only the soundtrack could complement the superb visuals. It can\u2019t do that at all. The complete lack of voice acting only worsens the bleak perspective of LBR\u2019s sound department. It\u2019s 2017, guys. Adventure games from the \u201890s had an obvious excuse for not including many sounds, considering that back then you would require a handful of floppy disks just to install a single quality title.<br><br>The story\u2019s been covered (for as much as I\u2019m willing to discuss without spoiling anything to you, my readers) and we can safely talk of the gameplay in Little Briar Rose. I\u2019d reckon that while initially targeted towards a younger audience (who should still enjoy the 1959 Disney animated film \u201cSleeping Beauty\u201d), there are enough reasons even for adults to play the game and have a good time. Sure, the puzzles aren\u2019t nowhere near as challenging as those we\u2019ve become accustomed from most adventure titles or even HOG types, yet at least they come in a wide variety along with several  mini-games to ensure not one dull moment is in sight. There is even an element of dark humor consisting in the (potentially rapid) succession of princes and would-be rescuers if you fail some of the aforementioned puzzles. Easy or not, should the protagonist you control miss certain challenge thresholds, a less pleasant fate shall await him and his noble quest of awaking the sleeping damsel in distress, will be undertaken by another prince (with obviously different tastes in regards to fashion as far as you can observe). It\u2019s always the little details that stand out. And humor is most certainly welcome in almost any gaming genre.<br><br>All things considered, Little Briar Rose is still being marketed at a slightly increased price tag from my own perspective. But as long you purchase it on offer from a Sale or bundled, absolutely nothing should stop you from giving it a fair chance. Especially so since it\u2019s not even that long to see it through till the predictable happy ending. Only slightly twisted if your in-game mistakes costed one too many princely lives. Alas, sacrifices in the noble pursuit of love, are fully understandable!<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Strong Points<\/div>+ Beautiful stained glass integrated concept.<br>+ Decent story and likeable
