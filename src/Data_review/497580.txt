I found <i>Agent Walker: Secret Journey<\/i> to be very middle of the road. It had a nice look, nice puzzles and hidden object scenes but lacked an engaging story. This title is one of six currently available on Steam by Brave Giant LTD, so far I\u2019ve played five of these titles and found most to be mediocre. It wasn\u2019t until I played this game the second time that I really noticed it. However, being mediocre doesn\u2019t make this title bad.<br><br>The story is set during World War II, you play as Agent Walker, who has been sent on a mission to find a powerful artifact so that the enemy doesn\u2019t get to it first. This MacGuffin is the Spear of Destiny -reputed to be the weapon that killed Jesus Christ, ending his agony on the crucifix.  Some legends declared its wielder became ruler of the world.  It ends up falling into enemy hands as you could imagine and you spend most of the game trying to track it down and destroy it. That\u2019s pretty much all the story is about. Agent Walker is never given any development, she\u2019s a total placeholder for the player with little personality. Despite being set during World War II there is never a huge sense of dread or urgency about anything, this could have easily been set during any war on any planet with the same outcome. There also isn\u2019t much of a supernatural element to the game, there is a brief explanation about the spear given to you during a mini game and not much else. I did find the title to have a decent sense of action though as you do move around often and explore quite a few locations.<br><br>What I enjoyed most was the gameplay here, there are plenty of hidden object scenes and puzzles to keep you entertained. The game starts off strong, adding some rarely seen variety to the usual hidden object scenes but that dies off around midway through and they all end up being the usual list of objects you have to find. The scenes do offer morphing objects, this is when an item you need to find continually morphs into another object so you have to keep an eye out when to click on it. The puzzles were enjoyable, most weren\u2019t too difficult at all. There were some fun ones like parachuting and even driving a boat which I don\u2019t often see as well some of the staples you tend to find in this genre. You\u2019re given a map that shows you where an action can be performed and also allows you to teleport, as well as three different difficulty settings and a custom setting.<br><br>Visually the game performs well. While some of the animations of the characters might seem a bit stiff, everything looks pretty good. It feels like the 1940\u2019s and like it\u2019s set during a war. The hidden object scenes are done well, the items aren\u2019t hard to see or blurry and overall everything is very clean. The audio, on the other hand, is a mixed bag. Most of the voice acting is pretty lackluster. Some characters speak like robots almost, most are just middle of the road and Agent Walker herself never speaks at all. Voice acting isn\u2019t required, but if you choose to add it, then it should be enjoyable to the ear. Thankfully the music is alright and fits well with the tone and setting of the game.<br><br><i>Agent Walker: Secret Journey<\/i> is a good game but it skirts that line. It offers pretty graphics and solid gameplay but if you enjoy a good story you may find yourself disappointed. If you play the game on Steam it does have trading cards and easy to gather achievements if you\u2019re a fan of those. This game was only published by Artifex Mundi so don\u2019t go into it expecting what you might find in some of their other titles, however I do recommend this title if you find it on sale or bundled but it\u2019s rather short for the price tag and offers no bonus chapter and not much extra content.
