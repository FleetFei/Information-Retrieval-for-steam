I avoid them like the plague after seeing so many greedy developers abandon the game completely after they start making money. The reason I feel confident recommending this game in it's current state is because the developers are very active on the forums, they listen to feedback and are working on issues we've addressed. <br><br><b> So what IS The Ember Series: A New Fire? <\/b><br> <br>It's an adventure RPG with turn-based battle. Sometimes you even have to be a little strategic,  and think carefully about how you want to use your mana and special attacks.<br>Theres 5 classes to choose from; warrior, archer, rogue, spellcaster and acolyte. Each of these have their own set of special attacks and spells that you can level up. You do so by using your skillpoints that you get from leveling and defeating enemies, and there's also a good amount of stats you can put your skillpoints into.<br> <br>What really got me interested in this game is the story. <br><br>The game starts with five friends playing a game of D&amp;D; Sophie, Jason, Jerome, Alex and Derek. During the game each friend pick their class (Clever way of choosing classes, may I add) They get into an argument with Derek, and decide to go home for the night. After a while, they start to feel bad about it and goes to check on Derek and find that he has disappeared. In his bedroom they find a mysterious book, and as they start reading it they suddenly find themselves in a new fantasy world. <br>(For a more in-depth background story you can go to the forums &gt; news)<br><br>As of right now, there are two quests available. That will take you approximately 2 hours to do, depending on how much you want to explore. There's currently three towns, a tradepost, and two forests available to explore. One of the forests is actually quite large and has a lot of interesting areas. It took around 4 hours for me to explore it all.<br><br> If you're unsure about getting the game I recommend trying out the demo. There's a bit more to the game than the demo, but as of now the game is still in early access and far from done obviously.
