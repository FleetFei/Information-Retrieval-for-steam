class=\"bb_h1\">Introduction<\/div>Grotesque Tactics 2  - Dungeons and Donuts is developed by Headup Games and Silent Dreams while being published by Headup Games. It is indeed a fortunate case of a sequel done right, since it improves certain areas in which its predecessor was lacking.<br> <br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay<\/div>Just like in Evil Heroes, we have a hybrid that intertwines elements from both role play and strategy games. Outside of combat, you have an inventory system (filled with ludicrous items in tone with the game\u2019s subplots) and conversations with NPCs that feature multiple choices, as any self-respecting role playing game would have. When you do have to fight, the action takes place in standard turn-based strategy mode: allocated action points which can be spent on either movement within a tile set pattern, or by attacking\/defending. Only during combat, you can access the inventories of your unlikely allies and equip them as the situation warrants it. Speaking of friendly characters which join you temporarly or not, they are just as over the top as they were in Grotesque Tactics 1. Once again we can relish in the company of \u201cHis Semidivine Humble Self\u201d, the Holy Avatar (the perfect caricature to any paladin\/goody two shoes type of personality found in most RPGs) and his scarcely clad Maidens (another well implemented moment of self-irony). <br><br>And you still control a world-weary emo goth kid that breaks the mold from conventional video game heroes or even antiheroes. If storywise no flaws can be reported, the gameplay did suffer from quite a few pathfinding problems that relate directly to NPC movement. I did have to save and load the game to resume play after an ally blocked my path and refused to step aside. Isolated cases but if they weren\u2019t pactched by now, you must be fully prepared to accept some minor issues. Luckly, the solution was simple enough and the game\u2019s main plot keeps you guessing what new silly quest you shall embark upon next.<br><br>Released just one year after its predecessor, Grotesque Tactics 2  - Dungeons and Donuts looks more than decent for a 2011 release. The textures are well detailed, asset variety is present and even the voice acting seems to have improved (or at least the audio quality). While many sequels that span over a short time frame, are mostly just graphical rehashes, in Dungeons and Donuts we have a new and well deserved game engine that is far better than the first game in the series from the stability perspective as well. I had no more resolution or scaling issues with it and I could take as many screenshots through the Steam Overlay as I pleased. A job well done from a dev team that clearly wants to improve its creation.<br> <br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Pros<\/div>+ Even more satirical than the first one<br>+ Pleasant blend of story-driven TBS\/RPG elements<br>+ Steam Achievements (Store Page doesn\u2019t mention this)<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Cons<\/div>- Occasional pathfinding issues<br>- No Steam Trading Cards<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Result \/ Final thoughts<\/div>It goes without saying, that if you enjoyed Grotesque Tactics: Evil Heroes, you can\u2019t go wrong with Dungeons and Donuts either. Since the stories are just barely related to one another (apart from the recurring characters) you can safely just pick up this sequel and enjoy its unusual portrayal of a fantasy world that is fully aware of its absurdity. There aren\u2019t many games on the Steam Store that can be both self-ironic and fun at the same time.<br><br><b>Rating 7\/10<\/b><br> <br><blockquote class=\"bb_blockquote\">This review was submitted
