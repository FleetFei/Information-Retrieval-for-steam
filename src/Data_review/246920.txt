class=\"bb_h1\">Bombernauts<\/div><b>Developer<\/b>: Eyebrow Interactive<br><b>Publisher<\/b>: Eyebrow Interactive<br><br><b>Note<\/b>: A key for this game was provided by the developer for the purpose of writing an unbiased informative review. No other compensation was given.<br><br><b>Introduction<\/b><br>==========<br>Bombernauts is a online multiplayer arena game with fully destructible voxel terrain. Think Bomberman, only a lot more fun; Your character is a little buggy-man thing, armed with tons of bombs and a baseball bat. Bombernauts is an incredibly fun game with friends, but therein lies it's largest problem: the playerbase is currently non-existant. While this isn't the game's fault, as the game itself is solid with a great concept and little to no bugs, it currently does not have any singleplayer or bot options for players to mess around with while they wait for other players to battle. However the devs are currently working on adding these features, so we should see the playerbase rise considerably after these are implimented. Now let's take a look at the game itself.<br><br><b>Gameplay<\/b><br>==========<br>Remember Bomberman? Well, this game is Bomberman without the grid layout on it's maps. You control a small 3-wheeled buggy-man-thing (let's just call it a buggy), that's armed with an infinite supply of bombs and a baseball bat. You can play against up to 16 other players (4 is the recommended number), where the objective is to blow them all up! Sounds simple, but it is insanely fun. You throw bombs in an arc, and you can aim how far your throw will be. Bombs explode a couple of seconds after landing, and if you hold off before tossing them while aiming, it'll cause a larger explosion...sort of like powering them up. The baseball bat can be used in a pinch to knock enemy bombs away from you, even before they land! Batter up!<br><br>At times, a small drone carrying a pinata will appear. If you bust up this pinata with your bat, it will drop a random powerup. These powerups are very numerous and do a variety of things, but usually only last for 1 bomb throw. For example there is one that lets you throw a cluster of bombs at once. When this explodes, it shoots many smaller bombs out, which then explode as well. Another powerup gives you a temporary jetpack, which improves your mobility greatly. There are a few referential powerups too, that give nods to various things. One great example of this is a powerup that gives you Dragon Ball Z Super Saiyan hair, and allows you to throw what I believe to be a Spirit Bomb!<br><br>There are many, many different maps that are rotated between each match. Every map is fully destructable, even if some parts take more powerful bombs to destroy them. There is also a map editor which you can use to create your own maps. I haven't tried it out yet because I have trouble designing decent maps or creations using toolkits for games, and sometimes the controls are a bit weird. But it's a great addition. <br><br>Right now the game is multiplayer\/co-op only, but as I mentioned before finding people to play with is a bit of an issue. If you want to play against others, it's recommended to get a 4 pack and give the extras to your friends who want to play, or host a game and hope others join, or advertise it on the game's forum.<br><br><b>Graphics<\/b><br>==========<br>Bombernauts is fairly simplistic, but the visuals are very crisp and look nice. The maps consist of mostly cubes and structures built from them, which can be blown up as much as you please. Some parts of each map react to explosions differently; for example some will catch fire after an explosion and turn to ashy blocks which crumble when touched. <br><br>Your player character is a little buggy thing on 3 wheels with goofy eyes. These look pretty comical and adds to the charm of the game. Each buggy can be fully customized with many color, eye, and wheel options.<br><br>Explosions look great. While not realistic with flames going everywhere, they are instead a shower of vibrant stars and smoke which fit the game's comical theme very nicely. <br><br><b>Sound and Music<\/b><br>==========<br>The bomb sounds effects are extremely satisfying. They work very well, and mesh well with the visual effects. The music is simplistic like the graphics, but it gives the game a light competitive feel, which helps as this is a competitive game at its core. It's pretty much the same tune repeated all the time, but you never really get tired of it.<br><br>I feel like the game could use a little more work in this area. Possibly a few new tunes and some more unique sound effects. What they have now is good, but it can only get better with a little time and effort!<br><br><b>Performance<\/b><br>==========<br>I had zero issues with Bombernauts. It ran very smoothly with a great framerate of 45-60FPS. I recommend a controller, as the keyboard controls can be more of a hindrance than help. The game loads up pretty fast, enabling you to get into the action quickly. I didn't see any random crashes, so that's a plus. <br><br><b>Final Thoughts<\/b><br>==========<br>With a little more work and exposure, Bombernauts could be a great multiplayer title. Bombs and powerups everywhere only add to the fun and mayhem of each game. With plenty of maps to play on, as well as a map creator, there is literally no end to the fun this game could bring.
