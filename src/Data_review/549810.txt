platformer, however it\u2019s difficulty doesn\u2019t come from where the designers thought it would. Perhaps the hardest thing to do in this game, is to listen to the ingame music. Unfortunately this game suffers from \u201crestarting the music whenever you restart the level\u201d syndrome, which in a tough platformer like this one can be as often as every 5 seconds. Luckily there is an option to turn off the ingame music, but it\u2019s a bit of a shame because a lot of the music is pretty good (especially that groovy main menu music). Even when you don\u2019t die, when the music loops, it ends and then restarts with a gap between (or maybe it was a fadeout, I don\u2019t remember) and it was pretty jarring.<br><br>There are a total of 28 stages and they range in difficulty from \u201cliterally you don\u2019t need to press any buttons\u201d to \u201cfffffffffffffffffffffffffffffffffffffffffffffffffffffffffffffffffffffffffffff\u201d, although excluding those 2 levels where you do nothing I\u2019d say the minimum difficulty is quite hard. Only get this game if you like challenging platformers (and have a tolerance for resetting a lot). <br><br>There are a wide variety of platform types in this game. Regular floating blocks, moving donuts, grey moving blocks that kill you, blocks that looks exactly like the regular blocks but they fall when you touch them, yellow moving blocks that kill you, disappearing(only invisible, they are still there) moving donuts, donuts that explode when you stand on them, boats, some random floating pieces of terrain sometimes, blocks that looks like other blocks but arbitrarily spawn more block\/change the level completely, the floor, sometimes roofs of houses, and my personal favourite: the explosions that the yellow donuts make. All in all there\u2019s a fair amount of different platform types, however I feel like there definitely could have been more to vary up the gameplay. By about level 14 you\u2019ve seen pretty much all the blocks the game throws at you and then you still have half the game left.<br><br>As a bit of an aside, what\u2019s with the disproportionally detailed crabs? I think those crabs have more detail and more frames of animation than all the other game assets put together. I think maybe the other enemies need to be a bit more detailed in order to match the crabs.<br><br>Pausing the game doesn\u2019t pause the game, if the \u201cresume\u201d button wasn\u2019t there I would be fine with it, games not allowing you to stop time while in a menu is fine as long as they don\u2019t pretend the game is paused. Also maybe you could add the controller button's that the pause menu options are linked to on the menu in game, I didn't even realise that they were mapped to buttons until I was writting this review and was complaining about how I spent the whole game switching to mouse to click restart, they controls weren't exactly the clearest to see on the control screen. <br><br>I\u2019m going to file that under \u201cbugs\u201d and while I\u2019m at it throw a couple more at you:<br>The explody donuts and the falling blocks trigger when you touch them from the side, they clearly shouldn\u2019t do this.  <br><br>A lot if not all of the moving platforms aren\u2019t consistant in their movement. I feel like this is most evident in level 15. If you don\u2019t move at all and just watch the donut on the right you will notice that it has no discernible movement pattern beyond just going left and right.<br><br>With that out of the way I can mention some of the other points of contention that I have, such as THERE ISN\u2019T ENOUGH TIME ON THE TIMER, unless you do everything on the earliest possible cycle without messing up you aren\u2019t going to make it to the end of the level, there should at least be enough time to miss a cycle of one of the longest cycles in each stage.<br><br>And finally my biggest point: what is the point of offering a choice between bunny and pig? As far as I could tell they were exactly the same but had different food\/face. A real missed opportunity there to have the bunny be better at jumping and the pig better at\u2026 something. There certainly wasn\u2019t enough difference between them to include both of them in the game title like that, especially because there is no co-op or using them both at the same time. It should be Bunny or Piggy.<br><br>I hope that the developers read this and maybe make a few changes based on my troubles here, as I think that this could change from being a frustratingly
