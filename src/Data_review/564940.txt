crew members. With the help of an AI partner, you are tasked to find out how are you going to escape out of this settlement.<br><br> The \u201cyou\u201d in Alone with you is the computer partner who also generates various holograms of the crew members who died in the disaster by calculating the data stored in the settlement which leads you to solve the mystery and escape. It plays exactly like how you would expect a classic point and click adventure plays like. You walk around the desolate station collecting various things, finding out bits of lore about the story and solve puzzles to progress further in the story. <br><br>These puzzles are not exactly difficult but require you to pay attention to your surroundings and the dialogues. There were a few times where the answer to a particular puzzle was given away in a previous dialogue and I had failed to realize that which lead to two hours of frustration.<br><br>Interactive games like these depend on a good quality of storytelling which can keep you invested throughout the journey and Alone with you succeeds here with flying colours. Telling much about the story in the review will not do it justice and you have to experience it yourself to see the various routes it takes and how emotional it gets towards the end. There are two endings to the game and they both are different enough to warrant some replayabilty. The dialogues are also a little different based on items you pick up through the journey and all of them are written very well. <br><br>While the story is really great, there is some stretched out sections in the middle section of the story. These involve you going back and forth to the same parts of the settlement without much progression in the story. These sections felt padded out and felt like the developers had to cram in these parts just to increase the length of the game. While this was a souring experience, it is completely overshadowed by the great way the story is told in the game. The characters are all very well developed and you grow a connection with them towards the end.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Visuals<\/div><br>Alone with you\u2019s pixelated art design helps it a lot. The environments themselves tell the story of the desolate settlement and even though there is no blood and gore, there were some rooms which feel gut wrenching when you walk into them. <br><br>The settlement with no other interaction other than you AI companion and the hologram representation of the mechanics makes you feel like you are abandoned in a place you don\u2019t want to be and yet towards the end, these creepy settlements grow to be your home. These visuals carry the game to a whole another level and while not hyper realistic, they do their job spectacularly.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Sound<\/div><br>Another field in which Alone with you excels in is its sound design. The soundtrack is brilliant with a sci-fi touch and adds to the desolate feeling. There is no voice acting to speak of and I think the game is better without it as it might have ruined the experience.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Overall<\/div><br>If you like Point and click adventure games then Alone with you would not disappoint you. It tells a great emotional story and is gut wrenching in parts. If you don\u2019t like games like this where there is little actual gameplay then Alone with you won\u2019t do anything to change your mind. But at this price, I think that you should give Alone with you a try, it might not be revolutionary but it is a solid experience nonetheless.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Pros: <\/div><b>Great
