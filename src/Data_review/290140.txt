love-hate relationship with Aeria Games since I first became a member in 2008.  Echo of Soul is the third of their published games which I've played for more than 2 days.  In fact, I've been playing this game since their Open Beta launch on June 1st.  But this isn't about my history with others of Aeria's games, this review is about this one in particular.  Let's begin, shall we?<br><br>=====CHARACTER CREATION=====<br>The first thing you'll notice about Echo of Soul is that there are a total of five classes (thus far) to choose from.  These classes are gender-locked, and each class has two spec mastery paths.  The gender lock is there to follow with the game's storyline.  The classes you can choose from are as follows:<br><br>1.  Male Warrior<br>2.  Male Rogue<br>3.  Female Guardian (Lancer, for those familiar with Tera)<br>4.  Female Mage<br>5.  Female Archer<br><br>As you will begin to notice, there is no dedicated healing class.  More on this later.<br><br>Each class has different customization options available to further make your character unique in physical appearance.  You then start off with a default costume (I can assume this to be random from a small pool, as I've created different characters of the same class, and ended up with differing clothing looks from the get-go.)<br><br>=====LEVELING=====<br>Leveling in Echo of Soul is very much geared toward the casual player.  For the hardcore speedlevelers, it's possible to make it from levels 1 to 60 (current cap, at time of review) in a matter of 1-3 days through the game's PvP Battlegrounds (accessible starting at level 10.)  For the more casual player, you can still make it to 60 relatively quickly or you can take your time with it.  Moderate-speed casual players can make it inside of a week.  Slow casuals can make it to cap in abotu 2 weeks of gameplay time.<br><br>Both PvP and PvE Questing offer a very generous amount of EXP, so reaching cap is entirely on how you want to do it.  You can go solely as a Battlegrounds resident, through the PvE story, or mix it up a bit.  For the best leveling experience, I would recommend doing either PvP or PvE (depending on which one you want to stick to primarily) just to try it out.<br><br>Grinding mobs in the overworld will provide very little in the way of EXP.  Grinding overworld mobs is an easy option to obtain enhancement jewels, crafting ingredients, or even some crafting recipes.  If you wish to do PvE grind for EXP, then your best bet would be to do so in the dungeons.  Though, grinding out the dungeons might not be a bad idea, as the sub and main bosses can provide your character with class-specific equipment sets to further enhance your stats and abilities.  There is also the option to take repeatables in maps called Infinite Hunting Grounds which also reward a fair amount of EXP per turn-in.<br><br>=====ECONOMY=====<br>There are a couple of things which new players will find out relatively quickly:<br>1.  Mobs do not drop money.<br>2.  Items from NPC vendors are really cheap.<br><br>This means it will be relatively difficult to make solid gold before your 50-60 level bracket.  I would assume it to be a tactic by the developers to maintain a relatively strong pre-endgame economy.  With that being said, there is no restrictions on the amount of in-game gold you can carry in difference to those at level cap.  If you wish to make some quick major gold for your earlier levels, your best bet would be to utilize the cash shop and in-game auction board.<br><br>As I have not hit level cap, as of yet (I'm a -very- slow player, and have taken more time to get to know the forum community due to my work\/sleep schedules) I will not post a comment on endgame economy.<br><br>=====QUESTING=====<br>PvE questing is quite linear, and very story-based.  While you do get a vast amount of miscellaneous quests (both overworld and dungeon) the magic of PvE questing truly shines with the story-based quests.  As with most typical MMORPGs, you get EXP, money, and the occasional piece of gear to upgrade your set.  However, it's also relatively easy to outlevel some of these quests, but the EXP you gain from some of the lower-level quests is still quite generous.<br><br>=====Player-versus-Player=====<br>Echo of Soul offers two types of PvP:  Battlegrounds and Open-World Guild vs Guild.  Let's expand, shall we?<br><br>Currently, there are two Battlegrounds maps (both accessible starting at level 10.)  Both are points-based Capture the Flag-esque types.  One requires teams to capture resources spawned on the map, and the other requires teams to capture and hold towers for as long as possible.  The actual player-kill aspect of these battlegrounds can be quite hectic and fast-paced.  There are also two tiers of Battlegrounds- one for Levels 10-59, and one exclusively for Level 60.<br><br>Open-World GvG takes place between guilds which have declared themselves to be hostile.  Players in hostile guilds are fair game to be PK'd at any time, anywhere by other hostile guilds' members.  Guildless and Peaceful-alligned guild players do not have to worry about being randomly PK'd while running around the overworld maps.<br><br>There is also a PvP event (run at least daily) called Valhalla.  Since I have yet to participate in this event, I cannot write a review about it.<br><br>=====CRAFTING=====<br>Like the rest of the game, crafting is geared toward the more casual player.  Relatively cheap and brainless, it's fairly easy to level both a profession and the two hobby crafting skills available in a short amount of time.  Certain tiers of crafting require certain character levels to be obtained.  Beginner-level crafters can gain most of their supplies directly from their crafting vendor.  However, there does come a time where some mob grinding is required to get higher level base materials.<br><br>=====UNIQUE FEATURES=====<br>1.  Echo of Soul has a hunger system.  This is determined by two values:  Energy and Satiety.  The higher your current energy level is to your max, certain buffs are applied to your character.  You increase energy by eating foods which are crafted or obtained as quest rewards.  Satiety is how much food you can eat.  With a max value of 1,000 and each piece of food taking up 200 Satiety, this leaves you with a maximum of 5 food waten at a time.  Satiety can be decreased over time, or with the use of a Digestant item.  Energy is depleted through the use of skills.<br><br>2.  The Soul System:  Through daily rewards or killing of mobs, you can obtain what's called &quot;Chaos Souls.&quot;  When purified (either near a purification point, or cooperatively with another player) these souls can grant you charges for temporary buffs.<br><br>3.  The Cash Shop:  While most free-to-play MMOs out there have a cash shop which is mostly geared to be pay-to-win, Echo of Souls isn't geared that way.  Mostly asthetics or RNG fun, there is literally nothing on the cash shop which you are required to get to advance in the game, or gain a significant advantage.  The things on the shop which seem that way are literally a pay-to-be-lazy mechanic.<br><br>4.  No Dedicated Healer Class:  The entire party works as a team to heal!<br><br>=====THE BADS=====<br>1.  There is no general server chat channel.  Primary communication is done at either close-range, through the use of the Loudspeaker items, or on the map-specific Shout channel.  Unless you get yourself into an active guild, or are part of a different chat system (i.e. Teamspeak) prepare to be playing mostly in blisful chat silence towards you.<br><br>2.  Your other Typicals:  Elitists, gold spammers, and general complaining doomsday naysayers.<br><br>=====TL;DR=====<br>If you're a casual gamer looking for a casual game which you can level relatively
