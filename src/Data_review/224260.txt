There are ten waves in total to survive, each wave becoming increasingly more difficult. If your zones do get damaged, you can repair them using a certain item. There are also supply drops and crates to regenerate your health. Survivors must utilize their environment and supplies to hold out until the end to escape the map.<br><br>Objective mode consists of the survivors working together to complete tasks and eventually escape the map. This ranges from gathering the common supplies, to securing the anti-virus from the research labs, and much more.<br><br>There are also modded modes of course such as dodgeball, but I won't get in depth with that, as it is not part of the base game.<br><br>You can choose from three difficulty settings as well: casual, classic, and nightmare. These modify infection rates, zombies, damage, etc.<br><br>The basic flow of the game will have you sticking with your team, covering each other, killing zombies that are in the way, and finding better gear that spawns in the map. There's many different melee weapons and firearms you can use, whether it be the popular chainsaw, the revolver, or even a bow. Besides that, there are several characters you can choose as well as voice sets with their own unique voice quotes for the command menu.<br><br>Survivors can sprint, aim down the sights of their weapon, vary the power of their melee swings, and shove zombies away, but they must manage their stamina carefully, otherwise they won't be able to use these abilities or their melee weapons at all. Flashlights are dynamic in this game, which is a feature I adore. In other words, if a survivor shines a flashlight on a wall, you will see that light, rather than it just being on their end. This is helpful because your teammates can light up dark areas to help see zombies while you take them down.<br><br>There are no &quot;special infected&quot; or mutants in this game. They are the classic zombies consisting of runners, crawlers, standard walkers, riot zombies, and children zombies. Runners and children are the deadliest since they move the fastest. If you can't handle killing children in this game then I don't recommend you play this. You're probably going to die when the zombie apocalypse happens. All zombies have a chance of infecting you; eventually you'll turn into a runner if you're not killed. There's a nice little feature of being able to commit suicide with your firearm, which is useful for when you're infected.<br><br>Now to get into what I think of the game. More specifically, its pros and cons. This game has a slow pace. If you're impatient here is a fair warning.<\/td><\/tr><tr><td>Great performance, and is optimized<\/td><td>The glory of Source games is that they can run on a variety of low-end and high-end PCs, with the appropriate tweaking on their end of course. On other occasions I've had objective items spawn in inaccessible places or not trigger at all. These bugs are extremely frustrating to encounter, especially when you're near the end of the map and are stopped by such a mundane item.<\/td><\/tr><tr><td>Poor AI<\/td><td>The zombies will often get stuck in areas, or they won't move at all.
