is based on the universe of Lost Lands, a hidden object adventure series set in another dimension or I suppose so because the games are done by the same people, with the same title. However, here, you take in your hands the destiny of the Elven Nation, crashed on a lost island, rebuilding their civilization and ready to set sail elsewhere if only a magical storm wasn't preventing them to do so.<br><br>This is the first free-to-play released by Five-BN. The second one, Lost Lands: Mahjong, shares the same story, the same quests and the same mechanics. It means that apart the core gameplay (hidden objects - mahjong), nothing changed when the second was released. I've played Mahjong first because I love the solitaire version of this game and A Hidden Object Adventure came after. It also means that the majority of this review will be identical to the one I did for Mahjong.<br><br>The gameplay itself is to find objects in different locations: a list of words, a list of shapes, with also a cycle of day and night as some quests required a &quot;night&quot;, in other words, the scene will be only partially visible, as if you were carrying a torch. Generally, the scenes can be done easily, even if objects are changing places. It's pretty rare to be out of time.<br><br>Quests, while needing explorations for anything (weapons, tools, ressources, etc..), can also involve &quot;fights&quot; with enemies, repairing buildings or using tools. Also, the more you're earning experience, the more you're leveling up and the more you're unlocking. There are also chests you can open, with various minigames (and not always easy). Everything is timed and the explorations, as the chests, cost energy.<br><br>And that's bothering me, the energy regenerating slowly unless you buy some potions with the crystals you're earning. Crystals are pretty rare but there is the easy way: spent real money to acquire them (or to acquire more golds). Gold is mainly used for weapons for example. Here's an idea for the cost: 10 crystals = 0.88 euros - I've checked the next price and it's already nearing 10 euros). <br><br>I don't mind having quests to do and I don't mind the level up system to access new locations. Even the shop isn't really bothering, as far as you can use the gold you're earning. However, preventing me to play a game because of a decreased energy on Steam, that's something I can't imagine. For me, that kind of gameplay is reminding me of my Facebook games time. Weirdly enough, it would have been on Facebook, I would have find it normal. Here, on Steam, I'm not at ease with the concept. Ubisoft released games about CSI and Assassin's Creed on Facebook, yet, the main series were totally different.<br><br>You'll probably find it weird that I'm bothered by the Facebook-like when the game is a free-to-play and that the concept of the energy is also there to push you to buy some (the game is also released on Android and iOS devices). But that's my feeling. Many people will feel that they're trapped and will want to advance. However, I tend to use a simple thought to avoid spending money: there is no rush. You're there for fun (I'm not reading the story though).<br><br>As for the rest, the graphics are acceptable and pleasant, the soundtrack being the same. If you played any other Lost Lands, you'll be in an familiar setting.<br><br>As I love hidden objects, I can overcome easily the energy bar and the game is still enjoyable. It's free basically, so try it but DON'T FALL INTO THE &quot;NEED ENERGY&quot; TRAP. If there are indeed micro-transactions, don't fall in that pit of addiction. You don't need to go forward immediately. Just quit the game and come back later.
