should have never seen the light of day. The game passed through at least four developers and three publishers before its final release in May of 2016. With that kind of development hell, it\u2019s frankly amazing that the game even came out and even more shocking that the game is even good.<br><br>Having played Homefront: The Revolution in March of 2017, I cannot speak to its supposedly rocky launch. When the game released, it was met with a wave of discontent and uproar about everything from performance and glitches to being flat out broken. There were even people claiming this to be \u201cThe Worst Game Ever Made\u201d. First off, let\u2019s dispel that hyperbolic nonsense, in no universe is Homefront: The Revolution \u201cThe Worst Game Ever Made\u201d. Homefront is shockingly competent given its development history, not that its history gives it a pass by any means, but I do think it\u2019s important to look at it with context.<br><br>As far as the actual gameplay goes, Homefront plays like an urban Far Cry. The long and short of it is that if you enjoyed Far Cry 3 and\/or 4 then you\u2019ll probably enjoy Homefront. It has all of the trappings of a Far Cry game: open world, climbing structures to reveal part of the map, light crafting and customization, etc. There are some aspects, however, that I think Homefront does better than the Far Cry games. Climbing towers in the Far Cry games are usually pretty straightforward but in Homefront, it\u2019s often a puzzle that must be solved. Rarely is a base or tower a straightforward affair. I often found myself circling the building multiple times to find my pathway up. This little wrinkle made a normally mundane process a bit more rewarding. This is also in part due to the excellent traversal. This game gets first person movement right, and is some of the best feeling in any FPS I\u2019ve played. <br><br>Running around and capturing strongholds was quite satisfying as I think the free roam nature of this game is its strong suit. In fact, I think the best compliment I can give Homefront is that I never even used the fast travel feature. Compare this to a game like Mafia 3 which was utterly painful to play by the end because of the lack of fast travel. In Mafia, there was nothing to do between your destinations whereas is Homefront there\u2019s always a pack of enemies right around the corner ready for a firefight. This emergence is really where Homefront shines.<br><br>The gunplay in Homefront: The Revolution is properly meaty and all the guns have a heft to them that feels satisfying. While I enjoyed the ability to customize my weapons on the fly, it would have been nice to have an extra weapon or two. The weapons, and the world, do have a nice sense of patched-togetherness that you would expect out of a crumbled Philadelphia.<br><br>I haven\u2019t mentioned the story yet because, well, it\u2019s pretty average. I like to play a lot of games primarily for the story but Homefront: The Revolution was not one of them. While not bad, the story was pretty boilerplate revolt and uprising stuff. There\u2019s nothing here that you haven\u2019t seen before. It\u2019s enough to string together sequences but don\u2019t expect a gripping thriller here.<br><br>I\u2019ll speak briefly about the DLC since I didn\u2019t give it much playtime: skip it unless you really loved the story. I found all three of the story DLC\u2019s to be subpar of the main game mostly due to the fact that they\u2019re extremely linear. Now I\u2019m not opposed to linearity, in fact I often prefer it to open worlds, but Homefront\u2019s strength is in its open world. When you change the element that it does best, it\u2019s going to suffer. Again, it\u2019s not terrible, but it\u2019s nothing that I would recommend buying unless you\u2019re dying to get more of the story, which they do expand on.<br><br>Despite its tortured development history, I think that Dambuster Studios managed to make a pretty darn enjoyable game in Homefront: The Revolution. The game has no right to be as fun as it is but its mechanics come together to create scenarios that remain enjoyable throughout its 13 plus hour campaign.
