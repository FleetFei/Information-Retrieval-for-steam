Thoughts<\/i><br><br><br>Tyd wag vir Niemand is an Early Access indie platformer\/puzzle game by the South African studio SkobbeJak Games.<br><br>Tyd wag vir Niemand translates to Time Waits for No One, a beautiful and philosophical saying that is contrary to the premise of the game. You control time in a world that is stuck in the Mirror Dimension of Doctor Strange. Your goal is to traverse through the strange world, using your abilities to control time to your advantage.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay<\/div>Much like SUPERHOT, you control time and use it to your advantage. Unlike SUPERHOT, however, you have direct control. Your time control isn\u2019t limited to whether you\u2019re moving or not, however, you do have unlimited time control. You have to use time control in order to successfully make a difficult jump or solve puzzles. From what I have seen so far (I am on the third stage), there is no combat. The closest you will come to actual combat is fleeing speeding metal balls on a farm.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Graphics<\/div>The world of Tyd wag vir Niemand is beautiful in it\u2019s own sense. Some of the textures aren\u2019t so pleasant on the eyes, but the grandeur of the maps is quite impressive. On the second level, you are in a crystal cave that makes you feel like an ant. The levels are massive and beautiful. The first map is by far my favourite with a beautiful sky world that you have to see to believe. The textures on some of the obstacles do leave much to be desired, however. <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Audio &amp; Soundtrack<\/div>Each level has it\u2019s own unique soundtrack, which is quite nice. However, I spent quite a long time on the 2nd and 3rd level, and the song became quite boring once after it looped a few times. It may be different depending on the amount of time you spend on one level, but if you\u2019re not the best at platformers (aka me), you will find yourself listening to the track looping endlessly. When you slow down time, you hear the tick tock of a clock that speeds up as your time control runs out, which is a great way of planning your next move. I quite like that mechanic and I tip my hat to the developers for including it.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Storyline<\/div>I am about an hour into the game, and so far, there is no story as to how and why the world is how it is. It looks like Doctor Strange\u2019s mirror dimension scene, but there is no exposition as to how. If the developer was able to piece in an extravagant story, the game would be so much better. I see great potential in the story aspect of this game, but at it\u2019s current state, there is none.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Developer Recommendations<\/div>Besides the storyline, I only have one quarrel with this game. The developer has said in previous posts that they are considering adding a sprint functionality. I support that idea. Whenever you fail a section of the level, it teleports you to a certain distance from where you are supposed to go. Sometimes you will be in front of the puzzle, but sometimes you\u2019ll have to walk a great distance. Of course, sprinting while time is slowed down would be quite over-powered and broken, but if you separated the two and made sprinting only available when time is moving at it\u2019s normal speed will solve the problem.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Final Thoughts<\/div>I enjoyed my time playing Tyd wag vir Niemand and I applaud the studio for making the game. South Africa isn\u2019t exactly known for its gaming scene, and I fully support the developers who are based in the country. Tyd wag vir Niemand is innovative and quite tricky during a lot of the levels. If the developer incorporates a storyline into the game, I can see myself playing this more in the future.<br><br>Rating:<br>Graphics: 6.6\/10<br>Audio: 5.7\/10<br>Gameplay: 7.8\/10<br>Overall: 6,7.10<br><br><blockquote class=\"bb_blockquote\">Enjoy the review?
