themed hidden object game from World-Loom and it manages to live up to the standards of the magnificent first game in almost every aspect. The game's main trademark is the alchemy, we have to concoct different potions from various recipes requiring many ingredients and use them to achieve our goal.<br><br>Unfortunately the game doesn't have much to do the with the original, altough apparently we are the same female alchemist and we serve the same king from the first part. The king decides to step back and entrusts his only son with his crown. We, as the king's most trusted alchemist and also the aunt of the heir, have to keep the prince - now the new king - out of harm's way, as an old adversary of the old king reappears: an evil witch who once almost managed to destroy the entire kingdom by raising an army of crystal soldiers. She had been banished and locked inside a magic mirror, from which she has now escaped. This time it will be our (the prince and a mysterious young girl will also provide aid) job to defeat her, once and for all.<br><br>The use of alchemy is quite similar to the method we've seen in Lost Grimoires: Stolen Kingdom. First we have to acquire the necessary recipe, then gather the 3 ingredients it requires. Once all 3 have been placed, there's a minigame, in which we have to connect the required amounts (a minimum of 3 must be connected in 1 move) of runes of the 4 basic elements, which are adjacent to each other within a limited number of moves. It might sound a bit complicated, but it's actually pretty simple, not as good as it was in the previous part though. The useful item we receive through the alchemy is usually a once usable magic potion or another consumable object, that'll  always be the key to progression.<br>Another important part of our repertoire is going to be a shard, a piece of the magic mirror, in which the witch was imprisoned. It is going to be invaluable for solving some minigames.<br><br>The minigames are refreshing and most of them are really good, but again they don't reach the level of the puzzles (I'm thinking of those fine text riddles primarily) from the first game, Lost Grimoires: Stolen Kingdom. Some of them have been encountered in other HOGs, not that it's a problem, while some are quite original. There are very few hidden object games, instead we get to solve many creative minigames. They are generally fairly easy, I could have used more hints with some of them though, as the instructions were quite vague about solving these. There was one, in which we have to reassemble a totem representing forest animals and it wasn't clear for me that we actually have to place the animals on top of each other (I was looking for a missing piece instead). Also, some minigames, like the one preceding the final showdown, where we have to steer a ship and dodge the crystal obstacles, don't seem to be making much sense (there's no time limit and no palpable penalty upon failure).<br>The collectibles come in the form of roses (1 can be found at each location) and some of them are really well hidden. Their general shape and size may vary, so good luck finding them! :)<br><br>The graphics is amazing for a hidden object game: we get to visit a wide variety of many beautiful locations, the ones I liked the most were the lush backgrounds from the forest area, in which we have to track down Rose, the mysterious girl. <br>The music is simply enchanting, some of the best I've heard in a HOG recently, I especially loved the ones in which flute was involved. I had the feeling that at some places music was missing (in the introduction for example), while at some parts the voiceover was way too quiet and hardly audible.<br><br>Lost Grimoires 2: Shard of Mystery is a great sequel to the excellent first part and a very good hidden obejct game on its own. Along the splendid minigames and the pleasant plot, there's a great deal of cutscenes that make the story more immersive and movie-like. I also loved how many elements of the prelude was introduced through mingames as we played.<br>Since the game is barely connected to the first, you can start playing this right away, even if you haven't played that one (altough you should, since the prequel is even better). For me, World-Loom has become one of the best hidden object game developer with this title.<br><br>PROS<br>+ alchemy and a wide variety of minigames<br>+ fairytale setting with a good little story<br>+ nice graphics accompanied by enchanting music <br>+ plenty of cutscenes<br><br>CONS<br>- altough comes close, isn't as good as the first part upon comparison<br>- a bit more informative
