SpiritSphere?<\/div><br>SpiritSphere is the result of combining air hockey with inspiration from fantasy classics like the Legend of Zelda and Windjammers. The game is easy to pick up and to learn yet difficult to master. As a result of the setting and the ease of play it is accessible to players of all ages. The game has been designed around its local multiplayer components but the game does feature multiple single-player components.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Childhood Memories Evoked<\/div><br>What I am most thankful for is the ability that the game gave me to relive childhood memories. These memories were of playing video games and air hockey with friends, partners and even my mother. These bonding experiences have helped shape who I am today. Therefore, I quickly became enamored with the game. I have played many of the games that the levels have been based around. It\u2019s difficult not to have these memories continually rekindled. I believe that many players who were raised on 1980\u2019s and 90\u2019s video gaming will quickly appreciate these same levels. Consequently, I feel safe in advising that the presentation is on point to deliver the atmosphere and the setting players would expect.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Retro Simplicity<\/div><br>There\u2019s something that works with the simplicity of retro-inspired gameplay. The ease of picking up the game and learning within moments how to play. Then comes the time playing to master it. The fact that you can then turn to a friend or family member and have them enjoy the same experience or kick their \u2665\u2665\u2665 until they too learn. This is an addictive experience. That is until your friend, or in my case my girlfriend, is able to learn the game so quickly that she pulls out a victory against you (sshh, don\u2019t tell her but I let her win!).<br><br>In all seriousness, the gameplay is very basic. After all, it is air hockey. This is a good thing. Thankfully there is some variety in gameplay thanks to the different balls that you can unlock and the numerous environments. I am unsure as to how many different balls there are available to you but you unlock them by spending coins at the Sphere Fountain. There are three characters that you begin with. I believe that the only differences are cosmetic in nature. I am hopeful that more will become available as you progress through the game or in future updates.<br><br>The gameplay is also very fast and furious. This is why there is no online multiplayer. If there is any type of lag, however miniscule, the game would become unplayable. While this is a bit of a disappointment as I would love to play with friends on the other side of the world.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Single-player Mode<\/div><br>Despite being designed as a local multiplayer\/co-op game I argue that Eendhorn Games are selling the single-player component short. I acknowledge most players will likely find more entertainment value in the multiplayer but I personally enjoyed the single-player equally as much. Firstly, the AI is surprisingly tactile and has been well designed. Secondly, there is some depth to it. The rounds are quick and fast capitalizing on the strengths of the game and players are rewarded with coins. These coins can be spent at the Sphere Fountain. The drops from the fountain are random meaning there will be times where you get a \u2018double\u2019 from your coins. Fortunately, the time investment isn\u2019t significant. This is about one successful round. I had amassed over four hundred by the time that I lost for the first time.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Is it Steamified?<\/div><br>Fortuitously, SpiritSphere comes jam packed full of everything that Steam players are looking for. It comes with a set of trading cards. There are also achievements. As of my first play there is only one achievement available (rookie \u2013 win your first match) but I imagine more will be available. Unfortunately, the game is local multiplayer and co-op only so no online components are available as the gameplay is too fast. Therefore, this game is fully Steamified.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Verdict<\/div><br>The truth is that I found a lot of joy in SpiritSphere. The simplicity of an air hockey game meeting fantasy battlegrounds inspired by the classics is a great combination. I can envisage a situation where one of the major studios releases a similar game based around their franchises and blows this game out of the water. However, for a one man development studio, this is an addictive gameplay experience that is suitable for both single player experiences as well as the core multiplayer. If you have friends to play with then this title is bound to give you hours of competitive entertainment.
