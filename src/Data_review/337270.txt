Besides the aforementioned lacking of customizable controls for the camera view, there are also no screen brightness settings (at my work computer I could barely see chests in the dark caves), and no mouse sensitivity settings (which, by being set to a lower value, might make the camera view control less abrupt and awkward at times). Some settings, like the screen resolution ones, should be available right from within the game during its playing, not only by accessing its main menu (the start screen). As for the graphical quality settings, I failed to notice any actual difference yet between those levels, but maybe it\u2019s just me. Anyway, I know for sure that settings (a specific configuration containing windowed mode of a certain screen resolution) failed to be saved and I have to re-enter it every time I start the game.<br><br>Also, I really think that toggle-able automatic running should be an available option. I\u2019m really not ok with the ostrich starting to run automatically after I keep the forward key pressed a little longer. Some people might be ok with it, but if I could, I would\u2019ve disabled it. If I wanted to run, I\u2019d rather press the \u201crun\u201d key and that\u2019s it.<br><br>- The background music is ok-ish but while some in-game sounds are pretty well-chosen (like the \u201cdeath\u201d sound when a skeleton runs you over), other are rather annoying, somehow &quot;cheap&quot;, and a bit too loud compared to the rest (like when you pick up an egg)<br><br>- The graphics are rather poor, not in the sense they\u2019re not advanced and sophisticated (which they are not, but that\u2019s not the problem), but in the sense that they\u2019re somewhat buggy and glitch-y. Otherwise the game feels a bit weird, chaotic, and superficial, mostly because of giving the feeling that random elements have been blended in, just for the sake of it (skeletons?!).<br><br>In conclusion, though it\u2019s got some quite a few issues at the moment, the game is still worth playing.
