I played the other day, The Secret of Pineview Forest is a short(ish) preview of a game to come, in this case the second game in the Pineview series, which is raising money right now.  I haven't played the main attraction, Pineview Drive, yet - bad LinustheBold! \/smack - but these two atmospheric dark spooky woods tastes make me want to spend time exploring it sooner rather than later. <br><br>This gamelet is more impressive and, I think, less successful than the earlier one.  It starts with a backstory excuse to run with - car crash, road blocked off on either side by dangerous downed electric wires and that elusive species of game-tree that can't be climbed over, with a dark guardrail behind that also doesn't allow jumping or climbing.  Which is here nor there.  Ahead is the menacing forest, which is where you must go.  In the dark.  With, of course, monsters. <br><br>And that forest is, as they say these days, Yuge.  I spent quite a while searching for the first relevant area - I followed a leaping buck into the dark, walked along the hilly zone walls, and generally wandered about until I finally found the eventual house.  And then promptly got lost once I was out again; I'm like that in dark computery fields.  Eventually I made my way to the lonely light quietly illuminating a chained fence, and entered the second area.  And then the third, which is *really* big, or seemed that way. <br><br>I wish this game had a save; games that force a player to sit through single-session play irritate me.  I understand that it's not that long, but it is an hour's trip, substantially more if you don't immediately find your path (there is no path) or if you miss a bit of inventory or need to backtrack for whatever reason.  It's a late-night game, and what if I get tired?  I have to start over.   In my case I didn't understand immediately that the Use key (E) was different from the mouse click, and yes that's obvious in its own way and I figured it out eventually, but if there was a tooltip about that I missed it and I was wandering back and forth for like an hour looking for something that didn't exist to open something so I could move on, and man, NO SAVE.  <br><br>Anyway, eventually I worked it out, and shortly after that the game was over. <br><br>I like games that give me an interesting, atmospheric landscape to investigate.  I never met the monster here, or in the earlier game either - I'm a hushy sort of explorer, given to moving quietly and stopping when there are strange sounds nearby.  The sounds were effective and I enjoyed the music, which was constant but not narrative or intrusive.  Most of the locations I found - I probably missed a couple of landmarks - were purely atmospheric, which is fine.  I was surprised that none of the mill doors opened, but this is a taste and not a full game.  I enjoyed the walking in the woods, though the plot didn't engage very often and the ending was puzzling and abrupt.  <br><br>In short: the selling point here is the creepy walk-in-the-dark part, and there's a lot of that.  The story is a little thin.  I played with the gamma at its natural dark setting, because I'm not sure why anyone would play a game about walking in the dark with their screen adjusted so it looks like they're walking on a cloudy day.
