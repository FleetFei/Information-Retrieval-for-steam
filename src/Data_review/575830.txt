rooMaze<br><b>Status:<\/b>  Released<br><b>Developer:<\/b>  ChairKilling<br><b>Publisher:<\/b>  Sometimes You<br><b>Genre:<\/b>  3D Role Playing Game<br><b>Release Date:<\/b>  3rd of March, 2017<br><b>Type:<\/b>  Singleplayer <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">General Impression<\/div>I can appreciate a game that feels underpriced for once, after reviewing so many overpriced releases on Steam that I could fill this page just with their titles. A game like rooMaze may not speak volumes just by glancing at its price tag, but I assure you that it is adequately packed to take you on an RPG nostalgia trip that you\u2019ll remember for quite some time. Keep in mind that this is also the developer\u2019s first foray onto Steam. They made an impression on me, that\u2019s for certain.<br><br>I was fairly young when I attempted to play dungeon crawlers for the first time. I couldn\u2019t understand them plotwise, nor was I well-versed in English. Yet such games, back then in crude (by today\u2019s standards) 2D were far more complex than their contemporary shooting and platforming titles as far as the gameplay was concerned. This particular type of RPG subgenre has had several attempts at revival over the last decade but despite all efforts, dungeon crawlers never attracted a substantial fanbase such as the cases were with story-driven D&amp;Ds or the ever modern hack 'n' slash. To be fair, rooMaze is tagged as being roguelike and not a full-fledged dungeon crawler, since you\u2019re playing solo in this game. <br><br>No party-based combat system and as a result you can mold your nameless protagonist however you see fit, since there are three classes to choose from. Ranger, Warrior and Mage. The last two are staples of the fantasy genre and subsequently most RPGs you played so far. Ranger is special though. It doesn\u2019t necessarily imply a bow and arrow setup since you\u2019ll be able to use firearms as well. Definitely not a 9mm handgun, but some primitive flintlocks are still better than the crossbows you\u2019ll be wielding most of the time should you pick the Ranger class. I personally did, since I\u2019m tired of the same old stereotypes with either the wizend but still amnesiac mage that has to learn all his spells on the go, or the even more boring dude that\u2019s waving a slashing\/bludgeoning piece of metal at his foes until they drop. And ever since playing Morrowind, I\u2019ve decided that the ranged stealth option was my favorite method of dispatching RPG enemies. There\u2019s obviously no stealth involved in rooMaze sadly.<br><br>What you do get though, is a randomly generated dungeon everytime you play. Whatever the objectives might be, survival is your first and foremost duty since the dungeon is filled to the brim with deadly foes and traps in equal measure. Couple that with a permadeath system and you can bet that you\u2019ll be dying a lot before you get the hang of it. The complete lack of a plot or even a flimsy tutorial only makes things worse but I\u2019m willing to be more forgiving with the game since it emulates the old school difficulty of its subgenre precursors. It even features an injury diagram where you can observe and mend those wound, whether they are bone fractures or lacerations. It\u2019s been quite a few years since I\u2019ve seen that being implemented in a video game. <br><br>Now onto the less stellar aspects of this title. As I mentioned above, the open-ended structure and procedural asset generation, leave little room for the conformity of a campaign storyline. You won\u2019t be left wanting for activities since the RPG barebones stuff is included (such as exploring, basic crafting or fighting) but the game surely deserved some context to it. What really bothered me though, was the absence of sound diversity and the maximum resolution of just 1080p. Trust me, I tried to force the game running in 2K and 4K to no avail. It might seem like a \u201cfirst world problem\u201d to some, yet we\u2019re in 2017 and some crazy folks out there are already designing, shipping and trying to convince everyone that 5K is the new must-have standard for eye candy. I\u2019m perfectly fine with running some titles at 1920x1080 resolution, but at least offer me enough antialiasing options to nullify those hideous jagged edges I kept seeing everywhere in-game. It's running Unreal Engine 4 assets so don\u2019t tell me that the 4K resolution is unwarranted. Still, even those cons can\u2019t force me to deduct a lot from its final rating. As if you needed me to remind you one more time, you get more than what you pay for. Grabbing rooMaze in a cheap bundle, is like receiving it for free at this point.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Strong Points<\/div>+ Procedurally generated assets.<br>+ Nice voxel graphics.<br>+
