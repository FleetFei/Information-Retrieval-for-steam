I couldn't really find enjoyment in them anymore. You're not so much shooting bullets as 'catching' them via your thrown ball. You need to defend a space tower &amp; destroy some alien vehicles. All the while keeping track of your ionball.<br><br><b>Replay<\/b>: Replay value is good (so far). I've played around 25 different levels of this game and I've found most of them interesting &amp; challenging. When I lose all my lives &amp; it's &quot;Game Over&quot; I don't feel utterly beaten &amp; ready to quit. Instead, I feel eager to try again. It encourages me to hone my reflexes and peak my curiosity over what the next level might bring. I feel the end-goal is far less about 'winning' the game, although there is a Leaderboard you can compete in if you want to try.<br><br><b>Graphics<\/b>: Graphics are up to date in this decade, with a 3D-look over a 2D format. There are two viewing\/playing options but my fav is Top-Down; in the style of the old arcade shooter\/pinball games. If you're getting frustrated, turn down the graphics to 'Fastest' &amp; then raise them up later to a fancier setting once you've gotten the hang of the game. Focus first on enjoying &amp; progress, rather than perfection.<br><br><b>Controls<\/b>: From what I've read this game is not compatible with joystick or controller. The great news is it's also a simple one-handed game; so if you've carpal tunnel in one hand you'll still be able to play with the other. Although playing this game with your off-hand will be a good challenge. :)<br><br><b>Price<\/b>: I got it during the holiday sale, so it was under $2 for me. It's easily worth $10, however.
