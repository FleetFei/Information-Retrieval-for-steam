The basic levels are also part of a vague story, which I found helped to break up the gameplay and give it a bit of a setting. Your spaceship is in trouble and you've got to fix all the components (engine, cloaking device, etc). <br><br>Levels consist of rows and columns of numbers - all linked together. The numbers in each row and column have to add up to the value specified at the end of the line of numbers. The object of the game is to select a number in a row (or column) that doesn't tally correctly, then switch it with a number that will help bring the tally closer to what is required. While this is all you can do, there is actually a lot to it at later levels, often requiring a great deal of thinking on your part.<br><br>Normal levels consist of single digits in the +8 to -8 range, but hardcore levels could possibly ask for rows\/columns to tally +\/-100. You might find yourself more likely to be out by +1 though, and in only one spot, making swaps entirely useless. <br><br>Perhaps the most fun feature I discovered is that it is impossible to go online hoping to find a solution. Why? Because every time you quit without finishing a level, the numbers will randomise into a new puzzle when you go to try again.<br><br>So when you see someone has 100% achievements for this game, you can be pretty confident they earned it.<br><br>I digress though. It is easy on the eye and doesn't detract from you trying to concentrate on your sums.<br><br>In short, it's a good game for anyone that likes puzzle games, the achievements aren't too difficult and it makes a nice break between larger games such as rpgs.
