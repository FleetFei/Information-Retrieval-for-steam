shooter with good pad locomotion, very good performance and graphics, plenty of spells and a motivating experience points level-up system.  <\/div><br>With time is passing on, Vivers become more and more demanding when it comes to new games. We start to look for more and better locomotion, freedom to walk around and specifically to have some great social multiplayer experiences. In consequence, indie developers have to move on and leave behind teleporting movement as well as single player wave shooters. I am happy that the developers of JaudaLabs (one programmer and one designer) are precisely doing this and came up with the great idea of Sorcerers of Kinetics (VR) to push the limits in competitive multiplayer VR gaming a bit further. <br><br>The underlying and long-term idea behind the project is to develop a fully functioning MMO where you play a mage that casts a multitude of spells and to fight against foes. I say the idea, because at the moment the game is still work in progress when it comes to the content, but actually very functional when it comes to gameplay and performance.<br><br>For the time being (on 14th of November 2016) you are fighting in a very pretty a functional medieval arena in some sort of free team deathmatch mode with capture the flag elements. In 3 vs 3 each team tries to capture the flag from the enemy base. Whereby this might sound easy in classical games, SoK adds some sort of PVP MMORG elements to it. If you walk inside the other camp, you are going to lose HP. In order to become better and stronger, so more HP, you need to level up your mage by killing the enemy team.<br><br>And it is especially the level-up system that kept me playing during my first session for almost 2 hours straight. For killing you get exp and gold. The exp allows you to level up you character and to get new casts and more HP. With gold you level-up your weapon points. Like in a classical RPG you need to reach a certain level to unlock new active and defensive spell cast or different perks the improve your defensive or your attack statistics.<br><br>Overall I would say the system is already well balanced and new player are able to become better very fast. The game saves your records and even has a live leaderboard what is a great motivation factor too. <br><br>The developers plan to implement many more spells and new weapons in the future. They are very active patching the game at the moment, improve performance, fixes minor things and bring more content. They are also very open for critics and try to implement them fast. Jaudalabs is doing a very good job when it comes to be active the community. The play the game online with you and show you at the moment all the characteristics. Something that you rarely see with other developers. I like this very open and friendly approach a lot.<br><br>The early version of the game offers furthermore at the moment one medieval map. More content will be released soon as I have been told.<br><br>Whereby the game is at the moment a pure but indeed very fun arena shooter that can convinces due to the very fast arcade style and respawn within 5 seconds (like what a lot of people were asking for Onward ;)), I can see a lot of potential for further developments, for further cooperative gameplay, PVE or PVP. To be clear at the moment there is no PVE and also no crafting or survival elements. <br><br>When I say, it is a shooter you might ask yourself about how can you actually cast the spells. At the moment, you cast them directly with the motion controllers. That means on the right or left hand (you have left-hand friendly options) you hold a magic staff. In order to cast the spell, you click the trigger and a magic bubble appears in the staff. With a very realistic and natural arm movement you accelerate the magic bubble onto your enemies. As stronger your arm movement is the faster the bubble flies through the arena. This all needs some training. After a certain time, you will be able to be more precise and faster. But never underestimate the work-out effect which is recurrent with a lot of VR games. <br><br>Besides of the attack spells you can heal yourself, you cast a shield spell or some sort of magic flamethrower. Later on, in the game you get a crossbow and the talent to cast a huge meteor falling from the sky with a lot of arena damage. This looks all very sweet in VR. Besides of those spells, there is still room for more creative spells and medieval fantasy weapons in the future. I am sure JaudaLabs have some cool idea waiting.<br><br>To be a bit more precise on the locomotion. At the moment, there is teleportation (which no one uses) AND a very precise pad locomotion. Without pressing the pad, you are moving by hovering slightly over it. In my opinion this works fast and natural.<br><br>Furthermore, I did not experience any uncomfortable feeling during my 2 hours fast offensive gameplay. <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">What I like<\/div><br><ul class=\"bb_ul\">    <li> fast competitive VR arena shooter<br>    <\/li><li> smooth graphics and good performance<br>    <\/li><li> to be a mage and to be able to cast some deadly spells<br>    <\/li><li> great implementation of pad locomotion<br>    <\/li><li> in-game voice ip<br>    <\/li><li> level-up system with exp points and gold \u2013 a lot of replayability<br>    <\/li><li> leaderboard and team spirit<br>    <\/li><li> fast respawns<\/li><\/ul><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Where I see potential for further improvements<\/div><br><ul class=\"bb_ul\">    <li> indeed more content for the PVP part, more maps, more spells, different game modes<br>    <\/li><li> coop PVE and maybe even some single player experience with real levels and dungeons<br>    <\/li><li> a bigger player base, at the moment you still able to find a match<br>    <\/li><li> some minor improvements on the locomotion and the way how you cast you spells<br>    <\/li><li> improvements on the character design and the level graphics<\/li><\/ul><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"> Very good early access VR competitive shooter in the sorcerers universe.
