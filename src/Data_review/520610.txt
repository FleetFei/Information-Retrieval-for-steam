I have played this game alot on PC and iPad. It is interesting but flawed in my mind. I like the system but the whole game will depend on special rules, like prepared offensives, command confusion and then weather which can raise and lower the defensive value of terrain for just one side. Some is realistic but it drives the game a little too much in my opinion. You must master movement planning and the special rules or you will always lose. At first it is easier to play the Soviets as they have no real movement problems and fewer units to move. The Germans rarely can get their army moving, especially if you spread out. I have had half my army stationary for most the game at times. In fact, if you are Soviet you can use it to your advantage.  <br><br>Against good or expert players it will be hard to beat the German player. You learn how to make about five or six key attacks, exploit through and get units adjacent to Moscow or even in it before the mud hits. There is not much the Soviet can do but react and hope for bad combat results. Defense is absolutely supreme and you cannot displace anything other than small units without special rules or some very good luck meaning if the panzers get close to Moscow you will have a bad time trying to dislodge them even if you severely outnumber them. <br><br>Having said that, it can be fun to play. If you are the Soviet and can survive the first five turns in good order and punish the lead mobile units you have a chance. It is a long long wait to your offensive and the German army is much stronger given you may lose 60-80% of your army at the start.  <br><br>I give it a middle of the road rating, say 3 of 5. I think with revisions it could be great like it's brother, Battle of the Bulge which I prefer. Given the low price I would say it is worth buying and then playing in multiplayer after you do some learning with the AI. This new version has better AI than the old which will help.
