In other words, it's the servants that are doing all the work. Gnomes Garden is a casual time management game where you have workers that are gathering resources needed (food, wood, stone and crystals), building mines (for the stone and crystals), farms and windmills (for the food), workshop (for the wood), upgrading these for a better production and  repairing routes and other objectives (like light a fire or activate portals). However, the servants can be helped by witches (needed for some objectives) and firemen (helping objective plants to grow or to scare the little monster that will incapacitate your witch or servant from doing their jobs for a few seconds). And you'll repeat that  for 50 levels.<br><br>However, there are temporary bonus, regenerating over time, that can be activated : run faster (your minions move faster), work faster (less time for the building to give the resources) and stop time (the timer is frozen for a limited time). And these are very handy if you want to reach the three stars. Indeed, you have a timer. If you go past a star, you're loosing it. The less time you spent, the more likely you'll keep three stars. And as it's an achievement, you're also motivated. Nothing is wrong to loose one or two in the first run, so that you can try different strategies for the level. However, keep in mind that sometimes, you need to manage tightly your resources: you may be lacking some of them when they're needed and can't advance anymore (example: no more stones for a hole in the road - the building that could have given you the resource is behind this hole and there is no other road to it - you're doomed to restart). One of the good points is that you're not limited in the tasks done by the servant, if you want to gather 3 woods and 2 foods, you can check them without restriction (compared to 12 Labours of Hercules where you couldn't indicate more than 3 tasks).<br><br>As you can see, the gameplay isn't hard per se, it's just a matter of being efficient and to make the right choices (no need to upgrade a building to the maximum if the current level is sufficient or to upgrade the house - I've made the majority of the levels with two workers only). However, you don't have any other characters that can help. It's just the minions, not even the princess is present in the level to do something.<br><br>The graphics aren't bad in itself, they're cute, however, the HUD could have been more reflecting this and the list of objectives less confusing. The soundtrack was also good.<br><br>However, Gnomes Garden, while being still enjoyable, didn't have the same magic than 12 Labours of Hercules. You have more stuff to manage, true, but you can't choose your difficulty, the map is poorly designed and there, you don't have really any help from an heroin, contrary to Hercules and Megara, that were present and needed for some tasks. For the price, 12 Labours of Hercules proposes more fun for less money spent. But Gnomes Garden is still a good little game.
