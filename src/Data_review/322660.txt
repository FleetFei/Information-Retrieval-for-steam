developed by Nicalis and designed by Edmund McMillen, <b>The Binding of Isaac: Rebirth<\/b>. The Binding of Isaac: Rebirth is the sequel to the original Isaac game that was released in 2011. It built up a cult following to the shock of Edmund, prompting the release of a DLC (Wrath of the Lamb) and a completely new and remade game which came to be <b>Rebirth<\/b>.<br><br>This soundtrack fits perfectly for the game, much more so than I originally thought. I was skeptical that Danny B was not producing this OST, but my fears have been completely revoked as I have now played the game for a few hours and listened to the soundtrack separately. While some still prefer the Danny B soundtrack over this one, I can tell you first hand it would not translate as well into this game. <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Pros:<\/div><ol><li>Tracks that don't go stale after hours of gameplay but are catchy at the same time<br><\/li><li>The tracks fit the atmosphere of their partnered level perfectly<br><\/li><li>They are unique stylistically and melodically<br><\/li><li>They have more depth to them then the original game in terms of details and sound FX<\/li><\/ol><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Cons:<\/div><ol><li>Some of the sound FX in the song can be distracting or annoying, however these are only really prominent when watching Let's Plays of Rebirth due to the fact that you are focusing on the commentators voice (see the Depths track 'Abyss' for microwave sounding noises) <br><\/li><li>It takes some getting used to if you've played the original game religiously over the last few years<\/li><\/ol><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Favourite Tracks:<\/div><ol><li><b>Diptera Sonata -<\/b> This is the new Basement and is a fantastic replacement to the original. The live drums have a great snap to them and the deep and almost bassy synths never go too over the top but layer nicely with the rest of the track for great effect. The strings are directed beautifully and are sectioned more than appropriately throughout the track. But the real shining star here are the guitars. The lead guitar comes in when you come into close combat with enemies which makes the playing and listening experience absolutely phenomenal. They have great multiple melodies which compliment the track incredibly. All these elements combine to make this a catchy and unforgettable track in the game, which at this point is my favourite song in Rebirth. <br><\/li><li><b>Everlasting Hymn -<\/b> The new Cathedral track which is very atmospheric and unique at the same time. The song contains what I assume to be Latin chanting accompanied by a choir overtop an electronic\/synthy-type beat with some mellow, yet jagged low pitch piano chords cutting in every once in a while. For such an experimental track, I didn't think I would like it. But the uniqueness and melodies make it very charming and easy on the ears. My second favourite track in Rebirth thus far.<br><\/li><li><b>Genesis 22:10 -<\/b> The new title track of the game is a testament to how far Ed will go to make sure his game has the feel he wants it to have. This track has piano\/synth arpeggios overtop a choir and string section accompanied by a clap\/percussion lead minimal beat. The best part of this track is easily the choir which do a fantastic job of setting the tone of the game the first time you open it up. At this point, this seems to be the most carefully crafted song in the game in terms of direction and time spent. <\/li><\/ol><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"> Least Favourite Tracks: <\/div>I really have no tracks here that I dislike, but I will give a dishonourable mention:<br><ol><li><b>Tribute - <\/b>This is the credits track and it seems out of place. Featuring fading in and out trumpets with an out of place choir, it seems unnecessary for a credits roll. This is one of the only tracks in the game that doesn't fit in well with the part of the game it is meant to accompany. A more minimalistic and depressing approach would've fit the credits more. Other than that, as always, the instrumentation is amazing, aside for the fact that it is a little out of place. <\/li><\/ol><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"> In Conclusion: <\/div><br>I completely understand how attached people are to the original game's soundtrack, but I am totally opposed to people dismissing this soundtrack because &quot;Ew!
