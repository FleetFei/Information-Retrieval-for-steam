Deranged Rabbits?<\/div><br>Developer Mikko Nykyri is one sadistic \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665. I mean that in the nicest, most complimentary, type of way. Deranged Rabbits is a frenetic platformer where the player takes the role of Fluffy the Bunny. The objective of each level is to get poor Fluffy to the end of the level where there is a carrot waiting as his reward for surviving. But as we all know with the greats in this genre, the majority of players will spend as much time killing poor Fluffy as feeding him.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Better Than Just Another Indie Idler<\/div><br>I know that many of you may already have the game in your Steam libraries. This is because the game has already been bundled twice. The first time was with the Remute X bundle by Groupees and the second being Friday Special Bundle #37 at Indiegala. Both sites are huge boons for avid game collectors. However, there is a tendency for players to simply idle the cards back from the games that they buy in those bundles. As a result, I know for a fact that many of them never get downloaded let alone played. However, I urge those of you who have do this to check out some of the games you idle. You may just find a hidden gem that gives you some unexpected entertainment. I believe that Deranged Rabbits is definitely one of those games for platformer fans.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Well Designed<\/div><br>Deranged Rabbits is a well-designed minimalistic platformer. It forgoes a lot of the high production features that many gamers look for while aiming for high level of playability. In this regard the game is very well designed. The game introduces new mechanics at a regular enough interval where the game never becomes boring. Furthermore, the way in which the game incorporates the Steam leaderboard system encourages players to improve their times on each level in order to gain bragging rights over your friends.<br><br>The game controls as efficiently and effortlessly as it should. I have found in my time with the game that Fluffy moves as well as intended. It doesn\u2019t feel floaty and consequently no deaths feel unfair. I\u2019ve often known immediately before Fluffy has died the error in judgement or control that I have made. I have not once felt cheated. This is of vital importance for a game of this type.<br><br>The game is also well presented for the most part. Although the graphics are simple they are pleasing to the eyes and the music is thumping. The UI is easy to navigate.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Demotivating<\/div><br>Although I admit to enjoying the game\u2019s leaderboard incorporation it ultimately becomes demotivating. This is because when your friends who have played the game have stopped progressing there are no longer any times to beat. It makes you realize that they have quit the game. I found that for me this was relatively early on in the game. There are 75 levels in the game. I believe that it was just over ten or so levels into the game where I ran out of times to beat.<br><br>Yes, there are times that you can beat where you are rewarded with a cabbage. If you unlock enough cabbages you unlock special game modes. For example, there is a map that makes the screen spin. If you unlock a cabbage on that screen you can make the entire game spin. However, this doesn\u2019t prove to be as motivational to me as what I had hoped that it would.<br><br>In order to improve this situation, I would ask the developer for this or their next game to incorporate global leaderboards rather than just the friend leaderboards. If this is already available, then it isn\u2019t obvious to me how I can see this within the game.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Is it Steamified?<\/div><br>Deranged Rabbits is Steamified. It comes with a set of trading cards and 32 Steam achievements. As previously mentioned the game also takes advantage of the Steam Leaderboard system. However, better integration with the global leaderboard between levels would work wonders when your friends have stopped progressing would be delightful.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Verdict<\/div><br>I don\u2019t normally like killing cute bunnies. I\u2019m not sadistic. But I had quite a bit of fun playing Deranged Rabbits.
