games of all time. It took what was good about the previous entries in the series, improved on their problems while also bringing new features to the table to set it apart from the rest. What we ended up with was a masterpiece, and a game I have had many hours of fun with. The gameplay here is simple: you start on a map with one or several targets to kill, and sometimes some documents or items to recover. It's execution is where it shines, as Blood Money allows you tons of opportunities to complete your objective. For example, there is a mission in the game called Curtains Down. In this mission, you have to kill a french actor and his producer, who are involved in a prostitution ring. You have the option of climbing into the rafters and sniping them, switching out a prop gun with a real one and killing him in front of an audience, switching out the gun and letting the other actor kill your target, blowing up a chandelier and having it drop on the producer, etc. There are so many ways to kill these targets, and I'm sure I haven't found them all. There are 12 levels in the game in total, and they all are very unique. Unlike the last game, which was very repetitive, this game has levels set in a Vegas Casino, Mardi Gras, a French Theater, a River Steam Boat, a standard house used by Witness Protection, and many more. Every level feels unique, thanks to great level design and good atmosphere. The npc's will speak with an accent or in other languages, depending on where the level is set, which really helps with immersion. The main focus of the gameplay is replayablility, and this game does it better than any other Hitman game. Every level has several opportunities for creative kills, and finding them all always kept me coming back for more. As far as the story goes, it's weak like every other Hitman game, but the story is not why you should play this game. This game does have a few flaws, but not enough to ruin my enjoyment. For instance, the ragdolls are very stiff, like the other games. After you kill someone, their body will re-straighten itself, which is a bit immersion breaking unless you push someone to their death so you no longer see the body, or use fiber wire which would normally result in a straighter body. Another issue is that you can't aim with a gun, which makes the gunplay less fun. If you want to kill someone with a gun, you have to put the crosshair on them, and the gun will automatically come up and go down when you fire. This is a shame for me since I love the blood effects in this game. A lot of detail was used in the cloud, wall stain and pooling effects but it's not as fun when you can't aim and you end up with a straight ragdoll. Other than that, this game is near perfect. $8.99 is too cheap of a price so unless a sale is right around the corner, buy it now at full price! If they charged $20 I would have gotten my money's worth, so I'm glad it's less.
