shooter. Miami Vice seems to be a big influence on the main characters and some of the backgrounds in the game. It's obviously a nice little tribute to 80's action films an television. From fighting roving bands of ninja's in China-Town, to mohawk an machine gun wielding punk gangs in the subway system. All those great 80's action cliches are represented. <br><br>It also contains some more bizarre things like a giant pile of green slime an super sized angry Alien Donut! The games sense of humor is certainly one thing that makes it stand out. Most notably on the final stage where you discover aliens did indeed kidnap Elvis and he's being kept alive! <br><br>The pixel art is well done, creative, an colorful. Really gives off the feeling of playing mid 80's early computer games quite well. The background detail is pretty good an far from bland. Each area looks unique an stands out pretty well. I especially like the graffiti covered subway that comes scrolling by on the subway tunnel stage. The main characters almost remind me of Leisure Suit Larry from their outfits.<br><br>While the game in itself is short (having taken me about an hour to finish on my first attempt) it does offer some replayability by offering a Boss Rush mode once you complete the game on any difficulty setting. Although I will say the bosses in this game are probably the easiest part. It takes me just barely about a minute to complete boss rush. The main stages are much more difficult. But you also have the option of trying to collect all 10 unlockable weapons which you have to collect badges dropped by enemies to obtain. Everything from Bazookas, Laser guns, and even a fairly useless but funny freezing ray gun. <br><br>The concept is simple but quite fun. All you're really trying to do is dodge bullets an lay waste to enemies, while hopefully avoiding shooting the occasional civilian that comes across your path. You're given grenades, a starting pistol an as you progress extra weapons, armor, donuts for extra health, power ups that super charge your weapon and sometimes exploding barrels you can use to kill groups of enemies. <br><br>The sound effects are pretty well done. The music is also pretty good and fits well with each stage. Movement controls are very smooth an responsive using a controller. Haven't tried it out with only keyboard. <br><br>Difficulty wise I found the game fairly easy to complete even on the highest setting. But I'm also the type of person that can 1cc Cave shmups and tend to be pretty decent at difficult shooters. I would say for the average player this game is in the middle of the road difficulty wise on normal setting. Not impossible but requires some good reactions to dodge bullets. <br><br>Even if you're not that great at this kind of game, on the &quot;Thumbsucker&quot; setting I think most people could complete it after a few tries. Once you complete a particular stage the game saves your progress so you can continue back from the stage you left off at if you exit the game. <br><br>Fairly short but sweet budget indie game. No online coop but the game supports local 2 player mode. I feel like the only downside to Donuts 'N Justice is just the fact it's so short. For the low price I'd say it's worth buying.
