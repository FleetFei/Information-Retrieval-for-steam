You play the nephew of a member of the Cartographer's Guild who has spent his life planning a heist on the castle town of Redfort in order to steal a powerful artifact from the evil Alchemist.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">==========================================================<br>\n----------------------------------------QUALITY-------------------------------------<br>\n==========================================================<\/div>Graphics - Graphics generally look nice and the game performs well. The trees in particular look very nice. There are a few weird clipping issues when you approach some objects and a fair bit of obvious popping in and out of objects in the distance, and although guard models are reasonably nice and animated well, they don't quite move across the ground properly, with feet sometimes floating or moving under the level of the ground. And for some reason your character always carries around a map in his hands, making it basically impossible for you to see anything useful when you look down. In the second area I fell off a cliff while trying to see what was down there because the map was in the way!<br><br>The game world is very sparsely populated with identical guards, and there are invisible walls preventing you from departing the action areas, but the models that are used are quite sharp and the game does generally look quite nice, if a little lifeless. When ascending stairs or navigating some of the more crowded areas (e.g. the market or kitchen) I found it was very easy to get stuck on game objects, requiring me to back away.<br><br>Sound - Sound effects are lacklustre, with loud footstep sounds that don't sound that good making any movement sound like you're scuffing your steel-cap boots through piles of gravel. There is also pretty terrible voice acting at the beginning and when you reach certain points in the game, with no way to skip it. I'd have preferred simple text instead.<br><br>The music is mostly okay, though, with a suitably medieval feel to it most of the time. I did wonder about the out-of-place jazz number in Redfort at night, though.<br><br>Gameplay - Castle Heist plays like a simple version of Thief without the combat. There are no sound or vision indicators telling you when guards might catch you, and it's not always obvious when you're hidden or not, but the basic stealth mechanic usually works well enough to make the game fun.<br><br>Controls are simple: WASD to move, shift to crouch (no option for toggle, unfortunately), and space to jump. Mouse looks around, and since there's no combat and no way to use anything, the mouse buttons don't do anything. Since crouching is the most useful action in the game and there's no toggle setting, I mapped it to a mouse button instead, making it easier to move while crouched for extended periods.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">==========================================================<br>\n-------------------------------------PROS &amp; CONS------------------------------------<br>\n==========================================================<\/div>Pros:<br>+ Nice looking graphics with mostly very good models and textures (available from the Unity asset store)<br>+ Level design makes sense; the developer has made good use of the available assets<br>+ Stealth mostly works well<br>+ Mostly good atmospheric music (from Incompetech and Sleep Facing West), except for the weird jazz piece that is completely out of place<br>+ Two achievements (both hidden)<br><br>Cons:<br>- Poor sound effects and pretty bad voice acting; the footsteps in particular are annoying<br>- Movement and clipping issues, and objects not interacting with the game world properly (e.g. guards hovering or walking in the ground)<br>- There's no way to tell when someone can see or hear you until it's too late, making it more frustrating than it should be<br>- 'Always-on' map when you look down is a terrible design decision; it just gets in the way<br>- Lacks polish<br><br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">==========================================================<br>\n--------------------------------------CONCLUSION-----------------------------------<br>\n==========================================================<\/div>I'm a big fan of stealth games, but they also frustrate me a lot when the stealth aspects are done poorly. Thankfully, that's not the case here.<br><br>Castle Heist: Chapter 1 is very short: probably a bit too short for the asking price, as it can be completed in less than an hour. It plays more like a tech. demo than a game, but it is just Chapter 1 at this stage.<br><br>Caste Heist has its glitches, but the developer has done a pretty good job of fashioning an interesting enough stealth mission from some generally very nice assets.
