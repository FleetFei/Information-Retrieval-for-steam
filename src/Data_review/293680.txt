This unconventional interpretation of the classical folktale <i>Cinderella<\/i> breaks all stereotypes and social generalizations, presenting an in-depth analysis of all its characters and probable intervening dynamics. Kudos to the developer upfront!<br><br>At this point, everyone with a semi-European cultural understanding is familiar with the story of Cinderella. Poor, golden hearted young woman who is treated like a slave in her own home by her ambitious stepmother and evil stepsisters. All her kindness only meets with mistreatment until a fairy godmother appears to bless her with a magical dress, a pumpkin carriage and glass slippers to attend the ball of the Prince. There, she is to steal Prince's heart with a glance and become the queen of the kingdom after a short pursuit involving those glass slippers. There, happily ever after, right? Nope, not so much. The original story was a brutal one compared to the Disney-fied version anyhow, and with <i>Cinders<\/i> now, we are in for a more realistic version of the old fairytale.<br><br>Here, our protagonist' conditions are more or less the same with the original story. Her father has passed away, and now she is living with her stepmother and two stepsisters, whom treat her little better than a servant. She wishes to change her life, take the control of her future and decide the outcome of her own story though, rather than waiting for magic to happen! Sure, waiting for magic to happen is still our option, but just one of our options nonetheless. We are to live through a week of Cinders to witness the events just before the guard captain of his majesty knocks on our door. What happened to present such a necessity? Now, that is gonna be our choice. We will create a Cinders of our own. We'll make various choices, pick how to act towards whom and what to pursue in the way we chose. Eventually, we will meet one of the various endings appropriate for our personality.<br><br>In this glamorous visual novel, every single choice sincerely matters. Our actions determine our romance option - yup, we don't have to go happily ever after with the prince - , our character and eventually our own ending. Every single character involving - whether their role in the original story were small or grand - are reimagined into actual people, with their own personalities, background and aspirations. Did you ever think why the stepmother treated Cinderella the way she did? Just because she was evil? Or did you ever take a second glance at the captain of the guards? Why the fairy actually aided our girl the way she did? Because that's what fairies are supposed to do? Definitely not! Here, you are presented with a decent analysis of the story, stripped away from its embellishment and take a realistic approach to the situation, wrapped in genuine medieval context. The result is an incredible story with layers of characterization and perfect narrative with philosophical value.<br><br>The display is topnotch. All the artwork - both characters and the environment are fitting for a fairytale alright. Yet, this is not the strongest suit of the display. All character CG are presented in a way to maintain noteworthy body language and facial mimics, fortifying the narrative furthermore. Add a breathtaking soundtrack with various instrumental pieces of folk and epic music, and voila: here is a display environment you wouldn't grow tired of playing over and over again.<br><br>Now, I've already noted that endings are various. There are mainly 4 endings: one fairytale, one lady of the house, one \u201cwhatever, I'm out of this place&quot; and one &quot;not so happy&quot;. All these endings carry their own varieties within themselves however. According to the romance you pursue, the way you treated people and the amount of information you managed to gather by the end, you'll get a different variety of these endings. Every one of them can be tracked and re-read in the menu too.<br><br>At the end, <i>Cinders<\/i> is one of the most imaginative, deep and successfully displayed visual novels that I've ever played. Even if you are not a fan of the genre, you may enjoy this one thanks to its narrative quality, depth and even sense of humor! At this point, I'll be looking forward for any VN that Moacube would throw my way.
