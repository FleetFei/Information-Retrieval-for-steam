REVIEW<br><br>So I just beat it almost for the second time. <br><br>I played the first time when Revision was first released and I got all the way to Area 51 on Realistic (the difficulty I play the original on almost always) and had absolutely no fun at all due to the extreme tediousness of getting one-shot killed so many times. When I got to Area 51 I was just bored of the constant quick save\/load gameplay getting in the way of my mostly non-lethal stealth run and the end level just felt to daunting to even try. So I quit - and I didn't play until recently again.<br><br>This time I opted for Medium difficulty and self-imposed certain restrictions - normally I would allow myself the option to kill the MiB, MJ12 Commandos, Gray's, Karkians and Greasels. My reasoning always being that they weren't human so it doesn't really matter. This time due to the lowered difficulty level I didn't allow myself that luxury - relying mostly on my baton, prod charger, and tranq darts to get through. <br><br>It was still an incredibly difficult game this way - although I didn't really have to worry about resource management as much as I think they the item drops are more plentiful on the lowered difficulty. That being said - I had a blast, the most fun I've had with Deus Ex in a little over 5 years. <br><br>The story holds up extremely well - still my favourite out of the series, especially since it is more traditional conspiracy theory based than the Square Enix outings and more rooted in the present day than the futurism of Invisible War. But that is always what I've like about the first Deus Ex - and doesn't really talk to what I thought about Revision.<br><br>The thing with Revision is - it is better in almost everyway to the simplistic level design of the original if you are talking about the more open hub world locations. New York, Hong Kong and Paris are all better for the redesign - feeling less like big boxes filled with vague building-like shapes and more like actual places. But the reason why this redesign works so well is the team was smart enough to only modify some of the better original maps slightly adding more decoration and better lighting to already great maps. Deus Ex already had some great level design especially for the action based maps - and by retaining the layouts of some of these maps and peppering them with added detail I still felt I was playing the game I love and remember. <br><br>The biggest point of contention (other than the fact that it was released on Steam) is the new music. As a musician, audio engineer and big fan of the original's soundtrack I was very interested in the new music. First off - although I love the original DX soundtrack (it is always on my mp3 player) - I always felt that it played over the game rather than with it. The new music is excellent - playing to the mood and ambience of the scene and not overshadowing the game in the same way the original did. Although I think they missed the mark on one or two ocassions (I still prefer the original Versalife music) - the music 99% of the time is spot on.<br><br>Would I recommend this game to fans of the Deus Ex series and of the first game in particular - most definitely! <br><br>Would I recommend this game to someone playing Deus Ex for the first time - again yes, as the original although I think better balanced on realistic difficulty, is not really a great looking game and the level design does end up looking fairly rudimentary (even for the time - I mean compared to Thief 2 and System Shock 2 some of the levels look positively abstract).<br><br>I also want to say I played with Reshade 3.0.6 on the Direct X10 renderer - I experienced very little problems, and was able to use Reshade to give the game a slightly cell-shaded look which to my eyes further enhanced the modern look of the game - although I feel some might prefer the stock look of DX.<br><br>Pros - a better looking and more polished game than the original, with achievements, loading screens, bigger levels and a more balanced soundtrack. Lots of options to choose from (game play modifiers, HDTP options, etc) and it still feels like Deus Ex.<br><br>Cons - the jankiness of the original is still ever-present, realistic difficulty is tedious and not fun, one or two times the music missed the mark.<br><br>Overall based on today's standards - 8.5 \/ 10 - still my favorite game of all time, and one that I think everybody should at least try - and if they haven't and are coming to Deus Ex after playing Human Revolution or Mankind Divided, I think Revision is the place to start.\t\t\t\t\t<div Honestly, just the improved lighting alone makes the original 'Deus Ex' much more playable on modern day computers; everything else is icing on the cake.<br><br>I highly recommend 'Revision' to anyone who wishes to replay 'Deus Ex' because just the graphic improvement alone is worth the (huge) download. Plus, this mod is endorsed by Square-Enix, making it as official as a mod can be. Still, note that not all of the bugs and glitches will be fixed by this mod. For example, stealth AI is still glitchy, with enemy detecting your presence inconsistently; and there is still a delay in tranquilizer darts taking effect, causing enemies to go into alert mode.<br><br>However, considering how the modders and creators have spent years putting 'Revision' together, and then releasing it for our enjoyment and consumption for free, I say there really isn't much to complain about. Thank you, Caustic Creative, for your hard work and generosity!
