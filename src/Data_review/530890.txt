It borrows many aspects from other games such as Tomb Raider, Resident Evil, and Metroid. The game is quite difficult, with little margin for player error and requiring solid puzzle solving skills, memory, and observational skills, while also demanding an amount of reflexive ability and precision.<br><br>Despite the marketing largely revolving around the character and her &quot;assets&quot;, the game is most definitely more than that and shows clear earmarks of a development team who understands level design on an intimate level. Enemy placement is extremely deliberate and oppressive in all of the correct ways, requiring careful assertion of the situation before engaging in some cases, and in other cases quick accurate reaction to the situation presented. Some people feel the game is unfair, but I assure you that if you slow down a bit and pay close attention nothing is actually ever sprung on you without a way for you to have noticed it beforehand.<br><br>There are a handful of large general areas in the game, each having a seperate theme to its puzzles and sometimes specifically requiring certain items to even progress within the area. The game definitely expects you to remember things that were locked off in previous areas so when you gain access to a new tool you can go back and open or interact with them, progressing further. The game gives you no ingame map, so it may be worthwhile to make your own. With the game in its fledgeling state no real walkthrough exists, so you're more or less on your own.<br><br>There are only two simplistic enemies in the game, however the developers placement of these enemies mean encounters can be somewhat complex. One type of enemy can be avoided if done carefully, however the other enemy is much more dangerous (And takes fewer hits). Headshots are crucial on the Hardcore difficulty as ammo and medkits are sparse and enemies are deadly. Missed shots are a major detriment to your chance of survival, or perhaps even continuing as some rooms require combat and you are not given a melee weapon near the start of the game. Hardcore is as the name suggests.<br><br>The music design is decent, it's not a soundtrack I'd necessarily buy and listen to on its own, but it fits the game and provides a decent amount of atmosphere. The game largely lacks atmospheric sounds though, no dripping, no sounds in the distance.<br><br>The art-style itself is simple but pulled off well, it takes a clean testing facility layout similar to portal, and doesn't try to do too much with it. The robots are modeled reasonably, however Haydee's model has obviously had a lot of time put into it with the amount of animations, the quality of the model in general, and the conscienciousness concerning her breast physics and the possibility of them clipping into herself. Haydee's animations are generally sexualized, with things such as &quot;crouching&quot; (Which involves her simply bending over 90 degrees at the waist) and climbing ledges nearly shoving her rear-end in your face. It does not cause issues with the camera (Which has never once got jammed in a position where I couldn't see what was going on), but obviously is meant to appeal to a certain type of person.<br><br>The level design is exquisite. The earlier portions lacked a few things I thought might be good additions (The first item you get that allows you to progress more does not require its use to leave the room you aquire it in, requiring you to fumble around a little to figure out how to continue. Luckily at this point there is not much to fumble with, so you come upon the answer relatively quickly.), and there was one portion extremely early into the game that requires a mechanic that is not explained anywhere... However later on this is no longer a problem and their design seems to refine. Some rooms being very reminiscent of memorable moments I've personally had in other puzzle platformers such as Tomb Raider. Puzzles are honest-to-goodness challenging, and I've missed that in games.<br><br>My issues with the game are few and mostly minor. The only major point is the marketing...<br><br>Trying to sell the game based on this character's figure may work in some cases, but as has been showcased by the steam forums for this game it has brought not much more than hordes of people bashing the game simply because of the character's design. People are unwilling to try the game and see how good it actually is, and though that's arguably the people's fault, ultimately this game will get less HONEST coverage because of the sexualized nature of the character.<br><br>Jumping in this game is strange considering the genre. As a cinematic platformer, jumping mechanics are generally very static, because jumping is a thoughtful action. You don't need to worry about pixel perfect jumps or what-have-you, generally you run at a ledge, press jump a bit before, and your character will jump upon reaching the edge. In this game you still commit to jumps, unable to readjust your trajectory while in air, but it's extremely dynamic meaning you can simply miss jumps by being a little bit off. The game crash courses you in this jumping by the third room in the game, forcing you to jump over a pit from a doorway, resulting in you more than likely bumping your head, falling down the pit, and dying if you are a bit off.<br><br>The lack of a map is actually an issue, and with a 3d game I'm not sure how much effort would actually be required in making a map for the game, but I personally don't have a fantastic memory. It's a personal downfall, but something other people definitely share.<br><br>Proximity mines have a very subdued sound effect and no visual blast, leading them to be somewhat confusing to die to or generally interact with.<br><br>EDIT: Ammo boxes, medkits, and keycards have nudity on them, and the censor option used to simply add a light blurring effects over the pertinent parts. I don't mind the original images, but light blurring didn't feel like it was enough for censoring it for streaming etc.<br><br>And my most minor issue with the game is the ledge climbing animation. Not the content of the animation, I do not mind the character's design personally and respect the author's decision to design the character however they see fit... However requiring multiple button presses to simply climb a ledge is ultimately pointless. One press of spacebar to jump and grab the ledge, this is acceptable. However requiring a second press of spacebar just to have her bend over the edge of the ledge, only halfway pulling herself up allowing you a view of her buttocks is somewhat gameplay hindering. I shouldn't be required to press a button to unpause her ascention up the ledge. It's a minor gripe, and I do appreciate the character's behind, however I also enjoy simply playing the game, and this slows an already slow methodical platformer down even further.<br><br>TL;DR: Good puzzle platformer reminiscient of other slow puzzle-y adventure games.
