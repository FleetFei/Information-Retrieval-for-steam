The power ups were plentiful even though it felt random what power up you actually got.<br><br>What set this game apart from other SHMUPS is that is added in a way to turn around. No longer are we obligated to move from left to right, but we can TURN AROUND! It's a nice novelty, but it doesn't change much other than direction. There's almost no real reason to use it.<br><br>Now for some of the downsides... Syder Arcade is more of a proof of concept than an actual game. It's way too short. I played 40 mins and beat the campaign, and even that time was with intermittent pauses so I could grab another beer. With all the effort they put into the player and enemy models, you'd think they'd throw in a few more levels. Another downside is the ships acceleration\/deceleration seemed sluggish. Almost like it was on ice, even though you're in a spaceship.<br><br>Pros:<br>- Multiple ships to select<br>- Assortment of power ups to collect<br>- Great graphics<br>- Fun Audio<br>- Responsive controls (other than funky feeling acceleration\/deceleration)<br><br>Cons:<br>- WAY TOO SHORT! Seriously, there is no reason this game should be so short.<br>- Power ups seem random and you never know which will be replaced when you pick up a new one.<br>- No multiplayer<br><br>Overall Syder Arcade is a decent game but feels like the devs didn't care about it and just wanted to finish it.
