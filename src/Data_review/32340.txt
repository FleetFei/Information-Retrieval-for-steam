6\/10<\/b><\/div><br>As part of a Lucasarts bundle I had this adventure for a long time. When I was a child I read in those paper things called videogames magazines about many graphic adventures nd one of those was Loom. In these days I had a chance to play something so I decided to start Loom... And Finish it. The playtime over there is correct. But let's talk about it later...<br>Loom is a graphic adventure directed by Brian Moriarty that sees You as Bobbin, a recluse and almost despised wizard apprentice. Bobbin is summoned by the elders of the village and for some odd reason they're all transformed into swans along with all the villagers, then they fly away. Now bobbin is the last of his village and can pick up his staff to go and search all the lost swans. VERY abridged version.<br>The very innovative interface of the game requires the use of the aforementioned &quot;distaff&quot; which can play all seven notes ( you start with three, and unlock one by one the other ones as you proceed with the game). You can combine the notes into quartets to perform determined actions... Or better, spells (you're a wizard apprentice after all). How do you know the combinations? Along the game you'll see things happen which will emit notes. Simply repeat those notes to perform that action. Very creative indeed.<br>Unfortunately the game is very short, even though the story was so promising, and some puzzles were quite interesting. And the worst thing is that the ending is kind of cut, or suspended. The story never really reach a conclusion, and if it does it's a really dark one (no spoilers, please).<br>There are dialogues, but they're just a little more of the essential. Still I really appreciated the voice acting of the characters.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">+++PROs+++<\/div><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li><b> INTERFACE.<\/b> This note interface was a very innovative system, it was a different and creative way to move into this adventure, it fitted perfectly in the game's story and setting and seems odd to me that no more games of the same kind were made.<br><\/li><li><b> STORY.<\/b> I have to say that considering the lenght of the game there's a nice and interesting story behind all the game, And with a nice revelation at the end...<\/li><\/ul><div class=\"bb_h1\">---CONs---<\/div><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li><b> STORY.<\/b> ...Too bad that the story is abruptly interrupted at the end. Without spoilers, The end is a complete suspension of the story, obviously leaving every situation as it is. There were plans for sequels which never saw light, there were fan sequels project which never saw light except for Forge, with one chapter out already, but I have no idead about the project actual situation.<br><\/li><li><b> DURATION.<\/b> And this is the other bad side of the game. It took me 98 minutes to complete the game... Speechless...
