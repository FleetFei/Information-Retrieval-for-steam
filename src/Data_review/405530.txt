As a puzzle game, navigating rooms with a teleporting arrow\/beam is pretty unique, relying on position and perspective to navigate small to medium sized environments. There's a gradual difficulty curve, which I personally appreciate; it gives you simpler puzzles to introduce new mechanics or obstacles, then ramps up the difficulty. There also seem to be multiple ways to solve the puzzles, which makes the time trials and leaderboards more meaningful.<br><br>The aesthetic is minimalistic, a lot of flat colors and an overall earthy tone, not strikingly beautiful like the Witness or Journey but fairly unique and pleasant to look at. The music is a bit sparse, it sounds okay the first 30 minutes you hear it but you'll most likely just mute it and listen to your own music or a podcast. That said, there's little to no story, and sound effects aren't important to puzzle solving so this might be right up your alley if you need something to keep your hands busy. <br><br>Gameplay is simple and the controls are good enough; move with WASD and aim with the mouse, click to fire the arrow into an orb which teleports you to the same position relative to the companion orb. So if you're standing 10 feet to the left of one orb, fire at it, you'll be ten feet to the left of the companion orb. The early levels are static, letting you grasp the mechanics of navigating the stage via relative positioning. Later levels introduce ramps that rotate the stage and moving platforms which add timing to the mix. There's also a simple but genius difficulty toggle in the game, a guide that shows you where your shot will place you. Seeing this makes the game significantly more straight-forward, but you can remove it for added challenge. You can change this option on the fly with a button press, which I think is a perfect balance of accessibility and challenge.<br><br>Overall I had a positive experience with the game: very easy to pick up and play, it became challenging over time without getting frustrating or punishing, and there's a decent amount of variety in gameplay mechanics.
