Stobbart<\/b><br><br>Not as intriguing and well-arranged as the first instalment of the series, <i>Broken Sword 2 - The Smoking Mirror<\/i> still is a nice adventure and a decent sequel to a wondrous game. First and foremost, I am happy to announce that there has been no content cut in this remastered version compared to '97 original. Everything there was in the first place, is still there contrary to Director's Cut version of the first game. Also, it might be significant to mention that this is the only game in the series that wouldn't center around a mystery concerning Knight Templars.<br><br>It is a couple months after the first game's end. George has been back to America for some time to attend personal businesses - no spoilers - and Nico has been left alone in Paris. Without supervision from George and already having a knack for getting into trouble, Nico starts to investigate a party of drug smugglers that would import cocaine from South America in the guise of importing historical artifacts for a gallery. Nico gets her hands on a small Mayan tablet and Andr\u00e9 arranges a meeting with Professor Oubier, an expert on the Mayan culture and artifacts for consultation. Just coming back to Paris in time for the said meeting, George meets Nico as they arrive at Oubier's house together, only to be apprehended and left in a burning house! As Nico gets abducted by a South American Indian alongside the mysterious tablet, George starts yet another adventure after a criminal mystery which will soon turn very much esoteric.<br><br>Well, let's take a look upon this lovely setting. I have to declare that the story and power dynamics behind the scenes aren't as elaborate as the first game. It is pretty much black and white, good vs. evil this time, which is slightly disappointing. George and Nico are as witty and as able as ever though, with a notch of sexual innuendo building in between. Humor is slightly crueler and highly sarcastic too. Nico is a highly influential character in this game compared to the previous one though, not only a pretty side-kick waiting for George to save the day. As another side point, the ending wasn't that much of a satisfaction, considering the game cuts player involvement just before the actual ending sequence, leaving us with a cartoon cinematic. Oh, well... you can't always get what you want.<br><br>Display is nearly identical to the previous game. Hand-drawn art with engaging atmospheres and animated cartoon cinematics with classic scenes. Music is lovely as always too. Sadly, we are presented portrait close-ups of characters up on the screen in this remastered version, very much like the Director's Cut. Facial expressions of characters are a bit limited with those, and they mostly end up presenting characters unsympathetic, contrary to the original tone of the game. In those portraits, George was practically pouting half the time, and Nico always had an expression as if she just stepped onto something unpleasant.<br><br>As for the gameplay, it is slightly lesser compared to the first instalment. Some combination actions cannot be achieved before proper dialogue is triggered, even though the action would be quite obvious. You gotta step through everything in proper order to proceed. Fast transportation between scenes aren't there anymore either, so you keep walking on a somewhat prolonged pace. Puzzles are okay, yet not challenging much. You figure out everything at the first glance. They just mostly take time to get through, rather than actually thinking how to deal with. On a note, there is a truck load of funny easter eggs in this game, winking to some pop culture references or other games from the developer team, such as <i>Beneath a Steel Sky<\/i>. Wanna see a goat in existential crisis? Intriguing.<br><br>Last call? <i>Broken Sword 2 - The Smoking Mirror<\/i> is a decent sequel to a glorious first game, a break from the Templar storyline and an opportunity for our characters to build some more common ground + character development. If you enjoyed the first game, there is no reason that you wouldn't like this one. It is the slightly lesser cousin of the previous anyhow.
