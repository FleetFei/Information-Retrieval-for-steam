great game and if you're a fan of player made worlds, interesting crafting\/building systems, or post-apocalyptic survival games, it's definitely worth checking out.<br><br>The long version:<br><br>I started playing Xsyon back in 2011, before the game was available on Steam. The game is early access, but don't let that scare you away - in it's current state, the game offers enough content to keep you busy indefinitely. Early access games have been known to contain game-breaking bugs and unfinished features, but that's not the case with Xsyon, as the features that are in the game work. I have encountered bugs from time to time (as in any game), but I have to say that once reported, they are fixed - fast. I've seen the game's dev come into the game and talk with me personally to fix one. The game's team of moderators (guides) are active in not only helping players out with any issues or even questions about the gameplay, but they also hold events and generally seem interested in what they're doing. There have been many major patches rolling out important new features since I've been playing.<br><br>There is a skill system in place leading to the gathering, crafting, food and building systems. The crafting system allows you to create a ton of different armors, weapons, and utility gear (carts, tools, etc) from materials gathered in the world. The building system allows you to take crafted and gathered objects and build structures in the game (other players can see and interact with the structures, and you and even let them work with you in building them). The building is extremely interesting and has a large array of pieces available, allowing players to create anything from small tents and huts to immense palaces and castles (I have to admit that I personally spend a LOT of time building with my friends in the game - by the time I finish 1 project I have 5 more ideas that I want to try out). The food system allows players to hunt, fish and grow and forage lots of different things, and cook them to make food - it's easy to do the minimum, but players that really invest themselves in this system can create very powerful food items that can help in every day things. The skill system as a whole works well - it's easy to throw yourself in one skill and master it, but harder to master 2 skills, and so on. Playing solo is possible, but the game is much easier playing as a group, allowing you to focus on your chosen skill(s) without worrying about the others.<br><br>The game is definitely worth checking out - I know I've spent a lot of time playing it (I only connected my Steam and Xsyon accounts a few months ago, even though I started playing in 2011, and am at over 600 hours on Steam) and will
