class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTalk about a complete sensory experience. Vivid graphics. Emphatic music. Meditative gameplay. Compelling interstellar combat. Polynomial 2 has it all.<br><br>It\u2019s time for you to enter a universe that will have you engaging all of your senses as you dash across a gorgeous universe full of vibrant color.<br><br>Polynomial 2 is the sequel to the original, The Polynomial \u2013 Space of the Music, which to the unknowing was an indie cult classic released all of the way back in 2010. Quoting Shadowrunner, \u2018Yep, it\u2019s a wallpaper generator lacking actual gameplay, but damn, the visuals are simply mesmerizing.\u2019 The same can be said of the sequel though I would argue that there has been a greater emphasis on the game creation side of things this time around although the same engine has been utilized to design it.<br><br>With 9 levels that are uniquely different with their own goals and scenarios there\u2019s substantial content for early access purchasers to experience. This is being refined and built upon throughout the early access program. I have no doubt that there is enough content to justify the very reasonable asking price. Along with the 9 levels to play there are ten enemies, four weapons and bonuses. I\u2019d like to see these increased over time and I am certain that the developer will do just that. For those who are afraid of being able to speed run through the game and completing it without anything more to do then the fact that the game has an \u2018insanity\u2019 auto spawn level should put your mind at ease. Essentially,  its basically a survival level with all guns unlocked, health regeneration and infinite difficulty leveling system. It should prove to be a ton of fun to play because it spawns random combination of enemies based on internal ranking algorithm.<br><br>There is already over 40 minutes of original soundtrack. I quite enjoyed the soundtrack and hope that they continue building it up throughout the early access process. However, if you find it becoming repetitive then you don\u2019t need to be concerned. You can add your own music and playlists. You even have the option, dependent upon your OS (Linux now, Windows 10 down the track) to capture sound directly from your playback device. There is just so much potential within this that many games within the same genre just can only hope to compete with. It\u2019s fantastic sound design and something I look forward to continuing tinkering around with.<br><br>The game has already been developed with controller play in mind. I use my Xbox One controller to a satisfactory level. Keyboard and mouse also works rather well and though I enjoyed controller play I would suggest that the latter is preferable, to me, at this stage. It just felt a little bit more natural and functional.<br><br>Ultimately, the game feels like one of those incredible tech demos that you always see at trade shows and always wished you could get your hands on. The sensory overload will capture your attention and then steal your soul.
