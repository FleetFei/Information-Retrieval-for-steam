Then there's beam aboard once they are below 50 shields, allowing you to go onto their ships and take it out using yourself and the mech's, allowing you to take over and control the ship, something I haven't yet been able to achieve, as the bugger's block me every single time I've tried to do it!!<br><br>The game has a rather light-hearted take on itself, which you soon find out when you first try and upgrade your mech's, as your crew-mate says, &quot;You may have noticed the AI is pretty bad, bumping into walls and all that. The exploring and sandbox elements are easily my favourite, as you're free to just go out and do what you want, pretty much from the very get go, something I wholeheartedly recommend to see what the game has to offer. First off, there's a little debuff you can get if you explore certain items, which is an alien disease which slowly decreases your stats if you don't deal with it quickly. It's nice to see that there is something a little detrimental when it comes to exploring, as your character is touching test tubes, picking flowers they've never seen before, who knows what in the world they could do to you, so it gives you a reason to think twice before picking up something up that may not be great for your health. As well as the random debuff, there's the tax mechanic. At the main hub of the game, also known as &quot;Earth&quot; is a number of vendors, where you can buy almost whatever you please, but! They have a 100% tax added to them, meaning you will be paying double for whatever it is you want to purchase. You can do a few things to combat this! You can explore the far reaches of space, and look for a black market, where their tax is significantly lowered for you, but will only remain there until you've left the sector. You can either do that, or swallow your pride and just take the 100% tax with your purchase, or!!
