Racer<br><b>Release Date:<\/b>  10th of January, 2017<br><b>Type:<\/b>  Singleplayer &amp; Multiplayer<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">General Impression<\/div>PAKO - Car Chase Simulator was first released on the mobile gaming platforms in 2014 and it took nearly three more years before it would debut on PC. The graphics themselves haven\u2019t been overhauled as far as I can tell and instead went for a faux VHS tape feel and look. To be fair, the dev team has had no previous experience on Steam or with PC games, so it might take them a while until they get used to the far more versatile and powerful platform that is, proper PC gaming.<br><br>I played PAKO on my Android terminals as soon as it became downloadable for free. It has gathered more than 5 million downloads on the Google Play Store alone. Those numbers speak for themselves in terms of quality and popularity. Sadly on the iOS it still has a price tag, but I have to admit it\u2019s still far more decent than the asking price on Steam. Ironically, the game currently looks better on mobile platforms, thanks to the implementation of a chase cam with perspective from behind the cars you control, opposed to the top-down view on the PC version. PAKO still has been released less than two months ago on Steam, so I\u2019m still hoping for a few updates that will make it the ultimate version of the game.<br><br>As for the gameplay, it is still \u201ctough as nails\u201d, exactly as the developer describes it. Is it a good thing though? Well for starters, a difficulty slider or option would have been more than welcome. You can revive the retro look of arcades of the past and cash in on the nostalgia even without offering just the old school kind of difficulty that encourages rage quitting. PAKO\u2019s plot line is quite simple and straightforward: you are being chased by law enforcement units and you must evade them for as long as you can, in your own vehicle. Ten levels of various size, offer a lot if visual diversity. The cars you may switch to and from as you unlock them, are also varying in speed and proportion. It is your foes, that ultimately hinder the game with a lot of repetitiveness. Squad cars, SWAT Vans and a dubious inclusion of tanks, as if they\u2019re trying to stop a Terminator or something similar. <br><br>The enemy vehicles always attempt to swarm you and bear in mind that a single hit taken by you, spells the end of that level. You have to avoid or fight back the Law for at least 40 seconds during each stage, in order to unlock the next one. Most of the time you won\u2019t even hit 30 let alone the 60 second mark, required for some of PAKO\u2019s Achievements. You may laugh and scoff at a seemingly easy objective to reach. There are few assets on the map that you may hit with your car and not result in instant failure. Your best bet is to stick to the road for as long as you can and even drift in circles from time to time.<br><br>The presence of power-ups and weapons to fight back, are a nice touch, yet they hardly save the game from boredom. Having to repeat the same level 10 times until you reach 40 seconds of survival, doesn\u2019t help either. Perhaps that\u2019s just me. In any case, PAKO played in short sessions, is quite a decent game and the Unity-powered graphics are easy on the eyes. The jagged edges found everywhere you look, are a game design choice, not an antialiasing oversight. Some stages and unlockable cars are clearly inspired by the \u201880s pop culture and that is always a plus in my book. Steam auxiliaries in the form of Achievements and Cards, tip the odds in PAKO\u2019s favor and I\u2019m willing to recommend you buy and play it, but perhaps wait for a price drop and some well
