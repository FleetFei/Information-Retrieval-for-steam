In the game you can accept quest with the first one being to steal back a watermelon. The dungeons consist of floors which are anywhere from 10 seconds to 3 minutes long. As you continue to play this game you will unlock more heroes, more potions, and more quests in which you will get your butt kicked a bit. A key thing to note is that the heroes are very, VERY, greedy. More than a couple times a few coins would be lying around, and my heroes would run to the coins getting themselves, and most of my other heroes killed because of their greed. If you go too far away from one hero they will no longer be part of your party and you will need to go back to get them.  In the game there are 7 different types of heroes (including peasants) and 4 different types of potion.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Graphics\/Sound\t<\/div><br>The graphics are cartoony and seem a bit paper-like as of now. My suggestions do not need to be taken seriously as they are my personal opinion for hopefully adding to the game to make it better.<br><br>- Controller Support <br>- Customization of heroes (different weapons and articles of clothing to choose from)<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Final Thoughts\t<\/div><br>I have greatly enjoyed my time playing Super Dungeon Run. I have seen what the developer hopes to add and I am looking forward to it (especially funny deaths). The quests are nice and it was fun to play through this game. The only bad things that nudged at me was how easy it was to lose a hero, and how the heroes\u2019 greed got me killed more than a few times. Overall I'm happy to have had the chance to play this early access game.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">TL;DR\t<\/div><br>Super Dungeon Runner is fast-paced with floors in a dungeon ranging from 10 seconds to 3 minutes. The heroes are a bit greedy sometimes getting themselves killed for some coins. You fight through dungeon, gain money, get heroes and potions, upgrade said potions with crafting, and upgrade heroes as you level up. class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSummary: Real time party based dungeon crawl<br>Multiplayer: No<br>Completion: 5 hrs<br>Cards: No<br>Cloud: Yes<br><br>Super Dungeon Run was a rather fascinating experience. It's rare for roguelikes to feature entire parties these days, and SDR takes that up to eleven by allowing you to gather 20 allies at once (or more if you find and free them) and wander around with your lynch mob to stomp over the opposition. It's a chaotic game, and easy to jump in and start whacking away at things.<br><br>Super Dungeon Run is a realtime isometric dungeon crawler. I'm not exactly sure if the game uses procedural generation. At the very least quest floors are all the same, but I haven't really noticed any other floors repeating. Combat is nice and simple. Merely move your men in the vicinity of an enemy and they will gang up to beat the poor sobs. Two units cannot occupy the same space, so it is recommended to bring some ranged classes along to maximize damage.<br><br>The party structure in the game takes some getting used to. Classes you hire are not automatically a part of your party. To actually make use of classes, you must find them in chests, which upgrades one of your peasants. I like that you can occasionally free a prisoner and add him to your party, which helps offset a bad room that nearly wiped the party.<br><br>The loot system is a bit odd. There's no equipment in chests. Instead you may find potions, crafting materials, class upgrades, or cash. Completing a floor quickly while grabbing all the coins and killing all the enemies will net you better rewards at the end of the floor. The crafting materials are used to upgrade your class equipment, which affects all units of that particular class. The drop rate isn't too bad and doesn't require an excessive amount of grinding.<br><br>At this time the game balance is a bit out of whack. Early on it is difficult as you have a small party with low levels. But a few upgrades turns that all around and makes floors significantly easier. By the end of the game enemies were nothing more than a nuisance. While there's a fair amount of enemy variety, they are all definitely susceptible to simply being mobbed. If there were some enemies immune to ranged or melee, that could help spice things up a bit. Huge bosses with aoe attacks could also be fun. Maybe some enemies who uses status effects such as poison or charm that can only be countered by a cleric would be nice to have around.<br><br>Traps are highly lethal in this game. As your party grows in size, it becomes very clumsy as your death blob moves around haphazardly trying to follow your mouse cursor. Otherwise easily avoided traps become meat grinders because your poor men do not know how to move intelligently or one at a time. It's not always easy to spot traps in time either, such as flaming traps firing from a south wall. If there's one class I would like to see in the game it's a rogue to disable traps because boy those traps kill. <br><br>This is the first game I've seen where a mere table can kill you by doing nothing. It manages this by blocking a path, so your troops may end up getting stuck and left behind. I've had a few rooms where I had to walk at snails pace trying to get everyone to avoid walking into a table. I would have liked the ability to change formations, such as single file, because rooms such as these, or traps you must walk around are among the most lethal in the game.<br><br>The graphics are pretty good. They are very clear, and show no slowdown, and the animations are fun to look at. For being early access there's a surprising amount of polish here. The music is is also good, though I would have liked a few more songs, as they get repeated often. I haven't noticed any bugs, so good job on that front.<br><br>The game doesn't exactly end as best I can tell. Once you've hit max level and finished the last quest, nothing new opens up. I can't help but want that last bit of closure. Thus I judged the end of the game to be max level and completing all quests, which all in all takes about 5 hours. Even though Super Dungeon Run is still considered in early access, in it's current state it's entirely enjoyable. Even the base price is quite reasonable for what you get. I give Super Dungeon Run a 8\/10.
