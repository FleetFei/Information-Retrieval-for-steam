class=\"postedDate\">Posted: June 18, 2015\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t<div class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a free visual novel with an admittedly corny name and more unhelpful joke reviews than I can count, so I wasn't expecting a great game out of this when I downloaded it. I'm a huge fan of visual novels and of dating sims in particular (or any game that features dating; Harvest Moon, I'm looking at you), so I decided to give it a try to see if I was pleasantly surprised.<br><br>And I was. After having beat the game more than once at the time of this review (the first playthrough will take the player about three hours, while additional playthroughs can be much quicker due to the convenient &quot;skip&quot; feature that allows you to experience different storylines from the different decisions you make without having to reread the bulk of the story, only that which is changed), I can say that I would have happily paid for this game, and I am admittedly frugal to a fault, so that's hard for me to say. This was the first game in awhile where after I beat it, I sat there wondering, &quot;How can I support this developer? How do I give this woman money?&quot; Through some digging in the game's options, I found a link that will allow you to support the upcoming sequel if you so wish, which is something I am seriously considering. <br><br>Seduce Me the Otome features a storyline that is interesting in its entwining of the world which we know and that of a fantasical demon realm complete with magic and otherworldly beings, many of which you can romance. I knew based on the screenshots above who I wanted to romance first, but I was surprised to find that ALL of the bachelors were unique, interesting, and charming in their own right. Each character is so distinguishable from the others that multiple playthroughs grant you completely different stories and backgrounds that add to your overall understanding of the world and its characters. While the protagonist can be subject to a couple of klutzy moments where it oh-so-conveniently leads to a romantic moment, I found that this wasn't overused to the point of being annoying, and she is strong and confident in her own right. I was surprised to feel right at home when I could tell my character to use the phrase &quot;F**k off!&quot; to an antagonist rather than blush and let the men handle it, because games like this usually have fairly weak women characters, and thus they are unlike any realistic women I personally know and are therefore unrelatable. This was a breath of fresh air.<br><br>In terms of content, the storyline was interesting (romance and otherwise) and built a world in which I would fully like to explore deeper, both through additional playthroughs and further games. This is a game meant for teenagers or older. It does have some violence (never shown) and uses curse words, but not to a fault, and you'll find no fan service that takes away from the maturity of the situations characters find themselves in. This game respects its characters and their relationships; while you may make the decision of whether or not to bed the character you pursue (and again, this is fully up to you), it is tastefully done. The emphasis in this game is on the emotional bond between two people-- not the limited physical connection, and thus, I found the relationships developed in this game tended to stick in my mind as stronger and more important than others.<br><br>Lastly, I want to commend the developer for tackling mature themes, and not making relationships shallow and based on attraction like many other games. This game isn't afraid to make its characters endure trauma and have resulting fears or personality changes based on such, and that is the reality of life. This isn't a dating sim where you give a date the right piece of food and they like you more; no, this is a mature visual novel for adults, and I couldn't have enjoyed it more.<br><br>Overall, the positives to this game vastly outweight the negatives to the point that the scale is broken in full favor of one side. I found very little to complain about. There were maybe three or four typos I came across during my time in the game thus far. I would have liked a few more intimate scenes (conversation-wise, between two interested characters). Through multiple playthroughs, the physical intimacy scenes can be repetitive even over several characters, though some do change. Also, there are romanceable options between women and men, and women and women, but no men and men. Your character is, by default, a woman, so this is to be expected; though I am a huge fan of male\/male romances and would have liked the option. It also would have been nice to be able to romance more than one character and to eventually make a choice (or even have both love interests confront each other). All of these things do not even come close to overriding the positives, however (and some come down to simple design decisions, of course), so with that said, I would give this game a personal rating of 9\/10.
