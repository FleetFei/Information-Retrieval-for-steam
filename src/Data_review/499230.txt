So here's my tale.<br><br>I awaken in the sleeping quarters. The ship's computer telling me some kind of alien organism is on board. I scramble out the door, waving a pistol down both ends of the corrider. Seeing nothing I take off running down the metal decks of the ship, my foot steps echoing much louder than I'd like.  I turn a corner and meet with a couple of my crew, Brice and Joe. The three of us know the alien can take on human form, so naturally we point guns in eachother's faces. <br><br>The stand off is cut short though as machine gun fire is heard rattling off in the distance. We three immediately lower our guns, an instant truce being formed. We take off sprinintg to see where the gunfire is coming from, and as we spin around a corner we are rooted to the ground at what we see. A frantic marine is pouring round after round into what I can only describe as a 'Thing'. The marine is decimated, the gore covering the bulkheads. The thing whips its terrible head around to us and charges. We open fire. Bullets tear through the charging beast and it drops just short of Brice's trembling feet. <br><br>As we take a breather a larger group of 5 crew members shows themselves from a far off corridor. They have no trust of the three of us. Brice approached them and  tried to reason with them, that we need to stick together and that the threat, for now, lay dead. But he's shoved back by a paranoid looking mechanic wielding a make shift flamethrower. He was right to be paranoid. We are led to the canteen of the ship under gun point. Meters from the entrance a horrid scream erupts from beside me. Joe lays dead. Behind him a sickly alien appendage has erupted out of the chest of a member of the other group. One of THEM was infected! The paranoid mechanic's mind breaks, he panics. A torrent of flame washes over alien and human alike. Waves of intense heat firing out in random directions as he tries to burn his fear away. <br><br>Brice yells at me to get out of here so I take off running with him. A scientist. and a couple of marines follow along behind us. We regroup in the machine room and it's not long till Brice and I are backing away from the other survivors. The scientist sensing the tension annouced that he's got a way of testing to see if someone is really who they say they are. We all agree to let the doc try it out and take off running for the med lab, our eyes sternly watching eachother as much as the dark corridors around us. <br><br>At med lab me and Brice tell the others to stay in the corner while the scientist does his work. One of the marines mentions a sentry gun could be placed covering the only hallway into med lab. Keeping an eye on him me and Brice allow him to set it up. Med lab is locked down and it's only a matter of holding out and waiting for the blood test. A couple of those creatures tried getting down the hall, but between the sentry and our rifles they stood no chance. Things seemed set.<br><br>Klaxons started, emergency lights swirled, and the ship's computer calmly announced a fire had started on the bridge. We all panicked, that fire had to be put out. Brice picked up an extinguisher and sprinted out of med lab. No one else followed, so cursing I ran after him to back my friend up, running away from the safe zone. Brice was ahead of me and not slowing down one bit. As I tried to keep up with him the ship's lighting grew dimmer in these sections. I faintly heard the sound of the sentry gun kicking off back in med lab, but I was already committed to my task. Smoke started filling the corridors and with the lack of lighting I started to feel disorientated. I forced myself on, barely keeping track of the twists and turns Brice took as he grew further ahead of me. To hell with the others I thought, me and Brice are the ones risking our lives to put this fire out, we deserve to be the ones to survive. The hanger isn't far from the bridge section anyway, we can do this. <br><br>Still running I turned a corner and slowed. At the entrance to the bridge Brice just stood there in the stygian smoke holding the used extinguisher. The computer announced the dangerous state of the bridge had been contained. Excitedly I walked over to Brice and told him we had to go! The fire was out, we can get out of here now! He turned around to me, dropping the extinguisher and said ''I was waiting for us to be alone''. <br><br>A slithering jagged mess exploded out of his chest impaling me. I slumped to the cold metal floor. The last sight I had was seeing Brice walking toward the hanger to get in the escape shuttle. Of course I know now, that it wasn't Brice.<br><br>SOOOoooo yeah, that's the game. class=\"postedDate\">Posted: August 2, 2016\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t<div class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSurvHive seriously caught me off guard - until the moment Dread Central's news flash appeared on my Twitter feed, I was totally oblivious to existence of this title. Belgian 1+ (one man operation with additional helping hands) dev team Rocking Hamster announced SurvHive will be multiplayer horror experienced inspired by John Carpenter's The Thing and that was it - there was no other way but to try it.<br><br>SurvHive is asymmetric horror experience pitting surviving humans against shapeshifting aliens who thrive on infecting other life forms. The goal's pretty easy - if you're human, survive at all costs. If you're alien, infect\/kill them all. At the beginning of every round, all players are informed who they are, but there's no way knowing who are the others players (except little tricks which you'll find out as you play the game). Everyone spawns on random locations throughout the map.  Each player can choose a class which differs in various traits players earn while playing. All these traits are helping with objectives on the map, meaning you can do the objective only if you've mastered a certain trait. <br><br>Now, objectives are the horror of it all because they usually pit a large number of people in the same room and that's when paranoia kicks in. There are many different objectives, but almost all of them require players to come close together in the same room. <br><br>SurvHive provides its players with supreme feeling of paranoia and dread, even in this Early Access phase. Game is multiplayer only with no bots, so all the games I've played so far had different outcomes because beside the fact everyone's hiding (and preying), people were literally arguing over chat to prove they REALLY ARE THE HUMANS! It fits The Thing niche nicely. <br><br>Humans and aliens are distinct in many ways, but in this game one can make you die easily and the other is super cool. Humans have a sanity level which drops with bad decisions and killing fellow humans. As your sanity level drops, you'll start hyperventileting which will let nearby aliens know you're human. Also, aliens go 2001's Alien in this one - when you shed your skin and explode into superior lifeform, you're able to wall climb. I find this feature super fun if you're into human hunt just as much as I am.<br><br>Game currently has two maps available. Both maps are wide, very SF inspired, lot of System Shock II hints. Although there are only two, dev is promising more and these two will certainly provide a lot of fun for the players. Graphics are not really the selling point of this game, alongside animations which seem crude, but I've never ended up stuck somewhere or unable to move which is fine. Also, I really don't give a flying \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665 about graphics if the game makes me want to play it over and over again - I'm the dude playing DOOM 2 online. It will certainly get better or it will not, let's just hope the atmosphere and fun stick around. Hit detection is a bit off which can annoy if you're an alien trying to infect a person, but in a strange way it makes preparation of every sneak attack a really complicated feat. Which, as it turns out, is a good thing.<br><br>SurvHive is definitely a diamond in the rough and fantastic gaming surprise. It needs a lot of polishing, tweaks to classes and traits, more weapons, better animations, better sounds, more disgusting aliens (YES!), but so far, I've had nothing but GREAT fun playing it. It's shaping to be a great game and it needs more players and bigger input from fanbase because, judging by the first few hours of playing, it delivered more fun than Evolve and Dead By Daylight combined. Sorry, big boys.<br><br>***<br><br>Hey, you've liked this review? Hated it? It made you buy the game \/ abandon the idea of buying this game? Super awesome!
