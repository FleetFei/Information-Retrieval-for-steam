Unless you are closely watching everything in real time, the various drum beats mean nothing and blend in with the sound track.<br>- There is no clear delineation between who is friend or foe until they start attacking you. There should be some colour system to indicate what your current diplomatic status is.<br>- There is no clear indication of garrison or garrison strength. There needs to be some visual cue that the camp is unguarded.<br>- Supply lines are not clearly articulated. I want to hover mouse over line and see what the flow is, and what the rate of change is. I want to know the network, of where supplies are going, and how long to get there.<br>- Diplomacy to way too simplistic. How is it can chose what treaty to have... can't the other side have a say?<br>-How do I know the stats of an enemy asset? Wouldnt this be a mystery?<br>- Faction flags are poorly done. These should clearly different from each other. Suggest and Icon.<br>The use of figurines in general is poorly done. Its like trying recreate a board game, The figurines lack detail, need more colour differentiation and better indication of what their strength is.<br>- Zoomed in Icons are too similar\/ not easy enough to distinguish between unit types. More details needed on icons.<br>- Resource problems... all the time... starving troops... swarming enemy... undefended settlements... no guidance from the tutorial, or manual. Its poorly presented.<br>- No visual cues on stockpiles of resources at a settlement.<br>- The icons indicating no food, poor morale, not in supply line, whatever the lightening symbol is etcare not clearly explained. If you get it, prepare to be disapointed, but maybe you'll see the good side too.
