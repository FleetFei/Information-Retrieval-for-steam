class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Great Whale Road marks the Steam debut for Sunburned Games. This is a development studio based in Valencia, Spain. They are studio of ten people who have poured their heart and souls into creating a story-driven RPG with turn-based tactics and cards.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">The Great Whale Road - The Story<\/div><br>The game is set in the seventh century. You travel the famous Whale Road, the North Sea. It is up to you, brave leader, to not only save your clan but to make it thrive. As a result this is an epic challenge that is full of danger but also full of reward. It's not going to be easy. These types of games never are. But it will be worth it in the end if you are victorious and become one of the legends of your time.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Hand Drawn Art &amp; Soundtrack<\/div><br>The locations are all drawn by hand within the game. This is undoubtedly one of the highlights of the game as they are beautiful. They help set the mood and the atmosphere. They remind me of some of the old viking picture books that I used to read as a child. As a result, the graphics were a resounding victory for me and kept me playing.<br><br>The soundtrack appropriate for the time and the setting. I'd like to see an increased selection of tracks by the time that development is complete. A little too early to be too critical in this department but a more varied soundtrack would add to the richness of the experience.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay<\/div><br>Winter<br><br>The primary gameplay mechanic utilized here is that of two seasons - winter and summer. Winter is where you do your planning for the upcoming summer. In summer you carry out your actions and perform your quests. I found that this system has been well designed and that it works well with the game. Many players will feel somewhat at home immediately.<br><br>Learning how to be successful in the planning stages of the game is of vital importance. Here you get to allocate your points into a number of different areas such as hunting, farming, traditions, etc. In many ways these concepts are borrowed directly from city building or God games games where you control your populous.<br><br>During the winter a number of random events occur. I've played through the game several times now and each time I've gotten a different event on the second and subsequent events. The first always appears to be the same. Selecting the right option can give you a helping hand. However, I believe that if you pick the wrong option you can ultimately lose just as much.<br><br>Summer<br><br>The summer is the most active part of the game but no more or less important than winter. Summer is where everything is active. The gameplay forces you to think on your feet especially when you get into combat. Random events can and do occur throughout the summer seasons.<br><br>Summer is also the part of the game is where card and turn-based combat enters the play. This was a system that has been utilized in different games but the developers have honestly tried to implement their own version of it. Some of the changes were relatively tricky to come to grips with and I admit I lost early on, quite devastatingly so. However, I quickly learned that this is where even more strategy came into play. This is an area of the game that needs tweaking and refinement. It's not there yet but there is an opportunity for it to become more<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Too Much To Absorb?<\/div><br>I have a feeling that this will elicit quite a few negative reviews by players who only put in an hour or so worth of time into the gameplay. It's not for that audience. This is a game that will arguably take a couple of playthroughs before people truly appreciate the game and what it has going on. I wont suggest that there is as much going on as a Civilization game. However, that is the type of crowd that I believe the developers have targeted. I certainly had similar feelings starting out with this game as I did in my first hour or so of playing Civ.<br><br>There is a lot, arguably too much, to take in at first and you will probably suffer through periods where you feel lost. You may even feel that you're playing multiple different games. So be warned - the tutorial screens are your friends. Read them thoroughly and take your time to absorb the information. Please don't just rush right through them even though they are all able to be brought back. There's not much worse than having to re-read and re-read tutorial screens that you rushed through in the first instance.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Early Access Bugs<\/div><br>I ran into a glitch in one part of the game. I feel that it is worth mentioning, however, it did only occur the one time. On my first quest I discovered a graphical bug when I attempted to click on the journal and subsequently the map. What happened was that whatever was supposed to load clearly didn't like what I was doing and refused to load. I ultimately quit my game and started again. Thankfully, upon reloading and not being over eager to do something I was able to continue successfully. Hopefully<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">What's to Come?<\/div><br>This is really just the tip of the iceberg for The Great Whale Road. Though what has already been released with the Early Access release is one storyline the developers intend to release three more. There will be one each for the Northumbrians, Franks and Picts. The second half of the initial storyline is to be developed and released as well. The developers hope to strengthen what is already a very reasonable AI. They also want to improve the trade system (I wouldn't call it bare bones at the moment but it can use some improvements). There is much more to come.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Is it Steamified?<\/div><br>No. The game has yet to make the additions of Steam trading cards or Steam achievements. As always these are a very simple addition to the game and will likely be added before the game leaves Early Access.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Verdict<\/div><br>There are questions that you must ask yourself before I can advise you to purchase The Great Whale Road. Those questions basically boil down to whether or not you have the time to invest into a game that, if you find yourself involved, will demand your attention. Are you willing to provide feedback to the developers? If you are happy to provide feedback and have the time to commit to the game then I believe you will be satisfied with your purchase. There is much to learn and a sizable learning curve.
