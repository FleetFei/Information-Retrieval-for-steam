These are not the kinds of conversations you would typically find in a video game, but they feel right at home in this vintage road-trip setting. And to be honest, it is in these conversations that you find the real meat -- and arguably the point -- of this game.<br><br>You see, the driving aspect is deliberately simplisitic, but provides a welcome sense of forward momentum. A perfect backdrop, in my mind, which does a good job of emualting the calming, yet distracted feel of conversing while keeping one eye on the road. The conversation mechanics are similarly simple: you either pick from one of two scripted responses\/questions, or you choose to stay silent. That's basically all there is to the gameplay, and it results in a fun, fluid, and deceptivley-breezy pick-up-and-play experience.<br><br>I say &quot;deceptive&quot; because the topics themselves can be quite heavy. This is a post-dictatorship Italy, struggling with economic and social crisies. There are progressive movements, but they face strong neo-fascist or conservative oppostion. In short, it's hard not to blame the player character for wanting to get the hell out of dodge. But as I've said, it's breezy, so don't expect a deep dive into these topics, either. For me it was just enough to pique my interest so that I began to read about some of the issues elsewhere, and the game injects enough charm and character so as to not weigh the player down at any moment. Breezy.<br><br>One thing to note is that each play-through takes somewhere between 15 - 20 mins, but each can branch in different directions depending on player choices and what seems to be a bit of a random element.
