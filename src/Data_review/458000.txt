sizeable role as one of the game's event designers. As such, this review may contain elements of bias, but I will try to be as honest as I can, from my perspective as a gamer. I played more hours than you can see through various dev builds pre-Steam, so I'd like to say I've seen much of what the game has to offer.<br><br>March of the Living has some similarities to FTL: Faster Than Light, an excellent game which was openly admitted to be a large influence. As one of four playable survivors, your main goal is to pass through three zones, each gradually increasing in difficulty, and reach the end sector, picking up other survivors and supplies along the way while dealing with situations and quests as they arise in each sector. All this is done while managing your supplies of ammunition, food and items, whilst balancing health, hunger and fatigue. It's a fairly simple concept that, for the most part, works pretty solidly. At least during my playthroughs, I tended to find a considerable amount of supplies as I was rather thorough with scouring the map, but you'd be surprised how fast that can go when you have several party members.<br><br>The combat isn't the most complex model out there, but it works well enough. You can control up to six party members in real time, with a pause option for sorting through each character's actions. This makes sure it doesn't get too overwhelming. There are four main weapon types, which are melee, pistols, shotguns and rifles respectively. Each character has their own preferred weapon type\/s, though they aren't restricted by said preferences, so characters aren't completely useless when using gear they aren't skilled with. You'd be well advised to keep one melee weapon on hand, but with the limited inventory space... eh. <br><br>You'll find both human and zombie enemies, which do have some subtle differences in colour palette and attacks. The former tend to be infinitely more dangerous however, so be wary. Rushing into a fight with a trio of bandits isn't wise if you're going solo, after all. Playing smart and knowing when to flee will come in handy. I personally found using the pistol's uncanny ability to headshot fairly often at short range to be a workable strategy throughout, though that may be changed. In a prolonged fight, you may cause enough noise to attract other zombies. This is most likely to happen in cities, but can happen out on the roads too. It'll eat through your ammo if you let it.<br><br>It's... not really a good idea for me to judge the quality of the writing, given the circumstances, but I do find that it is more than adequate for the purposes. There's much which could be done with the setting, but hopefully it can be expanded upon with mods or with future intervention from the devs themselves. I will say though that it's easy to add in custom events by following the simple structure shown in the games .XML event files. Notepad++ and a bit of practice\/study of the given files makes it a breeze.<br><br>It's not the most visually spectacular game out there, but that's a by-product of the art style more than anything else. It works fine, and can be edited\/added to easily enough. I found the music to be a nice touch, suiting the atmosphere of the game. It might get grating after a while, but that might also be because of the amount of time I spent listening to it pre-release during game testing... Of course, this is all replaceable, or will be in due time.<br><br>If I had any complaints, it'd probably be with the map's random generator, but the settings for that can be changed fairly easily. That's a running theme with the game, incidentally. Everything can quite easily be changed if you look through the files and edit some variables. Tailoring the experience isn't too difficult at all.<br><br>Overall, I find the game to be rather entertaining. It's not perfect by any means, but it's defintely competent in all the right places. It's a simple enough concept that pretty much anyone will grasp the main gameplay mechanics within a few minutes at most. The game as it stands is reasonable on its own, but it also functions works well as a base for expansion by enterprising players.
