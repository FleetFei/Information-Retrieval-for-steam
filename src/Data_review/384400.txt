I don't actually know what these little things you shoot at are. ANYWAY:<br><br>The game is simple and has you fending off waves of enemies with many different weapons, whilst dodging stage hazards. Spike traps, saw blades and fire pillars are going to make your experience a challenging one. The many weapons you acquire and upgrades you purchase will help you eliminate all of the enemies and progress to the next stage, before facing the end-of-level boss. Needless to say, it'll be challenging. Or will it?<br><br>The title of the game would have you believing you'd be up to your neck in these weird little blobs you have to shoot in the face, but the issue is that this is not the case. Enemy spawns are very sporadic, and you could be sitting for nearly half a minute at some intervals with no enemies to fight. What gives? Where's the horde? I thought this game was 'semi-hardcore!'<br><br>The hardest element of the game is really just based on luck. During waves, weapon chests will spawn and will contain a random weapon. It's down to luck whether you get a Laser Minigun or a freakin' Nerf Gun. It is quite humorous at times but it seems like a cheap method to make the game more difficult, when in the first place, it really isn't. Another method employed to make the game seem harder includes powerups, such as 'Win $300 or lose all cash', or 'Get Full Health or Die.' Bearing in mind that you pay for these upgrades with the cash you earn from killing enemies, and combine that with the infrequent and irregular spawn times of the enemies, and you're left wondering why these upgrades even exist. Maybe you could use the 'Full Health or Die' one at low health as a last ditch resort, but good luck even getting to low health in the first place. It's literally point and click at green blobs on the screen, yawn, wait 30 seconds for more to show up, repeat.<br><br>The game does include some cool little soundtracks, but unfortunately there aren't many, and they loop until you die or finish all waves and go on to the next level, which could be a lengthy ordeal. You might like the music the first time you hear it, but it will start to slowly have you descending into madness once you've heard it for the 30th time, for another 20 minutes.<br><br>The enemies kinda suck. You've got the standard green blob, which just follows you about until you end it's pitiful exsistance. Then you've got the red blob, which does the same, but a little bit faster. Then the robot ones, which do the same, but they take more damage, then the orange ones..... see a theme recurring here? You've also got the large green blob, which runs about the level, until you shoot it, then it explodes into harmful projectiles. It's really not at all exciting, and they aren't much fun to fight.<br><br>The bosses are decent though, and quite a challenge to beat. You will have to pay attention and keep light on your toes to defeat them, but getting there is the problem. You have to get past 10 waves of blobs first, and if the game is generous, it'll send them all after you at once. Otherwise, you WILL have to wait ages to finish them all off. If you die here, you'll have to do it all over just to get a shot at the boss again. And it's a painful procedure.<br><br>To conclude the review, I would like to say that although the game does have it's flaws, it would, in my opinion, benefit from one simple fix - More hordes! I want to be fighting tons of these blobs off while fending them off with Poison Shotguns and Scoped Revolvers. If the game was perhaps patched to increase the spawn rates of the enemies, maybe it wouldn't seem as boring. As I previously mentioned, there is a lot of waiting for enemies to spawn, and it is then where the flaws of the game start to appear.
