elements. While the game may not seem much there are a lot more into it as it takes a simple idea and involves it into something more. I was pleasantly surprised of the use of its numerous mechanics as I unlocked each one of them and using them altogether!<br><br>The game consist of 2 platforms that always cross, or to say it better, 'Intersect' each other at your location. Essentially you play a platformer at 3 dimensions, each dimension follows you and has a Cube in it. To finish the level you must get the 2 Cubes to touch. As easy as it may sound at first, after some levels the true fun will start, along with some frustration and a mild headache. The game's mechanics ramp up at a good pace, having none of the special blocks at start and each added after a set number of levels. Each is unique and it interacts with the others in a marvelous way that level design highlights even more!<br><br>In general the game give you a feel of chilling, zen, allowing you to play at your own pace. The UI and music fulfill this idea by offering calm earthly colors, wonderful animations, platform design and chilling music at all times. Most levels are meant to be beaten either by moving among the dimensions or by some use of the puzzle elements the game offers. However there are few levels that feel like a huge rock thrown in the peaceful lake this game is. They require precision platforming or unneeded use of a repetitive, in that case, mechanic. While the later most people will find it boring and eventually beat the level, the first can lead to frustration and even quitting as there is no skip button.<br><br>Additionally the game has a small story that is unfold by few select levels with a DOS screen behind a single platform. You are free to read it or simply run to merge the 2 Cubes and move on.<br><br>However the gray part of the game is its controls. You move with WASD jump with Space and move the camera with the mouse, but you cannot customize them at all. While there is no need for anything complex as the required input is basic the game offers partial controller support, or even better, unfinished or accidental controller support. On the 360 controller I could use the left stick to move but there was no button assigned for jump and I could not turn the camera with the right stick. If controls where customizable I could perhaps bind jump to a mouse button at least...<br><br>Overall, I do not think InterSection is worth its cost, as there are only 100 premade levels and even with workshop support, I cannot see myself returning to check workshop levels after I've finished the game. But by no means it is a bad game, in fact it is a really good one and I quite enjoyed it most of the time (that level 27....AAARG). It is perhaps a great example that not every idea has been used, or every mechanic has been made as it's simplistic visual design hides a huge innovating idea behind it.
