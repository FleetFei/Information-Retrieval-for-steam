class=\"bb_h1\">A need for <span class=\"bb_strike\">speed<\/span> braking early before a turn so you don\u2019t flip the car. <\/div><br>Cranks &amp; Goggles is a simulation-focused, top-down racing game which recently released on Steam through the Early Access program. C&amp;G features twenty-six different cars across four different classes from the 1920\u2019s and 1930\u2019s. You can race these cars on sixteen tracks from across the world either through the game\u2019s Quick Game mode or the Career mode with a multiplayer mode planned for later in development.<br><br>Being a racing game, most of your time in C&amp;G will of course be spent racing around a track in your car and let me tell you, it\u2019s rather challenging. It turns out that race cars from the 20\u2019s and 30\u2019s, fitted mainly with thin tyres and driving on predominantly dirt\/sand tracks are fairly difficult to handle. The cars aren\u2019t all the same though, even within a class there\u2019s huge variation. The car you start with in the career mode is reasonably stable and is capable of turning at speed without losing traction but then one of the \u2018better\u2019 cars in the class is much faster but I found I couldn\u2019t go around corners as fast without losing control. The actual vehicle physics in the game are very good (although it does go a bit crazy sometimes when you crash into something) and it seemed fairly realistic to me, though I\u2019m by no means an expert on how 20\u2019s and 30\u2019s cars handle.<br><br>While some cars may look better than others on paper, how well you perform in a race really comes down to how well you can drive the car you\u2019re using. When I started doing C Class races in the career mode I had enough money to buy the best car (on paper) so I did. When I started using it I realised that despite it having the best stats I really didn\u2019t like it so I went back and bought a different car and ended up doing far better in the races with it. It does take a while to familiarise yourself with how a car behaves, it took me a few attempts to perform well with the first D Class car but once I\u2019d got the hang of it I had great fun.<br><br>That\u2019s one of C&amp;G\u2019s greatest strengths, the variation between all the cars. You can \u2018perfect\u2019 your performance with one car but as soon as you buy and start using a new car it\u2019s like you\u2019ve completely started over and you have to learn how to play the game again. I found that it took me at least one full race (three laps) to get to grips with a new car and considering each class has four races followed by an event (apart from A Class which has three events) I\u2019d say that\u2019s pretty good. Those looking for a more casual experience might not be so keen on it but I find the variation helps keep the game interesting over the course of the career mode and it also adds to the overall realism.<br><br>Sticking with in-game variation, there\u2019s also a good amount of variation with the game\u2019s sixteen currently available tracks. Some tracks see you hurtling through the Italian countryside, some tracks take you to the Middle Eastern desert and some have you flying around tight corners in an English port. I\u2019m presuming that more tracks will be added since it\u2019s in Early Access at the moment but even if there aren\u2019t any plans for more tracks I\u2019d say that the sixteen in the game offer enough variation for the game\u2019s reasonably small asking price.  There are a couple of tracks which are just different versions of one another but other than that they\u2019re unique tracks from across the world.<br><br>Although I\u2019ve enjoyed what I\u2019ve played of Cranks &amp; Goggles so far, I do have a couple of problems with it. My first problem is that the AI opponents don\u2019t always appear to react to the player. There were a number of occasions where, due to what my car was doing, I would have expected a nearby opponent to change their course to avoid me but they didn\u2019t. My other main gripe with the game is that you can\u2019t sell any of your cars which means that if you don\u2019t have enough money for a car that you need\/want you\u2019ll have to go back and re-play races you\u2019ve already done. Lastly, there\u2019s no opponent difficulty option. I found this frustrating because, while I found the D Class races to be fairly challenging, I found the C Class races to be far too easy. It would have been nice to have the option to increase the difficulty of the C Class races I found particularly easy. I didn\u2019t find any of these issues to be bad enough to detract from my overall enjoyment of the game but they\u2019re definitely worth considering.<br><br>The only other issue I had was with the way the Quick Game mode works in the game, though I\u2019d imagine most people will be playing the game\u2019s career mode anyway. The problem is, you can\u2019t choose what car or track you want to play on, it\u2019s random. Not only would it be more enjoyable, in my opinion, if you could choose the car and track, it\u2019d also be quite helpful for choosing what cars you want to buy in career mode which would help avoid wasting your race winnings on a car you end up not liking. <br><br>There are a couple more things which, while not really issues, could use some improvement. Personally, I\u2019d like to see either a bigger mini-map or a more zoomed out mini-map. Since you can\u2019t see too far ahead you\u2019ll rely on the mini-map for knowing what\u2019s coming up and while the currently implemented map is acceptable, it\u2019d be good if you could see a little further ahead with it just to make it a tad easier to prepare for turns. The other thing is I\u2019d like to see full controller support. Have you got your goggles? Great, me too! Let\u2019s crank our engines and go racing in the 1920\u2019s! <br><br>Cranks And Goggles is a top-down racing game set in the 1920\u2019s. As you launch the game, the first thing you will be doing is to customize your driver. It took me quite a while do so, not because it was difficult, just because I was drifting from side to side listening to these fabulous 1920\u2019s tunes!  After drifting to the music and setting up your racer, you will arrive at the lobby screen with a sum of money that has been allocated to you. From here, you can either go to the garage or go racing. <br><br>At the garage, you will be able to see cars from all classes (D to A), with their specifications, which are: handling, acceleration and speed, of course. You can purchase new cars if you have the money, and upgrade them too. <br>Each class has four tracks to race and if you successfully step on the podium (1st, 2nd or 3rd) in each of those races, you will unlock a championship for that particular class before moving up to the next one. The only difference in these sequences is that you will unlock three championships if you triumph in the class A. Each win comes with prize, which is a large sum of money. Accumulate the wins and the money so you can upgrade your car at the garage, or buy your next car. <br><br>Races are usually between two to three laps with six racing drivers, including yourself. Depending which car you are using, you might find yourself off the mark in terms of acceleration but will probably catch up with your opponent fairly quickly. What you really have to remember is how good you are on the breaks and at handling your car.  There\u2019s a lot of drifting in this game - well, the roads in the 20\u2019 were not as good as they are now, and the tyres did not have as much grip as they have now.  The safety features around each track are limited to barriers, stacks of tyres, cones and crates. You can bump into the tyres and cones and consequently push them into the middle of the track. If, on your next lap, you run into them to fast, well . . . you will have an accident and probably lose several positions, too! <br><br>Difficulty wise, first you can only play with your keyboard, which is challenging but fun. I found the first class quite hard, to be honest, but it seems to become easier as your progress within the game. Cranks and Goggles does have couple of glitches but they are not drastic and you will have fun with this game. Careers and quick race modes are available in this early access, and online mode will come later on.<br><br>Fun cell shaded graphics and a great soundtrack add to the enjoyment.
