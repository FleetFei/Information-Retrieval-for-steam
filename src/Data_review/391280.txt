class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\t<b>Epic Manager - Create Your Own Adventuring Agency!<\/b> (or Epic Manager for short) mixes elements from management games like Football Manager with RPG elements from games like Adventurer Manager and from board games such as Dungeons and Dragons. - <u><b>this is going to be a rather big one so just jump to the bottom if you don\u2019t feel like reading everything<\/b><\/u><br><br><b><div class=\"bb_h1\">Story:<\/div><\/b><br>The story takes place after the end of a great war which unified various, once divided, kingdoms under the Realm of Astraeus. Despite bringing relative peace to the land, the end of the war meant that a lot of soldiers were left without a job. Unable to provide for their families many of them got together and formed guilds of adventurers that would roam the realm looking for quests but, due to bad management, a lot of them were led to ruin and some, even their deaths. In order to provide a relative safe space for adventurers to work a league is created for adventuring agencies to compete. The winner of said league will then be chosen as the ultimate Epic Manager. This is where you come in. <br><br><b><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay Overview:<\/div><\/b><br>You play the role of the manager of an adventuring agency and you get to create your own by giving it a name and creating a coat of arms. Since you\u2019re <i>only<\/i> the manager of the agency, you\u2019re not an adventurer yourself, so you have to hire adventurers to go on quests for you. This means you\u2019ll have to pay them weekly wages, how much that will be depends on the contract you\u2019re able to negotiate with them before you hire them. Still, if you can\u2019t afford to pay the cost of a week\u2019s work, that will be covered for you at the expense of some of your fame.<br><br>The way the <i>League<\/i> works is as follows. On the game map you move\/act in turns, which are split into 2 phases, first you give orders and then you see those actions unfold. Each turn corresponds to a week in game time and in each week you have a certain amount of scouts you can use to look for quests, sabotage other agencies, buy access to portals, which you can use to travel between towns, among other things. At the end of every trimester the 2 agencies with less fame will be eliminated from the league and at the end of each year the top 3 agencies will be promoted to the next league. Your fame is determined by things like taking contracts, completing quests and discovering new locations, where you can activate portals. As you play you\u2019ll eventually gain points and increase your agency level, allowing you to upgrade your agency granting you various different perks and abilities.<br><br>The Adventuring Agency League is definitely the most captivating and unique feature of <b>Epic Manager<\/b> for me, but the game does also have some other neat little mechanics that combined together make for a very appealing whole. For example, everyone in Astraeus worships a certain god and every now and then one god will be stronger than the other, granting bonuses to it\u2019s worshippers (your adventurers). I also like the fact that you can take quests with different nobles that aspire the throne of Astraeus and, in doing so you\u2019ll be opening new job opportunities in the future. However, dealing with one noble might create some friction with another one and the same goes for other agencies. Rival agencies can try to steal your funds, spread rumours about you - decreasing your fame - but they can also try to appease you with gifts.<br><br>Travelling throughout the map can be done by either moving your parties through the different territories or by using portals to instantly go from from city to city. Resorting to traditional travelling means or setting up camp can trigger random encounters which can be something as a fight, drinking challenges or some mysterious stranger proposing you something. This part of the game handles like your typical Dungeons and Dragons run by resorting to dice and percentage chances.<br><br><b><div class=\"bb_h1\">Combat:<\/div><\/b><br>As far as combat goes, it works pretty much like in most turn-based party games with the objective being to eliminate all hostile forces while trying to keep the losses to a minimum. In each round your party members will have a turn in which they can act and use their action points (AP) to attack, use an item or skill - unused AP carries over to the next turn and each turn your characters will gain more AP. The combat is varied thanks to different types of damage (e.g. ice, fire and physical), different buffs and debuffs as well as thanks to the huge variety of classes ranging from warrior, spellsword, witch doctor, cleric, monk, thief, archer, etc. Each character has 4 base stats that will determine their role, combat which determines their physical damage; agility which determines their chance to land a critical hit, dodge an attack and how early they act in a turn; arcana which determines the effectiveness of your spells; and charisma which determines how much AP does a character have and how much they regenerate at the end of each round. While fighting your characters can also get wounded and because of that they\u2019ll have their overall HP reduced. Also, like the way it works in Darkest Dungeon, once one of your characters loses it\u2019s HP, it will enter a \u201cnear death state\u201d in which the next hit will kill him permanently (still, you\u2019ll be able to recover their gear).<br><br><b><div class=\"bb_h1\">Complaints:<\/div><\/b><br>Considering it\u2019s still in Early Access, I\u2019d say it\u2019s already a pretty good and fun game, but that does not mean there isn\u2019t room for improvement. For instance, I\u2019d like to see actual animations of the various characters attacking their enemies, instead of just swinging their weapon in front of them. Also, as I took various different quests I came to notice a lot of repeated quest dialogues and I guess it makes sense, since they come from recycled random encounters and quests but I\u2019m hoping that more work is put into adding more variety and more different and interesting lines.<br><br>So far I\u2019ve only noticed a rather annoying issue and it appears to happen randomly. Sometimes when loading a saved game, or starting a new game, the game will just stop responding on the loading screen and I have to close it by force. This however doesn\u2019t mean that it won\u2019t happen again, for I\u2019ve had this happen like various times in a row. It is a minor nuisance and the developer is already checking the issue but I figured it should be pointed out nonetheless.<br><br><b><div class=\"bb_h1\"><u>Final Verdict:<\/u><\/div><\/b><br>Epic Manager can be described as a tycoon RPG game influenced by board games in which you manage your own agency of adventurers as you compete for the number one spot in the League in order to become the Epic Manager of the Realm of Astraeus. <br><br>Currently the major systems are all in place, now it\u2019s only a matter of time of balancing, refining and adding more content. As of right now the game is already fun and already has that \u201c1 more turn\u201d feel to it, it features lovely visuals, a solid combat system akin to any other turn-based party combat game out there and an intricate system of relations between warring factions.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">If you ever wanted something like Football Manager but in a fantasy setting, this is a game you shouldn\u2019t pass!<\/div><br><blockquote class=\"bb_blockquote\">This review is part of the Steamified Community Review program.
