This makes sense and in turn also gives some really good puzzles for you to solve. You will also die a lot in The Mooseman but this is not a game where it gets frustrating because of the said deaths. The Mooseman gives you more than enough chances to try again and again to solve these puzzles.<br><br> You respawn right before where you died and while deaths might feel cheap, they never go into the territory of frustrating. There is never that feeling of losing progression and that makes it all the more exciting. Some puzzles are difficult to solve since sometimes you might miss a couple of clues here and there. The main story itself is kind of stretched in parts but those are minor nitpicks in what is otherwise a great experience. This is one of a kind game which does not depend on your reflexes but rather on the surroundings you are in and it does a great job of it.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Visuals<\/div><br>Some people might compare this to something like LIMBO in terms of its visuals and they would not be all wrong but I found the visuals in this game far more interesting and engaging. The Mooseman\u2019s visual style made me creeped out and awestruck at the same time and this world is something I will remember for quite some time.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Sound<\/div><br>The sound design in The Mooseman is done very well and there is a certain creepy vibe to it that just feels right.
