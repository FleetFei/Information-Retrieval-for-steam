The more years a player has been in the league the closer your scouted ratings will be to a players true rating.<br>-A draft system that is truly more of an art than a science. If you do not like a play, you have to change each part of the play. You are not given a bank of plays to choose from. So unles you want to try every variation on your own, then you stuck with using the &quot;threshold&quot; feature. Later when choose which plays to run in each game situation, it is much easier to manage because you can then choose from, the bank of 100 plays chosen in the previous stage of the game. <br><br>Criticism of the MP aspect. The game system may be fine when playing as a single player because you can act as a coach during the game and make decisions yourself. In MP the games are sim, so you are at the mercy of the coaches you had chosen. Why is this problem? Coaches are key as a GM, but yet, there are very little attributes you can choose from a coach. In FOF7, you can choose the percentage of run to pass plays in a given circumstance. In FOF8, that percentage is no longer present. It is based on the plays you have listed for a given situation and most will have a combination of run to pass unless you set it up differently. it would be different if the game added position coaches and more attributes for the coaches it does include, so that you can make a better decisions on how you want your coaches to conduct the games. <br><br>Another criticism is drafting. You get a grade and given a rough sketch on the players ability. You can &quot;interview&quot; players, but you only have 12 you can perform. The interview only marginally make it more define. It would be nice have the players colloege performances as well. More interviews and more information to help you make a better decision or bad one.
