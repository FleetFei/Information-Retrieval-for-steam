class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGyrodisc Super League is Valiant Interactive\u2019s early access and Steam debut. It\u2019s an exciting and vibrant arcade sports game in which players throw a disc from one side of the field to the other and attempt to make the opposition miss the disc to score. Games are played similarly to tennis with singles and doubles matches. The game has the potential to be one of the best couch multiplayer sports games on Steam with the fast, frantic action becoming very addictive and laugh a minute.<br><br>I did not particularly enjoy the experience of the single player game. The reason for this is simple. It\u2019s far too shallow at this stage of development. You can play individual matches against an AI opponent and that is it. However, to the developers credit they are aware of this and are incorporating an \u2018Arcade mode\u2019 that will include multiple unlockable costumes for each character as well as a selection of mini-game modes that have been inspired by classic arcade games like breakout and space invaders. This sounds good in theory but I\u2019d love for them to include a season mode. I find that these games are so much more exciting when you\u2019re constantly battling to win matches in order to finish on top of the ladder and\/or to reach the playoffs so that you can win a tournament. Examples of recent games that feature great arcade season or tournament modes include Rocket League (to be featured later on the Steamified Countdown) and Kopanito All-Stars Soccer which we featured back on January 7. It\u2019s a very basic concept but it adds a lot of replayability to your game.<br><br>What is present in the game already is definitely enough for the local multiplayer component of the game. I spent a short time playing this mode and for what it was it was certainly enjoyable. I expect that with future updates there will be even more to enjoy on this mode. It\u2019s definitely going to be the biggest selling point for the game and while its still leaning towards more of a concept than a fully fleshed out entity it is already a lot of fun.<br><br>A bit of a disappointment is the fact that the developers have no intention of incorporating an online multiplayer game. This will definitely have an impact on the size of the potential player base that the game will have. However, the action is so fast that any latency issues would make playing the game a nigh on impossibility \u2013 at least to an entertaining and enjoyable level so I can certainly understand why online multiplayer may be an impossibility for this title.<br><br>At this present stage of development if you have friends to play with then purchase a copy of the game because you will already get your monies worth.
