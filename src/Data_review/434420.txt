loads of RPG Maker games out there in Steam. So, it will make things a lot difficult to choose what game is worth playing considering most are simply copy and paste straight from the engine itself. Well I decided to play \u2018The Chosen RPG\u2019. Why is that is because, at the time of writing this, there\u2019s a sequel. So, that means this game has to be reasonable at least\u2026right? Well, it does have its moments however it\u2019s worth playing with a few considerations to think about.<br><br>The storyline is extremely clich\u00e9. Bad guy wants to control the lands \u2013 hero having a troublesome past \u2013 three hot chicks helps hero \u2013 hero is the chosen one \u2013 hero\u2019s goal is to kill bad person. That pretty much sums it up. There\u2019s no twists or turns, no creative writing \u2013 just hero vs bad guy. What\u2019s even more clich\u00e9 is all the women you control, all wear skimpy clothing to the extreme. There\u2019s no reason for this (other than the obvious) and shortly enough, the \u2018novelty\u2019 wears off very quickly, just like their clothing. It\u2019s sad to say that, one of the women you control, Serenity, is fully clothed in a wallpaper background and she actually looks decent. But no, they all must have two piece bikinis and the male, fully armoured. However, I guess this game is not unique when it comes to this.<br><br>The gameplay, just like the storyline, is the same ole RPG Maker style. Actually, it\u2019s more akin to the Dragon Quest series (for which one can argue it\u2019s more akin to the Wizardry series however that\u2019s another story). And what\u2019s even more laughable is that, according to the \u2018About this game\u2019 blurb, this game, or should I say this RPG is inspired by the Dragon Quest series. Well if the game\u2019s engine plays pretty much like Dragon Quest, it\u2019s a logical statement to say it\u2019s \u2018inspired\u2019 by the Dragon Quest series. Anyways, such is life.<br><br>Now, because the title did state \u2018RPG\u2019, there\u2019s very little elements of RPG, or for the people who don\u2019t know this term, Role Playing Game. Granted there are very little elements of quests (for which are linear in approach) and a bit of reading to do, there\u2019s absolutely no stats management. Yes, there are stats however they auto assign as you progress in levels. Basically, each hero has a specific role \u2013 i.e. the main character Edge is your warrior, Serenity, warrior with hand to hand abilities, Myste, the mage and Trinity, warrior \/ mage.<br><br>So, with this setup, you really have no choice but to smash your opponents to oblivion. I guess you really cannot do much else considering the combat system shows the entire party to the right of the screen and the enemy\/s left. It plays out in a turned based sequence meaning it\u2019s your turn to assign actions then the enemy\/s. Combat requires very little tactics and, because each character has a handful of \u2018special\u2019 abilities, can spice up the combat somewhat.<br><br>The game does not explain about the mechanics at all. There are some stats I have no idea what they are. However, there\u2019s an option where you can select \u2018optimise\u2019 to auto select the character\u2019s best equipment at hand and I used this every time. Then again, because the game\u2019s overall simplicity (i.e. smash and smash again), you really didn\u2019t need to understand the mechanics like percentage ratios or damage mitigation (if there is any). A good example of this is, I have no idea what damage range is by the spell Fire II or Lightning II. I even have no idea if any monsters have resistance to any spells or attacks \u2013 they all feel the same. So, in a nutshell, spam your favourite spell \/ attack \u2013 it all works out in the end.<br><br>Speaking of graphics, most are straight from the RPG Maker so I\u2019m not going to talk about that. It\u2019s simply a copy and paste job. However, I\u2019m not sure if the main character\u2019s portraits are templates too. I think they are not however if you have seen or play an RPG Maker game, this looks just like any other \u2013 i.e. 16bit animations. And there\u2019s nothing really wrong with that actually. However, I really like the musical scores for this game \u2013 it actually suits very well and enjoyable to listen to. I\u2019m not sure if they are also from the RPG Maker templates and if so, then I retract these words.<br><br>As far as completion goes, it\u2019s not a long and drawn out game. Granted it does have some grinding to do however not to the extreme. There is a level cap at 30 (for which again does not explain \u2013 it\u2019s a matter of discovering it yourself) and the land is regarded as open world \u2013 meaning you can travel anywhere as long you have the vehicle to do so (e.g. a boat to sail the seas). However, if you try to enter a location where the storyline doesn\u2019t direct you to, it will block you out as every other location (other than the one where to story tells you to) requires a certain item to enter \u2013 meaning the overall progression is linear. Because I play like a sloth on a lazy day, it took me around 8.5 hours to complete. However, it\u2019s not unheard of completing this game much less than that. I have read people finishing this off in around 4 hours and less even.<br><br>With so much negativity going around in this review maybe makes you think it\u2019s not a good idea to play it. Well and surprising enough, the game does have some strengths. It has Steam trading cards however they all look poorly made. The good news is that there\u2019s only five to collect to create a badge. Also, there are 8 achievements to collect and they are actually achievable, meaning no 20000 hours of grinding and so on. There are three that can be missed however overall, they are pretty easy to hit that 100% completion. Also, I really like that feature near the end about answering ten questions about the game. That made me think the developers did place some thought about the game\u2019s lore. Lastly there\u2019s a little \u2018parchment\u2019 hunting that further explains the backstory of this game. However each parchment only contains just a handful of words.<br><br>Now the big question is whether is game is worth playing? Initially I would say no however there are some glitter of love in this game (like the 10 questions I have mentioned above). Also, at the time of writing this, there is a part II to the saga. Again, one can argue it\u2019s more of a cash cow than anything else. Yet, with a price tag of just USD $2.00 you really cannot go wrong with that. And considering this game goes on sale very often and, at times, forms part of a package where you can save even more, it\u2019s worth a bash if you want a quick win.<br><br>5 \/ 10 (worth playing
