platform game loosely based on the film Toy Story 3. Almost the entire Toy Story case from the films return in voice acting, apart from the two key characters, Buzz and Woody, for me, it is an absolute shame that they didn't get Tom Hanks and Tim Allen in...<br><br>Game play wise, Toy Story 3 is a platformer, where players control either Woody, Buzz or Jessie. Each of the characters have their own special move. There are two main modes in the game, Story and Toybox. The story mode has nine levels, this follows the events in the movie, as well as the opening sequence from Toy Story 2. Toybox mode, you can create and customize levels, fill it with inhabitants and missions. Completing various objectives and missions in this mode, you will earn money, which in turn can be used to purchase new objects, toys and to expand your city. Some of the characters are also from other Disney and Pixar franchises! <br><br>It almost looks as if you are watching the movie with the graphics on show here; it's a shame I couldn't play it in full 1080p, the resolution option just wasn't available. Also I was disappointed to see that it is capped at 30 FPS,  so it would look even better if you could play it on a proper resolution\/frame rate...<br><br>All of the levels are well presented, there are a copious amount of jumping puzzles, and BOOOOOOOOOOOY do I love jumping puzzles.....You will be playing in well known locations such as Andy's bedroom, Sunnyside Daycare and Junkyards. All of these look amazing and filled with lots of little nuts and crannies to explorer. My favorite level is the one based on the Buzz Lightyear Video game (the one Rex plays), it just resonates nostalgia. Everything is there from the laser eye, to the wall of spikes, to the secret passage on the left. <br><br>I have to admit, I did struggle at times with a number of the jumping puzzles, but then again, I struggle with them on any game,  other than that, there aren't any real hard points,  that should be expected from a game mainly based at the younger audience though. <br><br>Sadly the main issue I have with Toy Story 3 The Video game, is the length. I finished the story, all of the side missions and most of the collectibles in just under two hours. To me that is unacceptable for a game of this stature, I would of been satisfied if it was maybe five hours, but two hours is just not on. There is so much more which could of been included into the story mode to flesh it out. <br><br>With that being said, if you grew up with Toy Story, you will have so many memories and nostalgia towards the whole gang, and you will more than likely get a lot of enjoyment with this game if only for those reasons only. I would certainly not recommend anyone pick this up at full price. Sadly since it is a Disney title, it probably won't go on sale very often, so keep your eyes out. <br><br>If you found this review helpful, please consider giving it a thumbs up.
