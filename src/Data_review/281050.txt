using two characters - your point character and her assist. The entire roster consists of female characters drawn in ecchi style, so expect lots of fanservice, panty shots and heavily animated busts - if you're into that sort of thing. To top this off, the game runs at 100 fps, so everything is extremely smooth.<br><br>The game itself seems simple enough at first - the engine is that of a basic anime fighting game, there are only four buttons (light, medium, hard and assist) and the commands for the special moves are standard fare for the genre. What makes the game stand out is that there is no feature that allow move cancels - normals, throws, special moves, supers etc all have to be done separately from each other. Even basic 2-in-1 combos are only possible because of links.<br><br>But here's where your partner comes in. So one of your buttons is dedicated to performing attacks with your assist character, the trick is they can move and attack independently of  your point character. With clever use of tandem attacks you can create tricky setups reminiscent of Ultimate Marvel vs Capcom 3 - attack them high and low at the same time, sandwich your opponent between you and your assist, take turns attacking to create a chain of attacks that combos, etc. You can also create combos via counter-hits which are very important in the game, as they tend to open the opponent up for additional free attacks. There's a lot of depth to be found here but it takes some getting used to, especially if you're already into other, more traditional, fighters.<br><br>Arcade mode can be brutal until you get the hang of it and even then the AI is no slouch.
