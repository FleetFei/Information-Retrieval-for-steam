would give this game a solid B+. The concept is very cute for a platformer and I enjoyed playing through it immensely. The controls were tight and fluid, which I was not expecting coming for an indie-platformer, but it did control quite well. The momentum of the character was solid and consistent, you could accurately judge and calculate the jump distance. The game is very easy, but given the child-friendly and looking appearance, that could have been an intentional design choice. Rather than fail the level or be sent out from your attempt should you be hit by an enemy or fall from the screen, you are simply sent back to the start of the level. You do keep all the berries and water drops you collected, however, which does make the blow of being sent back not sting as much. The game is fairly short, I completed 100% of the achievements in just over an hour and a half, and the stages themselves only take 5-10 minutes to clear, which gives the game a brisk pace. You will naturally unlock all the upgrades through normal play and quickly, though there isn't much difference between the upgraded leaves themselves, it's still worth noting you will not be forced to grind out levels (unless you are going for all achievements the 3000 berry one will take maybe 20-30 minutes of replay). The music is very chipper and enjoyable and sets a great mood for the friendly atmosphere, though perhaps it is a tad loud in comparison to the sound-effect volume level as it feels quite drown out.<br><br>What keeps this game from getting a 9 or 10 in my book is just a few small issues. The camera could have done with a little work, and the ability to move it even say 45 degrees left and right to help you see around a few corners would have been helpful with some of the more tricky jumps. The game is also very short. Currently this game is listed for $3.49 dollars, and it was worth a playthrough to me at that price, but some more thrifty gamers may find the price to be a bit high considering the game can be beaten in an hour-hour and a half. The biggest issue I had however was the lack of options, specifically the lack of volume control. You can change graphical settings and controls easily enough, but even with the games upbeat soundtrack, I think it would have been nice to be able to turn it down or mute it when I was replaying a level.  With a few more options and control mechanics the game would be a perfect little indy platformer, but it just has a few key issues I hope the developers take note of for future releases. <br><br>I
