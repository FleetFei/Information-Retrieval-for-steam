So you know just by looking at Rocketbirds 2\u2019s install size, that you\u2019re in for a treat of this genre. Ratloop Asia developed so far only these two games from the Rocketbirds series but in their case I\u2019m willing to admit that sometimes, you don\u2019t need dozens of releases under your belt before you create a game worthy of being regarded as flawless by yours truly.<br><br>Released 5 years after the first one in the series, Rocketbirds 2 Evolution (R2E), pits an elite commando of chickens against their archenemy once more. The penguin dictator Il Putzki and his brutal regime of pidgeon and penguin soldiers (uniforms taking cue from Nazi Germany while the banners and cult of personality reek of North Korea) have to be stopped a second time it seems after aliens revived them. If you think the plot is over the top, you\u2019re right. It\u2019s part of this franchise\u2019s charm to emulate all the cheesy ideas and one-liners that made action movies from the \u201880s and \u201890s so\u2026unique. Add a few memes here and there and you have a perfectly hilarious story to set players in the right mood. Far too many side scrollers become dreary long before their jokes start landing. More like falling flat. Not the case with R2E.<br><br>Anyways, those passed years since the first Rocketbirds have worked in favor of this sequel. Not only is the story just as entertaining, but the gameplay mechanics and graphics have been upgraded as well. By the looks of it, you\u2019d say they reached the ceiling in terms of 2D sprites and animation which seem to transcend into 3D. It\u2019s just a clever use of motion and shadows but alas, the texture quality is spotless and I\u2019m starting to understand why the game\u2019s size only seemed large at first. Unless new methods of texture compression shall be developed, we\u2019ll keep seing 80GBs 3D action games and soon enough 15GBs platformers might be the norm too. So you won\u2019t be able to complain that R2E seems plain and boring. As with its predecessor, you have plenty of gore to feed those bullets you\u2019ll be spurting from a hefty arsenal.  Only that now you can forget about the classic run-n-gun and simply use a jetpack to create mayhem at an entirely new scale.<br><br>Split in two main parts, Rocketbirds 2 Evolution can be played and enjoyed even in singleplayer, though you\u2019ll know better than me that the fun\u2019s right at home in multiplayer. After you\u2019ve completed (or even before, if you like) Part 1: The Cockup campaign with the series\u2019 titular character, Hardboiled Chicken, you can engage in coordinated shooting in Part 2: The Rescue, with up to 4 buddies. Both parts eventually lead to a common endgame, so they don\u2019t feel like two distinct pieces, glued together out of convenience. I\u2019m also not gonna spoil any details relating to the levels or weapons you shall make good use of. There\u2019s just so much that screenshots and gameplay vids can\u2019t convey to potential players.<br><br>As it was expected of it, R2E has both Steam and in-game collectibles which serve as an almost unnecessary incentive to progress the funny storyline. We can\u2019t have everything though but we sure as hell won\u2019t stop trying. Should I add the fact that this title seems quite underpriced given its attention to details and sheer amount of activities, variables and customization? I\u2019m sure you figured that out already. I don\u2019t hand out perfect ratings very often and irony has it that platformers\/side scrollers and I, don\u2019t have a stable friendship. But every now and then comes a title belonging to this genre that makes me forget the other 90% subpar counterparts. Playing Rocketbirds: Hardboiled Chicken before its sequel may not be mandatory, but I recommend you purchase it, simply to offer your support to a developer that has shown great potential.
