that has been made using Warscape engine. The AI gives a challenge in the campaign map a big chunk of the time, province development and realm prosperity saw some really nice improvements like food supplies (vanilla and ROTS only), limited building slots, taxation level penalties and trade enrichment. Diplomacy is required to survive and you will be doing more than just declaring\/getting declared wars; you need trade as much as taxes. <br><br>There's also an incredible sense of balance for all clans, given that they all have the same units and all clans differ in that some of these units have their name and are slightly more powerful. This is a big no-no for people who have played other titles and enjoyed the diversity that each faction gave; in fact playing with different factions in Shogun 2 might feel repetitive. I honestly prefer this, since Creative Assembly has proven that they tend to make some factions with extremely OP units and some factions with terrible ones when they put too many of them in a game.<br><br>Having said this, this game is not perfect. Here's why:<br><br>- Warscape engine. Really, almost every negative aspect will be related to this terrible piece of software.<br><br>- 1v1 and blobbing. If you've played Medieval 2 and RTW, you know what it means to see your units spreading out, pushing or getting pushed by the sheer mass of bodies they're fighting. Shogun 2 has none of that. You're going to get constantly trapped in sieges when fighting in a gate with your katana wielding masters against 1 unit of the weakest, exhausted of melee units because units do not have mass in this game. It's ugly to look at, and it hinders gameplay.<br><br>- Sieges. Supposed to be dynamic and innovative from your usual &quot;storm the walls and fight in the streets&quot; approach from the other TW games. But thanks to the blobbing, it's a nightmare to fight and requires constant AI abusing or an insulting amount of troops to get just one stronghold. (lowest of castle level)<br>- (vanilla-ROTS) Ranged units are like homing missile launchers. You don't even need artillery to capture a castle, just the best of archers who can shoot with god-like precision above 15 meters of stone wall and some decent melee units.<br><br>- Lack of a castle-preview mechanic. It's impossible to form a strategy and say what kind of troops\/how many would it require to protect a province if I can't see the castle in question.<br><br>- AI, although the best of all TW games so far, it's still incredibly stupid sometimes. In legendary difficulty, when defending a siege, they'll keep a quarter of their troops in an area where there aren't even enemies around and let their castle be taken by timer; when attacking, they'll send 50% of their troops to scale the same part of the wall. They also tend to spam useless artillery units (in FOTS, even if they can make Parrott\/Armstrong guns, they'll make wooden cannons instead).<br><br>- Difficulty settings. Directly connected to the above point; the developers knew that they couldn't make a proper AI that could actually give you a strategic and tactical challenge, so they instead gave them insane bonuses the higher the difficulty. Their troops fight better, their economy lets them have 2 full armies even with just one province. This doesn't improve your experience at all or rewards a master strategist, it just forces you to find more ways to abuse the AI.<br><br>- Unit pathfinding and individual AI for regiments is terrible as it always has been. You'll suffer it more in FOTS, where 1 soldier who got stuck somewhere makes the entire unit not able to shoot.<br><br>- Terrible optimization. Unless you got an SSD capable of storing the almost 20GB of this game, expect the game to take around 2 minutes to boot and almost a minute to load one battle. Don't get excited, however, because with an SSD these loading times get reduced to half.<br><br>P.D.: Almost all of these issues could be fixed if CA just completely botched Warscape and spent more resources on the AI department. But they won't, and they're still developing and releasing games having learned nothing of their flaws. So I heartily recommend this game because, despite it's flaws, it's the only decent modern Total War game you're going to get in years to come. It's fun, in a very casual way.
