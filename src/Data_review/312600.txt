Since then, it made its way on Steam.<br><br><u><b>Story:<\/b><\/u><br>The game tells the tale of a couragous young girl by the name of Livia. Hailing from a village that has been pleagued with an uncurable illness, she sought to help by climbing a mysterious tower that contains a wish-granting relic at its top: the Hallows.<br>However, many &quot;Guardians&quot; patrol the tower to prevent intruders, and Livia must recruit &quot;dolls&quot; that live in the tower as she fights her way up.<br><br>As far as stories go, Rime Berta's isn't completely original by any means. This could be problematic when a skill you never use is part of the prequisite, and you're forced to grind that one skill to its needed amount.<br><br>There are also some glitches in some aspects of the gameplay, such as with certain statue effects, that brings the gameplay down a notch. Other than that however, the gameplay is fun and can be quite addicting.<br><br><u><b>Graphics:<\/b><\/u><br>The sprites in-battle look surprisingly good for a doujin game. They're crisp, smooth, and most importantly, cute. It's a shame because the OST doesn't come with the Steam version.<br><br><u><b>Summary:<\/b><\/u><br>Cutting to the chase, should you get this game?<br><br>I'd say, not with its standard price.
