<\/div><br>Syren is a survival horror game which tasks you with escaping a research facility where everything has gone horribly wrong and there are mermaid cyborgs everywhere hunting you down. That is the basic gist of the game and it doesn\u2019t differentiate much from the formula throughout this horrific journey. Syren is set in an underwater facility with a Bioshock-esque environment and it relies more on building up the environment to tense you up than the actual sirens.<br>When you do confront a syren though, it is always a game of hide and seek. It clearly draws some inspiration from Alien: Isolation and while the Syren AI is not that good, it is servicable. <br><br>The thing that works for it though is the fact that instead of being chased by one entity, you are being chased by multiple. There are lots of Syrens in the facility and they can kill you at a moment\u2019s notice if they notice you even from a distance. <br><br>Syren really benefits from the VR system and every encounter feels up close and personal. Using your own skills to peek around and brace yourselves while a Syren is passing is absolutely nerve-wracking and I have to give credit for that. The game does not present you with immersion breaking stuff like inventory or crafting. You are simply present there in the facility and even if you drop something, the Syrens will get alerted of your presence. <br><br>The few levels which are present in the game itself are pretty nice and they are designed quite well. The game does have a certain lack of levels and you can essentially run through the game once you have figured out the pattern of Syren\u2019s movements. <br><br>What really lets Syren down are the number of bugs that plague the game. Enemies glitching through rooms, constant pop ins and a number of set sequences all contribute to a frustrating experience. The game might also cause paranoia and dizziness to some and while that might be true for most of the VR game, Syren does make a special case being the genre it is present in.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Visuals<\/div><br>Syren is visually very impressive. The environments are richly detailed and provide a perfect backdrop for a game like this. The Syren models are also terrifying and while there are some issues, it does its job really well. A number of visual pop ins and some low resolution textures here and there might ruin your experience though but it is nearly impossible to not be impressed by the sheer level of detail present in the levels here.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Sound<\/div><br>Syren\u2019s gameplay depends a lot on its sound design as you have to guess when and where a Syren might be around. The soundtrack is creepy and lends to the atmosphere of the game really well. The game does an amazing of letting you know your mistakes through the sound design and it is absolutely terrifying when you drop something or hear something loud near you which can attract the Syrens. Overall while the sound design is not phenomal, it is creepy and does the job it sets out to do.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Overall<\/div><br>Syren might be a tough buy for some people as it is marred by a number of bugs and glitches but for anyone who is looking to properly utilize his or her VR for a scary experience, Syren might be right up your alley. The price is right but it is very buggy in its current state and is quite lacking in content.
