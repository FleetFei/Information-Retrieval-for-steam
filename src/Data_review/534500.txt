class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\t<i>Okay, before we get into this; yes, Tiger Knight has its problems and yes, some weapons and troops are more 'Powerful' then others in more scenarios in game but this is an Early Access game that is regularly having development done to improve the game. As someone who isn\u2019t a Game Developer, I can\u2019t sit and judge the Developers for not having a perfect balance or issues with the game here and there. The meer fact that when the Servers are taken down for patching and upgrades, the Developers gives VIP\u2019s as \u2018Compensation\u2019 is something that a lot of PvP based games don\u2019t. They also appear to listen to the players as well.<\/i><br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Tiger Knight Review<\/div>As someone who has a lot of hours in Mount &amp; Blade: Warband whom is also a big fan of Dynasty Warriors among other Koei releases; Tiger Knight is like a dream come true... well almost, because I feel there are missing features that don\u2019t quite tick the box just yet. Unlike Warband, the troop mechanics are a lot more simple and less clunky, so leading a troop and their behavior is considerably easier. If you have ever played the game BLADESTORM, imagine that, mixed with Warband, Dynasty Warriors and Kessen 3. <br><br>Tiger Knight essentially pits you as a General of a Unit; originally your units would be from the Three Kingdom\u2019s but with the Rome Expansion, you can now feel more like Marcus Valerius Maximianus then a potential offspring of Cao Cao. Each unit works in a Rock Paper Scissors style, in that every unit has its counter; this falls to the Armor and the Weapons. Armor consists of Leather, Metal and Cloth; with Weapons as Blunt, Slash and Pierce. In addition some weapons can make use of 2 types of attacks; i.e a Sword would be considered a slash but stabbing would be a Pierce. Much like real life, if you try to stab someone with metal armor then you\u2019re going to have a bad time. <br><br>In addition, a major facture also falls into Weight and Attack Speed. Weight can be a little clunky as logic and game mechanics don\u2019t always go hand in hand. The heavier you are, the harder it is to send you rolling across the floor when charged or bumped by a horse is the main thing to consider; but if it makes a major effect when running or swinging a weapon? Sometimes quite hard to tell. Weapons also fall under weight but the main difference is when bounce weapons together, the heavier weapon will cause the other to be stunned if the weight difference is a lot; but heavier weapons generally have a slower Attack Speed.<br><br>Generally, you would probably want to wear a mixture of armor type because they do protect different parts of the body and as a lot of slashing motions can cover the arms and body, it will factor in these differences. Being poked by a long spear in the body or face however, won\u2019t. Long Spears are also, for example, a Horses worst nightmare. I'd always advice carrying one, but trying to use it as a main weapon may not work out especially when fighting face to face with other players; striking them when they close the gap is like trying to thread a needle.<br><br>The game also has sub generals called Adjustants, who compliment your unit by giving Formations and passive boosts. Not all formations work for all units, so checking these before purchasing or assigning an Adjutant is adviced. Roman Units\/Adjutants work a little different in that the Unit has Formations as an Ability, so an Adjutant mostly has passive boosts; Chinese Adjutants mainly have Formations, so passive boosts are less common. <br><br>When loading into Command Mode, which is the main PvP play mode besides Dual and PvE; you will have a selection of pre-positions to select as a Spawn. With the last update, you can now see your allies units and the map before actual loading into battle; so you can switch your unit to compliment. PvE is a more Story Driven Command Mode where players work together to complete objectives. Dual Mode is essentially Team Deathmatch.<br><br>Unfortunately the Steam Reviews has a limit, so while I would love to write a lot more about the game, I would have to leave it here. I do recommend trying the game. Yes it does have its issues but for an Early Access, it a great game and the Developers seem to care greatly for it.
