I watched streams and YouTube videos of all the characters doing all their moves, and performed by various unknown and even known players (I've also watched tournaments). So I fully knew what to expect in terms of content, characters, visuals and so on.<br><br>BUT... playing it on my own, is a completely different beast.<br><br>I am VERY impressed by this game. I know it had a genuinely BAD initial release. It insultingly lacked content and I fully agreed with all the negatives that Capcom received from basically rushing the game out of the doors just because they wanted it to be officially available (and &quot;viable&quot;) for Tournaments which at the time were closing in really fast. They should NOT have done that... but now it's too late and the damage has been done. It's SO unfortunately that SFV is mostly being remembered for that, instead of being appreciated for what it is NOW.<br><br>As I said, I'm not Street Fighter franchise &quot;fan&quot;, and certainly not a veteran. I do know that - apparently so anyway - the most popular ones in the franchise are, in fact, the older titles still to this day. Such as the oh-so classic and still very much working and viable SF2 Turbo, or Alpha 2, or even the highly technical \/ complex Alpha 3 (I've read that Alpha 3 is still to this day the most complex and difficult-to-master Street Fighter in the entire franchise), or the nearly-perfected and fine-tuned Third Strike (the animations quality for a game that's made out of sprites is absolutely stunning to me). And I also often read that even SF4 is &quot;better&quot; than SF5. But then again, I usually realize that most of the 'negative' views about SF5 STILL stem from the failed and rushed nature of the initial release. But what about the actual CORE fighting of the game itself? How IS SF5 as an actual fighting game? Well I'm asking... but in reality I don't really care since I'm no veteran of the franchise anyway, but I do appreciate a quality versus quanity game any time of the day, regardless of the genre.<br><br>I've just started to play SF5 and after just one and a half day of play there's obvious high-quality stuff that jumps at me right from the start:<br><br>+ The animations (and animation transitions) in this game are STUNNING. It's so... how can I put it... &quot;organic&quot;? &quot;Natural&quot;? It's like there's a 100 &quot;extra&quot; frames of animations for small details on all body parts of every characters. If I compare SF5's animations to... say... Killer Instinct's or MKX's the one word that comes to mind is &quot;stiffness&quot;, or &quot;frames&quot; (or lack thereof). In favor of SF5, there seems to be more frames, it's just smooth. When something as simple as a character stands back up after being knocked down he doesn't just nearly 'appear' standing up after like 2 frames like you'd see in Killer Instinct (one example I can think of in KI is the standing up animation for Glacius, one moment he's lying flat on the ground and the next moment the transition of him standing up is literally composed of something like 3 frames, it's ugly to look at).<br><br>Anyway I don't have the technical terms to talk about this aspect in details, especially if there's actual animators reading this. But as a gamer who appreciates smooth animations in fighting games all I can say is that as far as 3D \/ polygonal-based fighting games go (I'm excluding 2D \/ sprite-based ones since they're on their own class) SFV is quite literally the best one I've seen to this day. If one day they make SF6 I can only hope that they keep the same animation team.<br><br>+ The music is good! What else can I say? It's memorable, it's not irritating to the point where I feel like I should turn it off, and it seems to fit this game in general like fitting a pair of gloves. In other words it's good enough to leave it on while I play (contrarily to the music in the MK franchise which I usually turned off in order to listen to my own playlist in the background).<br><br>+ The pacing, in general, is right where I like it. It's not too fast, but not too slow. To me what's important in a fighting game is when the speed at which the characters move and execute their special moves is &quot;slow enough&quot; for me to 1) See it coming, and 2) Anticipate what the other player is trying to do because not only the animations show it to start with but the speed isn't too high in order for me to have the time to REACT to whatever is on its way. I've seen faster-paced games and they're usually too difficult for me. There's other perhaps better examples but immediately I can think of Killer Instinct here. I actually DO really like Killer Instinct... BUT... that game IS fast-paced compared to SF5. I do not mind fast-paced fighting games in general but I just happen to suck at them as a result. A fighting game does not have to be fast-paced to be &quot;complex&quot; or &quot;competitive-friendly&quot;, in my book. So yeah I think that SF5's general character movement and moves execution speed is just right where it should be (at least for me... because some people said that SF5 is actually too slow for a SF game).<br><br>+ Characters, and character design. By that I mean I love the characters that they added (after release, but also including the default ones at release) and I just love how they are rendered under this new (UE4) engine. Now I too also REALLY love sprites and 2D or 2.5D fighting games. I LOVE how Third Strike is animated and presented in general, or Alpha 3 as well (just speaking visually here, animations, art \/ pixels-style, etc). But when it comes to the 3D and polygonal field of visuals then OH BOY is SF5 absolutely stunning to look at. I mentioned the animations already, but the stages look fantastic! The backgrounds are amazing. The characters themselves, WOW, that's all I can say really. I was thinking about going with my &quot;favorites&quot; but really I just like... ALL of them. <br><br>Now, there IS one character that I can only wish they'd add to SF5, and that would be Third Strike's 'Q'. His design is so... simple, yet so... great. I mean there's a bunch of characters I'd like them to add other than Q, I'd like to get Sakura and Blanka, and T. Hawk. We all have our character wishes don't we. But the characters that we do have right now are freakin-fan-tastic to look at and play.<br><br>* Conclusion *<br><br>Yeah long read but anyway... this Street Fighter is just great for me. I love the gameplay, the visuals, the sounds and music and the characters. It's the best fighting game I've played since... hummm... well I haven't played many in the past 10 years to be honest, but I'd say it's the best one since MK9. Actually I prefer SF5 over MK9. I think that I still do prefer the general lore and grittier setting of the Mortal Kombat franchise (especially with MKX, that one as far as visuals go is definitely the best in the MK franchise) but wow has Street Fighter come a LONG way for me since the Arcade and old-school console versions. I might have had even better impressions had I actually bought SF4 because I did like that one. But SF5 is definitely cementing the 'return to form' of this franchise for me. It's very ironic... considering that SF5 is seemingly considered an &quot;average-at-best&quot; fighting game even among the most SF veterans out there; when, for them, this game would be a 5 or 6 out of 10, but for me it's something like a solid 8, maybe even 9.<br><br>Just wanted to let all of this off of my shoulders (mostly because of the general &quot;meh&quot; impressions people give of this game...
