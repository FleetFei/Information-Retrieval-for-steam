class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\t<div class=\"bb_h1\">Introduction <\/div><br>RC Simulation 2.0 is the latest RC simulator to hit the Steam store. RCSIM2 offers cars, boats and quadcopters for use on default tracks as well as user-made tracks. As this is an RC simulator it is of course geared towards people who have at least some interest in RC vehicles, if you don\u2019t fit that profile, this game and therefore this review, probably won\u2019t be for you. It\u2019s worth mentioning while I do enjoy RC sims and I do have an interest in the vehicles themselves I\u2019m not hugely knowledgeable about them.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Presentation <\/div><br>RCSIM2 may not have ground-breaking graphics but what it does have are good enough to enjoy the game. The vehicle models are all high quality which of course is the most important part of the game\u2019s presentation as an RC sim. One thing about the game\u2019s presentation that I particularly liked was that, as there is an onboard camera for the vehicles, some of the vehicles have lower quality cameras compared to the third person view, such as on the smallest quadcopter in the game, just added a nice touch of realism for me.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay <\/div><br>RCSIM2 can be enjoyed in four ways, five if you count the map editor.<br><br>First up, we have Practice mode. This is pretty self-explanatory, you choose a vehicle and then select a map you wish to test out. The only limitation here is that boats cannot be used on maps without water which is pretty obvious. Once you\u2019re actually in the game you\u2019re free to explore the map as you wish, the checkpoints and lap markers do work so you can actually complete proper laps and check your time for them if you wish to improve your times for time trials or multiplayer.<br><br>Next we have the Time Trial mode. Once you\u2019ve selected your vehicle and track you\u2019ll be given a short warm-up time to get a feel for the track (this can be skipped if you\u2019d rather get straight to the action). You\u2019ll then be given a set amount of time, three minutes, to complete as many laps as you can as fast as you can. Obviously the amount of laps doesn\u2019t matter, it\u2019s your time which is important, but your fastest lap will be used so don\u2019t just sit there and do nothing after your first lap!<br><br>Multiplayer is the next mode on our list. Multiplayer basically allows you to do whatever you want. You can race against your friends in any RC vehicle or you could have a quadcopter paintball dogfight against your friends! I know which one I\u2019d choose.<br><br>The final proper game mode is the Challenge mode. From what I\u2019ve seen of it the aim of this mode is to shoot a certain amount of balloons using a paintball gun. This mode requires a vehicle with a paintball gun as well as a map that has balloons floating around.<br><br>As I mentioned earlier, RCSIM2 also has a track editor (it will eventually have a vehicle editor too). Now I\u2019m not particularly good at using an editor in any game but I did check out RCSIM2\u2019s, I must say it looks incredibly good. You\u2019re given all the tools you need to build whatever track you want. You can change how big the map itself is, you can choose what terrain you want to use and then of course you have access to spawn points and all of the objects in the game. Once you\u2019ve created your track it can be uploaded to the Steam workshop for other people to use. The idea is to create your own tracks and share them (though the game does come with some tracks made by the dev) and so this is obviously a very important part of the game.<br><br>There are of course other things I need to mention regarding the gameplay but I\u2019ll keep it short and snappy. RCSIM2 has a full day and night cycle and you\u2019re able to choose whatever time of day you want, though there\u2019s currently no weather in the game. There are eight different camera angles for you to use including a first person camera that allows you to move yourself around, it\u2019s worth mentioning the controls for the gamepad don\u2019t work properly for the first person view and controlling your RC is pretty much impossible in this camera view, it\u2019s mainly just to position yourself. The marshals in the game have ragdoll physics, it\u2019s pretty silly but it can be rather fun to just run them over with your RC or shoot them with a paintball gun every so often in practice mode.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Miscellaneous <\/div><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li>The RC physics seem realistic to me.<br><\/li><li>The sound effects are reasonably good but from what I\u2019ve read on the forums and other reviews from people who have a better knowledge of RC vehicles they could use some improvement.<br><\/li><li>Most of the settings aren\u2019t implemented yet, for now you just get a generic quality level for graphics and input options.<br><\/li><li>In regards to input options, you can use KB&amp;M (not recommended), an Xbox 360 controller (or any other kind of controller that works with windows) and of course, since this is an RC sim, you can use proper RC controllers if they have a USB connector.<br><\/li><li>There\u2019s not a huge amount of content at the time I\u2019ve written this review but as it progresses through development and more people start making and uploading their own tracks the amount of content will increase. <\/li><\/ul><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Verdict <\/div><br><b>Worth Purchasing. <\/b><br><br>RCSIM2 is a solid RC simulator that any fans of the genre should try. It\u2019s reasonably realistic, the RCs themselves look good and it will only get better as it develops. As I said earlier, this game probably isn\u2019t for your average gamer but if you like RCs then you should pick it up or at least follow its development.<br><br><b>Review Copy Provided.
