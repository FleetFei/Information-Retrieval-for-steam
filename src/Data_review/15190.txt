games&quot; or &quot;This story's amazing&quot;. The story's damn near non-existant. It's basically a bunch of levels that are cobbled together in a linear path. <br>I played the game when I was around 13. The first opening sequence was quite extreme and shocking back then. But the main reason was that I had never really watched war movies or seen anything remotely hardcore. Now that I have... it just left me feeling nothing. <br>Most characters are random and also pointless. There's also ludonarrative dissonance. The game's point is that war is hell, casualties are plenty and it's a huge struggle. In the gameplay, your squad can die and resurrect by the next mission. Same characters, same names. It's like nothing happened. Also, even in the narrative portions of the game (which are few and far between) no one really gets killed. I can remember 4 deaths in total. There's also another thing... I never gave a damn about anyone. The start of the game shows the last level of the game (the best part of it as well) and it just expects me to still get emotionally attached. It's like showing you how you can get your hand burned on the stove, yet it still wants you to put your hand on the hot stove.<br>The voice acting ranges from annoyingly awful to above average. The best example is Baker, the main character. He delivers some lines pretty well and some lines really well. The more annoying one is Leggett. The voice actor tries to put in emotion and it just ends up being on the level of a hilariously bad teen drama. Just awful. <br><br>GAMEPLAY<br><br>The main portion of the game consists of you commanding up to two squads with up to three members in each of them. The game expects you to use one squad to supress the enemy while you either send or go with the other squad to take them out with either just rushing them or flanking them. What it actually boils down to is you running around the enemy thanks to their awful AI and conveniently placed walls on the sides of the battlefield. <br>It all boils down to: 1) Spot enemy squad. 2) Set squad(s) to cover(s) 3) Wait for full suppression 4) Sneak around them 5) Empty magazine into the enemy<br>Very bog standard. <br>This would be more fun if the shooting was actually good. The ADS shakes all of the time, making it already difficult to aim. But then you also get the issue of half of your shots not hitting the enemy for some reason if they're more than a close distance away. Plus, your M1 Garand won't kill an enemy in one shot... Yeah, you read that right. Even with a headshot. It's so weird. Plus, the hipfiring is probably the most unrealistic part about it all. You'll fire at the enemy and the bullet can fly upwards of 45 degrees to the side...<br><br>GRAPHICS &amp; SOUND<br><br>The game's ugly. No other way of saying it. Even for its time, which is a long time ago, this game just looks mediocre at best and awful at worst. It's the modified Unreal 2 engine. At least it runs well.<br>The guns sound weak. Maybe it was the purpose, but it just made the guns so boring to use. <br>The soundtrack is very forgettable except for the main theme, which is very good. It'll haunt me for a while. <br><br>DIFFICULTY <br>The game's pretty easy. I did die a fair few times, but a lot of those were cheap deaths. Things like random instadeaths because of an LMG or something like that. It's hard to understand where the enemy can hit you. They even seem to be able to hit through some obstacles\/holes that you can't hit through. But once you get used to the game, you'll find out that the game REFUSES to give you any attention as long as you try and flank the enemy. The enemy won't find it out until they instantly react to you hitting some certain point.<br><br><br>OVERALL<br>Brothers in Arms: Road to Hill 30\u2122 (Don't forget the \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665ing trademark..) is a fun game that'll test your patience. I\u2019d played other World War shooters on the Playstation and PC prior to this, but it never really stuck. While it was cool to play as an Allied soldier working undercover for the OSS, I was never convinced it was a very good representation of the life of a soldier.<br><br>Brothers in Arms had me from the start. When I first popped in the disk and heard the Brothers in Arms theme, the music just hit me right to my core. The beautiful, sad but powerful marching music in the menus made me pause and sit for a few minutes to just soak it in, and I realized right then that I was going to be in for a journey that I\u2019d never been in before.<br><br>However, I\u2019m going to skip forward a few years. See after being in the military for a few years, I found myself coming back to Brothers in Arms. This time when I started the game, the haunting words of Sergeant Matt Baker struck me and really made me feel and understand why this game had me from the start. I find that most military shooters are just \u201cbang bang explosion, pow pow Nazi!\u201d Brothers in Arms makes you feel the soldiers pain, joy and regrets as they evolve from gung-ho soldiers at the start of D-Day to their final showdown at Hill 30.<br><br>As Soldiers in the field, every decision made can have horrible consequences. Most of us do not regret these decisions as we recognize that they must be done in order for others to enjoy the freedoms and rights that we have. The life of the soldier is almost never glamorous. It\u2019s a job and we do the very best we can with whatever scenario that is given to us. This is shown in Brothers in Arms several times over the course of the series. One character at one point in the game loses his mind over the grief of losing two squadmates under his command. Another character finds himself running into a burning building to rescue a soldier who himself was trying to rescue an innocent. Characters are killed, others hurt to the point of having to be sent home, and yet the rest soldier on. They do what needs to be done and that makes this game very special.<br><br>The words that open the first game in the Brothers in Arms series still give me pause whenever I hear them. Matthew Baker is tired of this damn war and everything that led to where he is and just wants it to be over. Read these words and imagine you\u2019d been in hell for over a week, each day feeling longer than the last.<br><br>\u201cMy dad said something to me after the divorce. He said that every soldier has two families, those you raise and those you raise Hell with. The last thing you see before everything goes black is a soldier \u2013 having finally snapped from the pressure \u2013 expose himself to enemy fire, calling for death, and taking a blast from a tank as a result. It\u2019s a very shocking and sudden introduction to a game that introduces you to amazing characters. In particular there\u2019s an awesome argument between them and another soldier about Batman VS Superman that cemented their awesomeness in my mind forever.<br><br>Of course I\u2019m getting away from myself with comparing my life to that of characters in what is still in the end just a videogame. Aside from authenticity, one of the big things that I really enjoyed about these games was the challenge and the tactical gameplay. Your squadmates were very smart and did their best to keep you alive, they followed direction but also knew better than to let you get them killed by ordering them to do something silly like attack a tank head on. The enemy functioned the same way making strategy key to victory. You had to be very careful with this as well because your men and you were very vulnerable. In Brothers in Arms, one very well placed shot could kill you in an instant, there are no magic health packs or regenerating health and running out in the open was suicidal.
