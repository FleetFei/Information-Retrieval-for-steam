class=\"postedDate\">Posted: September 30, 2015\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t<div class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\t<div class=\"bb_h1\">Introduction<\/div>Today is the day Steamroll officially rolls onto Steam via their Early Access program. With such a quirky name I just had to find out what it currently has to offer (the name should give you a good idea I think) and to see if it\u2019s actually worth your hard earned digital cash (spoiler: yes, it is).<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay<\/div>If you ever played The Ball then you pretty much know what to expect from Steamroll. The setting is different, however, and there\u2019s a bigger focus on puzzles and smaller areas in my opinion. If you have never played that game then I shall try to explain it shortly: you control a ball (which, in turn, is controlled\/powered by steam technology) and your goal is to get the ball to the end of the level while solving puzzles. That\u2019s actually a narrow and very shortsighted explanation of the game and saying this in general wouldn\u2019t do the game justice so let me use the next few paragraphs to explain it all better and in a bit more detail.<br><br>So generally you control the ball by generating steam, which in turn pushes you forward. There\u2019s a meter on the left side of the screen that shows how much you can still generate and once it\u2019s empty it\u2019s game over. So you have to balance how and when you use it and cannot just keep the button pressed. <br>You will have to move the ball across (homemade) platforms, while pressing buttons (usually in the form of a puzzle) and blowing things up (which also usually appears in the form of a puzzle).<br><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=527495816\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_0\">http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=527495816<\/a><br>About these last two points: on the right side of the screen you have your \u2018controls\u2019 UI (which could use some work, though). There are specific parts in the levels where you can install the ball and change into a shooting form. From that point of view you can then move the camera around for a better view and, more importantly, shoot balls with portable walls or bombs. It speaks for itself that the bombs blow up things like rocks (and yourself if you\u2019re not careful enough!) and that the portable walls can be shot and installed so that you can ricochet either bombs or \u2018regular\u2019 balls off of them (usually for hitting switches).<br>Alright, enough balls talk.<br> <br>Now for the puzzle part: the puzzles are nicely balanced, and while there are a few complex(er) puzzles most of them can be solved fairly quick. The levels generally only last between 5-30 minutes, by the way. And while you sometimes might get stuck on a puzzle they can most of the times be solved with some logical thinking. Thus far it always required a mix of setting up bombs and walls while hitting switches and physically moving around.<br>The camera is a bit low during the static puzzle scenes and I sometimes couldn\u2019t clearly see what I was doing. Some puzzles also depend on trial-and-error gameplay, but this is limited fortunately.<br><br>So that was all good stuff. I\u2019m afraid there were also one thing that influenced my experience negatively, with the first being performance issues. There was enormous slowdown in one section where I had to try a part again and again. The game would drop below &lt;5fps and sometimes it would restore itself after a minute or so, but one time it didn\u2019t fully respond anymore and the only option was to \u2018hard\u2019 quit the game and restart it. It doesn\u2019t seem to be a memory leak, as far as I can judge, but it does seem to happen if you take a (very) long time somehow.<br>Other things that caught my attention were the rather basic menus and the fact that when there\u2019s a lot debris the ball doesn\u2019t always roll the way it should (despite the \u2018ball prediction\u2019 working normally). Nothing too big - although the debris problem can cause gameplay problems - but as always it\u2019s at least worth mentioning.<br><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=527495790\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_1\">http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=527495790<\/a><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Audio &amp; Visuals<\/div>The music of the game is kind of peaceful, considering the setting. I expected something with a few more beats but I personally liked it. It also tries to keep your attention on the game itself: there are plenty of games out there with music so loud and in-your-face that I ultimately start to focus more on the music than on the game itself, which is never a good thing. So: ultimately it was a good choice here.<br><br>Graphically the game can be considered simple. With simple I mean it\u2019s not overly complex. You can clearly see the details in everything and all of it is nicely modeled, but the levels are very similar to each other and there\u2019s nothing in it that will \u2018wow\u2019 you. I agree with you if you say that games are not only about graphics (it\u2019s a clich\u00e9 argument, though), but I do think it\u2019s usually a combination of the two (with the second being the actual gameplay). So don\u2019t expect any bells and whistles here, but there are plenty of options that can be turned on or up to make the game look even prettier.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Length\/Replay Value<\/div>First of all: don\u2019t expect a really lengthy game here: the Early Access version currently includes 10 levels, and this is including two levels that act as a tutorial. The levels are rather short. A full playthrough should take you about 4-7 hours I think (max), and this includes puzzles where you might get stuck for a few or tens of minutes. Considering the price it is good value, but like many other puzzle games there isn\u2019t much incentive to replay it again once you\u2019re done. So I hope the developer will gradually release more content to keep players occupied and, of course, to test if everything works as it should.<br><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=527495869\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_2\">http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=527495869<\/a> <br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Conclusion<\/div>Steamroll is a good example of Early Access done right. It\u2019s not the biggest or most spectacular game out there but it definitely has its heart in the right place. The setting is done very well, but despite that it certainly would\u2019ve been nice to see a little more variety in its levels. The puzzles are balanced well and everyone should be able to play through it without getting (too) frustrated. It\u2019s a little light on content right now, but I was told the game will offer at least twice as many levels in the full and final release of the game. With that and its current quality in mind I expect that it will be nothing less than a fun, solid game of at least equal quality during the beginning of 2016, when it will release in its final form.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Too long, didn\u2019t read<\/div>Pros:<br>+ Nice Steampunk vibe<br>+ \u2018Simple\u2019 but effective graphics<br>+ Tight, solid controls (with controller)<br>+ Puzzles are generally nicely balanced<br><br>Mixed:<br>+\/- Sometimes it randomly drops to &lt;5 frames<br>+\/- Menus are still a little basic<br>+\/- \u2018Shooting form\u2019 UI could use some work<br><br>Cons:<br>- Camera angle during static puzzles<br>- Some trial and error puzzles<br><br><table class=\"bb_table\">    <tr>        <th>Please note: a Steam key for this game was provided by the developer.<\/th>    <\/tr><\/table><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"><b>Rating: 7.6 (out of 10)<\/b><\/div><br><br><table class=\"bb_table\"><tr><th>Want to read more of my unbiased reviews? Steamroll<br><b>Status:<\/b>  Released<br><b>Developer:<\/b>  Anticto<br><b>Publisher:<\/b>  Anticto<br><b>Genre:<\/b>  Puzzle<br><b>Release Date:<\/b>  19th of February, 2016<br><b>Type:<\/b>  Singleplayer <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">General Impression<\/div>\u2018Tis here game. I like it. Steampunk subgenre on Steam is certainly not as visible as cyberpunk. Being a fan of both, I knew it was only a matter of time until a dev team shall show proper attention to detail and affection for faux Victorian Age machinery. And I was right. That time has come and Steamroll is unlike any puzzle game I played in recent memory. Anticto impressed me. This is their first Steam project.<br><br>Unity Engine shows versatility once again, provided it reaches capable hands. Steamroll is hard to pin down to the puzzle genre alone since besides physics-based puzzles (minigolf-like, if you will) it strives to be a story-driven experience and for the majority of its gameplay it succeds this. A well written plot that\u2019s sprinkled with comic relief and constant engineering jokes. Let no one claim ever again that engineers are humorless. You roleplay as a rookie operator of a SCARABEUS Mining Mod.18 which is to put it in simplified terms, a mechanical sphere used by miners to work in Steamroll\u2019s hazardous environments. Dev team really made an extra effort in drawing up all those diagrams detailing  the exact measurements, effects and mechanical aspects of your new job, as if you were handed over a user\u2019s manual for your new Sphere.<br><br>It wouldn\u2019t be called \u201cSteampunk\u201d without proper use of steam power, right? The game\u2019s User Interface is straightforward but nicely simulated with a pressure dial on the left side of the screen and another dial on the right, with a steamball selector for ammo or walls and ramps. What\u2019s that all about? Well you didn\u2019t thought you would mine by smashing your state-of-the-art metal scarab head-on into whatever\u2019s standing between you and the end of each level, did you? Our trusty steampunk sphere has two operating modes. One involves rolling along while in transit and the other mode, is an anchored position from which you can shift into a steamball launcher. These balls serve as the main puzzle solver since you\u2019ll have to make clever use of them, by bouncing walls and other structures, in order to hit a switch or remove debris. Varies by situation and you are fortunate enough that these steamballs are quite versatile. They can be equipped with anything from mobile walls and ramps and even good ol\u2019 explosives. Because nothing spells \u201cmining\u201d more than blowing rocks up! And other things, including yourself if you\u2019re not careful.<br><br>Plenty of ways to fail a level since deathtraps are at every miscalculated turn. You see, the SCARABEUS being a steam-powered huge rolling sphere and all, you can\u2019t really dash in a straight line by your heart\u2019s desires. You will have to bounce off walls and ledges if you are to move from point A to B. Takes some time getting used to this, but I call it part of the experience. The game is never unforgivingly difficult and you always have a prediction line, clearly estimating the path of your launched steamballs. As with most video games, the key to success is a mix of patience and quick reflexes perhaps if you\u2019re into speedruns. I like to take my sweet time and explore the surroundings, collecting screenshots and all that jazz. You know, the regular stuff.<br><br>Steamroll isn\u2019t overpriced at all, given the state in which it presents itself along with the inclusion of both Cards and \u201cCheevos\u201d. In fact, it was bundled recently and it shall be found in such offers in the future as well. Quality titles such as Steamroll tend to appear every now and then in well-priced deals, just to draw attention to it while you neglect the run-of-the-mill titles bundled along. Surprisingly few cons to this title. I had to be nitpicky just to choose two weak points. Only 18 (relatively short, once you get the hang of it) levels so far. More would really be welcome, Anticto.
