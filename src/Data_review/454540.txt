Note this from the game devs -&gt; &quot;This is a social-economic sandbox RPG&quot;. So if you are looking for a fast paced PvP game or action packed adventure game, stop reading now and go away. This game requires patience, time, a little intelligence and certainly commitment.<br><br>This is a game where you work, trade, hunt or just blag your way to fortune. You start will a little airship hanging from a balloon and you set off into a world that is 500x500 miles in size to make that fortune. There are 2 playable races. The fantastic awesome Orcs, and the pink skinned lilly livered humans.  <br>Game progression is simple. You take some jobs, deliveries mostly. You float across the sky in your balloon and complete your job. You get paid some copper and get a little reputation. Rinse and repeat until you can afford a bigger better airship or two (yes you can command a whole fleet of them with skills). Then you can take on bigger jobs or try your hand at buying and trading in consumables between cities.<br>A little further on, a little more money, and you can establish a settlement of your own. Produce your own resources, airships, weapons, ammo etc etc. All can be traded on the market or directly from your own store.<br>Another viable option is hunting dragons. The other denizens of the world. Trophies from dragons are used in crafting some of the more advanced items in the game and are in high demand.<br><br>Level advancement is done in the Eve Online fashion (one of many similarities). You train a skill over time, even when offline. XP earned from skill training increases your level. Simples. There are crafting skills, pilot skills, magic skills, resource gathering etc etc. There are not too many at the moment but there are enough for you to specialize.<br><br>The community is great. At least the English speaking one. There is a large Russian player base too, doing what Russians do, but I have had no real interaction with them. The Dev team is really good. They are constantly updating the game and helping players out. They have a passion for the game, and that is usually one of the most important traits a dev can have. They want you to tell them what's wrong, and then they fix it. Communication can be difficult, but not impossible.<br><br>Combat-wise there is not much. Apart from fighting\/hunting dragons, there are also the pirates and rogue players. So far not a problem to me but they will be. Combat can be done with the auto attack feature or by manually aiming your weapons groups. There are also planes that you can launch from your ship like a carrier that will defend you, or you can station them in your settlement and have them fly combat air patrols over it. You can also launch as a pilot of one of these planes.<br><br>The economy is a work in progress. There is one, but it's a bit iffy with the small player base and the early players dominating the best settlement spots. Prices can be ridiculous too. That will change as more players join and there is more competition. There are 3 currencies in game. I shall simplifie them as copper, silver and gold. Gold is a premium currency you can buy. And it's actually dirt cheap really. You can trade all currency for one of the others but the prices are stupid in some areas. You need to look about for the best deals.<br><br>The Bad:<br>Well, there are certainly going to be no prizes for graphics. They are minimal at best. But, as you spend most of your time high in the air there is really no call for high level ground  detail. So it actually works just fine. And will run quite happily on most machines.<br><br>The economy is a bit broken, as I said above. However there are many players trying to improve this by selling their wares at sensible prices instead of the greedy ones. It will take time but it will happen. The majority of the community is interested in promoting the game and making it accessible to new players. Including the prices they have to pay when starting out.<br><br>Server restarts are a pain as they reset the consumables on the market and you have to wait for them to restock. This is an unfortunate consequence of the Devs being very active in fixing and updating things. We just have to suck it up.<br><br>Scammers are present. Some of the jobs you will see are just unrealistic. Very high deposits that require you to take ridiculous loads over long distances. I would not expect to reach your destination, you will probably be jumped on the way so you fail the job and the job poster gets your gold. There are also players with names I can not pronounce who like to add newbies as enemies in the hope they will attack them in a safe zone etc. They get nothing financially if you do, just a good laugh.<br><br>The game has no useful tutorial really and can be very confusing when starting out. It shows some tips but they can be a little bewildering and misleading. The whole translation of the game to English is seriously bad. The names of some items are just strange or wrong. There are a lot of things that just won't make sense at all or give the wrong information. Devs do seem to be fixing this when its pointed out though.<br><br>Conclusion:<br>I recommend this game if you are the sort of player who likes a relaxing game and doesn't mind the slow pace and time required. Economists and traders will love it. If you are a more combative type, then you probably won't like it much unless you go down the pirate route. That's your call. Even then though, combat you have isn't going to be fast paced and intensive. You are going to have to chase down targets over distance in a slow moving airship and work for your kills. And probably be hunted by a lot of others in return.<br>The game is rough around the edges and needs a lot of improvement. However it has 3 things that make it stand out. An active and passionate dev team, a great community and a whole boat load of potential. With some major improvements in certain areas, this is going to be a classic for players who like this premise. It's free to play so just try it. Introduce yourself in game and you will get a warm welcome and a lot of help.
