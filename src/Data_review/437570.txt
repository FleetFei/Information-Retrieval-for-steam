reccomendation. It's yet another indie-procedurally generated-permadeath-sidescrolling-action-platformer with a quirky artstyle. The saturation of these sorts of games in recent years has become tiring, and while GoNNER has some differentiating elements, it falls into a lot of the same pitfalls other games of the genre do. It's an enjoyable game, but it still has a lot of room for improvement.<br><br>GoNNER has a very simple premise. Run, jump, shoot and use items to get to the end of the level. Every 4 levels there's a boss, beat the boss to continue to the next world. Lose and you have to start the run all over. Starting a run you essentially choose a &quot;loadout&quot; of a Mask (which serves as your health pool and gives you passive bonuses like a third jump or reduced cooldowns), a weapon, and an item that can be used on a cooldown to give you more ammo or give you temporary invincibility. Anytime you take damage your stuff drops and you enter a state where you are invincible and sort of flop around so you can try and get your \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665 back. Since your Mask acts as your health bar, getting hit without it is an instant kill. Other masks, items and weapons will appear the levels and at stores where you can pay gylphs to buy new items, ammo, health and other useful things. Gylphs are currency found in some hidden areas, but are primarily aquired by comboing, every 5 enemies killed within a short period of time gets you a gylph and the more you combo the more gylphs you get. Gylphs are also used to revive you if you die, the cost increasing as you get further into the game as well as with each revival. <br><br>Compared to a lot of roguelites, the amount of items and the balancing is very different. There are only about 8 of each type and they are all more or less balanced to be equal, meaning you can beat the game with any set of items and, once you find a loadout you like, you don't have to look for or buy new ones unless you lose them by dropping them. This reduces the reliance on RNG to win that a lot of roguelites have, where getting good items makes or breaks a run. The only variables for winning are level layouts, knowledge and skill. On on hand I like this a lot because it makes it more like a classic sidescroller, where you just have to improve to win and can't rely on lucky drops to win or blame unlucky drops when you lose. But even though they are theoretically balanced, certain items in my experience rise to the top. <br>By default you can only shoot straight left or right, meaning in order to hit enemies above or below you have get to their level. This becomes a serious problem in the second world which features large amounts of flying enemies and enemies on walls postioned  above or below you. The randomly generated nature of the game creates massive chokepoints where it's incredibly difficult to go through or around enemies hitting you at all angles.  This problem is exacerabated by the introduction a bottomless death pit which can not only kill you instantly if you get hit, but also cause you to lose your items permanently if you don't have the retrieval skull. The only items really able to counter this safely are the the triple jump mask, which also allows you to rotate in midair allowing diagonal shots, and the usuable item that allows you to detonate explosions in a ring around you. The only other ways to get around this is to use the rocket launcher weapon, which has the weird tendency to hit the ground in front of you when fired, and the mask that causes enemies to explode on death, which is extremely unpredictable due to the physicy nature of the game. Later levels don't get much better. The increased enemy count adds to the unpredictablity and increase the need for you to rely on tools that hit at an angle.<br><br>More positively, GoNNER's biggest strength is its feel. Art in Heart and Raw Fury ultilize the Vlambeer suite of little tricks and effects to make simple gameplay feel fun and responsive to play. Jumping, shooting, and killing enemies all have a satisfying weight and pop to their animation and sound. The art style is colorful and quirky, but readable even when there's tons of action on screen. The soundtrack is almost silent when not engaging enemies, but kicks in as you fire. And it only gets better as you combo. Comboing adds a new stem to the music and every five more kills in a combo speeds up and increases the volume of soundtrack. Death effects are louder. Controller vibration gets stronger. Combo high enough and the color palatte will start rhythmically shifting to the music, changing permanently when you stop the combo. All these little effects encourage you to take risks and fight faster to get a higher combo and get a higher score. It's an ingenious system that unfortunately suffers because the rest of the game is mediocre. <br><br>There other weird small decisions that hamper the game. For example, after the end of each world, you meet or whale buddy who rewards you with an item. If you have don't have full health, she fills you up and gives you a sort of skull option that retreives your items when you get hit. But if you are full, she upgrades your weapon to have more max ammo. Because of the problems mentioned above about item recovery, the skull is far more useful generally, since the upgraded weapon can easily be lost when you get hit or die. In addition, if you combo to the point where the colors change, you are rewarded with a different skull that retrieves gylphs for you. But if you have this skull which you meet the whale, even if you have low health you won't get the retrieval skull. In both instances, this feels like the game punishing you for doing well.  Another weird aspect is that the level sort of forms around your character as you walk forward. This is a cool effect, but it can sometimes be a nuisance because you are unable to see what's ahead of you until you get close enough. In terms of bugs, the only thing I found was that if I fired while exiting a level, the pushback would carry over to the next  and sometimes push me outside the bounds of the level, forcing me to kill myself to procede.<br><br>I like GoNNER, but it's a rough game. I reccomend it because I think there's potential. I believe this is the developers' first commercial project, so they have room to get better in the future. They obviously know how to make satisfying action, so if they refine the mechanics and design they could really make something special. Personally I would like to see something with set level design instead of random generation. Where you're given specific tools to overcome a specific obstacle. <\/div><br>GoNNER is a rogue-like platformer developed by Art in Heart and published by Raw Fury. In GoNNER you play as Ikk, a small blob who enjoys wearing skulls and shooting things. He\u2019s a bit of a weird one. Your goal is to progress through different levels and worlds killing things and trying not to die. You\u2019ll want to try and rack up loads of points too so you can show all your friend(s) how badass you are on the leaderboards!<br><br>I\u2019ve always been a substance-over-style kind of gamer but GoNNER is too damn stylish for me to not mention it. For a start, GoNNER is beautiful. The bright colors of Ikk, enemies and any pickups in the level contrast really well with the dark background in early levels and still look good against the brighter backgrounds`. It makes the game pop. The simplistic character design also work very well for GoNNER. On top of all that, GoNNER has a fantastic soundtrack and it\u2019s incorporated into the game in a really interesting way. As you get higher combos the music speeds up, getting faster and faster as you kill more and more enemies without a break. I found this to really help build up tension in the game when fighting off a buttload of enemies.<br><br>As I mentioned, Ikk is a blob. How does a blob fight off enemies you ask? By picking up things he finds during his travels of course! In other words, you find items during your runs (one of each item type for each world as far as I\u2019ve seen so far) and you can then choose which items to use next time you start over. The three different items types are: heads, weapons and equipment. The head you choose determines how much health you have and how quickly you move, some of them do other things too but you can find out about that yourself! The weapons are up next, so far there\u2019s some good variation and while the weapons seem balanced you\u2019ll probably end up choosing one you like more than the other. Each weapon has a set amount of ammo and once you run out of that ammo you\u2019ll have to find a bullet casing dropped by an enemy, this is the pickup that replenishes your ammo. Lastly you\u2019ve got equipment, basically the equivalent of spacebar items in The Binding of Isaac: Rebirth. These all have different effects and they recharge over time. One of them gives you a bullet pickup, another acts like a personal trampoline. As with weapons, you\u2019ll probably find one in particular that you like more than the others.<br><br>There are two other kinds of pickups in GoNNER: hearts and purple runes. Hearts can be found by opening blue chests in the levels, they\u2019re a welcome sight let me tell you. The purple runes are pretty interesting. They\u2019re dropped by enemies (the drop rate seems to increase as you rack up combos) and they\u2019re used for two things. Firstly, if you have enough of them they can be used to resurrect yourself when you run out of hearts\/die without a head. The cost of doing this increases each time you do it and it increases each time you progress to a new world and when you do resurrect yourself you start at the beginning of the level you died on. The second use for them is in the item shops before a boss fight at the end of a world. The shops stock one of each item type as well as either a bullet or a heart. The different items have different prices and you can also spend a couple of runes to swap out what\u2019s on sale.<br><br>So speaking of death (Ikk has a friend called Death funnily enough) it\u2019s going to happen a lot. As you\u2019d expect from a rogue-like game. There are two ways to fully die in GoNNER. You either lose all of your hearts and then get hit again or you somehow manage to get hit while not wearing a head. When you get hit you lose a heart, drop all your items and Ikk returns to blob form. As a blob, you have a few seconds to bounce around with no risk of losing health while you either try to grab your items back or move to a safer spot. Returning to Ikk\u2019s headless chicken form will send out a little shockwave that kills anything standing too close to you. Comes in pretty handy.<br><br>Between each world is a sort of intermediary level home to Sally, a giant landbound whale and Ikk\u2019s best friend. Sally loves you. When you reach this level and go see Sally she\u2019ll be kind enough to increase your weapon\u2019s ammo capacity and give you an extra heart container. Both of which come in very handy as the game gets even more difficult with each world. One thing that\u2019s worth noting is that if you die and resurrect yourself with runes you\u2019ll lose the extra ammo and extra heart container.<br><br>Going through the levels and killing enemies earns you points which then end up on the leaderboard. I really like the way the leaderboard is set out. It shows you who got how many points as well as how long that run was and what items were used during it. It seems to me like the only way to earn points is to kill enemies in the level and each enemy type gives you a different amount of points (there are four or five enemy types per world). As you would expect, the higher your combo is, the more points you earn.<br><br>As much as I\u2019ve enjoyed what I\u2019ve played of GoNNER so far, I do have one main problem with it. It lacks control re-bindings. Now the default bindings for the M&amp;KB and for controllers are decent and I\u2019ve enjoyed playing using both, however, they aren\u2019t going to perfect for everyone and there were a couple of bindings in particular that I had some trouble with\/wanted to change. It\u2019s not a huge issue but I would definitely like to see key re-bindings patched in at some point soon.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Verdict <\/div><br>GoNNER combines superb style with some great substance to create an all-round satisfying experience.
