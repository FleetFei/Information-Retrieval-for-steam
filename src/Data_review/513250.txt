class=\"bb_h1\"> <b>Overview<\/b> <\/div><br>Rogue Stache is a crazy and fun-filled rogue-like platformer. It's retro styled with a modern feel. This game follows the last manly man alive, after every other man was mutilated and de-moustached. From the opening cut-scene till the last boss, Rogue Stache will have you hooked with its insane bloody combat and huge collection of hats.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"><b> Components<\/b> <\/div><br><b>Visuals<\/b> - The general visual style of this game is retro 16 bit styled graphics (to my knowledge). It's fairly pretty for the most part. My favourite visual aspect of this game is the enemy design, particularly the bosses. They felt unique and really creative, making them more of a joy to battle against. However, the visual aspect falls a bit short on the level design, for each level seems simplistic and looks repetitive, and each stage is almost identical save for a different colour palette. The visual effects are pretty good, you'll see TONS of blood from shooting enemies, and the explosions and weapon projectiles are fairly good. All in all, Rogue Stache delivers solid, yet improveable graphics.<br><br><b>Audio<\/b> - The game has a few music tracks, but they sound really good and fit well. The sound effects are great and every attack or action is accompanied with a sound effect. The sound design is reminiscent of classic retro styled games and overall they've done a fairly good job.<br><br><b>Gameplay<\/b> - Rouge Stache is a rogue-like platformer, where each level requires pushing a button to open the exit door before proceeding to the next level. There's 4 stages, each with 5 levels, and a boss at the end of each stage. After beating all 4 bosses, you will fight the final boss. Firstly, the actual platforming feels responsive and well polished, so it delivers in that aspect. The shooting is spot on, and every gun has its use. One thing I didn't like was the fact that gun drop-rate was way too high, because on the first level alone I had every inventory slot filled and there was still more guns I couldn't pick up. The levels aren't procedurally generated, there's only a bunch of preset levels, however enemies, loot, the button and everything else is randomised. I found the game to get slightly repetitive due to a lack of unique levels. One awesome feature I really liked, and which greatly extended the longevity of the game, was the hat spin machine. Using tokens acquired through gameplay, you can spin the hat wheel machine to get a random hat with random positive attributes (such as extra inventory, extra jump, more health etc). It's really fun trying out different hats with their different abilities and they are really helpful. Overall, the gameplay of Rogue Stache is fairly mediocre but offers fun and unique gameplay elements.<br><br><b>Story<\/b> - After every other man has been mutilated and their moustaches taken, it is up to you to save mankind by eliminating all the enemies. There's not much of a story here but it gives you a bit of context, which is all that's really needed for this sort of game.<br><br><b>Functionality<\/b> - Generally, this game runs quite smoothly but in the midst of heavy action, especially using explosives against crowds of enemies, the game really starts to slow down. The optimisation could be improved slightly, but for the most part it runs well. I encountered no glitches or issues while playing. Overall, Rogue Stache is a well functioning game but suffers from minor performance hiccups.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"><b> Pros<\/b> <\/div><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li> Hats with random attributes<br><\/li><li> Lots of enemies and lots of guns to shoot them with<br><\/li><li> Very bloody<br><\/li><li> Solid platforming and shooting mechanics<\/li><\/ul><div class=\"bb_h1\"><b> Cons<\/b> <\/div><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li> Level design is fairly mundane and repetitive<br><\/li><li> Very short game, only took me an hour before I was able to kill the final boss<br><\/li><li> Performance issues<br><\/li><li> Lack of unique levels<\/li><\/ul><div class=\"bb_h1\"> <b>Conclusion<\/b> <\/div><br>In conclusion, if you're looking for a fun and hectic rogue-like platformer, then Rogue Stache may be the game for you.<br>I give this game a 5\/10 - a well made platformer which is fun and unique, but lacking content<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"> Bonus Early Access Review for the Developers <\/div><br>While this game is in Early Access, I suggest that the performance issues are fixed.
