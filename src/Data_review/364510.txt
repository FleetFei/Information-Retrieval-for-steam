Here's the <b>tl;dr<\/b>: you don't have to believe me, <a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"https:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/linkfilter\/?url=https:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/linkfilter\/?url=http:\/\/transcendence.kronosaur.com\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\" >you can try the base game (which contains a good 75% of the content for the main storyline) for free<\/a>, to your heart's content, while also enjoying any modifications available for it, which can be accessed through <a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"https:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/linkfilter\/?url=https:\/\/forums.kronosaur.com\/viewforum.php?f=24&amp;sid=99c4e7c6e1578a0e175f93701ccf81c5\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\" >the community forums<\/a>, and all future updates. Yeah, for free -- no, not free2play, but free as in free to have fun.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">What will you be buying?<\/div> <br>So, Transcendence has a long term development plan. New versions are going to be released with no end in sight, as far as I know. Transcendence, the base engine, allows you to play adventures in the Transcendence universe. This base engine is free. <br><br>The primary adventure <a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"https:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/linkfilter\/?url=http:\/\/transcendence.kronosaur.com\/guidePage.hexm?id=909\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\" >(yes, there are others, well explained by this infographic)<\/a> is that of a character who hears the voice of a hyper-intelligence calling them to the centre of the universe, and listens to the voices in their head -- this adventure is called &quot;Domina and Oracus&quot;, and is also free. Currently, Part 1 of the story has been released, along with a content expansion pack called Corporate Command, but the expansion pack is not free.<br><br>There is another official adventure that has been released, and this one is called &quot;Eternity Port&quot;. It is about a character who is left for dead in the depths of space after an attack on their ship, only to be saved by a selfish (I saved you, so *obviously* you owe me) entity representing the virtual citizens of Eternity Port. They want you to do something for them, starting off the adventure. This adventure isn't free. If you want to see how Eternity Port fits into the lore of the Transcendence universe, check out <a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"https:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/linkfilter\/?url=http:\/\/transcendence.kronosaur.com\/guidePage.hexm?id=909\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\" >the infographic<\/a> I mentioned earlier.<br><br><b>You're buying Corporate Command (the expansion content for the main adventure), together with Eternity Port (the other official adventure); in other words, you're buying all the content that exists so far, and costs money.<\/b><br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">How are basic things like the graphics, UI and sound design?<\/div><br>The <b>graphics are pretty<\/b>, as you can see from screen shots. Starry space backgrounds, mixed with the foreground composed of ships, planets and stations with textures that fit in perfectly. Even though this is an independently developed game, it doesn't skimp out on graphics by pretending to be &quot;retro&quot;, or trying to get into the good books of old-schoolers with trashy UI design. The game's graphics are modern, and suit the gameplay.<br><br>The game <b>has a soundtrack that's original and beautiful<\/b> (I am humming the music that starts you off on an adventure right now), and <b>has sound effects that aren't archaic<\/b>. While allowing for some mouse interaction in menus, the <b>UI is mostly designed to take keyboard input, but isn't painful to use because of that decision<\/b>, and most gamers will feel at home right away: the arrow keys move your ships around in interesting ways, and major buttons like the spacebar or control keys handle the firing of weapons. Keyboard shortcuts usually make sense etymologically (e.g. &quot;D&quot; for Dock, &quot;J&quot; for jump), and there is an excellent help infographic that is accessible any time in the game that lists out all the keyboard shortcuts.  <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">So, space adventure game. What will I actually be doing?<\/div><br>The storylines, depending on which adventure you are playing, give some background as to why you are setting off in space, and then some occasional guiding structure throughout. What do you do in between? Well, it's up to you. <br><br>You can trade: there are minerals to be mined, the ore sold or forged into useful armour, which can be sold for an even higher price. Or, you can go about simply buying low and selling high, making a living for yourself that allows you to buy fuel and the ammunition needed to stay safe. Maybe you decide you're interested in also running a side business in the black market. Well, as long as you have a murky sense of morality, the connections to get started, and the know how to hide your goods from the authorities, maybe you'll be successful.<br><br>You can sign up with the Commonwealth Forces, or some of the other regional powers (depending on which adventure you are playing), and rise up through the ranks. You can meditate in the halls of the Sisters of Domina's temples. Maybe you can figure out what this voice in your head wants. Maybe you are interested in donating items to the Sisters' cause, and helping the needy that you meet in beaten down habitat stations that are nothing more than cargo containers strung together. <br><br>Maybe you free slaves held in living stasis inside coffins by the Sung Slavers, as you attempt to make sure you aren't squashed by them. Maybe you get involved in story of the Sung and the Huari, the Aztec\/Maya-esque space culture that was decimated by the Sung's relentless ruthlessness. Maybe some zoanthrope buddies tag along because you beat their masters, and thus are next most powerful thing they know to follow. Maybe you willingly submit yourself to the nasty experiments of humans who have genetically modified themselves to such a degree that they might as well be aliens. <br><br>Maybe you actually bump into aliens, and other creepy-crawlies. Maybe. It's your call. The game is detailed enough to waste hours of your time, with nice flavour text to spice up every piece of the universe. Bring your imagination along, and go for a ride.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">What about mods?<\/div><br><b>Transcendence is heavily modifiable, and has an active community of modders<\/b>: everything in the universe can be described and programmed with XML files and a custom LISP variant (TransLisp). The C++ guts of Transcendence are completely open as well, and available for viewing and participation on GitHub. Modding documentation is sparse, but community participation makes up for it.<br><br>So, if you're into modifying games, Transcendence will give you lots to do. If you like playing with other people's modifications, Transcendence won't disappoint you there either. Popular mods include a complete revamp of the mining system, AI based modifications that allow the game world to be even more dynamic by randomly generating conflict and rebuilding stations, content packs that add new weapons or features such as the ability to craft new weapons from components, and so on. <br><br>I can't help but quote something really relevant from one of the prominent modders, pixelfck, on why he loves the game:<br><br><blockquote class=\"bb_blockquote with_author\"><div class=\"bb_quoteauthor\">#Discussions_QuoteBlock_Author<\/div> The storyline is quite deep, with promise for even more; There is a lot of content (devices, armour, misc-items), many of which hook back into the background universe; Its open source (\u00a9 George Moromisato); I can mod it (step 1: initial notion); The modding system has a quite powerful scripting available (step 2: the promise); All vanilla content has been made with the modding options available to me, no hard-coded 'magic' (step 3: the realisation of full potential); <\/blockquote><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Final words.<\/div><br>My recommendation is that you try out the free content. Get yourself into the community and modifications, if that interests you. If you love it all, and want more, buy the Steam version that contains all content, or buy the content piecemeal as you want to using Kronosaur's &quot;Multiverse&quot; infrastructure.
