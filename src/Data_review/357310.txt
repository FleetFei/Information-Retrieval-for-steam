class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\t<div class=\"bb_h1\">A 4X tribute to the East, Oriental Empires provides hours of strategic TBS gameplay for fans of the civilization building strategy games.<\/div> Though you see elements of Civ V and Age of Empires games at times, don\u2019t expect the game to be true to either franchise. Instead, what you find is an experience that comes across as a comic book combined with a board game from another era, and spawned into digital format; born again in some alternate continent\u2019s code of conduct. If you are looking for something different in the world of 4X, this may be for you. At this time, it released in Early Access, only because the multiplayer is not finished. So, only single player is available at this point.<br><br><b>Art &amp; Music:<\/b> <br>In a beautiful world of mountains &amp; rivers, laid out on a map that allows you to panoramically zoom to any degree in or out, the Empires of the Far East come to life in the beautiful scenes that seem to be reminiscent of charcoal drawings. It\u2019s an art style sure to be appealing to everyone. When you zoom in, you become a part of the village &amp; the daily lives of the people. Yet, when you zoom out you see the whole countryside and can plan &amp; plot combat moves &amp; colonization. If you continue to zoom out, you can even see other empires and the cities they are building. All of this beautiful scenery is augmented with lovely tunes from the Orient that are as high-quality as those made by Kairis for Paradox Studio\u2019s lovely music dlc\u2019s. The tunes are lively &amp; provide ambience that certainly makes the gameplay enjoyable.<br><br><b>Graphics:<\/b><br>With simplified graphics reminiscent of This War of Mine, Oriental Empires makes the story come alive on a map in an imaginative way that can be intensely pleasing for many people. It could be seen as quaint, or maybe retro. It certainly calls to mind playing old Stratego &amp; Risk boardgames. This may not appeal to some gamers, especially the simple characters, who appear as 2d in the bottom corner, &amp; fight in stiff 3d scripted forms. It\u2019s certainly not a Triple A title, but it manages to bring a sense of atmosphere to the world of the Orient. <br><br><b>Narrative:<\/b><br>The storyline progresses through 5 eras of Chinese &amp; Mongolian history. You are able to play as different factions, and you have some control over when you move into the next era, based on which tech you choose. The techs are limited, but provide story-rich choices to the way you will develop your nation. Some narration appears between eras, and the voice acting is as great as the music. But, don\u2019t expect a Western game set in the East. The game itself &amp; the story are very Oriental in design. <br><br><b>Mechanics:<\/b><br>The combat allows you to plot all your moves, &amp; then watch them play out before you. This forces you to plan for contingencies. You are able to change the way the units face at waypoints. You are able to select their fighting stance; whether defensive or offensive, in a variety of strategies. And, unlike most games, it seems often that the best method is to avoid conflict, and defend your villages. I\u2019ve seen the raiders act just like huns; pillage, burn, run, &amp;  while you chase after them, another unit is coming in behind to raid your unprotected village. The mechanics are not cookie cutter, and seem to be unique to the game, despite the fact that many components are shared by other 4X games. <br><br>The tutorial needs work, though there are no bugs at all that I\u2019ve seen. I like the way the advisor stays in the corner greyed out until you need her. Also, all of the overlays are unobtrusive, since they can be toggled individually to allow for great screen shots, which is very important to many people. I wish some of the bigger 4X titles could learn that idea. The biggest problem I see with the game for some is that you need to read the text info provided behind the question mark, in something similar to an infopedia to play the game. It\u2019s one of those TL;DR things that some just won\u2019t care to deal with. But, for me, I read everything from street signs to cereal box small print anyways. In the case of this game, you will probably need to read the more info area to proceed. Also, the advisor seems to offer advice periodically, just sometimes that advice seems to come after you need it instead of before. <br><br><b>Pros:<\/b> <br>*Art style is very engaging<br>*Combat is fun &amp; fresh<br>*Gameplay is realistic for the Far East territory<br>*Interface is user-friendly with total control <br><br><b>Cons:<\/b><br>*Tutorial is scant leaving a learning curve<br>*Graphics are simplistic <br>*In EA until MP is complete<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">I recommend Oriental Empires for those who enjoy fresh strategic turn-based gameplay set in particular historical locales.
