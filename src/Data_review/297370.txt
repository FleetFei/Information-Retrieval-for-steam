2014\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t<div class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHero Of Many tells the story of an orb-like creature who\u2019s on a quest to defeat \u2018black\u2019 (evil) and at the same time save as many of its friends as possible. It draws inspiration from several games, including Journey, Limbo and even a bit of Pikmin. \u2018Three awesome games ? This must be awesome too then\u2019, I hear you say ? Well, yes and no.<br><br>Let me start by saying why I think it draws inspiration from the aforementioned games: <br>Journey because it tells a story without saying or showing any words; you start the game and you can pretty much immediately control the main character. Limbo because of its silhouette art style, even if it uses some background colors later on and Pikmin because you\u2019ll be guiding your friends through obstacles, while avoiding and killing enemies. <br>But you don\u2019t directly control your tadpole-like creatures. You can send them into a certain direction but you don\u2019t control their actions. They\u2019ll do the best they can, so if they see something edible they will consume it and if they need to break something they\u2019ll break it. Maybe not all of them, but some of them, at least. Still, it\u2019s all mostly an automatic process.<br><br>You\u2019ll guide your character and the rest of the creatures through 26 levels. Except for a few of them, these levels are built in largely the same way. Navigate through maze-like levels while saving your friends and killing\/avoiding enemies and get to the exit. This is fun and interesting at first, and like I said, the game does throw a few other challenges your way later in the game, but you\u2019ll be doing mostly the same stuff for the entire duration of the game.<br>This isn\u2019t a long game with just a few hours of gameplay but halfway through I grew a bit bored by it. There wasn\u2019t much of a challenge and you\u2019d rarely die. Was the entire game going to be this easy ? The answer is no. Because halfway through you\u2019ll also start earning new powers. These don\u2019t seem like much, but they\u2019re actually quite handy once you realize the game would\u2019ve been so much easier if you\u2019d have started with them. So, they\u2019re a good addition to your character and they fit the game; nothing over-the-top. You\u2019ll earn a power to attract your buddies, you\u2019ll earn a power to bounce off enemies\/open doors and you\u2019ll earn a power to destroy things. And yes, during the course of the game you will eventually die. Probably several times. At times it won\u2019t always be exactly clear what\u2019s expected of you and it might take you a few tries more than it officially should take you. This is one of the shortcomings of trying to let people experience the story instead of actually telling it. You\u2019ll probably learn it after a while anyway, but I made me feel bitter about the game, especially because things should feel natural if you do it this way.<br><br>As I already mentioned the game\u2019s graphics reminded me of those of Limbo, but with some much needed colors. Your creature will have a bit of color inside of it, and the backgrounds, but the rest of it is all black and white. It\u2019s a good contrast and the use of colors give you an idea of progression.<br>It all looks beautiful in motion, though there is a bit of stutter every now and then. Note that the framerate doesn\u2019t drop, not even for a second, but somehow the game does slow down from time to time. Really weird and this shouldn\u2019t happen in a 2D game.<br>The interface is clearly made with mobile phones in mind and it\u2019s one of the things that could\u2019ve used a bit of extra work. Don\u2019t get me wrong, it all works very nicely. It\u2019s just shown in such a way that I was frequently reminded that I\u2019m dealing with a port here.<br>The music is beautifully orchestrated and has a good, bombastic atmosphere. I\u2019m not sure if a band was really used here, or that it was all manufactured but I was absolutely impressed by it. Excellent work, really.<br><br>Hero Of Many isn\u2019t a long game and it should take you roughly 5-6 hours to complete. After that there\u2019s no real reason to go through it again, but that\u2019s okay. I think it\u2019s a game meant to be played just once, or at least once every (couple of) year(s). For me personally it was long enough, I actually would\u2019ve enjoyed it a bit more if it was shorter, but most of you will enjoy it regardless.<br><br>Hero Of Many isn\u2019t my hero, but it might just be for the rest of you. In that case its title <i>was<\/i> carefully chosen. It\u2019s an enjoyable game that drags on for a bit too long but it\u2019s not in the least boring or bad enough to not play it. Nor will you stay a small blob.
