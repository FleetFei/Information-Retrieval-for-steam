this game is very good. I played it all day long and it was a nice break from life XD. Though there are some things that might be a bit off, Starlight Vega is a very enjoyable visual novel. Now onto the review <br><br>Story <br>I find the story to have some very strong points but also some weak ones. The story overall is very interesting from the book, to how Lyria became trapped in stone, and the curse plaguing Vega. My problem though (and this depends on the route) is that thehe story can be weak at times. I think the story stayed pretty strong in Lyria's route. Not only do you find out who Aria's grandpa is, but you also get to know how Lyria came to be in the stone. Same goes for Melody's route to. Where it falls weak though is when we get to the curse and the book itself at times. I sometimes felt like the curse put on Vega wasn't shown enough and wished to feel more of that dread. I admit in Scherza's route that the curse is a bit better played out, but it kind of ends just like that. Still the story was written well and I will give major kudos to how the creator handle the intimate scenes. <br><br>Characters <br><br>One of the best parts of the game and they are what hold the story together. I'll give my thoughts on all of the main five. <br><br>Aria- Our main character. She is an average yet interesting girl who is put into a tough situation. I'll say Aria is pretty likable. She has a personality and tries her best to deal with all these new things thrown at her. I admit sometimes I kind of wish she had more of a spark, but for what we got I did enjoy her and she made the romance feel true with the girls. <br><br>Lyria- I think the best girl. From the moment I saw her I really did enjoy her personality and flirtatious, yet sometimes forceful attitude. Her route I believe was the strongest and I felt Aria and she were the best match. What I liked most about her route was the pacing with the romance. It felt very genuine and the pair had good chemistry. Also her story felt the most complete and I kind of wish the bad guy was in all of the routes since it made more sense. If I had to pick something I didn't like....well I can't think of it XD. Lyria was really a good and well thought out character. My fav to. <br><br>Melody- Aria's best friend. From the moment I saw this girl's design I knew what she'd be like and I was right. Now don't get me wrong, Melody is a good character and the type of friend I'd want. I admit though she was the most boring of the three, but don't get me wrong, I still liked her character. Her love and care for Aria was really sweet and though I found her route to be the less interesting of the three, I did care for her. Only thing is I think she'd be better as a friend to Aria, but that's just me. Her route was cute though and she really has some beautiful CG's<br><br>Scherza-I'll admit, I was a bit surprised by this character. I knew she'd be the cold demon and some may find her not too likable. However Scherza, especially in her own route, shows a great deal of sadness and loneliness through the story due to the events in the past. What surprised me even more was she wasn't a Tsun type character despite being cold. In fact I really felt for the girl in all the routes. Her relationship with Aria was done pretty well to and that last CG in her route is pretty amazing. I did feel like Scherza was the second best matched for Aria to. Some points I didn't like about her route was how sometimes it felt rushed. Though the curse is touched upon more and you do feel sorry for Vega, I wished that it was a bit longer to show more of the problem Vega was facing But the creator did do a good job with pacing in her route, so it's not that big of a problem <br><br>Sid- At first I hated this little guy, but he does grow on you. I can't say much without spoiling anything, but I'll tell you that you will feel sorry for him as you read. <br><br>Art<br><br>Art is Starlight Vega is very good. I love how the sprites have  unique style. I admit I wish they had some more outfit variations. It was odd to go from sprites and then to CGs where the girls are wearing something entirely different. Still the sprites are very well done. <br><br>CGs are exceptional. Every single one was done very well and I'll tell you I'll be looking back at them XD. If you can save them do, because Razz did an awesome job. <br><br><br>Backgrounds are pretty good to. I admit some are better then others, but I never got put off by any. I think the demon castle had some of the best backgrounds. I do think that the castle bed rooms needed just small variations as well, but that's a minor thing. <br><br>Music <br><br>Music is good. Some songs may be a bit off putting, but overall the track was nice hearing. I never turned it off.<br><br>Closing Notes <br><br>Starlight Vega is a very good and  fun visual novel. It's the first one I've played straight through in a while. The characters, art and story are overall great and I thank the creator for doing a GxG visual novel that can be enjoyed by all people and not just a certain group. Another thing I appreciate is how she handled the intimate scenes. They are done very well and I feel like if she did a more adult game she'd handle them well. <br><br>Anyway if you're looking for a fun, pure GxG, romance story this is it. a yuri visual novel. It allows you to make some choices here and there that guide you towards a particular ending, but otherwise you'll be spending your time reading--which is fine, because the story is engaging and complex.  <\/div><br>.Story.<br>It's so difficult to comment on this without spoiling anything, so I'll just say that it's cute and fluffy at times, raunchy and suggestive at others. There's political intrigue, humour, fantasy\/magical elements, supernatural elements like ghosts and other realms, combat, romance, non-explicit limes... The list goes on. I imagine there's something here for everyone, especially if you're a fan of reading. <br><br>There were only a few typos, which I greatly appreciated. Thank you for your level of polish, Starlight Vega, you beautiful beast. There are also a couple of plot holes (and one big enough to fall into), but I was so focused on the *adorable* romance that I didn't really mind all that much.<br><br>.Game play.<br>What are you, new here? This is a visual novel. Click your mouse and read the text that appears. <br><br>You can save and load at any point, barring when you're in the middle of making a decision, simply by pressing the right mouse button, which is handy. There's also a gallery for all of the CG you unlike by triggering events in the story or as part of the normal playthrough. <br><br>You can play in full screen or windowed, though I preferred the latter because full screen gives you black bars on the sides.<br><br>.Soundtrack.<br>There was never a moment where I felt as though the music didn't suit what was going on in-game. Romantic scenes featured softer, slower tracks and fight scenes featured upbeat electronic scores. The background music kept me immersed in the story, never drawing away from what was happening, but also making what I read that much more memorable because it was so well-suited. <br><br>Hell, I went to the store while playing to see if the store had the option to purchase the soundtrack separately.<br><br>-A minor gripe is that some of the sound effects used for things like gusts of wind and magical attacks sound a little hokey.<br><br>.Art style.<br>The game looks great. Kudos to whoever handled the art.  It's all done in a brightly-coloured anime style, and the backgrounds share the same feel. <br><br>The main character's portrait, which is almost always presented in the bottom left corner of the screen, changes expression depending on what's being said. It's a nice touch and very well detailed. The other characters have a host of different positions and facial expressions that are cycled through, which is another nice touch to make them seem more than just some beautifully-rendered image.<br><br>-The backgrounds were pretty limited and, though the game takes place in a variety of settings, you only ever see the same series of images; i.e, the bits where you're at school utilize the same image of the woods that you see when you're at home. Again, this is minor. The backgrounds were still nice to look at, it was just a matter of taking a moment to ground myself in what setting the action had moved to.<br><br>.Characters.<br><br>There are eight main ones who get detailed portraits. Everone else is just a name on the screen. The cast is varied without fulfilling too many over-used tropes like the emo pretty boy or the girl who wants his attention. Not a single character was annoying, which I find tends to happen a lot with this kind of game. That's a major plus.<br><br>Characters include demons, humans, and a wyvern. His name is Sid. He's adorable.<br><br>Again, not much to be said here without spoiling anything.<br><br>.Bottom line.<br>If you're in the market for a visual novel, this is definitely worth your money. The game looks good, it's well-written, and you'll get multiple playthroughs out of it. I adore this game. It's one of the better visual novels I've played on Steam; I'm only stopping here because it's very late at night. <br><br>Needless to say, I'm hooked.
