Neither of those games bring new life to the genre but Melissa K And The Heart Of Gold makes a bold attempt anyway.<br><br>There isn\u2019t much to tell because the game is meant for casual gamers and the gameplay is always kind of fun, but ultimately still too shallow. The story brings you to Thailand where you eventually have to solve a case about a missing Buddhist. Further into the game you\u2019ll stumble upon new discoveries which require you to travel to new places and solve even more puzzles. <br>The game is shown to you from a first person perspective, which isn\u2019t entirely fixed, but I\u2019ll talk about that in a minute. Your objective is to then find the correct objects to click on and to occasionally solve a (relatively easy) logical puzzle. No, that\u2019s right; it\u2019s not all about hidden objects.<br>Clicking on certain objects will reveal puzzles or other objects. These objects can sometimes be used in combination with other ones, or you have might have to find a whole set of them. From time to time you have to click on an object and rotate it to unlock a certain item. So there\u2019s actually a very decent amount of diversity to be found here.<br>Later on you\u2019ll also acquire objects like a gecko and spyglass that you can have access to all the time. The gecko for example will help you grab items from tiny holes and the spyglass can be used to zoom in on far away objects, usually to find hidden\/secret messages and codes.<br>If you don\u2019t want to solve a puzzle or don\u2019t know what to do next you can click on a Skip button (which becomes available after a few minutes) or you can press the Hint button. The Hint button doesn\u2019t give any penalties and can be reused after about 30 seconds, free of charge. See it as some sort of \u2018Win\u2019 button. In my opinion, there should be consequences for using things like this, but on the other hand I can understand that the developers want anyone to be able to play and finish this.<br>A lot of the puzzles later on in the game are farfetched, however, and seem to be made to give the game a longer life. You can definitely tell that the developers stretched out the story. This ultimately hurts the game. Again, this is a game that would\u2019ve been better if it was shorter. These games don\u2019t have to be very long, as long as they\u2019re enjoyable all the way through. I enjoyed it very much until 4 hours in, after that I couldn\u2019t wait to just finish and quit it.<br>I do have to admit that this is still one of the better hidden objects\/casual adventure games out there so even though it might seem that I\u2019m bashing the game that\u2019s not my intention. It\u2019s just that with games like these it\u2019s easier to talk about the negative parts since everyone usually already knows what to expect as far as the gameplay is concerned.<br><br>A cool feature that the developers introduced with this game is that the game looks 2D but in reality it is actually 3D. That perspective I was talking about in the last paragraph can also be moved to the left and right. It\u2019s nothing fancy; don\u2019t expect complete freedom here, but it looks great and it\u2019s something that gives the game a little more depth. I wish more games would use this because it makes the game a lot more interesting visually. The art is beautiful and colorful and there\u2019s plenty of detail to be found in them, but sometimes things are tucked away a bit too good. <br>The actors also did a good job with the voice acting. Some of it is still terrible, sure, but most of it is at least decent.<br><br>Melissa K And The Heart Of Gold is again one of those games that doesn\u2019t reinvent the genre but hey, at least it tried. The developer has integrated some smart (gameplay) elements that should be available in more games like these. It\u2019s nothing big or revolutionary but still good in any case.<br>For now this remains one of the better hidden object\/casual adventure games. If you\u2019re into those I can gladly recommend this.
