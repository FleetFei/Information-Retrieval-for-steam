class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off, a big thanks to the devs on bringing us this Early Access version of Perfect Golf. I'm running the game in windowed mode 1280x768 on a 5 year old laptop with AA on and Bloom on. Trees are set at 350, shadows 200, Grass 100 and camera view distance 1500. All things considered the game runs good apart from a few stutters here and there that I can live with and haven't yet affected the playability.<br><br>Ball Physics: the team has done a wonderful job on the ball physics, especially after the latest update. I considered the ball physics in Links v1.07 to be one of the best I've seen in a golf game but in PG they've taken it to another level of realism. Sure, there is still some work to be done but having played a few different golf games this is the best I've experienced as yet .......<br><br>BUT .... while I feel that tee to green the devs have created up to now the best simulation of real golf yet seen in a computer game, I do feel an element of realism is then lost with the putting model. To help explain this I should point out I am a novice real life golfer, having only played the game for around 18 months. So it is fresh in my mind what it is like to be a beginner and having to learn the game. I feel a simulation should have the same sort of difficulties and learning curve with all aids off as the real thing. That may also mean I'll never be able to shoot under par for a round as in real life. A par on a hole should feel like an achievement. Unfortunately the putting model destroys this sense of achievement because it feels like I have too much help. Sometimes I misjudge my long putts on a real course massively - and that never seems to happen to me in PG due to the putting meter. And sometimes in real life I've putted from the fairway just off the green and given too much power and ended up off the other side of the green. I can't seem to do that in PG as my power is limited by the meter.<br><br>My suggestion - a straight swing bar that follows the movement of the mouse. The putter head placed two thirds down the bar with movement then for back swing and power and length of follow through for movement. Putting aids can be markings on the bar to help judge distance but true simulation mode with no aids would be with no markings and just from feel as in real golf. Don't know if something like this is possible. It would mean that by selecting a putter the graphic would automatically switch from an arcing swing meter to a straight putting bar. In simulation mode full back swing (of course restricted to a point) and full, fast follow through would always result in a maximum power putt.<br><br>A free roam mode would be nice, even if it was only restricted to a 20 or 30 yard zone around the golfers position, to allow for better planning of shots like in real golf when views are restricted. Of course, these restricted views should also be set to golfers eye level for realism.<br><br>Look forward to future update improvements and hopefully more courses. I wouldn't play a real golf course without learning how to hit a ball..!<br> <br>The graphics on my 4yr old system run fine. I'd love to improve them, but to ensure the game runs with a good frame rate, I opted to keep the graphics down. This isn't like older or other golf games, it feels more Sim than Arcade. Granted it has a few elements that are needed to transfer anything simulated to a computer game, but they are minimal and necessary.<br> <br>Real Time Weather is pretty cool, and works a treat. With the promise of many more to come, it will soon become a tough decision choosing what course to entangle yourself within.
