SUPERHOT<br><b>Status:<\/b>  Released<br><b>Developer:<\/b>  SUPERHOT Team<br><b>Publisher:<\/b>  SUPERHOT Team<br><b>Genre:<\/b>  3D First Person Shooter<br> <b>Release Date:<\/b>  25th of February, 2016<br> <b>Type:<\/b>  Singleplayer <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">General Impression<\/div>Better dead than Red! And since it\u2019s either kill or be killed, you will be the untimely demise of Reds aplenty. It is really surprising how a seemingly simple idea can turn into a success story. Even more so, considering the rookie dev team (with a rather uninspired name, to boot).  Still, the game represents a brilliant example of quality independent gaming and thinking outside the box at the same time.<br><br>In the absence of a cohesive plotline, players have to contend with a seemingly randomized succession of stages which always increase the stakes in terms of survivability. To better understand this game, you must first summarize it at the most basic level. What are you representing in this particular title? You are not only a silent, nameless and genderless protagonist but you appear to be the remedy to the software\/digital illusion game world which I might compare to The Matrix. You control a black figure, statue, firewall personification (call it what you will) that must battle and defeat countless scores of \u201cRed Dudes\u201d as the game itself refers to your foes. I consider them the inherent problem that needs to be solved, the viruses which plague a literal immaculate environment (all the backgrounds are white). Maybe I\u2019m going too far in the pursuit of finding reason where there is none to be found. But I thoroughly enjoy chromatic aberrations and contrasts as they break the mold of conventional graphical representation. SUPERHOT contains just 4 colors (also their shades, if you want to go into detail): White, Red, Black and the occasional Yellow.<br><br>If you thought the gameplay itself is unconventional, you\u2019ll be pleased to find out that even the main menu draws attention to itself. You have a faux jailbreak\/communication interface which simulates the discussions between two hackers dealing with warez. Funny dialogues about barely being able to crack the game and having to deal with password protected files. It\u2019s all very meta and it might even add a dash of black humor for those which actually play a pirated copy of the game. I honestly hope they don't. Spectacular titles such as SUPERHOT don\u2019t deserve to be stolen by any gamer. Save up cash, wait for a Steam Sale, do what you want but stop pirating software. It\u2019s 2017 and digital distribution platforms have made gaming more affordable than ever.<br><br>All this talk beating around the bush and you\u2019d think I\u2019m gonna pass the chance to explain to you, exactly what makes SUPERHOT so special and unique? It\u2019s time manipulation, folks. The aforementioned Matrix franchise and also the Max Payne video game series have popularized this visual effect in contemporary times. As the game so eloquently puts it in its tutorial level, \u201cTime Moves Only When You Move\u201d. From this relatively straightforward gameplay mechanic, the entire action and entertenment value of SUPERHOT tip the odds in its favor. Shooting and dodging bullets in real time, would have already represented 99.9% of shooters out there. Gun and melee fights gain a new tactical layer as you can plan your next move under the safety of a slow motion effect which let\u2019s you see the enemy projectiles racing towards you and have the chance of avoiding them while taking precise aim at your assailants.<br><br>A large enough arsenal which contains both firearms, swords, baseball bats and even regular objects which range from bottles to TV sets, can be used to cause deadly harm upon the Red Dudes. It doesn\u2019t even matter where you shoot them, for example. They break apart (more specifically shatter as if made from glass) even if they get shot in the limbs. Headhshots while spectacular, shall earn you the Achievement \u201cPointless but cool\u201d. Many more challenges await to materialize in the form of creative kills while using the stage objects and backgrounds to your advantage. In the end, Black Dude (or Dudette) shall always win against the Reds since it is controlled by something unrivalled yet by artificial intelligence in terms of quick reflexes: the human mind. <br><br>The lack of a multiplayer component to SUPERHOT seems like a missed opportunity, even if I\u2019m never one to care for the inclusion of MP. I reckon many amongst my readers would have enjoyed sparring against each other, in this title. All my metaphors aside, SUPERHOT is simply fun to play whether you have a lot of spare time on hand or if you desire a  short gaming session to unwind from daily stress. Aforementioned Achievements and Cards ensure that absolutely all types of Steam collectors shall be satisfied. But honestly I would have recommended this game with my perfect rating, regardless by the presence of any Steam auxiliaries. SUPERHOT is a fine chapter within independent gaming\u2019s proud book of accomplishments!<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Strong Points<\/div>+ Excellent visual style.<br>+ Entertaining gameplay with a
