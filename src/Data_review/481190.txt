mainstream world of gaming we all find ourselves living in today, a world where anything can be simulated, from table tennis to truck driving, space invaders to space stations, everything is fair game.<br><br>Which is great for us, we're living in a golden age of virtual entertainment, since Pongs release in the 70's the games industry has gone from strength to strength, but can we say the same about our extraterrestrial endeavours?<br><br><br><b>Stairway To Heaven<\/b><br><br>Remember that space station we spoke about? Salyut 1?<br>That was launched in the same year as Pongs release, but it wasn't quite so long lived, orbiting the Earth for just 6 months before it fell like a led zeppelin in to the upper atmosphere.<br><br>The death of Salyut 1 was followed by several other short lived stations before the construction of the more enduring Skylab and Mir platforms in the 70's and 80's (both of which have subsequently died a fiery death).<br>Of course we now have the International Space Station, with 4 giant solar arrays providing power to over a dozen pressurised modules, powerful batteries and sophisticated telemetry.<br><br>The ISS is a modern wonder and one of mankinds greatest contemporary achievements.<br><br><br><b>The Long and Winding Road<\/b><br><br>So we have the disco decade to thank not only for a permanent manned presence in space but also for the greatest hobby in the world, gaming.<br>That's a significant legacy and one which has culminated in the development of a game called Stable Orbit, a space station simulation title by Codalyn which gives you the chance to indulge in your gaming hobby and celebrate our space based ingenuity at the same time.<br>The first thing you should know is that at the time of writing Stable Orbit is in Early Access (and paradoxically not that stable) so you can expect a lot of missing features, a few bugs and the occasional crash, you can also expect some nausea but I'll get on to that later.<br><br><br><b>Go Your Own Way<\/b><br><br>So Stable orbit gives you the opportunity to construct your very own space station.<br>As it stands at the moment, you start the game with a core module already orbiting the earth.<br>This provides a limited amount of power, cooling and communication capabilities.<br>There's also just enough life support to accommodate one brave astronaut.<br><br>From these humble beginnings you're tasked with constructing a station to surpass the ISS itself.<br>You're going to need a source of income to fund your construction ambitions, building a lab module is a good place to start as at the moment this is the only way to generate money (although this will likely change as development progresses).<br><br>You're going to need some extra power to keep things ticking over so putting up a solar array might be a good idea, maybe you could build \u00a0living quarters too, it'd be good for your solo astronaut to have a friend up there.<br>Of course you'll also need to construct a shuttle dock so your intrepid new astronaut can board the station, the dock is also important as it allows your station to receive vital supplies from Earth.<br><br>Your astronauts have developed an addiction to luxuries like food and water so it's a good idea to fit a &quot;space door&quot; to accept the online grocery orders, after all a dead astronaut isn't great for productivity.<br><br><br><b>Seasons in the Sun<\/b><br><br>The next problem you've got is that whilst the solar panels are providing electricity to keep the lights on, they only work in sunlight, which isn't much use when you're on the dark side of the Earth and actually need to &quot;turn the lights on&quot; so it might be wise to attach some batteries to help keep things powered when you're orbiting through the shadows.<br><br>So now you've got a lab making you money and a living quarters for well... living in, everything is powered and your astronauts are fed, watered and oxygenated but it's still far from an orbital paradise.<br><br>Your technological tin can will get pretty hot with all that machinery running and unfiltered sunlight blazing on to its surface, you're going to need to build a radiator or two to regulate the temperature before you cook your cosmonauts.<br><br><br><b>Wuthering Heights<\/b><br><br>A few button clicks and a short while later you'll have a Station happily circling the Earth without the need for further input from yourself.<br><br>You can click on a module and order some repairs if anything has gotten damaged, you could expand further, build another lab, a larger solar array, a bigger living quarters etc.<br>Bigger is always better and you can definitely build an impressively large station by repeating basic module construction over and over but therein lies the problem of playing through an early access title like this.<br><br>The current lack of content means that things are going to get stale pretty quickly and once you've built a station or two there isn't much left to do apart from admire your handy work in all its glinting metallic glory.<br><br>Watching your station orbit around the earth is fun for a while and a game of &quot;find your house from orbit&quot; will entertain for a few minutes more but you'll soon get sick of that and as I mentioned early, you might also get sick in the physical sense too.\u00a0<br><br><br><b>Killing Me Softly<\/b><br><br>Whilst this &quot;space sickness&quot; might add a little something to the immersion it's an additional level or realism I could definitely do without.<br>The nausea is caused by the rapidly rotating Earth constantly spinning like a crazed merry-go-round beneath your shiny new space station.<br><br>The game currently has 3 speed settings, Too Fast, Way Too Fast and Vomit Now.<br><br>From a design point of view there's no denying that you need the ability to fast forward time speeding up the construction of modules etc, but you should also be able to slow things down and catch your breath.<br><br>Taking a few minutes to enjoy looking at your creation is part of the fun with this sort of game but with your station hurtling around the world at what feels like light speed, taking in the view can be an eye watering experience.<br>Stable Orbit desperately needs a &quot;real time&quot; setting so we can enjoy the view without the need for a sick bag.<br><br><br><b>Another Brick In The Wall<\/b><br><br>There's a game I haven't referred to so far and I'm
