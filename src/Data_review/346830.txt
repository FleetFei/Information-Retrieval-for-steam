It's not great, but it's not terrible either. I ultimately decided to give it a positive rating because I want players to focus on the good (which it has loads of) and not the bad (just a few things). Ultimately, it's Steam. If a game has questionable ratings\/reviews that make you hold off on taking the plunge and purchasing the game, just wait for a sale. They happen pretty often. With that said, let's move on!<br><br>Final Fantasy IV: The After Years is a continuation\/sequel to Final Fantasy IV that was originally released as a downloadable title for the Nintendo Wii. Since then it's had a few other releases on various platforms. Taking place 17 years after the events of Final Fantasy IV, it's a great sequel, although it has some major pacing issues. The majority of the game feels like a huge slog, and it really takes a lot of patience to get through. However, once you make it to the last couple of chapters (called Tales), the story payout is huge and the game really starts to shine.<br><br><b>Gameplay<\/b><br>==========<br>This game is much the same as FFIV, however it is episodic. Not episodic like TellTale Games titles, but more like it plays out in chapters. Each chapter (Tale) centers on a different character and the other characters they join up with, and in the very last Tale they all unite into one huge group that you are able to choose from to tackle the last sections of the game. The biggest draw of this game is the continuation of the story from IV and how each of the original characters has changed and moved on. Some of them have had children, and other characters that crop up are completely new. <br><br>The After Years utilizes the same battle system as IV, and it works wonderfully. Don't fix what isn't broke, right? Aside from the pacing issue I mentioned, my only other problem with The After Years is that it's missing a sizeable amount of content from previous versions. Final Fantasy IV Complete for the PSP (includes FF4, FF4 Interlude, and FF4: The After Years) had Challenge Dungeons at the end of EVERY Tale, and over a dozen extra cameo bosses in the final dungeon of the last Tale. While the Challenge Dungeons weren't too too much, and just served as a more efficient way to level your characters in preparation for the last Tale, the extra cameo bosses are a huge loss. The fights featured bosses from other entries in the series, such as the 4 fiends from FF1 (Lich, Tiamat, Marilith, and Kraken), and these fights were a lot of fun. They could probably throw up a patch to add these if they really wanted to, or even add them as DLC, but it's most likely not going to happen. <br><br>Another complaint is that most of the game is going through the same areas found in FFIV multiple times. And while this is true, they are all tied into the story to an extent. Each time you run through them with a different set of characters you'll run into different events (as well as refilled and different treasures!!).<br><br>A new feature The After Years utilizes is the Moon Phase System. The Moon has 4 distinct phases, and each phase affects certain abilities directly by changing their attack power or effectiveness...which can drastically alter the difficulty of battles. Some rare monsters also only appear during certain Moon phases.<br><br><b>Graphics<\/b><br>==========<br>Featuring the same 3D graphics as FFIV (they both use the same engine), The After Years looks just as good as its predecessor. This is an upgrade from the 2D graphics the PSP version featured. The new characters that were added look just as good as the original characters as well. <br><br><b>Sound and Music<\/b><br>==========<br>Frequent readers of mine have probably figured out that I love video game music, and that I like pointing out particularly good tracks in a game's soundtrack. Most of the game reuses tracks from FFIV, but it adds a few new tracks that were composed by Junya Nakano. While different from Uematsu, Nakano has managed to pump out a few memorable tracks that really stand out and fit the game very well. The track above, in particular, manages to capture the wrongness and desperation of the situations it plays in PERFECTLY, and I cannot praise him enough for that. <br><br><b>Performance<\/b><br>==========<br>The After Years runs on the same engine as FFIV, and as such runs more or less the same. FPS isn't a huge issue for an older classic game like this, and I did not encounter any game-breaking bugs or glitches. I played with a controller the whole time and found it very comfortable. Not much to say here.<br><br><b>Final Thoughts<\/b><br>==========<br>One of the extremely rare sequels in the franchise, Final Fantasy IV: The After Years delivers a great continuation to a beloved story, although it does suffer from a couple of glaring problems with the gameplay itself. Despite this, fans will undoubtedly find something here to enjoy even if it takes some work and a lot of patience to reach. If you have never played The After Years before but have played FFIV and would like to see what happened to the beloved characters, this would be a great version to try out.
