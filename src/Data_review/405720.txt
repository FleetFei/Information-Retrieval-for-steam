It may in fact prove to be one of the best packages of mini games that I\u2019ve had the privilege of playing. The highlight of the game for me are the hand-drawn graphics. These are simply delightful and are very pleasing to the eye and demonstrates that black and white is still a very much viable color palette in 2016.<br><br>There are three primary game modes followed by a fourth. The first of these modes is titled \u2018Perfect Moon.\u2019 In this mode you need to run around objects and avoid obstacles while collecting all of the different gems on each level. This is probably my favorite mode of the game. Giving you the ability to glide as well as leap provides you with a significant range of strategies that you can utilize. Sometimes the strategy that you feel will be best ends in spectacular failure. The tutorial levels \u2013 and this holds true for each of the three primary modes \u2013 begins easily enough but by the third or fourth level the difficulty really begins to ramp up. By the time that the difficulty reaches super crazy levels I was having so much fun that rather than getting aggravated by my miscues I was quite happy to try and try again. Unlike some other games that make you wallow in your defeat starting a new round is exceedingly quick. The load times are minimal all throughout the game which was another advantage.<br><br>The second mode, \u2018Moon Life,\u2019 is one that I have seen before. It\u2019s essentially a copy of the mechanics from QWOP only Sykes has taken the mechanics and taken the gameplay to the next level. You\u2019re essentially tasked to do what you did in Perfect Moon \u2013 collect the gems on every level in the fastest possible time while using these mechanics. I\u2019ve never been very good at QWOP and I wasn\u2019t very good at Moon Life. However, don\u2019t allow my inability to succeed at this mini game deter you. I can definitely seeing those who enjoyed QWOP falling in love with this mode.<br><br>In the third mode, \u2018Star Light\u2019 you control a rocket and again you\u2019re tasked with completing each level in the quickest possible time. The rocket powers along on a well designed physics engine so you\u2019ll have to bare in mind speed, thrust and gravity in order to succeed at being fast. At times this mode felt a little like breakout because of how zoomy the rocket actually is. But that\u2019s not a negative in my book as the mode gave me the most laughs.<br><br>Sports Day features six multiplayer sports games based upon the core gameplay mechanics featured within the single player modes. Each of them can be played as a single player \u2013 there has been AI built into the game for all but the golf mode which can be played alone \u2013 but I am certain that the most fun in this mode will be had with local co-op couch party play. My favorite of the modes here was the mini golf. This is probably because I found that the AI in the other ones was almost flawless and without dedicating significant time into improving my game I just wasn\u2019t skilled enough to challenge the tough AI.<br><br>In all, this is a wonderful collection of mini games. Other developers would have, and have in the past, dedicated entire games that feature less levels to just one of the modes featured in Perfect Universe and have not been criticized at all for it. That Sykes has developed them all himself as well as the graphics and decided to combine them into the one package for $9.99 is an absolute bargain that will present to you several hours of entertainment. An easy recommendation.<br><br>Steamified score: 8\/10 \u2013 beautiful hand-drawn graphics, fantastic value for money and a purely relaxing experience.
