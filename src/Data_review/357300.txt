I had heard it would be difficult, but I, even though I hadn't played many puzzle games, had played many logic puzzles for a few years and considered myself &quot;good&quot; at puzzles, and so thought this game wouldn't present much of a challenge.<br><br>How wrong I was.<br><br>Level 0? Easy. It teaches you how to move and what the goal of the game is. Eat all the fruits, exit the level via the rainbow spinny portal.<br><br>Level 1? Easy. It does introduce the undo button because almost everyone goes the obvious route (which is quite intended), and that gets them stuck. But the level is still innocent and easy; the developer laughs with you. &quot;Don't worry man, it's just a small joke, you still got this.&quot;<br><br>Level 2? This is your first taste of hell. Many people who played this game on iOS rated the game 1 star and accused the developers of not playtesting their game well enough because they thought this level was unsolveable. But it *is* solvable, if only you take the time to reason through things logically, and make sure you don't repeat your mistakes. It's definitely brute-force-able if it comes down to it.<br><br>Many of my friends whom I've since introduced Snakebird to have gotten stuck on that level for quite a while. And I'll admit, I too got stuck on that level for a few minutes. You look at the puzzle and you think it's easy. Then you try to solve it and think it's impossible. Then you realize what you've been missing all along (it's not even a new game mechanic; it's just a possibility that you've for some reason excluded from consideration), and you feel humbled and stupid.<br><br>Despite its colourful aesthetic, this had been described as &quot;The Dark Souls of Puzzle Games&quot; before Stephen's Sausage Roll came out, and it soon became clear why at around level 6. I was in fact playing this game on stream. It took me a whole 15 minutes to figure out how to complete that level, even with a few subtle hints from a watcher. Sebastian, the employee at Noumenon Games who had designed that level, was actually watching me, and when I completed the level, I said &quot;Right, you need to be given a raise, and fired simultaneously&quot;. Because that describes the sheer amount of awe, frustration, and admiration I have for the level's design. &quot;Don't worry, it's not a small joke any more. Welcome to the cute fluffy hell of ripping your hair out at colourful snakebirds.&quot;<br><br>And it only gets harder from there. Later levels will require you to control two or more snakebirds, all of which must get to the exit; boxes that can be pushed around by your snakebirds, and portals that teleport your snakebirds. Often the snakebirds don't work the way you might naively think they do, but then you think about it a bit more and realize that that's how they've always worked. <br><br>Even with all these mechanics, though, each level has a very narrow solution path (or on occasion, two different solution paths). Remember that undo button? Yeah, that has a reason. Without it, this game would be nigh-unplayable. The final level took me over a full hour to work out how to do, even with undos.<br><br>Yet at each minute I still felt I was making progress.<br><br>Because, like Elya Yelnats, who got stronger as the pig he was carrying got fatter, so did I get better at puzzles as the levels got harder. I was repeating my previous mistakes far less. I was realizing what I needed to do far faster. I was becoming one with the snakebirds, and one with the puzzle.<br><br>I came out of this trial by fruit far tougher than I ever was. This game honed my puzzle skills to a far stronger level. It showed me my place in the puzzle hierarchy by stomping me into the dirt, then it forced me to claw my way to the top with tooth and nail and pure brainpower. Without this, I would never have been able to complete any of Stephen's Sausage Roll.<br><br>If you're interested in becoming great at puzzles and you're at an intermediate or even strong beginner level, play this game. If you're an &quot;expert&quot; and you still haven't played this, you should definitely definitely play it. If you're below &quot;strong beginner&quot;, I recommend that you stick to the easier games, like A Good Snowman Is Hard To Build, or Another Perspective.<br><br>Thank you, Snakebird. Thank you, Noumenon Games.
