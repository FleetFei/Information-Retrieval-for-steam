developed by Dharker Studio and also published by Dharker Studio. Welcome to Edo, an alternate realm where history took a slightly different route. The Samurai of Japan were never outlawed and the emperor lost power preventing Japan from opening its borders to the other nations of the world. Edo is now a very different place from the modern Japan that we know where tradition still holds sway over the people and it's leaders. As time has passed the Samurai have been given ever increasing levels of power by the Council of Nobles and their leader the Lord Regent. This has given rise to the creation of a special core Samurai unit known as the Justicars. Though members are appointed by the council, they remain an autonomous unit able to investigate and judge any threat or criminal they find. It is their duty to safeguard the continuity and protect the people. But Edo is not a land of peace and plenty, strife exists in all corners of the once great empire. Rival factions constantly battle for land and power whilst rebellion grows from within. Enter Asumi, a female samurai of the Black Clan, trained from birth to kill on command. Now newly appointed as a Justicar of Edo, our protagonist finds herself in a place she never thought she would be...outside of the shadows and within an academy trying to root out a rebel before civil war can engulf Edo and its people. <br><br>As Asumi, it's up to you to blend into the Samurai academy as a first year student and find out who the terrorist code-named &quot;Raven&quot; is before they can launch an attack that devastates the country. In order to accomplish this task it is vital that you mingle with your fellow students and faculty as well as apply a little cloak and dagger to obtain as much information from them as possible to hopefully discover your targets identity and complete your mission. As the story unfolds, you'll have the opportunity to participate in various social functions, sparring matches, tests and some rather dubious situations during your time in the school. This visual novel is wonderfully written and includes a huge amount of in-game choices that truly affect the story which is a quality that I really adore in these types of titles. <br><br>Graphically, this novel is beautifully illustrated and Dharker Studios presention is top notch as well. It, of course, has a manga-inspired visual style which is always pleasing to the eye especially in visual novel format. And despite the terrible event that you are trying to thwart, the games colors remain bright and cheerful as the engrossing narrative unfolds. The soundtrack is also quite appealing delivering upbeat tempos, dramatic selections and heart-rending scores as appropriately needed throughout the story. Call me bias because I love videogame music but I find myself listening to this O.S.T. even when I'm not reading the novel because it really is fantastic. On another note, some fans seem to prefer spoken dialogue in their visual novels but I prefer the unspoken lines best and The Sword Of Asumi delivers on that. This title is also packed full of extra content which includes a codex on the lore of Edo, an unlockable C.G. gallery, an ample amount of save slots, Steam trading cards, Steam achievements and last but not least the wonderful soundtrack that I mentioned previously in this review.<br><br>Visual novel fans rejoice because this title would make an excellent addition to your library. The story is compelling, the characters are interesting, you have a tremendous amount of choices to make that actually tailor the story and there are multiple endings depending on the decisions that you make thereby giving this title some replayability.
