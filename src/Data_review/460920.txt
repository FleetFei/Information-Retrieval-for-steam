mountain of fun <\/div><br>Steep is an extreme winter sports simulator developed by everyone\u2019s favourite company, Ubisoft. (Don\u2019t let that put you off). It predominantly features the Alps but Ubisoft recently introduced some Alaskan slopes too as part of a free update. In Steep you take to the slopes with a snowboard or skis or you take to the skies with a wingsuit or a paraglider. You can complete challenges and events already in the game or you can simply explore the mountain, finding new points of interest, challenges and the best lines you can come up with.<br><br>When you first get to the Alps, you\u2019ll be struck by the game\u2019s beauty (even if you\u2019re running it on Medium\/Low like I am). The dev team did a great job at creating the Alps in-game and you\u2019ll get some truly stunning vistas fairly regularly in the game. Hurtling through a lush forest or gliding your way to a different peak, it\u2019s going to look good. There are some muddier textures which look a bit rough when you get up close (particularly rock textures) but I didn\u2019t see those too often. <br><br>The \u2018main way\u2019 to play the game is through the challenges and events. Of course, you don\u2019t have to these but they are the best way to unlock Steep credits (used to purchase customization options) and earn EXP (some events have level requirements). There are a few different kinds of events. You have Freestyle events (snowboard or skis) in which you have to score a certain amount of points, you also have Extreme Rider events where you score points by narrowly dodging objects. Bonebreaker challenges can be wingsuit or ground events, they feature doing ridiculous things that inevitably lead to you knocking the ever-living \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665 out of your rider several times before success (they\u2019re a lot of fun). You also get race events for the wingsuit and paragliding disciplines. There are also some story-ish \u2018challenges\u2019 called Mountain Stories which attempt to tell you a story while you complete objectives such as destroying snowmen or following another rider. Personally, I didn\u2019t see the point in trying to tell a story with the game but the challenges themselves are pretty fun. <br><br>So, as I mentioned, some challenges require scoring a certain number of points to be successful. Unfortunately, the game\u2019s trick controls suck and there are no tutorials to help you. The only tutorials are for how you can ollie and then flip or spin, which is good to know but will net you hardly any points. For snowboarding or skiing it\u2019s all about the grabs you do and the game doesn\u2019t teach you how to do anything other than the most basic grab. I had to look up a separate tutorial to figure out how to do and start getting decent scores on jumps. Getting combos is also very important for racking up high scores, the only way to end a combo is to fall over which happens if you take a few hits (bad landings or riding into an object) or if you just go head first into the ground, that\u2019ll mess you up pretty good surprisingly enough.<br><br>Now, as much as I enjoy Steep and I think Ubisoft Annecy did a good job with it, there is one part of this game which is simply unforgiveable. It\u2019s always online (and Ubi servers can\u2019t be connected to if you have University WiFi without a VPN connection). It\u2019s one of the most ridiculous design decisions I\u2019ve ever seen in a game. The multiplayer aspect of Steep isn\u2019t even slightly interesting, you can see other riders around the mountain (since you\u2019re always online) and you can group together with them but that just means that the game lets you know what you\u2019re doing and lets you compete with their scores on challenges you\u2019re both doing. Sure, it\u2019s an okay implementation of multiplayer and it can be kind of cool but it most certainly isn\u2019t worth having the game always online. I really hope they add offline functionality before they inevitably shut down the servers. As much as I like the game and want to like Ubisoft for making it, you can\u2019t argue anything other than this is a super \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665 move on their behalf.<br><br>As I mentioned earlier, there are a few different rewards for completing challenges. Levelling up your rider is done by earing EXP from challenges which then unlocks new event challenges (like a Red Bull Extreme Rider event) and new Mountain Story events. Some challenges reward you with items such as new helmets, boards, wingsuits etc. that can only be unlocked by completing said challenges. Lastly, you earn Steep credits (the in-game currency) which can be used to buy more items from the in-game store such as ridiculous unicorn costumes, an imp wingsuit as well Helicopter tickets. Helicopter tickets are used to drop you anywhere on the mountain, very handy if you don\u2019t fancy a trek up a steep incline to find a new dropzone. The customization options can be pretty fun to mess around with but they\u2019re definitely not the main focus of the game and it\u2019s really easy to earn Steep credits so there\u2019s no need to pay for microtransactions.<br><br>One of Steep\u2019s strongest points is how quickly the game changes pace. If you want to find your own line from a dropzone it can be an incredibly relaxing experience that\u2019ll take your mind off of the real world for a while. It can also be a controller smashingly infuriating game when you\u2019re playing the hardest challenges, failing time and time again before you finally get the sweet satisfaction of beating the challenge. <br><br>Last but not least, this is not a $60 game. I paid $30 for the base game which I\u2019m happy with but I think I\u2019d probably feel different about the game if I had dropped $60 on it. There\u2019s a lot of content here, I still have plenty more to do, and you can create your own content too (finding your own lines etc.) if that\u2019s your kind of thing but this is one to pick up on a sale of at least 50% off.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Verdict <\/div><br>Steep is a niche game and it doesn\u2019t come without issues but it\u2019s an incredibly enjoyable game that takes you from exhilarating wingsuit challenges, dipping and diving down cliff-sides, to relaxing snowboard rides through a snow-covered forest.
