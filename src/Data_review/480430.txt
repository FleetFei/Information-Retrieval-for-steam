Funcom's MMORPG is basically my first. Alright, I did play a bunch of them even before realizing TSW exists, but I didn't enjoy any of them on the same scale I've enjoyed TSW. The only reason why I am not currently playing The Secret World are daily life obligations.<br><br>But enough about TSW.<br><br>Apart from The Park and The Black Watchmen, Hide and Shriek is third TSW spin-off. It's a devious, multiplayer take on hide'n'seek in The Secret World. Hide and Shriek is two player multiplayer game where players need to score as many points as possible by collecting colorful orbs or end a game instantly by scaring their opponent three times in a row.<br><br>But it ain't gonna be easy - simply because both players are invisible.<br><br>And here's simple, yet powerful concept worthy of slow and thunderous applause. Why didn't anyone think of that earlier? So how do you know your opponent is somewhere near? The only way you will be able to recognize your opponent's presence is witnessing changes and interactions. If you see the doors opening or colorful orbs levitating, you know your enemy is there. Remember that awesome scene with ghosts and babies from &quot;The Frighteners&quot;? That's what I'm talking about. <br><br>But things get more complicated then that. First of all, camping is basically a no-no, because it would be pretty easy to stalk near someone's altar (place to drop your orbs and earn points) and scare them while they're approaching. Altars vanish when you place your orb on them or after some time to stop people from camping. Players can also collect various runes. These runes can be used to place traps on objects or floor, summon haunting demons or curses. Even better, you can easily combine various runes and create more powerful spells that will reveal your opponent or make you hunt him down with precision. But, you must be asking... does that mean players can aim to create the most powerful spell in the book? Well yes... if the random rune picker at the beginning of the match allows you to. <br><br>It's easy to scare your friends - once Shriek-o-Meter (I've made this up, please continue using it so one day I end up in game credits) has been filled, you will be able to scare the other player by pressing Spacebar when you're near and facing him\/her. If you miss, your Shriek-o-Meter will drain and you will be visible for a few seconds, giving a chance to your enemy to scare you. If you get scared, a jumpscare will stop your heart for a few moments. It consists of loud sound and 90's Halloween influenced 'deadhead'. Of course, the effect is stronger when you're playing the game with headphones and not expecting to be scared, e.g. in situation when your opponent sees you or perform an ambush in unexpected situations.<br><br>Matches last for ten minutes and tend to be pretty tense. Hide and Shriek forces players to move and eventually make mistakes that other players will notice. It is not scary game nor it tends to be - it is supposed to be funny and devilish take on hide and seek and it works so damn well with its Goosebumps aesthetics. <br><br>Hide and Shriek is definitely a spin-off game and a damn good one, but I don't think Funcom is really aware of what beast they have created. With its progression and unlockables, it will keep the players busy for some time, but Funcom should really consider making it something more than digital sideshow attraction. More levels, masks, spells, multiplayer options and game modes could definitely launch Hide and Shriek to indie stardom. Let's keep our fingers crossed and hope Funcom will realize it themselves.<br><br>***<br><br>Hey, you've liked this review? Hated it? It made you buy the game \/ abandon the idea of buying this game? I have been able to absorb the full Hogwarts experience, with spells, runes, orbs and <b>jumpscares<\/b>. In fact, I\u2019ll make sure I bold every <b>jumpscare<\/b> in this article, just because the game is essentially one big <b>jumpscare<\/b>. In the game the frightening pop-ups are referred to as \u2018shrieks\u2019, but I would rather call them what they are: <b>jumpscares<\/b>.<br><br>I first entered this game without pressing the big and obvious \u2018How to Play\u2019 button on the main menu, which I did regret as soon as the match started. My friend and I started a private game because a duck would have more luck beating the pros than us.<br><br>Our first session consisted of running around, asking \u2018Where the flaming hell are you?\u2019 to hear the reply, \u2018You\u2019ll have to find me!\u2019 My opponent\/almost former friend kept breaking into sudden laughter which freaked me out as I hadn\u2019t a clue what would unfold throughout the match, but it turns out he just set down a mild scare trap. Ten seconds later he screamed \u2018Oh for God\u2019s sake, I ran into the damned thing!\u2019<br><br>It was slow and we didn\u2019t manage to <b>jumpscare<\/b> each other at all because we didn\u2019t know what we were doing, but as soon as we read the rules and tips, (it\u2019s a man thing), that all changed dramatically. In the next match, we both knew the basic fundamentals of play, and it got very intense. <i>Very<\/i> intense.<br><br>Anyhow, let\u2019s delve into it.<br><br>As stated above, the game is all about <b>jumpscaring<\/b> the other person. If you manage to <b>jumpscare<\/b> them three times in a row without getting <b>jumpscared<\/b> yourself, you win. You also have a designated colour throughout the match, and you get points for doing numerous different things, acquiring most of them by performing \u2018ancient rituals\u2019 (placing your colour\u2019s orbs on your colour\u2019s altar). If you have the most points at the end of the ten-minute match, you also win.<br><br>Placing your orbs on your altar isn\u2019t the only way to get points, you also get them for <b>jumpscaring<\/b> the other person and having them run into your traps. You can also lose points by being in the \u2018void\u2019 for extensive periods of time, and you can send the other person to the void with the use of a few spells like the \u2018black hole\u2019.<br><br>Spells can be obtained by collecting one to three runes, each combination of the runes (which have different colours, shapes and names) creating a different spell. If you wish to keep track of your discovered spells, you can go into the \u2018Grimoire\u2019 in the customisation menu and see all that you\u2019ve used before.<br><br>The runes and orbs can be found scattered around the five-room school map in cupboards, on benches, in bins, in lockers, on window sills, on the floor \u2013 pretty much everywhere.<br><br>\u2018Both of you are invisible. How will you know where the other person is?\u2019 Well, that is a good question that you probably haven\u2019t asked. You need to keep an eye on your spells which can guide you to the other person and your traps which will activate when the player is near. You can also see the other person when they\u2019re carrying an orb, sprinting, or when they\u2019ve failed a <b>jumpscare<\/b>.<br><br>So your goal, basically, is to figure out where your opponent is, whilst making sure they don\u2019t know where <i>you<\/i> are.<br><br>The matches in Hide and Shriek are very intense and unpredictable. Not knowing when your opponent will jump out, not knowing what trap lies behind a door and not knowing whether your opponent knows where you are at any given moment really keeps you on the edge of your seat.<br><br>Oh, and you can dress your <b>jumpscare<\/b> face up and choose what you want it to look like from a range of different faces and decorations that you earn through levelling up or finding in the matches.<br><br>For me, finding a match can be somewhat of a dilemma. I\u2019m in Australia though, so for you it might be a completely different story. Also, there is no communication with the other person throughout the match that I know of without adding them on Steam, which is rather odd. It\u2019s sort of like Dead by Daylight in that sense.<br><br>There isn\u2019t a big backstory behind the game except for a few \u2018The Secret World\u2019 references and the following paragraph from the games Steam store page: <i>\u2018It\u2019s Halloween and the students of magic at Little Springs High and Innsmouth Academy are about to honor an ancient tradition: sneaking into school after midnight, turning themselves invisible, and scaring the living hell out of each other!\u2019<\/i><br><br>I\u2019ve encountered a few unbalanced things, namely the \u2018Scry\u2019 spell, which allows you to see your opponent through walls. It\u2019s a \u2018tier 3\u2019 spell, which means it\u2019s harder to discover and takes three runes to make, but once you know it it\u2019s not that hard to get. Scry lets you see nearby orbs and your opponent, wherever they are on the map. This is extremely overpowered, it would be better if you could only see them in the same room.<br><br>I\u2019ve also discovered that the altar can spawn in the same room numerous times in a row which can be unbalanced. I know it\u2019s random but that would be an easy fix by ensuring that the altar changes rooms after use. I got the altar in the same room five times in a row, and with all of the orbs lying around I got an easy 50,000 points. The other player couldn\u2019t catch up.<br><br>That is all fine and dandy. A few things need balancing here and there, but besides that, it\u2019s a cool game. The one thing the game truly lacks is the \u2018horror\u2019 aspect, the creepiness, something that makes it eerie and worthy of the \u2018horror\u2019 tag. It comes up in \u2018horror\u2019 on Steam, and if something comes up in \u2018horror\u2019, I expect there to be \u2018horror\u2019, and lots of it! The <b>jumpscares<\/b> aren\u2019t enough to justify a horror, a true horror needs spooky atmosphere and a chilling backstory with lots of suspense \u2013 the only suspense here is the <b>jumpscares<\/b>. I don\u2019t think the tag should be there, because the game isn\u2019t a horror in my humble opinion.<br><br>Despite the lack of horror, I\u2019m enjoying unlocking new characters, owning and getting owned by some <b>jumpscare<\/b> master that I\u2019ve never met before, and playing with my mates on it, even though their screams are highly exaggerated.<br><br>Overall, I believe Hide and Shriek is something you should try if you are interested. It isn\u2019t meant to look as if it was a real place because it was built not to be.
