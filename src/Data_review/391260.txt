class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm a fan of RPG and the description of this game said it was heavily inspired by 3 similarly amazing games. So, decided to try this and yes, I've had moments where I enjoyed it (story mostly) and moments where I hated it (ugh).<br><br>To begin, the story starts off very mysteriously and nearing the very end, you find out what goes on. It does have a story, but it is not as story rich as one hoped. In fact, not much of a story going on until you encounter the new party members, whom joins you near the end of the game.  Yes, I don't understand why the game was developed in this manner, but oh well. The game mechanics are pretty much turn and agility based. Characters take turns to perform an action and whomever has the higher agility goes first. Soundtrack is nice and kept me entertained during gameplay (all ~10 hrs of it). Not a bad soundtrack overall. Graphics are as mostly decent for an RPG 2D game. Some monsters did appear very pixelated, which I'm not sure if that was intended or not. One big concept with graphics is that most cue items are differently designed to have their graphics be different than a 'normal' object. This and the golden circles for important info in the game are highlighted and should be commended. Otherwise, you will be stuck clicking on many items throughout the game (or just miss it all haha).<br><br>Past all that comes the downside of this game. When you begin the game, there is absolutely no sense of direction of where to go. No 'navigation' and you can honestly wander off to do something completely out of order. While what you can or cannot do is limited, you can easily frustrate yourself by doing something out of order and have to backtrack. I got lucky and I believe I went in a pretty much correct order, but I can see someone else playing and screwing up at the beginning, because they chose differently. Next, the concept of a looping map is very cool, but not enjoyable. In Alathar and the Desert of Illusions, the maps are gigantic and much larger than I honestly think they should be. You can easily wander the map for an hour or two and just starting to understand where anything is at before you can progress to the next part in the game. It was at this point I had to refer to a guide to even progress, because I spent quite awhile trying to locate what I needed to get to progress in the game. Again, lack of direction\/navigation from the game. This is especially true if you stop playing the game and come back to it, you probably won't recall where you left off or what to do next. Another mechanic of the game where I think needs improvement, is that once you have access to Artemis and you have free reign of the world, you really need to keep the airship closeby and remember to go back to it. IE: I went to the desert and went through it all to the other side. Learned the hard way that Artemis remains where you left it (facepalm). Lastly, the concept of money and items throughout the game. At the beginning of the game, I tried to pinch every piece of gold and didn't purchase the most 'powerful' items at the time. If you have played other RPGs, you may have developed a habit hoarding and pinching your resources habit. In this game, not so much of an issue. If you do run out of gold, go fight a few monsters and you will earn it back easily. If you aren't strong enough, wait a little bit and explore the section of where you are and look for the chests. They will most likely yield items for you to be stronger\/strong enough to pass through this section and a little bit in the next. So most items aren't necessary until the final section of the game. However, at that point, money is everywhere and you can easily amass it via grinding on some monsters (werewolves are nice, but there are those carnivorous trolls, which are definitely the best!). Some items just come too late in the game too. IE: Crown of Ely, flower that revives you with 50% HP, rings that boost elemental defense. If they came up earlier in the game, it would've been more enjoyable and it could've been a reason to make certain enemies stronger.  <br><br>Overall, still a fun RPG game to play. I can see someone rushing past the whole game in about half the time I played. I took my time and tried to not refer to the guide as much as possible for about the first half of the game. If you don't have a guide, I think you are looking at 15+ hours of trying to figure everything out. With the guide, straight to finish, probably 5-6hrs.
