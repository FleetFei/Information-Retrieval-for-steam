Surprise surprise, the devs were prepared again. <b> \u201cIf frequent updates and\/or a determinable release date is very important to you, we advise you to wait for the finished product!\u201d <\/b> Again, those complaints lose a little luster once it\u2019s clear that there was a warning. However, as of today on day of writing, March 27, 2017, the devs posted an update that ends the area that will be the largest until the game\u2019s very end, which means the update frequency should increase. (Sidenote: I don\u2019t blame reviewers for not knowing that. I only learned it existed twenty minutes ago. But, it is important to note.) In any case, the update frequency is pretty good for three people on this intensive of a game. With that out of the way, let\u2019s move to:<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"> The Gameplay <\/div>This is the star of the game. The swing arcs are clearly defined for each weapon, the animations are smooth, it feels good to hit enemies, with enemy recoil and appropriate sound effects, and a wide variety of character expansion options. In Grindea, there is no class system. When you level up, you receive points that you can put into skills, spells, and stat bonuses of your choice. Want to be a brutal tank who crushes their enemies? Do it. Want to be a deadly mage who roasts and electrocutes their opponents from afar? Do it. Want to be a glass cannon who can crit 25% of the time for 165% of their damage? Do it. That\u2019s where the replayability comes from: a wide variety of combat options and stat choices. I personally have all nine character slots filled with hard-hitting tanks and long-range mages and spellswords and summoners and so on. There are even a couple support spells, with more planned for implementation. You can also get a pet by taming some of the common enemies in the game. You can feed them, and they\u2019ll give you stat bonuses if you put enough into one stat.  Grindea is also very playable with a controller, and I recommend it. While keyboard is good, a controller hearkens back to SNES-era RPG\u2019s and feels great. This game also doesn\u2019t want you to grind. Yes, it\u2019s in the name. That\u2019s the joke. The more you try to farm in this game, the more enemies that appear. You can get overwhelmed if you aren\u2019t careful.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"> The Story <\/div>I honestly love this game\u2019s story. While the core plot is pretty simple, get some things so the world doesn\u2019t explode, it\u2019s really the characters that make Grindea feel alive. Every character\u2019s text box portraits are detailed and have multiple expressions. Their in-game sprites look good and have smooth animation. These animations give personality to the characters and distinguish them from \u201cblue-haired dude\u201d and \u201cis that Cloud?\u201d There\u2019s growth and blooming relationships between characters. Even with the protagonist, I was pleasantly surprised. Rather than being The Great Hero of the Ancient Prophecy, you\u2019re just a kid doing their job as a Collector, like a lot of other people. You fall into having to save the world rather than being born as the Savior of the Earth or Whatever. You end up \u201cborrowing\u201d some Really Old Super Dangerous and Powerful Artifacts not because a legend told you to, nor because you woke up and decided to, nor because your dead parent spoke to you in a dream or whatever. You just end up having to save the world. There\u2019s charm, wit, humor, and no end of pun names.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"> Singleplayer vs. Multiplayer <\/div>There are arguments for both sides of this. While singleplayer is viable and the same experience, you\u2019re just not as strong as with others. Some bosses are very tough without help. <span class=\"bb_spoiler\"><span> Looking at you, Seasons Hydra. <\/span><\/span> With friends that also enjoy this genre, Grindea is excellent. As long as everyone has a half-decent router and a computer that meets the minimum on the store page, you probably won\u2019t have issues. While large areas do take a while to load on less superb computers, it\u2019s nothing unbearable. Unless <i> someone\u2019s <\/i> ping is 30,000. So, if you\u2019re playing this alone, it\u2019s still fun, but you\u2019re going to have to work harder and longer than with others simply because more people make the game easier.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"> The Verdict <\/div>If you want a finished game, don\u2019t buy this. If you want a perfectly polished game, don\u2019t buy this. If you want a game that absolutely positively has no bugs, don\u2019t buy this. If what I and the store page have told you has you itching to buy this, buy it. It\u2019s a lot of fun and a good game. If you\u2019re on the fence, pick up the demo. It\u2019ll give you a better sense of gameplay than words ever could. I came into this Early Access game expecting just that. I was pleasantly surprised.  I recommend this game for stellar characters, graphics, animations, music, and mechanics. It\u2019s not finished, certainly. It takes time to make a game, certainly. There are issues, certainly. But what\u2019s there is good.
