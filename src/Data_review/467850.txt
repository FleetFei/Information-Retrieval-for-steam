looking at this, right? Like Legend of Dark Witch (another highly recommended Mega Man-like Run n' Gun for super cheap) this is a basically a gender swapped Mega Man. It's a retro-style run n' gun platformer pitting your mechanized heroine against a bunch of Android &quot;Gals&quot; vs. Mega Man's various dude Robot Masters (excluding MM9's Splash Woman).  Unlike Dark Witch, which adds in it's own ideas (like a Gradius style upgrade bar) this one sticks to the MM formula pretty much exclusively. But it does pretty well! <br><br>Visually it looks fantastic; the pixel art here and the color palette used is as good as, or better than, genuine SNES\/Genesis\/Amiga quality. The chibi character art really evoke cute, Japanese sidescrollers from the era. The quality of the chiptunes is great too. Controls are absolutely on point. They're super responsive and tight; it feels great with a 360 controller. The levels are very well designed, with areas being themed around various classic MM stage hazards like timed drop platforms, flame throwers and conveyor belt sections. Once you defeat the four main Gals and move onto the levels beyond, they even start making great use of your powers! See a wall you seemingly can't get passed? Fire the black hole-thing at an enemy on the other side to switch places and warp over there! See a gap that's too large for a dash jump? Jump anyway, then use Magma Gal's Flame Dash to cross to the other side! A ladder you can't reach? Shoot out your Mines, then jump on them to use the explosion to give you a boost! It gets REALLY GOOD once you have all these powers; the levels know that, and are designed thusly. <br><br>Meanwhile, enemies are positioned in ways and combinations that almost resemble an &quot;action puzzle&quot;; they'll shoot bouncing projectiles to get you to move to certain safe spots or you'll be able to perfectly take them out one-at-a-time from certain vantage points. It's just like the good old days! Speaking of, the difficulty is tuned to be &quot;NES Hard&quot;; really difficult, but never impossible and rewarding when conquered (and able to be more speedily conquered after you get it down). Sit there and watch patterns, try different weapon &amp; ability combinations, or die and learn from failures when you try again: you WILL make progress... most of the time. <br><br>There are *some* issues that affect the difficulty. The timing windows on some of this stuff is absolutely brutal (particularly the fullscreen flame throwers in the second half of Magma Gal's stage and the explosive, timed platforms on Buster Gal's stage), enemies respawn THE SECOND you leave their &quot;screen&quot; (there's definitely places you can stand still that will cause them to continuously fly in from the edge of the screen, one after the other. Kind of good for grinding, but awful for combat where you have to maneuver in the air while jumping).<br><br>EDIT: The developer is doing a good job currently of patching and otherwise updating this game constantly. Issues I had a week ago (I previously mentioned no way to change the controller config here) are no longer here! Meanwhile, the balance on things seem to be constantly tweaked for the better. I definitely have to give a shout-out to the devs for continually polishing this up! <br><br>In the end, I just have a tiny issue with everything. Namely, the music compositions are a little repetitive (they all seem to be variants of the same song to me). But this game is $4 so I know its petty to bring up. What's here sounds good, but it bares mentioning anyway. <br><br>That's it. Every other little issue I had is now gone :D   <br>--<br>Overall, it's definitely recommended if you're looking for a cheap Mega Man style run-n'-gun retro action game. This is an extremely well-crafted little game and definitely provides the fun and challenge you're looking for. This title could absolutely go for $10 and it would put some other games in that price range to shame. RetroRevolution has a quality game here for the price of a sandwich at Burger King.
