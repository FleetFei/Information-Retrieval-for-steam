My standards are not your standards.  All I ask is you do your research.  This game does not reveal itself in your first pass.  6000+ hours and I am still learning.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Background<\/div>Perpetuum is an indie, sci-fi, sandbox mmo, with target-to-shoot combat with cover\/Line-of-sight obstacles, 3rd person WASD piloting (with auto-pilot outside of combat).  Player-driven market, player-run organizations (corps), open and closed pvp zones to accommodate all play styles, hardcore\/casual.  No griefing, no crime\/punishment system.<br><br><b>Disclaimer<\/b><br>This is not Mechwarrior.  This is not an FPS.  This is not a AAA subscription MMO by a big publisher.  This is an indie developer team, that loves their work, and want to share it with others, and for a very affordable price.  And its Not pay-to-win.<br>I think most negative reviews can be summed up with expectations for a game it clearly wasn't but research was not done.<br>TL;DR<br>Do your research!  There are screenshots and videos on steam to watch.  Check them out!<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Noteworthy updates since this hit Steam in 2014<\/div><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li> Skill progression is Activity AND Time based.  See <a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"https:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/linkfilter\/?url=http:\/\/www.perpetuum-online.com\/Changelog:2016-06-24\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\" > patch notes <\/a><br><\/li><li> 11 new bots still being released! See <a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"https:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/linkfilter\/?url=http:\/\/blog.perpetuum-online.com\/posts\/2016-07-02-syndicate-robots-inbound\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\" > blog<\/a><br><\/li><li> Assignment system update: <a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"https:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/linkfilter\/?url=http:\/\/www.perpetuum-online.com\/Changelog:2015-06-15\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\" > patch notes<\/a><\/li><\/ul>I'd list all these as Pros, in addition to the fact this demonstrates a consistent pace of new content development.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Pros<\/div><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li> Price (cheap, no sub!)<br><\/li><li> Combat system<br><\/li><li> Industry and resource gathering<br><\/li><li> Player-driven market<br><\/li><li> Skill-point system (Time and Activity-based)<br><\/li><li> Gamma (player built bases and terraforming)<br><\/li><li> Risk:reward<\/li><\/ul><b> Details <\/b><br>Combat:  All combat involves line of sight or ballistic pathing for weapons to reach their target.  In addition, plant life, buildings, and other obstacles act as cover when this intersects the weapon's path upon firing.  Terrain elevation, slope, and obstacles also can block shots.  Having the high-ground actually matters in this game!  And I've certainly seen it change the tide of a battle, for and against!<br>There are other anti-blob mechanics that are unique to perpetuum.  Interference, and bot explosion damage on death (beta\/gamma only).  <br>Resource gathering: Gathering, or the acquisition of raw material, comes in a number of flavours.  Ore mining, liquid mining, plant harvesting, artifact hunting.  This gives indy players a number of different activities with more or less risk:reward and activity:reward.  Liquid ores can be mined mostly afk, but they are cheap as a result.  Rare ores are on beta\/gamma but this comes with risk.  Look at guides and videos for more!<br>Industry: Complex, eve like, but the analogy doesn't get you very far.  Research unlocks the ability to prototype, prototypes are made and consumed to make CT's, CT's can be install in a factory and run until its rating decays to 0.  No BPO's, everything is perishable in this process, and that keeps the economy churning.  A high skill investment to do well, but there are many guides on how to get started even as a new player.  <br>For the skill point system, outside of the new change to it, I would say having a cache of 'points' that you can accumulate without limit and invest in skills whenever is a much better system than a training queue or skills that require you to do that same activity to get better at it.  This way skilling is more flexible and gives new players a chance to learn and research it before investing!<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Cons<\/div><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li> Dated graphics<br><\/li><li> Inflexible UI<br><\/li><li> Small community<\/li><\/ul>Graphics:  With a custom, homegrown, &quot;the-way-your-grandma-used-to-make-it&quot; game engine might be a great experience for a software engineering team, it also means features it doesn't have might be hard to implement and add.  It's something devs have expressed interest in updating, but it falls behind many other things.<br>UI:  The ui is also the artifact of a custom game-engine with a rigidly implemented ui component.  For example, the text size is fixed to pixel size.  This means high res displays might render text too small to read.  I recommend running the game at 1080 even if your rig is capable of higher...<br>Small Community:  Now enter the cyclical argument of &quot;I won't play a game because no one else plays it&quot;.  This plagues all small MMOs.  The game world is also small, but some people just need numbers before it registers for them.  If this is you: don't bother. you up from the start, the game might become more interesting for you through participating in organized PvP.<br>There are a lot of different corps in the game, before you join one, read the official that EP package for every day.<br>Harvesting, mining or assignments is boring work, but will provide you with NIC on your own, to afford your first Mech.<br>Buying a Mech means, getting enough NIC and much, and depending on from where you have to transport your new items, you might run into trouble along the way and loose your robot and cargo.<br>If your new shiny Mech gets destroyed at some point, few items back, and those have to be repaired, before you can use them on another robot\/mech.<br>But if your ending happened during a PvP fight, the &quot;spoils&quot; belong to the winner.<br>If cover the NIC you paid for your Mech empty... say thank you to players, that abused the insurance system a while back.<br>About assignments, you can only choose between &quot;industrial\/combat\/transport&quot;, but all of them are more or less combat assignments, even those that are marked &quot;industrial\/transport&quot;, there are only a few different assignments, and be prepared to travel slow and far in the beginning to accomplish them.<br><br>The ugly:<br>Other players won't stopp at using any kind of exploit or bug they can find against you. That is of course something, you might find in any other game, but in a &quot;full loot&quot; game, the developers need to keep an very close eye on that.<br>Last but not least,if you want to get the best Mechs with the best equipment, you need a lot of EP, which will take a lot of real time.<br>And even if you take the &quot;shortcut&quot; over an EP booster, it will still take you a very long time to reach that goal.<br><br>Keep all of that in mind before you consider to buy Perpetuum - then why the thump up?<br><br>I think the game and the developers deserve a shot at this, therefore i
