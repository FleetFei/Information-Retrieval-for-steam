really know how to explain. It's a sandbox game, a building game, a shooter game, and an exploration game. Space Engineers takes you out into the galaxy as a space engineer (obviously), starting you off with anything from a fully kitted out base with a large red ship, to nothing but empty space for you to just fly around in until you find something. The game incorporates what I assume to be a randomly generated universe with randomly generated planets and asteroids. Players can choose to play however they want in Space Engineers, you can be a military commander with a giant armored behemoth to a simple farmer, tending to his asteroid (by that I mean mining resources). The game does not have a story to follow, in fact when I was dumped into the world I wasn't given any direction as to what I'm supposed to be doing, so naturally I just went around and blew \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665 up.<br><br>Building in Space Engineers is very easy, as blocks all snap together seamlessly. There are many different types of blocks that can be used, my personal favourite being military orientated blocks which include heavy, armoured pieces that allow ships to take large amounts of damage. Unfortunately, these heavier blocks require a lot more materials to be able to build, and when my ship took a hell of a lot of damage in a battle with another friend I cried myself to sleep because I had nowhere near the required amount of steel to repair even the tiniest part of my beloved ship. Military oriented blocks also refer to the weapons that ships can use  to destroy each other. To my knowledge, there are four blocks that allow ships to attack; Missile turrets, missile launchers, gattling turrets, and sentry turrets. Missile turrets are best used as asteroid defense and to directly attack smaller ships that may be attacking yours. Missile launchers are best used to attack other capitol ships such as battleships, cruisers and carriers as they can be grouped together closely, and can do a large amount of damage in a small area. Gattling turrets are best used as a missile defense, and  the sentry turrets are best used to fend off enemies that may board your ship. The lighter building blocks are useful for creating mining ships to gather resources, as they are light, easy to repair and allow for increased mobility in ships.<br><br>Mining in space engineers is a very well done system. The jackhammer tool that the player starts off with does a very good job of mining different ores in order to create your ships. Building a ship and attaching drills allows for a much easier mining experience, as larger areas can be mined at one time, and the player does not have to worry about running out of oxygen when going to other asteroids to ,mine.<br><br>Overall, I'd give this game an 8.5 out of 10.
