'conquer the city, street by street' game in what plays like a board game format, with some interesting twists.  I'll list here what I feel are some PROS, CONS, and some SUGGESTIONS FOR IMPROVEMENT that I feel might make the game even more interesting \/ challenging.<br><br>PROS<br><br>1) Semi-permanent, random events have effects on your gang's success \/ failure.  Some useful random events are: Police On Strike &amp; Loyal Stand.  Some detrimental random events are: Religious Holiday, Flu Bug, Crime Tariff.  I've only played for a handful of hours so far, so I'm sure I haven't seen all the events yet, but I do like how they add some significant upside \/ downside to the game, &amp; it's a good idea to keep an eye out for them.<br><br>2) You can bribe the police, temporarily ... initially, this is a rather costly thing to do, as it can burn through your available resources fairly quickly.  But it can also help you greatly to get that initial toe-hold you need to get your crime syndicate up &amp; running. You also get an icon that indicates 'bribe in effect' &amp; goes away on the day that your bribes cease to help you out.<br><br>3) Even bribed, the police aren't exactly your friends.  So, you have a deal with the police not to arrest any of your gang members this week?  Good for you.  But this week, the police decide to 'clean up' a neighborhood festering with corruption &amp; restore it to the residents.  Nice.  Too bad it was one of YOUR neighborhoods of festering corruption ...<br><br>4) Your main opponent finds different ways to pester &amp; annoy you.  From things as simple as &quot; I stole some of your money !&quot; to things a bit more troubling, like &quot;I stole some of your resources !&quot; to things quite a bit more personal, like &quot;I stole one of your best gang members!&quot;  Yeah, like the one I bothered to just equip with a nice shiny new gun &amp; body armor, WTH, lol ...<br><br>5) In a pinch, you can convert your resources to cash.  There's a lower $, lower resource liquidation option &amp; a higher $, higher resource liquidation option.  This can be useful early on, but you can get a bit carried away with this ...<br><br>6) Occassionally, random help shows up.  Maybe you've got plenty of cash, but resources are tapping out.  Or you need cash &amp; can't burn down resources to get it.  Sometimes an outside source will make an offer to you that involves a swap of some kind.  These don't generally occur often, but they can be helpful from time to time.<br><br>7) Pretty Much Bug-Free.  So far the one &amp; only thing that I have noticed has also already been pointed out by others.  Whenever I click the desktop icon to launch the game, it will put up an install window.  Past the point of installing it once, you have to ignore this popup &amp; then the game loads normally each &amp; every time.  But it is a bit of a nuisance b\/c it will show this each &amp; every time.  But as far as game mechanics once playing the game itself, I haven't found any bugs at all.<br><br>CONS (it appears I ran out of my max allowed words when describing the CONS, so I'll rewrite and be briefer, lol).<br><br>There are a number of these, but I'm not without hope that at least some of them might be addressed, as the Dev appears willing to listen to constructive criticism &amp; still appears to be adjusting the game to make it more challenging \/ interesting.<br><br>1) My most serious beef deals with the judge\/lawyer\/bribe\/assassinate\/trial system that is set up here.  Quite a few issues with this.<br><br>1a) Biggest complaint is that once your gang member is arrested, there is no description indicating which of the 3 judges in the city will be hearing his case.  This is largely baffling - how is it that a crime syndicate entrenched enough to bribe both judges &amp; cops not capable of figuring out which of 3 judges is going to try their gang member the next day?  At least give me an option to 'incentive bribe' someone in a position to know that answer so that I can go from there.  This would help give me an idea if my lawyer (stats prominently displayed when you hire 1 of 3 lawyers) has a snowball's chance in he11 of dealing with that particular judge (those 3 judges stats are also prominently displayed, but don't help much if you can't do anything in light of it that might improve your chances of successfully defending your client).  Of course, if you have the cash, you could just bribe all 3 judges, which leads me to ...<br><br>1b) 'Bribed Judges' still find your gang members guilty.  And not just guilty, but often 'bury them under the jail &amp; throw away the key' guilty.  I get that hackers are dangerous, street thugs can be vicious, etc.  The game runs on quite a few turns passing before a full year goes by, so I guess some of these 'life sentences' could, in reality, be 10 - 20 year convictions (which would effectively take them out of any reasonable chance of you seeing them again in the course of your game).  But it'd be cool to see the number of years they got vs. hearing a life in prison sentence for something as simple as being arrested for guard duty at your hideout.  Yes, it does happen.  Also, if a 'bribed judge' is going to get indignant over being offered a bribe &amp; then 'throw the book' at my gang member, I'd like to hear something back after the bribe was offered to that effect.  It might get me to consider sending someone his way ... which leads to another thing ...<br><br>1c) Even if you guess correctly &amp; knock off the correct judge, the trial of your gang member will take place first, &amp; then the judge will get waxed.  As near as I can tell, there's no taking out the judge and stalling proceedings until a new (and hopefully more sympathetic) judge comes along.  A couple of other things about the whole lawyer\/judge thing ...<br><br>1d) You can't get rid of your idiot lawyer to hire a better one.  Your stuck with him or her until the 2 year contract runs its course.  Presumably, you get the one with the best stats from the get go, but one nice thing is that occassionaly, old lawyers &amp; judges go out (in the judge case, often feet first) and new ones come in, sometimes presenting you with new lawyers with better stats.  This system could really hum if you could drop one lawyer for another from trial to trial, depending on you knowing which judge your member would be up against.  This would give you some feeling of say in the final outcome, even if it meant that you shelled out some more $ to rip up contract with one lawyer to pick up a new one.  At the moment, that feels like an area that needs some improvement.<br><br>1e) Bribe all 3 judges, shoot the ones that don't toe the line, the new ones come in listing as bribed as well.  This seems like it's likely just a slight oversight.  I wouldn't think the judge would include in his or her last will &amp; testament that their bribes be passed on for the good of the next judge to take their place.  It does kinda make me laugh a bit though whenever I see the new judge listed with 'bribed' already under their heading ...<br><br>Okay enough with the judicial system, onto a few other cons ...<br><br>2) Guns &amp; armor dry up after you buy the initially offered items at the store.  While these do eventually get replaced, it can sometimes take many, many turns before any new weaponry or armor comes down the line to be offered to your gang. <br><br>3) Can't swap guns &amp; armor from member to member.  Argghh ...<br><br>4) Resources to cash but no cash to resources, unless you get really, really lucky with an outside offer. So keep your resources up. Or else.<br><br>5) Only hacking &amp; searching for your rival seem to grant resources. Chaos only seems to ever add money. Eh.
