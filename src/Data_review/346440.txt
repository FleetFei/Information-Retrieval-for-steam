It's a top-down keyboard-controlled racer (which means you need to learn how to engine brake if you're going to do any good here) that features a daily user-made track - and yes, that means they can be quite crazy - with leaderboards for 10 or so different cars, each with their own strengths and weaknesses, though some feel closer to each other than others. This is in-game currency, so no worries about any extra charges.  These coins are used to unlock other cars, but more importantly, their variants as well as the tires they run on, and believe me, if you want to be in the top spot, you'll need to know exactly which variant and what tires you need to run.  Placing a decent time will earn you a silver medal, a great time will earn you gold, and the top spot will earn you an achievement, bragging rights, and possibly the chance to wave your virtual fiddlestick around.  Each possible position silver and above will net you more coins, which will allow you to buy even more cars, more variants, and even a fancy helmet (totally cosmetic).  For the ultimate prize in coins and prestige, win the &quot;daily overall&quot; spot by being the best in the most car classes.  I have no idea what the actual prize for that is because there are TWO SPECIFIC dudes who play this that will make life difficult for you haha<br><br>At any rate, it's easy to sink lots of time into this game if you are chasing the top spot because you will need to set the fastest times in the most car classes possible, and that means gradually unlocking all of them and running them on the track for that day.  This can take a significant amount of time if you become obsessed with it.  You might constantly get that feeling of, &quot;I can shave off just a -little- more time on the next lap&quot; and round and round you go (protip: turn off the automatic-end-run option or you might mess up your mojo).<br><br>Your opponents?  Other people from around the globe.  I constantly see players from the UK, France, Sweden, and many more.  By 'see', I mean you're not just out there on the track alone; you are racing ghosts (they can be turned off if they are too distracting) and those ghosts are constantly mocking you as you cross the plane just a hair behind them.
