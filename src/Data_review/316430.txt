During gameplay players will have a variety of units to command: infantry, artillery, tanks, etc and each have their own individual inventories to allow total customization based on your combat needs. The combat is real-time and the maps are fully interactive. This means that obstacles can be destroyed, used for cover, and manipulated over the course of the battle as well as reflecting the changing landscape of the battlefield due to artillery shells, bombing runs, or even from building make-shift sandbag barricades to be used as cover. The amount of detail put into the units to reflect the time period is admirable and the effort does not go un-noticed. BoE truly feels like a WWI timepiece.<br><br>The game is extremely vanilla in its current Early Access state but still very playable. For an Early Access game there is a lot of content and playability available and I experienced no bugs or crashes so there is no need to wait.<br><br>There are a few things that I hope to see improved before the final release. The sound and voiceovers are mediocre at best and still leave a lot to be desired. Another issue is with the limitations on squads. The user interface feels cluttered and unorganized. It gets the job done but overall it just doesn't feel very efficient. <br><br>BoE includes a tutorial along with a skirmish mission before actually beginning the campaign in order to familiarize players with the various controls and such. However, at times this tutorial is vague and can be confusing, even more so for a player not familiar with this type of game and a lot of information is stuffed into this small sampling so it can quickly become overwhelming.
