You are also able to choose from a variety of different leaders which give a unique starting bonus. One of these bonuses that I love is the one that gives you a second starting ship, since you only start with one.<br><br>After you choose your starting conditions and possible victory conditions (there are many ranging from population, to even domination.), you then jump into the game. It's quite easy to win even on hard difficulties. The player takes their ship to a neutral planet in an attempt to make the planet join their side. After a few missions (that you have to pass), they join you, but don't think you can just go and take it with no competition. Your ship\/ships have limited movements, so choose which planets you want to take wisely. Other civilizations will try to compete against you for the neutral planets until there are basically none left, leaving only one way to expand. Domination. Or you could just win a different type of victory by then and have the game end, it's quite possible. <br><br>Combat is quite easy, as long as you don't throw all of your ships into the grinder to get shredded apart. The problem is that if you have enough supplies to upgrade your ships to be overpowered, then you will be unstoppable. Resources are quite easy to get too, so that means overpowered ships are as well. So you could just win the game with one ship if you really wanted to, instead of making a ton of ships. Do this by getting a max level hangar and spitting out little fighters to wreck your enemies.<br><br>There is also diplomacy in this game, so feel free to explore that option as well. You can befriend others and team up to take down another, or force others to give you resources or ship upgrades. Last but not least I will mention this: each planet has upgrades that you can do to increase resource production, population, etc. Most planets also have great wonders you can build that give (sometimes overpowered) upgrades to your ships depending on your playstyle. Some upgrades could be useless to you if you don't want to use a certain thing.<br><br>Basically, if you aren't a fan of easy games I'd avoid this, but I still enjoyed my experience with it.
