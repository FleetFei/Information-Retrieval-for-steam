looked more like human workers to me) were released and would walk through the landscape, following each other like the archetypal lemmings, blindly walking off cliffs to their death. Your job as the player was to guide the lemmings by giving specific instructions to them, for example to build bridges or dig into the landscape, in order to guide as many of them to safety as possible.<br><br>Flockers uses essentially the same concept, but with sheep instead of lemmings. Appropriately so, since sheep also have the habit of following one another. This time around, rather than natural landscapes, you have to help them navigate through extremely dangerous factory complexes, filled with saws, spikes, hammers and other nasty objects, often with gory results as your mindless sheep stroll blindly into them.<br><br>Each level is a puzzle. They're large, and get progressively more complicated as you progress through them. You can zoom in and out to get a better view of the puzzle you have to solve. Sheep may wander off in different directions, you may meet more sheep along the way, transporters teleport sheep to different locations and it can sometimes be a bit confusing to understand what's going on the first time you try a new level.<br><br>As you go through each level, sheep step on buttons which activate machinery, sometimes in a good way, sometimes in a very bad way, so you have to be very careful what you let them step on. They also encounter crates, which when opened give you new abilities that you can assign to your your sheep. It may take a few attempts to figure out how to get through a level.<br><br>While you often need to get only one sheep to safety (in the early levels at least), getting more will score you more points, as will doing it in a faster time. You also get a bonus for using fewer abilities. You can retry levels to get a better score, and you can compare your score to your friends.<br><br>The levels are very detailed, perhaps overly so, which can make it tricky to figure out how to solve them. But maybe that's the point, distracting you with unimportant details. Fortunately, although it's an action game, you can pause it at any time, giving you the opportunity to study the puzzle at your leisure. You still have to solve it in real time though, giving commands to your sheep as they move. Pressing the right mouse button gives you a bigger &quot;brush&quot;, enabling you to give commands to multiple sheep at the same time.<br><br>The graphics are very good, and very gory as sheep get smashed and sawed, spiked and gored, with blood splattering everywhere, which is rather amusing if you find that sort of thing funny. For this reason the game has an age rating. But if you don't like that sort of thing, you can simply turn off the gore in the video options.<br><br>I haven't played this game for very long yet, but my first impressions are very good. I'm finding it both challenging and amusing, an enjoyable casual way to pass the time.
