Want to be able to control how much a drug sells for? If you answered yes to both then this will be the DLC for you.<br><br>Marketing &amp; Malpractice adds a few nice features and some new content to the base game. The marketing aspects have not just had new additions, it's also been revamped. This DLC allows you to control directly if you want to undercut the competition by offering discounts on products, or charging a premium for a drug that's in high demand. You can also adjust the amount of stock to keep in your warehouse, useful for those drugs that you are making more of then you are selling.<br><br>No longer are your sales solely affected by cure rating. You will recieve executives at certain research points that will allow you to increase (or at least attempt to) sales. Executives have quite a few means to do this, including but not limited to sales reps, bribing doctors with gifts, cross-platform, social media, disease awareness campaigns and (probably my most favourite) clinical trials. Some give a straight boost, others like clinical trials can lead to bad publicity if your product is below par and if you start doing some dodgy stuff (like disqualifying bad results) can also lead to it being banned. Good Stuff.<br><br>Ingredients also have the possibility of spawning with &quot;Booster&quot; effects. When you meet the conditions these have, they will give you huge increases in sales. There are also perks, you can select up to 2 before you start a game. Some perks provide utility (like the &quot;Mixology&quot; perk which gives the Multi-Mixer and Centrifuge a process time of 1 instead of 2, reducing costs and space requirements) while others can really shape up how you are going to approach making your drugs before you have even built your first machine (like &quot;Trust Me I'm A Doctor&quot; which gives a flat 10% sales bonus for drugs that have no side effects).<br><br>Lastly, there are a few new machines to play with. There is the socket to socket transfer which is as useful as it sounds. A Booster Mixer which is similar to a multi-mixer but only mixes the booster effects with effecient processing costs, time and takes up little space. There are also stock gates, I haven't been able to use these effectively yet but allow production lines to be able to set up multiple drugs if the demand for one drug is under what you can produce.<br><br>If there is one thing I was slightly disappointed with, it was the fact there are no new missions (other then the new marketing tutorial one), could've made a new mission tree which revovled around making boostered cures or something similar. Even so, at this price point it is a much welcome addition to part of the game that needed fleshing out the most.
