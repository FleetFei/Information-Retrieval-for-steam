truth. <br><br>Currently, a young man by the name of Benjamin Moss resides in this cursed abode. No matter where he moves, the walls and rooms of this cursed place grip his very being, making him restless and hollow. Stained on his walls are the numbers, \u201c5676.\u201d Are these numbers linked to the mysterious, paranormal activities occurring in his home? Desperate for answers, he begins his search for the truth but will he drive himself into madness in the process?<br><br>---<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"> Gameplay <\/div><br>It consists of solving puzzles in a form of riddles and the occasional searching for items to proceed forward through the story, e.g., hammers for destroying brittle walls. <br><br>The appearances of the threats are mostly scripted, and they don\u2019t chase you around so you are free to take your time and absorb the environment. There isn\u2019t much to explore anyways but certain events change to an extent depending on the number of your playthrough. For example, there\u2019s a part in the beginning of the first run where you have to mess around with a circuit breaker (the power cuts off). The same thing happens in the second playthrough, however, instead of going to the breaker, you must exit the apartment to complete this sequence. Since the threat isn\u2019t present all the time, the game relies on sounds and narrative to set the atmosphere. I did notice that some of the background sounds are reused sound assets. I recognize them from other games. <br><br>---<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"> Graphics and Technical Difficulties <\/div><br>It is a RpgMaker game so I think most people know what these games look like. I didn\u2019t run across any gamebreaking bugs or crashes but I did have a problem putting the game on fullscreen. After several button pressing later, I found that F5 seems to toggle the fullscreen and windowed mode. I don\u2019t play many RpgMaker games so this might be standard procedure for them.<br><br>---<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"> My observations <\/div><br>The game has a slow start and the plot isn\u2019t fully explained by just playing the game one time. It is only towards the end that the story opens up. I do like how certain actions dictate what the ending will. The Steam page says there are hidden storylines. I have no idea what they are but when I find some time, I will be playing around with the game to scour for them. <br><br>My first playthrough took me an hour and subsequent runs might take another hour, depending on how long you solve puzzles. I think the game is interesting enough to keep you playing because the plot twists are handled well. Just don\u2019t expect a scarefest.<br><br>---<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"> Wrap Up <\/div><br>For its price range, $7.99 (has a small discount, $6.79, at the time of this review), I recommend this game.
