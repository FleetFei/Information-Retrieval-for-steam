It is a massive improvement to Bus Simulator 2012, by the same developers. I do agree that the format is a bit strange, but it just takes a bit of getting used to. The graphics are of a very good quality, especially the weather effects and I usually get good FPS as well. Please be aware before you purchase, that this is quite a graphically demanding game and you will require a high end system. Let's begin with what makes this game really enjoyable:<br>- The bus is very detailed inside, with nice textures and all of the switches and buttons are clickable, unlike other bus simulators out there.<br>- There are lots of really cool view modes all around the drivers seat, and I love the way the camera zooms in and out on the panels.<br>- Nice effects such as cab sway.<br>- The co-pilot is very useful and you can use to alter settings such as announcements and routes.<br>- There are about three different buses you can choose from, so a nice selection.<br>- The AI passengers are quite realistic and they can knock on the window of the bus if you leave then behind.<br>- Lots of traffic to enhace the realism! I have been waiting for this in a bus game for quite some time.<br>- Weather effects are incredibly realistic, with beautiful cloud and sunlight textures.<br>- There are also special effects such as trams and bus diversions.<br>- You are informed about events such as diversions, or shift duties, via the radio.<br>- You can be put on rail replacement bus routes.<br>- Extreme events can occur. For example, a car accident and you may have to take a diversion.<br>- Like most TML games, you can free roam the city and walk around inside your bus.<br>- There is a tutorial, which is very handy. You can turn it off in settings. However, it is written at the top of the screen, so doesn't offer any voice guidance.<br><br>Now let's mention the improvements that could have been made and some of the issues:<br>- It's a shame that there's only one bus route in the game, with an extra little run to the depot. It would have been nice to have some more variation.<br>- Upon launching the game for the first game, I was baffled by a white screen with three small colours to click on. It took me about 10 minutes to realise I had to double click on a green dot, in order to load up the main menu! What was the point in that?<br>- Sometimes, I find the game a little bit too German. Things such as the radio, is only available in German, with English subtitles at the top of the screen. It would have been handy to have an English voice as well.<br>- Some people may see this as a positive, but there is no cash involved in the game. Now it is much quicker to board passengers onto the bus as they just walk straight on. I used to like printing tickets and giving change, but that's just my opinon. Your choice, which do you prefer?<br>- The menus in the game are very perculiar. They are complex to understand and some of the text is in very small font.<br>-Some of the AI passengers can act very weird at times, for example walking round in circles and I have even seen a couple of people fly up into the air.<br>-A very minor flaw. When you are sitting in the drivers seat, you cannot see the icons on the dashboard as the steering view obsures them. So when someone presses the stop button, you can hear the beep, but not see the icon come on the dashboard. So you have to press NUM 8 to zoom in on the dashboard to check if it was pressed (whilst still trying to drive).<br><br>Although this is overall a very good game, I would say that the price may be a little expensive, so I'm glad that I bought it in a sale. It is one of the best bus simulators out there for PC, as well as being by far the most realistic. It does have some flaws, as just mentioned, but they are nothing too serious. So in conclusion, I do recommend this game for any simulation enthusiasts, like myself, as it provides an extremely good simulation of being a bus driver in Munich.<br>Personally, I would wait for a
