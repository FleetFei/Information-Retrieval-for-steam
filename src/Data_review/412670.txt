I love the thrill of the score chase, the art of the bullet patterns and figuring out the best path through brutal levels. This game has all of that plus an absolutely insane amount of depth, both to the scoring system and to the amount of customisation that's available for your characters and weapons. There are three difficulty levels which can make things accessible to new players but the level of brutally hard you expect from this kind of game. Most arcade shmups are only a handful of levels but this one has over fifty which is crazy, even if they are each rather short. You'll quickly reach a point where things get very tough, even on the lowest difficulty setting so repeated play is encouraged and indeed necessary.<br><br>Succeed or fail, you'll earn different amounts of in-game currency which you can use to buy new characters, new weapons and costumes for them which all have benefits and even cheats and handicaps which impact your score when used. This has the major advantage of you still being able to qualify for the leaderboards if you use them. The leaderboard integration is very slick and while it would have been nice to see each difficulty level get its own roster, each level does have its own and you can sort by a variety of means. You're also shown your current position before starting a level and right after you complete it, assuming you're online.<br><br>Bullet Heaven 2 has a very cutesy\/twee aesthetic which really doesn't do much for me but in Bullet Hell games, you don't really have a lot of time to pay attention to the art. If you are, you're kinda' doing it wrong.<br><br>This game is an incredible value for shmup fans and if you are one, you should pick it up without hesitation. It astounds me that this was made by only one person.
