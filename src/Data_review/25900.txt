I DARE YOU to beat this game in one setting, because you won't! There are randomized elements to the game, such as what enemies you'll face and what items you'll collect. Epic, moving, and sweeping fantasy tracks.<br><br>- Standard and uninteresting fantasy storyline. Made worse by the spelling\/grammar\/translation issues.<br><br>- Magic Crystals are too plentiful and become incrediblly useless over time. However, if you are an active magic user, then they are harder to come by.<br><br>- Can be TOO difficult. If you are a masochist, play this game on Hard or Impossible. Normal mode is perfectly challenging enough.<br><br>- Poor and very strange audio mixing. The sound effects, for some reason, are much more quiet than the obscenely loud music. Some sound effects, in fact, are completely missing.<br><br>- Numerous typos, translation issues, and grammar errors that are often hilarious, including names such as &quot;Ruin Guy&quot; or &quot;Furious Paladin&quot;.<br><br>- A few very annoying and very overpowered enemies\/units. Wolves are irritating with their stupid Howl ability, Dryads are quite possibly the worst with their ability to put your entire army to sleep repeatedly, and Necromancers are just plain overpowered! Necromancers are broken, being able to raise other Necromancers from the dead for nearly endless battles if you don't take them out.<br><br>- Unfair and ridiculous &quot;Item Morale&quot; and &quot;Supression&quot; system. I do not know what affects the morale of an item, but it might be determined by the Race that the items are associated with, that are IN your army. Or the Races your other equipped items are associated with. This is possibly the worst aspect of King's Bounty: The Legend.<br><br>Overall, King's Bounty: The Legend doesn't have many flaws ( most of the above problems are minor and hardly hurt the game that much ). played!<br><br>King's Bounty: The Legend is a RPG with tactical battles. The battle system is reminiscent of Heroes of Might and Magic, but King's Bounty is not a strategy game, like Heroes. Instead, you explore the (huge) game world at your own pace, without having to worry about some opponent getting stronger if you take your time.<br><br>You explore the maps (in real time) to gather resources and troops, then defeat monsters that are roaming the map, or guarding some chest... or even just waiting for you to try to kill them. The game doesn't have many mandatory battles, each enemy you defeat gives you experience, so you need to kill them to get enough XP to level up your hero.  When you get close to some enemy, the game changes to a hexagonal grid, and places your troops vs the enemy troops in a turn-based strategic combat. There's LOTS of strategy involved here, BTW. You can't just move your troops casually in the battlefield, this WILL get you killed quite fast! Instead, you have to think before moving each troop, even ONE badly placed unit can mean your defeat.<br><br>Just like the first 3 Heroes games, you don't use your hero directly in battles. Instead, you control your troops, but can use magics and skills of your hero once per turn. The hero is VERY important, since a well placed magic can turn the tide of a battle in your favor. <br><br>The game is very extensive. The map is huge, there are LOTS of optional battles, a big variety of troops for you to acquire, a robust skill tree for your hero (that includes some passive skills to make your troops better), a simple marriage system and tons of side-quests scattered around. The game is HUGE. While you can beat it in 50 or so hours, you can play it for 100 hours and still have something to do and some enemy to kill. Not only that, but the game has a random placement of enemies, and 3 different heroes to choose, so there's a nice replay value.<br><br>Lastly, the technical aspects aren't bad. The graphics are beautiful (with a big variety of textures in most environments), your units are very detailed and the game has some very good looking special effects (spells, detailed shadows, bloom). The sounds are very good too, and each unit has its own grunts and taunts. Also, the game has some AMAZING musics by Lind Erebros. Each map has its own playlist of songs, and they usually mix well with the environments of the map you are in.<br><br><br>Anyway, King's Bounty is amazing. It has some minor flaws, like a cruel difficulty spike and lots of backtracking (specially to replenish troops), its not a friendly game towards newbies (good troop units aren't finite, you can't reset your skill trees allocation without restarting the game, etc). Also, the story is very forgettable, and the dialogs are usually very stupid. But the flaws don't hurt the game. Its a huge game, with LOTS of things to do and huge maps to explore. Its also slow paced, so you don't have to rush, like in the Heroes series.<br><br>I QUITE recommend this game.
