And there is no change in the gameplay for this one, so, don't mind my lazyness, I'll copy\/paste that part from the first game review.<br><br>Like for 12 Labours of Hercules, the main character isn't helping at all. In other words, it's the servants that are doing all the work. Gnomes Garden 2 is a casual time management game where you have workers that are gathering resources needed (food, wood, stone and crystals), building mines (for the stone and crystals), farms and windmills (for the food), workshop (for the wood), upgrading these for a better production and repairing routes and other objectives (like light a fire or activate portals). However, the servants can be helped by witches (needed for some objectives - they can restore trees, plant them, activate portals, make the trolls or the sleeping golems disappear, grow lilypads) and firemen (helping objective plants to grow or to scare the little monster that will incapacitate your witch or servant from doing their jobs for a few seconds). And you'll repeat that for 50 levels.<br><br>However, there are temporary bonus, regenerating over time, that can be activated : run faster (your minions move faster), work faster (less time for the building to give the resources) and stop time (the timer is frozen for a limited time). And these are very handy if you want to reach the three stars. Indeed, you have a timer. If you go past a star, you're loosing it. The less time you spent, the more likely you'll keep three stars. And as it's an achievement, you're also motivated. Nothing is wrong to loose one or two in the first run, so that you can try different strategies for the level. However, keep in mind that sometimes, you need to manage tightly your resources: you may be lacking some of them when they're needed and can't advance anymore (example: no more stones for a hole in the road - the building that could have given you the resource is behind this hole and there is no other road to it - you're doomed to restart). One of the good points is that you're not limited in the tasks done by the servant, if you want to gather 3 woods and 2 foods, you can check them without restriction (compared to 12 Labours of Hercules where you couldn't indicate more than 3 tasks). Also, I found some improvement: the task that will be done is highlighted AND finally, the resplenishing for the bonus isn't no more affected by the use of another. On the downside, the timer bonus is now very slow to be full.<br><br>However, I found the levels more easier than in Gnomes Garden, as I only restarted a couple of levels and not many of them. I don't know if it's because I was more aware of how the game worked or not, but well, I finished it much faster if I compare the time: 5,8h against 7,8h. Well, when trading cards will be available, my time will raise.<br><br>As you can see, the gameplay isn't hard per se, it's just a matter of being efficient and to make the right choices (no need to upgrade a building to the maximum if the current level is sufficient or to upgrade the house - I've made the majority of the levels with two workers only). However, you don't have any other characters that can help. It's just the minions, not even the princess is present in the level to do something.<br><br>I found the graphics being more polished than in the first one. I don't know why but the map looked better, the presentation of the objectives were less harsh and I found the buildings and characters more alive. I don't know if it's a question of colors or a question of being used. However, the objectives in the bottom of the screen is still confusing, though now that I know of it, it seems less inconvenient than in the first game.<br><br>The soundtrack is also nice.<br><br>But while I preferred Gnomes Garden 2 to the first game, there is still the magic missing from 12 Labours of Hercules. The gameplay didn't improved at all apart for rebuilding a cable car and a statue of the king, the rest is the same as in Gnomes Garden.
