But perhaps here, in this surreal and hypnotic greenhouse atop a cloud, it can.<br><br>There is an immediate urge among many I know to label products of the internet age as ephemeral and thematically shallow. In this way Gardenarium\u2019s penchant for blunt, matter of fact wit and uninhibited social commentary feels refreshingly honest. These thoughts may stem from the odd and inscrutable inhabitants of Gardenarium\u2019s organic pastel world, but they carry enough weight in them to pull you out of the clouds and back to earth. Gardenarium is not an exercise in linguistic fortitude, but of accessibility and honesty, spoken in a style too quickly cast aside as the degeneration of literature by the next generation.<br><br>Gardenarium arrives at a time when the concept of what constitutes a game\/art\/literature is being redefined by the day. At this point it seems pedantic to continually qualify what have always been loose labels bestowed largely for the sake of exclusion. Whether or not Gardenarium\u2019s 30-minute walk through a sky garden is a game is irrelevant to the experience itself, one that is both beautiful and poignant, and more emotionally honest than most games could ever hope for.
