levels, in their entire Early Development glory. This includes, but isn't limited to:<br><br>- Ridiculously basic appearance of the levels;<br>- Some of the models and sounds being replaced with placeholder ones;<br>- Very obviously and hilariously half-arsed solutions, like floating film reels with names (or rather, numbers) of the levels on them, or the levels having very visible borders;<br>- Comically bad voice acting;<br>- An obvious style clash of placeholder and final-version elements being mashed together.<br><br>All of this results in a setting so hilariously unlike the final game and so hilariously amateurish that it all crosses over into the &quot;So Bad It's Good&quot; category. Does that mean that these elements were created straight away in their final form? Or is it simply because the devs lost the early placeholder files for them? If it's the latter, then it surely would be interesting to see what these objects looked like in their early versions...<br><br>Do I recommend this DLC? Well... I personally liked it, some parts of it better than others.
