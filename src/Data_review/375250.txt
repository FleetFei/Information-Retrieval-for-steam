that I have always been reluctant to attempt has been sim\/management games due to the depth and complexity they seem to project in either previews, lets plays, videos, or reviews. I would give both Yippee Ki yay Mr Falcons quick look video for Pizza Express and the absolute giving it away price of .65 that finally pushed me over to finally give it the ol' college try. Im glad I did because Its totally accessible, addicting, simple, challenging and fun. <br><br>Pizza Express is a restaurant simulation game where you are given the reins to your own pizzeria. Here you will manage everything from working hours, advertising, restaurant interior, specials and most importantly the pizza. Their is a story attached to Pizza Express that has multiple ending and choice but its all totally skippable and to briefly summarize in a nut shell you are trying to become the #1 restaurant in town in 30 days. There are also other modes outside the story such as challenge and arcade both of which have online leaderboards. There is a lot of content here and I can't praise the tutorials enough as they are done extremely well to get you started.<br><br>The management aspect of Pizza Express is pretty simple as each day begins you will be given the opportunity to upgrade your restaurant with additional tables, change the hours of service or improve the atmosphere with disco lights, music, air conditioning, etc. These upgrades improve the restaurants chic factor which will help you gain customers, improve your restaurant status while also allowing you to increase your profit if you pay attention. You will also manage advertising opportunities with radio, internet, flyers, etc. but on my 2nd playthrough I found advertising to not really be needed and since most choices were expensive I ignored it for the most part. Overall the management opportunities are not overwhelming which makes decision making pretty quick and allows you to get into the real challenge, pizza.<br><br>After getting the restaurant ready your next task will be setting up the kitchen with your ingredients, quantity, quality, creating your own pizzas, delivery type, etc and also the menu of what pizzas you will serve and how much you will charge. This component is pretty deep with a lot of variety and important choices to be made and its all customizable to your liking. As a numbers guy I loved everything about this and just the quality of ingredients you choose and the size of your containers can make an impact to the day. After my initial playthrough just moving ingredients around for ease of access, size of the containers for the most popular pizzas, adjusting my hours of service helped increase my profits . There is a slight learning curve but when it all come together its very satisfying.<br><br>The action really heats up when you hit the start the day button and you are placed in the kitchen because its Go, Go, Go. Your orders for each table(max 6 customers per\/ table 9 max) populate on the left hand side and if you hover over the order it will show the ingredients(thank god). You simply roll(click) out a dough and click and drag each ingredient onto the pizza. That's it. Simple, right? OMG, it is so frantic and there is a timer to serve each order which at first will seem impossible but as you learn recipes and your layout its manageable. It really feels like a dance as you are eyeing the orders and watching your supply because if you run out the wait time to refill an ingredient can be up to 60 sec depending on the service you choose. Once you near the end of day 20(if you don't go bankrupt) with your restaurant close to max capacity and your rep has increased each day can feel like the end of a boss fight and seeing the positive results will provide you with a feeling of victory or in some cases defeat.<br><br>As the day ends you are given a score on your service and the restaurant. You will also see your profit or loss for the day which basically is telling you to review what is or isn't working. Are the prices too high, ingredients any good, not enough tables, using the wrong supplier, etc so many factors go into winning. While there are a few graphs provided the one important missing factor I would' like to see is a graph detailing what are the popular pizzas and at what price for each day. If I raise the price of a certain pizza, did I sell less or more on this given day? Its a minor exclusion but you I guarantee you will be asking yourself that question as you continue to grow your menu.<br><br>Graphically Pizza Express does not stray to far from most indie games using a very NES coat of paint. Its bright, pixelated and it does the job but I would like to really see this with a more updated look. Sound also takes cues from the NES era and I found them to be a nice mix of beats whether doing upgrades to the restaurant or during the action in the kitchen. Another nice feature I was surprised to find is you can add your own custom playlist. I didn't try it but it looked easy to complete. If Pizza Express is one thing its is driven to provide a lot of player customization which should be applauded.<br><br>Pizza Express is a perfect entry for anyone looking to try out a restaurant\/ sim management game. It keeps the management aspect simple with enough choices to not overwhelm a player and still provides enough strategic price\/ cost depth that will keep even veterans entertained. The strength of Pizza Express still lies in that satisfying, addicting, one more day gameplay.
