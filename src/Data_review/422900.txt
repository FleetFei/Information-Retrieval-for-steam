Damaging things essentially eat their way into your ships, popping turrets and eventually destroying the core if they reach it.  But the SHAPE of each ship can determine how easy or not it is for that to happen.  <br><br>These ships are constructed around any Energy Mines... which are the source of your resources in this game.  Unlike CW3's all-consuming Network, Energy Mines can only be constructed in specific locations, and have a certain range at which they can project energy.  You'll use this energy to build ships and other things, and to recharge ships as they continue to use their turrets.  You also have Omnis, which look like little turrets themselves, but can be used to capture land, or do anything that your HQ ship or Lathes could do (destroying\/capturing structures, breaking up Struc and landmass, other things).   These are interesting in that unlike, say, reactors in CW3, these can be used in a variety of aggressive ways, since they can be flown around similar to how turrets moved in CW3.<br><br>And that's one interesting thing about this game, is that your units tend to be very versatile in terms of how you can use them (with certain exceptions).  This means there are a ton of different possible approaches, and all sorts of playstyles that you could use, that are all totally viable.  What's more, these different ships are just real fun to use.  My favorite is the Hammer; a huge blocky thing with a massive frontal hull, and the core at the very back of the ship.  Having next to no ranged weapons, this thing is meant for blocking damage.... or ramming stuff.  I'll never tire of doing this, it's just hilarious fun.  Other units tend to be very creative and entertaining in their own way too, things like Grabbers for instance, which can direct blue Particulate around the map.  However, there are restrictions: unlike CW3 you cant just build as many things as you want.  For example, the default fleet allows you to have only two Lathes, only one Hammer, only two Grabbers, and so on.  And a max number of Omnis.  This may sound restrictive, but you'll soon find that you feel like you have TONS of units.... there's alot to keep track of.  And build order ends up being very important on tougher missions... you'll be making some tough choices here, which makes things extra interesting.  As always though, there are many approaches to build order depending on your playstyle and strategy.  Nothing is ever set in stone in this game.  I cant even tell you just how much I appreciate this fact.<br><br>About the Particulate:  This serves as the enemy in each map (the red ones anyway, the blue ones are on your side).  As with CW3, this stuff isnt even remotely like a traditional enemy force in RTS games.  These are massive waves of individual particles that flow around the map in a variety of ways... different varieties of particles have different behavior, but individual particles are very weak; these are dangerous in the fact that they're in huge numbers, and they just... keep.... coming.  Like CW3 there are emitters around the map that never stop generating this stuff.   And what's more, these are used in tons of interesting ways in each map... you never know just what you're going to get from these until you start the map.   There are other threats, of course, such as Emergent (funky cubes that roam around awkwardly) or Doppels (big groupings of particles that look and act somewhat like your ships), among other stuff.  The creativity of the enemy units\/behaviors constantly amazes me.<br><br>And of course, there's alot of interesting mechanics here, such as Amp Gems (can be used to grab &quot;techs&quot; that can be switched out at any time, or can be attached to ships to power them up) and other fun stuff.   All these mechanics and things are just as versatile as your ships.   Oh, and did I mention you can make your own custom ships?  You can have a seperate fleet, choosing either your custom-made stuff, or the default fleet, and there are seperate scoreboards for both.<br><br>The campaign itself is pretty interesting, but it's also quite short:  This is very clearly a game where the vast majority of content is found AFTER that.   Like CW3 it's pretty much endless... you can get procedurally generated maps, or download maps that others have made via the extensive editor.  The campaign is mostly there to tell a story, slowly teach you about every aspect of the game, and generally prepare you for greater challenges ahead.   There's TONS to do here... one of those games you can get hundreds of hours out of.<br><br>And that time is well spent.  This overall is just a very fun, deep, complex strategy game like no other, with so many possibilities.   If you're up for a strategy game that really does something NEW and different, something that's just darned fun while having the potential for great challenge.... this is the game to get.   Oh, and consider grabbing Creeper World, while you're at it, if you dont already have that.
