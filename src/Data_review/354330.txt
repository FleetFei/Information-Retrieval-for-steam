and sleeping with everyone's wife right away. This DLC adds tons of new mechanics to the game that no one has ever seen before. Initially, you'll have absolutely no idea what's going on. So to save you the trouble, here's what I know so far:<br><br>1) Clans.<br>When playing as a nomadic faction, vassals no longer exist. Factions now consist of several clans (houses if it were in Europe). Instead of having to worry about making one vassal in specific, you have to worry about making his entire family happy. You can do this by; a) marrying into his family (obvious), or b) forming a blood pact with him. A blood pact swearing an alliance with his house until one of you die. This makes you his eternal ally (I believe if a relative of his owns land, you are their ally as well, not sure) NOTE: If there are 3 families in a nation and you form a blood pact with one of them, they have the tendency to revolt immediately and declare themselves Khan. If you are a ruler it is VITAL that you form a pact with a very powerful clan.<br><br>2) The Silk Road.<br>Along with the extended map (Eurasia) we get to enjoy the wonders of the silk road and the bloodshed it brings. The Silk Road brings wealth. The Silk Road brings love. The Silk Road brings life. If you're smart, try to control The Silk Road. Unlike &quot;The Republic&quot; DLC where you establish trade posts via sea, you can establish trade posts in counties that The Silk Road flows through. NOTE: You do NOT need The Republic to build a mass mongol trading horde. Seriously guys, control the Silk Road. Just do it.<br><br>3) Tribute.<br>WHAT I'VE ALWAYS WANTED HAS ARRIVED. As a nomad you have the option to declare war on a nation to force it to pay you tribute. If you win, they will pay you 40% of their monthly earnings. NOTE: If you either just want more money (which who doesnt), or want to weaken a nations economy before you launch a full-fledged assault, this is a very good idea.<br><br>4) Population.<br>Army size is no longer dependent upon how advanced your holdings are. In the top right stat bar you have 2 new things. You have &quot;Population&quot; and &quot;Manpower&quot;. Population shows you how many people you have in your territory and Manpower shows you how many people in your population are elligable to fight. (I think its like anywhere from 15-33% of your population is your manpower, please dont get angry if im wrong). Population is very important!! If you have a low population, you can't raise many troops and you get bulldozed.<br><br>7) Population wars.<br>In most nomadic counties there are no buildings whatsoever and all you can build is a temple. I don't suggest this however, because it will take land where your people live, aka, your population will decrease. Counties can only hold a certain ammount of people, and if your population grows over your cap, you have some problems on your hands. To solve this problem, you can now declare war to gain more land for your people to live on. <br><br>8) Build holdings in counties.<br>Nuff said.<br><br>9) Hordes.<br>This was the hardest thing for me to learn, mainly because I never got &quot;The Legacy of Rome&quot; DLC. Hordes are basically just a standing retinue. They are more important than retinues though, for the fact that you cannot raise troops from your demense (because counties dont have buildings). IMPORTANT: Every single start I had, I begun with a large horde, which my economy could not support. I highly suggest immediately disbanding your horde at the beginning of the game and buying a cheaper one!<br><br>That's all I can recall right now. I will update this if I find other things that can help. Please don't get angry at me if some things aren't 100% accurate!
