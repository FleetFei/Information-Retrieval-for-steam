The AI still sometimes bugs out and either decides to become a pacifist or starts entering and getting out of a building non-stop. I haven't found enemies spawning on the same places, but I find an optional mission that's bugged. Around the start, you can inspect some locations and might get information about a train that's coming with stuff. After knowing this I went right ahead to whoop their asses, then kept searching locations... And one of the locals told me about said train, and the game gave me the same optional objective, I played the same mission, I got the same rewards, there was the same lines of dialogue, it was literally the same thing twice. I don't think that was supposed to happen.<br><br>Second thing is that the narrative is changed on this extra episode, although I might have to cover this all with a spoiler. <span class=\"bb_spoiler\"><span> So, in the base game, everything is narrated by Death. You kinda get used to it thanks to the voice of the narrator nailing every damn quote so well. Well... This chapter doesn't have that at all. It's kinda odd they'd do a change like this, but it isn't really THAT bad as thankfully, both voice actors do a good job as well.<\/span><\/span><br><br>The last negative thing I could find about the DLC is that one moment is not quite explained well and felt bugged as hell... But it's on the last level, so I'm gonna cover that too. <span class=\"bb_spoiler\"><span> So, at the second part of the last stage, you start with a simple looking objective. Either kill the doctor or subdue him. Now I didn't personally felt like he was THAT much of an \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665, so I tried to subdue him... But clicking subdue doesn't automatically select the nearest enemy like it does in the rest of the ENTIRE GAME, instead you gotta select subdue AND THEN select the doctor. I almost killed him because of that! I had to search online if it was truly broken or not, and I bet more people will be confused once they get there. Also, on that same stage, I found really weird and lame how the last objective appears. You were tasked with killing everyone, and just as you kill the last guy, a door opens with around 8 more guys. You're only required to kill one of them, but that's not the deal, the deal is that they appear out of nowhere and might kill any important character suddenly in a rather unfair way. Would have been great if the game gave you one turn to actually reposition yourself before you get any important character to the story one-shoted and you're forced to play THROUGH THE TWO CHAPTERS AGAIN just for something you couldn't have predicted. <\/span><\/span><br><br>That being said, I'm actually rather impressed. The developers could have taken the easy way and made a DLC that's just sh#t, but instead for a really low price you get quite a lot of content.<br><br>The story of the chapter is quite interesting. It's not as tied with the rest of the game, just has a few references and a recycled map, but it takes a different approach to the land they builded on the main game, mainly talking about (you probably knew already by the title of the DLC) freedom. And the three main characters of it are interesting, just wondering why the other 6 or 7 characters on it get near to nothing about themselves, something the game itself is guilty of.<br><br>The few new aditions are also cool. There might be more, but I found only one new weapon, however I found myself using it a lot (a pistol with rifle rounds that deals low damage but deals it no matter what cover the enemy has), and the whole card system has gotten a <span class=\"bb_spoiler\"><span>Frankenstein<\/span><\/span> remake that I liked it a lot. Well... There's one thing that was changed and I liked. There's more ricochet targets, and you'll need them in some maps as there's more cover than before as well. And I like that! I think I wouldn't have gotten the Trickshooter achievement on the base game, here I got it without even trying.<br><br>Lastly, the lenght of the DLC chapter is actually quite good. I believe it took me a bit more time to finish than some of the base game's chapters, which is great value. It doesn't have that much replayability, but hey.<br><br>Do I recommend this DLC? Yeah! It could be better, it could fix the base game's issues, but at the same time it could be WAY more expensive and be a pain in the #ss. 3\u20ac is a reasonable price already, and I got it during a sale for even less than half of that. I think it's cheap enough as it is, however if you don't yet have the game and are just checking if the DLC is worth it, I'd say wait until both are on a sale, pick the whole package (and the soundtrack too, if you're into that).<br><br>TL;DR Edition: A cheap DLC that adds a quite good chapter. It doesn't fix pretty much any of the base game's flaws but it's still fun to play through. Kinda sad there's not more of these, but I'd gladly recommend it, even at base price.
