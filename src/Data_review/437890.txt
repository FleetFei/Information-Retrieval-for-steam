rough, yet very compelling space adventure. Tasked to explore space, establish alien relationships and discover the whereabouts of a missing craft, a considerable amount of time will be required to see everything this game has to offer. <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay<\/div><u><b>Galaxy Exploration<\/b><\/u><br>Ordered to locate the disconnected Aurora 1, the Aurora 2 is deployed, taking heed that a successful journey through the Star Systems require resources, relationships, and information. A large galaxy map outfitted with a coordinate system contains many Star Systems, creating the world in which the Aurora 2 will navigate and explore. <br><br>Upon completing the extremely lacking \u201ctutorial\u201d, intel lead the crew of the Aurora 2 to the Klisk\u2019s home world. As a race known for working as space merchants, with a very strict code of honor, the first step towards finding the missing craft is achieved with an exchange of favors. A quick escort quest rewards locational information of the major races\u2019 home worlds. With no restriction on exploration, the player is then free to lead the Aurora 2.<br><br><u><b>Star System Exploration<\/b><\/u><br>When traversing the galaxy map, Star Systems provide locations of major interest. Inside are fully instanced, \u201cgalaxy maps\u201d of a smaller scale. Star Systems are comprised of: gas giants, planets, moons, ships, etc. Each object of interest may be scanned or interacted with. Ships (if friendly) can sell goods or information. Planets, moons and other non-gas giant objects can be scouted using a Landing Party. <br><br><u><b>Scouting &amp; Landing Party<\/b><\/u><br>If a planetary object allows for landing, a quick scan will allow the player to choose the location of landing. A small map will aid in determining whether landing is beneficial by marking resources (needed to repair the ship, etc.), life (for research purposes) or key points of interest. Upon landing, a terrain will be generated and the Landing Party will begin their exploration. <br><br>The Landing Party is comprised of variable crew members (Scientists, Engineers, and Military Personnel). Their weapons and inventory may be personalized before deployment. Unless the planet is oxygenated, a decaying oxygen meter will dictate how much oxygen is remaining before a return to the spacecraft is necessary for replenishment. The Landing Party is controlled as a singular unit. A numerical health indicator represents the life of a crew member. Once that number fully depletes, a crew member will die and the number will reset until all crew members are dead. <br><br>Each planetary object will have a different terrain\/biome. Some will be inhabitable, eliminating the need to utilize an oxygen reserve. Others will have environmental hazards or creatures that will attack upon sight. A flat, rectangular map is provided to mark the relative locations of interest, including the shuttle. Although flat, the boundaries of the map do overlap to create a continuous seam. Regarding planetary exploration, a few minor grievances experienced are listed below.<br><br><b>Planetary Exploration Grievances<\/b>:<br><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li>Portions of the terrain are un-crossable, yet are not distinguished in any way from places that can be crossed<br><\/li><li>Meteor showers create an un-skippable animation that occurs <i>every<\/i> turn<br><\/li><li>No motivation to promote physical exploration of the planet, as opposed to re-positioning the Landing Party<\/li><\/ul><br><u><b>Battle System<\/b><\/u><br>If a hostile creature\/ship is discovered, it can be defeated utilizing the crews\u2019\/ship\u2019s weapons. The battle system is a hybrid between real-time and turn based systems. Upon encounter, pressing the \u201cFire\u201d button will project a grid of the applicable range. If in range, enemy targets may be individually attacked. Running away (by physically moving the Landing Party\/ship) may result in some free hits from the enemy. <br><br><b>Game Saves<\/b><br>If the ship happens to be destroyed, the game will end. To alleviate this burden, AuroraRL maintains an autosave feature, along with 4 dedicated save slots that can be used at any time. If a save is utilized during a dialogue option, the re-load will start the dialogue from the beginning.<br><br><u><b>Maintenance &amp; Research<\/b><\/u><br>The ship hull starts with 10 pips of health by default. If damaged by an enemy ship, repair is performed by assigning Engineers to repair the hull, along with utilizing resources (bought or discovered from planetary landings). Research, which rewards the player with new weapons, maps, etc. are progressed by assigning Scientists to a particular task. There are only a finite number of Scientists\/Engineers, and if deployed on a task, they may not be used in a Landing Party.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Presentation<\/div><u><b>Graphics<\/b><\/u><br>The graphics are somewhat lacking in regards to the planetary terrain and outer space. The screenshots in the Store description will help provide some insight. During important discussions\/plot-related events, or dialogue, higher quality retro style images will be used. Although the graphics leave a lot to desire, it is a far cry from being bad, just sufficient for immersion and plot progression.<br><br><u><b>HUD<\/b><\/u><br>The HUD needs to be reworked to improve the quality of gameplay. During space exploration, it is a bit tedious to continually hunt down the information needed from a quest log, or to assign crew members to a task. During planetary exploration, the HUD will sometimes block the view of a creature in line of sight, creating an opportunity for ranged damage if the player continues moving the party. An auto-hide feature would remedy this particular problem. <br><br>Every screen in AuroraRL, with the exception of the space background, is presented through a window (similar to a computer screen window). It would be more immersive if the dialogue and menus would be more incorporated into the background\/game. Also, not allowing \u201cEsc\u201d to exit a window also adds tedium to the gameplay. <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Conclusion<\/div><u><b>Early Access<\/b><\/u><br>For those that are not aware of the Early Access concept, it is perfectly acceptable for bugs to be present (only minor ones observed) and game mechanics to be missing. AuroraRL is still relatively new, and a developer update was released last month. <br><br><u><b>Verdict<\/b><\/u><br>With everything taken into consideration, AuroraRL is definitely recommended to those that are fans of space adventures.
