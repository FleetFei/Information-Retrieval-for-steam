Much to my delight, it shot beyond both of those desires and kept me hooked for two days straight to finish all of the levels.<br><br>Let me be upfront here: I have never played a resource manager game. I tend to be awful at real-time strategy games, and there is indeed a time limit for ranking purposes. Thank goodness it does not result in game overs, or I would have gotten frustrated with it. Instead, I found it a lot of fun to piece together how to best beat the levels and gather a ton of resources. This was my first experience with the genre, and I could not have asked for a better introduction.<br><br><b><u>Pros:<\/u><\/b><br><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li>Hilarious dialogue and story-telling. Check out the character profiles for some really amusing writing. Many of them can be linked to old ones with a slight change, but others are completely new. Both are welcome changes when they arrive, keeping the game from getting stale.<br><\/li><li>There are forty-five levels with no two being quite the same. Fifteen more are added to the collector's edition, making for sixty levels total. This length is great, with it taking me about seventeen hours (of my current eighteen) to beat them all. People used to this genre could probably be quicker at it.<br><\/li><li>Rather than having to play through the game again to get to levels, you can choose to replay each level from the map. It's great if you missed one of the hidden objects or want to try getting a top rank for a stage.<br><\/li><li>Despite being very easy to learn, it is difficult to master. I love games like this. It lets casual players have fun with simply playing it, and it has the difficulty needed to entertain those who want a challenge.<br><\/li><li>It ran smoothly for me without much lag. I played on Windows 7. It's not a huge game in terms of MB size, either, so that's a bonus.<br><\/li><li>The art is charming, and the graphics are pretty good. You even get some full-fledged art screens during important parts of the story.<br><\/li><li>Most of the voice acting is great. The one exception for me is so hilarious that I forgive it by default.<br><\/li><li>There are hidden collectables in each level. With four different types to look for, and at least one being in every level, this is great for people who enjoy hidden object challenges (like me.) They are just for fun, so anyone who does not want to can safely ignore them.<br><\/li><li>If you need help, there is a very handy strategy guide included in this edition. I did not use it for my run, but I checked it out after I was done and found it to be quite useful.<br><\/li><li>The achievements are, for the most part, not too difficult to obtain. I enjoyed collecting them and am glad they are here.<br><\/li><li>You have a zombie butler named Rufus. Really, that should be the only justification you need for buying this game.<\/li><\/ul><br><b><u>Neutral:<\/u><\/b><br><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li>There are only five music tracks in the game. I actually like all five. However, they can get repetitive at times.<br><\/li><li>Some of the pop culture references could be seen as either amusing or groan-worthy.<br><\/li><li>The &quot;man runs from woman because she is ugly&quot; cliche could be considered present here. However, given the personality this woman has, I can understand why Dracula is freaked out by her. His fault for trusting a dating site.<br><\/li><li>There are no trading cards. I found this to be really annoying and not much fun. Thankfully, this only occurs in two levels and never shows up again.<br><\/li><li>For me, it is difficult to tell which ruined buildings are which without hovering over them. The zombie noises when you give them commands get tedious after a while.<br><\/li><li>There are no names for each level. But I also admit that I was glad for the excuse, because I was hooked. I even plan on going back to try and get some better ranks in the levels. I had an absolute blast with this game, especially with it having legitimately good humor.<br><br><b>Rating:<\/b><br><br>9\/10. No really, I tried to find a justification for rating it lower than that and I couldn't. That's how much fun I had.
