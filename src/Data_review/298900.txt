in the early 90's when I played it on my Commodore Amiga.  When I saw the first video for this game I was really excited.  There is little doubt in my mind that 40k fans will enjoy this game more than newcomers, but it may well serve to draw newcomers into the grimdark of the 40k universe.  I can agree with those who say that the launch was anything but stellar, but happily the game has come along way since then and there is more to come still.  There are, at the time of this writing, still a few minor bugs in the SP, but nothing game breaking.  For example, some of the relics one can collect in the SP sometimes fail to load and, while annoying, this can be overcome by simply reloading the most recent autosave and more often than not the missing relic will be back.<br>At its heart, Space Hulk:  Deathwing is a First Person Shooter that uses a well worn formula similar to the Left 4 Dead series.  As a long term gamer, this neither surprised nor did it bother me.  To me, a shooter is a shooter!  What makes SP:  DW stand out is the level of detail put into all the maps.  40k fans are in for a visual feast, plus, you get to shoot hordes of genestealers.  Personally, I enjoyed the Single Player campaign, but the game really shines in coop mode.  Many complain about lack of people online to play with, but I prefer to play with Steam friends so that really does not bother me, but it is something to be aware of if you are the sort of player who just likes to jump into random games (I typically don't).  <br>The weapons look and perform well, generally speaking, and once you get a feel for how they work (and how they don't work) chances are you will stick with the one that gives the most satisfying &quot;kill feel&quot; (my favorites are the Hellfire Storm Bolter and the Plasma Cannon).  In single player, the squad A.I. is not the best, but it does not take long to figure out a way to make your squad mates useful.  The Apothecary is a decent shot and the heavy weapons brother is at his best when you give him melee weapons.  <br>The enemies come at you in both waves (mostly), but also indivdually or in small groups.  Some have complained about the &quot;conga&quot; lines that often come at your squad down the cramped hallways, but this really is the essence of Space Hulk and is faithful to the original board game.  Playing the game on the harder levels is the best way to get the real SH experience.  You may be able to hold of a &quot;conga&quot; line one moment, but what if your weapon jams or you need to reload?  On easy the game provides little , if anything, in the way of challenge, but it is useful for those who wish to hunt for the relics or simply enjoy exploring the maps and take in the visual details (and there are plenty of those!).  On the harder levels you and your squad are much more vulnerable and the feeling of isolation and claustrophobia increases greatly, making for a truly immersive experince.<br>At this point, I find myself wanting more and I hope the developers will add more content to the game.  Steam's policy of refunding a game provided one has played it less than two hours suits this game well.  Provided all things work out on your PC (many have had problems initially, I never did so I can't really speak to that) chances are that within two hours you will know whether you will enjoy this game or not.
