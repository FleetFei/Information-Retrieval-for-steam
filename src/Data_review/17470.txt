Others wear their inspirations on their sleeves. Then there's the games that are just their inspirations.<br><br>Dead Space manages to be the latter, and still be more than the sum of its parts.<br><br>Dissecting its frankenstein nature, you'll find a setting that's 3 parts Event Horizon, 1 part Alien, with enemy types from The Thing, with gameplay straight from RE4 and colour filters straight from the Discount Cellophane Garbage Disposal. <br><br>EA Redwood, now Visceral, adds something to this derivative slurry, and that's commitment. Sheer belief in a butcher's vision carries Dead Spare where so many of its peers have failed. The dedication to aesthetic - where absolutely every player interaction and HUD is justified and explained ingame - if nothing else adds one metric hell of an aesthetic. <br><br>One of videogame's best GUIs is joined by some remarkably decent levels, that's never too mazelike nor too streamlined, which breakneck pacing sees you speeding through to great production values and lab-brewed SFX that manages to make lazer guns - traditionally the most pew pew of weapons - hit hard and hit heavy.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">&quot;Make us whole again.&quot;<\/div><i>- About half the NPCs<\/i><br><br>Enemy variety is much more than you'd expect. You have your worthless mook, your ranged mook, your fast mook, your faster mook, your fat pregnant tank mook who'll explode into a horde of &quot;Up yours&quot; fleshbugs who treat your health bar like candy while calling you Daddy, and a couple of minibosses besides.<br><br>You're given enough tools and mechanical aptitude that no situation feels like it's forcing you to take damage. From your comically-overpowered stasis to begin able to sprint backwards, to that every shot hitstuns an enemy for more than enough time to get past it, it hits all the right marks.<br><br>But post-industrial blood-splattered wonderland that Dead Space is, it's certainly not perfect.<br><br>The bosses suck. Shoot the big yellow glowing points at a midranged distance on our generically gory-looking fleshblobs. I'm fairly sure at least two of them are impossible without the hitscan weapons.<br><br>Speaking of which, half the weapons are absolute garbage. Sure, Line Gun, Pulse Rifle, Plasma Cutter, more than carry the game. But the Flamethrower and Disc Ripper are infamously awful, with the Force Gun not lagging far behind. The divide in usefulness is so wide it boggles the mind how this reached final release.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">&quot;I will leave you with my child.&quot;<\/div><i>- Dr. Mercer before unleashing Dead Space's Nemesis<\/i><br><br>The incentives to play multiple times are also pretty thin. It mostly just amounts to enemies jumping out of vents - only vents - and screaming you in typical monster closet fashion, which is hack talk for &quot;please be scared&quot;. Although some people couldn't finish the game due to being too scared, so what do I know.<br><br>Now, this is a PC port. And not a fantastic one. The control issues are fairly infamous, and even once you apply the fixes it's fairly janky to get used to. <br><br>Disable V-Sync, jack mouse sensitivity to full, navigate menus with keyboard only, treat actually aiming like ironsights and you have a setup, that while it takes some getting used to, makes the game a fair bit easier than it ought to be.<br><br>There's a couple of inherent quirks - not being able to strafe diagonally, or that you can't initiate a reload while walking but it's fine to walk while reloading. Odd little details like that, that once you adjust to aren't an issue. <br><br>Certainly not insurmountable issues for a PC port, and this is a PC port worth playing.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">&quot;Cut off their limbs.&quot;<\/div><i>- wall graffiti, followed by an audio log, followed by an ingame conversation, followed by a tutorial prompt<\/i><br><br>Dead Space is a mechanically-decent aesthetically-driven game that has a dream, or at least a nightmare, to share and does it well.
