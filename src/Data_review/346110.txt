Review<\/div>\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\t\t\t\t\t<div class=\"postedDate\">Posted: March 7\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t<div class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst of all, this review is from a singleplayer standpoint and directed towards newcomers to this game. I tried playing in a server once last year, and absolutely hated the experience. The PVP element seems to be mainly why this game has gotten so many negative reviews. But instead of getting stomped all over in the servers by the trolls &amp; alpha tribes, if any of you are interested in playing by yourself or on LAN, read on!<br><br>All I heard about this game when it first came out was that it was really poorly optimised and in early access. So I avoided it like the plague. Until last year, when I started watching Let's Plays on Youtube, and I fell in love. It still is the game I play the most out of all my games. Last week I tamed a T-rex for the first time. It felt awesome. Yes there is so much grind, and yes I do have all the XP\/taming rates turned up, and it still is a grind. But damn, it's a fun grind. And I learned so much about dinosaurs in the process! It makes the inner dino-obsessed little girl in me so happy :)<br><br>You start out naked and alone, and with tools you craft yourself you slowly build up a base. Location is everything. In the north, the cold and large carnivores will defeat you very quickly. But in the south, the weather is milder and the dinos are not so big and scary. Taming dinos is fun and frightening, and often frustrating but it makes the grind a lot easier as different dinos help you to gather different materials with ease and of course help protect you. I used to wonder what the point of this game is; you grind to get better gear, and then you grind some more. But if you enjoy the grind and being scared out of your wits most of the time, then you will love this game and get a very rewarding experience out of it. <br><br>But it still is very brutal. Sometimes you spend so long taming something, or building a base, only to die and lose it all in an instant. Be prepared to be very very frustrated, it definitely is one of the most challenging games I have ever played. The original Island map is fun and massive to explore, but the free Center expansion map is even bigger and much more beautiful and varied.<br><br>There are caves you can explore underwater and the devs are adding more and more aggressive water dinos that make it very challenging and rewarding to explore the seas. If you make it to end game, there are several artifacts you can loot in dangerous caves you have to fight your way out of, and there are bosses you can summon &amp; take on for very big rewards. You can take your time, and gather &amp; build, or you can take out the big guns, level up, tame high tier dinos, expand and technologically improve your base. There is an insane amount you can do, and you can play it your way without being restricted by a linear storyline.<br><br>Now on to some cons, it is super glitchy sometimes. The worst one is when you get stuck on knocked out dinos, or rocks, or in doors, or.. really anything. It is frustrating and sometimes all you can really do to fix it is to wait it out, break the rock you are stuck in (if you're lucky), or log out &amp; log back in again (which takes forever for me). This doesn't happen often but when it does, it is very irritating. It is also a common sight to see wild dinos stuck in trees, which makes taming them easy &amp; cheat-y. Though some of the weird graphic glitches can actually be quite funny :) Another thing I dislike about this game is the building mechanics. It is not intuitive at all, and sometimes you can't build or place certain items &quot;because Ark&quot;. This means that you frequently have to tear down what you have built, which ends up costing you half the materials you used to make it.<br><br>And the other thing I really dislike about this is the fact that the game is STILL in early access. I was very disappointed when the devs made the Scorched Earth map a paid DLC. Not that it isn't fun to play, it really is - but I felt like they really should have finished the main game first before making DLCs. There is a new minor update almost every time I play this game, which is a bit annoying as you have to download it every time - but all that they seem to be doing recently are adding more and more interesting &amp; slightly OP dinos, which is great, but makes some of the older dino models now obsolete. I really wish they would focus more on ironing out all the little problems with the base game rather than adding more and more new dinos. I would love to also see some new and varied character designs &amp; more in depth customisation on the way your character looks &amp; sounds (the devs are now sponsoring mod makers to develop their mods, which I think is great news for Ark).<br><br>Even after all these flaws, I still keep coming back for more. If you love survival games, exploring, crafting, building, gardening, breeding (!), fighting, and let's not forget - if you have any sort of interest in dinosaurs, you will love this game. Make sure you have a good PC to run this and start taming!
