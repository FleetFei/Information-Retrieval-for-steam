Now that that's out of the way let me just say in it's current state (Posted during build 0.9.2) You don't need to know anything about the warhammer universe to enjoy this game. I didn't even realize till I was 12 hours in. Now if you're looking for a story, you're not going to find one just yet save for one of your own making. Which if that's your thing, you're going to get a fair amount of enjoyment out of this game. The fights are fun and work well for me. (Though I have a bad computer and suffer horrible lag issues) But even so with the right settings the game still manages to run a decent 15 to 20 frames on the open sea.<br><br>I've only played human so far, but there is a Chaos campaign as well. Which you are pretty much stuck being the bad guy. But as human you have total freedom. There are plenty of missions to keep you busy as a human, and plenty of trading opportunities. I recomend getting the three trading based perks as soon as possible if you can't keep track of what ports are buying and selling high or low. But bare in mind your max level is 10. (Unless it's been updated since the guide was posted) Speaking of which I recomend reading that in the steam tab ingame. It's great help for beginners, but a few things are outdated.<br><br>Sea monsters are fun and challanging fights as well. And overal i'm pleased with that this game is so far, and hope the devs continue to work on it. But as for the negatives. There's a few. The missions can start to feel samey, and it's got a fair amount of bugs. But not enough to ruin the full experience. Boarding is a bit OP if costly to your crew if you're not careful, and can be done as long as you're within range of a ship's side. But just spam strong attack with a decent weapon. Sooner or later you'll win. Next Armor is all cosmetic at the moment. FOR THE CAPTAIN! Ship armor makes a difference. And hope you like hoarding weapons and shields cause currently you can only sell goods. Not weapons shields or clothes. Thankfully they do not take up cargo space. Also, you're not told this in game, but water is gotten when it rains or when you dock at a port. I was confused for a long time how to get this.<br><br>Next the auto saves can get a bit cluttered so remember to delete ones you KNOW you're not gonna go back to, or it'll eat up space. And remember to save before doing a mission, some are bugged BADLY. And finally. The journal records every action you do in regards to ports that is of importance. While this is useful when remembering how much you bought something for when trying to sell it. Finding it becomes a pain when you have to go through a hundred pages to see your most recent purchace and or sale. My recomendation to the dev for fixing this is two fold. 1 Add a seperate tab for all financhal related stuff. And 2 give us the option to erase unimportant entries. Cause seriously. This thing fills up FAST. But that's all I have to say. If you can ignore those honestly minor negatives, I think you'll enjoy the game a lot. And at the very least get a few hours of fun out of it.<br><br>If you want a simple sailing game where you fight pirates and hostile creatures including megalodons sea dragons, wyverns, and also trade and sell goods. This is a good one for ya, as it's right to the point. And mind you, the fighting IS OPTIONAL and so is the trading. You can do WHATEVER YOU PLEASE! But if you want a story, or just MORE content. Maybe wait a bit on this one, but deffinetly keep it in mind. Sorry for the long review.
