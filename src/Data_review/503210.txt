I have a pretty decent computer so it\u2019s never dipped below sixty, but this tells me they really cared about everyone being able to play their game, which is a good first impression.<br><br>That continued as I actually started the game. Sentry Knight Tactics is a real-time, squad-based RPG not unlike Dawn of War 2 or Company of Heroes, albeit in a lesser format as you only have four members to control, not counting any guest characters who join you. At the start you have Knight the tank\/damage dealer, Priestess the healer\/buffer, and Marksman, who handles ranged attacks. After the initial area you recruit Rogue, who also attacks in melee but is more of a glass cannon. Those aren\u2019t typos, incidentally, as all of them don\u2019t seem to have names beyond their profession.<br><br>The game\u2019s combat is what made me think it was a mobile port at first. You select members of your team with the left mouse button, and move or target enemies with the right one. It\u2019s definitely something that could appear on mobile, but it controls well here, and there\u2019s several hot keys to select heroes if you need to do so quickly. Which you often will: each battle has multiple waves, and while the ones you run into on the overworld are as simple as you\u2019d expect, fights in dungeons (which are essentially just gauntlets of battles) can be tricky and keep you on your toes. You\u2019ll contend with enemies that can trap you, attack from range, heal other enemies and spawn more, and of course bosses, who have a lot of tricks up their sleeves. Thankfully if you\u2019re not used to this sort of gameplay, there\u2019s also an easier difficulty option available.<br><br>Naturally like any good RPG, you also have experience and loot to consider. Like the rest of the game, both are streamlined. The party gains experience as a whole, and each member gets a skill point to use when that happens. So it doesn\u2019t have that moment of frustration where you beat a boss right after a party member dies and doesn\u2019t get experience. Of course, if you ask me those moments are part of the experience of most of them, but that\u2019s what you give up sometimes for convenience.<br><br>After every battle you get at least one bag of loot, which may contain vendor trash, equipment, or items that can heal or boost characters in battle \u2013 these get used up automatically when their conditions are met, which is a little annoying since you have to equip them first.
