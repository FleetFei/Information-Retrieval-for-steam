Yes, please.<br><br>It has its own form of a politics system. There are a handful of Vampire clans and your standing with each one determines what kinds of supernatural powers you can develop (and other things).<br><br>The powers are well-thought out and enjoyable and, most importantly, very different. They're what carry the game, from a design standpoint. You have the mobility of a Vampire, able to jump great distances... past traps or upwards 15 feet to reach beyond human bounds. Although there are a lot of things I don't like about the game, I've been waiting for a long time for a Vampire game where you can pick up some alcohol-sodden bum, give him a battle axe and a taste of blood, and send him at your enemies. Bloodlust lets you do that.<br><br>I really wish someone would have slapped whoever was responsible for the name and said 'Come on!
