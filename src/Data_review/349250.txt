Let's face it, Boki isn't exactly the Bill Rizer or Lance Bean I was originally looking for, but the mobility and power combination mechanic displayed in the trailer were more than enough to pique my interest.  It sort of reminded me of Mega Man and Kirby in a way, but in the end it turns out Copy Kitty does a great job of being its own game.<br><br>The general story is that the main character Boki receives a virtual reality style combat simulator game as a present for her birthday from her uncle.  Boki is sucked into the simulator where she must battle her way through each level.  Despite not being particularly confident in her abilities, it turns out she is quite powerful.  Other than her wicked kung-fu kicks, she is able to take powers from defeated enemies and make use of them herself.  Boki can hold three powers at a time and can use them individually or combine them all together for a unique power attack.  In addition to the powers, there are also power-up items that bestow assorted temporary abilities.  The various powers not only serve as weapons, but also as tools for navigating stages.<br><br>In story mode each level is divided up into roughly ten stages.  The stages are short, fast paced, and filled with combat.  Unlike some platformers where the goal is to navigate your way to the the end of each level, in Copy Kitty the objective is to destroy the enemies.  It's a game that is much more focused on combat than precise jumps with instant death pits and spikes.  As of writing this I've almost completed the game on normal as Boki and I don't recall seeing any instant death hazards at all.  Boki is easy to control (though I do recommend using a gamepad) and combat is satisfying. You can also eventually play as Boki's Uncle Savant.  I haven't experimented too much with him yet but he has a completely different play-style.  Other than story mode, the game also features a randomly generated endless mode, and a level editor to create your own stages.<br><br>As far as presentation goes, the game looks good for the most part.  The enemies are creative and there are some incredible boss battles.  During combat there are colorful explosions and rainbow bursts all over the place.  The only criticism I have regarding graphics is that I think there is room for improvement in the way some of the stages appear.  The majority of the stages look good but overall I felt that Boki, the enemies, and the bosses all seemed to be a lot more visually polished in comparison.  The soundtrack is solid and I've had a few of the songs stuck in my head since I started playing.  It's worth mention that the game features an awesome extras menu with a detailed bestiary, concept art, sound-test, and play stats.  Why don't more games have bestiaries?<br><br>Even though it's in early access, Copy Kitty is a very solid game right meow.  There is already a lot of content and the developers are planning to add two additional worlds.  According to the store page the game should leave early access about a year from now.  I'm definitely looking forward to see what will change between now and then as I feel the offered package is already strong.  The available content is certainly worth the asking price of 9.99 and I'd recommend it to people looking for an action platformer that leans more towards the action side than it does platforming.<br><br>Edit: Started hard mode, it kicked the explosions up to 11.
