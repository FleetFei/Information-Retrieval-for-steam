That's just an unfair assessment and this game has gotten most of it's hate simply for that reason.  Since this game is a hack 'n slash action RPG, then that's what I will be comparing it to.  So games such as Champions of Norrath or Dungeon Siege 3 since those are ones I've played in the past.<br><br>This game is actually pretty decent.  It accomplishes what it's trying to do, which is provide a fun hack 'n slash gameplay.  It's a simple system that anyone can easily jump into and be good at.  You're wiping out big groups of enemies, using spells or combat arts for more wide spread destruction, performing executions on knocked down enemies, evading attacks, and it all happens with just a few buttons.  There's no complicated control scheme of having to hold one button in order to open up alternative attacks or something.  It's just a fast paced, simplified system that allows you to get into the action and accomplish things in a visually pleasing way.<br><br>And visually, this game is gorgeous.  The environments are extremely well done.  A lot of detail was put into this world and it's something that you can just stop and admire.  The character design is also good, but maybe a bit lacking.  I suppose it's understandable since the camera view is always pulled away and so you don't get a good look at the characters anyway.  As I mentioned, the combat is visually pleasing.  With different colors happening on the screen that are associated with your weapon swings or the different abilities.  It's a very pretty game.<br><br>So if the game is nice to look at and it's a decent hack 'n slash gameplay, then you're probably wondering why a lot of people hate it.  The biggest problem this game has is there's no real depth to it.  Every good action RPG of this sort will always have the ability to progress your character.  They will allow you to level up the character and distribute attribute points, you will collect &quot;loot&quot; or equipment to further improve your character, you will be able to change their appearance, etc.  Sacred 3 has none of that.<br><br>Character development is pretty much non-existent.  You do gain experience points and level up but it's never shown if that actually does anything.  There is no stats to say how strong your character might be or anything.  So the only purpose I've seen with leveling up is to meet the level requirements to unlock new skills.  These skills, combat arts, spells, whatever you may want to call them, are one of the very few customization that you can do.  You're only allowed to have two skills equipped and you get to choose which ones you want from a handful of them.  You can also upgrade each skill to make it more powerful.  The same thing applies with weapons you obtain.  You don't find any loot in this game but instead you get an item drop from a level boss.  These weapons can be upgraded in the same way as the skills.  There is also &quot;weapon spirits&quot;, which for the most part are kind of useless, and they mostly will annoy you.<br><br>Which brings us to the voice acting.  In general, it's not too bad.  They won't be winning any awards for their performance but I've definitely heard a lot worse.  Some characters aren't as good as others and will probably make you laugh at how bad it comes across.  But the worst part about the voice acting is they don't know when to shut up.  Whether you're being told what to do by the guide character, or you're being taunted by the enemy, or just the constant ramblings from your weapon spirit, it gets a bit annoying.  On top of that, somebody thought it was a great idea to include jokes in almost everything that is said.  Some of the humor is good but a lot of it is just kinda meh, and it gets irritating after a bit when almost everything is being said either in a joking manner or they try to slip a joke into the line of dialogue.  Such as the narrator, for example.  The purpose of a narrator is to explain what is happening to progress the story.  But this one is tossing in joke after joke and you just get tired of it and don't want to listen to him anymore.<br><br>The lack of depth that I mentioned also applies to the gameplay itself.  There is so much potential that is clearly visible with this game, but, whether by choice or not, they stripped it down to the absolute basic stuff.  The environments are so detailed and there is little pathways or doorways and stuff and it makes you wish you could go explore in that direction.  But there's really no deviating from the main path.  For example, in Champions of Norrath there was caves and dungeons scattered around the map and multiple pathways you could take.  Sacred 3 has a linear path that you follow with the odd side path going into a dead end that has a chest of gold.  It's very bare bones and stripped down to just going along that path and slashing away at enemies.<br><br>In conclusion, I do recommend this game but there's some conditions that go with it.  For what it is, it's a fun hack 'n slash game.  It allows you to kill swarms of enemies with a relatively decent combat system.  There's just not much to it and basically what you see in the first 5 minutes of the game is what you'll be doing for the next however many hours.  It's not a game to sit down and pull an all nighter with because you'll probably be bored after an hour or two.  So if you're looking for something simple when you need a break from everything, then this is the game to do that.<br><br>As a side note...this really shouldn't have been called Sacred 3.  If they wanted to go down this style of gameplay then it should have been a spin-off game.  A lot of people hate on this game for the sole reason that it's not like the previous two.
