Giveaway x10<\/div><br><i>NOTE: keys provided by developer<\/i><br><br>In Lost Moon, you're an astronaut stranded on an alien planet. Other than that basic premise, everything else is a mystery you'll have to uncover along the way. There are no tutorials or clues or prompts of any sort. This game does not hold your hand in any way whatsoever.<br><br>Gameplay is a blend of tough puzzles, basic exploration and challenging boss encounters. Fans of metroidvania type platformers should find a lot to love here.<br><br>Don't let the adorable graphics fool you, the puzzles here are quite devious even if they are never cryptic. There are only so many things our little space cadet can do so solving them is a fun and rewarding process of elimination.<br><br>Staying with the topic of difficulty, bosses are no pushover either. The first boss handed my \u2665\u2665\u2665 to me on a plate. Similar to the tough-but-fair puzzles though, once you realize the method required to defeat them, it's just a matter of timing your actions right. It's an addictive &quot;just one more go...&quot; process.<br><br>One mechanic that fits the story and setting but otherwise seems a tad unnecessary to me is the oxygen timer. If you're running low, you can either retrace your steps to the nearest oxygen chamber checkpoint or save yourself a few steps by letting it empty, which will kill you and return you to that checkpoint anyway.<br><br>Graphics and animations are very reminiscent of the NES era and have a cute sci-fi look to them that I really like. The mini-map is automatically populated as you progress through each small area. Because of the jump-right-in, no-frills gameplay and the low number of objects on each screen, I can't help but think this game would be equally suitable for handheld gaming.<br><br>There is a slight delay built into the jumps and shooting, so it's no gung-ho affair.
