Turn based, HP, MP, etc etc. Instead of elements instead all of the attacks and enemies are themed by color, with an easy to remember rock\/paper\/scissor mechanic. Red beats Green beats Blue beats Red. Every enemy with a few exceptions has a color, which either makes up most of their color scheme, or one part that changes between different versions (eyes, core, etc). Your characters are also colored, determining what their natural color is, as well as what most of their attacks are. All three get attacks of the other colors, along with buffs\/debuffs and heals. The strategy elements of battles are a bit fluid early on, but the game does become a bit rote later. Pretty much every random encounter is finished by AOE-ng all three colors, and bosses end up being a fairly simple routine of buff up speed\/def\/attack, use all your big attacks that hit their color until they go down.  With how short it is though, it doesn't have long to sit at that level of gameplay, so it works out well enough.<br><br>Story wise, it doesn't break any new ground or do anything truly amazing, again, it fits the amount of time you spend, doesn't feel rushed or over-extended. Perspective is a bit wonky in places, bookshelves don't have tops, etc. The biggest is that they clearly used the same sized sprites as battle enemies, and when blown up they look particularly bad. Even the first Final Fantasy used larger, more detailed sprites for enemies. Nothing major, the game still looks nice, just nitpicking.<br><br>So overall, a good game, not amazing or groundbreaking, but short and sweet.
