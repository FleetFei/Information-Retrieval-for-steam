was provided by the studio.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">ESPRESSO REVIEW<\/div><br>Stranded in orbit around the planet, you must survive in a damaged EVA suit after your space station explodes.  Traverse the haunting debris of your former space station as you carefully pilot your EVA suit on limited oxygen while unravelling  the story of what led to the destruction of your crew.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">PROS<\/div>Stunning visuals<br>Very detailed game environment that simulates space and the damaged space station<br>Increasingly tense gameplay as you progress<br>Free roam to complete the mission as you wish<br> <br><div class=\"bb_h1\">CONS<\/div>Short gameplay<br>No puzzles or deep story content<br>No scripted action sequences <br>Can be easy to get lost from one checkpoint to the next<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">FRENCH PRESSED FULL CUP REVIEW<\/div><br>I grew up in a rural town with an open sky and stars to stare at most nights; no city lights to drown them out.  When the moon was dark, it was the best thing about living outdoors.  You can look up for hours at the Milky Way Galaxy if you find it intriguing.  Constellations, planets, nebulae, the occasional shooting star - sparkling and pulsating with light in the empty blackness.  So, when playing Adr1ft I looked back the other way, down at the planet amidst the debris from the sky.  It was breaktaking until you realize you will die soon without oxygen and need to begin surviving. Yes, there is a game and story here but the real draw is being an astronaut stranded in that dark firmament.  In my opinion, this is Adr1ft's greatest achievement  - creating a game that gives me that same sudden gasp of captivation when lying on my back staring at the night sky.   The amount of thought and detail into making the environment realistic is astounding.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">GAMEPLAY<\/div><br> After a brief tutorial on how to move around in an EVA space suit, you start out as a lone surviving astronaut floating in the rubble of a catastrophic space station explosion.  The whole station is torn to pieces and scattered in a trailing jumble of plants, solar cells, insulation, and metal.  It\u2019s your job to survive amidst this devastation and figure out a way to get back home. <br><br>The first order of business is getting oxygen.  There is plenty around, it\u2019s grabbing it that takes some practice. I\u2019d say it\u2019s akin to catching a butterfly, except *i* am in the glass jar instead of the butterflies.  Often you are just shy of reaching it or you miss, overshoot, and pass right by it.  If you run out, you die and start over from the last checkpoint.  It\u2019s not overly stressful, but it does require some patience to get good at grabbing oxygen.   It comes in little spray-can sized canisters, so an essential part of the gameplay is surviving from one tiny container of oxygen to the next unless you locate a oxygen fill up pump or an EVA repair station.  Time is always running out on your oxygen, so it\u2019s constantly on your mind.  Not only is it needed to breathe, but it\u2019s your only method of propulsion, so it really doesn\u2019t last long before you need a refill.<br><br>You maneuver around what is left of the station and attempt to repair the cerebrum, it\u2019s control center. I was hoping for some survival gaming with crafting materials or stockpiling oxygen, but that\u2019s not the case.  It my opinion, the game doesn\u2019t feel like you are meant to camp out, but to get the missions done and move on.  While it would be a neat add-on to the game, I think survival crafting would likely slow the game down to a crawl, drastically affecting the pacing.<br><br>Missions are linear, but you have a free world to move about in to complete it.  At first, it was straightforward finding each checkpoint by using my little HUD on the screen and scanning for objects nearby.  At certain points, though, it\u2019s easy to get lost in the debris.  The areas just look all too similar and you end up traveling through a labyrinth of space station sections with bits and pieces of data recordings and oxygen strewn about.  So, after awhile in each mission I got utterly lost.  The best way to figure out where I needed to go was drift up and check for something big or blinking that I probably had not visited yet.  Sometimes I made it there, other times I would run out of oxygen and die, restarting over again.  This isn\u2019t too bad at first until you have to maneuver through dangerous areas, using up all your oxygen.  Traversing those areas is very fair, though, and it\u2019s more a matter of me not driving my EVA suit carefully  than it is hard or frustrating.  <br><br>Each mission consisted of repairing a portion of the space station by activating something and then locating a data core to fix it up.  There were no puzzles that I encountered or major cut scenes that disrupt the game.  From what I see, this is a first person explorer game.  The actions of exploration and using the suit while trying not to die is the whole of the gameplay.  You come across audio data files, crew member personal items, and collectable SSD\u2019s that give you insight into who your crew were and what your role was on the space station.  Towards the game\u2019s end, the story becomes forefront as you find more audio files and listen at monitor login stations more often than before.  Crew personal items are floating about here and there, and if you are lucky you come across some to collect.  I found about half of the them, but I was expecting some personal notes to read once I obtained them - that didn\u2019t happen.  All the personal moments came from the audio files and a few emails.  I\u2019m guessing the personal items are  more for achievements, but I could be wrong.  The story is mostly conveyed through the extremely well voice acted dialogue on the voice recordings.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">GRAPHICS<\/div><br>Often, despite my dangerous trek through my ninja-blendered space station, I\u2019d stop and look at the Earth below.  It was stunning. Especially, the night scenes where you see all of Australia lit up like an LED Ikea lamp.  You could say this game is eye candy, and you\u2019d be right.  I feel no shame is saying this game is worth playing just for the pretty factor.  It\u2019s gorgeous, absolutely gorgeous.  You may need to lower the settings a bit if your card cannot handle it, but I stuck my settings on high with my meager set up and enjoyed the scenery all the same.  As for crashes, I had only a few and it was at startup.  Once the game was rolling, at most I\u2019d get a hiccup frame here or there.  I did notice several hiccups in framerate happened before my controller vibrated.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">CONTROLS<\/div><br>I played this entirely with a Xbox 360 wireless controller; no issues to report. I\u2019d venture to say it\u2019s probably the better way to move about.  I wish I had a VR, this title SCREAMS play me in VR. With the immersive graphics, the single player movement, and motion of the EVA I would assume the VR look to the game is mind bending.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">SUMMARY<\/div><br>I wish I could say more without giving anything away, but the game is not exactly long.  I\u2019d say an average player would spend 5-7 hrs to complete this game.  I\u2019m a slow-poke who also tends to explore a bit, so I took 10 hrs with a few hours AFK in there.  The EVA Free option without worrying about oxygen is there if you need it.  I enjoyed the interesting juxtaposition of the green, vibrant plants amid the station's destruction in the vacuum of space.  It was so striking to travel through the obliterated pieces of the station followed by the peaceful dance of leaves floating about; the live plants defying the inherent carnage around me.<br>The story is well acted, yet very sparse to the point of barely being there.  It\u2019s all about exploring in an EVA suit and I think it hits the bullseye. Don\u2019t expect a survival exploration crafting game or a giant fleshed out story exposition.  What you should be looking for is a short but beautiful game about surviving in a space station explosion.
