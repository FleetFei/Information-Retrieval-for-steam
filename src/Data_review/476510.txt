free<\/div>\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t<div class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\t<div class=\"bb_h1\">Portable Description<\/div>Cally\u2019s Trials is the updated, roguelite version of Cally\u2019s Caves 2 that was released for free on the iOS Appstore, Google Play, and the Amazon Appstore. Permanent character and weapon upgrades, used alongside a leveling system, fuels this cute action-platformer through 110 levels of monster-packed mayhem. <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Story<\/div><u><b>Cally\u2019s Narrative<\/b><\/u><br>Set before the events of Cally's Caves 3, Herbert imprisons Cally\u2019s parents in his massive lair deep inside the Caves. It is up to the young child to fight through Herbert\u2019s nightmarish creations and rescue those she holds dear. Lloyd, a knowledgeable guitarist and occasionally helpful companion, aids Cally in her quest.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay<\/div><u><b>Roguelite Structure<\/b><\/u><br>Cally\u2019s Trials utilizes a roguelite structure \u2013 entire game must be completed in one continuous run due to \u201cperma-death\u201d (shortcuts can be unlocked). Avoiding the roguelike vs. roguelite semantics, the core mechanics and features defining Trials include:<br><br><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li>\u201dPerma-death\u201d (sometimes an area\/zone reset instead of a full reset upon game over).<br><\/li><li>Character and weapon leveling system that does not reset upon death.<br><\/li><li>Currency that does not reset upon death.<br><\/li><li>Permanently unlocked character abilities\/health: triple jump, damage boost, etc.<br><\/li><li>Unlockable\/purchasable shortcuts to each subsequent zone upon defeating the previous boss.<\/li><\/ul><br><u><b>Cally\u2019s World<\/b><\/u><br>Trials is a relatively vast world, containing 110 levels across 6 different zones each with a unique biome (water, ice, fire themes etc.). Cally\u2019s move-set includes 2-D movement, jump, double\/triple jumps, weapon fire, and sword swings (melee). Typical platformer elements are present, including: ledges, cliffs, stationary spikes, ranged\/melee enemies, platforms, and triggered events. Regrettably, each level is not procedurally generated; upon death and resetting, the player will be re-creating the exact same experience trying to get back to the previous point before dying. Enemy and coin placements are pre-determined. Loot chests, however, do award random items. Although repetitive at times, the levels are fortunately not too long and getting back to the death location should not be too difficult.<br><br>Roguelites\/roguelikes traditionally employ full resets upon death. Although Cally\u2019s Trials follows the same principles, it alleviates some of the mental anguish with such a system by using partial resets depending upon the manner of death. If Cally perishes by falling into water\/spikes or succumbs to a boss, she will reset at the beginning of that particular level, instead of the beginning of the entire game, with a portion of her health missing.<br><br>After defeating a zone\u2019s boss (6 zones\/biomes\/bosses total), Cally has the option to purchase a shortcut bypassing that zone. To utilize the shortcut, Cally must have the triple jump ability unlocked. The coins required to purchase these shortcuts will require a little bit of grinding in addition to a normal play through by the time a boss is defeated.<br><br><u><b>Combat<\/b><\/u><br>Cally fends herself with ranged attacks in the form of firing one of her guns, or with short melee attacks in the form of a sword swing. Both actions may be performed simultaneously. Cally\u2019s guns may be leveled up by continually using them (an experience bar indicating weapon usage is provided). Leveling up her guns have the possibility to increase: damage output, fire rate, accuracy or appearance. Specific gun levels act as milestones where a gun\u2019s physical appearance and bullet animations get upgraded. <br><br><b>Guns<\/b><br>There are 12 weapon classes to level up (pistol, shotgun, assault rifle, etc.) each specializing dominance in certain situations. Some of the main differences between each weapon class include: bullet damage, bullet scatter or projectile trajectory. Changing between weapons are instant which is a very helpful technique when jumping into a group of enemies. Although the fire button can be held down to automate shooting, doing so will lower the gun\u2019s fire rate. There is also infinite ammo.<br><br><b>Sword<\/b><br>Cally\u2019s sword protects her when enemies get too close for comfort. Unlike her guns, Cally\u2019s sword progresses differently. Sword upgrades do not follow a leveling system, but instead must be purchased. Her sword also has the ability to knock away most projectiles, handy for when sneaky shots are incoming.<br><br><u><b>Character Progression<\/b><\/u><br>Starting at level 1 with 3 hearts (each damage hit removes 1 heart), Cally progresses by leveling up. At each level up, Cally will fully heal herself, occasionally gaining another heart container. XP in order to level up is gained in the form of loot, dropped from defeated enemies. These loot drops may fall into the water or off of a cliff, so timing a kill is rather important. Lloyd\u2019s shop sells other useful items\/abilities (permanent and single-use) to help Cally with her progression. Some of these include: coin multiplier, damage boost, level up, sword enhancements, etc.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Controls<\/div><u><b>Controller Support<\/b><\/u><br>Although Cally\u2019s Trials has full controller support, the shoulder buttons generally will not be used for anything, and the D-pad is used by default for character movement. At the moment, it isn\u2019t possible to re-assign the button mapping for a controller, while using only a controller (DS3 used for this review). This makes it impossible to rebind movement to the analog sticks.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Conclusion<\/div><u><b>Possible Exploits<\/b><\/u><br>There are a couple, possibly unintended, \u201cexploits\u201d that may need to be addressed listed below.<br><br><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li>It is possible to continuously re-enter a level in order to repeatedly open a chest, skewing the expected gold-gain trend. Possible fixes to this include allowing a chest to only be opened once per life, or removing the ability to travel to a previous level\/zone.<br><\/li><li>After entering the shortcut for Zone 2, it is possible to go back and repeatedly kill Mama Bear. The issue is that it is too easy to defeat her this way because her back is turned. This also allows the player to easily level up the newly acquired assault rifle in Zone 3.<\/li><\/ul><br><u><b>Advised Suggestions<\/b><\/u><br>There are a couple suggestions that would improve gameplay; these are listed below.<br><br><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li>Allow camera control to a certain extent in order to see depth of a fall\/ledge location or to time a long-double jump.<br><\/li><li>Change game over screens to differentiate between water death vs full death.<br><\/li><li>Make Lloyd's tips optional, or present it as a hovering dialogue box instead of an abrupt screen change.<br><\/li><li>Add achievements for defeating bosses: all current achievements are based upon the number of eliminated enemies. <br><\/li><li>Procedural level generation (if possible).<\/li><\/ul><br><u><b>Verdict<\/b><\/u><br>With everything taken into consideration, Cally\u2019s Trials is definitely recommended to fans of the action-platformer or roguelite genres. It is very important to note that Trials will periodically feel a bit repetitive at times, but progression is really fast and simple.<br><br><table class=\"bb_table\"><tr><td>Review copy provided by
