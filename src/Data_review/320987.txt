The quality and consistency of the photographic images across Wales is very good and natural looking.  I would recommend this volume.<br><br>The other three volumes suffer from a lack of colour consistency, which Just Flight claim is down to the earlier quality of the photographic material used for these, and this tends to destroy any real sense of immersion ... be warned!   Unless flying in cloudy\/misty conditions with poor visibility to mask this then I would not recommend these.  Clear conditions, which most people would choose to admire the scenery of this type of product, reveal the general washed out colour and changing colour casts of photographed areas stitched together.  There are also noticable compression artifacts in a lot of the images.  <br><br>As with all photographic texture scenery you need to fly at a reasonable height (2000 ft. plus) to get the full effect and you need Anisotropic Filtering selected along with Anti-Aliasing.  I also tend to set the camera zoom to the minimum to improve the sharpness of the textures.  With this done the effect is pretty stunning with clear detailed terrain and almost a 3D effect on woodland areas etc.  Autogen is useless with photographic terrain as it's randomly generated and bares no relationship to the real locations;  so this is switched off by default.  Custom bought scenery will still display, just ensure its been designed to line up with photographic scenery products.  The UK2000 VFR and Extreme airport series work nicely with this product and really enhance it.   Products like Treescape can help to reduce the flat look of photographic scenery at lower altitudes but to be honest it looks better (more realistic) at higher altitudes without additions like these!<br><br>If only the other three volumes had been up to the quality of this one it would have been an amazing set.
