Roi<br><b>Status:<\/b>  Released<br><b>Developer:<\/b>  Sylvain Seccia<br><b>Publisher:<\/b>  Meridian4<br><b>Genre:<\/b>  2D Puzzle Platformer<br><b>Release Date:<\/b>  23rd of February, 2017<br><b>Type:<\/b>  Singleplayer <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">General Impression<\/div>Only a handful of Steam games focus on the French Revolution and none of them offer an alternate reality in which you might save Louis XVI of France from the guillotine. An indie developer sought to change that. I don\u2019t know if the name \u201cSylvain Seccia\u201d rings any bells to you but he\u2019s the talented albeit fallen from grace, developer of D\u00e9sir\u00e9. A more than decent 2D point &amp; click adventure title which was removed from the Steam Store after the developer was found guilty of using multiple accounts in order to type as many positive reviews for his game as he could. Huge mistake on his part. That\u2019s why there are supporting folks such as myself on Steam, Sylvain. Of course, minus the \u201cmultiple accounts and certain positive review ratings\u201d parts.<br><br>Still, both D\u00e9sir\u00e9 and Vive le Roi are the clear work of a dedicated individual which I shall judge objectively from the perspective of his most recent creation and not for his past shady activities. Almost everyone deserves a second chance and the publisher Meridian4 agreed to support Mr. Seccia. \u201cVive le Roi\u201d is French for \u201cLong live the King\u201d and our nameless protagonist shall ensure that his king will survive the revolutionary ordeal. In all honesty, it\u2019s hard to pick a side during the French Revolution since I don\u2019t find any sympathy for neither the ostentatious nobility nor the illiterate rabble which demanded the formers\u2019 heads.<br><br>The gameplay is pretty straightforward: avoid the guards\u2019 line of sight and reach the guillotine in order to save Louis the 16th. Naturally as you progress, the challenges will intensify, both in the number of foes to avoid and the complexity of the objects you must manipulate before reaching the royal hostage. The main objective, which also quantifies the number of stars you shall receive at the end each level, is to reach the king in as few clicks as possible. The in-game cross-shaped mouse pointer in Vive le Roi is similar to that in D\u00e9sir\u00e9 only that instead of red, it\u2019s colored in white most of the time or in blue once it needs to highlight certain interactable objects, such as barrels or switches. There are 30 levels in total and the maximum rating you can attain in each of them is composed out of 3 stars. Reaching a 2-star rating is not a problem most of the time but in order to reach 3 though, you need to plan your movement carefully based on the patrol pattern of the guards. If any of them so much catches a glimpse of you, they shall fire their smoothbore muskets in the air and wake the sleeping executioner who promptly \u201cshortens the royal neck\u201d, if you catch my drift.<br><br>The graphics might resemble a mix between Angry Birds and LIMBO, but Vive le Roi is more difficult than it seems. And that is a good thing if you ask me, since otherwise it would have become boring much faster than it already does. Repetition in gaming as in other topics, can never be considered a mark of distinction as far as entertainment is concerned. Enemies become more diversified only in the later stages near the end and by then, you would have mastered all the necessary tricks in order to outsmart the predictable AI. I\u2019m surprised this title isn\u2019t present on the mobile platforms yet, but time is not lost and I for one rarely play on my smartphone anyways.The game's Store Page mentions some mobile awards and nominations but I couldn't find Vive le Roi iOS or Android versions online. I will still recommend you offer the PC version a chance to test your patience and reflexes but be wary that there are no Steam auxiliaries yet (such as Achievements or Cards) and the price tag might a bit too high at the moment. Wait for an inevitable bundle or Seasonal Sale and you\u2019re all set to save the head of an unpopular ruler who decided to starve his subjects in the wrong place, at the wrong time. <br><br><div
