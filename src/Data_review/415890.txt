The game is fairly well-done except for some problems currently existing. I will add a TL;DR at the end.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"> Gameplay <\/div> <br>Crystal Picnic is an RPG designed with colourful creatures and interesting characters. You start off as Egbert a kind-hearted and pure Duck who has been trying to join the Royal Guard for quite some time now unsuccessfully, and he is with his good friend Frogbert, a common gardener who helps his friend. You are near the castle when all of a sudden ants dig out and walk right on by with crystals from the castle, which begins the main plot of the storyline.<br><br>Control-wise the game is fairly simple, however do note that the controls are not actually introduced to you at first. This makes it so that depending on how you play (Controller\/Keyboard) you will have to do a bit of a guessing game. The combat portion of the game is not like most RPG games with a turn-based mechanic, but rather has a real-time combat situation where you and your computer controlled companions will fight against whatever you come across. The combat is not bad at all, however the programming of your companions is another story. What would usually happen is that I would fight and my companion would end up running into the enemies usually getting themselves injured or killed. It became a tad bit frustrating and I would prefer it there was an option to not include them in combat (You would fight independently and if you fall you can come back as your companion to help minimize the mistakes a CPU is capable of making). The game also has a nice mini-game of whack-a-skunk and overall is a very playful and nice environment. The maps however can get a tad bit large and annoying to traverse especially with the ability I can always slip over a banana and get attacked by one group, then another, and one more just in case. Once you are able to grasp the controls nicely this becomes quite a relaxing and enjoyable experience for the more casual gamers, and the story is just a tad silly.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Graphics\/Sound<\/div> <br>For an pixelated RPG the graphics are rather impressive and overall polished for the characters and level design. Very reminiscent of the older days of gaming when it all started to develop. The music in this game is superb and I honestly loved it, it was perfect for the game and was able to make a very relaxing experience and improve the game's overall quality. The only problem is I'd like an option to disable all other sounds except the music at some points so I can just listen while playing.<br><br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Pros\/Cons <\/div> <br><b>Pros <\/b> <br><br>+ Very polished Graphically <br>+ Fun style of combat<br>+ Good relationship between Egbert and Forgbert making an authentic feeling of friendship between the two<br>+ Music is beautifully relaxing <br>+ Not hard<br><br><b>Cons <\/b> <br><br>- No introduction to controls<br>- Random combats can become annoying when you get caught in a loop of more than one battle with no time to even move<br>- No sort of map system or assistance with objectives <br>- Problem where to consume the potion\/item you want you have to first use whatever item is first then you can select the item you want<br>- No options menu<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Suggestions <\/div> <br>These are suggestion that I feel should be added to the game to polish it and make it more enjoyable<br><br>- Quick introduction to controls at the begging or an options menu where you can see the controls<br>- Option to fight independently in combat so you don't have to worry about your companion<br>- An objective assister or local map which can help guide you <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"> Final Thoughts <\/div> <br>Crystal Picnic has overall done a good job expect for the problems which have already been listed. I was able to enjoy my time playing this even with the little snips that gave me a bit of rage. The music always helped me to relax into this game and I honestly love Egbert and Frogbert's relationship, truly enjoyable there. The storyline was quite silly in my opinion, but I was a duck trying for the royal guard and whose best friends was a frog. Overall yeah I'd say this game was worth my time and be for a more casual gamer who simply want's to relax and enjoy something silly (I especially love the &quot;The Accused&quot; achievement showing the developers have a sense of humor).<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">TL;DR<\/div> <br>Although an enjoyable experience, Crystal Picnic has its fair share of issues. The most annoying being that there is no introduction to the controls. The game succeeds in its goal of feeling like a classic RPG from the simpler time of the gaming industry. The soundtracks helps to fully immerse you into the game and overall is one of the things I was able to love about this game. Crystal Picnic is definitely enjoyable for those who like the genre and are not too picky, <br> so I would recommend this to more casual gamers who are able to look at the work and effort seen in this During battles, you use sticks, stinky socks, and other so-called weapons to fend off against wolves, ants, pooping birds, and other enemies, all the while performing platforming feats. The battle sequences are unlike those I have experienced in other games, and always pose a challenge.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Visuals<\/div>I love the game's art style. I love it!<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Audio<\/div>The game has a pretty nice soundtrack. Each piece is appropriate and well-suited to its perspective area. I especially like the title screen music. No two boss battles play out the same way, so you always have to be on your toes if you do not want to die.
