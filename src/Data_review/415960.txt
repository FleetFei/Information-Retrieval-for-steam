Rather than looping immediately when a track is finished, it moves into a new one, and will eventually cycle through several tracks of high quality. Some are quick, but most of them take a lot of thinking to overcome. Randomly clicking around the level is unlikely to help much. Having to put your brain cells to good use is a requirement for great puzzle games, and Energy Cycle succeeds in this.<br><\/li><li>Time trial is a mode that has you see how many puzzles you can solve before the clock runs out. The puzzles are randomized, which makes it fun to replay.<br><\/li><li>Infinite mode brings randomized puzzles to the table, but without a timer or any sort of limit present. If the player does not want to deal with the potential stress of running out of time, this is a great option.<br><\/li><li>Achievements are present. Some of them are simple to get, while others require beating some tough levels and getting pretty far in time trial. I enjoy that combination of difficulities.<br><\/li><li>The price is absolutely reasonable. I have played three hours and probably have at least three more to go, if not more. That is a lot of fun gameplay for a base price of $0.99.<br><\/li><li>The game mascots are electric kitties. ADORABLE electric kitties.
