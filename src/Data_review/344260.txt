Yet I always end up picking the ones with tremendous emotional and ethical depth. <i>Agapan<\/i> is the latest example of my extraordinary luck.<br><br>Let's start with the meaning of this rather alien word: Agapan. Agapan means &quot;to be ready and alert, to be able to prevent a grievous event because of being alert&quot; in Tagalog, an Austronesian language with about 57 million speakers in the Philippines, according to Wikipedia. It also happens to be the name of the sleepy, backwater town our protagonist, David Garcia visits during the game. Interesting, eh?<br><br>Story starts up with David's annual visit to his mother's grave. We are told that David lost his mother at a very young age, and never knew his father. Luckily, he was adopted by a rich and attentive family and grew up happy. During his last visit to his mother's grave, a strange old lady appears and asks whether David would like to meet with his grandmother, Remedios Garcia. As the strange lady told us before: we have 4 days to learn.<br><br><i>Agapan<\/i> is an interactive story, with 12 distinct endings. 12! Wow, and I've been only able to discover 3 so far. On that part, it has huge replay value. The game is rather slow pacing, and the element of choice doesn't carry the consequential information before you make a choice, half the time. You take shots and hope that you are doing the right thing. On gameplay terms, it is quite simple. You keep visiting locations within the town during different times to progress. It is a bit repetitive and sometimes you end up stuck by missing some detail. Considering there is no walkthrough or guide to the game nowhere in the internet, good luck. My only advice is for you to be stubborn about it. Combined with beautiful soundtrack, eerie setting and the well-weaved story, your time is worth discovering <i>Agapan<\/i>!<br><br>Thanks to some detailed research, I discovered that <i>Agapan<\/i> was actually an interactive story board game created by the developer during his college years and the RPG Maker game is based on the board game.
