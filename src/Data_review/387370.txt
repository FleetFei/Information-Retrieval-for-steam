<\/div><br>Sol 0: Mars Colonization is a colony building simulation game from one-man developer, Chondrite Games. Humans have at last advanced beyond sending astronauts to the moon, we are now capable of sending humans to Mars. You are in charge of the first mission to Mars, it\u2019s your job to choose a suitable location on the Martian surface and establish a self-sufficient colony. You\u2019ll need to manage your resources while expanding your colony and performing research. If you run out of food and\/or water your astronauts and colonists will perish. This is a momentous event for all of humanity, make sure you don\u2019t screw up!<br><br>Full Disclosure: A review copy was provided by the developer.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Presentation <\/div><br>Being a small game, Sol 0 is modest in appearance. The overall appearance of the game is solid, it\u2019s all high quality and the graphics don\u2019t cause any issues with actually playing the game. The individual models all look good when fully zoomed in as well as when zoomed all the way out which is great for those who put their camera close to one of the two extremes. My only real issue with the game\u2019s appearance was the UI, while it does look good I found that the bar seen at the bottom of the screen was a little too small for my liking, especially the small buttons and displays.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay <\/div><br>Sol 0 is all about building a colony, finding the necessary resources and then sustaining it. The first step is choosing which part of Mars (<a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/id\/solzerogame\/myworkshopfiles\/?appid=387370\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\" >other locations are available and can be found on the Workshop uploaded by the developer<\/a>) you want to establish your colony on. Each location has a different number of resources to be found and so the less resources there are, the harder the game is. The game contains eight maps (nine if you count the special tutorial location) and the developer has already uploaded quite a few maps to the Workshop including locations other than Mars such as Titan.<br><br>Once you\u2019ve decided where you\u2019ll build your colony, it\u2019s time to send the first supply mission. While you can send astronauts straight away it\u2019s best to send up a rover and some supplies on at least the first mission. The rover is what you use to find resources. You can set up an exploration route for it and when it detects resources they\u2019ll appear as different colours and they\u2019ll be shown as different heights depending on how much of the resource is available there (the higher up the bar is, the more there is to be collected). It\u2019s best to survey the majority of the location before sending humans to Mars as resources are drained incredibly quickly and so you need to be harvesting them as quickly as possible in high quantities. <br><br>After 120 Sols (Mars\u2019 equivalent to a day) you\u2019ll be able to send the next rocket to Mars. The basic rocket has a maximum mass of 7000kg and since the rocket itself is 6200kg you\u2019re very limited in what you can take. Deciding what to take is possibly the most important part of the game, from a strategy standpoint, as you need to be able to survive on whatever you take up until the next mission. Later on in the game, once you\u2019ve built certain things, you\u2019ll be able to send larger rockets which can carry different equipment as well as colonists (the basic rocket can only carry astronauts).<br><br>The majority of the game is, of course, made up of actually building your colony. You\u2019re given access to quite a few different structures, some of which are available right away, some of which depend on the presence of other structures and lastly some that require a minimum population before they become available. Structures are made to fit together in certain ways and the building is completely on-grid, i.e. there\u2019s no flexibility when positioning your structures. Personally I prefer building like this as you end up with a much neater structure and you save on space but I realise not everyone likes it.<br><br>Back to those resources you\u2019re managing. The main four are food, water, methane and power. Food and water can both be brought to your colony on your rockets but there are dedicated structures for their production on Mars, their purpose is pretty obvious, to keep your humans alive. Methane is used in conjunction with water to refuel the parts of your rocket which land on Mars so that they can be launched and sent back to Earth. While it\u2019s not hugely important to do this the landing craft does take up quite a bit of space so you will need to send them back every so often. Power is again pretty self-explanatory. While the harvesting of the other resources is fine I wasn\u2019t too happy with how power was handled. Firstly, you can only use solar panels to generate power and they become completely useless during a dust storm meaning everything loses power and so you lose resources. My second issue with power in Sol 0 is that there\u2019s no way to store it. If you could build power banks which stored excess power than my first issue would be balanced out but since there isn\u2019t it feels, to me, like power has only been partially implemented into the game, i.e. you can\u2019t really manage it.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Miscellaneous <\/div><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li>The graphics options are incredibly basic and can only be changed through the default launcher that you see when launching the game.<br><\/li><li>The soundtrack and sound effects are good and fit well with the game.<br><\/li><li>The pricing of the game is great for what it offers.<br><\/li><li>When you launch a new rocket it arrives on Mars instantly which, of course, isn't realistic.<br><\/li><li>As well as dust storms, you'll find yourself up against meteor showers which, while rare, will cause serious damage if any of the meteors hit your colony.<\/li><\/ul><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Verdict <\/div><br><b>Worth Purchasing. <\/b><br><br>Sol 0 is a great colony building simulation with enjoyable yet challenging gameplay and plenty of locations to keep you busy with. Sol 0 isn\u2019t perfect but it is good fun and for such a small asking price you\u2019d be silly to pass it up if you\u2019re a fan of similar games or want to try something new!<br><br><i><a
