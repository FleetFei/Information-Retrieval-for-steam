a lot of reservations.  The TL;DR version?  If you're a fan of the Overlord world and story, buy it during a sale.  If you're like me and you really, really like Overlord and need your smashing sheepies fix, then maaaaaybe it's worth buying now.  YMMV.<br><br>First, the good.  The story is funny, with Pratchett's humor put to good use.  The minions are entertaining, as always, and the dialogue is cheeky and fun.  The world designs (especially the Netherdeep hub) are quite neat.  The Netherghul designs are fantastic, and it's fun to be able to play a variety of characters.  It seems obvious that Codemasters put a lot of love into the game.  The minion upgrades you can purchase were also pretty useful and unique compared to the previous games.  The voice acting is fantastic, and I really liked the new characters as a whole.  It was pretty cool to see the remnants of the old Overlord's empire, too, such as statues of the minion wolf riders and Fay.  The animated cut scenes had a really interesting wood-cut kind of style, which was unique and kind of neat to see in a video game.<br><br>Gameplay wise, I liked the fact that you could &quot;carry&quot; minions in reserve and use them when you needed them, rather than have them trail along behind you, inevitably getting lost or stuck somewhere.  The minion hammer was really useful.  I loved the fact that my blues could heal me.  The gameplay mechanics with the Golden was also pretty interesting, and it was very satisfying to finally be able to burn it back.<br><br>And now...unfortunately...on to the bad.  I really, really wanted to love this game whole-heartedly.  I love Diablo, and I absolutely love the Overlord universe.  It would have been really, really easy for this game to win me over.  The fact that it didn't really speaks to its flaws.<br><br>First of all, it's very, very short.  I was hoping for something a bit meatier.  I was hoping for more backstory.  The ending was very ambiguous, and while they're obviously setting up for a sequel or DLC of some kind, I'm just not sure if that's going to happen based on all the bad reviews.<br><br>The game play feels, as others have mentioned, very &quot;clunky&quot;.  You're not able to recall your minions, which leads to a lot of them running off and getting themselves killed, which is annoying.  You also can't sweep them or plant them on a marker.  Yes, I know, you're a Netherghul, not an Overlord, but story shouldn't get in the way of good gameplay.  The reds were suicidal, which yes, was the point, but also massively, massively stupid.  They seemed to have no targeted mechanic whatsoever.  I'd point them at an enemy, and I'd be a *few* degrees off, and they'd go sweeping past the enemy to blow themselves up against the wall.  Ranged units would have been much more helpful, to be honest.<br><br>The attacks and battles are quite frankly boring, and this is coming from someone who once lost fifteen pounds over a summer spent playing Diablo.  I was hoping for a spell tree that I could use to do different attacks, at the very least.  The battles last too long, the enemy attacks lack variety, and the whole time I spent fighting just felt like a grind.<br><br>The traps were irritating.  I had trouble controlling my character (Inferna) using a keyboard and mouse, and often found myself blundering into something I thought I'd gotten out of the way of.  The time trials were honestly just really annoying, and didn't serve much purpose.<br><br>I would also have liked it if I'd been able to visit previously-conquered stages again to gather more loot.  The game was linear to a fault.  I'm terrible at maps, and often have trouble finding my way around complex stages, and even I found the linear nature of the game to be dull.<br><br>I don't regret my purchase...I love the Overlord universe, and I love Rhianna Pratchett's writing.  That said, I feel like this game had so much potential, and could have been so much more.
