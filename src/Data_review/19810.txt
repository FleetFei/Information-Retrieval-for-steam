Maybe this will take the focus off of 9\/11 and this will be the attack everyone refuses to stop milking for the next three decades. That's enough to get the entire country \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665 enough to form a special task force in order to hunt down these monsters.<br><br>\tFootball is great and all, but what makes me mad about all of this is that the nuke was detonated late December, 2001. Mother. \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665ers. Okay, you don't have to tell me twice, let's go kill everyone even remotely related to the plot.<br><br>\tWhat did stay in this game is the stiff, tactical shooter gameplay that made Ghost Recon so much fun. One well-placed bullet can take a bad guy down, but that goes both ways. The AI can be a little dumb at times but Jesus Christ, once they get you in their line of sight, it's a mad trigger dash that usually ends in you eating a bullet. I know the game is tactical, and it's trying to be as realistic as it can, but when you get shot and don't die, your character takes about three minutes to forget about everything around him and fliiiinnnch.<br><br>\tI appreciate that the developers actually made your character acknowledge when he's hit in a way other than annoyingly jerking your camera around, but it's really just a death sentence. If you gave me a choice, I would definitely choose Ghost Recon over this, but I'm happy that The Sum Of All Fears exists. class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall, despite it's drawbacks, I'd have to say I recommend this game.  It's the last game in the era of &quot;classic&quot; tactical shooters, like SWAT 3, Rogue Spear, Ghost Recon, etc, where you had to play it extremely slow, you actually completed objectives, and the game revolved more around the atmosphere of the levels and the actual gameplay, rather than the cinematic events and story-telling that would be featured later.  Some games such as GRAW 1 and 2 for PC and Operation Flashpoint Dragon Rising tried to recapture this sort of style, and did so admirably but were never as influential as the previous titles as they weren't able to gain the traction and acclaim that their predecessors had done so seemingly with ease.<br><br>Unfortunately, as the last game of the &quot;classic&quot; tactical shooter era, it's not all that great.  The AI can become glitchy and shoot walls, some lights sear right through the textures and you can see them from anywhere on the map (this is a rare glitch, thankfully), and planning your own routes and picking each individuals gear is completely absent.  Instead, you're given a preset path which you can choose to follow (you often probably should, since it feels as if the missions are designed around following these paths, and if you stray off the path, you'll often find nothing of interest.  Instead of choosing each soldiers load out, you pick &quot;kits&quot; for the whole team, which are pretty diverse, but picking your own gear is much preferred.  As for the AI, you'll often see your team mates shooting walls, trying to get at enemies, and they won't budge until the enemy is dead, or they die.<br><br>So how, how can I recommend this game despite all this?  Well, for one, the HUD is very neat and tidy.  There are a few squad commands at your disposal which work quite well and is very easy to adjust.  The missions have a nice flow to them and are quite addicting.  While not as large in scope as Ghost Recon's, they offer interesting settings with very nicely detailed environments which the mappers clearly at least TRIED to make feel &quot;realistic&quot;.  Some of the very first levels you play are actually quite memorable.  In terms of replayability, the game is largely more linear than in the previous titles I've mentioned here, but again, it tries to make things more interesting with the nicely detailed levels and memorable settings.  The graphics are quite nice, but are tarnished by the &quot;sweat&quot; effects which replace the brutal blood and gore you'd see in Ghost Recon after firing on enemies (though thankfully there's mods which replace the sweat with blood).<br><br>Overall, the sum of its parts adds up to a game with a more linear structure to the levels which makes the overall gameplay feel faster paced, with a lot of the more complex features of the previous titles either being skimped over, or altered\/simplified for ease of play.  If you're looking for a game with memorable settings, decent gameplay, and is easy to get into, I would recommend this to you.  If you're new to the tactical shooter genre, I would also recommend this to you.  And thats where its strengths are, it services both groups while still managing to be fun.
