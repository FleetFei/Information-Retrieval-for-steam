<i>Black Hole Hazard<\/i> is an action, indie, sci-fi, platformer, puzzle game with particle physics, gravitational pull and portal physics. In Black Hole Hazard you play as the main character, Dr. Albert Armstrong whom was in his lab testing a new discovery in his research when suddenly, something in the machinery of the space shuttle broke. You are still stuck outside on the back panel before, within seconds, you get electrically shocked. Now that the experiment of quantum mechanics is amiss, you have to go out into space and restore the gaps in the black hole. There are many dangers as you lurk through the depths chapter to chapter within these galaxies of the universe. You have spikes, pitfalls, lasers, bouncing walls and did I mention the terrible saws that you find in <i>Super Meat Boy<\/i>, <i>VVVVVV<\/i> and <i>Geometry Dash<\/i>. <br><br>     Now, if that was not enough, you have geometric shapes similar to the ones in <i>Geometry Wars<\/i> that you shoot with your attached laser while trying to flip, transverse, propel and drive your character around enormous pieces of a black hole. The main purpose and goal of this game is to get through each level unharmed while avoiding death around every bend and corner. You reach the end of the level once you find a purple portal which transports you back to the main HUD.<br><br>    <i> Black Hole Hazard releases August 29, 2016 to the PC market. You have a laser and your jetpack to be able to shoot these geometric shapes. In the earlier chapters, chapters one through two, you mainly go up against rhombuses which have three different variants. One is just a basic yellow color that takes around one shot to kill it, the second type are larger red rhombuses that take slightly more and lastly, the red rhombuses with shields take around eight to nine shots to defeat.<br><br>    <b>Gravity, Gravitational Pull and the Manipulation of Gravity Based Puzzles<\/b><br><br>     In each level, your enemy is the gravity and obstacles that will try to deflect your steadiness to shift the direction constantly where you are moving toward. For example, there are many levels with panels and switches in them. The panels are like the hidden blocks in Mario where you have to be right under them first before they appear. This can be very frustrating in case it bounces you toward saw hazards. You can manipulate gravity based on the direction your character is facing. What I mean by this is you can perform 180 degrees and 360 degree flips to avoid gravitational pulls and an easy death. <br><br>     The easiest way is controlling the jetpack in the opposite direction you are going. If you have to go up for example, make sure that the Jetpack propels you from the opposite end. Once you reach the levels in which you have to go between two pillars, sometimes pillars of saws; you have to wait until those saws and pillars transverse which is the first time they side-scroll across the area usually left to right, also, up and down. If you remain in the middle it will crush you in half, whereas if you wait, you have to be directly in the middle and you will have to carefully control your jetpack in order to prevent you from going too far to the right or left on either side. <br><br>      <b>User Interface<\/b><br><br>     The user interface, hud is graphically like The Undergarden, another puzzle; platformer game that have portals per chapter section. For example, in chapter one, there will be individual areas labeled off in sections such as 2-1, 2-2 located in corners around a circular area. You must enter the portal based on which level you want to play, once you do beat a level it will mark out the portal in gray instead of the original purple so you know you have played through that level at least once. There are fourteen logs separated into fourteen chapters and 150 separate levels. Likewise, of most puzzle based action, indie, platformer type games within this genre, the levels always get progressively harder as you move on taking much more patience and time. Another objective of the game,although not the main objective, is beating your friend's scores and that against others in the world based on their clear times.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Pros &amp; Cons<\/div><br>     <b>Pros<\/b><br>\u2022Challenging levels with a great variety of level design, enemy differentiation <br>\u2022Each chapter is given a separate color landscape in the main hud which transfers over to the main color for the levels located in each chapter<br>\u2022Complex quantum mechanics and physics based platforming in order to solve levels by hitting switches, avoiding potential hazards and propelling through the madness<br>\u2022Unlike many big publishers, the trailers have no false promises nor advertising that would undermine the game's potential<br>\u2022Simple controls<br><br>     <b>Cons<\/b><br>\u2022Surprisingly I have nothing to add toward any negatives so far in the game from what I have played up to this point of my review. If you love the aspects of the different components of the environmental hazards of <i>VVVVVV<\/i>, <i>Super Meat Boy<\/i>, triple spikes; obstacles in <i>Geometry Dash<\/i> and other great platformers, you will love this game.
