The game was released on PC, PS4 and Xbox One in July 2015.  Whilst the game has got a tag of being Indie, I don\u2019t really agree, given the catalogue of games both the developer and publisher has to their names. <br><br>You control Tembo, the bad \u2665\u2665\u2665 elephant (no kidding huh?). Your aim is to stop an invasion on Shell City, to stop the bad guys called The Phantom. As it is a platforming game, you have all of the regular platforming style game play mechanics with a few little twists to them. For instance you can jump and hover for a few seconds rather than having a double jump. You are also able to squirt water out of your trunk to put out fires and to interact with a few objects in the game world to help you solve puzzles.  As you progress through each of the levels, you can also take out enemies, by using various different attacks, such as charge, uppercuts and stomps. The killing of enemies is really important as they act as your currency in the game\u2019s built in gating system. You have to kill a certain quota of enemies before you can move onto the next stage of levels. You can also rescue citizens of the city that have been captured as well, but these add no bonus or server no purposes to the game play it. <br><br>The art style in Tembo looks awesome. It\u2019s not mind boggling graphics, but the comic book style just captures the game\u2019s aim perfectly. Whilst it is in the cartoon style, it doesn\u2019t come across as cheesy or childish though. There isn\u2019t any voice acting sadly, but even if there were voice acting, there wouldn\u2019t be much as there isn\u2019t really any dialogue either. The entire story is played out via a comic book strip in between levels and it does it really well. <br><br>This game is balls to the wall hard; each stage has three to four levels in it, and they will gradually get harder and harder as you would expect. A few of the levels will have challenging bosses and enemies to overcome, whilst others can also have puzzles and gauntlets that you have to complete. This game had me raging at times when I died, but I was still hooked and wanted to keep going. The welcome addition of a number of checkpoints throughout the levels really helps, and keeps you going when times get tough. I never actually ran out of lives though, so the game is beatable with a bit of skill.  <br><br>All of Tembo\u2019s levels are really well designed. None of them are your standard platforming style stages. Each one has a different theme, soundtrack and puzzles in. I never felt like I was replaying previous content that was just copy and pasted.  Like I said above, you can collect trapped citizens, and you can also aim to get 100% kills of enemies on each stage if you want to go for the achievement hunting. The game itself playing to completion took me just over 3 hours.  I may go back and try to get 100% rating on some of the earlier levels, but even if I don\u2019t I am very happy with the time I got out of this title. <br><br>The only technical issue I had was that I could not get the game to run in 4k. The resolution settings seem to be capped at 1080p but I was able to get 60fps 100% of the time though. Whilst it isn\u2019t a major issue on this type of art style game, the options would always be nice. Game pad is highly recommended due to the platforming elements. I tried with keyboard and it was just disastrous. <br><br>Tembo the Badass Elephant was a little bit of a surprise in the rough for me.  I am not surprised at how well the game turned out given the teams behind it, but it is really nice to see a simple idea put into such a clearly carefully made game.
