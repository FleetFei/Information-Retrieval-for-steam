The game offers both single and local co-op, with online co-op coming \u201csoon\u201d.<br><br>There are 3 different modes in the game right now. Youv\u2019e got the Arcade mode which lets you play through a series of levels, defeat a few bosses and then, if you manage to survive, your mission is complete. The other 2 modes you\u2019ve got are the daily and weekly challenges which, at this moment, still don\u2019t have a leaderboard system in place but I feel like they are harder than the Arcade mode. The thing that really makes me excited about this game is the Campaign mode which will come at a later date which is a mix of the current combat gameplay with turn-based base management. Right now, you\u2019ll be moving around through different hazardous materials, dodging projectiles with your jetpack while blasting your way through piles of alien filth. Shooting itself is pretty satisfying but I feel like there needs to be more visual feedback as to how destructive your weapons actually are. <br><br>At its core the game has some features which are reminiscent of rogue-likes\/lites that might appeal to fans of the genre. The game has permadeath but it does so in a rather unique way. You carry around a backpack and once you die a countdown timer will begin for you to respawn at the backpack location and this can go on forever. If, however, your backpack gets destroyed you\u2019ll have no more respawns. The game also features some sort of permanent progression in the sense that as you complete missions you\u2019ll unlock more cosmetic items. As you clean up hives and kill aliens you\u2019ll also pick up goo which acts as a form of currency. At the end of each stage you\u2019ll be able to upgrade your weapon, purchase a new special ability such as a auto-turret or a shield, and you\u2019ll also be able to get a new type of grenade such as poison or remote detonated explosives. In terms of weapon variety the game already offers a nice range of weapons and mods but it would be nice to see more added down the road, and the same goes for enemy types. Stages are divided into different biomes (e.g. lava and ice) and they can have special hand crafted challenge rooms in them which, upon completing will grant you a passive bonus. At the end of each one you\u2019ll face a boss, overall they\u2019re pretty easy once you figure out their patterns so maybe having a bump in difficulty would be nice, as well as more boss variants.<br><br>One thing I can\u2019t stress enough is how good the soundtrack is and how it has a very retro feel to it (the composer has worked on Mass Effect 2). The controls also have a very retro feel and remind of something in the veins of Contra, Gunstar Heroes or Metroid. In terms of visuals, the pixel art is on point and the combination of different colors helps distinguish things on the background.
