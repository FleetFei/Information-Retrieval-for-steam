Samples from the original movie make us nicely feel not at home. Sigourney Weaver as Ellen Ripley is always welcome. Second impression: lovingly made up like 1986! It borrows the old-school visuals from 80s arcade cabinet pinball machines. Well... except the terrific Alien head hovering over the far side of the table. Nicely done!<br>The table has a wonderful mechanic feel to it, a &quot;rattle and shake&quot; feel that none of Zen's slicker Marvel tables offered. The table has a good flow at an medium difficulty level. As usual: the outlanes are traps par excellence, but with its easily attainable kickbacks the table is controllable at normal speeds. The missions are varied and appropriately hard to finish successfully. (Update for the wizard mode will follow as soon as I have reached it...). A fun table!<br><br><u>Aliens vs Predator<\/u><br>Playing as the Predator, this table pits the player not only against a lot! of these ugly alien s**kers, but also against a ton of different modes. Six main missions, ramp missions, some 3 to 5 multiballs (in my second game I could stack some of them to juggle 5 balls at once - fun time for the ball tricksters) and a ranking system that rewards deep play towards some more complicated goals with permanent boni (similar to Epic Quest, but less invasive). Plus some smaller stuff to do. Yikes... I am not sure I even triggered everything this table has to offer.<br>Visually and acoustically it is the weakest of the tables. Still above par, just not as impressive as Aliens and A:I.<br>The table design itself is interesting for its curved ramps that seem to play a little different then the other tables. They make for a fast play style that is easy to pick up, but again way harder for the tight missions (many shots within relatively tight time limits). <br><br><u>Alien: Isolation<\/u><br>And here is something different... A:I sticks to its original game in that it forces the player to be constantly watchful what s*he is doing. As soon as the Alien lurks around the table's decoration, some ramps become taboo areas. Hit them and the Alien starts to look for Ripley, leaving only a small time window to hide (in a cabinet! Where else?). Missing it does not result in ball loss, but has as an impact on the scoring. An interesting mechanic that makes for harder gameplay when going for high scores.<br>And as seems to be this pack's trademark, this table also comes with a lot of stuff to trigger and some nifty mini-gaming (a funny, though not exactly complicated Breakout clone is the table's &quot;Hacking&quot; mode).<br>It is the trio's hardest table with unforgiving missions and an exceptionally large table size. Think snooker! Aiming a long shot requires a fair bit of training and a good eye... Good thing that it also has tight control and a &quot;stealth rhythm&quot; to it. This table counts on the player to just wait through tight periods, or play considerably higher risks with the Alien breathing down Ripley's neck. A mechanic new to Zen's tables (at least in standard play) and one that makes for engaging play on a very elaborate table.<br><br>None of the tables is a high scorer. Numbers tend to be medium high - enough to be motivated but no 8 digit fireworks. All are complex, but not merciless. All offer a very good amount of deep content. So, this is a thumbs up. <br><br>Only drawback: now it's 3 tables for the full price again, instead of the usual 4? happy. I would recommend this pack to anyone who's enjoyed the PFX series so far, though you'll get a lot more mileage out of the pack if you know the source material. Then again, why wouldn't you? <br><br>ALIENS follows the plot of the Cameron movie, one of the strongest entries in the Alien franchise. The table is heavily mission-based, which is a good thing as it recreates the movie's plot while also giving the player a good sense of progress. In terms of layout and design, ALIENS is surprisingly &quot;old-school&quot; for a PFX table, with very few gimmicks and actual props instead of an overload of eye candy. The flow's good and the physics do their job just fine. The table comes with a ton of voice clips and iconic sound effects from the movie and a sparse, but efficient soundtrack. A great table that's easy to recommend.<br><br>AVP is based on the (not too popular) crossover movie that sets the Xenomorphs against badass warrior creatures, the Predators, with some humans trapped between both sides. The table is making the most of it- as the lead Predator you get to fight aliens while some human researchers deliver a flustered running comment, which can get downright awesome. The layout's full of curved ramps and needs some getting used to, but the flow, again, is pretty darn good. AVP is the most &quot;gimmicky&quot; of the tables, making use of Predator heat vision, several minigames and even a mode where the playfield is flipped around. I'm usually not a fan of that but it makes some sense to highlight all the cool gadgets of the Predator.  It quickly grew on me and the artstyle is downright cool.<br><br>ALIEN ISOLATION is based on the video game of the same name and serves as a sort of spiritual sucessor to what the original Alien was all about: One utterly deadly Xenomorph that you have no chance of ever beating by conventional means. As Ripley's daughter you face one Alien, and your best chance is to run away. The table has an interesting mechanic focussing on &quot;stealth&quot;- basically, you want to only hit ramps and targets that are &quot;safe&quot;, and careless hits will alert the Alien to your presence, which can cost you quite a bit of score. It's certainly interesting and really puts the focus on &quot;skillgame&quot;. And again, the artwork is extremely good, the layout clear enough to make the table challenging but not frustrating, and both music and sound effects are spot-on, creating a sense of tension all the time.<br><br>All in all, even after just a couple rounds, this collection really stands out- PFX did a great job here and delivered three tables that are deep and challenging enough to really get you hooked. Even at full price this is easy to recommend (though usually packs have four tables and I would've loved to see an original Alien table...). <br>If you want high-scoring, fast tables, this won't quite
