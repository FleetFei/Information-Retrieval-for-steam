It's both enjoyable in a strategical and personal sense.<br><br>Lets just straight get into why.<br><br><b>Gameplay<\/b><br><br>- The game manages to be detailed with their strategy, forcing you to actually flank and position your troops. It doesn't just account to how large your force is or whose troops are more disciplined, though, those two are factors to consider as well. In multiplayer, you might even be able to factor in Psychological Warfare! It gets pretty in-depth. And I love that.<br><br>- In the Campaign, your Generals, Merchants, Spies, Diplomats and Assassins can gain, lose, enhance and diminish traits. It forces you to treat them as actual people to win the war, not the game. Your General is too hot-headed? Have him take up an Administrative Role for a while...Oh, he's too scary? Have him spare the peasants. He's a good guy, despite all the villages he burned...Probably.<br><br>- The building and unit descriptions are just superbly fun to read, I find myself spending minutes reading the buildings and units on the queue. As well as the trait descriptions..It makes the game more immersive.<br><br>- General Speeches are just the best thing ever. They can either be very good, like &quot;Pray now for the intercession of Saint Michael in our Imperial Cause..If you ride a horse, add a prayer to Saint Martin for your devotions!&quot; Or they can be extremely savage, &quot;They say that the French are better loves than fighters, this is true: Ask any goat, pig or other farmyard animal.&quot; And, my favorite, can be hilarious, &quot;Well, here's the thing. I look at our foes, and all I see are a bunch of \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665-weasels!&quot; These things don't matter. They don't. But they're so good. And the fact that the later Total War games remove this makes it feel like something was missing in their core.<br><br>- If you're ever bored with native gameplay, the modding community is ready to make you waste more hours on their mods like Stainless Steel, Third Age, DarthMod, etc.<br><br>- The dead bodies don't despawn. The unit sizes are 80-160 men per unit. Maybe even more. I think that speaks for itself. It does? Of course it does.<br><br>- While the campaign is great, I can't deny, nor can anyone deny, the fact that the AI is bad. I'd like to take this short moment to just be thankful that it's not as dumb as Empire: Total War's..<br><br>- The unit variety is very large at first, but after a few hours of gameplay, you'll start to realize how little it is..<br><br><b>Community<\/b><br><br>- The Community isn't very bad, but not very good either. From my experience, the people are usually nice, and everyone follows the rules given by the host. (And apologize if they accidentaly don't.) Though, you will still occassionaly run into rude people, cheaters, campers, etc. Still, I still find a lot of fun in Multiplayer.<br><br><b>Graphics<\/b><br><br>- The Graphics in Medieval II isn't very impressive, but it's not bad either. But hey, it's an old game, so the lack in graphics is justified. Besides, charging knights will still look epic in any Total War game, no matter the graphics.<br><br><b>Experience<\/b><br><br>- The best thing about any total war game, or any game, is the experience. I have so much misadventures and adventures in this great game. I had a great general who died in defense of our Great Capital. (His name was Richard the Fearless.) I had an Assassin who assassinated every Pope that was elected until only the Cardinal from my nation was left to be elected. (His name was <span class=\"bb_strike\">Ezio<\/span> Borgognion the Killer.) I had a faithful unit of Mailed Knights that I brought to in every single campaign since the moment they were recruited early into the campaign. It's just..It's great. To be honest, this is probably why Medieval II is considered one of the best strategy games, and one of the best Total War games. It's the adventures you get through this.<br><br>TL;DR<br><br>Battles - Multiplayer - 10\/10 | Singleplayer - 8\/10<br>Campaign - Multiplayer - 9\/10 | Singleplayer - 9\/10<br>Gameplay - 8\/10<br>Graphics - 8\/10<br>Community - 9\/10<br>Immersion - 13\/10<br><br>Overall - 10\/10. Go get it, if you're a fan of Strategy, you won't regret it.<br><br><br>Oh, I forgot to warn you.
