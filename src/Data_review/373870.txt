That about sums it up. It's a tired and overused plot, and in this case it's also not executed very well.<br><br>Many plot elements introduced in the first scenes are seemingly ignored for the rest of the game, and there's literally no way to know how the heck it all fits together unless you read the store page description. Reasons. <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay<\/div>The core gameplay is alright-ish, but the majority of point-and-click elements are very unintuitive. Many items you find make no sense for the situation, and make even less sense when you figure out what they're for. Why did I just find a monster's severed arm, and why is it only useful to burn a hole in a rock? Why can't I use paint unless I mix it with angel tears? And so on. Also, when you use tools you have to actually click multiple times to use them. Need to dig a hole with a shovel? Click to dig. Then click to dig. Then click to dig. Why can't the animation just play 3 times on its own? Ultimately the game is still playable and enjoyable, but you'll probably be making liberal use of the hint button or strategy guide.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Graphics<\/div>This game is gorgeous. Even the more 'drab' locations were drawn well - just not as &quot;ooh, screenshot!&quot; worthy. The art is on par with (or even better than) several of the top Artifex games, which is saying a lot. Though I honestly wish the game supported widescreen\/1920x1080 res (it's 1024x768 windowed), because then I'd have a whole new bunch of desktop backgrounds to cycle through. <br><br>The character design is good, but the animation can get awkward. The non-human characters are way more appealing. The dragon in the bar is one of my favorites, and I really appreciated the fact that the artist thought it through enough to plan out where the *rest* of the dragon would fit in the building. It was creatively done and quite amusing, and the kind of detail other fantasy games never really take into account.<br><br>Cutscenes are surprisingly almost as clear as the static art. Definite plus there too.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Sound<\/div>The music is lovely. It may not be top dollar voice work, but it's far better than countless other titles. I enjoyed them though, which is what counts. Unfortunately this is also one of those games where the puzzle window poofs the second you find the solution, so there's no way to see what you did correctly if you were just trying random things. On the plus side, there's an in-game strategy guide that shows all the solutions if you find yourself stuck.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Extras<\/div>The bonus chapter doesn't make sense as a bonus chapter.
