You have quests that villagers give you, and these are typically \u201con the way\u201d so they're easily completed as you're moving the story along, you merely have to explore the maps fully and not go directly to the next objective. Even the bosses are easy to take out (shockingly easy), so there's no need for complexity when everything around you is so simple. Later on you'll most likely just fly over enemies to move to the next map screen, since there's nothing exciting here, albeit nothing is broken and all works as intended. You CAN have a lot of options in combat whilst maintaining the inputs and mechanics simple and basic, so a few points off for that only because games like Muramasa have spoiled me on how good combat can be.<br><br>I'll also give a critique on the levelling up system: I can't go down a strict attack route and have to have my lowest stat be no more than 4 points behind my highest stat. The quality on this game is impressive for a team of one, and it ultimately stands on its own as an above average experience in the 2D hack and slash department that carries its own weight.
