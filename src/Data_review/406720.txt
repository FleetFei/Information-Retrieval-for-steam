class=\"bb_h1\">Introduction<\/div>Who remembers (or has played) the Death To Spies series? I think few of us do. That might just be the reason why development for the third game in this series was halted a few years ago. Fortunately, Maximum Games still saw enough potential in the game to pick it up and finish development eventually, hence the reason why it\u2019s now a spiritual successor called Alekhine\u2019s Gun.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay<\/div>The game\u2019s name refers to a famous chess move and \u2013 like chess \u2013 the developers state that you have to anticipate your opponent\u2019s moves here as well.  We all know that this is usually the case for stealth games but does it really apply to Alekhine\u2019s Gun? To be perfectly honest, no, it does not. Except for a few moments maybe. As always I\u2019ll do my best to try and go through all of the (im)perfections during the next few paragraphs.<br><br>Anyone who has ever played or seen Hitman should know what to expect here. The goal is to silently complete your objectives, but you can also choose to take the more action oriented road, or use a combination of the two. <br>Before you start a mission you\u2019re given the objectives and then you can select which weapons and items you would like to bring. You can take up to 6 things with you, and you can unlock some of these items later on with the points you have earned during your earlier missions. There aren\u2019t a whole lot of different weapons but more than enough to get the job done.<br>You can also use weapons and items found in the levels themselves, although I was disappointed to see that this was kept to a minimum. There\u2019s very little stuff to take and use. This in turn makes it less fun to explore and\/or replay the levels and even if you find things to use it usually still isn\u2019t clear what you have to do with it exactly. I found this to be quite disappointing, but I guess it\u2019s just a personal preference.<br><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=642855650\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_0\">http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=642855650<\/a><br>Your character can also use Focus, which lights up items that can be interacted with as well as which characters are looking your way or are suspicious of you. At times helpful, especially in locations that are packed with characters, but most of the times plain useless. I also still have no idea how and when it refills.<br>A different meter also fills once you\u2019re being suspected and when it has filled up enemies will swarm in. Most of the times. Sometimes they just won\u2019t take action...<br><br>Which brings us to the AI. Since the game\u2019s title refers to \u2018anticipation of opponent\u2019s moves\u2019 this part should be (near-)perfect, right? Well, wrong, but I think I made that already clear in the last paragraph. Sure, sometimes it works, but most of the time they\u2019ll react in the most unrealistic way possible. I once threw a knife at an officer sitting in a mess hall \u2013 with at least eight characters sitting around him \u2013 and all they did was check to see if he was dead, stand there for a couple of minutes and then carry on with dinner. Ten minutes later the guy was still lying face down in his soup and people were acting like nothing happened. This happened in many different scenarios, too, and even though it doesn\u2019t make the game unplayable it does detract from the experience it\u2019s trying to aim for: realism.<br><br>There are 11 levels in total and you start with a rather generic, boring World War II flashback mission. I think this was actually a terrible choice for a first level because it\u2019s something that has been done to death. I personally think it would\u2019ve been better if they had found a way to introduce it later on.<br>The levels complement the story, in a way, but except for a handful of them they just don\u2019t make for very interesting locations. Most of the levels are dull, uninspired and sometimes even too large\/complex (like the aircraft carrier).<br>Depending on your playstyle each level can usually take anywhere between 30 (action) to 60 (stealth) minutes. <br><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=642855696\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_1\">http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=642855696<\/a><br>But the game doesn\u2019t seem to be made with shooting in mind. Don\u2019t get me wrong; it works, but most of the locations have so many enemies walking around that you usually don\u2019t stand a chance unless you\u2019re well prepared and save often. It just feels a bit like an afterthought.<br><br>Since Steam reviews still have a character limit I will go through some of the bugs\/glitches in one sentence now. Here we go: map markers showing the wrong floor, crashes in general, heart rate sound bugging out and a spotty cover system. <br>Apologies for this, I wish this was different and I could be more thorough.<br><br> <div class=\"bb_h1\">Audio &amp; Visuals<\/div>The audio is yet another department in which the game suffers. The voice acting is terrible and lacks any authentic emotion. Most of the men sound almost the same, with the exception being your own character who has a bit of a Russian accent. I had a hard time keeping up who\u2019s who, especially during the animated black and white cutscenes.<br>Also, gunshots just sound hollow and lack power. The rest of the sounds suffice, though, and the music, while minimalist, is generally speaking fairly good.<br><br>Visually it isn\u2019t breathtaking. There are a ton of other games that have better graphics than this game but it isn\u2019t ugly by any means. Sometimes it just lacks that extra bit of detail, though. I also wish there were more (smaller) objects located in the levels. They feel rather empty at times. <br>The upside to this is that the engine can handle large levels without breaking a sweat and performance never once dips, even with everything maxed out. Most of you should be able to run this without any issues since the game isn\u2019t all that demanding. But the game does crash from time to time, without good reason. During my sessions I had the game crash at least 6-7 times. So make sure that you save often.<br><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=642855673\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_2\">http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=642855673<\/a> <br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Length\/Replay Value<\/div>I already mentioned this during my \u2018Gameplay\u2019 paragraph so I\u2019ll go through it again really quick: there are 11 levels and if you play it as an assassin it will take you about 12+ hours I think. This doesn\u2019t include reloading save games, by the way, which will happen frequently. Or the fact that you can try to tackle levels differently. In any case; overall it has plenty of content.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Conclusion<\/div>The fact that Alekhine\u2019s Gun releases around the same time as the new Hitman game might not have been the smartest move. But if you\u2019re looking for a budget version of the Hitman games then this game could definitely be worth your time. <br>Is it a great stealth game? No, not in any way. But it is a decent one. I\u2019ve played plenty of titles that were worse than this. Just don\u2019t expect greatness and you shall not be disappointed.<br>As for the question if it\u2019s worth the money? Yes, but not for the current asking price; I suggest waiting for it to drop to around \u20ac15\/$15 or less before even considering picking it up.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Too long, didn\u2019t read<\/div>Pros:<br>+ Large levels<br>+ Multiple ways to complete objectives<br>+ Variety in objectives<br><br>Mixed:<br>+\/- Visuals are okay-ish but the engine can handle large levels easily<br>+\/- But despite them being large the levels are mostly plain boring<br><br>Cons:<br>- AI occasionally acts unrealistically<br>- Voice acting and gun sounds<br>- Action at times feels like an afterthought<br>- Crashes and some bugs\/glitches<br><br><table class=\"bb_table\">    <tr>        <th>Please note: a Steam key for this game was provided by the developer\/publisher.<\/th>    <\/tr><\/table><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"><b>Rating: 6.1 (out of 10)<\/b><\/div>(Ratings aren\u2019t calculated based on a formula, but on my personal feelings)<br><br><br><table class=\"bb_table\"><tr><th>If you have any kind of feedback - positive or negative - please feel free to leave a comment (in case of negative feedback please try to keep it constructive).
