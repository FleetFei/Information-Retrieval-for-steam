class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\t<div class=\"bb_h1\">What is Switch 'N' Shoot?<\/div><br>Switch 'N' Shoot is the epitome of a game that appears too simplistic to develop any hopes for. It's an arcade shoot 'em up that only gives you one button to play the entire game with. Developed by Matt Glanville I was certainly not anticipating spending several hours with the game. I was certain that it was going to be one of those games that became highly boring and tedious after about ten minutes. Boy, was I wrong!<br><br>You control a spaceship that has to destroy incoming alien spacecraft. Your single button controls both the ship's navigation as well as weapon firing. Every time a button is pressed it fires a single shot but it also switches the way your ship is traveling. For example, if you're traveling right and press a button you'll fire a shot and begin traveling left. You can go through your screen, similar to Pac-Man, so you don't have to avoid hitting the sides of your screen.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">What Makes it So Addictive?<\/div><br>It's a simple enough concept and one that may sound less than intriguing. However, it is addictive. Why? Because you're always concentrating on killing enemies but also making sure you're getting from A to B quickly enough to do so. Therefore, what this means is that there will be a great number of times in each game that you will fire just to switch without having any intention of attacking an enemy. This takes a high level of concentration.<br><br>New players will die quick and fast - this is to be expected - and here they will find something that I find a combination of humorous and addictive. Players do not input a name into the game. The game gives you funny little names. Each time you die you'll get a funny quote such as &quot;This was expected of Schloot&quot; (or whatever that round's character's name was). You'll also get assigned a character name at the beginning of each game generally with a word of luck.<br><br>There is a high score table that for whatever reason begins out of order. I'm not sure if something is supposed to be implied by this but it may be something for a future update. This yet another challenge. I'm not close to getting top spot but it's certainly not impossible.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Presentation<\/div><br>The presentation is as simple as the concept. While I understand that this will deter some players I would urge you that it shouldn't. The game doesn't need a fantastic art presentation and it's much more than suitable. If the game did have fancy artwork - aside from the arcade cabinet stylings on each side of the screen - it would have a detrimental effect on the game. The simple presentation is fantastic game design in my opinion.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Is it Steamified?<\/div><br>No. Unfortunately, it hasn't been Steamified as of yet. Of course, I'd like to see trading cards and Steam achievements added to the game as I do every game. However, this is a game that would do very well in having a global leaderboard system implemented into it. I don't see this as being a game that would encourage friends to compete locally against one another (though it might be fun) so using the Steam leaderboards would give the game a competitive edge.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Verdict<\/div><br>Switch 'N' Shoot is US$1.99. I believe that most players will get much more than their monies worth within the first time that they play the game.
