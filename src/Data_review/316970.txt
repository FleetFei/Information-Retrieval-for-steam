I\u2019ve played plenty of Metroidvania games in my life, so I was willing to give it a try and see if it\u2019s anything like those revolutionary games.<br><br>In this game you control a king who kills a strange looking beast in the very beginning of the game. This is also one of its main problems.<br>Although you have a map in your possession you can only use it to see all the places you can go to. This is the general idea behind Metroidvania games, by the way; to find better equipment so you can go back and progress where you previously couldn\u2019t. But one thing\u2019s for sure, this isn\u2019t fun. It would\u2019ve been much better if there was just a way to fast travel to places you\u2019ve already visited. A missed opportunity.<br>In the beginning of the game you\u2019ll have just your basic sword to combat enemies and later on you\u2019ll find better and other weapons (like your bow), and even new abilities and items. Fighting enemies seems fun, especially in the beginning, but every time you reenter an area the enemies will have respawned. Most of the enemies aren\u2019t killed by one hit so they usually all need a couple of hits before they die. After fighting the same enemy for the 10th time or so you\u2019ll grow bored by it and you\u2019ll just try to jump over them. This becomes easier to do once you have the ability to double jump. I don\u2019t think this could\u2019ve been the developer\u2019s intention but I think it\u2019s what most people will do anyway. There\u2019s no real incentive to kill enemies because you won\u2019t gain any experience or items from them except for randomly dropped (poisoned) food.
