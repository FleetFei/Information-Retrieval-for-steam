review.<br><br>I will start off by saying that I was late to the Witcher series. (Still haven't played Wild Hunt) With that being said, they are games that should not be overlooked by RPG fans, even though the first 2 are 5+ years old. I played The Witcher about 3 years ago and I wish I would of played it alot closer to my Witcher 2 playthrough because I had forgotten details about some of the previous characters and locations. A quick trip to google cleared up all my confusion and my gameplay experience was great.<br><br>Story -&gt; 9\/10<br><br>I was fully immersed in the story once I cleared up a few things I had forgotten from the first one. It moves along a steady pace and ramps up to a good finale by Act III. I imported my character from the original which added a few weapons and armour (as far as I could tell) as well as a few references and minor story alterations. There are some very tough decisions to make which can alter the course of the game. The voice acting (English) was a little wooden from some of the characters (Dandelion, Triss and to some extent, Geralt himself) but it didn't diminish my experience any. The dwarves in particuliar were great as they are vulagar as f*** and are always good for a laugh. I am also happy to report not a single character took an arrow to the knee.<br><br>Sound -&gt; 8\/10 [Creative SB X-FI Platinum]<br><br>The sound effects were very well done, especially in combat. Ambience while walking around really pulls you in. From the idle chatter in towns to the various sounds while out for a stroll in the wild. I found the music score to be great but there was not as many tracks and got a bit repetitive. The music in the taverns is burned into my memory probably forever now.<br><br>Graphics\/Performance -&gt; 9\/10 [Asus Maximus VII - 4790K - 16GB 1600MHZ - R9290X Crossfire - Freesync]<br><br>From a 2011 point of view, the graphics are amazing, from a 2017 point of view.....I still thought the graphics looked great.(Looked better and ran better than Skyrim all modded up) The enviroments were extremely detailed, in particular the forest in Act I. Textures were fairly hi-res and the lighting was very nice. Character models look good and the clothes\/armour all looked great. Some minor clipping and characters teleporting around abit but nothing major.<br>From a performance standpoint everything was top notch. The game scaled amazing with multi-gpu, I had no problem maintaining 144 fps. (Dips down to 115-120fps in some places) With that being said I played alot of the game with a single gpu with the framerate locked to 90 fps because my monitor only can run Freesync up to 90hz. If I ran Freesync on with multi-gpu, the framerate would just rocket out of control (sometimes to 200fps) turning my room into a sauna. I ran all settings on max except the Uber setting, which just tanked performance, at 2560x1440 resolution. I experienced about 5 crashes in 45 hours of gameplay which is exponentially lower than the Bethesda games I've played on the same system.<br><br>Gameplay -&gt; 7\/10 [Normal Difficulty]<br><br>The Good: The overall feel of the game was generally good. I was able to map the keys in a way that I was not fumbling around like some games. The combat was improved from the first one. It feels rewarding as you start to accumulate better talents and equipment. By the end cutting down 6+ enemies at once was easy using a combination of weapons and signs. I didn't use alchemy much as I couldn't be bothered to meditate everytime I wanted to drink a potion. Also it is nice you don't need to be near a fire to meditate anymore.<br><br>The Bad: The quicktime events are horrible and whoever puts these in the game and continues to use them to this day should be shot. I was kind of let down by some of the mini games, especially the dice, you can't win!!! The economy is sort of crappy. I found a rare elven sword and I went to sell it and I was given enough money to buy 5 apples from another vendor. WTF? If you are a completionist and need every book and every crafting diagram (which I will do on another playthrough) you better enjoy killing harpies for hours on end. Money is scarce. I believe there is a mod for it which I may use on my second playthrough.<br><br>Conclusion -&gt; 8.5\/10<br><br>I would recommend this game to anyone that even had their eye on it in the slightest. You can always pick it up for cheap. Halfway through the first act I realized I should had the difficulty alot higher since normal was a leisurely stroll. I put 45 hours in one playthrough and like I said I never went through it with a fine toothed comb. I see myself putting one maybe two more playthroughs so I could easily have over 100hours in it before I never look at it again. I recommend play the first Witcher prior to playing this one because it sets up the world and events happening. I felt that this game felt smaller in scale compared to the first Witcher. I felt that way about Mass Effect 2 &amp; 3 as well. But still, 45 hours for a game I probably paid 5 bucks for on a Steam sale is great value.
