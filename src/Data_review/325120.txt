SinnerS is the first Angels Blue novel published on Steam, and a fairly solid entry at that.  Despite being clearly inspired by Higurashi When They Cry, the intriguing story, imaginative ideas and gorgeous artwork make up for a somewhat shaky translation.<br><br>The story opens with Yuhito Asakawa trapped in a dream-like world filled with death and horror.  He discovers his childhood sweetheart amidst the mayhem and attempts to escape together, only to end up lost and alone before he awakens from what was only a nightmare.  He soon realizes that the nightmare may not have ended as young, mutilated girls begin appearing throughout town.  Determined to catch the murderer, his pursuit will lead him on a nightmarish journey that will change his understanding of the peaceful world he's always known. <br><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=460089412\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_0\">http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=460089412<\/a><br>What started off as a fairly generic slasher tale ends up a lot more complex and involved than I was expecting.  Everything experienced early on is turned on its head as details and explanations emerge.  Plenty of clues are there, but without knowledge of the inner workings of this world it's hard to guess at the ultimate outcome.   This isn't a bad thing; far too often I figure out these stories well before they've ended.  But too many curveballs lead to a sense of incredulity when improbable plot developments seem to come out of nowhere, not to mention the liberal use of <i>deus ex machina<\/i> to conveniently move the narrative forward when the story has written itself into a corner.<br><br>That said, there are plenty of interesting ideas explored here, and the horror elements are visceral and terrifying.  Many of the bad endings have the protagonist in creatively horrific situations that will make your skin crawl.  The mythos is also imaginatively conceived and leaves the door open for more stories in this interesting universe.  My biggest disappointment, however, are in a few intriguing story elements that were hinted at but never returned to.  In some ways, these ideas were more interesting to me than the overall narrative.  Ending the game without exploring them left me feeling  shortchanged, like I was missing an important story point that explained away some of the plot holes that were never addressed (<span class=\"bb_spoiler\"><span>Why was Rika so strong? How did the cave keep Nanami alive?<\/span><\/span>). <br><br>Things start off slow, however.  There's enough horror and mystery to hook you early on, but the plot takes its time to draw you in.  It often feels like Yuhito is wandering between random, unrelated events with little clue on how to piece them all together.  It wasn't until Chapter 6 (out of 11) when a back story begins to materialize that I became fully engrossed.  And let me tell you, once some of those revelations hit, you won't be able to pull yourself away.  But the challenge is getting to that point.  I blame part of the slow burn on a shaky translation that fails to engage the reader with more descriptive writing.<br><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=473275148\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_1\">http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=473275148<\/a><br>For the most part, the dialogue and narration is acceptable: everything is clearly understandable without any glaring grammatical errors.  However, I feel the translation likely followed the literal meaning of the original language too closely as the style can be stilted and plain.  While the graphic depictions of gore and violence were extremely vivid and creative, the bland dialogue often left me bored in between these instances of deliciously gory detail.  There were also many points where characters repeated themselves over and over.  Perhaps this was done to emphasize the emotional impact of certain scenes, but I suspect it may have been the result of linguistic nuances lost in translation.  Cutting out some of these lines would have smoothed out the narrative flow and reduced a lot of the melodrama.  Writing tone was also a little too casual in some areas with wording that was far too light for the themes present.  Utilizing words with heavier connotations corrects much of this, especially in darker scenes where conveying the severity of the situation is contingent upon them.  <br><br>Story and writing aside, the artwork is phenomenal.  Character sprites are beautifully detailed and creative, all appearing as if they came right out an official Japanese visual novel.  Event CGs are equally as gorgeous with emotive imagery that greatly enhance the scenes they take place in.  My favorites have a slight impressionistic edge to them which helps convey fear, despair and pain much more effectively than words alone.  The backgrounds range from unremarkable interior buildings to truly breathtaking outdoor views and abstract images that build the chilling atmosphere spectacularly. <br><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=480795418\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_2\">http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=480795418<\/a><br>The novel is quite long.  As a slow reader, it took me 10 hours to finish my first play through plus an additional 10  searching for all 14 endings.  And you'll want to get them all as there are important details hidden within each that shine a light on several enigmas earlier in the game.  Depending on your level of determination, you could spend upwards of 25 hours unlocking every ending and achievement, which is impressive for a novel with a $10 price tag.<br><br>Despite any misgivings I had with the translation, Notch's intriguing mystery, creative horror, and imaginative ideas were enough for me to overlook deficiencies in its writing, which is saying a lot as I consider writing to be the most important element in visual novels.  Would the game have been much more enjoyable if the translation were better?  Absolutely!  But if you can get past the problems, you'll find plenty to entertain you.<br><br>Bottom Line: Recommended for visual novel fans who enjoy creative horror storytelling with high production value to back it up.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Note - This game was provided by the publisher with the request to write an informative review.
