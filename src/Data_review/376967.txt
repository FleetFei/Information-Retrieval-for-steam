An Honest Review<\/div><b>UPDATE: The honest review is now available in video form. Check it out here: <a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"https:\/\/youtu.be\/KlbxIuyLgd8\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_0\">https:\/\/youtu.be\/KlbxIuyLgd8<\/a><\/b><br><br><b>Introduction<\/b><br>I think it's time I lay this Gary Dolzall &quot;controversy&quot; to rest. For those who don't know who the guy is, Gary Dolzall is an old railfan who has done work for Dovetail Games recently, with his articles on the Train Simulator website, and his scenario packs on Steam. Back in 2012, when TS2013 was released, TS introduced Steam Workshop support, where everyone can contribute to making scenarios for Train Simulator. Gary Dolzall (known as Elphaba in the Steam Workshop), has worked on over 250 free scenarios, and has over 1,630 followers, making him the most popular Workshop scenario creator. I consider his work to be from average to good, however there are other better scenario creators out there who've stood up to Gary's scenario quality and have tried their best to get on top.<br><br>Now, in the year 2016, Gary Dolzall has gotten so popular, that he started creating paid scenario packs, in which I didn't like when it first came out. But after purchasing the latest two (NYNH and Donner Pass), I changed my mind, and his scenario packs weren't as bad as you think. So, in this review, I'll be doing an honest review of the NYNH Scenario Pack by Gary Dolzall, and see if it's worth the value. I'll be covering all 10 scenarios in this pack, so...there kinda might be spoilers for those who haven't bought it yet. Anyways, enjoy my honest review of the NYNH Scenario Pack!<br><br><b>Scenario #1 and #2: Amtrak Train 148<\/b><br>This scenario is split into two parts. Part 1 is about 55 minutes long, while Part 2 is about 50 minutes long. In Part 1, you drive an Amtrak train from New York Penn Station to Stamford, while making a stop at New Rochelle along the way. In Part 2, you finish your trip from Stamford to New Haven, while making a stop at Bridgeport along the way.<br><br>The first scenario is pretty well done. The amount of AI is decent, and there are some interesting moments, such as a chase of a CSX freight train. You'll start seeing Amtrak trains when you go through the first half of the scenario, however while you're going through the second half, you'll start to see Metro-North trains coming. As for the second scenario, it does have its flaws. The amount of AI is somewhat decent during the first half, however in the second half, there's BARELY any AI. However, that won't stop from interesting moments, like seeing a CSX freight train. Most of the AI in the second scenario are Metro-North trains. Both scenarios make use of the NYNH route and Metro-North M8 DLC (for AI use only), so you'll need both of those in order to experience these two scenarios. Other than the flaws in Part 2, the first two scenarios were relatively good.<br><br><b>Scenario #3: Bridgeport Bound<\/b><br>This scenario makes use of the Metro-North M8 DLC, but also uses the Metro-North P32 DLC for AI use only. This scenario is about 50 minutes long. This was the scenario that DTG employee, Matt Peddlesden played on his Twitch streams. In this scenario, you drive a Metro-North train from Stamford to Bridgeport, while making stops at South Norwalk, East Norwalk, Westport, Green's Farms, Southport, Fairfield, and Fairfield Metro along the way. Near the end of this scenario, you would need to take your train to the yard.<br><br>This scenario is relatively okay. The amount of AI is somewhat decent, with mostly Metro-North trains, and a couple of Amtrak trains and Metro-North shuttle trains as well. No freight trains though. Overall, it's not too bad.<br><br><b>Scenario #4: Shuttle from Stamford<\/b><br>This scenario makes use of the Metro-North P32 DLC, but also uses the Metro-North M8 DLC for AI use only. This was actually the FIRST scenario I played with the P32 locomotive. The scenario is about 30 minutes long, so it's a relatively short scenario. In this scenario, you drive a Metro-North shuttle train from Stamford to South Norwalk, while making stops at Noroton Heights, Darien, and Rowayton, along the way. But before that, you would need to prepare your train by coupling the passenger cars and cab car to the back of the locomotive at Stamford Yard.<br><br>This scenario requires you switch in the yards to get your train going, which in my opinion, offers an interesting challenge. As for the AI, there's not much, but it's enough to keep you going. Half of the AI are Amtrak trains, while the other half are Metro-North trains. It's a pretty short, yet decent scenario.<br><br><b>Scenario #5: MNCR: All Stops Eastbound<\/b><br>In this scenario, you drive a Metro-North train from New Haven to Stamford, while making stops at all stations between the two cities, on a rainy summer day. This scenario is about 1 hour and 10 minutes long, one of the longer scenarios in this pack (alongside the Oak Point Night scenario, which I'll get to soon). This scenario makes use of the Metro-North M8 DLC, but also uses the Metro-North P32 DLC for AI use only.<br><br>This scenario is one of the hardest in this scenario pack; I managed to get 999 career points in this one. This scenario does offer somewhat of a challenge, but it's basically just a generic &quot;stop at station, continue, stop at next station, continue&quot; kind of scenario; it gets boring after a while. On top of that, the AI is LACKING during the middle of the scenario. Near the beginning and the end, there is a somewhat decent amount of AI, but during the middle, there is almost NONE. Most of the AI are Metro-North trains, with some Amtrak and Metro-North shuttle trains as well. Overall, this scenario is OK, but it gets boring after a while due to lack of AI and repetitive stops.<br><br><b>To read the full review for this pack, click <a class=\"bb_link\"
