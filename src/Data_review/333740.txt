In fact I actually enjoyed a lot of aspects about it. It's not Unity engine fodder by any means, and utilizes the engine quite well. There is a decent story set in WW2 thats focused on the Japenese side, which is new and refreshing, and it has high potential for it being early access. However, there are quite a few issues that come with it. While the ambience is pretty good, most monsters roar loudly wherever theyre placed in the map, and even louder when they die. Killing them in mass results in an ear ache. The campaign seems to have unlimited ammo for the only weapon you have, which is a pistol. To counteract that though, you die in one hit, and the monsters are faster than you are. In the horror aspect, there was a handful of moments i felt tension. Walking through a jungle filled area not being able to see what was ahead was a bit nerveracking, especially since you die in one hit. And towards the end there was a holdout where the amount that rushed at you was a little intense. The cutscenes seemed to not work for me, it just  stayed in one frame where there was only audio so i had no idea what the hell was going on. I understand there will be more acts but the campaign was only 20 minutes or so. But a fun 20 minutes. Decent voice acting, and the gameplay runs quite smoothly.  As for the survival mode, a lot can be tweaked. While fun, theres only 3 maps. 2 where you can barely move from one position and one where its a very small area, but definitely the most enjoyable to me.  The survival aspect has limited ammo, keep moving to ammo pickups to survive. the rush of the enemies does add tension, but like i said, the ear ache is real. and the amount of corpses to see is obnoxious. While fun to stand amongst the corpses and bathe in the blood of your enemies, the lag is present. not gamebreaking, but annoying. However it is addicting for me to go back and try to beat my high score. all in all i have high hopes for this game, being in early access, theres room for more improvement, possibly more weapons,
