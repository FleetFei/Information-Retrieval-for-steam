This game was given to me by the developer for review purposes. This did not influence my review in any way.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">==========================================================<br>\n---------------------------------------OVERVIEW-------------------------------------<br>\n==========================================================<\/div>Note: Earth Liberation is currently in Early Access. The version reviewed was the latest as of the date of this review (20161226): Version 0.5.<br><br>Earth Liberation is a combat-oriented Real-Time Strategy game from new indie developer, PoRovoz Studios.<br><br>In Earth Liberation you play a commander of the Human Resistance, fighting back against the invading reptilian aliens. What that means, in essence, is that you're the person who sends countless waves of human soldiers to crash relentlessly against the alien hordes, in an endless tide of bullets, rockets, and mortar shells.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">==========================================================<br>\n----------------------------------------QUALITY-------------------------------------<br>\n==========================================================<\/div>Graphics - The first thing you'll notice about the graphics is the title screen, which has a very nicely modelled alien space ship. The in-game units are nicely modelled and animated, as well.<br><br>The introduction falls a bit flat after the 'wow' factor of the title screen, but it's functional and looks okay. You'll probably only ever watch it once, anyway.<br><br>Other than the normal resolution and full screen \/ windowed setting, graphics options are limited to a single detail selection: low, medium, or high. I ran in 1920x1080 on 'high' on my new gaming laptop and didn't experience any issues with performance.<br><br>Sound - The first thing you'll notice about the sound is the title screen music: there isn't any! There's also no sound or music through the introduction and the whole thing felt very weird being in absolute silence. I assume music will be added to both of these sometime before the game's release.<br><br>In-game music is very nice: a sort of atmospheric chill electronic track that reminds me a bit of Jean Michel Jarre. I found my foot tapping and my head nodding to the beat on occasion.<br><br>Game sound effects are okay, but limited to weapon sounds. There are no unit acknowledgements or movement sounds, which seemed a bit strange, particularly at the start of the game. These would be a good addition to the game before it leaves Early Access.<br><br>Interface sound effects seem a bit loud in comparison to the game sounds and music. A bit of volume normalisation might be in order.<br><br>Gameplay - Earth Liberation plays like an older RTS game, similar to the early Command and Conquer titles. There's a focus on training\/building and deploying troops, rather than on resource gathering and base improvements, and because of this the levels are relatively quick to play. You won't often find yourself playing one level for hours on end. The only resource is money, which you earn at a steady rate as you play. You'll also receive some 'free' drops on some missions, which give you a boost to begin with. There don't seem to be any building or unit upgrades, either.<br><br>Unfortunately this focus on combat means that most levels quickly degenerate into a cycle of building as many troops as you can before the next alien wave and, after taking out any aliens you find, sacrificing your nameless marines on suicide runs against the enemy turrets and buildings.<br><br>Controls are a bit fiddly. The game uses keyboard and mouse, but for some reason introduces the use of the 'A' key and left clicking to attack, rather than just right-clicking like basically any other RTS game. Right-clicking does work against buildings, but against enemies your troops will usually end up running into the fray rather than attacking. The old shift-click doesn't work to attack while remaining in place, either; it has to be 'A'. There's no way to rotate the view, and even panning only works with arrow keys or moving the mouse to the screen edges; WASD can't be used.<br><br>Pathfinding is a bit rubbish, too, making your troops need far more babysitting than they should. Strangely it seems worse when using the A-click for attacking than when just right-click moving, although one would think the pathfinding algorithm would be the same either way. Given the focus on combat, controlling large groups of troops is something you'll need to do often, but when you have to navigate around any corners or terrain obstructions, you'll often find stragglers being left behind; they seem to just give up and stand still until you select them individually and tell them to move again. There's no way to set waypoints or see what path will be taken to a destination, either.<br><br>Another annoyance is that there's no grouping of units, so once you receive airborne troops it becomes a nightmare trying to separate them from the ground units so you can send them off to do their own thing. The developers need to add grouping by selection as well as quick select by unit type to remove these frustrations. But the game is still in Early Access, so I'm hoping these features will be added before final release.<br><br>The story is nothing new: powerful alien species invades Earth and wipes out most of the defence forces, leaving bands of resistance fighters left to try to win back the planet. It's done reasonably well, but the in-game text has a number of errors in it and could do with some editing.<br><br>There are a few little bugs, too. The worst are the poor pathfinding and units that just stop and stand around while their buddies are dying in numbers around them, but I also found that ground units can attack raised buildings by shooting rockets through the terrain (see one of the screenshots in my profile for an example). They're not game-breaking problems, but now is the time for the developers to address them, while the game is still in EA.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">==========================================================<br>\n-------------------------------------PROS &amp; CONS------------------------------------<br>\n==========================================================<\/div>Pros:<br>+ Great unit graphics and animations<br>+ Excellent in-game music<br><br>Cons:<br>- Poor unit AI: pathfinding and reaction to enemies needs attention<br>- No music in the intro or on the title screen<br>- Needs some unit response sounds, movement sounds, etc.<br>- The story text needs some work<br>- Very limited options for strategy<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">==========================================================<br>\n--------------------------------------CONCLUSION-----------------------------------<br>\n==========================================================<\/div>I'm not a big fan of RTS games, and Earth Liberation is a good example of why: it always seems to me that the 'strategy' part of the genre should be in quotation marks, since in games like this the only viable way to win is to build hordes of units and go kamikaze on the enemy. That aside, though, Earth Liberation knows what it wants to do and, mostly, does a pretty good job of it. If the developers can fix the pathfinding and unit AI so that your marines always go where they're told and actually do something useful once they get there, this will be a pretty fun game. And the price is about right, too.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">6\/10<\/div><br><div class=\"bb_code\">For more recommendations by LL's Game Giveaways and Reviews on quality indie games or ones with
