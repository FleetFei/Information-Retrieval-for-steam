among my all-time favorite PC games. In my opinion these are still the best submarine simulation games I have played. They have the right mix of simulation and playability to give you the feel of being in crontrol of a sub without some of the minute details that make some sub sims unplayable. For instance, you don't need to worry about running aground and sinking when you are on the big map, though you do need to keep an eye on your fuel tank. These games date back to the early days of computer gaming. The first copy of Silent Service which I owned ran on a 5.25&quot; floppy disk in one floppy drive with the DOS OS running from a similar floppy in the other floppydrive, for in those days hard drives were still unknown. Windows had not been invented yet and there was no such thing as a computer mouse. A lot has changed since those days, but these games are still very enjoyable if you are able to go through the learning process. There is no tutorial such as we know tutorials in modern games. Back in those days the operating manual was the tutorial and you learned the game by reading the manual and trial-and-error. For those who, like myself, played this game many years ago and remember it well, you can skip to the back of the manual and refresh your memory on the keyboard controls and be back stalking convoys with very little time practicing to refresh your skills. For those new to the game, it is well worth spending some time to look through the manual and at least skim some of the basics. A thorough read will give you some very useful information that will help you get up to speed faster once you begin play. I was not able to get the game manual link in Steam to work, but you will find .pdf files containing the English and German versions of the original game manuals in the installation directory once you have downloaded the game. On my PC running Windows 8.1 the path for the SS2 English game manual is &quot;C:\\Program Files (x86)\\Steam\\SteamApps\\common\\Silent Service 2\\Manual (English).pdf&quot; Good hunting,
