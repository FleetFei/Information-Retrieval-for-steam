Any experience accumulated in missions is converted into points which you can use to purchase new gear for your Helldivers ranging from different weapons and upgrades to strategems, which is where the game boasts it's tactical depth. Stratagems are specific abilities you can call in mid mission, you do this by holding down your map button and inputting a sequence of button combinations, not all too disimilar to entering cheat codes, these relate to the strategems you chose to bring with you on the mission of which you can carry up to four. Strategems come in three flavours, offensive, defensive and utility. Offensive stratagems are generally attack based such as calling in an air strike, napalm and orbital lasers. Defensive strategems take the form of AI controlled gun turrets, mine fields, defensive walls and other such tactics. Utility strategems are the most varied, these can be weapons, vehicles, mechs, ammo drops, or even extra equipment such as an energy shield, a jet pack or a utility drone. The drivbe to unlock all of these really gives you an incentive to keep playing.<br><br>As for the Helldivers themselves you unlock aesthetic gear as you level allowing you to change their appearance slightly by picking from a range of helmets, armor and cloaks that don't stray too far from the stormtrooper motif that the Helldivers represent. To best describe them they're like a mix of the Imperial Guard from Warhammer 40k, the Stormtroopers from Star Wars and the titual army of the movie\/novel Starship Troopers. They're basically an army of elite soldiers fighting for the totalitarian regime of Super-Earth that seeks to &quot;liberate&quot; the galaxy, see Helldiver's has a very tongue in cheek style in the way it parodies imperial governments and the propaganda they utilise to push their agenda. You'll often hear your characters yelling about freedom and liberty as they blast the hell out of the three enemy races and it's played for laughs as you're very clearly playing as the bad guys masquerading under a facade of political benevolence. They may say they want to liberate the cyborgs, wipe out the threat of the bugs and establish diplomatic relations with the illuminates but what they really want to do is quell the cyborg rebellion, breed and slaughter the bugs for fuel and steal the illuminate's tech and they don'tr really go to great lengths to hide it, this is all told to you through news bulletins as the campaign progresses that really give the game a snarky charm that wants you to be in on the joke.<br><br>All in all I can't recommend this game enough, I've sunk a lot of time into it playing with friends and randoms online and I've enjoyed every minute of it. I really hope Arrowhead puts together a sequel in the future as the creativity displayed here would yield much potential in a future installment.
