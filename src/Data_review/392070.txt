controller, I suppose it's much better. The main flaw with a keyboard is that there are no hints in-game as to what does what. It's simple enough to figure out though: Arrow keys are one stick, WASD are the other stick, shift (either one) is a modifier button and space bar is to take impulse. Once you figure those out, that's it. <b>Oh, needless to say, there's no way to change the key binds either.<\/b><br><br>With that said, I have the impression that keyboard makes it much harder to do some of the tricks. Instead of using only two or three fingers to different directions, you ought to use at least eight fingers. That gives your brain a knot! haha. Especially since some tricks are stick diagonal, which in keys mean pressing AW or WD at same time, together with an arrow, potentially shift, and space bar at the right time. Good luck with that. XD<br><br><b>b) Levels:<\/b> They are good fun and well designed. I've seen people complaining that there aren't enough, or that the game is too short. There are 5 groups of levels, each having at least 8 levels or so? Hence not so little. With 1 hour of gameplay I haven't even finished the 1st group yet, so we're talking at least 5 hours to complete them all. I think I'll take longer. But I'm taking my time, and I'm trying to clear all challenges in all levels, in all three easy\/medium\/hard. If you don't do that you can zoom past all levels in a few minutes I guess.<br><br>But the big thing is: After you've done all the challenges in the 3 difficulties, there's not that much to do I guess. Unless you create your own challenges, and come up yourself with new and wicked tricks of increasing difficulty, or try to beat your own high score, that kind of thing. But I can see myself losing interest rather quickly after completing the levels. <b>User made levels would make this game a true gem worth playing many many hours!<\/b><br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">All the rest is good fun!<\/div><br>The physics are marvelous. I like that the physics and tricks are &quot;realistic&quot; in a way, not too crazy or bogus. The tricks feel smooth and very satisfying when you land just right. The art is pretty cool. I catch myself laughing at the crashes fairly often. It's very amusing and fun, at least for some time.<br><br>If you like these kinds of games, just get it. I am a big fan of games like the flash Bike Mania series, and have tried many clones and similar ones, and Pumped BMX is by far the best and the most fun.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Edit: about physics and pedalling<\/div><br>I see reviews of people not recommending this because you can't pedal your bike. Wut?<br><br>It's just a game mechanic, indeed you can't. Anyone who knows BMXs know that if you're doing tricks at speed you won't pedal anyway because the gear ratios are just wrong. So it's all about getting the right impulses with your body to launch and land, and that's marvelously simulated in the game! It's great, and it works.<br><br>Press space bar to give a first impulse down hill. When you release space the dude will throw his body up, so you need to time your release with your jump for maximum air. Once in the air, press space bar to bring your body down again, that adds a lot of impulse downwards. It's very satisfying.<br><br>Sure, if you fail and you scrub all your speed you can't pedal and the dude will just come to a stop and jump from the bike. It's weird at first but it's a game mechanic that works. If you'd start pedalling mid-level from a stand-still you wouldn't have enough speed to clear the following jumps anyway. Time your impulses right and keep your momentum, it's a great mechanic and it feels great when done right!<br><br>Also, some tricks kill momentum. As they should. A 360 in the air will slow you down for example, much more than a backflip. A bar spin barely slows you down, a table top slows you quite a bit more. So in the more difficult levels it's all about gathering momentum in the first 2 or 3 jumps in order to get massive air in the latter big jumps. If you try to put too many tricks into the first few you'll not have enough speed to nail the last ones.
