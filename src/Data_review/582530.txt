But Wacky Spores: The Chase tells the boring story by completely shattering the fourth wall and providing a lot of fun moments throughout the journey. Now that we have gotten that out of the way, let\u2019s see how the actual running and gunning hold up.<br><br>Wacky Spores: The Chase lets you control three mushrooms at a given time who can run, shoot and perform special attacks. You are given a specific amount of time to run and dodge the enemies in your path while trying to collect coins and select which path will your mushroom be in the next turn. In the battle mode, you can choose to attack the bugs with your blaster to get them out of the way of your mushrooms or drink potions and do other stuff. Every mushroom has a different special powers like creating singularity, shooting canons and others.<br><br> These powers take some time to recharge after use and while you are waiting for these to recharge and you have enough health, the best technique is to target the bugs which are nearest to your heroes. While there is not enough depth in these turn based battles, these are fun to play through especially later on when different bosses arrive to hinder your run. By collecting coins you can unlock various characters and buy potions to use in your run. You can also complete side objectives in your run to get some bonuses.<br><br>While the combat part of Wacky Spores: The Chase is fun, the same cannot be said about its running mechanic. In an endless runner, you expect some thrills of dodging some close calls but here you won\u2019t find it because of its design flaw. When the timer of the running starts the three mushrooms have to run at the same time while dodging and collecting coins at the same time. The movement feels clunky and since there is not much excitement during that part, it quickly gets boring and repetitive.<br><br> The game is also pretty grindy in the start and takes some time to really get going. Another flaw with Wacky Spores: The Chase is its hit detection. While your heroes can aim anywhere if there is nothing blocking it, there were times when the bullet would simply miss despite it clearly showing that it would be hitting its mark. What frustrates more is that sometimes bugs from other lanes can also hurt you and take your life quite quickly.<br><br> The majority of enemy types are bugs which come at you relentlessly but some enhanced bugs like the thief bug can easily keep you on your toes due to its tendency to steal the coins you\u2019ve fought hard for. <br>While it is nothing revolutionary, the battles are good enough that you can pick up and play the game for a few runs if not for a longer time duration.<br> <br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Visuals<\/div><br>While it is nothing spectacular, Wacky Spores: The Chase is a vibrant game full of colors which gives a relaxing feel to it. The backgrounds are well done and the character models all look pretty good. It has a arcade like feel to it and does justice to its overall theme.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Sound<\/div><br>Wacky Spores: The Chase features different sound cues for different places. All of these are done pretty well and is a nice addition to the game.
