Blitz and want the good stuff, just scroll down to the Pros\/Cons section. &lt;3<br><br>Nova Blitz is one of the few Online CCGs that manages to carve out a unique niche for itself in the post Hearthstone era. As someone who has played NB since early, early Alpha I've had the pleasure of watching this game go through it's many, many transformations. From the days when Energy used to be in card form ala MtG to when creatures sported ungodly statistical numbers even at early levels. So I can say with complete confidence that the game you see here is a long running work in progress, and very much the best version of the game ever presented.<br><br>Curious? Well, allow me to be concise then so we don't waste your time.<br><br>Gameplay Summary:<br><br>Like Hearthstone, NB uses a One-Energy-Gain Per Turn. . <br>Like Magic the Gathering, it utilizies a faction\/color system. <br>After that, the similarities end.<br><br>You're allowed only two of the five facions per deck and (as of now) there are no 'neutral' factions. Unlike any other card game at present, both players take their phases in unison. This allows for a chaotic yet very tactical style of gameplay where you have to consider what your opponent might do, what they might have, and how your hand matches up with or without initiative.<br><br>Games are fairly quick. Lasting anywhere from 3 to 5 minutes on average. (There are slower deck that can boost this average, but even a control heavy game likely will not go beyond Turn 12 maximum.)<br><br>Pros &amp; Cons:<br><br>P's<br><br>-Big, big, BIIIIIG plus here for any Hearthstone player reading this. The Devs actually NERF and BUFF cards, in a timely manner no less!<br><br>-And while I'm addressing that playerbase. That RNG-factor most post-Hearthstone CCGs are know for? Not gonna find it here. There are very few 'random' effects and none of them are game-deciding. The ones that do exist you can mostly play around as well. (Play out your units early to counter Goblin Fireworks, hold your units until your opponent plays their Blastbarrel Ogre, ect.)<br><br>-The Development Team is one of THE most accessable for any CCG on the market. They respond to their forums, Steam forums, Discord chat, ect., ect. in a very timely manner. Not even Blizzard responds as quickly as they do to bug errors, feedback, ect.<br><br>-The Development Team actually does consider player suggestions. Reasonably, they're not going to act on each and every one. But if you have a really, really good idea it might wind up in the game. The effect  'Double Block' is one such proof of this.<br><br>-You can earn quite a few cards for free. If you're having troubles playing against humans play against the AI for a while. Quite literally I've played over 100 AI matches and it took quite a long time for the game to stop handing out freebies. Not just Commons or Rares either. Once you have what the game considers a 'base set' even the AI games will super rarely toss out an Epic. No to even mention the free Smart Packs you'll get from Achievements while doing so.<br><br>-You can, and WILL win with the cards you recieve to start and earn against VS AI games. Most of the learning curve comes from figuring out when to attack or block, how to synergize your deck, ect. The card pool is deep enough to allow diversity but shallow enough to prevent you from being swallowed whole by it. Take it from an old veteran who hasn't earned enough cards to make anything other than 'random' decks yet. The playing field is pretty fair. (Minus a rare exception or two, which I cover in Cons).<br><br>-And hey, most important yet objective Pro in the batch. Nova Blitz is FUN. Like...legitimately play and smile fun. Can't say that about many multiplayer games anymore.<br><br><br>C's<br><br>-As of writing this, there are no Daily Quests or anything to help spike your income should you only be able to play for a small amount a day. Granted, your first win seems to always give you 50 gold. But after that the rewards are kinda small for play-and-dash players. <br><br>-If you weren't part of the Kickstarter Campaign, you might wanna shell out a little money in the shop just to fluff up your collection. Even though I list this as a con; the price points for their \/month options are pretty fair. If you buy all three bundles ($30 total) you get the following (assuming a 30 day month).<br><br>*35 Smart Packs<br>*7000 Bits (The crafting currency)<br>*3500 Gold (10 Drafts or 28 Packs)<br><br>-Certain Decktypes, and their counters, are very Legendary Rarity heavy. Want a Construct deck? At the time of writing this; that'll set you back at 'least' 6+ Legendaries. A whopping 24000 Bits, not even counting Epics. Want to counter a Construct deck? Most of the good answers are also Legendary (such as Nature's Brawl card). So a 12000 minimum investment.<br><br>Thankfully, such decks are very rare right now and might possibly be adjusted in the future or their counters might be made more readily avaliable to the common player.<br><br>-At the time of writing this, certain small Quality of Life features are missing. Such as a 'Disenchant All' button for your spare copies. At present you have to manually go in and deconstruct each card on a case by case basis. You cannot search for 'Power\/Spell' cards without having the units that effect them pop up as well; and there's enough of them to turn that into a visually scrambled mess depending on which faction you're rooting through.<br><br>_____________________<br><br>That's it for me. class=\"postedDate\">Posted: October 25, 2016\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t<div class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNova Blitz is a fun and fast-paced card game with similarities to both Hearthstone and Magic: the Gathering. Unlike those games, however, in Nova Blitz both players take their turns simultaneously.  This can lead to a quick series of actions as players act and react.<br>As well, the combat system is bluff based, making for fun strategic decisions. When combat starts, each player decides what they want each of their units to do in the combat. They can attempt to attack the opponent directly, attempt to block an enemy unit you think will be attacking, or just not participate at all. So to make the proper decisions you will need to get in your opponent's head and try to determine the choices they will make. Of course, they'll be doing the same thing, getting in your head, which may change their decisions from what you think they'll do! Choices are not revealed until they are all made, and then combat resolves. The player with Initiative has their attackers resolve first, in the order that they were declared as attackers (this can be important! some units have effects that could change other units in combat!). Then the other player's attackers resolve. <br>You gain Initiative by NOT being the last person to play a card in that turn. This leads to interesting decisions in the main phase of the turn, too. Do you play cards quickly before your opponent so that they'll give Initiative back to you? Or do you wait to see what they play so that you can react to it but lose Initiative?<br><br>The cards themselves are very fun. There are lots of neat and varied effects, including some which affect cards still in your deck, which is possible due to it being a digital CCG.
