All too often I see a user on the forums calling for heads to roll after he reports a player suspected of cheating and nothing happens. The paranoid player would do well to remember that suspicious does not equal guilty and that Firefly will not disclose the outcome of any investigation to the player who filed the report. <br><br>If it appears nothing has been done it's likely because the investigation turned up nothing illicit and remember the more information you include in your report the more likely the accusation is to be investigated. <br><br>The other half of blame sits squarely upon Firefly's shoulders in my opinion due to the sheer ambiguity of the game rules. Time and time again I've had players coming to me with multiple accounts firm in their insistence that they weren't breaking any rules because they &quot;had premium tokens in play on both accounts&quot;. <br><br>Despite what anyone might say to convince you otherwise the above allegation is flat out untrue. Regardless of premium tokens, players are only allotted one account per game world. All Firefly here has to do is update the game rules page to be less ambiguous in regards to the IP restrictions and premium players clause but they will not and this apathy towards one of the top complaints about Stronghold Kingdoms promotes the continued perpetuation of the myth that Firefly doesn't care and that they don't investigate informed allegations against suspected cheaters. <br><br><br>Pay To Win - The belief that to succeed at any level in Stronghold Kingdoms the player must spend liberal amounts of money to obtain their own virtual crown. <br><br>Pay to Win (or P2W) is a hotly debated topic in many 'Free to play' games and the same applies to Stronghold Kingdoms and there is no doubt that in this case it's a grey area for sure. But to answer whether or not the game is p2w one must first define what winning means to them and what winning means to Firefly. <br><br>For many players 'to win' is to beat another player in a one on one mano a mano fight. Providing both players are of the same skill level and neither receive any outside help there is no doubt that whichever of the two players spends more on the game will ultimately be the winner. There are just too many cards in the Stronghold Kingdoms deck that artificially boost combat stats such as army speed or the strength of castle structures and Firefly doesn't help the matter by continuing to introduce more powerful combat cards all the time. <br><br>Sure, the only players who use those aces are probably the top 0.5% but in worlds with dwindling playerbases those players hold the entire server at their collective whim. <br><br>In contrast, many experienced players realize that winning is actually more than a one on one fight and that loosing one battle doesn't mean the war is lost and that there are dozens, if not hundreds, of players participating on both sides of the conflict, carder and non carder alike. This means that while you are going up against a house with spending players your house will likely have spending players of it's own which levels the playing field. <br><br>Firefly's definition is similar to the above, Houses compete against one another to gain glory, ultimately eliminating all others houses to be the last one standing. That final House is considered the winner of that age and inducted into the hall of fame while their members all share in on the spoils of the victory which mostly consists of a few quest wheel spins, card packs, and new charges for their coat of arms. <br><br>This means that to 'win' Stronghold Kingdoms all one has to actually do is be a member of whichever house is left standing when the dust settles. <br><br>As you can now see the definition of winning in stronghold Kingdoms is actually quite subjective and by paying attention to ongoing house politics virtually anyone can achieve victory regardless of their financial status. <br><br>## Now that that's cleared up.... ##<br><br>Although my Steam time doesn't show it, I have played Stronghold Kingdoms via the Standalone client for almost 3 years now and in that time I have been active in many roles. The AI is quite predictable and stupid at times. <br><br>- The ingame chat system is so unintuitive it actually drives players away from the game. <br><br>- The entire card system requires too much micromanagement. For a game that requires more and more of your time as you grow having to juggle cards daily to stay competitive gets pretty old pretty quick. <br><br>- The formula used to generate AI castles needs to take the number of players in that parish into account, not only the rank of the players. It's a real kick in the head to get the strongest wolf castle in your parish every week when the only 3 villages there are yours. <br><br>What I'd like to see added in the future:<br><br>&gt; Castles and villages merged into one design, this would mean that defensively strong players would have weaker economies and the inverse for economically strong players. This would encourage greater diversity in playstyle. <br><br>&gt; Actual RTS battles that allow direct player control of the attacking armies and the defending garrison if the player so wished. <br><br>&gt; More units, especially siege units. Even the original Stronghold had twice as many units as there are currently in Stronghold Kingdoms. <br><br>In short, yes, I strongly recommend this game. It's probably the best free to play RTS on Steam at this moment, and while the graphics hail from a previous decade they still retain the charm of the original Stronghold. <br><br>- IGN
