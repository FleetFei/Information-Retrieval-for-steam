game is based on a patch released on 17th September 2015, meaning that some of the problems I have with the balance of this game might  be changed if you are seeing this review on a later patch :) <\/b><br><br>Starting off, I don't consider this game pay to win like a lot of people are saying. The people saying this probably got matched into a couple of bad games and thought that the cards that other people had were unlocked through in game transactions. I got all of necessary cards by simply playing the game.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"> Gameplay &amp; balance <\/div>In terms of gameplay, the game is extremely unbalanced and can be hard for new players who don't understand how the level scaling works. When starting off, only having 3 cards and not having a solid strategy, the game can seem boring and unfair (not saying it isn't, but maybe not as much as the first impression might suggest). It generally consists of throwing out your robot and reaper in the lvl 1-2 range, beating your opponent in 30 seconds if they put their resources into production and didn't plan to counter your attack. Until lvl 7ish the game will consist of these cheesy strats, from my experience it seems you get random cards from matchmaking and so you might have a bad experience between these lvls, but by the time you get to around lvl 7ish you should have enough of the cards to make a solid deck. The problem I have at this point is that the &quot;Sabotage&quot; card is way too strong and completely controls the game. Whoever uses this card effectively, stopping your opponents resources and research will win. There are not enough cards in the game to allow varied decks, and the Sabotage card has to be in your deck at the higher levels or you lose. This is the main problem I have with the game, there is no where near enough variety in cards making the game boring after you get to this point. <br><br>I will give the game some credit, it can be fun for those who hate the large amount of micro-management in modern rts games but like the idea behind it. Getting a strategy together with different cards and finding the best way to manage your resources can be fun, but it still doesn't help the fact that there isn't enough variation in the games mechanics for it to be fun for over 10hrs.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"> Bugs and &quot;Bugs&quot; <\/div>So what about how the game runs? Does it have noticable bugs? What about the community?<br>Well I can't say there is much positivity here. The game runs fine which is somewhat expected from what is essentially a mobile port. The big problem comes with the random crashes and some weird bugs that will force you to restart the game. Can't say much about the crashes except that they are random and somewhat frequent, never found a cause, never found a pattern as to when they happen. Adding to this, me and some friends who played the game encountered a bug in which the game would stick on a loading screen after a battle. The only way to get past this as far as I know is to restart the game. This bug seemed very frequent when I first started playing the game, though it seemed to happen less and less the more I played the game.<br><br>So onto the main bugs I encountered in the game. The bugs are mostly just annoying, but annoying is still taking away from the game experience and I think its important to point them out. First up is a wierd bug in which your heroes will just teleport back and forth as they are going along. This can be a problem when using spells on your heroes, but its not a huge problem outside of just being really annoying. Somewhat similar to the first bug is the strange way that rooms work. Resources seem to update very slowly, clicking to build a room can be slow with the building appearing a second after clicking to build the room. Probably doesn't seem like a big problem, but it just makes the game seem so out of sync and awkward. Final bug I've encountered a few times is a bug when searching for a match. So you want to play a game against another player, click play and wait... and wait.... and then you get impatient so you try to cancel the matchmaking and it does nothing. Turns out it was glitched and it was probably not searching for a match anyway. So you have to restart the game and click play again, takes 10-30 seconds and you are in a game. Again, its mostly just annoying but when annoying is so frequent in a game its hard to start liking it.<br><br>So I'll quickly mention the global chat and move on. Most of it is russian which is fine because you are not very likely to make friends on this game anyway. The problem comes with the random spam and racism that appears in the global chat. There is no community for this game so don't expect to find other people willing to make friends.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"> Conclusion <\/div>So if you want to pass the time with a fun little game I would say go play another indie title like binding of isaac or FTL. But if you don't have the money to get those titles and you happen to come across this game, give it a try. I enjoyed what I played then got bored really quickly. It has bugs to fix and needs to sort out its balancing, but its free to play and has a very small file size, so just try it out.
