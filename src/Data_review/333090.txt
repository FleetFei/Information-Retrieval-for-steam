As advertised, perfect for a puzzle game, especially for something similar to <a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"https:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/linkfilter\/?url=http:\/\/rpgmaker.net\/games\/7110\/\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\" >Account Mu<\/a>.<br>Since this is a pack of music, I'll review each song one by one.<br><br>1)A song less traveled-This is one of my favs &lt;3 It just feels so calm and happy. Would be really useful for a colorful game, town or some sort of lobby.<br>2)Endless-Again, pretty calm and upbeat. Would probably not suit a town or a lobby like a song less traveled, but could still be used for a nice colorful puzzle game!<br>3)Mysterious Mayhem-A little less puzzle-y and more vast. Tbh, I don't think this could be used for much. The thing I don't like about this song is that it mixes cyberpunk and fantasy which makes it so that you can not use it for either.<br>4)Obscurity-One of the darker songs in the pack, this is pretty good for a mysterious area, or for giving some atmosphere before a boss battle.<br>5)Quizzical-The song itself is awesome, however it makes the same mistake Mysterious Mayhem did, but this time instead of mixing fantasy and cyberpunk, it mixes happy and sad. It does have the perfect tune for a memory sequence, but it just makes you think ''would I use this in a happy or sad memory?''.<br>6)Sand Falling-I can't really say that the title suits this song. You'd think this would be a desert styled music, but it isn't. It has a more visual novel or tamagotchi feeling to it. Just like most other songs, could also be good for a puzzle game!<br>7)Slight Suspicion-This one is pretty dark while still having an upbeat theme to it. It's great for the final levels of a puzzle game, as it gives me that impression at first.<br>8)The Riddle-Another cyberpunk-ish theme, atleast this time it has no fantasy mixed in it! It does feel like it would be great for a calming scene in a sci-fi game.<br>9)Unobtained-Another slightly dark tune. It does have a horror feeling to it, but could be used in a fantasy rpg!<br>10)Wondering-Oh god, I'm getting the underwater vibes already! This one is possibly the calmest song on the list.<br><br>Before finishing off, I'd like to point out that you should get it when it has a sale. In my region, it's 10 try, which is the same amount of songs in the list. And with that money I could get atleast 3 games from my wishlist. Luckily though, I got it when it got an 80% discount, and I feel that this price is waaaaaay more suitable. One downside of it is that most songs are short, mostly below a minute or a little above it. It isn't too much of a problem, but it does make me wish they were atleast slightly longer. Do get this if you are making a puzzle game, or just want some upbeat music in general, the songs are really well done and cute.
