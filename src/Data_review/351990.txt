Racer<\/div><b>Developer<\/b>: FOAM Entertainment<br><b>Publisher<\/b>: FOAM Entertainment<br><br><b>Introduction<\/b><br>==========<br>It's not often that I take interest in a game that even remotely resembles a racing game. However, Riff Racer is one of the extremely rare exceptions. Using your own music (or the included bundle of 25 songs) to generate racetracks, Riff Racer has you racing the music itself to earn points and coins. Your experience depends entirely on YOU and what music you load up into the game.<br><br><b>Gameplay<\/b><br>==========<br>Riff Racer creates racetracks based on the songs in your music library. When you boot up the game you can direct the game to any folder on your PC with music in it and have it generate a special track specific to that song. All the different verses, choruses, drops, and more are styled to the music you load and become checkpoints.  Even the car you drive shares some characteristics of the music. <br><br>The point of the game is to stay in time with the music and score as many points as you can. Staying in sync offers a 4x modifier, while being ahead offers a 10x modifier. If you fall behind, either by not using boosts or hitting obstacles, your modifier drops until you can catch back up with the music. Each track you load has a 1 to 5 star rating. The more stars, the more obstacles you'll face on the track. <br><br>As you race on each track, you'll run into barriers, jumps, and pits that may impede you. There's also these small white cubes that fill up your boost meter. Collect 10 of these to get a boost at the press of a button. There's also coins to collect that are used to buy different cars and paint jobs for them. The points you earn go towards increasing your Driver Level. All this does, as far as I know, is show how much you've played the game. I've not seen or found any benefit to having a higher Driver Level.<br><br><b>Graphics<\/b><br>==========<br>This game is pretty psychadelic. Sounds and colors everywhere, just blaring and blinking in time with the music. Mostly various shades of red and yellow, but it honestly looks pretty awesome. The backgrounds look pretty cool, as well as the buildings and structures you'll ride past. A small detail I noticed that's probably really obvious is that your modifier and your boost meter are shown on the back of your car. As your modifier goes up, your car will crackle with energy.<br><br><b>Sound and Music<\/b><br>==========<br>The music depends on you. Play using your favorite songs for maximum enjoyment! I like video game music, so the vast majority of the songs I played are from some of my favorite video games. The gameplay demo videos below feature a Mega Man X remix and a track from Ys VI.<br><br><b>Performance<\/b><br>==========<br>Riff Racer ran nigh perfectly on my custom rig. No FPS drops, played at 60+ FPS at nearly all times (dropping ever so slightly during Super Sync and Boosting), and no stuttering. I played mostly with my controller, but it's easy enough to play with a keyboard if you don't have a controller. You just have to steer and hit the boost key...simple enough right?<br><br><b>Final Thoughts<\/b><br>==========<br>This is an awesome racer, even moreso if you play with your favorite tracks! Pretty much anyone can enjoy Riff Racer. You can tailor the game to your liking with different songs and car customization. If you get the chance to buy this game, I urge you to pick it up...especially if it's on sale.<br><br><b>Gameplay Demo<\/b><br>==========<br><a class=\"bb_link\"
