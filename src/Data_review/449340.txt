target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\" >http:\/\/vnvortex.co\/2016\/04\/15\/serafinas-crown-review\/<\/a><br><br>Released back in May of 2015, Serafina\u2019s Crown is a fantasy visual novel with branching storylines and strategy game and dating sim elements. It\u2019s available on Windows and Mac OSX via Steam for $7.99, includes Steam achievements and trading cards as well as partial English voice acting. Serafina\u2019s Crown is a sequel to 2014 title Serafina\u2019s Saga, but playing the previous installment in the series is not necessary to get to the action. It is however available for free on Woodsy Studio\u2019s website, should one wish to play it first.<br><br>Serafina\u2019s Crown is a title that has been brushed off by quite a few visual novel fans on Steam, which one might assume springs from the non-anime art style used in the game. This is a shame, since underneath it all, there is a very decent game with an interesting and varied storyline. Like said, the art style will most definitely split opinions into two camps, but it also gives the game an unique look as well. While there are few sprites that do look rather ridiculous, in general I found the art being fine and won\u2019t have all that much to complain about it.<br><br>On Steam Serafina\u2019s Crown is marketed as having full English voice acting, but this is not the full truth. The voice acting is something between full and partial, meaning that while most of the lines are spoken, there are also many that are either just few words long, or just scoffs. I found this a bit confusing on my first playthrough, thinking that the game wasn\u2019t functioning as it should. It also eats out some of the immersion when you\u2019re expecting a spoken line, but won\u2019t get one \u2013 only to get one after that.<br><br>Otherwise the audiowork is fine, and while I personally didn\u2019t find the in-game music all that memorable, the soundtrack is sale on Steam for $3.99 for those who do. But no complaints is a good thing, meaning that at least the audio managed to do its job and not irritate unnecessarily.<br><br>And in this corner\u2026<br><br>The highlight of interactivity in Serafina\u2019s Crown is the debate system, which allows the player to have a debate regarding the current state affair at hand by using numbers and their values in order to crush their opponent \u2013 or be crushed by them. The system at its core is very simple: the round starts with one party choosing the direction of the arrow which indicated whether lower or higher number wins. Afterwards player and the computer start choosing their numbers and possible house-related perks.<br><br>It all might sound a bit confusing, and for the few first rounds it is, but afterwards players will find themselves dominating the debate field, especially when aligning fully or at least hardly with a specific house. And this brings in the problem: the debate easily becomes a gimmick that, while not fool-proof, is really hard to lose at. For a casual player who might want to run through only a route or two this won\u2019t be an issue, but since the game has multiple endings 26 achievements to collect, completionists will surely just start to sigh at the debates at one point or another.<br><br>This is not to say that I would be complaining about the length of the game, quite the opposite. Serafina\u2019s Crown lasts 4-5 hours on the first playthrough, and collecting all achievements will easily take 10 hours, give or take. This means that at $7.99 Serafina\u2019s Crown is a visual novel with great price-to-hour ratio.<br><br>In conclusion<br><br>Serafina\u2019s Crown can easily be recommended if you\u2019re able to look past the Western artwork (that is, if you have problem with it in the first place) and only partial voice acting. The branching storyline provides an incentive to replay the game multiple times, to finally uncover the killer of the queen, as well as to get to see the possible romance options in
