Deathstate, Nuclear Throne, Starward Rogue and Enter the Gungeon are some I've enjoyed, but Flamebreak is one that seems to have flown under the radar, which is unfortunate, as I also think it's one of the best.<br><br>The game takes place on your typical fantasy world, not long after Pyrion, the top god, has finished making the world and populating it with humans, elves, dwarves, minotaurs, skeletons (yeah I dunno either), and other races. Kiting is also pretty important, as even the bulkiest races can't take too much punishment, and you generally can't heal in the middle of combat.
