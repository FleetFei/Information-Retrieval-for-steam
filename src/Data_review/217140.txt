By gibbing many enemies at the same time with a rocket weapon, this will result in a pair of eyeballs smash into your screen alongside a big spew of blood and other body parts (including a lovely middle finger)).<br>Besides being fast-paced, it's just basic shooting! Rather than taking cover, the game rewards moving fast and strafing to avoid enemy fire, while hunting for secrets (which are very nicely hidden *looks at Hard Reset and Shadow Warrior '13*) that are very helpful along your journey, as not only they are a must find to 100% every level, but also give the best guns in order to help you take out the bosses which are quite tough.<br>Gameplay is pretty basic but it's so much fun! You have 3 bullet weapons (the single pistol, the dual pistols and the MP40 SMG) which all have infinite ammo and do not need any reloading whatsoever. And while this sounds like you can just hold LMB until the game finishes, you will not want to do that because the bullet weapons don't do all that much damage, and even some enemies are immune to bullets (the robots come to mind).<br>You have then the rocket weapons (and new patched Shotgun (or Dommstick), which doesn't make much sense, but it's quite amazing and feel like a Dommstick for real!) which have limited ammo but are avaliable pretty much everywhere so that you (pretty much) never run out of rockets.<br>Like I said, extremely basic gameplay but it's so fun to gib everything and the guns do feel very powerful (the Split Missile feels just, epic!) and that's what really makes the game so replayable in my opinion.<br>Moving to the story, it's non-existant (guys, it's a 90's remake ^^) or at least, pretty much non-existant, since you get introduced into it at the start of the game, where you are a HUNT team member (some special forces task force of extreme forces against other forces of enemy force) that go into an Island filled with suspiciously active triads and you must uncover what kind of \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665 they are doing!<br>After the starting cutscene (it's a comic book page flip btw) you get no more story indications except this: &quot;If it moves, kill it!&quot;<br>The end is...weird, it seems like Interceptor (the developers) will release Single Player DLC (paid or free I have no idea). Maybe they won't, who knows, I haven't seen any SP extras (except 1.4's Shotgun) since the game's release which was almost a year ago!<br>Moving on, audio is fine. All weapons sound great, interactions sound great as well (picking up the coins, jum pads making that distinctive sound, etc.) but the voice acting is very cheesy and poor, but it seems like in was on purpose, like on that B-movie &quot;so bad it's good&quot; type of voice-overs. Even the enemies, like Big John on E2L1 which sounds like a poor impersonation of Arnold Schwarzenegger, and it was quite funny.<br>Graphically the game is mediocre. It doesn't look fully realistic, it looks a bit (just a smidge) cartoony and it sets well the mood of the game. I must comment on the gibs which look absolutely terrible, and that is a bad thing since this game is all about the *ludicrous gibs*! Most of them look very poor (the torso gib looks decent).<br>I do have some issues with the game, and they aren't slight ones:<br>The Performance is terrible! The game doesn't look anywhere impressive to bring my PC to it's knees! The game is (like I said) decent at best when maxed out! And yet! I get most of the time 45 FPS, though it may go up to 65 or even 70 but that is quite far and between. Sometimes it drops to 23 FPS when there's &quot;too much on the screen&quot; and when I mean &quot;too much&quot; I mean &quot;not that much&quot;, so the performance is really bad overall, though I must admit it was way worse back a few months ago! A game this fast paced is almost unforgivable for the performance to be this bad! Though the game feels not so high budget, so there you go...<br>Another issue is the bugs\/glitches. The are many clipping issues, some stuff can be exploited, they even let us access parts outside of the map (which they shouldn't) which, just like it sounds, will cause you to go under the map, clip through materials and will FORCE you to reload a checkpoint (or a quicksave, if you remember to do that) which are very far away from eachother!<br>Another gripe is the first person platforming which is absolute \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665t! You move WAY too fast for such precise platforming, which will cause you to rage sometimes. I raged once, though I didnt quit, I just kept trying and nailed it, and that feeling of accomplishment is really \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665ing awesome, I must say!<br>Angry gets stuff done I guess...<br>The enemy AI is \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665 in epic proportions, though it feel like it was on purpose, to catch that 2.5D feeling into full 3D. And even though they are really bad, the game is reall really really REALLY hard! You must pay attention to avoid traps because they will take lots of health and then just a bullet will take you back to a checkpoint you reached half an hour ago!<br>I never tried multiplayer and honestly, I couldn't care less, the Single Player is just so much fun I just don't even look at the Multiplayer icon in the menu!<br>The game is also quite long, I beat it in 11 hours, but the last boss fight... It took me maybe 30 or more retries to get the boss, and I must adress: I killed him because he got stuck in the terrain so it was easy to kill him.<br>Overall, Rise of the Triad succeeds at what a every video game should go for: being fun! In fact I would say it's some of the most fun I've had in the past couple of years and maybe (just maybe) it's one of my GOTY nomenees of 2013 (better than Shadow Warrior 2013, in my totally honest opinion), despite it's massive flaws that really stop it from being the amazing game it could've been.<br><br>Thus I'm giving it 2 scores, a biased and an unbiased one:<br>-Overall, yeah it was fun, but everything about it mediocre except the gameplay and the level design, so it gets an unbiased score of 5\/10.<br>-I found it just so ridiculously fun, gory and replayable that I want to ignore it's issues, though some persist and do break the experience for me, making give it a biased score of 8\/10, thanks to the huge amount of fun I've had with it.<br><br>Fun is really the word here, so go out, buy it for 14\u20ac or 15$ or whatever; or if the issues bother you, wait for a sale.
