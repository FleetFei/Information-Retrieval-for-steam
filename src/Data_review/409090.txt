With beautifully hand-painted artwork, imaginative setting and puzzles that will have your mind in knots, there is plenty to appreciate in this gem.<br><br>The mayor of a technologically advanced steam punk town has been kidnapped, and it\u2019s up to the player to figure out who is behind the plot and rescue the beleaguered leader.  There\u2019s a bit more to the story with some twists and turns to the plot, but the premise itself merely plays second fiddle to the exploration and puzzles.  <br><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=552606426\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_0\">http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=552606426<\/a><br>The first thing that impressed me upon starting the game was how similar it plays to the <a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"http:\/\/store.steampowered.com\/app\/51060\/\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\" >Drawn<\/a> series of games.  For those who are unfamiliar, they feature minimal story which serves as just enough premise for pretty hand-painted artwork and challenging puzzles.  And the puzzles are indeed challenging, assuming you\u2019re courageous enough to play on Detective difficulty.  There is no hand-holding here; players will come across clues during exploration, though it takes a bit of critical thinking to apply them to the puzzles.  Even the descriptions are rather vague, often giving just enough detail to get you started, but little more.  If you find yourself sufficiently stumped, skipping is an option; however the timer is quite long.  I\u2019d suggest sticking with it, as the feeling of achievement after figuring out that particularly puzzling enigma is reward in of itself. <br><br>Although the game has some mild HOG-elements, there are no traditional hidden object scenes.  You\u2019re tasked with searching for a number of hard-to-see puzzle items as you progress through the game areas.  This may be disappointing for those who enjoy the standard HOG gameplay, but make no mistake, the items are difficult to locate and may require a bit of clicking around to find.  There\u2019s no skipping the search either, as you\u2019ll need them to complete puzzles which you simply cannot initiate without. This blending of adventure and hidden object play works incredibly well, however.<br><br>Presentation is very important in any game, and Big Secret does not disappoint.  The artwork is lovely, with creatively drawn settings that have a storybook-like essence to them.  The music is also pleasantly high quality featuring interesting and atmospheric selections that accentuate the adventure elements quite well.  My only disappointment is that the mouse-over text fades entirely too quickly.  As a slow reader, I often have to hover over items 2-3 times to fully read the descriptions.  There are also in-game achievements that would have greatly benefited from Steam integration.  I realize doing so may be difficult for older games, but this was only released earlier this year.  Perhaps it\u2019s something we can look forward to having added in the future?<br><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"https:\/\/www.youtube.com\/watch?v=dxnT0mdZDLU\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_1\">https:\/\/www.youtube.com\/watch?v=dxnT0mdZDLU<\/a><br>All said, I really enjoyed my time with this little title. I\u2019d certainly love to see more like it, especially ones with puzzles that do not belittle the player\u2019s intelligence.  And with the seamless blending of adventure and hidden object play bringing in both types of players, it\u2019s definitely worth a look for the price.  <br><br>Bottom Line: Recommended for both As for objects in the game and such they look pretty nice but again the resolution does lower their quality.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay <\/div><br>Big Secret\u2019s gameplay is just a mash up between your standard puzzle game and your standard HOG (Hidden Object Game), with more of a focus on the HOG side of things. You\u2019ll spend most of your time in the game looking for objects which will either have a basic use to help you progress or will be used in one of the game\u2019s puzzles. The HOG side of things isn\u2019t overly difficult for the most part though it may sometimes take you a while to find objects, really that just depends on how good you are at HOG games.<br><br>The puzzles are more challenging in comparison right from the first one onwards. There is a lot of variation between the puzzles and none of them are similar to each other, making each one interesting. I would give examples of the puzzles but since they\u2019re one of the main offerings of the game I won\u2019t in order to avoid spoiling it for any prospective players.<br><br>Throughout the game you have a hint system as well as the option to skip puzzles at your disposal. The hint system offers you hints (well duh) which usually help you find an object that you need to progress or if you\u2019ve found all of the objects in an area you\u2019ll be given a hint on how to use said objects. Once you\u2019ve received a hint there\u2019s a cooldown before you can request another one, the length of this cooldown depends on which of the two difficulties you select when you start the game. The option to skip puzzles is only available to you when you encounter those more substantial puzzles that I mentioned earlier. The skip isn\u2019t available straight away though so you are forced to at least have a go but after around twenty seconds the option does become available to you (not a huge time to wait if you simply can\u2019t be bothered to do the puzzle). While neither of these have a particularly negative impact on your experience with the game if you use them sparingly, using them regularly will effectively remove the gameplay from Big Secret entirely as there\u2019s nothing to the game, gameplay-wise, other than the puzzles and object finding.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Audio <\/div><br>Both the voice acting and the soundtrack of the game are really good. The music that plays on the main menu is particularly good\/fitting but I couldn\u2019t find any faults throughout the game when it came to the music. The sound effects were about average, they got the job done but they weren\u2019t worth writing home about.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Miscellaneous <\/div><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li>The only graphical option the game offers is a fullscreen toggle, nothing else. Again search an area carefully for all items and all those little places that are hiding items.              <br><br>Puzzles include sliders, assembly, straight logic, rotaters, and mazes?
