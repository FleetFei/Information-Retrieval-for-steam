runner game with a great soundtrack and a brilliant story...if you care to listen close enough<\/b><br><br>The idea of NO THING is very simple: you are a simple office clerk with a message you must deliver to the Ice Queen. The gameplay consists of you navigating levels with nothing more than your left and right keys. Though it sounds easy at first, NO THING is anything but. <br>You start on level one which is an easy introduction of what is to come. You must follow the path in front of you turning left and right when needed. There is a voice speaking to you as you play, telling you things, some which make sense and some which don't (not right away anyway) and a rather awesome soundtrack playing. You'll see images whilst you play that'll change depending on what's being said. You should spend your time playing NO THING listening and looking very carefully.<br><br>The levels progressively get harder, not just from level one to level two, but whilst you are actually playing them. Platforms will move, appear and disappear. Graphics will flicker, glitch (intentional) and change colour. Images will change, enlarge, shrink and move through platforms. Everything that happens is there to test your focus and concentration; <b>this is not a game for those who suffer from motion sickness or anything similar.<\/b> NO THING is all about your perception of things and requires you not only to evaluate your surroundings quickly but to also ignore all of the background distraction. Platforms come in different sizes, some being thin and some wider. There are others which are slanted, some which don't properly connect and some which are slightly off centre. You must be alert at all times whilst playing because even clicking half a second too late could be the difference between winning a level and starting all over again. As you run you gain speed continually though I've not yet worked out if this is linked to the amount of turns you take or how long you've been running for. Your speed can work for you or against you, often being helpful for flying from one platform to others that would have been out of reach at a lower speed, but it also makes things a lot harder as your reaction speed has to increase with it. You can find red blocks in certain places to reduce your speed if things start getting a little out of control.<br>Falling from a platform doesn't always mean death though. Many levels weave in and out of each other and are on different heights so if you can react quick enough you can find yourself at another part of the level and just continue running.<br>Your paths will grow as you progress in the stage and some of them require you to repeatedly run the same path multiple times before something new opens. Often you'll find this will happen quickly and throw you off guard. You can also take different paths, though they'll all eventually lead to the same place, after all, choice is just an illusion in a totalitarian world. <br><br>But the enjoyment of NO THING doesn't just come from the gameplay itself, what really grips you is the story and the way you experience it. Everything, from the dialogue to the music to the images, it just keeps you wanting to play, to find out more, to reach the end. You'll spend the game trying to work out what's going on in this world, with you, and the voice but slowly you'll piece things together bit by bit. <br>I found NO THING to be oddly relaxing. When the gameplay wasn't frustrating because i'd failed so many times, the pressing right and left at the right time combined with the soundtrack was oddly calming. At some points when you find your rhythm the game flows perfectly and the levels are long enough that when they aren't too hard and you know the route you need to take, it just works. <br><br>There are only two negative things I can really find about the game, the first is the lack of some kind of a free runner mode. Given the games intense difficulty it would be enjoyable to have endless (or large) levels to practice on so you don't have to continuously repeat a level you're stuck on until you beat it. This is directly link to my second issue which is the fact that the game can feel repetitive at times if you cannot complete a level. There was one particular part where I was reaching 99% and missing the last turn meaning I had to replay the entire level over and over hearing the same dialogue. Though the voice-over is what makes the game so good, hearing it for the 12th time after doing the same bit of a very long level 12 times is rage-inducing. I think a checkpoint system would help but in the same breath I think it'd take something away from the game. The grinding and repetitiveness is a good mirror of life in a totalitarian regime. <br><br><b>I don't know if it was the intention of NO THING to be a dark mirror of reality but I think it does it perfectly. I've never found a game for $4 that's as addictive as this and I really can't recommend this game enough, the experience has been amazing so far. Though difficult at times, the challenge is worth it. How hard can it be, after all, you are just the simplest office clerk.
