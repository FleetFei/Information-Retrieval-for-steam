I have to say, despite a few reservations, I am not dissapointed.<br><br>I think many reviewers don't understand what this game is trying to do.  This game is not trying to be the next platformer.  It's not trying to be an award-winning story.  It tries to be just a bit of a different experience.  This game just wants its own place in this world, and it deserves it.<br><br>You play as a man who struggles with alcoholism after the loss of his daughter.  There's a story about him being a detective on a case trying to catch a killer with the help of a robot, but it's all just an excuse.  After all, the entire thing is just a metaphor for the internal struggles of this character.  The world he inhabits is strange, imaginative and visually trippy, but the details are vague.  Is he really a detective working on a case and all the details are exaggerated as his mind hallucinates or is the entire thing happening on his head as he tries to find a reason to pick up his life?  Is the robot actually a friend of his or merely a metaphor for his conscience?<br><br>The gameplay is where things get both more and less interesting.  This is a platformer, in the strictest sense of the word.  There are no enemies to fight here, there's only running and jumping.  Your other only action available is drinking.  Press a button (or key) and you take a sip from your (apparently bottomless) flask.  This produces the effect of warping the world a bit, as if you were dizzy, but also makes you faster at running and better at jumping.  The metaphorical effect being that alcohol makes you troubles go away.  It makes it all easier.  Do you leave the bottle in your pocket and face the challenge ahead the hard way... or take a sip and make things easy now... but harder later?<br><br>And this is where I think the game falters.  The platforming is just too easy, so there's no real trade-off.  Things are easy enough that you don't need to sip your drink (except maybe in sections where your character starts to walk slower after a dialogue which, for all I know, might be a bug), and if you do it it's just for the hell of it.  There's also no real consequence for abusing your drinking, as the ending depends merely on a choice you purposely make at the end.  This is another missed opportunity to make the ending only as hard as you tried to make things easy before.<br><br>But, again, this game still succeeds at what it was trying to do and I fully recommend it.
