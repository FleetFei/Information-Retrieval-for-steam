first person puzzler with good sci-fi story and themes. I Definitely recommend getting it if you like similar games - buy it during sales if you feel the asking price high for it's content.<br><br>A really interesting room-based first person puzzle game (similar to the well-known Portal) with some interesting dialogues between levels about intelligence, logic, creativity, morals, &quot;good and bad&quot;, and how a person or a computer perceives these things. It's not a fully-fledged story for the game, so don't expect groundbreaking conclusions about these topics, but they serve a lot to think about, and read after if you're interested. It's still akin of a classic sci-fi story, which is a huge plus in the enjoyment-factor for me.<br>The game's pace is like Portal's, but to be honest the puzzles are simpler - use &quot;batteries&quot; or energy charges shot and retracted by a special gun to activate switches that in turn activate sliders, briges, stairs, doors, etc. I say that they are easy because they are kind of ... small, and in majority of cases (~50-60 levels out of 70) I completed them in 2-3 minutes tops, either for the first try, or by just trying and seeing what's not working and fix it. They are very sequential, often the order of activable things is all what's needed to complete a level.<br>I want to point out a really great thing of the game: it doesn't tell you anything. The game uses 4 kinds of energy nodes to activate stuff, but all do that in a different way, and it's up for you to figure out exactly what they do, and how can they be used. They are not too complex, but seeing them work is the way the game tells you what do they do, not in text or in a tutorial mode. The extra levels (sadly only 7) also use a few new mechanics, figuring out how these new things work felt really rewarding - it would greatly raise the value of the game if they would be implemented into a challenge room-pack of some sort, because the mechanics have so much more promise that they were used.<br>Afterall, it was a pleasant game with a compelling storyline, a few interesthing
