compared to other Total War instalments are unrelated to the improvements Darthmod applied. However, if what I found to be positive in the game pertains to the vanilla, so do many of the negative points I could of come across in a vanilla game, which were addressed in Darthmod. So, ultimately, indeed my review is not based on the overall quality of the product as downloaded from Steam.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"> Way better than the most recent TW\u2019s <\/div><br>Like mentioned above, I consider Empire to be superior to games such as Attila or Rome II, both of which were my very first Total War games (sadly, I didn\u2019t start with the good ol\u2019 ones). Compared to the latter, it seems to have more interesting technologies, meaningful historical events and campaign objectives, as well as an intriguing unit variety. These superior features, so far allowed me to fully enjoy a 50 hours long campaign run, and I\u2019m only at the 45% of it, still fighting and struggling and most importantly enjoying the game. Unlike some most recent TW\u2019s I feel Empire is really <u>rewarding<\/u>.<br><br><b> The technology Tree <\/b><br><br>Before we delve into this, let me assure you I am not discrediting Attila and Rome II because of their theme and period. I am not just a gamer but first and foremost a scholar of the whole medieval period, from 400 to 1450 AD <i> (there is disagreement among historians regarding the start\/end dates)<\/i>. What I love of Attila is in fact the time period, as I know many of the tribes and civilisations included in the game, and I have studied their migrations and customs during the V\u00f6lkerwanderung. Back to the subject, compared to the stuff we have here, I find Attila\u2019s Tech tree really uninteresting, bland, shallow. I don\u2019t remember a single remarkable upgrade that was stuck in my head as something amazing I couldn\u2019t wait getting my hands on. The opposite feelings I have for Empire\u2019s tree: multiple improvements on bayonet types, on formations, or even new infantry technology (rifles in the mid-late game); then we see through the campaign an exciting development of our and other nations\u2019 artillery, both in the engines themselves and the ammo types too. And that is just the military tree\u2026 Which on Attila I find is the most delusional one. The changes and improvements applied by the other two trees are very appealing. <br><br><b> Campaign objectives <\/b><br><br>Short point really. What keeps me going and going on conquering without losing interest for my campaigns is that they match with historical events and conflicts that actually occurred and had an impact on how the world looks now. It\u2019s not just random wandering and slaughtering, which some may like, and I do too, but which gets a tad boring after you run out of fantasy. ETW gives you several possible campaign \u2018missions\u2019 as you walk your path to reconstruct a historical empire or something even greater \u2013 changing the curse of history. (e.g. Austria winning the 7 years\u2019 war, the British maintaining control over the colonies, etc). I think in this case the fault is not the most recent titles of the Total War franchise are shallow, but that the periods treated in the games are missing some relevant background for several of the factions. Maybe it\u2019s just me, but even sailing to Britannia and making it Englaland with my Saxon spears didn\u2019t feel to rewarding \u2013 not as much as defeating the Ottomans with Austria (see the Austro-ottoman wars for further reference). Again, it\u2019s just my feelings and my opinion as for this point, and I can\u2019t blame anyone or anything.<br><br><b> Unit variety <\/b><br><br>I have always been concerned by the extent of unit variety in strategy games, and I must say seldom have I been fully satisfied. Empire Total War is one of these very few cases. I have detailed regiments, which vary depending on the region. I can muster basic line infantry, grenadiers both basic and Hungarian, and the list goes on with line infantry units, first pandurs then grenzers and further on jaegers. (Darthmod mainly adds regional versions of these units, giving some more variety). Of artillery and cavalry units there are many and all work in a different way, although cavalry diversity in Attila was proper enough and siege does not much fit the setting. However as for ETW, I am amazed at the beauty of my army composition, whereas Attila has very bland unit trees for the \u201cbarbarian\u201d factions. If I can tell I have been impressed of some units in that game it\u2019s the Lombards\u2019, and unsurprisingly that faction is in a paid DLC. ;)<br>All in all, this is just how I feel about it, and I
