Some well-known sites like the Cologne Cathedral and Hohenzollern Bridge are represented very well, and there is lots of nice German architecture on display as you go between the two cities. However, there are also some annoying &quot;invisible&quot; speed limits, i.e. speed limits not shown on the HUD or even on the track itself, which will catch you out when you first play the route. For example, just after you leave Cologne (or just before you enter it), the speed limit is supposed to go from 30 to 60, but instead it only goes from 30 to 40. Then when you're approaching the Leverkusen-Mitte station, the speed limit changes from 160 to 120 with no warning when you're about 0.27km away from the station on the Cologne side. Last but not least, on the final approach into D\u00fcsseldorf, the speed limit plunges from 80 to 40 with no warning at the final signal before the station. It's very easy to be heavily penalised for speeding with these sudden and unexpected speed limit changes, especially when you're driving an ICE high-speed train. However, after playing the route a few times, you can work out where they are and adjust your driving accordingly. Once you've done that, you can do pretty well in the career scenarios.
