and I decided to play <i>Mystery of Unicorn Castle: The Beastmaster<\/i> because it was recommended to me. It was a fun game to play, with a decent story and good gameplay along with nice artwork and decent voice acting. Something about the game, to me, felt like it was trying to hard. Some of my issues with the title are nitpicks, and it is, overall, a solid hidden object game that I even played twice for both endings. I will state right now that you can play the game only once and still get a very fulfilling experience. <br><br>The story is rather generic, a small child named Sophie is kidnapped and the main character is a detective tasked with finding her. He is never given a name but I liked him alot, he was full of witty lines and humorous observations of the situation he was in. What starts in a modern world some how leads the detective to a mystical castle that, despite being near wherever Sophie lives, looks like it\u2019s straight out of medieval times. This is never actually explained and honestly could have been avoided if the time period that Sophie and the detective were from was changed. What also doesn\u2019t make sense is some of the items you find in the mystical abandoned castle, like modern day lipstick. Nitpicks aside, to save Sophie you must also help the ghosts trapped on our mortal plane, people who died because of the evil sorcerer who has Sophie. The game has a choice system, either good or evil, and promises that you can change the story by picking your own path. But you can\u2019t. Everything is very linear and the ending barely changes at all depending on the path you take. I was very disappointed by this, why offer choices if they barely affect anything?<br><br>Because of this \u201cchoices matter\u201d feature, I played the game twice. I\u2019ve seen it before and was hoping that in this title the choices would affect something but they don\u2019t. You can choose to decline to help a ghost but they just scold you and you are forced to help them anyway. You can also choose peaceful or violent methods to handle issues, like using a bag to move a snake or a slingshot to hit a bird. In the end, you feel like a jerk for choosing the bad options and little else.  Aside from this, the gameplay is very solid. The hidden object scenes were fun, though I did find some items a bit small to find. Also, you will have to revisit the scenes, thankfully only once, but still wish there were just more scenes instead. The puzzles are for the most part not too challenging but there aren\u2019t many of them. Most of the gameplay is item collecting and backtracking to use said item to then get another item to use, repeated the entire game. The game has a very detailed map making this a lot easier. Also, if two items need to be combined they do it automatically, though they have no initial indication that they can\u2019t be used alone. The game offers four difficulty modes, one being \u201chardcore\u201d where there is no hint system at all which is pretty interesting.<br><br>The game is aesthetically lovely. The artwork is well done as are the animations and cutscenes. My major complaint is that, as mentioned before, some of the items were really small in the hidden object scenes so I did have to squint a bit due to my eyes being not the best. However, nothing was blurry and everything was rendered well. The game also has full voice acting and for the most part everyone does well. The main character talking is always a nice a feature and the actor does a good job bringing the witty detective to life. I will point out, there is an option to turn off the main character\u2019s voice if you want to, but not the other characters. The music in the game is fitting, nothing too amazing but in no way was it bad.<br><br><i>Mystery of Unicorn Castle: The Beastmaster<\/i> is a fun time and a satisfying entry to your game library if you\u2019re a fan of hidden object games. I will say, there are in-game achievements but there are no Steam achievements if you\u2019re playing the game there which is a shame since it would make playing the game twice for both the \u201cgood\u201d and \u201cevil\u201d routes more worthwhile. There are also no Steam trading cards. This game does, however, have a bonus chapter that gives us more backstory on the Unicorn Castle and the mystical unicorn itself. For a game with a main game that you can play twice for a different ending and a bonus chapter, the price point is very reasonable. I recommend this title for hidden object game fans or even new players to the genre.<br><br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">My Rating<\/div><br><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li>Must buy.<br><\/li><li><b>A good game
