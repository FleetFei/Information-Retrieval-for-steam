LogicBots<br><b>Status:<\/b>  Released<br><b>Developer:<\/b>  Incandescent Games <br><b>Publisher:<\/b>  Incandescent Games<br><b>Genre:<\/b>  3D Simulation<br><b>Release Date:<\/b>  8th of February, 2017<br><b>Type:<\/b>  Singleplayer <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Introduction<\/div>Incandescent Games made a hot game for engineering\/robotics fans. Pun intended and as far as I\u2019m concerned, LogicBots can be enjoyed even by a \u201cneophyte\u201d such as myself, who would sooner learn a few more foreign languages than pick up a college-grade maths textbook. Yes, I did volunteer to review a game who\u2019s topic doesn\u2019t really fall into my area of expertise, yet I am always eager to learn new things. Exact sciences may be more or less of a mystery for yours truly, yet curiosity could only be sated through a constant push towards bettering oneself. It goes without saying, that the dev team have started off very well with their Steam debut.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Story <\/div>LB cannot really be categorized as a video game with a storyline, since you don\u2019t have a set location, cast of characters or action\/intrigue. What you do get though, is an open-ended campaign of sorts, in an undisclosed area and time frame. Creativity plays a secondary role to more in-depth knowledge about electronics. The drones or robots you shall build from scratch, are to be tested around specially conceived tracks within warehouses and you must constrain yourself to a given budget. Success is measured in advancing to bigger facilities and  more equipment at your disposal. So stop looking for a story and enjoy the game regardless. Lots to learn so it might just as well be fun, right?<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Graphics<\/div>Fully 3D simulators have been on Steam for many years already. Some look subpar indeed, but fortunately for LogicBots, its dev team skillfully used a handful of assets and they focused on them until you couldn\u2019t complain about any low-res textures or performance issues. Apart from a few typos (such as \u201cfucntional\u201d), I could find no cons to this title's looks. The robot parts, the assembly &amp; testing locations and even the circuit boards you shall wrap your head around, all feature the right amount of details and sufficient feedback, for even the most pretentious of gamers.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Audio<\/div>Sounds are a hit &amp; miss, sadly. LogicBots only has a few sound effects and a repetitive soundtrack that shall force you to listen to your own songs soon enough. Or mute the sounds altogether and focus on programming in a quiet environment. I can\u2019t imagine real life engineers listening to elevator music while tinkering away on a contraption or another.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay<\/div>That\u2019s where LB\u2019s spark is displayed in all its glory. The gameplay is unique since I haven\u2019t had the pleasure before, to step into the virtual shoes of robotics enthusiast outside of a Sci-Fi setting. LogicBots is as grounded into reality as you can expect and by the looks of it, you start out small with seemingly simple designs (consider them highschool projects), only to quickly advance towards drones of a complexity I couldn\u2019t have imagined. And unfortunately, so far we don\u2019t have any flying\/hovering blueprints, so all projects shall be land-based and rolling around one wheels of different types.<br><br>The well-explained tutorials should keep you busy for an hour or two, as you definitely can\u2019t argue with their invaluable help. If you\u2019ve never seen the insides of robot before, you probably can\u2019t reach very far in this game without LB\u2019s tutorials to hold your hand. Don\u2019t even get me started on the circuit programming. If it feels like making\/defusing a bomb, you\u2019re on the right track. It\u2019s both complex and as intuitive as it\u2019ll ever get in a video game. I won\u2019t bore you with the details pertaining specific parts, but rest assured that LogicBots is anything but arcade or flimsy. <br><br>It\u2019s a fleshed out simulator approaching its rarely touched subject, with a user-friendly demeanor and also sufficient complexity without alienating the folks which build such machinery as a real life hobby. Skilled robot assembly and testing, are the ticket to early recognition for many young students and enthusiasts. It is an expensive hobby to boot but as with most things in life, as long as you\u2019re finding peace of mind and pleasure in it, no price is too high. <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Verdict<\/div>I sincerely admire those who can create such gizmos in their spare time and out of their own pocket. It shows the kind of dedication to knowledge and constant improvement, that are not that foreign to me, despite my lack of applied technical proficiency. Ambition carries many masks and you cannot disrespect the labour of those which turn notebooks filled to the brim with diagrams and mathematical formulas into something very palpable and ultimately, enjoyable. The muscle has its well defined role, but it shall never hold a candle to the brain. The game is fully worth its price tag. Go buy it.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Strong Points<\/div>+ Pleasant graphics.<br>+ Complex simulation.<br>+ Comprehensive tutorials.<br>+ Steam Achievements, Trading Cards and Workshop
