point-and-click adventure with the visuals of a pop-up book together into one neat, small little package. The keyword here is \u2018pop-up book\u2019; does it have the depth of a real pop-up book or does it actually tell a deeper story? Hop on to the next paragraph and read along. <br> <br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay<\/div> The game starts off almost immediately. You choose New Game (or Continue if you\u2019ve already played, of course) and off you go. You\u2019re first shown a small \u2018cinematic\u2019 that shows - not tells - a part of the story and after that you can immediately play and eventually finish the game without almost any interruptions.  And yes, you read that right. The game doesn\u2019t use any speech and just a few lines of text to tell parts of its story, and these lines even feel a bit cryptic. <br> <br>You only have to control the mouse to control your character. You just click on the ground and your character will walk towards the place you just clicked. If you can interact with an area small, pulsating circles can be seen where you can grab with the mouse and then you can slide it towards a specific direction to show more of the book, or to initiate a puzzle. <br> <br>The puzzles in general are fairly simple. There were maybe 1 or 2 puzzles that required a bit of attention but other than that these can easily be solved by pretty much anyone.  Especially if you\u2019ve played point-and-click adventures in the past you\u2019ll probably breeze through it all. You can definitely tell that these are aimed at a casual audience. I personally didn\u2019t mind, because the game has a relaxing vibe and it allows you to take your time to do whatever you want. The problem is that there just isn\u2019t enough to do. Except for solving puzzles and traveling through one amazingly, gorgeous looking world there are no side activities or collectibles whatsoever (or anything else for that matter). And since the puzzles don\u2019t offer any real depth and the game is very short this a bit disappointing I have to say. I do admire the developers for focusing purely on the story here, but once you\u2019ve played through this that\u2019s it; you\u2019re done. There\u2019s no incentive to replay this, not even once. On the other side, I know of very little (point-and-click) adventure games that offer any other activities besides solving puzzles and unfolding a story so I can\u2019t really blame it. Therefore, it is excused. <br>In any case, the puzzles mostly consist of moving pieces of the environment around so that you can pass to the other side or by moving certain objects. It all feels natural and the controls are extremely tight, as is the pathfinding of the character. Bravo for this. <br>Now, if you\u2019re too lazy to complete a puzzle, or you just need some help because you really can\u2019t figure it out, the game offers a hint system that helps you on your way. Whenever you arrive at an \u2018important\u2019 puzzle you can bring up the menu to let the game offer you a hint. It won\u2019t really be necessary for most people, but it\u2019s still good that it\u2019s there and it can still be helpful for the younger players out there. <br>All in all it\u2019s a very simplistic point-and-click adventure. <br> <br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Graphics &amp; Audio<\/div> This is where Tengami really shines. With beautiful high res art this game looks nothing short of amazing. I have one small nitpick, though and that is that I wish the camera would zoom in a bit more often because the close up intro \u2018cinematic\u2019 and the ending look terrific with the brilliant use of colors and the sharp art. Yes, even the colors are sublime. As you can see I can\u2019t praise the graphics enough. Think of Paper Mario with more realistic environments and characters and you have an idea what it looks like. <br>The audio is very minimalist and has a Japanese vibe to it. It blends in perfectly with the rest of the game and you\u2019ll enjoy it and not get distracted by it. Again, nothing but praise here. <br> <br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Length<\/div> This is the department where the game fails a bit. I managed to finish the game in roughly 90 minutes but I can\u2019t imagine anyone taking over 2 hours to complete it. And let\u2019s face it, that\u2019s short. Sure, it doesn\u2019t overstay its welcome, but it\u2019s a good and cool experience that I wish would last just a tad longer. I\u2019m not necessarily talking content here, but the puzzles could\u2019ve been harder and maybe some more (story related) cinematics to extend it all a bit. <br> <br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Conclusion<\/div> Tengami is a fine little game that\u2019s a fun, but short experience for casual and experienced point-and-click gamers alike. For me personally the quality of the puzzles dragged the game down a bit but it certainly compensated this by providing a more than terrific audiovisual experience. As far as the story is concerned; I don\u2019t think it will blow anyone away, but overall it\u2019s a charming adventure that you should absolutely give a try if you have the money and\/or time for it. <br> <br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Too long, didn\u2019t read<\/div> Pros: <br>+ Gorgeous visuals <br>+ Audio blends in very well with the rest of the game <br>+ Tight point-and-click adventure
