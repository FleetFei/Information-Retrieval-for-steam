2017<br><b>Type:<\/b>  Singleplayer <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">General Impression<\/div>An Early Access Racer with a lot of issues and equal amount of potential. There aren\u2019t many racing games on Steam using the Unreal Engine 4 and even fewer conventional ones, featuring regular cars powered by internal combustion engines. I\u2019m glad that didn\u2019t stop developer, Tero Lunkka from trying though. He released three games on Steam and Nash Racing is his best so far in terms of scope and attention to details.<br><br>That isn\u2019t to say that Nash is all polished and ready to be enjoyed. Far from that since calling it \u201crough around the edges\u201d would be an understatement. I had to contend with resolution issues, frame rate drops and glitches that would be more suited in Goat Simulator than in a 3D Racer that wants to be taken seriously. There is some eye candy present but don\u2019t expect Project Cars level of detail. Instead, appreciate it as the work of a single individual which has shown the talent and ambition to attempt multiple gaming genres so far, instead of calling it quits after his first flop. The Steam indie scene isn\u2019t made up of just success stories as I\u2019m certain you stumbled across several games filled with blatant lies and equally \u201chonest\u201d devs. I\u2019m willing to give Tero, the benefit of doubt in regards to his current Early Access project. I\u2019ll keep an eye on its progress and if all those promises fall flat, I\u2019ll be sure to readjust my review enough for it to reflect the changes or lack thereof, as the case shall present itself.<br><br>Racing games with vehicle part and color customization options, aren\u2019t a new concept, yet each year less racers seem to offer this kind of freedom. I\u2019m always a fan of adding a personal touch in the games I play so the fact that you can modify to a certain degree, the 4 car model presets currently found in-game, is a big plus for me. Track diversity is also present, along with a fairly convincing damage system but Nash Racing\u2019s single biggest issue stems from its dumber than bricks, Artificial Intelligence. I kid you not, I\u2019ve seen racing AI in smartphone games that seemed like actual simulations compared to Nash. The developer needs to iron out this problem as soon as possible, before focusing on adding more vechicle and racing track options.<br><br>I have decided to recommend it since I appreciate a decent asking price when I see it. Nash Racing isn\u2019t overpriced at all, taking into consideration all that it offers so far. Dev team still debates if they shall increase the pricing when out of Early Access stage or not, but I advise them to keep this price tag and thus contribute to the fledgling niche for affordable indie racing titles on Steam. They won\u2019t necessarily fill the gap, since demand is always on the rise. But at least I can see a spot for this game as well if the developer plays it smart in the end. Long story short, you can safely purchase Nash Racing already instead of waiting a few more months. Just be prepared to wait a bit for some updates or otherwise you shall have to accept an underwhelming racing experience.
