Utilizing quick stabbing abilities and threat generating taunts, you make sure other people in your group are not being eaten alive.<br><br><b>Templar<\/b> - Magician that utilizes electricity and healing abilities. Did not play this class much.<br><br><u><b>Bartuk<\/b><\/u> (Really Bulky Humanoid):<br><br><b>Warrior<\/b> - This game's berserker. You utilize a two handed weapon and slash your foes in two (sometimes quite literally. This game is <i>gory<\/i>, I'll touch up on that later.<br><br><b>Shaman<\/b> - Utilizing flame abilities, you can give your own attacks flame power and add flame damage to others abilities. Didn't play this one much either.<br><br><u><b>Kruxena<\/b><\/u> (Most similar to commonly recognized dark elves):<br><br><b>Rogue<\/b> - Fast, brutal, and relentless. You shred your enemies to pieces with blindingly fast attacks(heh.). You use razor sharp claws or dual swords as this class.<br><br><b>Soul Hunter<\/b> - I never personally played this class, as it's a debuffing class. You magically curse and weaken your enemies.<br><br><u><b>Xenoa<\/b><\/u> (Smaller elvish looking creatures that wear high heels....yep. Even the males.):<br><br> <b>Hunter<\/b> - You utilize a comically oversized blaster and abuse the physics engine by BLOWING your enemies away. Uses ammo and a launcher, chaining abilities. This is the only class I've played where the monsters fly away when they die from how &quot;hard&quot; you're hitting them, as a consequence I had the most fun and am most experienced with this class.<br><br><b>Battlemagician<\/b> - Good at combat assistance with elemental magic. Utilizing mainly short-range magic attacks and &quot;throw&quot; magic when at a distance. Only class I know of in a game that has an ability that lets your throw Hydrochloric ACID on your enemies.<br><br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"><b>Gameplay<\/b><\/div><br> Alright, down to the interesting stuff. Depends upon how you look at it. When you first login, if it's your first character you're asked if you would like to go to the tutorial level. <b>Don't skip it<\/b>, it introduces you to the fundamentals of both your class and the game....well, mostly the game and it's controls, and it's always useful information to have. As you level you gain skill points, which you can use to &quot;buy&quot; skills by the tier for your class, earn enough points in one tier you unlock the next tier and so on. If you've bought all skills in a tier simply start upgrading the skills your already bought, as a couple in the top tier unlock skills on the third tier if fully upgraded.<br><br> Combat is, for lack of a better word, slow. At least in the beginning. I was playing for about 15 minutes before I started to mow through enemies that were giving me trouble just two levels prior, as I was leveling and giving myself skills. <u><b>You start with no skills<\/b><\/u> as you level it becomes easier to chain combos and utilize the rotation for each class.<br><br>  Quests are the same monotonous dribble you get with the standard issue MMO, this one leaning more on the grinding side. After you get past level 15 it starts to slow WAY down, to the point where I bought a XP booster back in the day just to make it seem like I was still making progress. In a game where the max level is 81...this is a problem.<br><br> This game has special monsters that come out at certain times in the game at certain points of the game map, called <b><u>Nightmare<\/u><\/b> monsters. These monsters are tougher, have more health, and actually utilize abilities against you. To give you an example of difficulty scale, in a level 6-7 area you're liable to run across a level 10 nightmare creature, at level 16-18 you're liable to run into a 20-22 Nightmare creature. These monsters give A LOT more experience, and a higher chance of dropping better gear and loot. You will need a full party of eight people most typically at the higher levels to take them down.<br><br> Because this is a free to play game, it has an in-game cash shop where you can buy costumes, mounts, XP boosters(you'll need them if you don't want leveling to feel like a snail race), etc. I have not looked at the shop in depth to determine whether this game is in the &quot;pay to win&quot; category of free to play games.<br><br> I never delved into the PvP aspect of the game so I cannot give a definitive review of it.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"><b>Graphics<\/b><\/div><br>  This game was released in 2008, and the aging graphics engine is starting to make that apparent as more time goes on. This is not an ugly game, but it is very blas\u00e9. When I was in my WoW slump back in '08 I looked at this game and it looked amazing. Now, with better graphics every where, it's outclassed in almost every facet. Textures are very low resolution, characters have too much bloom for my liking, and characters just look too....glossy. The environments have this drab shade of brown that just doesn't go away, even in snowy areas, it's just depressing to look at for long periods of time.<br><br>  One thing that drew me to this game was the <b>gore<\/b>. There's buckets of it. When you kill an enemy their limbs fly off, blood sprays every where, and their body flies away. It gets a chuckle out of me to see it because of how exaggerated it is.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"><b>Final Verdict<\/b><\/div><br> Alright, chances are you just scrolled down here, or you're getting tired of reading a wall of text. I did, I enjoyed it, it was fun, but there are just simply more fun games out there.
