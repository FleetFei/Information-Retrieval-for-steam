The only problem...is that there are indeed some small problems... <br> <br>   The most obvious one, which was also SOMEWHAT present in the games this so clearly plays homage to - though to not quite as frustrating an extent - is the fact that you can't necessarily tell what areas of the screen you can and cannot enter or travel through, until you actually walk up to them and try your luck.  Then there's the little problem of actually spotting objects which you can pick up.  <br><br>   As far back as the early Resident Evil games, the developers had a simplistic - if somewhat cheesy and slightly implausible - solution to this:  Namely, making the items in question GLINT! The items in this game blend in with the scenery just a bit too well, basically forcing you to comb every inch of the screen whilst spamming the &quot;use&quot; (in this case, space) button.  <br><br>   I personally reached a stalemate where I couldn't progress, checked someone else's walkthrough, but alas, couldn't work out just where the hell he got that second key from!  I subsequently proceeded to comb every inch of the game I was able to explore, and whad'ya know...there was a front section to a bus, a section which I never even realised I could enter, and if I hadn't been spamming space at the time I wouldn'a found that key on the bus' dashboard, neither. But perhaps that isn't the aim of the dev on this occasion, and fair enough I'd say.  If it's just meant to be a mildly intriguing, reasonably compelling exploration-adventure game, then it's certainly heading down the right path.  \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665 it, I like it.  I'm nowhere near LOVING it yet...but I like it, and the direction it appears to be going in.<br> <br>   Look, bottom line is it's a free demo, and potentially shaping up to be a quality game, so if anything I've written above even faintly suggests that it might be up your alley, why not give it a super-quick, super-tiny download and have yourself a quick go?  And the game-to-be offers ways to &quot;follow&quot; its progress from there on in.  On the strength of what I've just played, I'd happily pay a few bucks for the finished product - probably not much more than that, but certainly that much - as it certainly succeeds in its aim of evoking memories of a style of game which has long since gone the way of the Dodo.
