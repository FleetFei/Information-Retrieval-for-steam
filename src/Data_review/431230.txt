Lunalie has the power to summon guardians to protect her via the usage of cards\u2014a talent I\u2019m assuming her master taught her\u2014and she\u2019ll make use of it as she continues her quest. <br><br>.Gameplay. <br>This game is played entirely with your mouse.<br><br>In typical tower defense style, it\u2019s your goal to protect a base of sorts by creating units\u2014only, in this case, your \u201cbase\u201d isn\u2019t some stationary building or crystal, but a mage who marches across the battlefield. As such, the units you create have to protect your base from all sides against enemy mobs.<br><br>You select a stage from the world map and the description tells you a bit of the story. Upon starting the stage, you select which units (up to five) you want to take with you. Then, you can choose which unit\u2014or units, depending on how well you utilize your mana, which is displayed at the bottom right corner of the screen and on the cards you use to summon guardians\u2014you initially want to protect you. Then you start the game and Lunalie begins her trek across the map<br>.<br>There\u2019s a blue ring around Lunalie that indicates her range: you can summon guardians and cast spells within it. The guardians are also limited in their movement range by this ring; they will stay within it and have their own attack ranges which can be displayed by clicking on them. In order to summon guardians, you have to drag the corresponding card to one of the available nodes on Lunalie\u2019s magic circle. <br><br>You\u2019re able to upgrade both your guardians and Lunalie. Upgrading the former will add to their health, damage, etc and the latter will allow you to control more units. In order to upgrade, you need to have enough mana and, much like summoning, you simply drag the upgrade card onto the battlefield.<br><br>Lunalie\u2019s spells are available to you on the bottom of the left corner of the screen. Spells don\u2019t cost anything, but they have a cooldown. Simply drag the card and.. Well, you probably get it already. There\u2019s a lot of sliding\/clicking and dragging here. The game is a mobile port after all. <br>In typical mobile style, you get a rating\u2014one to three stars\u2014for each stage you clear. You fill up a bar at the top of the screen by clearing waves quickly and efficiently and some waves can be released more quickly (they\u2019ll have a timer ring around them, so you have to look out) in order to gain extra points. You\u2019ll lose progress towards the next star if Lunalie takes damage. <br><br>*Clearing stages in addition to completing challenge missions (more on that later) nets you gold coins if you complete them in the Tavern tab.<br><br>Stars are used to purchase upgrades for the four unit classes: warrior, range, magic, and stealth. There are three units associated with each class:<br><br>Warrior: Knight, Paladin, and Berserker <br>Range: Archer, Sentinel, and Hunter<br>Magic: Sorceress, Cleric, and Wizard<br>Stealth: Rogue, Dancer, and Assassin<br><br>Upgrades apply to all units that fall into the different categories. You can also upgrade the effectiveness of Lunalie\u2019s spells.<br><br>There are a total of 30 units to unlock and you start off with two. The rest, excluding four you unlock by earning coins from completing quests, are unlocked as the story progresses.<br><br>.Atmosphere.<br>The graphics are cutesy and colourful. Your little chibi units march to battle against little chibi enemies on battlefields that are done in the same bright, cartoony style. <br><br>The music is nothing to write home about. In fact, I typically play with the music muted so I can play my own.<br><br>.Longevity.<br>There are three difficulties: easy, normal, and hard. Difficulty effects how much mana you start a match with and how quickly you gain mana as well as enemy strength.<br><br>There is corresponding achievements for defeating the game on the highest difficulty, so that offers a incentive to add a few more hours on your game clock if you played through on an easier difficulty to learn how best to approach the stages.<br><br>In addition to the campaign, there are challenge stages. These limit your unit usage, number of times you can upgrade, the spells you can use\u2014anything to make the game more difficult for you. Completing challenges gives you coins so that you can purchase new units. It\u2019s important to note that challenge stages don\u2019t have ratings, so you don\u2019t have to do well\u2014you just have to finish. Which is good because, true to their name, they can be quite challenging. <br><br>.Pros and Cons.<br><ul class=\"bb_ul\"> <li>Simplistic gameplay that\u2019s easy to pick up and put down.<br><\/li><li>The ability to change resolutions. These add a layer of strategy to the game.<br><\/li><li>The cutesy character models and portraits are charming. The portraits change when upgrading a unit, which is a nice touch.<br><\/li><li>Boss fights require you to actively command your units in order to dodge oncoming attacks. They\u2019re tense and challenging, allowing for a feeling of satisfaction once you\u2019ve figured out how to best them.<br><\/li><li>The A.I. acts in a specific way and the game tells you that: i.e, Knights attack whatever\u2019s closest and Assassins will protect Lunalie. No stupid A.I. here; they act as specified.<br><br><\/li><li>The game is difficult in the worst of ways. Many of the stages have to be played just <i>so<\/i> or else you\u2019ll fail.<br><\/li><li> No sound options other than to turn it off entirely.  <br><\/li><li>The price point versus the iOS and Android versions. You don\u2019t have to be an aficionado of the tower defense genre in order to enjoy it and the simplified gameplay allows for just about anyone to pick it up.
