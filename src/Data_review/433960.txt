It\u2019s a game that has me very excited for its future. I haven\u2019t really played an old school style point and click adventure that left me in awe for a very long time so to see this game having this effect on me is more than a little surprising. I honestly believe that this game, if development goes according to plan, make it onto our Steamified Select and then Steamified Countdown after its release. I\u2019m a huge fan and thoroughly impressed.<br><br>Thus far there is about three to four hours worth of content here. The most important standard features of a point and click adventure game are very well represented here. The puzzles are logical and extremely well designed. The story itself is so far fascinating although I feel that I am not deep enough into it yet for it to even start pulling all of it together. The dialogue between characters as well as that between the game (main character) and the player is above average. The art work and the sound are strong enough to aide the immersion process. It\u2019s just a very well rounded product. And that\u2019s without ever considering the intelligent innovation that is, for me, the absolute highlight feature of Shadow of Nebula.<br><br>Intelligent innovation in adventure games, especially point &amp; click adventure games, do not come around often.  When they do the game captures my attention. I believe that is what Bisonia have absolutely accomplished with what they are billing as their CyberSpace parallel world game. Early on in the game you get to create the pieces of a CyberSpace scanner. With this scanner you can scan the various people that you meet. The people that you meet early in the game have all had their real life brains scrambled. They have become mentally handicapped due to overdosing on drugs. However, a miracle happens when you successfully complete the scanning mini game. You enter your portal and visit their scanned address. You are able to speak with them through CyberSpace. Imagine if someone was brain dead in reality and you were able to speak to them as if everything was normal in a physical Internet chatroom. That\u2019s what the CyberSpace parallel world game allows you to do. Not only is it innovative but it\u2019s a fantastic feature that really allows the game to push through important story elements.<br><br>It\u2019s this feature and the way in which everything demonstrates that this is a passion project by gamers for gamers that has me thoroughly impressed at this stage of development.
