Akio.<br><br>Sakuya is a woman wishing to be a man since an incident in her childhood. She makes everything in her power to look like one. Her cousin Daiki works for Dreamworld and needs her help to try a new virtual attraction called West Tower, reserved for women. However, Sakuya managed to convince him to let her try the East Tower, reserved for men. She ends with four other testers, trapped in the virtual world, though she's happy to see that she's a man in it...<br><br>This is the same beginning in each Each Tower game. Once in the virtual world, Sakuya will team up with one of the guys. In the first of the series, she's Akio's partner, whose virtual form is a child. East Tower is an attraction that will confront everyone to their own insecurities and fears.<br><br>I will go straight the point: this is a visual novel, where you have choices to make. Each stage must be cleared to advance in the story, you don't have any skills or relationships to improve\/destroy. However, choices are mattering: you can get a bad ending immediately where you have to begin the stage again (not the entire story), a best friend ending (by scoring A on each stage) and a romance ending (by clearing each stage with a S). If you follow the romance route, you'll end up with an extra chapter. However, some stages don't have a A rank, only a S one - in that case, for the best friend route, it's considered as if it was a A.<br><br>Choices are generally made during the challenges proposed for the team. It's much rare to see them outside of them, before or after them. True, without skills or anything to worry, the game is easier than any other visuel novel I've already played (apart Shan Gui, which is more an interactive book), even the first Heileen was more complex and even Blue Rose was more complicated. I don't mind at all this lack of difficulty.<br><br>I find very interesting the fact that the guy will be your best friend at the end, as the bad ending isn't a &quot;you're alone&quot;, &quot;you're killed&quot;, etc.. However, should you choose to pursue him, it means that your character is also evolving and accepting herself more.<br><br>Let's speak about Takashi. He's a sensible young man, that admires his older sister, who seems way more stronger than him and that's why he wished to be her and that he adopted her appearance. Takashi is also the typical comical teenager boy, a side more developed in the romance route: he's hit often by Sakuya, he's slow to understand that she's a real girl and he has every corny line to deliver. However, given the fact that he's very sensible, he's wishing to be strong and the only time he did, well, he managed to get his footh in the mouth because the girl he protected was believing that she didn't need any protection. In the end, the fact the team was a girl wishing to be a boy for escaping some stereotypes and a boy that is wishing to be strong like his sister is very interesting. I largerly prefer Akio than Takashi but well, Takashi can be mature if he wants. Besides, his voice isn't as charming as Akio.<br><br>Indeed, the visual novel has voice acting... in Japanese. I don't understand a thing but as the subtitles are in English, I could follow the story. The only character not voiced is you. However, I find it really good. It's always fun to hear something in a foreign language, it's reminding me some good memories with some anime.<br><br>As for the style, it's really in a manga style, reminding me of The Rose of Versailles or anything directed to girls - because it's clearly stated in the official website that it's a visual novel for them (but everyone is welcome to play it). I loved it. I also loved the HUD, kinda hi-tech and with a good shade of green.<br><br>The music is very good too, very adapted to each scene. I really liked it. And with Takashi being comical, you bet that you heard more the funny music than anything else.<br><br>East Tower: Takashi is a little good moment I've spent, though I wished that it was a little more serious as in Akio, without having to think too much.
