the quests are dreadfully repetitive, and the world art design is nice. As far as MMOs go, it's probably one of the best I've played in recent years. Though please take that with the consideration that I'm someone who is largely burnt out on MMOs after years of WoW and a spurt of others. Thus I tend to avoid them. Though with that mindset taken into consideration, the fact that I was able to be interested in and for the most part enjoy my 30plus hours with Devilian should be taken as a fairly strong endorsement.  <br><br><b>Disclaimer<\/b> I'll start off by saying that my review of this game is based off the 30+ hours (probably way more) I spent playing Devilian during its Beta and Alpha stage. The last time I played it was around two weeks prior to official release. I played the game mostly to completion with a max level Cannoneer on the Beta server. Although my experience with the max level content was limited, what was available was pretty much the same earlier dungeons just scaled up. <br><br>So.. let's get started!<br><br><b>The Good:<\/b> <br><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li><b>As far as MMOs go, this is a prety good combat system.<\/b> If you're familiar with Diablo 3, Neverwinter, DOTA2, LoL, etc.. it's a lot like that, just with more abilities available to use at any one time. Depends on the sort of movement style you choose to play with, I suppose. The game includes options for typical WASD style, or left or right click to move. I personally went with click to move and that worked fairly well. My only complaint here would be that it's very spammy. As in you need to click or hold a button or key to spam out the same ability a lot.  <br>Some games hurt my hand after prolong periods of play, and towards the end of my time with this game, it got onto that list (my problem is nothing major, but it was definitely uncomfortable). If you have problems like carpal tunnel syndrome you might need to get creative with your control setup, particularly where you bind your spamming skills.  <br><\/li><li><b>(World) Art style is good, imo.<\/b> This is of course subjective but I personally liked the artstyle for the world in this game. The game is very clearly heavily inspired by the Diablo series, most notably Diablo 3. If you have played that game you will no doubt find many of the areas around the world and even some enemies to be quite familiar. And I don't view this as a negative, as I did like Diablo 3 and in general I think the influence it seems to have had on this game's art direction is a positive one.  <br>My complaint on the art style would be that I'm not a great fan of the gender-locked classes, although I can understand it being done most likely on budget concerns. However adding gender options in the future is definitely a way this game could be improved upon. I'm especially not a big fan of my favorite class to play, the Cannoneer, being a Loli (little anime girl). The character in this particular game is not as offensive or perverted as they can get though, so it wasn't a deal breaker for me, but I'd still have preferred an alternative. <br><\/li><li><b>It's a good candidate if you're looking for some mindless entertainment when you're tired.<\/b><\/li><\/ul><br><b>The Bad:<\/b> <br><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li><b>The game is far too easy for basically the entirity of the leveling phase<\/b>, and the only real challenge comes when you reach max level and begin running Hell difficulty dungeons with the blues you were leveling with. To me this is bad pacing, plain and simple. A tutorial is something that should last maybe 10, 20, 30 minutes. Maybe an hour or two if your game is really complex. But let's be honest, this is an MMO that plays very much like Diablo 3 or a moba\/dota-style game. The complexity found here is not exactly on the level of Chess. So for you to tell me that the entire leveling process up to max level is a 15 to 20+ hour tutorial or &quot;learning phase&quot; can only come across as an insult. I know this is par for the course for MMOs to have stupidly easy leveling content, but it's still bad game design in my honest opinion.  <br>If you want my advice, skip parts of a zone when you get the chance to move on to the next zone, and tackle dungeons you don't really have proper gear for and are the minimum required level, and you might find a reasonably challenging and fun experience with the game. <br><\/li><li><b>The PvP is completely lackluster. <\/b>Devilian form PVP has you spamming one ability to generate your resource bar and another to consume it, with another 3 or 4 cooldown-based abilities. As you might imagine this combined with lock on targetting on most abilities leaves very little room for creativity and a relatively low skill ceiling. They only started testing regular-form PvP in the last closed beta, about 3 weeks prior to launch, and the kindest way I can describe what was presented to us is that it was an unbalanced mess. Some changes were made since then but frankly I didn't feel a drive to play on the Open Beta prior to release so I can't really say if I would deem the current state playable. From reading the changes made at that time, I do believe it would be significantly better, but I would caution you to know going in that it is a work in progress. But they do seem to be taking player feedback and making efforts to balance that game mode, so that's good. <br><\/li><li><b>Enemy variety is greatly lacking.<\/b> Even bosses are reskinned and copy pasted across several encounters. Then see them again for the Heroic+Hell versions!<br><\/li><li><b>Voice acting was few and far between, and when it was present it sometimes may have been better absent.<\/b> I don't know if they are planning to add any new and hopefully better voice acted lines to the game, but in the Alpha and Beta stages it definitely left room for improvement. I grew to absolutely hate the Warehouse Manager and the generic injured soldier quest-giver NPC that was all over the place for their annoying, often-repeated (one or two) lines. Disable voices or get used to hearing &quot;cough cough cough.. GOT TO STOP THEM!&quot; and &quot;It's easier to lose things than to find them, don't forget that!&quot; (This would also disable other slightly less annoying NPCs as well as your own character voices..) <\/li><\/ul><br><b>The Ugly:<\/b> <br><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li><b>By far my biggest problem with the game was the absolutely dreadful, painfully generic, and obnoxiously repetitive questing.<\/b> Played WoW or one of the many MMOs like it? Remember how much FUN all those &quot;kill x,&quot; &quot;gather y,&quot; and &quot;interact with z&quot; quests used to be? Well for your sake I hope you found them fun, because that's pretty much all that questing in this game has to offer. And you'll be doing quite a lot of it. Have fun!! (The level of sarcasm herein should be smacking you over the head). <\/li><\/ul><br><b>The questionable<\/b> <br><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li><b>The max level grind? You might want to look for a second opinion here.<\/b> This is the one subject where I don't necessarily feel confident offering a conclusive assessment in due to limited exposure to it. From what I saw in the Beta, it was possible to obtain at least entry-level purple (heroic) gear from dungeon through a somewhat reasonable random drop chance, although only from hell+3 dungeons. However I'm not entirely sure how you go about getting the real top tier gear. The only way I saw was collecting tokens via daily quests, which would have taken a very long time to get a single item, which would then have largely random properties.  <\/li><\/ul><br><b>Conclusion<\/b> The game is fairly decent. Definitely not without flaws, but if you enjoy MMOs or did in the past, this one may offer enough with its more interesting combat to warrant giving it a try. It comes with fewer abilities than your normal class. Some forms of PvP take place only in Devilian form. Outside of that, you have to kill monsters to fill a gauge. Once the gauge is sufficiently full, you can enter Devilian form for a short time.<br><\/li><li>There's the persistent world, which is broken up into instanced zones. You can switch between instances of any zone.<br><\/li><li>There are instanced dungeons. You can either solo a dungeon or go at it with a group of up to 2 other players. If you want to run a dungeon with a party but don't have any friends online, there's a dungeon finder tool that will match you with other players. While leveling, you encounter a new dungeon every level or two from level 20 all the way up to the level cap at level 52.<br><\/li><li>There's also PvP. There's world PvP events, PvP world zone instances, 3v3 arenas, 20v20 arenas, and guild vs. guild PvP. There doesn't seem to be much interest in PvP. 3v3 arenas have really long queue times. I haven't even managed to get into a 20v20 because the queue time is so long.<\/li><\/ul><br>Now that we\u2019ve covered the basics of what the game covers, I'll be breaking this review up into 3 parts: The Leveling Process, End-game, and Everything Else.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">The Leveling Process:<\/div>You gain levels by killing monsters, doing quests, or killing other players. <br><br><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li>Like I mentioned earlier, I haven't done much PvP because there's just not that much interest in it. The classes aren't well-balanced, so anyone not playing the Evoker will have a bad time. <br><\/li><li>The quest system is about as basic as it can get. You either kill things, gather things, or interact with objects, like destroying shacks or putting out fires. There are a lot of quests, and there's virtually no differentiation between any of them. The quest text is less than engaging. I'm not even sure what the story of the game is since I skipped all the quest text after about level 3.<br><\/li><li>As with just about every other ARPG ever made, you'll spend the lionshare of your time killing monsters. Each dungeon is static. Each takes less than 10 minutes to complete. There's no Diablo-type random maps. When you run one dungeon once, you've seen all there is to see of that dungeon. There is no limit to the number of regular dungeons you can do per day, which is a good thing since you'll have to grind the hell out of them to get your end-game gear.<br><br>Archdevil dungeons allow two attempts per day. Guild dungeons and the tower dungeon allow for one attempt per day. Guild dungeons allow nearly a dozen players to participate. The tower dungeon contains multiple floors. Each floor you progress to is more difficult than the last. You are limited to doing 10 floors per day.<br><br>World PvM is kind of a mixed bag. There are world bosses that need killing, but they don\u2019t reward much in the way of loot to anyone but the top 2 or 3 players based on damage done and number of deaths. If you die once or twice, you might as well just leave. There are also special areas where you can group with friends to kill elite monsters. Some of these areas also allow for PvP, which makes for a unique experience of killing monsters and players at the same time. This is one of the few areas of the game that I feel really strikes a good balance between PvM and PvP.<br><br>As far as dedicated PvP goes, I have very little experience because there's really not much engagement from the player-base. Devilian form PvP gets stale quickly. Each class's Devilian form has 5 basic abilities with very little depth.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Everything Else:<\/div>Despite what you may have heard, the game isn't Pay 2 Win. The only things I've seen that really upset people are the limited release vanity items and mounts. Most of the store is devoted to selling you glasses, wings, or clothing. Nothing in the store has stats on it, so there's no P2W gear. There are also buffs you can purchase that increase XP, gold drops, and the chance for better dungeon drops. Other than that, just about everything else you can buy in the store can be purchased with currency you earn in-game. It will, however, take a while to buy most of that stuff without shelling out real world currency. It's for this reason that I find this game to fall squarely into the Pay 4 Convenience category of Free 2 Play games.<br><br>Like many Eastern F2P games, there are a lot of subsystems to mess with. Most of them just add to the grindy tedium of the game. There\u2019s the Talisman system, which is similar to the card collecting smartphone apps. The cards give you stats. You can equip up to 5 cards. It\u2019s horribly grindy and less than fun unless you like digital card collecting. There\u2019s an equally tedious gear socket\/gemming system. A system to permanently increase your elemental resistances which requires a lot of grinding for reagents. You get the idea. It\u2019s all minutiae, and it doesn\u2019t add much to the game other than to give you a reason to keep grinding the same 4 dungeons over and over again.<br><br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">The bottom line:<\/div>Whether you spend real money on the game or not, if you like grindy games with lots of (generally tedious) depth, you\u2019ll find a lot to like in this game.<br><br>If you play games for the art and scenery, you\u2019ll enjoy the leveling process.
