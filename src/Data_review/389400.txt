class=\"bb_h1\">Introduction<\/div><br><i>The chronicles of Emerland. Solitaire.<\/i> is exactly what the title suggests, a solitaire game. The most basic of games and one of the few that has come already installed on PCs for years is now given an updated look with updated features. There are many games like this on the market, taking a simple card game and adding bells and whistles to keep the interest of the player and attract new ones. However, in my opinion this game got it right and created something that\u2019s more than just a game of solitaire. <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Story<\/div><br>One aspect that <i>The Chronicles of Emerland. Solitaire.<\/i> has going for it is that it has a story. Your character is an apprentice under an old gray bearded wizard and things are going fine until an evil sorcerer attacks and makes threats against the world. It\u2019s up to you to travel to the four kingdoms to save the elemental stones and gain the power you will need to defeat the forces of darkness. The kingdoms are basic, you have Humans, Elves, Dwarves and Mermaids, but you also get a companion from each kingdom creating a sort of party like in a role playing game. Together you and your team travel the land and try to save the world. It\u2019s a pretty simple fantasy story but enough to keep a player entertained.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay<\/div><br>If you\u2019ve played regular solitaire then you should have all you need to get into this game. The board has cards laid out randomly and you have a draw pile, your goal is to stack cards in order from your drawn card to create combos and score points. If you\u2019ve played modern day solitaire games then you can expect to see some added features to spice things up. Roadblocks, a common one, come in the form of ice, vines, bricks, etc. Each one requires you to unlock the roadblock by finding an item like a sword hidden under another pile of cards. This adds more of a challenge, especially in later levels, as you have to plan your moves precisely to continue on and sometimes may not get the draws you need to even get past the roadblocks. Each level has ten stages to it and in between each level are small hidden object scenes, which I enjoyed.<br><br>I mentioned earlier that the game gives you four companions, each character helps in a different way, the archer for example can shoot down one card off the field which is useful if you have a lingering card and want to get a perfect rating. You can also power up your companions by buying certain amulets! There is one amulet for each party member and then other amulets to add bonuses to the game such as getting more gold or experience or even seeing every possible move on the board. The last aspect is leveling up, when you do this new perks are added that can pop up randomly on cards that are on the board and each perk has two levels to it so instead of being able to get two extra cards you can then get three.<br><br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Graphics \/ Audio<\/div><br>To top off this fun game are some great visuals. The cards are all beautiful though I would have liked an option to change the design of the deck. The scenery is wonderful and fits the mystical theme of the game. Bright colors along with lovely character designs help round out the visual aspects of the game. The music is also great, if you play the game long enough you\u2019ll notice that the tracks tend to repeat quite often, I think there are maybe three or four unique ones. I didn\u2019t mind this but the music was so lovely and soothing I wouldn\u2019t have minded more. The game is also fully voice acted so every time a character speaks you will also hear them. The acting is not the best but it\u2019s passable.<br><br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Pros<\/div><br>+ The game is cheap.<br>+ Offers a good amount of gameplay for the price.<br>+ Overall presentation looks good.<br>+ I love fantasy so this was a personal plus for me.<br>+ The game has two different difficulties.<br>+ It has trading cards and achievements.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Cons<\/div><br>- Levels can get repetitive, so it\u2019s best if played in short bursts.<br>- There is a bug in the game, if you\u2019re playing a level and want to exit the game, the game counts that stage as a failure for some reason. This has been addressed on the Steam forums but the developer sadly cannot fix the issue.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Final Thoughts<\/div><br>I personally loved this game and would find it perfect except for the bug. I\u2019ve played quite a few solitaire games but this one is definitely a favorite for me. I loved the fantasy theme and I enjoyed having party members with different helpful skills. The game can be a little challenging even for solitaire veterans but the game gives you plenty of perks and ways to power up to help level the playing field. Plus, all of the achievements are relatively easy to gain if you simply just play through the game.
