class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOld School Memories:  This is a side-scrolling beat \u2019em up game. These were the absolute bees knees when I was a younger gamer. The fond memories of the genre came flooding back to me from the very moment I began playing here. That, of course, means that games such as Double Dragon, Golden Axe, Final Fight, Altered Beast and Renegade were on the top of my mind. The answer I would immediately ask is how does The Ables: Freepoint High stack up against the classics? Surprisingly for an early access title not too badly. Qiwi Games acknowledges the roots of the title\u2019s genre by implementing 90\u2019s-esque graphics with a soundtrack to match. However, they\u2019ve also recognized today\u2019s standards and are working hard to implement modern expectations. It\u2019s a balancing act but one that they\u2019re well on the way to meeting.<br><br>Almost perfect gameplay: This will be one of those reviews that tracks back to the first point throughout. However, that\u2019s because everything meets such a high level of consistency throughout the game that it\u2019s impossible not to. The game play is tight with one caveat. I\u2019d really like to see the double jump feature improved immensely. It\u2019s going to be pivotal to defeating a number of the challenges throughout the game yet it\u2019s not quite as tidy as it should be.<br><br>Based on a book: The game is based on the best selling book, The Ables, by Jeremy Scott. Admittedly I have never read the book but the game already has encouraged me to pick it up. I would encourage fans of the book to pick up the game. Jeremy Scott is also the co-creator and narrator of CinemaSins, one of the more popular channels on YouTube.<br>Awesome soundtrack: I would be remiss if I didn\u2019t make a specific point about the soundtrack. The score has been created by Ronald Jenkees. Jenkees started out as one of the most talented musicians on YouTube but his rise to fame can be tracked directly to Bill Simmons. Simmons, the famous sports commentator hired Jenkees to create the theme music to his popular podcast. That particular theme is titled \u2018Derty.\u2019<br><br>A lot of work to be done: Currently only four of the seven levels are complete. However, what is available is super fun to play. I\u2019m a big fan of the tutorial level which demonstrates what all of the super powers will enable you to do once the game is feature and content complete. That\u2019s not to say that the price is high for the value that you get at the moment just that there is a lot more content to come.<br><br>The Ables: Freepoint High has the potential to be the very best side scrolling beat \u2019em up in recent years. It may even be on par or ahead of the classics but at this stage as a gamer it\u2019ll be a very tough ask for it to be considered better because of just how much sentimental value those games hold. But for someone who treasured those games I couldn\u2019t ask for much more than what Qiwi Games is offering me.
