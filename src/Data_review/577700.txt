VIDEOKID<br><b>Status:<\/b>  Released<br><b>Developer:<\/b>  PixelTrip Studios<br><b>Publisher:<\/b>  PixelTrip Studios<br><b>Genre:<\/b>  Arcade<br><b>Release Date:<\/b>  31st of January, 2017<br><b>Type:<\/b>  Single-player<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">General Impression<\/div>It\u2019s been a while since I played a decent 3D endless runner, so THE VIDEOKID hit the right spot for me. \u201cRunner\u201d might be a bit of an understatement since you\u2019re actually a skater. This is indeed a cute little title from an indie dev team at the start of their journey onto Steam. As long as you don\u2019t mind the inherent  repetitiveness of this arcade subgenre, you\u2019re good to go. Well, skate.<br><br>A trip down Memory Lane indeed, since the main visual diversity offered by this title comes from the multiple film and video game references it alludes, without actually naming them per se. Obviously that is just one of the many caveats imposed by copyright laws. Alas, you shall be able to easily recognize quite a few characters from the \u201880s and early \u201890s. The large pixel visual style is still pleasant on the eyes and the sounds are diversified more than enough.<br><br>The gameplay itself is somewhat lacking though. In both depth and the initial presentation. No tutorial (apart from a brief start screen that indicates which buttons to push or when), no cutscene and definitely not a very complex storyline or motivation. You\u2019re the title\u2019s eponymous Videokid. A silent protagonist whose apparent mission is to skillfully throw VHS tapes from a distance, while avoiding the many traffic and sidewalk obstacles that will surely get in the way. And yes, apparently the game\u2019s goal seems to be meeting \u201cJessica\u201d at the park. Now that\u2019s something easier said than done. In the good ol\u2019 tradition of arcades from the past (which this game really tries to emulate at least in spirit), THE VIDEOKID is hair-raisingly difficult to progress through, let alone master it. I shall not mark this as a weak point simply because I feel that such a game shouldn\u2019t be too easy. It maintains the legacy of coin-hungry arcade machines which both forced players to focus and learn the game\u2019s patterns and at the same gave a real sense of accomplishment for those lucky and skilled few which could best the title and reach an ever impressive highscore.<br><br>Most certainly the presence of a difficulty slider would have been welcome. In part, THE VIDEOKID is also hard because of the randomly generated obstacles which offer a new perspective and challenge every time you restart the level. And you will have to do that quite often. Distributive attention and multitasking aren\u2019t unheard of in video games, but in this particular case you will often fail the stage by focusing too much on collecting coins (which help you unlock new outfits and items for your skater) or hitting the red mailboxes with the tapes instead of minding the road and incoming vehicles.<br><br>Videokid can aim at many objects and NPCs with grace and doing so is actually encouraged by the scoring system on the upper right side of the screen. You really can\u2019t complain about the pacing but the gameplay itself gets boring eventually. Constantly hitting hurdles and having to restart the entire level, doesn\u2019t help either. At least Unity Engine ensures a smooth and nicely detailed experience. The developer should definitely sweeten the deal by adding Trading Cards and Achievements in a future  update. The price tag is decent but if you\u2019re still not convinced, buy THE VIDEOKID when it shall be inevitably bundled. I\u2019ve decided to recommend the game for its sheer nostalgic value, if nothing more.<br><br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Strong Points<\/div>+ Old school feel with plenty of pop culture references.<br>+ Affordable &amp; challenging.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Weak Points<\/div>- No Steam Achievements nor Trading Cards.<br>- Repetitive gameplay.<br><br><b>Rating 60\/100<\/b><br>
