More efficient crafting and building makes for a much more relaxing and interesting creative building experience. The frustration of waiting for my &quot;hearthlings&quot; to construct scaffolding and ladders and carry blocks one by one to a new building is gone with Kindred because I design and build the houses myself instead after the &quot;Kin&quot; do the work of mining and crafting the materials. There are some camera angle issues and occasionally a hiccup or two after an auto-save but for the most part, even with 50 Kin all running around doing their various jobs, the game runs nicely and doesn't bog down. Again, games like Stonehearth haven't managed to get their AI and pathing to work nearly as well as this little game has done! <br><br>The modern look may turn some people off but I found it to be a refreshing change of pace from games like Timber and Stone, Stonehearth and others where 'fantasy\/medieval' is the main theme. That's fun but it's nice to have a change of pace. Clean lines for the textures and wind generators and electricity are great and work well for the art-style.<br><br>All that being said, this game is VERY early on in it's development and there are some issues that may make you want to wait a bit for more development such as:<br><br>- Simplistic unfinished interface does not always work well yet<br>- Missing or bugged out tooltips not appearing makes it difficult to see what materials you need or are looking for.<br>- Work assignment menu is a little cumbersome and needs work.<br>- Notifications for Kin growing up, growing old, being born or falling in love are either missing or easy to miss if build menu is open.<br><br>Most of these issues I have are interface (UI) related and are definitely going to be fixed and polished by the developers before it's ever close to being finished. If you're good at figuring things out without a perfect interface telling you everything then you'll probably still enjoy this game. I got used to the quirks of it and have now put in 30+ hours of enjoyable building. My Kin Town has nearly 50 Kin and 3 generations of 'natural born' Kin. I love the way the Kin will naturally pair off (gender does not matter) and you can build them a house with beds and a cot and the stork will eventually bring them a baby! The tiny &quot;Pixel Baby&quot; will grow into a child in about 3 game days (approx 3 years) and join the work-force as a kid (child labor!). This reminds me a lot of Dwarf Fortress except the babies stay in their cots instead of the Mother Dwarves carrying them everywhere. A lot of these colony-builder games avoid children and aging but I never understood why. It seems like such a great addition to the gameplay as your community grows. People are born, they grow up, they fall in love and they have kids, that's the circle of life and really helps give purpose to your village, town, city as it grows. <br><br>Lastly, the game is fairly easy right now because resources are abundant and they have not yet implemented 'enemies' of any kind.
