before. Everything is physics based to the point you're not just taking a hit from a hitbox coming from the right, you're taking a hit from the exact part of the weapon that comes into contact with you, and when I say you I mean on your shoulder pad and tank most of the damage, or just between that pad and your bracers and take an actual injury. The actual damage comes from the momentum of the swing more than else too, so that's pretty cool.<br><br>This mechanic is very refreshing because it makes the whole thing feel a lot more immersive than usual. The effects of the weapons and armors you use comes from their shape and weight more than from actual stats, much like they would in real life. A lot comes down to preference. The fighting mechanics end up being easy enough to manage once you get used to the controls, but it's easy to tell that the skill ceiling is pretty damn high. With that said, because it is physics based, sometimes it does end up coming down to luck, to an extend, because a character can mess up a parry on its own and end up taking damage for example. (The characters always tries to parry when not attacking, you just need to face the right angles.)<br><br>The game itself is very hard and punishing. Imagine dark souls without bonfires and only 1 healing item per century. The checkpoints are not really obvious and if you <i>die<\/i>, chances are you just lost a ton of progress. The whole map layouts are basically labyrinths, you don't get a map, and a lot of the rooms look similar....even though people with a wild imagination will tell you that every planks on the floor and every chair on its side has a huge story. Speaking of story, most of it you have to figure out on your own dark souls style, there's only some bits and pieces on random parchment every now and then, giving you some backstory.<br><br>In reality, this is a game you should get into fully expecting to have to restart multiple times as you improve and try to get better. You will end up walking around avoiding most fights and most likely getting lost while trying to find keys more than you'll do any kind of fighting. Just keep that in mind. The fighting mechanics are probably the selling points for most, but the game is very much about exploring a dungeon and trying to avoid damage at all cost more than else, unless you play in arena mode, but even then, death is permanent.<br><br>When it comes to character builds, the choice in weapons is interesting, but when it comes to armor, you want a big armor, no questions about that. There is a skill tree, but because taking injuries is so bad, you don't have a choice but to use heavy armors, which means heavy armor skills, and the obvious offensive skills you need. Not very many options there, outside of do I use a shield or not.<br><br>I eventually finished the story (or as far as it goes for now, early access duh) and I must say I liked the first few levels better. There comes a point where to me, it felt tedious more than else. There was an interesting fight here and there, but for the most part I had to chose between getting lost running around empty rooms forever until I find a specific something, or take forever to deal with the problem cheesy style, in another long and tedious way. I loved more than the first half of the game, but at the end I was glad it was over.<br><br>Overall, I have to recommend the game because the mechanics are very interesting, the immersive feel is pretty intense and it feels very rewarding when you manage to do well, I mean it's a good game, but because there's not really any build variety to try, and it's always the same dungeon layout, which removes the whole exploring aspect after your first run, I find there's not much replay value. The game is still in early access though, so they certainly have more skill ideas and such to add to the game that could change this feeling I get though. I even heard about somekind of magics, who knows.<br><br>I'll sure be keeping an eye out for the open world game the devs are building on this concept, but seeing how the <i>end game<\/i> felt like on this one, I'm not sure what to expect from the upcoming content.<br><br>TLDR: Do try it, it's pretty well made, but the game won't hold your hand, so be prepared to get lost and frustrated along the
