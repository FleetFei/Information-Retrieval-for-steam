It doesn't happen TOO often or else I wouldn't be able to recommend these games.<br>   - Both games are short. Boo.<br><br><br>Now I'll list the differences in Clockwork Man: The Hidden World:<br><br>   - The length is longer. Because of the higher difficulty, possibly much longer.<br>   - This game has a journal where Miranda records her adventures and anything of note. Like its predecessor, the basic goal is to search a series of beautifully drawn scenes to find a bunch of hidden objects. However, the sequel increases the scope of the original, both in length and gameplay. Where the first game lasted maybe 2-3 hours, this one seemed a bit longer (maybe around 3-4 hours, depending on how slowly you take things). The interface and quality of the puzzles have also improved as well. This time, the puzzles are much more involved, and actually require some real thought at times! Amazing, I know.
