2016<br><b>Type:<\/b>  Singleplayer <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">General Impression<\/div>A side scrolling shooter with RPG elements and an interesting storyline. Now that\u2019s a hybrid of multiple genres that shows the versatility of the Steam indie game scene. The developer\u2019s first project as well, so you can bet I\u2019m impressed. If you followed this game since it\u2019s Alpha stage, you\u2019d know that it has progressed quite a lot and from another dreary laser-shooting  game featuring spaceships, Sector Six has opted for missiles and mines, in the end. A more tactical approach than the hectic action of most shoot 'em ups.<br><br>And indeed a welcome change from monotony regardless and for once, a 2D shooting game that seems to be story-driven. You control a brave pilot, in his quest to save his homeworld from a mysterious race of flying machines. You will be battling multiple foes at a time or some which are far greater than you in terms of sheer size and firepower. You must overcome them with a mix of hit-and-run tactics since the two ship classes you can choose from and customize, feature either homing missiles or remote detonated mines. So Sector Six employs a non-direct firing mechanism which forces players to think before shooting, unlike most games within this genre that seem to focus on the fire-and-forget doctrine. Apocalypse-class uses missiles and Carrier-class, mines. They are both interchangeable between missions (themselves structured in a non-liniar manner) along with a rich plethora of power-up and customization upgrades.<br><br>While altering the ships\u2019 aspect and structure, doesn\u2019t seem to affect in any way their speed &amp; maneuverability, the fact that you can create your own vision of the figher ship, does offer the freedom and personal touches which matter to me. Any game that allows me to create my own version of the character I control, is a title worthy of mentioning. I don\u2019t have to tell you how many of Sector Six\u2019s genre counterparts feature that kind of gameplay liberty. It\u2019s a shame that despite its custom ships and even foe diversity, the objectives and maps don\u2019t seem to vary a lot from one another. Along with the severe lack of a diversified soundtrack, the repetitive mission roster really drags the game\u2019s rating down by a bit. I understand that it\u2019s still in Early Access stage, but Sector Six has got to show more diversity for its current price tag. At least it already features Achievements and Cards for any collector to be swayed into buying it.
