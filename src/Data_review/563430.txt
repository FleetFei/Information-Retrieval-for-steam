first alpha release, and it's gone from an idea and setting that was enough to pique some initial interest to something that's truly impressive. I'm super proud of what Mr. Hagen has accomplished.<br><br>Void Pyramid's design philosophy is almost paradoxical. It strips the dungeon crawler RPG elements to a minimalist, barebones approach that resembles mechanics you might find in an early to mid 80s CRPG for IBM or Apple II, with a relatively tiny game world to explore. And yet the game world is full of a densely packed and highly detailed variety of novel situations and features, NPC and narrator flavor text is taken to a high art, and the hazy history and ecology of a dismal, far-future posthuman society is yours to discover and piece together as you go along. And of course there are a lot of ways to die horribly.<br><br>The tone of the narration is simultaneously dire, morbid, flippant and occasionally hilarious. It's rare to see descriptive text that's done this well in an RPG. If you enjoy games where the graphics are chiefly symbolic and the real details are to be found in word form, this is a must try.<br><br>The combat is frequent, yet mercifully brief - both you and your foes are liable to die in 2 or 3 blows, or even 1. Only health, attack and defense stats come into play during battles, and there are no spells or special abilities, only a few consumable items at your disposal. But each fight is tense, as the damage dealt by enemies needs to be gauged closely. Misjudging a single turn in combat even if you're at mostly full health can quickly get you immediately slain by a critical hit, randomly losing some or all of your gold or items, and sent back to respawn. Even with such simple mechanics at play, the combat is very strategic; players of tough single-hero RPGs like Dragon Warrior 1 will feel right at home. (There's no autosave on death, so save scumming is totally viable if that's how you want to handle the death setbacks.)<br><br>The music and sounds together have a trippy, somewhat psychedelic feeling and add to the atmosphere of the game without being obtrusive. The graphics are minimalist but done with style and originality, with a lot of bright pastels contrasting with brown and yellow neutral tones. The monster designs are generally pretty fantastic.<br><br>The lack of gameplay depth in some areas may be a turn off for some players, as well as the retro style and rough graphical edges, but if you enjoy exploring a finely detailed game world and overcoming challenges at every turn, you need to give Void Pyramid a try.
