serious, or not?<\/div><br><i>Make it Indie<\/i> has received a LOT of negative comment since its release on April 1st (ahum) of this year. The description on the Steam Store page explicitly asks not to take it too seriously, and so people have mostly jumped in expecting some fun and entertainment. Which the game ... does not provide.<br><br>At its core, this is a (slightly) interactive story, which &quot;teaches&quot; the player how to succeed or - more likely - fail at becoming a developer of Indie games. During one week you're every morning presented with a choice: to work on your game, or rather do something else. If you work on the game enough, and you succeed at the fairly stupid but not that easy clicker-mini-game, then you finish a game and it may be published. Goal accomplished.<br><br>If you choose to other things, you're presented with one out of eight different full-motion video's, which feel like being directed by a decent amateur cinematograph (or first-year student) and are shot in Russia. They contain some nice images, but are mostly unsettling in their vagueness. There's no interaction at all in this part of the game: you just watch and wait (or skip and proceed to the next day, quickly getting all the achievements, but that would completely beside the point).<br><br>Now, based on these all-too-meagre contents, it could be a straightforward affair, and I could dismiss this little &quot;game&quot; as  an utter failure. But the game has stirred something inside me while playing, something I've been thinking about. Maybe there is a &quot;deeper&quot; meaning to it, such as: if you do what you're expected to do (working every day on your project, in order to succeed), life is boring as hell. Which, to me, makes sense, and not at the same time. It all depends if you can put something of yourself, of your dreams maybe, into your work. But if you choose not to focus, and to do whatever you feel like doing on a given day, life becomes unsettled, even upsetting. That's where the nightmares come in: every night, there's a short but disturbing dream. Nothing spooky or horror-like, no, just unsettling. It's difficult to describe it so I suggest you just pay the dollar or euro, and go and watch (play) this.<br><br>Try to do it with an open mind, not expecting some quick fun, and maybe you'll get the same feeling as I had: that this game wants to tell us something, about life, about dreams; surely in a clumsy and unpolished way, but stll: I feel there is more to it than just &quot;a game not to be taken seriously&quot;.<br><br><b>Recommended for the really curious, but definitely to be avoided if looking for some easy fun.<\/b>\t\t\t\t\t<div
