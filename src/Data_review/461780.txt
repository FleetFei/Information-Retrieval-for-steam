There are plenty of different quests to go on from finding  eggs to hatch to collect new pets, through collecting resources, to slaying dragons. It is let down very slightly by the combat which is basically shoot stuff with guns and wands, or hit them with your sword (depending on your class). Personally I prefer to rely on breeding up a strong accompanying pet, or hiring heroes rather than doing much fighting myself. You can get new eggs by going on quests and by breeding your creatures using genetimagic (don\u2019t know how to pronounce that). Breeding creatures is simple yet fun, although crafting the magical ingredients can be difficult.<br><br>Crafting is a huge part of the game. Alongside cooking there are so many armors and weapons, magic potions, scrolls, and magic gems that it can be, at first, a little overwhelming. Learning where to get all the ingredients from (which there are definitely over 100) is a challenge in itself.<br><br>There is of course a story, and although it uses some common fantasy tropes, such as a blight spreading across the land, it does so in a way that is new and interesting. I especially like that the elves seem to be the bad guys, although, as in all games by these devs, the whole world is cast in shades of grey rather than in black and white. The story is also linked directly to your success as a tavern manager which is obviously important. I\u2019ve not finished the main storyline yet but I think I\u2019ve guessed where it is going due to reading some of the books located throughout the world.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">\u00a0My Last Hour of Play: <\/div>\u00a0<br><br>In my last hour of play I purchased another tavern (you don\u2019t get to manage it but you do get profit).<br><br>I went on a quest for some magical ink to write out a scroll but I still need to find a feather before I can craft it.
