simple, but powerfully intuitive economy sim which doesn't get stale as you play on. Now this game doesn't rely much on careful placement (as all of us economy sim fans had to endure with any city building game), as it relies on careful strategy of how you expand your economic empire.<br><br>So here are the details:<br><br><br>Picking an idustry is similar to choosing a character in an RPG - any of them are good to win the game with, they have skill trees, you can adopt more characters (different industries), and every move makes intuitive sense. Via the tutorial you learn how the game works with one of the simpler industries, but as I said above, you can (and sometimes need to) expand into other sectors, but ultimately if you're a conglamorate or a niche one item business, it won't matter so long as your instincts make for sound business decissions. Oh and if the market is favourable :)<br><\/li><li> Interface is functional but lacking - so both a pro and a con.<br><\/li><li> Very little annoying of the &quot;mindless&quot; tasks exist, so don't worry about spending too much time doing repetitive things, unless of course you decide to change over one of your warehouses... that's a pain later-on so plan ahead or spend 30minutes paused.<br><\/li><li> The game design has disguised the barebone mechanics behind a nice experience. I can't describe it, it's the art of game design, but some games are compelling and you don't see through their veil of experience (what you're meant to see); onto their design skeleton (the mechanical feel and behaviour of the program).<br>An example would be Democracy 3 - it has this deep emotional investment about politics and how to run a country, but ultimately this veil is thin and instead you soon realise it's just moving a bunch of sliders left and right adjusting decimal points one way or the other.<br><\/li><li> It's fun - it really is enjoyable, I haven't had time fly by as quickly since Civilization.<br><\/li><li> Price - in the hands of any other studio the game would cost at least 25% more. This makes it one of the cheapest economic sims out there.<\/li><\/ul><br><b><div class=\"bb_h1\">The Bad<\/div><\/b><br><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li> Graphics and font - I don't care for the former, they're not bad. However the game does lack a certain level of polish which would have been nice to have. However I would never swap graphics for the amazing functionality Industry Manager has.<br><\/li><li> Bugs - After 4 hours or so I encountered some issues with supply and demand. Being the only person offering XYZ in town, and at a loss, I still couldn't sell my goods even though they were in high demand and I had them in supply.<br><\/li><li> UI could use some improvements, I praised the game for intuitiveness but it does lack some more intuitive clicks instead of getting access to things in a round-about way.<br><\/li><li> Options\/Settings - the game is thoroughly lacking here and I wish the developers gave more freedom on customising UI, controls, etc.<\/li><\/ul><br><b><div class=\"bb_h1\">The Verdict<\/div><\/b><br>The game is very polished.
