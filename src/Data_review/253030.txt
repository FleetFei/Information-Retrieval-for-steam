<br>Race The Sun is an atypical single player racing game that puts you in control of a geometric solar powered craft on a never-ending, futile journey to keep a fully charged battery and keep the sun from setting. As you race towards the always distant sun, you\u2019ll have to circumvent an ever-changing obstacle course and collect power-ups and energy boosts to keep the sun in the sky for as long as you can. Watch out though, as one frontal collision and your run is over and back to the beginning you go.<br><br>The game itself comes at you from a very minimalist approach, though there\u2019s a bit more to the game at first glance. There\u2019s some real satisfying work that goes into completing it and it really kept me hooked, but Race The Sun suffers from longevity issues which makes leaving a lasting impression on you difficult to master. <br><br>The main game, as mentioned above, you pilot a winged solar craft through courses that change once every 24hrs. While many of the obstacles will remain similar at first glance, the position at which they come to you, the order and the location of the power-ups will all be different each day. There\u2019s an in-game counter stating when you can expect a new course to play on. This is a wonderful device that keeps the content fresh without having to constantly release DLC content. The game, to an extent, creates it for you. <br><br>As you proceed through these levels, you\u2019ll be given side challenges that will help level up your craft. These can range from how many icons you collect [called tris], your bonus multiplier, how many power-ups you use and so forth. They are scaled from a simple easy to hard scale and the value of tokens earned is based on that scale. As you level up, you\u2019ll gain new, permanent abilities that you can toggle on and off based on your play style or the type of challenge you\u2019re working on; these range from a magnet to draw in tris, the ability to hold additional powers like shields and jump abilities, a larger battery pack and more. <br><br>There are no traditional difficulty settings in Race The Sun, but instead different game modes that stand in their place. Your typical playthrough would fall under the \u201cNormal\u201d category, while you can unlock an \u201capocalypse\u201d mode; the game\u2019s version of \u201chard\u201d. In juxtaposition to those, there is also a casual mode with no sun that allows you to just play the game in a stress free environment, as well as a top-down version.<br><br>I never ran into many problems while playing. There were some instances of FPS drops that were really irritating on such a minimalist game; a few times it cost me my run. They didn\u2019t stop me from playing, but it wasn\u2019t a problem I was ever able to locate and fix either. The only other issue I have with the game was the difficulty spike between normal runs and the heightened \u201capocalypse\u201d mode. I consider myself a junkie for difficulty, but even as I learned the ins and outs of the courses and their routines, it felt far too scaled upward to remain as enjoyable. I found myself completely ignoring this aspect of the game, which is a shame.<br><br>The height of the game\u2019s enjoyment came, for me, with doing the in-game challenges. However, once you fully level up your craft you no longer have a list to work on, making the game fall back on just racing the sun. While fun, there felt like a chunk of purpose was removed from the game when I had nothing else to strive for other than my own personal record. That nearly tangible feeling of excitement vanished just like the challenges did. Aside from the Steam achievements, I no longer had a reason to keep playing\u2026so I didn\u2019t. I wish I had a reason to go back.<br> <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"> Pros <\/div> <br><ul class=\"bb_ul\"><li> A great test of reflexes and skill if you enjoy racing-type games.<br><\/li><li> Friendly UI setup.<br><\/li><li> Easy to drop in and out of for short or long sessions.<br><\/li><li> Lovely art style with a good use of colors and B&amp;W canvases. <br><\/li><li> There is a level editor, though it takes some practice.<\/li><\/ul><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"> Cons <\/div> <br><ul class=\"bb_ul\"> \n<li> There are instances of FPS drops for some users.<br><\/li><li> Lacks a proper end game to keep user presence and longevity.<br><\/li><li> The \u201capocalypse\u201d setting can be daunting for some users with no way to adjust it.<\/li><\/ul><br><br>Overall, I really enjoyed Race The Sun. The simplicity in its design balanced out the sharp difficulty curve as you learned to play the game. It balanced it\u2019s color scheme and rigged layout with splashes of color and smooth [for the most part] gameplay.
