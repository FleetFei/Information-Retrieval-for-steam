But if you can see pass all that, this game in its entirety is one of the most well-designed multiplayer co-op strategy games I have ever played, and because I feel like it here's a somewhat detailed look at the reasons why.<br><br><b><u> (Do I have to pay?) <\/u><\/b> - <i> TLDR: You can enjoy the game without paying. <\/i><br><br>Lets get this out of the way first. This is a free game. But like most free games, parts of the features are unavailable unless you go 'Premium'. You get to play both multiplayer and single player modes for free, although in multiplayer the maps you are allowed to join are randomly selected and open to public, while in single player 1 scenario from each of the 6 races' campaigns are available. Special units\/heros won't be available at first, but will pop up randomly as you play (more on this later).<br><br>In most free games out there you can expect a certain level of microtransactions, and this one is no exception.<br><br>If you didn't already instantly recoil from the M word hissing and spitting, then you will be glad to know that you don't actually HAVE to buy anything to enjoy the game. Premium currency (gold coins) are given out based on the number of people you manage to finish a multiplayer game session with (6 players, 6 coins) and how well you did in that session, as well as whenever you finish a singleplayer map (1 per map, maps are repeatable and can present a slightly different challenge every time). Plus, rarer and more interesting and\/or powerful cards will appear in both game modes in the game itself, letting you use them immediately and giving you the option to add them to your collection permanently for 1 premium currency each, which you can then use again in all future games.<br><br>Simply put, the more you play, the better you play, and the more people you play with, the more interesting and fun the game gets. It's actually a really good way to ease players into the more advanced side of the game.<br><br><b><u> (Multiplayer) <\/u><\/b> - <i> TLDR: Perfect for busy bees and tabletop gamers <\/i><br><br>This game is perfect for the modern world of too little free time, requiring maybe 15 mins of attention tops before you can get back to what you were busy with. Even then, it's packed with just the right amount of strategy to keep you fully engaged and planning your every move, discussing and coordinating your actions with your buddies. My group often spend more time strategizing than actually playing, the discussion becoming part of the gameplay and makes it all feel like you are actually commanding an army in real life.<br><br><b><u> (Single Player) <\/u><\/b> - <i> TLDR: Throws just enough of a curve ball <\/i><br><br>The single player mode is a whole different beast. Where in multiplayer everything happens in real time, in single player you can fast forward time, but the amount of time is preset unless you adjust it by paying up an ingame resource. This adds another dimension to the game and helps it stand apart from the multiplayer experience, but not so much that you have to relearn everything.<br><br><b><u> (The Special 'Premium' Stuff) <\/u><\/b> - <i> TLDR: Flavor of the game, free players get them slower <\/i><br><br>So lets get back to the whole Premium side of this game. To better understand it, here's how it works. At the start of each game, each race in the game starts off with a basic set of unit\/hero summoning cards tailored for that map, and you can then throw in more cards of any race to add to your 'deck' that you will bring into the game proper. Where do you get these cards? Either you buy them from a lucky draw choose-1-of-3 style mechanic, or you wait for them to appear ingame as you play and you can 'recruit' them permanently for future games. These cards are really where the flavor of the game is at, with most of the combos-making and synergistic abilities reserved for these rare cards.<br><br>Its no surprise that a lot of people get turned off if they dont run into these cards early, so if you are eager to find these then definitely try out single player where you can fast forward time. <br><br><b><u> (Conclusion) [\/b]<\/u> - <i>TLDR: It's awesome<\/i><br><br>I've thoroughly enjoyed playing this game so far with my buddies, and I haven't even fully played or talked about every aspect of it!
