Basically you can shoot everything in a map and destroy it to earn points which also raises your chaos level. You can also jump and double jump in this game. Jumping can be used to climb on top of buildings or shoot down soldiers with jetpacks. This did not influence my review in any way.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">==========================================================<br>\n---------------------------------------OVERVIEW-------------------------------------<br>\n==========================================================<\/div>Note: Blamdown Udder Fury is currently in Early Access. The version reviewed was the latest as of the date of the review (20160609).<br><br>Blamdown Udder Fury is a rather silly twin-stick shooter in which you control a cow intent on causing destruction with your bazooka. It doesn't make a lot of sense, but that's fine; it's not supposed to.<br><br>The game starts with a quick and simple tutorial-like introduction. You're given control of a cow, you jump a few fences in search of another cow, and you discover that the farmer has taken her and left the farm. Vowing to destroy the farmer and get her back, you pick up some weapons that someone carelessly left behind and you start shooting everything in sight.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">==========================================================<br>\n----------------------------------------QUALITY-------------------------------------<br>\n==========================================================<\/div>Graphics - Graphics are bright and colourful, with cartoon-like models and simple textures. There's no zooming in or out of the view, and no way to rotate it either, often leading to frustration when your character or the enemies shooting at you are hidden by level geometry.<br><br>There are a number of detail level options that I didn't play around with; running at maximum for everything caused no problems to my old gaming laptop, so any new semi-decent graphics chipset should be able to handle the game easily enough. But even on maximum settings Blamdown won't win any awards for best graphics. They're suitable for the casual style of this game, though.<br><br>Sound - The game's music track is a bit frantic and I found it a bit grating within the first hour of play. It's reasonably well produced, though. Sound effects, too, are effective and do their job solidly.<br><br>Gameplay - Control of the game's bovine hero is via controller or mouse and keyboard. The default control layouts for both work well, but after getting a bit frustrated with the controller I reverted to mouse and keyboard, as it seems to be a bit more accurate. Either way, you can move and aim independently, like any twin-stick shooter, and you have a primary and secondary weapon. You can also do a quick dash to evade enemies (most of which are faster than you), or jump to dodge, shoot flying enemies, or reach above-ground areas.<br><br>Each large level of Blamdown Udder Fury gives you a number of goals to complete before either being picked up by your seedy-looking friend-cow in a helicopter, or confronting one of the bosses, depending on the level in question. These goals are almost always related to causing chaos by blowing up buildings, people, and vehicles with your bazooka.<br><br>As you raise the chaos level, however, the game throws more powerful enemies at you in greater numbers. It's not too long before you have cow catchers, machine gunners, cartoon tanks, and jet-pack-powered heavy weapons guys hot on your hoofs, causing a fair swathe of destruction around them.<br><br>Unfortunately, dodging enemy bullets while simultaneously dishing out destruction of your own is not as easy as it could be, as each structure or vehicle that you destroy breaks up into a number of indestructible jagged pieces, each of which can block your movement as well as your bullets. It's a weird design choice and it makes the game more frustrating than it should be.<br><br>Blamdown is more of a casual game than a die-hard twin-stick shooter, but it doesn't try to hide that fact. It lacks the vast equipment or upgrade trees that tend to be common to the genre, essentially leaving each level to stand on its own, with no real progression. It's fun, but limited.<br><br>Bear in bind that Blamdown is still in Early Access, too; there's a chance that some of this will change.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">==========================================================<br>\n-------------------------------------PROS &amp; CONS------------------------------------<br>\n==========================================================<\/div>Pros:<br>+ An original story with some humour<br>+ Colourful cartoon-like graphics<br><br>Cons:<br>- Exploded objects often get in the way of movement and gunplay, causing more frustration than is necessary<br>- The lack of progressive achievements, such as character improvements, weapon upgrades, and even Steam achievements, makes the game feel a bit pointless<br>- Level geometry sometimes hides the action<br>- Poor grammar in some of the in-game text<br><br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">==========================================================<br>\n--------------------------------------CONCLUSION-----------------------------------<br>\n==========================================================<\/div>Blamdown Udder Fury is a competent casual-style twin-stick shooter.
