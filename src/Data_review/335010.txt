Steam and Metal is not afraid to be silly (steampunk jokes and all) and its lofty writing is smirk inducing. The hand drawn slides area nice touch to give the game some character. Your ship can take a limited number of hits (depending on difficulty), but you can receive a bonus for not being hit at all. However this is a neat experience for those who are in the market for a streamlined and brief experience. amateur, yet very solid game! I was happy to play it, and it doesn't have too much that's wrong with it, which is surprising, as many people tend to ignore crucial details. This game does not. <br><br>However, it doesn't add anything to the genre, and while it's a solid game, it pains me how similar it is to Jamestown! Not just in setting and general vibe, but also in the way it fills the screen with little cogs, and some other details. In the credits, the developer says the game has been slowly &quot;baked&quot; in 4 years, and looking at his youtube channel, there were prototypes as far back as 2010, so it doesn't seem to me like a rip-off. <br>Perhaps it's just bad luck, but it's impossible to recommend this over <b>Jamestown<\/b>, since that adds a lot more to the genre, and is generally much bigger in scale and options.<br><br>Regardless of that fact, I enjoyed playing this, and I'll review it by its own merits.<br><br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Presentation<\/div>The <b>visual<\/b> style of this game reminds me a lot of an higher-res version of SNES games. The color-palette, especially, reminds of of A Link to the Past. It's colorful, but has a sort of <i>low contrast<\/i>, with more passive colors.<br>Honestly, the game looks much better in motion that in the screenshots.<br>The Boss visual design is also pretty cool. Won't blow your mind, but they're cool. There could be a bit more work into their details, but overall, I liked it.<br><br>One problem people might have with it, is that it's very bare-bones. Levels have some motion to them, but not a lot. Not many details that I saw either. No particle or lighting effects or anything of the sort. It's very basic and somewhat static, but other than that, I think it looks really cool! Very <i>clean<\/i> looking.<br><br>Now, the <b>problems<\/b> related to the visuals.<br>First off, the bullets' colors are too varied. That's a bad thing, because it makes it harder to balance the backgrounds with the bullets, and you're very likely to get hit by something you didn't see. <br><br>Related to this problem, is how the background has buildings which you never know if you'll get hit by or not. This makes dodging bullets a nightmare, and you're also trying to avoid everything. This is partially solved, with an option they introduced. You can &quot;mute&quot; and &quot;darken&quot; the backgrounds. This helps significantly with the backgrounds' distinction, but not a lot with the bullets. Especially when some bullets are very dark themselves.<br><br>Also, <b>explosions hide bullets a lot<\/b>. It's really annoying, in harder levels.<br><br>Other than these problems (which aren't too major, and the developer tried to give us options to deal with them), the visuals are fine. A bit too basic for some people, but they're well done!<br><br><br>Now, on to the <b>sound design<\/b>!<br>The sound-effects were a mixed bag. Some aspects I liked, others not so much. The positives outweigh the negatives, though.<br>When you destroy enemies, they explode with a loud boom. A <i>satisfying<\/i> boom, I should say! They chose the sound well -- the downside is, when you destroy many enemies at once (there's a specific point in the game where this happens, otherwise it's totally fine) where the sounds just play exactly at once, which seems to up the volume a lot. The sound then gets overly loud and distorted. It's a very minor thing, but it happens everytime, so I thought I should mention it.<br><br>The other problem, is the gun sounds. It sounds like you're shooting rubber bullets. (There are some Bomb enemies that &quot;pop&quot; when they die, a very toy-like sound that does not, in any way, sound like a bomb)<br>I really like when the weapons sound like freakin' cannons (War Thunder and Blue Flamingo had spot on sounds for this!), and shooting rubber bullets sound a bit underwhelming. I'm aware that bullet sound often sound annoying when repeatedly shooting, and this was certainly a measure to solve that problem, but I'm not too keen on this &quot;solution&quot;. <br>Still, it's not too important -- just a minor gripe I had with it!<br><br>Moving on to the <b>music<\/b>. As I said before, it was inspired by Grieg. You probably know <b>In the Hall of the Mountain King<\/b>, at least. Anyway, expect classical music! On the victory screens in sound almost like military march-music. I think it fits.<br>And the music sounds good, certainly! Also some Final Fantasy touches here and there, although it's all <i>orchestrated<\/i>.<br><br>The only problem I've had with it, is that it sounds a bit thin, and not very majestic. Just the way it was mixed, probably. Can't expect a real orchestra in a 5$ game, hehe! Still, it's very good, and this &quot;issue&quot; I have is completely fine for the scale of this game. Well done nonetheless!<br><br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">General Structure &amp; Mechanics<\/div>There are 3 difficulty modes, and 6 levels. Each level has a Boss-fight at the end.<br><br>The difficulty affects the damage you deal, that you take, and also some enemies' shooting patterns. The levels don't change significantly (aside from the same enemies being more powerful), but it's not a matter of &quot;more is harder!&quot;. <br>Every difficulty is available from the start.<br><br>The levels start locked, and you need to unlock them by beating the previous one. I think this was slightly bugged. When you start Hard mode, you're also locking the Easy and Normal levels, strangely enough. Not a big problem, but hey, it's there.<br><br>The mechanics are very simple. Your ship has a primary fire, and a secondary, more powerful weapon, instead of a Bomb. Your <b>primary weapon<\/b> has 3 states. It fires 1, 2 and then 3 bullets. You get cogs when you destroy enemies, which fills your weapon bar. I'm not sure if there are linear damage changes to the weapon, or whether it's a simple matter of being at different levels. <br>The <b>secondary weapon<\/b> is a torpedo &quot;barrage&quot; -- this is too powerful of a word, though. You need to hold a button to charge it, and then launch them, doing significant damage. This plays a big part in the game's tactic, I believe. Knowing when to use them (as they're limited), and which enemies drop them is important to succeed, especially if you're not a very skilled player (*discreetly whistling*).<br><br>Other than that, if have a health-bar and lives. When your life-bar depletes, you lose a life. There are periods of invincibility.<br><br>Each level has a different theme, both visually, and in regards to enemies and hazards. If you're not good, you'll need to learn the levels before doing being able to beat the game all in one go.<br><br><b>Enemy variety<\/b> is surprisingly high! I wasn't expecting that at all. Usually, these indie games tend to make the most out of a few enemies and combinations. This game, however, introduces several enemies in each level, and they behave significantly different. It's quite impressive, honestly.<br><br>The <b>Boss fights<\/b> are also really cool. They're all multi-phased, with patterns you'll need to learn. You usually have to destroy their weapons before going for the &quot;big kill&quot;. It's good!<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Shortcomings<\/div>There's only one ship. And it's a very basic one, without any interesting feature. It's unfortunate. In a game with such a rich enemy variety, one would expect more in terms of arsenal.<br><br>As I've mentioned, there are some issues with the distinction between background and foreground -- it's dampenned, but not completely solved. And it caused me many deaths.<br><br>The biggest thing of all, is that it doesn't bring anything new, really. It's a solid game, almost <i>by-the-book<\/i>. <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Conclusion<\/div>The setting, music... it's too similar to one of the best of the genre, and it's unfortunate, since it pales in comparison.<br>Unless you <i>want<\/i> to play a more casual 'shmup, there isn't a big reason for you to try this.<br><br>Otherwise, it's an <i>honest<\/i> game, and a very solid one at
