I also mentioned I was a fan of Two Worlds...so yeah. After playing the game for roughly 4 hours. My first impressions of this game are<br><br>1.The main lead is an \u2665\u2665\u2665, but his deep voice and overall appearance make me like him. (plus one spin on the stereotypical pirate attire is instead of owning a pet parrot....he owns a pet raven and that makes it much cooler.)<br><br>2.The story is interesting enough to make me care, yet it's a little cliche.(Christopher's parents were murded by a group of pirates, and he finds a lead by chance, so he decides to go after them for revenge.) (It is a decently entertaining unlike Reality Pump's well known Two Worlds series where the stories aren't very good.)<br><br>3.The graphics and overall atmosphere are very good with even some tiny touches that I didn't notice. An example is when it's raining, you can literally see the drops hit the ground. You even notice water sliding down your character's clothes. It's pretty cool, yet the water shading isn't the best.(By that I mean the water in the sea.)<br><br>4.The combat is a little clunky, but I usually find ways to manage through it. The biggest issue I see is that sometimes the blocking can be unresponsive like there's a tiny delay that can make a weird difference depending on when you do use block, and you getting outnumbered is not good because anybody hitting you from behind kills the flow of the combat because it staggers you. (If I didn't have the fear skill, it would be worse, but the fear skill does allow me to kill one enemy when the bar fills up instantly with some cool execution animations.)<br><br>5.The ship combat reminds me a little bit of Assassin's Creed IV Black Flag, but it feels more down to earth. You have to manually set your aim by changing how high or low you aim the cannons and fire from either side of the ship using the left and right mouse button. You even have different keys set to move the camera to different view points to aim your shots better. The only thing I had trouble with was boarding the ships. If there are any surviving crew on the enemy ship, it becomes a weird turn based battle system where you can choose three different tatics and even have the cannons loaded. It feels a bit clunky, and I prefer the method of Black Flag where you literally board the ship with your crew and slay enemies till you take over the ship.<br><br>6.I have no idea how to get more generic crew mates. The more unique ones I can get easily, but the only way I see getting new crew members is to board ships with some of the crew still on there then pray to god you have enough of your own crew to take over the ship. You then can take some of the surviving crew as your own. If anybody knows another way, please for the love of god, let me know.<br><br>UPDATE TO #6<br><br>Oops. I found out a while ago you need to go to either the crew or ship menu (can't remember which) and use a slider to adjust how many men you want on your ship. It also automatically does it for you, but I prefer manually setting it. My bad XD. <br><br>7.The economy system has a good amount of depth. You take cargo from ships and can sell them to traders for money. The biggest pro I think is that some places have a surplus and sell\/buy certain goods at lower prices but also buy supplies at higher prices that they don't have. So, it's possible to get a bunch of one supply cheap and find a trader on another island who will buy it for much more.<br><br>8.I feel the music is well composed and the tavern songs are well executed enough to be good.<br><br>9.There's a decent amount of humor in the quests that made me laugh a bit. One example early on is when Christopher has to steal a ship with his first mate Marcus, and the captain of that ship is drunk and accuses them of taking his ship. Christopher just calmly says,&quot;Nope.&quot; So, the captain replies,&quot;Y-You're not?&quot; To which, Christopher replies,&quot;No everything's good here, Lay back down. Get some sleep.&quot; He just replies with a thanks and goes back to sleep...I found it funny.<br><br><br>10. Animals man. There are literally monkeys that show their feces at you....yeah I chuckled a bit, but it is a decent attention to detail, but I do have one issue..some of the animals are hard to hit with your sword. The cougar..leopard...or whatever is a bit difficult to hit and what makes it worse is that these things are fast and bite. You can shoot them, but you better make sure you haven't been in the water or your pistol might not shoot because the gunpowder is wet.<br><br>That's the good and the meh, but this is going to be the bad.<br><br>1.The optimization on this game is not very good. If you have a medium rig, I would recommend turning the settings down a little, so the game is playable. Hell some people even say they can't get the thing running on their computer. My rig involves a amd multicore processor and a radeon video card with the OS Windows 10, so I have a weird theory that maybe the game doesn't like nvidia hardware or older versions of windows. I did not experience a single crash yet, but the performance isn't the best. I know this game could reach 60fps on high settings if the game was improved. I will say though that the game is playable, so it has that going for it.<br><br>2. This is a more personal complaint, buuuut WHY THE FLYING \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665 CAN'T I UPGRADE TO THE DELUXE VERSION?! I don't want to buy the game all over again to get the content.
