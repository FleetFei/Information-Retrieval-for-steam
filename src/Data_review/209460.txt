2015\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t<div class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRAGE The Scorchers is a DLC that adds new content to single-player FPS portion of a game. Nothing to multiplayer. They say that developers planned to make many DLCes for RAGE and sequel after finishing it, but Bethesda shifted all the work toward Doom 4. So RAGE got only this DLC and it came out late, one year after original game, in 2012.<br><br>It added few things to main game. First thing you may notice is addition of Ultra-Nightmare difficulty. Not much to say, it's even more mean than Nightmare but doesn't seem to have any unique additional rules. Because there are always players who complain that games became too easy.<br><br>Another one is that you can now continue the game after the ending. You are just going to be thrown back into the game world in the last state that it was before final mission. Well, aside from two main resistance characters being gone. That means that no one recognises what you did in the last missions, world is same and Authority is still spilling that annoying propaganda in Wellspring town.<br>Seeing how this is linear experience with hub world, the only reason to continue playing is to finish whatever optional mission you missed, or minigame, or race for achievement or to go straight for the main content - The Scorchers campaign!<br><br>The path starts in a Hagar settlement, a location near beggining of the game, and then keeps going to location in the mid of a game. It means that players who started campaign a new can play it along the main campaign line. But take note, the challenge in those levels don't seem to fit with locations, they are harder than other missions in same timeline where DLC missions become available. So it makes a bit more sense to beat it after completing the game and it will not be walk in a park.<br><br>And during these 6 challenging levels you will be fighting new enemies, mostly new bandit clan - The Scorches. Which they say were supposed to appear in original. They are a bit generic in the way they communicate, but they really love fire and a bit similar in how tough they are to Gearheads, with some common enemies even using jetpacks. Yep, seen some actually making use it during battle, jumping between two places. Bonus points for having a mutant horde level which gives another reason to place turrets around. AND this DLC comes with proper final boss!<br><br>You also going to be accomplished by two sidekicks: Sarah and nailgun. Not much to say about Sarah aside from being obvious hot bully. Nailgun on other hand is a cool new weapon, shame that it doesn't have ability to bind to number on keyboard, so you will have to pop that console-like weapon selection up. It has three ammo: Nails just make it yet another automatic weapon in a game. Rebar makes it as cool as stakegun in Painkiller. And Railgun mode gives you unique thing where you can see enemies silhouetes through wall when aiming down scope and you can actually shoot them through walls.<br><br>And that's not all, as developers also introduced Scenario Selection where you can play any missions you already have done on any save. No, outdoor missions, where you have to snipe attacking force from one spot, aren't included. It's not exactly as straight at you think.<br><br>Well, first, you can't really pick up anything at all during Scenario. No additional ammo, no corpse looting, nothing aside from whatever object you need to interact to progress. And you don't have any crafting either. What you get is that you before each mission you get to select a pre-made loadout. Like &quot;Settler Pack&quot; which is mostly about early weapons, or &quot;Item Pack&quot; where you have lots of wingsticks, grenades and turrets, or &quot;Sniper Pack&quot; with, you guessed it, sniper rifle with tons of ammo.<br><br>And I will tell you, I started to appericate this mode a lot when by the end of Wasted Garage level I had only one grenade and one wingstick left, with no ammo. It really makes it feel more about survival with more limited supply, making you more carefully. Also, being able to play any level on Nightmare with limited ammo without having to replay whole level is great.<br>There is a small flaw that menu wasn't designed fully for PC, as you have to use Enter button to confirm selection, nothing for mouse to click. Still nothing fun ruining. Unless you are some kind of console-hater, then bleh.<br><br>I sure can recommend for anyone who liked RAGE to grab this DLC for single-player FPS part. It's great. It gives both new challenging campaign with final boss and also allows you to play any old missions using loadouts and with limited ammo.
