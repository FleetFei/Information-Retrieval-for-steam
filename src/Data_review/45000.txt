Each officer has a unique mix of towers and skills at his or her disposal. <br><\/li><li>The tower placement happens on a grid based mesh that is restricted in some areas. Tower placement needs thinking and thinking ahead. Although the selling of tower is possible it is also less efficient from a resource perspective.<br><\/li><li>Speaking about resources: You manage your available energy for active skills and your available resources to build towers. Energy regenerates over time and resources are collected from defeated enemies. (With the need to collect them by hovering or clicking with the mouse like in some other games.)<br><\/li><li>What else makes it worth a second look? Features! Single Player Campaign and Survival Mode, Co-Op Survival mode with up to 4 Players (Online and LAN), Campaign mode Co-op for 2 players (one builds the turrets the other controls support skills), Co-op Campaign with up to 4 players (each player builds and has support skills), Wave based Survival with shared lives (up to 6 players) and a Versus mode where the last player standing wins (up to 8 players) \u2013 There may not be many Players around in Online Multiplayer anymore so bring your friends! <\/li><\/ul>One thing that set Sol Survivor apart from other games in this genre is the fact that you don\u2019t get a preview of the upcoming waves of enemies and their composition. This forces you to adapt quickly and according to your current skillset and tower setup. I enjoyed this very much and found it to be more refreshing than getting a heads up before every upcoming wave.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Look and feel and sound: <\/div>Like I hinted in the introduction the game does not look very fancy. It also lacks some satisfying booms and `splosions and laz0rs soundeffects. If that turns you off, move on. If not: Please consider looking at the game past the tutorial. It really has the ability to drag you in. You can for example set up the camera at every angle and distance you like. That is pure freedom that other games do not offer and also very enjoyable and convenient in a tower defense game. Word of advice on one feature that is missing: Speeding up the game is not possible. If you are not patient this may trigger a nervous breakdown. On the other hand it includes a color blind mode. That is awesome! <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Captains Orders:<\/div>If you have played all of those tower defense games in a shiny suit you may need a fresh perspective. Sol Survivor does offer that, but it is delivering on the core mechanics that makes this genre enjoyable. A solid and somewhat indie \/ underdog game that deserves your attention if you enjoy Tower Defense as a genre.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Verdict: <\/div><b>Cargo!<\/b> Sol Survivor offers a good alternative to other tower defense games. It is also one of the few Tower Defense games with a satisfying Co-op mode.
