Anytime I find a game similar to Mega Man, I get my hopes up and try it out, only to be disappointed. Capcom has been giving Mega Man fans the middle finger for several years now, while Mighty No. 9 has been delayed until 2016. All hope seemed lost.<br><br>But then I found 20XX. It's not exactly a Mega Man title, and I keep making that comparison. Why? Because it is an action-platformer that's heavily inspired by Mega Man. What's interesting is that it combines the mechanics and gameplay of an action platformer like Mega Man with those of a rogue-like. And it's amazing. It scratches that Mega Man itch in all the right ways! Even though it's Early Access, I can see how great this game is going to be in the future, and I fully support it.<br><br><b>Gameplay<\/b><br>==========<br>I apologize in advance, but I am going to make a lot of comparisons to Mega Man during this review. If you have played a Mega Man title, then you know what to expect. There are 2 playable characters: Nina and Ace. Nina is the equivalent of Mega Man or X, a ranged character who shoots emenies from afar. Ace is the equivalent of ProtoMan or Zero, a character who favors melee combat with his saber. Both characters are able to jump, dash, wall-climb, wall-jump, and charge their basic attacks into Power attacks. <br><br>The game also has all the typical elements of a rogue-like: There is perma-death and it has randomly generated levels. Once you die, your run is over and you have to restart from scratch in the Ark (the game's Hub). In the Ark, you can switch between Nina and Ace, take on Daily and Hardcore Challenges, invite a friend to play with you, buy items, and activate Skulls--extra gameplay modifiers. Let's go over each of those things in turn.<br><br>Daily and Hardcore challenges are just that: Extra stages that you can access once a day for some extra challenge. Hardcore is very, very difficult, with more monsters and less power-ups. <br><br>When you choose multiplayer, you can opt to play locally with a friend, or invite a friend via the Steam client from your friends list. Multiplayer is very smooth, with hardly any bugs. I have read of some minor glitches on the forums, but I never experienced any personally. Co-op is a fun addition to this title, and something a mainline Mega Man game has never had. <br><br>At the shop, you are able to purchase items with Soul Nuts, which are dropped by monsters with a blue glow or aura in each stage. They'll drop anywhere from 1 to 5 Soul Nuts from what I've seen. Items bought from the shop then have a chance of appearing in each stage, so make it a point to buy what you can to improve your survivability each run! You will also find normal Nuts in each stage, which you can use during each run to buy health and power-ups from shops you find mid-run. These are good places to bolster yourself for the challenges ahead, provided you pick up any Nuts you find.<br><br>One of the extra features 20XX boasts is the Bonus Timer. In the top-left of the screen is a timer, usually a few minutes. If you reach the end of each stage and defeat the boss before the timer ticks down to zero, you get an extra bonus item at the end of the stage. Speaking of which, after each boss you are treated to  a room where you can choose 1 of 3 items, in addition to 1-2 bonus items depending on your bonus timer. You can then choose from 1 of 3 stages to play next. Stages get harder as you go, with enemies doing more damage and having more health. There are about 6-8 different stages and bosses, with more on the way in future updates. The nature of rogue-likes helps to keep things fresh!<br><br>Skulls are extra gameplay modifiers, which serve to increase the difficulty of the game for added challenge. You can activate as many as you want at once...but only if you are feeling brave! They range from things like poisoning you when your bonus timer runs out, or disabling healing items from appearing. Choose wisely if you decide to take on these challenges!<br><br><b>Graphics<\/b><br>==========<br>Everything is sprite-based, but they are so very detailed! The animations are fluid and look very good. I honestly don't have any problems with how things look. The character designs in the trailers and what's in-game now say a lot about how the game is slowly improving. Enemies look great as well, and there is a lot of variety between models. There is some simple recoloring though, but not much.<br><br>The little details here and there are really what make the game. Things like the little charge gauge on Nina's Buster. The 3 little sections light up as your are charging a Power Attack. The Dash effect looks great, with little afterimages left in your wake.<br><br>All the power-up icons look really sharp, and are easily distinguishable. Attack animations look smooth and well-crafted. Ace's saber strike combo is great, something they added based on player feedback from what I've read.<br><br><b>Sound and Music<\/b><br>==========<br>The music is perfect for the game. Upbeat, dramatic, and heart-pounding. It was composed by a guy named Brandon Ellis, also known as Cityfires. I've never heard of him, but I like his work with 20XX. The developers have said the soundtrack will be available for purchase, possibly in a bundle with the game, in the future. <br><br>The sound effects are great. Attacks, charged shots, saber slashes, taking damage, death, enemies...all of it is very clear. Picking up items and power-ups has a very satisfying little jingle, very reminiscent of how Mega Man handled items.<br><br><b>Performance<\/b><br>==========<br>The game runs beautifully. I observed a full 60FPS the entirety of my playtime (you can see the FPS counter at the bottom left of my videos, found below), even with a lot of action on-screen during a Hardcore run. 20XX has full controller support, in fact a controller is HIGHLY recommended. The default controls are very good, but can be reassigned to fit any player's tastes. They are very tight and responsive.<br><br><b>Final Thoughts<\/b><br>==========<br>20XX is an excellent action-platformer, and an excellent rogue-like. Tons of features and additions are on the way, which promises this game to be quite amazing the more it develops. The developers have even taken a very ballsy step: there is a timer on the title screen counting down to the next update. For Early Access, this creates a very volatile situation between devs and players if these updates and promises aren't kept. So far, from what I've read, this timer has never lied! They are also very active within the community, and frequently respond to player questions and feedback. Frankly, some of the actual Megaman series wishes it could be this one, too.  <br><br>So what exactly is 20XX?  Overall, it's a platformer that's extremely similar to the old Megaman X series (on the SNES), with alot of roguelike elements, and even more general brutality than the old games were known for. <br><br>Starting things off, you choose from one of two characters:  Nina, who uses ranged attacks, and Ace, who uses a sword (they sound a bit familiar?).  When you begin a run, the game's first stage is chosen at random... you dont know which of the game's 8 bosses you'll be going after.  The old idea of being able to always choose between the full 8 is gone, to prevent players from always choosing an &quot;optimal&quot; path... you cant do that now.  When a level ends, you are given a choice of 3 randomly selected bosses to go after.  Since, as you'd expect, each boss is weak against a specific power, this gives the choice of order some weight.  There's more to it than just that though... but we'll get to that in a bit.<br><br>The first thing you're likely to notice (other than the excellent graphics) are the controls.  They are, frankly, perfect.  This game just outright FEELS like that old series did.  You have the same moves, too... running, the sliding dash, basic jumps (right down to the same height), and clinging to, leaping off of, and generally scaling walls. And of course, your weapons... you can fire normal shots, or you can use charged attacks.  Also like in the old X series, there are various suit enhancements; these can give you additional special abilities, such as the ability to double-charge your weapon, or the ability to temporarily fly in any direction for about a second.<br><br>There are also boss powers to use, of course.  You have quite the array of attacks to choose from, and they all have their unique uses.  You've got things like the boomerang blade, which can be thrown in 8 directions, the shockwave, which can smash enemies and cancel bullets in an area around you, or something like the power I call the Death Pixel... you'll see why I call it that if you try it out (it can be charged, give that a try).  Each power is both useful and satisfying.  But not just that, each has an additional special function.  For example, remember those nasty vanishing and reappearing platforms?  Yeah, they're here too.  If you're having trouble with them, and if you have Eternal Star's power, you can smack them with that to lock them into place.  Something like the Death Pixel can smash up vending machines, giving you some free money.  Each of the 8 powers can do something special in addition to it's main function.<br><br>Now, this being part roguelike, the different levels you go through are randomly generated, being made of tons of possible &quot;chunks&quot;.  These are all very well made and they work well together... but the difficulty is very, very high here.  A gazillion traps and nasty foes await you in the horrible hellholes that the bosses call home.  Fortunately, again, this is part roguelike, which means you have a bewildering array of powerups that can help you.  All sorts of ideas, both basic and very unique\/creative, can be found as items in this game.  You'll find them in chests, in stores, or as rewards for defeating a boss.  Some of them get pretty quirky, and the more powerful ones almost always have a downside:  Something that gives your basic attack a huge boost might also lower the damage of boss powers, for instance.  Some of them get pretty complicated.  As with many games like this, knowing when NOT to take an item is just as important as anything else.  There are even special weapons for both characters that entirely replace your basic attacks.   <br><br>Between runs, you'll be able to spend Soul Chips (granted from special enemies with massively-buffed HP) to unlock new items, or buy a couple to take with you into your run.   The game has a nice sense of progression, as there are LOTS of items here.  So many.  There are also a variety of modes and difficulties to choose from, including dailies.  You have a ton of stuff to do here.<br><br>And did I mention the enemies?  There's lots of them, of course, and they can get pretty devious.  Learning their patterns, and also knowing when\/how to use boss powers to your advantage, is very important.  It's very easy to die in this game.  The bosses in particular are extra difficult, and WHEN you fight a boss determines how hard it is.  Death Lotus might seem like an easy win if he's the first boss... but if he's number 8, he'll drown you in flowers and horrible exploding things... good luck with that. <br><br>Overall, while the difficulty means this isnt for everyone (there IS a &quot;casual&quot; mode though), the sheer fun of it, and the incredible amount of polish, make this the &quot;Megaman&quot; game I'd always wanted, one with endless replay value and stuff to do (and powers that are actually useful).
