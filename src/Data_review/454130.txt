The other samurais would banish and exclude a r\u014dnin, isolating them from their groups.  In the modern world, the word can be used in comparison to a person who has trouble fitting into certain social groups; always wandering around, searching for a place to be at ease.  <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"> Overview\t<\/div><br>Nil-Ninjahstic: Ronin combines the elements of wandering samurai into a platformer.  Essentially a derivative of super meat boy without all the crazy speeding and sliding, you play as an outcast of society, where you\u2019re always picked to do the dirty tasks that no one else wants to do.  As such in this game, you are put through a \u2018test\u2019 where everything and everyone tries to kill you.  In order to pass this initiation, players will need to run, jump and slash through 5 chapters of increasingly difficult levels of platforms and their respective level bosses.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"> The Good\t<\/div><br>Being the third game in the series of Nil-Ninjahtic, the most well done part of the game is the control scheme in relation to character movement.  Blaze Epic has mastered the basic controls for the game throughout 7 games and the result is a smooth and polished feel.  The character does not move to fast and is extremely responsive in terms of fluidity.  Air strafing is done extremely well and allows for predictable pathing and enables speed runs.<br><br>The alternative to running past everything would be to slow chip your way through a level.  Blaze Epic has provided the tools necessary to accommodate for this playstyle by incorporating a sword slash function and block.  Block stops most projectiles and melee attacks, while allowing you to strafe in a direction with no real penalty except moving slower and being unable to turn.  However, it comes at the cost of being not able to counterattack, and limits your movement by restricting the use of block while in midair.  <br><br>Nil-Ninjahtic Ronin might not have the best music around, but it serves its purpose extremely well.  Since there is no auditory feedback from enemies in the game, Nil-Ninjahtic Ronin can afford to play the role of metronome and pace the game for the player.  Entering different areas of the game whether it be in the streets of Japan, or in the locker room of a gym, will change the music to suit the appropriate setting, a feature not usually found in smaller platformer games.  <br><br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"> The Bad <\/div><br>Nil-Ninjahtic: Ronin is a five chapter game with many levels in between.  However, the most obvious flaw is that it only saves at the end of a chapter.  In order to save your progress, one must completely the whole chapter completely.  Since this game\u2019s skill requirements are quite high, players may find it difficult to find the time or even the abilities to make it to the next chapters.  Implementing checkpoint saves between each sub-chapter would allow for greater re-playability and would make the game feel more complete in a sense.<br><br>Though the game\u2019s difficulty increases at a reasonable rate, the game gets pretty frustrating in chapter five where the game slowly becomes more of a chore.  I cannot fathom the amount of times I was so close to completing a level only to accidentally run back into the previous screen, resetting all the progress on the current level, or running through the most annoying chainsaw run only to be slashed down by an edge-guarding samurai on the last platform.  <br><br>Another huge flaw is the fact that the chapters are also too long, with no dialogue in between the smaller sub-chapters.  The small bits of monologue between the chapters feel out of place and only serve to string the game together.  The writing is not that interesting to begin with and by the time you finish a chapter, you will have difficulty remembering what the story was talking about already.<br><br>Nil-Ninjahtic: Ronin not only test your skills, but also your patience as seen with its pixel perfect platforming.  Jumping horizontally into a wall will result in your character bouncing off, but jumping vertically into the ceiling will allow your character\u2019s head to clip through it.  I personally found this out after spending a good 15 minutes trying avoid spikes in a tunnel, thinking I would bounce off the ceiling and die to the buzz saw below.  <br><br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\"> Overall Score <\/div><br>I personally rate this game 7\/10 for the really fun gameplay.
