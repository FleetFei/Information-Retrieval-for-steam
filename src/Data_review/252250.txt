If your colonists need to go outside (to build solar stills for water, wind turbines\/solar arrays for power, collecting researchable things like non-sentient alien creatures and plants, or meteorites, etc) they will use the airlock. To sell this game by its emotional air is mainly noting it's hilariously depressive tone. Everything is terrible: setting up a colony is not easy, and the human condition has so many requirements to retain the spark of life - your people will complain about being sad, tired, hurt, lonely, cold, hungry and thirsty, and then they will break down and die. Sometimes the game may deal you a foul hand by simply ruining the crucial piece of your initial setup, like caving in on your starter construction IMP robot just as you have spent your last building materials, leaving you to wonder who's doing the digging to get up some material production necessitated by your workshop attempting to construct the air processor to stop your folks from the good old freeze-and-choke of having no warmth and breathable air. You'll experiment a bit and learn a few things, browsing your available rooms and items and seeing through the mouse-over descriptions explain a couple things more. The tutorial is not a heavy chore, save for a few bugs at the time of writing this, that may confuse you when you are for instance ordered to build a room, but the tutorial step might not get checked and carry on if you didn't build a large enough room; something which will surely be corrected in improved instruction and polish soon enough.<br><br>All in all I got some bugs, some frustration out of confusion when I could not identify when colonists were not doing their tasks (were it an intermittent alpha AI bug, or was the colonist just too far gone in hunger and depression?), but for someone who looks up to building and growing an operation, nostalgizes about dungeonkeeper-alikes or maybe enjoys these sordid little histories that the game generates with these sorry folks trying to survive, there is a lot of promise in this one.<br><br>I'll be logging in a few feedbacks and a bug report soon and am looking forward to the day when I agree with the level of challenge as entertaining rather than frustrating when a few of my issues are being fixed in the coming versions - so far the developer communication and reactivity has given me nothing but trust for matters to proceed just so. Also, for whatever reason I keep getting pulled back in for a new try even though I feel the current build is just prone to quick failures with unclear or unfair situations in play.
