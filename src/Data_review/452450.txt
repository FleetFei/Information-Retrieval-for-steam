developer for review purposes. This did not influence my review in any way.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">==========================================================<br>\n---------------------------------------OVERVIEW-------------------------------------<br>\n==========================================================<\/div>Rising Islands is a third-person 3D platform game from a new indie development team.<br><br>You play an unnamed heroine with assumed responsibility for saving the world from chaos as a corrupted guardian attempts to use three relics for his own selfish means. By taking the power of dimension swapping and using various acrobatic feats to traverse the broken landscape floating in the sky, your goal is to retrieve the relics and restore their protection to the world. Or at least, that's what I've been able to pick up from a few hours of play, anyway.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">==========================================================<br>\n----------------------------------------QUALITY-------------------------------------<br>\n==========================================================<\/div>Graphics - Rising Islands' graphics vary considerably. The game uses cel shading to achieve a cartoon-like look, but even then, some of the textures are quite bland and the terrain geometry is uninventive and a bit boring, looking rather blocky and dated.<br><br>The character graphics are a mixed bag, too. The main character model is very good, but the textures aren't great. The corrupted guardian model and textures, too, are not that impressive, but the other guardians are quite creative, reminding me of Okami, for some reason.<br><br>The main character animations are really good, but even then there's a bug where they get stuck sometimes and you end up 'flying' around the level, stuck in the jumping animation instead of the sprinting one. The death animation is not that great, though, and the automatic camera sometimes gets stuck, making the screen bounce around like crazy.<br><br>The special effects when changing between the two dimensions and dashing are quite nice. Oh, and the title screen looks very nice, except for the broken model intersections that make it look like the grassy rise on which the character sits is swallowing her. This happens sometimes in game, too; I've had to restart levels because I've been stuck in terrain geometry or fallen through the floor.<br><br>Cut scenes are pretty bad, with a whole lot of seemingly slow motion animations and a lack of good sound effects or voices making them seem very unprofessional when compared with the rest of the game.<br><br>Graphics options are relatively standard for Unity, with a single detail setting ranging from 'Fastest' to 'Fantastic', resolution and windowing options, and anti-aliasing. I encountered a few minor performance issues on some levels on an old gaming laptop, but any new gaming machine should cope very well.<br><br>Sound - Sound effects do the job, but are basically forgettable. There are no voices. Music is great, with a nice oriental theme, but there seems to only be one track through most of the game (and title screen), so it wears thin after a while. The other track (on relic levels) is okay, too, but not quite as impressive.<br><br>Gameplay - Rising Islands plays like a typical 3D platform game: run, climb, and jump as you navigate through each stage from start to finish, collecting sometimes-hidden gems along the way. Each stage provides you with a power crystal, which is eventually used to open up a route to the next of the three game worlds, at the end of which you restore one of the protective relics. Each world of five stages includes a timed stage as well, but I found these more frustrating than fun, particularly the second one.<br><br>Right at the beginning of the game the primary gameplay 'hook' is introduced, albeit poorly, and you are given the ability to swap between two dimensions: red and blue. This gameplay hook is then used for nearly every platforming challenge in the game.<br><br>There's no real in-game explanation for the dimension-swapping ability, however, or for the guardians' choice of the player character, or her ability to run up walls, for that matter. It's unfortunate, too, because providing more information on the character and the game's lore would have added to the experience immensely. Contrast this with something like Prince of Persia (2008) and Rising Islands feels hollow and superficial, with no emotional investment from the player. <br><br>Controls are simple and work reasonably well on both keyboard and controller, with full controller support. I played with keyboard to begin, just to test out the controls, but if you have a controller then use that; it's definitely a superior experience. Unfortunately they're a little bit 'loose', and you'll probably find yourself running off some ledges to your death when you're sure you pressed the jump button.<br><br>There are a few problems with the mostly good level design, too, with some challenges being very frustrating and based at least partly on the uncontrollable camera angle, and some others being easily circumvented (e.g. the first switch puzzle doesn't actually require you to use the switch at all). Prepare for very high frustration levels and rage quitting -- I've found it frustrating enough that I haven't yet been able to force myself to finish it.<br><br>The game includes Steam Achievements that you'll gain by carefully completing the game (with one requiring collection of every hidden crystal), and also includes a time trial mode to increase replayability.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">==========================================================<br>\n-------------------------------------PROS &amp; CONS------------------------------------<br>\n==========================================================<\/div>Pros:<br>+ It's mostly pretty good fun<br>+ Some graphics are very good, including the main character animations and title screen<br>+ Full controller support or useable keyboard and mouse controls; both work well<br>+ Steam Achievements and Trading Cards<br><br>Cons:<br>- Camera, control, and level design frustrations hiding a relatively short game<br>- Game background and lore is minimal, but what is here has some grammar errors and could use a bit of work<br>- There's really only one hook in the game, so it gets a bit boring more quickly than it should<br>- Some graphics look rather dated, and there are lots of geometry intersection issues<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">==========================================================<br>\n--------------------------------------CONCLUSION-----------------------------------<br>\n==========================================================<\/div>Rising Islands is a mostly fun 3D platformer that could do with a bit more polish. It's definitely not at the top of its genre, but it's worth playing.
