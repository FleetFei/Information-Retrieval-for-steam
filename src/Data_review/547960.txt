class=\"bb_h1\">Gameplay:<\/div><br>Space Beret is a sci-fi top down horde based shooter where you are tasked with killing insect like aliens and ramp up your score. First of all, let me get this out of the way: Space Beret is not a bad game for its asking price. It is one of those games where you play in short bursts of around 15-20 minutes then put it down. You play as a marine who is left stranded on a spaceship and is left to fend for himself. That\u2019s the only back story you are going to get out of this game. You start out with a simple gun in a room and you have to kill the aliens coming onto you while getting weapon and armour upgrades. Enemies are different visually but technically differ only because of their hit points. Even the smallest of enemies can kill you with one hit.<br> <br>There are plenty of bugs and glitches present in the game at the time of writing and they can hinder your experience with the game. One of the major bugs is that enemies can kill you when they have not even come in contact with you. This is really annoying especially when coupled with the fact that you only have to get hit once to die.<br><br> The game is brutally hard in the start and will take some time getting used to. Unlike many other top down shooters, the movement of the character is clunky and makes it difficult to move and aim at the same time. But as you put in your time, you start to learn these techniques and adapt to them. There are minor frustrations sometimes like a beam out of nowhere hits and kills you which stops your overall progress but you slowly learn to play around all that. <br><br>There is not much technical variety in Space Beret. There are a few maps to play around in but they all feel the same to play through. The items spawn quickly enough but the starting weapon feels really trash as even the smallest of enemies takes 2-3 hits to die and the game start throwing a lot of enemies from each side from the get go. Also if you just touch the arena\u2019s corner, you will die. <br><br>On the positive side, the UI is really clean and easy. It takes one click to go back to the menu to change levels. There are minimal controls and are listed out in the menu. There are three achievements in the game which are really easy and take 15-20 minutes of your time to be unlocked. <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Visuals<\/div><br>Visually, Space Beret is pretty bland. The levels feature the same colour palette throughout and the enemy designs just feel like bigger versions of the smallest ones. The green blood and weapons do not create a visual spectacle or a gorefest. Aside from the clean and simple UI, Space Beret has nothing going for it visually, you might want to check out the visuals before you buy it as it might not be up everyone\u2019s alley.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Sound<\/div><br>Space Beret features loud techno music which feels apt while you are in the game but feels completely out of place when you boot up the game and it is blasted in your ears. I know for certain that there are many people who won\u2019t like this kind of music and I myself am not sure if I totally like it. It fits the in game action but is too loud.<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Overall<\/div><br>Space Beret is a game that won\u2019t turn any heads. It is a fun little time waster which is suitable for its asking price.
