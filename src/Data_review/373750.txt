Well, theres the tl;dr answer, its awesome, go get it.<br>Now for a proper review...<br><br><b>Game Mechanics<\/b><br>Dinosystem is not your average, typical survival game. It is a deep, in-depth simulation that aims to simulate a prehistoric environment as accurately as possible. Trees aren't just static objects here, they will seed and die and burn. Same goes for sources of water, puddles will form, lakes will dry out in the summer. On top of that, the island you get to roam about in is procedurally generated, so you get to see a slightly different environment every time you start a new game. This level of depth in the mechanics is also shown with the player character and animal species on the island. Dinosaurs need to eat, sleep, drink and to reproduce. Different animals have different behaviours as well, for example, Ankylosaurs are more solitary animals, preffering to wander on their own or in very small groups, whereas Triceratops will move about in large herds.<br><br><b>Player Mechanics<\/b><br>Most survival games will have a basic hunger and thirst system, dinosystem takes this even further, you have to manage your stomach contents, drink and eat enough, not too little, not too much. Every action you perform tires out your character. Depending on your lifestyle, your character can grow to become fit and healthy, or they could get weaker, or fat. The way you play the game determines how your character develops.<br><br><b>Early Access<\/b><br>The game gets updated fairly regularly, roughly fortnightly to monthly. The developer is very dedicated to the project, he is very active on the forums and discussions and we talk pretty regularly about the game and stuff over steam (so, little disclaimer there). Coming very, very soon should be a really awesome update, followed by an even awesome-er one next month :D. So yeah, the commitment to the early access is really good and the developer has a clear plan for the order of the updates, so the finished release is planned out with all the content, there wont be any releasing early here. The finished game should be ready about late 2016.<br><br><b>Gameplay<\/b><br>A lot of the game revolves around keeping your character healthy and... not dead. There is a crafting system with a decent amount of items to craft with what you can salvage, the higher level of which normally require harder-to-get materials, such as dinosaur pelts. The combat and movement will feel strange at first, as it controls just a little differently to some other top-down games (mainly talking about the combat system there) but stick with it, because its worth it and becomes a really rewarding and fun experience. There are 4 very different seasons to each year (so, real life stuff) that force you to change your lifestyle to adapt to the new environmental conditions. Winter is, undoubtedly, the harshest of all the seasons due to the unrelenting cold.<br><br><b>Positive<\/b><br>- Deep, Complex Gameplay<br>- Nice Stylised Graphics<br>- Very Rewarding (especially class=\"postedDate\">Posted: June 5, 2016\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t<div class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\t*UPDATED JANUARY 2017*<br><br>DinoSystem is a top-down survival game with a lot of potential. It's kind of like Don't Starve... with dinosaurs! Although it's currently only in Early Access, I still had fun playing it for 20+ hours. There are major improvements that need to be made before its final release, but it is shaping up to be great game.<br><br>In my very first playthrough a wildfire destroyed half the island, a T-Rex almost ate me, my home got turned into a desert after an intense summer drought, and I tried drinking seawater to survive before dying to a Troodon that I tried to kill as a last resort. 9\/10<br><br>This game looks simple, but it's challenging. If you can manage to live through an entire year, that's a pretty impressive feat. You will get your \u2665\u2665\u2665 kicked by droughts in Summer and by, well, *everything* in Winter.<br><br>POSITIVE:<br><br>+ The &quot;realistic survival mechanics&quot; really have a lot of thought put into them. There are things like sun exposure, wind insulation, humidity, muscle soreness, etc. You need enough protein in your diet to be healthy, so you can't just get by on eating apples, you must go hunt fish or dinosaurs for meat. There are many small details to be aware of in order to survive optimally. <br><br>+ The entire ecosystem is &quot;alive&quot; - animals and plants live, reproduce, and die over time. Dinos never spawn randomly. You can choose to hunt babies for easy prey, but it can lead to the extinction of that species. It really makes you feel like you are part of an ecosystem.<br><br>+ Crafting and leveling system. You can build a hut, fire, etc as well as level up your skills.<br><br>+ Dino AI is interesting. There were some hard times when I came across a large kill and had to share it with Troodons, all of us too weak to risk fighting the other, all deciding to share the same kill. Herbivores sometimes turn aggressive during breeding season. <br><br>+ Island is randomly generated so it's new every game. Good replayability.<br><br>+ God mode lets you observe the world (stop watching the dinosaurs have sex you perverts!!) or move the rocks around so your character can have a better shelter *cough*. <br><br><br>NEGATIVE:<br><br>- Items decay VERY quickly. This is my major gripe with this game. After just one playthrough, I felt annoyed by having to re-craft and repair everything almost constantly. Yes, this game is intended to be hard, but I would prefer a challenge that is less repetitive. <br><br>In Winter, I spent most of my time looking for leaves to repair my thatch. I'd get it fully repaired, go out to get food, and the next day it was time to collect more damn leaves cause hey the thatch was half-destroyed by the wind again. I kept a stockpile of extra leaves in my extra fire pit, but they would decay and disappear extremely quickly. With frequent droughts, eventually the landscape becomes barren and it's nearly impossible to even find sticks or leaves. One day I chopped down a tree, had to come back the next day because there was a lot of wood to carry, and most of the wood I left behind had already disappeared. Axes break down after chopping down 1-2 trees. I collected some meat from a dinosaur carcass and it was spoiled after 2 days. It's not like you can keep killing dinosaurs all the time for meat, since you will run out of dinosaurs, so these things should really be preserved longer. Note: If you keep your items dry and away from sun exposure, they last a bit longer (but still not long enough in my opinion). Hold X to see details about the item's condition.<br><br>- Your skill levels don't really seem to increase your skills by much. Related to the above issue - the game says that if you increase your &quot;Crafting&quot; skill, it increaes the final quality of the items you make. But at level 50 my things are still breaking left and right. Once I put Fishing skill to level 100, and it still sometimes took the whole day to get 1 fish (it's a matter of luck of course, but I feel that at max level your skills should feel stronger). <br><br>- Game is kind of short. I think it would benefit from a higher tier of items and buildings to craft. e.g. Stone house with better durability than thatch, basket that collects rain water; etc. More wildlife would be great. e.g. Small prehistoric mammals you can hunt for more sources of meat, and more big dinos. This would not only give you more to do, but make the game a bit easier as well. Don't like it easy? Difficulty settings are always an option. I know this game is intended to be challenging, but having easier options would probably make it more popular.<br><br>- Jumping is glitchy and kind of useless<br><br>- Herbivores never fight back when they're getting eaten<br><br>- Game came out over a year ago and is still in Early Access. Updates have become less and less frequent.<br><br>NEUTRAL:<br><br>~ Very simple graphics and &quot;animation&quot;<br><br>~ You lose all character progress when you die. I think there should be an &quot;Easy Mode&quot; where you at least keep your levels\/stats, if you want to. Currently, the game's hardest mode has your stats decay if you don't use them, for players who enjoy the challenge of real old-school RPG elements. In &quot;Normal&quot; mode your levels don't decay, but you do lose them upon death.<br><br>~ Movement controls are odd. You hold WASD keys and your character follows the direction your mouse cursor is in, a movement system apparently based off of Notrium. It would be smoother and easier to pick up if it followed the point and click system of Don't Starve, League of Legends, Starcraft, etc -- all games which are very popular and also have a top-down view.  I put this under &quot;Neutral&quot; because some people don't mind it, while other people I know have outright refused to play the game because of it.<br><br><br>Overall it's a fun little dino survival game that is great if you want a challenge.
