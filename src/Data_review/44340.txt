You can flank, suppress fire, hold positions etc. When given the opportunity you'll have access to mortar fire and airstrikes. The complaints about the AI being pretty dumb are unfortunately pretty spot on. Guys will run in front of you, I've had armor wipe out my team and I was run over by HMMWVs more than once. Graphics are servicable and the maps are large but you're also given checkpoints that tell you where to go so they feel limited in that aspect.  Mission variety...well you're in a fire eam so you're going to take and defend buildings, rush the enemy, destroy AA etc. There's a mission where you're falling back position after position because wave after wave of enemies are coming to get you. It can be frustrating but satisfying when you finish. The gunplay is a bit different since you have to account for bullet drop etc. It gets a bit easier as you go along since there's a level up system which allows you to put points towards gun handling, accuracy etc. There is an absolute ton of voice acting and while there were times it was a bit much I didn't mind it too much. Whoever played the Staff Sergeant was very good and reminded me of some of my leadership when I was in the Army. I also got lucky because I had zero issues with the GFWL setup. Saves worked perfectly and got plenty of achievements.  Overall I recommend OF: RR. OF: Dragon Rising is still the superior game but I've seen where you can get both games for $5.
