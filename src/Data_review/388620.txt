Sounds like a good game with lots of replayablity right? Well, yes and no.<br><br>Let me first explain something. This is NOT a Rouge-like! A normal Rouge-like game loves to provide little to no info on ANYTHING, and the only way to figure out what things are, like an item, you have to use it and suffer the effects if it's good or bad. Rouge-likes focus more on trial and error, and whether you feel lucky or not. This game does not do that. This game tells you EVERYTHING you need to know! It tells you exactly what each item does (before you even pick it up,) the stats of each monster, and whether you will survive the fight or not. This is a pet peeve of mine when people miss-use the term Rouge-Like and even Rouge-Lite. This is 100% a puzzle game with slight RPG elements, and heavily relying on RNG. *mini rant over.<br><br>The main objective to DungeonUp is to level up your character's stats, so you can fight and kill the harder monsters without dying, then fighting the boss at the end to move onto the next zone. You repeat this until you finish the game. However! This is not a straight forward run and kill everything. This game requires a little bit of strategy, as most monsters are way more powerful than you at the start. You will be avoiding most monsters at first, finding small upgrades and big upgrades to increase your damage and defense. Only then are you able to start attacking monsters and having them deal less damage to you or no damage at all.<br><br>How the procedurally generated levels work, is there is certain floors that will always be the same, such as the starting room, altar rooms, boss rooms, and certain special rooms. Otherwise everything is randomly generated, and you are at the mercy of RNG. This does provide lots of possibilities, but also limits the game as well. You will end up seeing tons of useless rooms, doors, monsters just being put into areas that you really don't want to waste keys just to go kill that one monster for 2 gold coins.<br><br>You don't gain any XP in this game, instead you earn gold coins from defeating monsters. Your main currency is Gold Coins, and you use it to purchase an item(s) from randomly placed Merchants through out the game, or save them for purchasing upgrades from altars. The upgrades you can purchase from the altars is either small health boost, more attack, or more defense. Each time you purchase 1 upgrade, the price goes up. Now it's in your best interest to NOT buy any upgrades right away, but to actually wait till you get farther to the later alters, as the upgrades are might higher. When you reach the first altar in the first zone, each upgrade only gives you +2. Now if you waited till the third zone, the upgrades will be +8 Attack\/+16 Defense, for the same price! Meaning if you didn't purchase any upgrades, your first upgrade would only be worth 20 coins! You can really up your stats that way and make the game that much easier.<br><br>You also have a second currency, Soul Stones. These are only be acquired from defeating bosses. You can use Soul Stones to purchase rare artifacts in the altar rooms, use them to give you 1 yellow key, or even use them to revive yourself if you die (which reverts you to the start, or last boss you defeated.) Even if you die and choose to retry and it keeps your Soul Stones, making the game slightly easier so you can purchase the expensive rare items.<br><br>Now this is considered a puzzle game, as you only have a limited amount of keys to open doors. There is 3 different keys, Yellow being the common key, Blue keys, and red keys being the boss\/final locked door. You will find these scattered around the levels, mostly guarded by monsters. You can sometimes be lucky and find a hidden passage that can let you bypass a door all together and help you save your keys. There is points in the game where you do need a blue key to progress, and a red key to open the boss battle. You have to plan out your actions, cause if you open too many wrong doors, you can screw yourself over and not be able to progress.<br><br>Now this is where I have a complaint. As all the maps are procedurally generated, you are at the pity of RNG. For most of my games, I've been f*cked over by RNG MANY times! One example is having the game spawn the same rare item 3 times! Causing the other items you need to beat the game to not spawn. My case was having the &quot;Wand of Flight&quot; spawn 3 times, so I could never get the &quot;Holy Cross&quot; and the &quot;Ring of Anti-magic&quot; thus not being able to beat the game. Another case was having all the Blue keys being blocked by enemies that were impossible to kill at my current stage, and could not progress because I needed to pass a blue door. So I could not beat the game.<br><br>So all the boss fights have a &quot;strategy&quot; that will help you either make the boss a little easier to fight, or it's the only way to beat the boss. You are given hints about this from NPCs you find along your path. Some are not as obvious though, and can make certain boss impossible to beat. So you need a guide to be able to beat the bosses. Combine that with the RNG and possibly not spawning the correct item(s) you need to beat said bosses, can make the game seem unfair.<br><br>There is another gamemode called &quot;Maze Mode&quot; where you have limited resources and limited information. Maze Mode is played on a HUGE randomly generated map, where you will end up just going in circles being lost and trying to finish the one map. Having played a few maps, I was not able to complete a single map. I strongly do not recommend playing this mode, as it's not very fun.<br><br>DungeonUp also includes a map editor that can upload to the Steam Workshop.
