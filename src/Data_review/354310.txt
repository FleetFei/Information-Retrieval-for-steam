addictive and challenging puzzle game from Alawar Entertainment.  A fusion of Match 3 and puzzle fair, the gameplay is pretty unique and fun.  And despite looking a bit like Angry Birds in the art department, it is anything but.<br>  <br>Birds are striking back after a gang of nasty cats stole their eggs, but rescuing their offspring may be trickier than they thought!  The gameplay is more complex than it first appears.  We're given a game board with birds and cats sprinkled about.  Matching 3 of the same colored bird will remove them from the board, and to remove the cats and reveal the keys they're hiding you need to surround them with birds of the same color.  This is easier said than done as any move that does not result in a match adds colored eggs to the board.  An additional non-match move and the eggs hatch into full birds which will quite often block your path.   Too many bad moves, and you run the risk of getting blocked in and not being able to complete the stage.  Planning your strategy and working out the best series of moves is required to get through the game.  <br><br><a class=\"bb_link\" href=\"http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=414264314\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noreferrer\"  id=\"dynamiclink_0\">http:\/\/steamcommunity.com\/sharedfiles\/filedetails\/?id=414264314<\/a><br>Each stage has an objective that must be completed in order to earn a gold egg.  While failure doesn't prevent you from progressing on to the next stage (as long as you free the required number of chicks), the gold objective adds an extra layer of strategy to a lot of the game boards and helps bulk up play time as you'll often have to replay levels multiple times to earn gold.  This is something I really enjoy about a lot of these casual games; while you can certainly play them more casually, goals that encourage challenging yourself opens the game to even the not-so-casual crowd.   The difficulty ramps up pretty quickly too, so don't think it'll be easy sailing getting gold eggs on all stages, even the earlier ones will have you stumped and replaying multiple times. As you get further into the game, additional mechanics (bombs, chained birds, fireworks) introduce even more complexity to the level designs, mixing things up and keeping gameplay interesting and challenging.   Some stages are pretty dang clever, too.<br><br>The nice thing is that outside of a handful of stages with timed gold objectives, you can take as much time as you like to complete each.  So if you need to study the game board for a few minutes to plan out your moves, there's no ticking clock to worry about, it's just you and the birds.  This is great for those looking for less hectic &quot;casual&quot; gameplay  but also works well for the times you can't dedicate your full attention to a game.  My only complaint is that the timed levels can be rather hectic and stressful, the later ones taking dozens of tries to perfect, but this is only one small gripe for a game that is a ton of fun otherwise.<br><br>The presentation is top notch, as well.  I don't know why, but the sound effects are one of my favorite parts of the game; I just love the chirps the birds make as they fly off, it's so silly and cute.  The music is also great, reminding me of classic cartoons with their jazzy and lighthearted scores. Graphics aren't anything fancy, but cute and fitting for the type of game this is.  And really, who doesn't like a mustachioed bird?<br><br>With 56 levels and 30 achievements, there's quite a bit to play through.  Progress tracking on the achievements is a much appreciated addition, but they could definitely use Steam Integration.  At least it has trading cards to help sweeten the deal.  Although it took a bit to get the hang of the mechanics, I'm now squarely hooked on these birds.  There's just something about those trickier levels that makes you feel awesome once you finally snag gold.  Claws &amp; Feathers is definitely worth a look if you're searching for a good puzzler without (for the most part) the craziness of time crunch games. <br><br>Bottom Line: Recommended for strategy game enthusiasts and anyone needing to scratch that puzzle game itch. <br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">Note - This game was provided by the publisher with the request to write an informative
