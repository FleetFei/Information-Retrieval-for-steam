Alien<br><b>Developer:<\/b>  Devdan Games<br><b>Publisher:<\/b>  Devdan Games<br><b>Genre:<\/b>  First Person Shooter<br><br><div class=\"bb_h1\">General Impression<\/div>I have to admit that there are few examples that come to mind, in which the environments take center stage within a video game. And yet Action Alien is just such a title. A development debut for the team which have published it on Steam, the first person shooter I had the chance to review today is not trying to stand out at all, but merely to project the developer\u2019s vision into something palpable. I can respect that and I always try to encourage any idie dev at the start of his or her career.<br><br>By no means does my introduction imply, that I shall not judge the game through the same lenses I use for all my previous reviews. There are plenty of flaws in Action Alien and I fully intend to discuss them in detail. First of, I wish to present the more pleasant aspects which ultimate convinced me that this might still be a game worthy of being recommended. Though it features only three, medium-sized maps, there is plenty of room to maneuver, avoid or fall back from the foes that shall become increasingly more violent and aware of your presence, as you accomplish primary objectives. What is the mission in question? Survival and escaping from the alien menace. That is easier said than done since the will try to swarm you, wave after wave and there will be times, in which running from them and seeking ammo crates and simply resting and reloading your gun, shall prove to be just moments of respite.<br><br>The Wasteland map, presents a Route 66 alike scenario with a scenic approach towards the abandoned motels and gas stations that contain your necessary tools and weapons in order to survive the alien onslaught. \u201cSurvive\u201d is also the name of a spin-off map similar to the first one mentioned, but with increased difficulty and my favorite out of these 3, has to be \u201cThe Tropical Hotel\u201d. A mix of elements from both the lush jungle landscapes of Far Cry 1 and Dead Island\u2019s beach bungalows (themselves copied from real-life island of Bali). You even have some temple ruins within that jungle terrain and my comparison to Far Cry 1 (released 13 years ago), is still an achievement for an indie dev. The cherry on top for these maps is that they also contain destructible structures.<br><br>I had to write about the maps and their static assets first, since the parts about the weaponry and the alien enemies, have not been to my liking at all. They both look as it the game\u2019s still in an Alpha stage of development. Ugly textures and poor animations that really stand out in a negative way when compared to the landscapes and how nicely textured those are. So if you would like to purchase this title, at least know what you can expect from a visual perspective. The sounds themselves were more than decent as well as the general gameplay. It\u2019s an indie FPS alright, that much is certain from the get-go. But it still manages to be fun through sheer diversity of weapons and enemy types, no matter how poorly rendered they are.<br><br>I don\u2019t consider Action Alien overpriced but if you\u2019re still uncertain, wait until you can pick it up during a Steam Sale or from a bundle and surely you won\u2019t regret it then. I do hope the developer will attempt to provide some better textures to this game or at the very least, learn from his first project and pay more attention to details next time.<br><br><div
