for free<\/div>\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t<div class=\"content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShadows of Kurgansk is a singleplayer, soon to be multiplayer, Survival Simulation game with elements of horror developed by Yadon Studio and released on Steam in Early Acess on October 9th, 2015 for $14.99. It releases into an oversaturated genre that is riddled with sub-par copies and has very few successful titles. Is it yet another poor attempt or is it one of the few games that actually gets it right? <br><br><br>Surprisingly, I say yes, even at this current build. It gets a lot of things right and has quite a few minor quirks that set it back slightly.  Only time will tell though if the polish shines through enough to push this game past the mediocrity that is so common in this genre. All we can do right now is look at the game as it is and speculate its future. <br><br><br><b>The Visuals:<\/b> First off, how are the visuals? Nobody wants to play a game that isn't visually pleasing to them. Shadows of Kurgansk sports a dreary, lower quality version of Borderlands' graphics. Everything has a greenish-gray hue to it that is totally reminiscient of post nuclear radioactive landscape. There are clouds that perpetually look down upon the landscape and provide a haze that dulls everything around you. I like this aspect, It makes me feel like I'm in a dangerous enviroment that has horrible plans for me. The textures are of the lower quality that you expect from the lesser known titles in this genre; They're not ugly, but they're not particularly pretty. They simply get by. The animations also look decent, you won't notice anything bad nor good about them. They also simply get by. The one quirk I have with the visuals, is that the trees bleed when you chop them. That simply doesn't make sense, trees shouldn't have red blood shoot out when you hit them. <br><br><br><b>Gameplay:<\/b> As said before no one wants to play anything that isn't visually pleasing to them. Well, that goes double for gameplay; absolutely nobody wants to play a game that plays like rubbish. This game has many features that other survival games have, in fact it generally has all of them. Except a jump button. Please add a jump button, It's 2016, we like to jump off of things. The crafting system is actually really nice, every material can be found in the open world and harvested or picked up, and there are quite a bit of items you can craft. What's brilliant about this game though, is that everything has a purpose and at some point you will need to craft them. Whether it be shelter, clothing, food, medicines, or weapons, everything has a vital purpose. For example, the campfire is used for more than just cooking food and providing warmth like in other survival games. It is actually one of the most vital things in this game; it keeps you sane at night. I know, you can go insane in this game, how cool is that? The reason is that there are anomalies that appear at night and ravage your psyche causing you to go mad and blackout, waking confused somewhere far from where you were, much like a college student's typical friday night. Whilst these ones have a cool side affect, there are others that are absolutely deadly, ranging from poisonous clouds to electrical storms that fry your insides. These are generally easy to avoid, and if you run into them there are concotions you can craft that will dull their affects and regain your sanity. But, those aren't the only terrors that go bump in the night. There are beasts and zombie like creatures, most likely the natives gone mad from exposure to anomalies, whose only purpose is to end your life. These can be extremely frightening at night, the sounds they make and their swift attacks out of nowhere will leave you short of breath. While the beasts are only really a problem at night, these zombies are out at all hours of the day. They hit like a train and are very fast, so a range weapon is recommended to take them out quickly at a distance. <br><br><br><b>Modes:<\/b> You may be thinking, what does he mean modes? Aren't survival games open world sandboxes that you just spawn into and play? Well, yes they are, but this one sports three modes, including a story with two different story lines. There's story, survival, and adventure. Story mode is exactly how it sounds, you spawn in and follow a story line, completing quests while crafting to survive. The cool thing about this is that the story isn't complete rubbish, you actually want to find out what happened this area to make it so hostile. The quests are generally crafting and fetch quests, the characters are voice acted and the dialogue is decent. They can get repetitive, but it's a refreshing change in this genre of games. The story progresses nicely as well, never having you stray too far from the main storyline. This mode is the most complete and has the most to do, you will spend most of your time here. Next, survival mode. This is just like every other game, you spawn, you craft, you die. Except, when you die, you can spend points to get perks or items for your next playthrough. This is a really nice mechanic that promotes replayability, now when you die after 30 in game days of survival you won't feel like you wasted your time. However, it is too easy to play it safe. I eventually went looking for trouble for lack of excitement. This ended in my death, anomalies don't play around. Honestly, I didn't spend much time in this mode. It got boring, the map is farely small and there's not much to do. More needs to be added to actually promote play in this mode. Lastly, we have adventure mode. This is a mix of survival and story. You're free to survive as normal, but have the option to leisurely complete quests as you do it. Now, this may sound great, but the quests are a bit lackluster and you don't feel the need to complete them like in the story mode. It also has the same faults as the survival mode, it's generally unfun to play. This game really shines with its unique story mode, I recommend staying there to fully enjoy your time playing. <br><br><br><b>Extra:<\/b> Shadows of Kurgansk has been in Early Access for nine months now. In that time Yadon Studio has accomplished quite a bit. The menus are responsive and pleasant looking. There are multiple settings for you to tinker with, including graphics, controls, and sound. The sound is also top notch, at night you hear everything, it really adds a sense of tension, especially when you leave your campsite and start to hear weird noises all around you. This game has plenty of polish already accomplished, they've gotten the hard stuff out of the way, the rest is just time to make it into a full game.<br><br><br>I did crash a couple times. hopefully the devs can fix this right away - Crash report folder - 2016-07-14_155448<br><br><br><b>Overall:<\/b> If you enjoy survival games and want a story to go along with that, then I totally recommend Shadows of Kurgansk for $14.99. Not only do you get an enjoyable game, but it isn't even finished yet and the price may go up once it's out of Early Access, so get it while it's cheap.
