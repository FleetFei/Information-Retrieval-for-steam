Thing is, Timegate filed for bankruptcy just short of the game's release date, so half of the game was incomplete and the release date was approaching rapidly. Thus, Gearbox had to rush to fill in the holes as quickly as they could, which is why the singleplayer was so awful - because it was rushed in a desperate attempt to cover for another company's failed work. Does it justify how bad the base game's campaign is? He's the giant &quot;reveal&quot; in Colonial Marines, but as overjoyed as I was to see and hear him again, I was kind of left empty since the game didn't really explain what had happened. They DID, but not effectively.<br><br>Stasis Interrupted does that, and it does that well. The story does focus on Hicks, but also it ties into the events of Alien 3 (the highlight is showing the origins of the body that was thought to be his when the prison medical detail examined the EEV crash in the film), and the events of the main game, and even alludes to things that we didn't see during Aliens, including an audio log that contains a message that Burke sent to Weyland-Yutani confirming that there were in fact Aliens on the planet and that he had a backup plan on how to smuggle them off-planet, and Newt recording something of a thank-you letter to Hicks for how nice he was to her compared to the other Marines. It's really close to home for Aliens fans.<br><br>There are three main issues with Stasis Interrupted, however, that deserve emphasis. Number one; even though the story revolves around Hicks, you don't play as him - there are three missions with three separate characters; the first is a colonist woman waking up on the ship that brought the Aliens onboard the USS Sulaco, the second is a retired Marine who wakes Hicks up from hypersleep and helps him escape the ship, and the third is a rogue Weyland-Yutani employee who tries to help the Corporal send the distress call we see in the opening cutscene of the base game.<br><br>Issue #2 is that it's the same game. The same engine. Same bugs. It's much more of an improvement, since Gearbox and the other studio that was brought in to work on it were both working together on it and weren't rushed in any way, so they were able to make a finished expansion campaign, which was good, but they didn't rewrite the game engine or anything, so you'll still have problems with glitches and \u2665\u2665\u2665\u2665.<br><br>Issue #3; a very minor one, is that the only value in playing this DLC is both to get a second helping of lore and to bag some achievements. You cannot rank up, complete challenges, or gain experience points in any of the three Stasis Interrupted levels, nor can you use or customize your weapons (including DLC weapons; only the base game weapons are available, and only the base, unupgraded variants are usable). The hard part is that you don't even initially begin with all weapons; you gain them as you progress further through the game. It isn't enough to condemn the DLC, but it is kind of frustrating.<br><br>So final verdict: buy the season pass if you want to try this, since you'll get so many other content bundles with it, but don't buy it standalone for full price.
